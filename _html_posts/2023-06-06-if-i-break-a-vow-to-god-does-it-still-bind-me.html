--- title: '"If I break a vow to God, does it still bind me?"' date: '2023-06-06' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Woody Allen once said, "If you want to make God laugh, tell Him about
your plans."

I'm curious. You say you broke a vow to God. So what you did is you
said, "God, I promise I will never do X," and you consider that a vow to
God?

Yep, I can hear Him laughing too.

News flash: *You didn't make a vow to God.* You told God your plans,
and He laughed. In order to be a true vow, you would have to make a
promise to someone, and they would have to agree to accept your promise.
Like in marriage, I pledge myself to my wife, and she pledges herself to
me, and we do this in front of witnesses, where we make a vow to each
other. You can't really do that with God.

It doesn't really matter what you think you're going to do. It doesn't
really matter what you say you're going to do. And it doesn't really
matter what you promise God you're going to do. **God is going to
fulfill His plans through you**. Not your plans, His plans.

He has a plan for you, to give you a hope and a future (cf Jeremiah
29:11). And God does not "change His mind" just because you "made a vow"
to Him. He's still going to fulfill His plans through you.

So instead of making promises to God that you simply do not have the
power of keeping, maybe you should instead ask God to show you His
plans, and allow you to come alongside Him and assist Him with
fulfilling His plan for your life *with Him*. It's much easier to work
together with God than to try to do your own thing, even if you promised
to do it.
