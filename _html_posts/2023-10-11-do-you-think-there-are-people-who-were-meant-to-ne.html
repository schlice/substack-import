Content: 

While it might be difficult to think about it, the answer is yes.
This is something Christian believers understand as part of the doctrine
of predestination.

Predestination is the Christian doctrine that states that before the
universe was created, God decided the eternal destiny of all rational
creatures (angels and humans). God's choice to save certain people is
called election.

Predestination may not be the central theme of the Bible, but it is, in
fact, biblical doctrine, not mere speculation. Predestination is
discussed all over the Bible:

Predestination is a feature of the larger doctrine of *salvation by
grace alone* (*sola gratia*), which states that it is God's power,
wisdom, and righteousness that save us, not anything we do.

That said, He predestines those He saves to salvation *through Christ*
and His salvific work on the cross. Those who have been predestined,
therefore, must still have faith in Christ (*sola fide, soli Christo*)
in order to be saved. Therefore, you cannot simply say, "If God has
predestined that I will be saved, then I don't have to believe or go to
church." God determines both the end and the means by which you
are saved.

We might say that God is evil because He didn't choose to save everyone.
But actually, considering the fact that we are all sinners, the correct
question should be, "Why doesn't God send everyone to hell?"
Unconditional election is the friend of sinners, not the enemy, because
without it no one would be saved.

I know it's a tough doctrine to wrestle with, but that doesn't make it
unbiblical. It doesn't make it worthy of our ignorance, either. We
should understand it and rejoice in it, because God has given us hope
through Christ.
