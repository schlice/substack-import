--- title: '"How can I tell the difference between civilian and military aircraft with only my eyesight?"' date: '2017-01-27' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Unless you know the actual aircraft (model, manufacturer, etc), it would
be challenging to distinguish a military aircraft from a civilian
aircraft while in flight.

If the aircraft is close enough for you to see markings, you might be
able to make out military designations. For example, Air Force aircraft
will usually say "USAF" on them somewhere. You may also see this image
on the aircraft:

This is called a *roundel.* Military aircraft used these for quick
visual identification, allowing others to see quickly which nation or
alliance the aircraft belonged or was aligned with. The image above is
the current roundel in use by the Air Force, Army, Navy, and Marine
Corps. We have been using roundels on aircraft since the early 1900's.

Expedition](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a1a876&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pancho_Villa_Expedition){.external_link

You can see this A-10 has a *subdued* roundel on the side. There's
little doubt who flies this pig, so the roundel is more of a marking of
historical and traditional significance rather than identification of a
US Armed Forces aircraft. Note that this roundel will also be present on
the top and bottom sides of the wings, for easy visual identification.
