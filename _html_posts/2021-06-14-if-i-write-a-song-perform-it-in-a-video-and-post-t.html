--- title: '"If I write a song, perform it in a video, and post that video on YouTube, would that be enough to protect my rights so that people know I wrote it?"' date: '2021-06-14' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

First of all, as soon as you write a song, you have a copyright over it.
It is yours, and no one can legally copy it.

The problem is *protecting your work*. The way to do that is to file for
copyright protection. [[This
pamphlet](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=047131&url=https://www.copyright.gov/circs/circ50.pdf){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="copyright.gov"}]{.qlink_container} explains the process to
go through to register your song's copyright. *That* would be enough
to protect your rights so that no one can reuse your music.

Recording your song on YouTube is not enough to protect your rights.
Filing for copyright protection is.

\

Original question:

If I write a song, perform it in a video, and post that video on
YouTube, would that be enough to protect my rights so that people know I
wrote it?
