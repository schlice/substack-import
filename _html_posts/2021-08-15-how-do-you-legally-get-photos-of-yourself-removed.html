--- title: '"How do you legally get photos of yourself removed from a website that is displaying them without your consent, and are refusing to remove them?"' date: '2021-08-15' tags: - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

If the website does not have your permission to post the photo over
which you have copyright, you can send them a DMCA Takedown Notice.

[https://copyrightalliance.org/fa\...(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=404049&url=https://copyrightalliance.org/faqs/how-to-send-dmca-takedown-notice/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="copyrightalliance.org"}]{.qlink_container}

Larger service provider sites even have DMCA forms online that you can
simply fill out.

Of course, if they still refuse to take it down, you will have to retain
a copyright attorney and sue them for violating DMCA. So at a certain
point, the answer will come down to "you need a lawyer."
