**Will the developers of a commercial game be in trouble if an artist
suddenly changes the license of the music used in the game from
something like CC-BY to a non-commercial license when the game has
already been released?**

CC licenses are non-revocable. Once a content creator publishes content
under CC, licensees can use that content based on the terms of that
agreement for the duration of that license.

You can certainly stop distributing new content under the CC license
at any time, but once you publish content using the CC license, you
cannot retroactively go back and re-license that existing content, or
change the terms of the license agreement on content already released
under CC. Anyone who has a copy of that CC-licensed content can continue
to use it based on the terms of that license.

As a content creator, therefore, you should think carefully before
distributing your content under CC.\[1\](#TCUCK){#cite-TCUCK
