Is forcing people to wear masks against ADA?

Let's see if we can bring all this together.

- Businesses are required under ADA to provide reasonable
  accommodations to those with disabilities.
  - Disabilities are well defined
- The inverse of that is that businesses are not required to do business
  with someone when reasonable accommodations cannot be met.

To that end, there are HHS guidelines around acceptable accommodations
for those who cannot wear masks; primarily, this is face shields. The
business is not required to provide those face shields. In other
words, if you can't wear a mask, it is your responsibility to provide a
face shield in its place.

**If you cannot wear either, there is no requirement that the business
allow you entrance.** The business does not have to alter its business
so drastically that it affects others. Allowing disabled patrons to
simply wear nothing is too great a risk to the business and to other
customers. Therefore, the business must provide some other accommodation
for those who cannot wear a mask: service at your car, home delivery,
curbside pick-up, etc.
