Can someone get in trouble for lying about military service?

As with all things legal, the answer is "it depends." What it depends on
is *whether or not you get financial gain from your lie.*

- If you do, this is a crime. At the state level, this is called
  theft of service. At the Federal level, this is prohibited under
  the Stolen Valor Act.
- If you do not, then it is technically not illegal to lie about
  being a military veteran, or being active duty, or being a retiree.
  It's protected by the 1st Amendment, and case law has held the same
  thing. There are Federal laws which prohibit non-military personnel
  from wearing military uniforms, but they are largely unenforceable
  because of 1A protections.
