Proving medical malpractice is difficult. You basically need to prove

- The physician and/or other health care providers *neglected* to follow
  standard, accepted medical procedures.
- Because of this negligence, the physician and/or other health care
  providers *misdiagnosed* you.
- Because of the misdiagnosis, you suffered preventable physical,
  emotional and/or financial harm.

If you believe that the doctor did not follow standard, accepted medical
practices which led to the misdiagnosis, you should definitely engage
the services of a medical malpractice attorney where you live. They can
help you file a lawsuit and receive compensation for some or all of
these potential damages your mom may have suffered because of the
misdiagnosis:

- Mental anguish
- Physical pain and suffering
- Diminished capacity to enjoy life
- Loss of companionship and guidance for family members
- Current and future medical expenses
- Lost income
- Diminished earning capacity
- Other damages recoverable under your state's laws

Seek a consultation with a medical malpractice attorney in your area
sooner rather than later, or the time period within which you can bring
an action may expire.

My mom was diagnosed with MS in 2011. She took many medications
