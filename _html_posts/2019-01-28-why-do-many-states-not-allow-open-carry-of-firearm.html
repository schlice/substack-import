**Why do many states not allow open carry of firearms? Wouldn\'t it be
better for police to see when someone is armed from a distance so they
won\'t be surprised, in addition to deterring anyone about to commit a
crime?**

Your assertion is incorrect. "Many" states do allow open carry of
firearms. "Many" is generally accepted to mean "at least half," and
that's not even true.

> Thirty-one states allow the open carrying of a handgun without any
> license or permit, although in some cases the gun must be unloaded.
> Fifteen states require some form of license or permit in order to
> openly carry a handgun.\[1\](#AAwJR){#cite-AAwJR .citation_link}

So by this count, there are at least 46 states that allow open carry in
some form or another.

However, there are *many* cities within those states which restrict open
carry, as they are permitted to do. *Why* they do this is entirely up to
them. The mayors of these cities have determined, for better or worse,
that their city would be a better place if no one was allowed to openly
carry firearms. [[Cities have done this since the beginning of the
nation](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=65700c&url=https://www.smithsonianmag.com/history/gun-control-old-west-180968013/){.external_link
