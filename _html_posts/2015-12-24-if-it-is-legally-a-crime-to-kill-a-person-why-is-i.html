--- title: '"If it is legally a crime to kill a person, why is it legal for the criminal justice system to kill the offender?"' date: '2015-12-24' tags: - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

The simple answer:

Murder is defined as \"the *unlawful* taking of a life with
forethought and malice.\" An execution is not murder because the taking
of the life is not unlawful.

Execution is a lawful taking of life because it follows a legal process
documented in state law. Therefore, execution, when conducted by a state
after the state-determined process is completed (in accordance with the
law), does not meet the standard of murder.
