I was treated using a similar protocol to Dr. Coimbra's approach. I was
on 5000 IUs of Vitamin D for a year because my Vitamin D levels were so
low.

I ended up with kidney stones.

Turns out, high-dose vitamin D is [[really bad for
you](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=947750&url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7218089/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. From the study:

> Vitamin D intoxication might become clinically relevant in persons
> using very high doses (mostly \>50,000 IU/d), resulting in serum
> 25(OH)D concentrations â‰¥150 ng/mL (375 nmol/L). These doses and serum
> concentrations can lead to hypercalcemia, hypercalciuria, and
> hyperphosphatemia, which can manifest as nausea and emesis, muscle
> weakness, polyuria, calcification of the kidneys, and in extreme cases
> kidney failure. Therefore, ultra-high-dose vitamin D regimens such as
> the "Coimbra-protocol" in MS with suggested doses of up to 400,000
> IU/d pose a considerable safety hazard for patients.

I guess I was fortunate that my hypercalciuria *only* led to calcium
oxylate kidney stones, after which I (wisely) stopped the vitamin D
therapy.

This type of therapy should only be done under the strict guidance and
supervision of a doctor, and as of yet, there are no repeatable, proven
results.

We know that MS occurs with greater frequency in higher latitudes. It is
*presumed* that this is due to the prophylactic effect of vitamin D in
the development of MS. However, we also know that beyond age 15, moving
to a new location (i.e. with lower rates of MS, or a lower latitude)
won't manifest in the current generation, but in the *next* generation.
So the effect of increasing vitamin D in adults and its effect on MS
have yet to be proven, at best.

As always, take everything you read on the internet with *massive*
grains of salt, never make any extreme dietary changes without
consulting your doctor, and never ever change your medications yourself
without your doctor's approval.
