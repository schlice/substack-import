**While traveling abroad for years, I\'ve routinely seen mobile phone
SIM cards being re-loaded at 7/11. Why isn\'t this available in the
US?**

On the contrary, this is definitely available in the US. It's just less
popular.

What you're referring to is called prepaid cellular. Airtime is paid
for up front, before it is used. You can either refill your airtime by
buying a card at a retailer (like 7--Eleven or Walmart), or you can use
the phone itself and enter a credit card number to refill your airtime
over the phone. These phones require no contracts.

Most US users use postpaid cellular. You are billed at the end of
the month for your usage, after you use it. These are contract services.
