What makes a sign ADA compliant?

ADA has many requirements for signage, but there are a few rules of
thumb to follow when it comes to signs.

Size and Space Requirements

For 5/8 inch letters (the most common size), you need to allow 2 inches
of height per line of text, including the Braille translation below. For
larger text sizes, allow 2 times the height of the characters, plus 1
inch per line of Braille. This is a general guideline; actual space
needed may be more or less depending on the text.

Other spacing requirements:

- Pictograms: You need a 6 inch high area for a pictogram. There are no
  rules regarding the actual height of the pictogram, but 4 to 4.5
  inches is a good starting point.
- Braille: You need to leave a minimum of 3/8 inch clearance on all
  sides of Braille lettering.

Text

Text should be in simple, sans serif fonts in bold weight. Do not use
script, italics, or other hard-to-read text decorations. All text must
be UPPERCASE. Lowercase lettering is only allowed on visual-only signs,
such as pictures, directories, or direction
signs.\[1\](#LFFYR){#cite-LFFYR .citation_link}
