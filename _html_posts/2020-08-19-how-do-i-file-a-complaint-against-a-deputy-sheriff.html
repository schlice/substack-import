How do I file a complaint against a deputy sheriff?

It's easy. Just visit the Sheriff's Office.

You will have to swear to your statement. This means you will have to
sit in an interview room, fill out your complaint, show proof of
identity, and then swear that your statement is true to the best of your
knowledge, and acknowledge that you can be prosecuted for knowingly
giving a false statement to the police. Just be aware you must do this.

You must also be aware that there are always three sides to every story:
your side, his side, and the truth, which is usually somewhere between
the two sides. The truth is most generally not in anyone's story because
reality is colored by our view of it, which includes our biases as well
as physiological issues which cause us to remember things differently
than they happened. Therefore, while you may issue a complaint, the
results of that complaint are up to the department to address. Perhaps
the deputy will be counseled. Perhaps he will need to attend additional
training. Or, if there is a group of people who have gone though the
effort to file a complaint, perhaps the deputy will be officially
reprimanded, or maybe investigated. But not always. You must accept that
the best you can do is to file a complaint, unless you plan on retaining
an attorney and suing the department because you believe the deputy
violated your rights or the law.
