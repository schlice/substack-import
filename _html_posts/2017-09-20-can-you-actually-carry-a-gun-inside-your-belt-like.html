--- title: '"Can you actually carry a gun inside your belt like in the movies?"' date: '2017-09-20' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

*Can* you carry a gun like that? Sure you can. All you have to do is
stick it there, and you can carry it. It may not be lawful in your
jurisdiction to carry a firearm in this manner, but not every
jurisdiction has carry laws dictating how you must carry a firearm.

*Should* you do this? Absolutely not.

Modern firearms are designed to only discharge when the trigger is
pulled. Therefore, to prevent accidental discharge, you must protect the
trigger while the firearm is being carried.

*This means you have to carry that firearm in a holster.* This is
the only way to safely carry a loaded firearm, if it's not in your hand
under positive control.

If you fail to do this, you risk accidental discharge. Here's just a few
stories of recent self-inflicted gunshot wounds caused by improper carry
of a firearm:

There are various scenarios here, but the root cause of all of these
accidental shootings is improper carry of a firearm.

The moral of the story: never believe anything you see on television,
Don't tuck a firearm into your waistband, ever. Always carry a firearm
in a holster. If you want to tuck the firearm inside your waistband, you
need an "inside-the-waistband" holster (otherwise known as an IWB
holster). Don't be lazy and stick it in a pocket or just shove it in
your pants. It's not like cell phones which went from waistband holsters
to pockets because it's cool. It's definitely not cool.
