--- title: '"Do service members on military transport aircraft get served hot meals? Is it better or worse than airline meals?"' date: '2017-11-16' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Box Nasties. Also known as
[A-Rations(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d5a153&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-ration){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. These are "cold meals"
which generally consist of a protein, some fruit, some pre-packaged
snack items, and a drink. A-Rations are distinguished from B-Rations,
which consist of mostly canned and preserved items for areas where
refrigeration is not readily available.

Notice I didn't say anything about heating, because that's a luxury.

As you board the cargo hold of the aircraft you'll be stuck in for the
next several hours, you're handed a nondescript white box and a set of
foam earplugs. Yay.

The last time I did this, I boarded a C-141 (yeah, just dated myself) to
fly from Peterson AFB in Colorado to Hickam AFB in Hawaii. My box nasty
had a few pieces of cold fried chicken, an apple, some assorted snack
items which I don't remember, and a water. You should also note that the
141 was decked out in passenger mode, with pallets attached to the cargo
deck with "airline seats" (a generous term for these seats) as well as a
"comfort pallet" which was a self-contained unit housing two full
bathrooms. For long-haul passenger flights, cargo aircraft are often
fitted with these, but not necessarily for passenger comfort, but
because it made it easier to fit more people inside than could be fit on
"jump seats."
