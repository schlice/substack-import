--- title: '"What do doctors, not know about multiple sclerosis? Even my neurologist seems astonished when I describe symptoms"' date: '2017-01-24' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Your relationship with your care team is vital. You want a neurologist
who will listen to your concerns and work with you, as a team. If this
does not describe your relationship with your neurologist, it's time to
shop around.

[Fatigue(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4c2dce&url=http://www.nationalmssociety.org/Symptoms-Diagnosis/MS-Symptoms/Fatigue){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} is one of the
most common symptoms of MS. One type of MS fatigue even has its own
name: *MS Lassitude*. It's estimated that over 80% of people with MS
have experienced this symptom at some point, some more severely than
others. I certainly know I have, but not all the time. When I push
myself in the heat, it kicks my butt at the end of the day. I've
literally fallen asleep in my clothes because I was so drained. I had to
have my wife drive us home one night after a particularly exhausting day
in the heat. I sat in the seat and instantly realized that there was no
way I was going to be able to safely drive home. It's a learning
process; you learn how hard you can push yourself, and you learn what
the consequences are to doing so.

Sorry that I don't have SSDI experience, but [[your local MS Society
chapter](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=28f09c&url=http://www.nationalmssociety.org/Chapters){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} has people who
do. Please get in contact with them and they can find you someone who
can assist you with your claim.
