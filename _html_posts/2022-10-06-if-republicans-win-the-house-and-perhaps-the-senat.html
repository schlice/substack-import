--- title: '"If Republicans win the House, and perhaps the Senate, should they impeach Joe Biden? On what charges would be of greatest effect?"' date: '2022-10-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

> *If you do what you've always done, you'll get what you've always
> gotten. --- Tony Robbins*

Presidents have been getting away with more and more these days, because
no one is stopping them.

In 2012, former President Obama said, more than 20 times, that he
did not have the authority to suspend deportations through executive
order. Some of his quotes from that time:

- "because there are laws on the books that Congress has passed. \[...
  Those\] laws on the books by Congress, are very clear in terms of how
  we have to enforce our immigration system."
- "I'm not a king, \...\ I'm not the emperor of the United States,"
- "There is a path to get this done and that is through Congress,"

Then he did it anyway.

In 2019, former President Trump tried to get funding for his border
wall, and Congress would not provide it. In a desperate attempt to do
what he wanted anyway, he found an "emergency provision" that allowed
him to re-appropriate certain money that had already been allocated by
Congress to the wall...by declaring a national emergency at the southern
border. And he took \$6.5 billion from the Treasury. "The president's
declaration is a grotesque abuse of power," said Elizabeth Goitein,
co-director of the Brennan Center's Liberty & National Security Program.
"Emergency powers are for emergencies, not for circumventing the will of
Congress on questions of policy."\[1\](#qWXJb){#cite-qWXJb
.citation_link}

After watching two former presidents get away with violating the law
without consequence, Biden believes that he can do it as well. He
"double, triple, quadruple checked" whether or not he had the power to
implement yet another eviction moratorium without Congress. As he
suspected, he did not.

Then he did it anyway.

His defense? \"I can\'t guarantee you the court won't rule that we
don\'t have that authority but at least we\'ll have the ability to, if
we have to appeal, to keep this going for a month-at least. I hope
longer.\"\[2\](#hQEsY){#cite-hQEsY .citation_link}

In other words, I know it's illegal, because I double, triple, quadruple
checked, but since it's going to take you guys so long to bring charges
against me that I'm just going to do what I want and screw the law.

But why is this worse than what Trump did? Trump took \$6.5 billion.
Biden has now taken **between 100 and 200 times that
amount.**\[3\](#XMplJ){#cite-XMplJ .citation_link} Trump found a
"legal loophole" by declaring a national emergency. The unethical part
was declaring the national emergency *after* Congress denied his funding
request. Biden, on the other hand, is intentionally doing something he
knows is illegal, because he looked it up. He's simply relying on the
fact that prosecutions take time so he can unlawfully funnel money to
the places he wants it to go, with the full knowledge that even if a
court finds that his money-transfer program is illegal, the money's
already gone. The best the court could do is to tell him to stop. There
are, he posits, no other ramifications.. That's not a "legal loophole,"
that's bald-faced theft.

Presidential violations of law continue to happen, and they keep getting
worse.

Congress should have impeached Obama when he did it. Congress should
have impeached Trump when he did it. If Congress allows this violation
to stand, what laws can we pass restricting the President's actions that
will ever be able to stand? The President will exercise unlimited
authority with no consequence. If we continue allowing these
presidential violations of law, we will have to live with the system
that we get --- a president who is far more powerful than the
Constitution ever intended...and we won't be able to stop him.

**TL;DR: Congress should impeach Biden for one of the most egregious
violations of Article II of the Constitution that we have ever seen in
this country.**

\

Original question:

If Republicans win the House, and perhaps the Senate, should they
impeach Joe Biden? On what charges would be of greatest effect?
