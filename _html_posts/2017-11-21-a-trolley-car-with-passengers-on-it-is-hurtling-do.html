--- title: '"A trolley car with passengers on it is hurtling down a track heading for a cliff. There is a switch that will divert the car to safety. Is a bystander required by law to pull that switch?"' date: '2017-11-21' tags: - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

No.

In the United States, there is no law that requires a civilian assist in
any circumstance. There are a handful of states with a limited set of
circumstances under which civilians are required to report, but even
there, there is no duty to assist.

The best we have is a series of Good Samaritan laws which protect
bystanders from liability when they do intervene.
