--- title: '"How much must I walk in order to reap the health benefits of walking? Is walking enough to help my health and cognitive abilities? Must I necessarily brisk walk?"' date: '2016-03-03' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

First of all, anything is better than nothing.

But if you can walk, 10,000 steps a day is a good goal to shoot for.
Using a simple pedometer that tracks steps taken (there are many
available for very little money), you can easily achieve this goal. For
most people, it\'s about 5 miles of walking.

Yes, but does it help?

> A 2011
> [study(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=15d6d9&url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3169444/){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} published in the
> *International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity*
> found walking 10,000 steps a day had a significant impact on body fat
> and waist and hip measurements when compared to walking 6,000 steps a
> day. Women who averaged more than 10,000 steps were 4 to 6 inches
> narrower in their waist and hip measurements and had 40 percent less
> body fat than subjects who averaged less than 6,000 steps.
>
> Doing 10,000 steps per day not only helps with weight management but
> it can also help reduce blood pressure in women. A 2000
> [study(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=393917&url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11131268){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} published in the journal
> *Hypertension Research* found walking 10,000 steps per day or more is
> effective in lowering blood pressure, increasing exercise capacity,
> and even reducing sympathetic nerve activity in hypersensitive
> patients --- regardless of exercise intensity or duration.
> Participants\' blood pressure dropped after just 24 weeks of the
> 10,000 steps per day program. \1\

Remember, always talk to your doctor before you start any exercise
routine, but yes, something as simple as walking can be very beneficial
for you.
