(This answer is specific to the United States)

In the US, disability is governed by a piece of legislation called the
Americans with Disabilities Act. It defines a person with a
disability as

- a person who has a physical or mental impairment that substantially
  limits one or more major life activities.
- a person who has a record of such an impairment, even if they do not
  currently have a disability.
- a person who does not have a disability but is regarded as having a
  disability.

If your surgery impairs one or more major life activities, you are a
person with a disability.

Major life activities include, but are not limited to, caring for
oneself, performing manual tasks, seeing, hearing, eating, sleeping,
walking, standing, lifting, bending, speaking, breathing, learning,
reading, concentrating, thinking, communicating, and working.
