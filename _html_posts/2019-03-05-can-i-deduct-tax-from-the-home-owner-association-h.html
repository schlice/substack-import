Are HOA fees tax deductible?

From our friends at
[TurboTax(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b48b5f&url=https://ttlc.intuit.com/questions/1900477-are-homeowners-association-fees-tax-deductible){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="intuit.com"}]{.qlink_container}:

> Unfortunately, homeowners' association (HOA) fees paid on your
> personal residence are not deductible.
>
> However, if you have an office in your home that you use in connection
> with a trade or business then you may be able to deduct a portion of
> the HOA fees that relate to that office.
>
> If the home is a rental property then you can deduct the HOA fees as a
> rental expense.

As always, consult a tax professional before you make any tax-related
decisions or alter your tax filings.

If you use TurboTax (as I do), it will help you calculate what portion
of your HOA fees may be deductible, depending on your circumstances.
