While there have not been any mRNA *vaccines* approved before the
COVID-19 vaccine, we've been running trials of these vaccines for
decades for various things including treating cancer and infectious
disease.

> Although there are no previously approved mRNA vaccines, these
> vaccines have been trialed in humans for oncologic therapies for
> nearly a decade
> ([NCT01684241(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=276422&url=https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT01684241){.external_link

> trialed in humans for infectious disease for over 3

> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"

> data from phase I influenza mRNA vaccine and phase III COVID-19 mRNA

> swelling, fever, fatigue, headache, chills, muscle pain, and
> arthralgia are more common in the vaccine group relative to
> placebo.[3(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=25f98c&url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7956899/%23bib0003){.external_link

These studies show that there are no long-term effects that are
specifically related to using mRNA delivery methods. The only long-term
effects we have seen so far are related to the payload, not the delivery
method, and these are the same that we see with any vaccine.
