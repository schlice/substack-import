--- title: '"If you are in a wreck only involving your own property, do you have to report it to the police in Indiana?"' date: '2022-05-19' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

> In Indiana, you are required by law to report any car accidents that
> involve injury, death or entrapment of another person. You must also
> report accidents that involve damage to property, excluding the
> vehicles involved.\[1\](#jbWAt){#cite-jbWAt .citation_link}

So if your accident caused no damage to property other than your car (so
like, for example, let's say you drove into a drainage ditch at the side
of the road and damaged your own car but that's it) and no one else was
injured, you would not have to report that.

If there was damage to property and you *didn't* report it and you just
left, you could be charged with leaving the scene of an accident, so
don't do that.

If you are in a wreck only involving your own property, do you have to
