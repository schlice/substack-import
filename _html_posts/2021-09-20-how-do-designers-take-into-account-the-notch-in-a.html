Answer









Content: 

In addition to the other answers, the ejection of a shell does not
involve the movement of enough mass to make a difference against the
inertia imparted by the firearm itself and your holding of it.
Therefore, the kinetic energy of the shell casing would be pushing
against both the mass of the firearm and your mass. Therefore, the
action of ejecting a cartridge should not impart enough kinetic energy
to cause a large enough motion in the firearm (or you) to need to worry
about.
