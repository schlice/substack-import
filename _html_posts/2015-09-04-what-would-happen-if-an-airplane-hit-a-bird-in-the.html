If you recall, US Airways Flight 1549 had both of its engines disabled
by striking a flock of Canada Geese on takeoff out of LaGuardia on
January 15, 2009. Capt Sullenberger successfully ditched his aircraft in
the Hudson River, saving all 155 passengers and crew aboard.\
\
Aircraft fuselages, engines, and windscreens are routinely tested to
withstand bird strikes using the \"[[Chicken
gun](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=42df39&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicken_gun){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\" which fires whole, dead
chickens (preferrably not frozen) at aircraft windshields and engines,
as chickens have been found to accurately mimic the characteristics of a
bird strike by a live, fully-grown bird.]
