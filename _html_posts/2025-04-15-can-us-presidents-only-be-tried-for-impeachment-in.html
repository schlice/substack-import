--- title: '"Can US presidents only be tried for impeachment in the Senate? What is the reason for this rule?"' date: '2025-04-15' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Impeachment is a *political* function, not a *judicial*
one. This is why only the House can draft Articles of Impeachment,
and only the Senate can hold a trial.

I know the Constitution calls it a "trial" and that sounds a lot like
what happens in a courtroom, and I know that the Chief Justice of the
Supreme Court presides in the case of the trial of a president, but it
is not a judicial proceeding, period. It is a political proceeding.

Therefore, it takes place in the hallowed halls of politics (Congress),
not in the hallowed halls of a courtroom.
