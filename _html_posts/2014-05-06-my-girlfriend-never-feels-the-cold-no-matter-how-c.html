Content: [There are lots of things that could contribute to the fact
that she doesn\'t feel cold. Some of these are neurological disorders,
like MS or Guillain-Barr√© Syndrome. In these cases, she may not feel
the cold but she still may be cold, which could be dangerous. The

mechanism employed by the body to protect us against the damaging
effects of cold on our extremities.\
\
Some of these are things to be worried about. There are others that are
very very worrying, but she probably would know about that by now. For
example, there is a very rare group of diseases called **hereditary
sensory and autonomic neuropathy** which is a series of conditions which
inhibit nerve sensations. Usually though, when one cannot feel cold
because of this disease, they also can\'t feel hot, or pain.\
\
So usually, the causes are anything from annoying to worrying to very
worrying. It will take more investigation to determine what the cause
is, but she should definitely schedule a visit to the doctor to discuss
it.]
