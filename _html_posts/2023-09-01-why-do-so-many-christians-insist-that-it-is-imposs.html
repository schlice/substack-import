When someone becomes Christian (i.e. they accept Christ as their Savior
and commit their lives to Him), it is because God has already called
them to belief, and He has changed their heart from their old "hearts of
stone" and has given them hearts which can only love Him.

> I will sprinkle clean water on you, and you will be clean; I will
> cleanse you from all your impurities and from all your idols. I will
> give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you; I will remove from
> you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh. And I will put
> my Spirit in you and move you to follow my decrees and be careful to
> keep my laws. Then you will live in the land I gave your ancestors;
> you will be my people, and I will be your God. --- Ezekiel 36:25--28
> NIV

This process is what we called "being born again," or the more correct
terminology is *regeneration.* This new "heart of flesh" we receive
is only capable of loving God. Notice that it says that God will put His
Spirit in us which will move us to "follow His decrees and keep His
laws."

If God has done this, then you will love Him.

Therefore, logically speaking, if you "stop" loving Him, you did not
have His Spirit in you, because His Spirit is incapable of not loving
Him, and therefore you too would be incapable of not loving Him.

So for someone to say they are an "ex-Christian," it is perfectly
reasonable for Christians to question whether or not that person was
ever truly saved in the first place.
