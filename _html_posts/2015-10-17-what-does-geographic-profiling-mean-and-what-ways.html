> [[Geographic
> profiling](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2eeb94&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographic_profiling){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} is a criminal
> investigative methodology that analyzes the locations of a connected
> series of crimes to determine the most probable area of offender
> residence. By incorporating both qualitative and quantitative methods,
> it assists in understanding spatial behaviour of an offender and
> focusing the investigation to a smaller area of the community.
> Typically used in cases of serial murder or rape (but also arson,
> bombing, robbery, and other crimes), the technique helps police
> detectives prioritize information in large-scale major crime
> investigations that often involve hundreds or thousands of suspects
> and tips.
