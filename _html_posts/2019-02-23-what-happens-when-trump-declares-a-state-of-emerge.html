--- title: '"What happens when Trump declares a state of emergency?"' date: '2019-02-23' tags: - environment - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Under the National Emergencies Act, the President can use certain
emergency powers which have been written into Federal law at a time of
war or declared national emergency. Basically, when Congress writes a
law, they add a section that the President can activate in time of war
or national emergency.

> Congress has delegated at least 136 distinct statutory emergency
> powers to the President upon the declaration of an emergency. Only 13
> require a declaration from Congress; the remaining 123 are invoked by
> an executive declaration with no Congressional input.
>
> Emergency presidential powers are dramatic, and range from suspending
> all laws regulating chemical and biological weapons, including the ban
> on human testing (50 U.S.C. § 1515, passed 1969); to suspending any
> Clean Air Act implementation plan or excess emissions penalty upon
> petition of a state governor (42 U.S.C. (f) § 7410 (f), passed 1977);
> to authorizing and constructing military construction projects (10
> U.S.C. (a) § 2808 (a), passed 1982) using any existing defense
> appropriations for such military constructions (\$10.4 billion in
> FY2018); to drafting any retired Coast Guard officers (14 U.S.C. §
> 331, passed 1963) or enlisted members (14 U.S.C. § 359, passed 1949)
> into active duty.\[1\](#tadel){#cite-tadel .citation_link}

The President is required to promptly notify Congress of the national
emergency, and notify them of the section of Federal law enacted via
national emergency.

Congress has the authority under the same law to pass a joint resolution
to terminate a national emergency. The President can veto the
resolution, and then Congress would need a 2/3 majority in both houses
to override the veto.

However, any funds obligated during the national emergency would remain
obligated after the national emergency ends.
