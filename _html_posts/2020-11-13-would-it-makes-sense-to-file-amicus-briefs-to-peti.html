**Would it makes sense to file amicus briefs to petition various courts
to declare Donald Trump a vexatious litigant?**

A vexatious litigant is one who files frequent frivolous lawsuits. A
frivolous lawsuit is one which has little to no evidence to support its
filing.

*There is no evidence to support the claim that Trump contesting
selected state elections is vexatious.*

In fact, there are hundreds of sworn depositions which support their
claim. Remember, *evidence* is different than *proof*. While *evidence*
exists in sufficient quantity to justify the claim, *proof* may be
fleeting, but that follows legal investigation. There is more than
enough evidence to prove that these suits are not frivolous.

You may believe that his lawsuits will ultimately not change the result
of the elections. It's true, they may not. ***But that does not negate
his right to file them, nor does that lead their filing to be
vexatious***. In fact, I would say that if he has evidence that voter
fraud has taken place in a state, he has a *duty* to pursue the case,
for the sake of the integrity of the election process.

Compare Trump's lawsuits with some other notable vexatious litigants:

> - Lawrence Bittaker, who together with his partner Roy Norris was
>   convicted of torturing, raping and murdering five young girls
>   in 1979. He filed 40 separate frivolous lawsuits against the state
>   of California, including one claiming \"cruel and unusual
>   punishment\" after being served a broken cookie. In 1993, he was
>   declared a vexatious litigant and is forbidden from filing lawsuits
>   without the permission of a lawyer or a judge.
> - The Church of Scientology. \"Plaintiffs (Scientologists) have
>   abused the federal court system by using it, *inter alia*, to
>   attempt to destroy their opponents, rather than to resolve an actual
>   dispute over trademark law or any other legal matter. This
>   constitutes \'extraordinary, malicious, wanton and oppressive
>   conduct.\' As such, this case qualifies as an \'exceptional case\'
>   and fees should be awarded pursuant to the Lanham Act\... It is
>   abundantly clear that plaintiffs sought to harass the individual
>   defendants and destroy the church defendants through massive
>   over-litigation and other highly questionable litigation tactics.
>   The Special Master has never seen a more glaring example of bad
>   faith litigation than this.\"
> - Francis \"Coyote\" Shivers, actor and singer, declared vexatious
>   by the Los Angeles County Superior Court in 2008.
> - Dorothy Squires, singer and former wife of actor Roger Moore;
>   declared a vexatious litigant in 1987; legal fees led to her
>   bankruptcy.
> - Leo Stoller, a trademark troll, was declared a vexatious
>   litigant by multiple U.S. federal courts, including the Supreme
>   Court, in 2007.\[1\](#KfNsj){#cite-KfNsj .citation_link}

I'm reasonably sure Trump's lawsuits do not rise to the level of
vexatious litigation.
