Answer









Content: 

What is a religion? A religion, at its core, is a specific set of
beliefs and practices. But here's the secret: **just being a part of a
religion is not what saves you.**

You may consider yourself Christian. You may believe you are a
Christian. But does that fact save you?

NO!

Salvation is through belief in Christ and acceptance of His gift of
salvation through His substitutionary death on the cross.

> Jesus said to him, "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one
> comes to the Father except through me. --- John 14:6 ESV

It's funny, because Jesus actually talked about this very question while
He was alive:

> "Beware of false prophets, who come to you in sheep\'s clothing but
> inwardly are ravenous wolves. You will recognize them by their fruits.
> Are grapes gathered from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? So, every
> healthy tree bears good fruit, but the diseased tree bears bad fruit.
> A healthy tree cannot bear bad fruit, nor can a diseased tree bear
> good fruit. Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and
> thrown into the fire. Thus you will recognize them by their fruits.
>
> "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of
> heaven, but the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven.
> On that day many will say to me, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in
> your name, and cast out demons in your name, and do many mighty works
> in your name?' And then will I declare to them, 'I never knew you;
> depart from me, you workers of lawlessness.'" --- Matthew 7:15--23 ESV

Notice that Jesus contrasts *doing good things* with *having faith in
Christ.* Just doing all the things you mentioned by themselves:

- loves like Jesus
- lives like Jesus
- does works in Jesus' name
- sacrifices in Jesus' name
- adorates Jesus

are not what saves you. ***DOING ALL THOSE THINGS ARE A RESULT OF
RECEIVING GOD'S SALVATION, NOT THE CAUSE OF SALVATION.***

Once you are saved, you will *naturally* do those things, as you will be
a changed person and this is what saved people do. This is why you
recognize the *changed person* from the *fruit*. The fruit, without the
changed person, is useless. Similarly, if you don't see the fruit, you
can doubt whether or not the person is changed. But as Jesus said, a
good tree will not bear bad fruit, and a bad tree cannot bear good
fruit. So you will know them by their fruit, but at the same time, just
doing the good things is not what saves you.

TL;DR: Someone who does all these things but who has failed to
accept Christ as his savior will hear Him tell them on the last day,
"Depart from me, I never knew you."
