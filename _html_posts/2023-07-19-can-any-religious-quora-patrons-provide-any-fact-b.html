If that's what God wanted, He'd have His name in big flashing lights in
the sky and everyone would see it and no one would be dumb enough to
ignore Him.

But presuming that God exists, and He doesn't make His presence
*blaringly known*, then there clearly is a reason.

That reason: free will.

> One beauty of God's creation is this: if you're not willing to accept
> Christianity, then you're free to reject it. This freedom to make
> choices---even the freedom to reject truth---is what makes us moral
> creatures and enables each of us to choose our ultimate destiny.
>
> This really hits at the heart of why we exist at all, and why God
> might not be as overt in revealing Himself to us as some would like.
> For if the Bible is true, then God has provided each of us with the
> opportunity to make an eternal choice to either accept Him or reject
> Him.
>
> And in order to ensure that our choice is truly free, He puts us in an
> environment that is filled with evidence of His existence, but without
> His *direct* presence---a presence so powerful that it could overwhelm
> our freedom and thus negate our ability to reject Him.
>
> In other words, *God has provided enough evidence in this life to
> convince anyone willing to believe, yet he has also left some
> ambiguity so as not to compel the unwilling.*
>
> In this way, God gives us the opportunity either to love Him or to
> reject Him without violating our freedom. In fact, the purpose of this
> life is to make that choice freely and without coercion. For love, by
> definition, must be freely given. It cannot be coerced. That's why C.
> S. Lewis wrote, "the Irresistible and the Indisputable are the two
> weapons which the very nature of \God's\ scheme forbids Him to use.
> Merely to over-ride a human will (as His felt presence in any but the
> faintest and most mitigated degree would certainly do) would be for
> Him useless. He cannot ravish. He can only
> woo."\[1\](#XHGBi){#cite-XHGBi .citation_link}

So if you're looking for that "smoking gun" that ***unequivocally,
beyond a shadow of a doubt, proves that God exists***, you're simply not
going to find it. Because if that existed, you'd be forced to love Him
because the evidence would be so overwhelming that it would overwhelm
our free will and eliminate any ability to reject Him.

But He only wants those who freely accept Him.
