There is, of course, one major exception to this: the US Military.

We *can* shoot someone in the back who is fleeing.

The military provides 8 scenarios in which deadly force is authorized:

1.  Self defense
2.  Defense of others (i.e. serious crimes against the person)
3.  Protection of property or information vital to national security
    (i.e. stopping someone escaping with Top Secret information)
4.  **Protection of property or information substantial to national
    security** (i.e. stopping someone escaping with Secret information)
5.  Protection of property inherently dangerous to others (i.e.
    stopping someone escaping with High Explosive ordnance or a
    thermonuclear detonator, or something similar).
6.  Apprehension
7.  Prevention of prisoner escape
8.  Enforcement of a lawful order

Note that five of those eight (I highlighted them above) could be
effected against someone who is running away.
