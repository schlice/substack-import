**If you do not get read your Miranda Rights when being arrested does
that mean the arrest doesn't count?**

No, it does not. In fact, in most cases, your rights under Miranda are
not read to you when you are arrested.

Miranda rights only apply when you are being questioned, not when
you are being arrested. Before you are sat down to be questioned,
your Miranda rights will be read to you, and you will usually sign a
small form which indicates that you were read your rights, you
understand them, and you will also tick a box indicating whether or not
you invoked your right to counsel.
