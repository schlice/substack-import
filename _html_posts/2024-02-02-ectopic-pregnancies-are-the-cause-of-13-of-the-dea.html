An "ectopic pregnancy" is a misnomer. It's not actually a pregnancy that
can be carried to term. The baby cannot survive outside of the uterus.
It's not like a 5% chance that it could survive, or a 1% chance, but a

will rupture the Fallopian tube in which it is (usually) incorrectly
implanted and likely cause fatal hemorrhaging.

Calling it a "pregnancy" is a misnomer. It is only a "pregnancy" when
the baby is growing, healthy, in the uterus, not in the Fallopian tube
within which neither the baby nor the mother can survive. It is only a
"pregnancy" when "doing nothing" means the baby grows to term and is
born normally.

And since abortions to save the life of the mother are allowed in 100%
of states in which there are abortion restrictions, the answer is for
the mother to get an abortion of the ectopic pregnancy.

but this is not one of them. Even pro-lifers agree with this. Let's not
