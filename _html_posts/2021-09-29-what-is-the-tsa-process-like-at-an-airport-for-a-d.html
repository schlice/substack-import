--- title: '"What is the TSA process like at an airport for a domestic flight if your license is expired or lost?"' date: '2021-09-29' tags: - veteran-benefits layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

As long as you have one of the other approved forms of identification,
you can still get through TSA and the process basically remains
unchanged.

Acceptable forms of ID (besides your license):

- U.S. passport
- U.S. passport card
- DHS trusted traveler cards (Global Entry, NEXUS, SENTRI, FAST)
- U.S. Department of Defense ID, including IDs issued to dependents
- Permanent resident card
- Border crossing card
- State-issued Enhanced Driver's License
- An acceptable photo ID issued by a [[federally
  recognized](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9e1e9a&url=https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2021/01/29/2021-01606/indian-entities-recognized-by-and-eligible-to-receive-services-from-the-united-states-bureau-of){.external_link
  rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
  onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
  qt-tooltip="federalregister.gov"}]{.qlink_container}, Tribal
  Nation/Indian Tribe
- HSPD-12 PIV card
- Foreign government-issued passport
- Canadian provincial driver\'s license or Indian and Northern Affairs
  Canada card
- Transportation worker identification credential
- U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services Employment Authorization
  Card (I-766)
- U.S. Merchant Mariner Credential
- Veteran Health Identification Card (VHIC)\[1\](#bdhaT){#cite-bdhaT
  .citation_link}

If you don't have one of these, you won't even make it into the
screening area.

Original question:

What is the TSA process like at an airport for a domestic flight if your
license is expired or lost?
