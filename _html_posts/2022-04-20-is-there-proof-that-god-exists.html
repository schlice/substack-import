--- title: '"Is there proof that God exists?"' date: '2022-04-20' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Of course there is. Ample evidence of God's existence is available to
anyone *willing to believe.*  But God has left just enough ambiguity
so as to not compel the *unwilling*.

This is for a very important reason: God has given you *free will*.
You're not really acting under your free will if God compels you to
believe. This, by the way, is why God might not reveal himself as
overtly as some might like. I read a Quoran say that he wants God to
just please write on the clouds, "I exist, ffs". But God's not going to
do that.

> The beauty of God's plan for creation is that if you're not willing to
> accept it, you are free to reject it. The freedom to make
> choices---even the freedom to reject truth---is what makes us moral
> creatures and enables us to choose our own destiny.
>
> God has provided each of us with the opportunity to make an eternal
> choice to accept or reject him. But in order to ensure that our choice
> is *truly free*, he puts us in an environment which is *filled*
> with evidence of his existence, *but without his direct presence*,
> which would be so awesome and overwhelming as to overpower our ability
> to make a free decision, and compel us to accept him.
>
> In this way, God gives us the opportunity to accept or reject his love
> without violating our freedom. That's the purpose of this life: to
> make that choice freely, without coercion. For love must be freely
> given, it can never be coerced.\[1\](#JYINw){#cite-JYINw
