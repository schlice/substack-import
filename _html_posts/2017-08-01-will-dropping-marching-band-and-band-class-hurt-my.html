--- title: '"Will dropping marching band and band class hurt my chances of getting into the US Air Force Academy?"' date: '2017-08-01' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

The answer is "it depends."

Take a look at the [[Academy Admissions
website](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60e33c&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container}. There's a
truckload of information on that site which explains how the admissions
process works. It says:

> *To gain admission to the United States Air Force Academy, you must
> strive to supply your best possible performance regarding each
> admissions requirement and at each application step. Your performance
> on all admissions factors combine to create your overall competitive
> standing amongst prospective students, or cadets, and may lead to an
> offer of appointment to the Academy.*
>
> *A Selection Panel, composed of a cross section of senior Academy
> staff, reviews each completed admissions file according to our
> admissions guidelines. The quantitative admissions factors reviewed by
> the panel are your Weighted (Objective) Composite score, which is
> derived from your Academic Composite and Extracurricular Composite
> scores. The panel also evaluates qualitative components of your
> application to assess each candidate's motivation and potential for
> success at the Academy and future performance as an Air Force officer
> of character.*
>
> *It is in this qualitative review that Character Assessment items such
> as your writing sample and Academy Liaison Officer Interview as well
> as your Fitness Assessment and everything else in the admissions file
> is considered in evaluating the "whole person." The assignment of a
> Selection Panel Rating to each file is the final step in the selection
> process and occurs as soon as your file is complete (note that your
> Medical Evaluation and your Nominations can still be pending). Your
> Selection Panel Rating combines with your other scores to derive your
> Selection Composite score.*
>
> *Your scores are used to derive your overall competitive standing
> amongst applicants. Note that strong academic qualifications alone are
> not enough to make you competitive for appointment to the Academy.
> Conversely, if your academic qualifications are not extremely high,
> you may have the potential to become competitive through the remaining
> qualifying factors. The relative weighting of the scores that go into
> your overall competitive standing are:*
>
> - *Academic Composite: 50%*
> - *Extracurricular Composite: 20%*
> - *Selection Composite: 30%*
>
> *After review of the entire file, the selection panel will also make a
> qualitative/holistic recommendation on admission. This recommendation
> is considered along with the Selection Composite, the needs of the Air
> Force and any legislative requirements in selecting individuals for
> appointment.*

If you haven't done so, review that website. Check out the
[[Extracurriculur
Composite](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b8d301&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/the-application-process/extracurricular-activities/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} and what goes
into it. You may be able to drop band and still be okay in terms of your
EC score.

This would be a perfect question to ask your ALO. What, you don't have
an ALO yet? An ALO is an Admissions Liaison Officer who will guide
you through the entire admissions process. Check the [[ALO
Locator](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3baf43&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/outreach-programs/find-your-admissions-liaison-officer/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} on the admissions
website to find your ALO.

Good luck!
