**Why does Syracuse have the highest rate of multiple sclerosis
nationwide?**

The short answer is that we can't be sure that it does.

The study which shows Syracuse has the highest rate of MS in the nation
*actually* shows that Syracuse has the highest rate of people making
multiple sclerosis related claims against insurance.

> Because the report's findings are based on medical claims from insured
> patients, they may not reflect the actual incidence rate of MS in
> Syracuse, said Dr. Corey McGraw, an assistant professor of neurology
> at Upstate Medical University.
>
> "When people are well insured, they are going to see their doctors
> more often and get diagnosed earlier," McGraw said. "It may not
> represent our total community here in Syracuse.\"
>
> Because MS is not a reportable disease it's unknown how many cases
> there are in the Syracuse area. "But we do have a high population of
> MS patients in the area," McGraw said.\[1\](#FJTRt){#cite-FJTRt
> .citation_link}

We know that because we can see how many people seek medical care funded
by insurance.

But we don't know why this is. Perhaps Syracuse has the type of people
living there who overwhelmingly have commercial health insurance.
Perhaps medical care in Syracuse is just that much better, and so many
people are visiting doctors there.

But this may or may not directly translate to the number of people in
Syracuse who have MS.
