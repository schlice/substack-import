--- title: '"Is there a legally sound argument to ask money back if you find out that a company that you had prepaid for a service has a very bad reputation?"' date: '2017-03-24' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

There is not.

The company's reputation  is of no consequence. The company's
deliverables is what is most important.

If you have prepaid for a service, and the company delivers that service
within the timeframe agreed, then the reputation of the company is
inconsequential. It may personally characterize you in the future, but
that's not a reason to terminate a contract for which you've pre-paid.

I simply don't see how the company's reputation has any bearing on the
deliverables you have paid for.
