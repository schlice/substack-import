An excellent question!

But you realize you asked it in the *Christians & Christianity* space,
and here we believe in the Trinity.

To wit:

1.  The Father is God.
2.  The Son is God.
3.  The Spirit is God.

But:

1.  The Father is not the Son.
2.  The Son is not the Spirit.
3.  The Spirit is not the Father.

So to answer your question, Satan was running the world.

*Huh?*

Yes, that's right. Satan was cast out of heaven and became the ruler of
the world. Do you not remember this from the Bible? When Jesus was being
tempted, Satan offered Him the world:

> The devil led him up to a high place and showed him in an instant all
> the kingdoms of the world. And he said to him, "I will give you all
> their authority and splendor; it has been given to me, and I can give
> it to anyone I want to. If you worship me, it will all be yours." ---
> Luke 4:5--7 NIV

As you can see here, Satan was given authority over all the kingdoms of
the world. Until Jesus comes back to destroy Satan once and for all, and
send him into the deepest part of Hell for all eternity, of course.

But at least for now, Satan rules the world.

Another verse which describe this rulership:

> Then war broke out in heaven. Michael and his angels fought against
> the dragon, and the dragon and his angels fought back. But he was not
> strong enough, and they lost their place in heaven. The great dragon
> was hurled down---that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan, who
> leads the whole world astray. He was hurled to the earth, and his
> angels with him. --- Revelation 12:7--9

Only he who has dominion can "lead the world" even if it is in the wrong
direction.

\

(EDIT) That answer was mostly tongue-in-cheek.

I will say that "running the world" is a poor way to describe how either
God or Satan interacts with the world. Because neither God nor Satan
actually "runs the world." The world runs on its own. God set the world
in motion, and it just runs in accordance with the rules of science and
physics that He set in place.

So to what extent do either "run the world?" They really don't; *they
interact with people*. We know that Satan intervenes in the lives of
people by "leading them astray," and we know that God also intervenes by
leading people towards His goals, but as far as "the world" goes, there
isn't anything that needs running.
