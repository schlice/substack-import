Answer









Content: 

No offense, but you need smarter friends.

Oil doesn't grow in the ground. While oil is formed in the ground, it
doesn't "grow" like a plant. And God may have created the world and set
the conditions under which oil can develop, but he doesn't make the oil
appear in the ground any more than he actually moves my muscles to make
me walk. He built the world and created the natural rules under which
the world operates, but he doesn't directly "cause the oil to appear
with magic."

> Petroleum, also called crude oil, is a fossil fuel. Like coal and
> natural gas, petroleum was formed from the remains of ancient marine
> organisms, such as plants, algae, and bacteria. Over millions of years
> of intense heat and pressure, these organic remains (fossils)
> transformed into carbon-rich substances we rely on as raw materials
> for fuel and a wide variety of products.\[1\](#nDnTG){#cite-nDnTG
> .citation_link}
