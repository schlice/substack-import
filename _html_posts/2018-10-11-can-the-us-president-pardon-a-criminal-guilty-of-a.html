**Can the U.S. president pardon a criminal guilty of a any federal
crime?**

Yes.

Can the president pardon any federal crime?

Yes.

The President's pardon power comes from the Constitution, Article II,
Section 2. Under it, the President \"shall have power to grant reprieves
and pardons for offenses against the United States, except in cases of
impeachment.\"

The courts have interpreted this provision of the Constitution to mean
that the President can issue pardons, conditional pardons, commutations
of sentence, conditional commutations of sentence, remissions of fines
and forfeitures,
