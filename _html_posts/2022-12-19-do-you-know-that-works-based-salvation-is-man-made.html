--- title: '"Do you know that works-based salvation is man-made and could never get us to a pure God?"' date: '2022-12-19' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

You're right, you need faith alone to get into heaven. *Sola fide*.

However, faith produces works. Every. Single. Time. Therefore, if
you don't see works, you can rest assured that the requisite faith isn't
there either.

Faith begets works. So works is a good indication of faith.

The passage that comes to mind is James 2:14--26:

> What good is it, my brothers, if someone says he has faith but does
> not have works? Can that faith save him? If a brother or sister is
> poorly clothed and lacking in daily food, and one of you says to them,
> "Go in peace, be warmed and filled," without giving them the things
> needed for the body, what good is that? So also faith by itself, if it
> does not have works, is dead.
>
> But someone will say, "You have faith and I have works." Show me your
> faith apart from your works, and I will show you my faith by my works.
> You believe that God is one; you do well. Even the demons
> believe---and shudder! Do you want to be shown, you foolish person,
> that faith apart from works is useless? Was not Abraham our father
> justified by works when he offered up his son Isaac on the altar? You
> see that faith was active along with his works, and faith was
> completed by his works; and the Scripture was fulfilled that says,
> "Abraham believed God, and it was counted to him as
> righteousness"---and he was called a friend of God. You see that a
> person is justified by works and not by faith alone. And in the same
> way was not also Rahab the prostitute justified by works when she
> received the messengers and sent them out by another way? For as the
> body apart from the spirit is dead, so also faith apart from works is
> dead.

That's why faith without works is dead. The believer is *justified* by
his works. **Faith without works may be dead, but works without faith is
lame.**

How do we know this? Matthew 7:21--23:

> "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of
> heaven, but the one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven.
> On that day many will say to me, 'Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in
> your name, and cast out demons in your name, and do many mighty works
> in your name?' And then will I declare to them, 'I never knew you;
> depart from me, you workers of lawlessness.'"

They did the works, but they didn't have the faith in Christ alone.
Works without faith will get you nowhere. It is the faith that produces
the works. The works are the sign that points to the faith. Works which
come from faith are the only works that count.

**TL;DR: It is faith that saves, but faith without works isn't faith
that will save you.**
