--- title: '"Why is nationalism bad other than in certain situations like Independence Day, the Olympics, and when discussing foreign relations?"' date: '2017-08-13' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

I've answered this a few times already, but you're conflating two terms
which are actually very different: patriotism and nationalism.

> *Patriotism is fundamental to liberty because pride in one's
> nation-state, and a willingness to defend it if necessary, is the
> basis of national independence. Patriotism is the courage of national
> self-determination.*
>
> *By contrast, nationalism is patriotism transformed into a sentiment
> of superiority and aggression toward other countries. Nationalism is
> the poisonous idea that one's country is superior to somebody else's.
> Nationalism is intrinsically a cause of war and
> imperialism.*\[1\](#YlbsP){#cite-YlbsP .citation_link}

*Patriotism* is a love of country. Patriotism is necessary because to be
proud of one's country is the basis for independence.

*Nationalism*, on the other hand, is patriotism run amok. Nationalism
stems from a belief that your system is not *better* than other systems,
but is *superior* to other systems, and deserves to be *forcibly
exported* to the rest of the world.

Patriotism causes freedom; nationalism causes wars.
