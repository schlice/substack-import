**When a witness in a Congressional hearing has committed wilful
perjury, what is required to ensure that witness is punished if the
chairman is biased? Can the chairman be sanctioned?**

When a witness in a Congressional hearing has committed willful perjury,
the Congress must bring the evidence to the US Attorney's Office, who
will file charges of Lying to Congress, which is a Federal crime.

Whether or not Congress presses charges is dubious; not everyone who
lies to Congress is charged with Lying to Congress. In fact, people seem
to lie to Congress with great regularity.

[[5 people who lied to Congress, and what happened to
them](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=108264&url=https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/congress/5-people-who-lied-congress-what-happened-them-n941936){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"

The Chairman cannot be sanctioned when a witness lies. The lie is all on
the witness. Charges are not required, so the Chairman is not derelict
in his duty if he does not press charges.
