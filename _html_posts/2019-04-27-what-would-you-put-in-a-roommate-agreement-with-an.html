**What would you put in a roommate agreement with an illegal sublet?
(Yes it's not ideal, but there are extenuating circumstances on both
sides).**

Subletting your apartment when your lease agreement does not permit
subletting, or violating the city's rules regardless of what the lease
says, leaves you in trouble with the landlord, not your tenant, so
your roommate agreement is useless because you'll both be on the street.

Best advice: try to work with your landlord to either allow the sublet,
or add the roommate to the lease.

If you have further questions, you should try to find a tenant advocacy
group in your area and consult them for legal help, or consult a real
estate attorney.
