--- title: '"What happens if I get a speeding ticket while vacationing in another state?"' date: '2017-10-06' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

You'll be charged with a fine for violating that state's traffic laws.

Whether or not you get points assigned is complicated and depends on the
state you're from and the state you're in. Most states will report the
ticket back to your home state. 45 states plus the District of Columbia
have agreed to the Driver's License Compact (DLC) which requires them to
share information about moving violations back to the home state. Points
*may* be recorded depending on the state you're from and the state
you're in. Some states do assign out of state points, and some do not.

> A few states, such as Colorado and Pennsylvania, do not record the
> violation if it is considered a minor offense, like a speeding ticket.
> Other states, like Maryland and Nevada, record the violation but do
> not assign points for out-of-state tickets. Florida and Texas place
> both the violation and points on your driving record for all
> out-of-state convictions -- which will likely increase your car
> insurance premium.
>
> Then there are a few states that have unique laws. If you're a New
> York resident who received a minor traffic ticket in Quebec or
> Ontario, Canada, New York will record it and assess you points. But if
> you receive the ticket within the country, it will not go on your
> record. New Jersey adds two points for all out-of-state traffic
> convictions, even if your points for the violation would have been
> different in-state.\[1\](#SkDHA){#cite-SkDHA .citation_link}

If you want to go to court instead of simply paying the fine, you'll
have to return to the state to do so.
