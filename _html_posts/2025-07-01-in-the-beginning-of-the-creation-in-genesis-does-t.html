> **In the beginning of the creation in Genesis, does the heavens
> represent a head and ten fingers and the Earth a body and ten toes?**

Some symbolism does equate heaven and earth as head and body. In the
creation account of Genesis, the phrase \"heaven and earth\" is a way of
referring to all of creation. While heaven is the space of God and earth
is the space of humanity, these spaces are not always separate, and
there\'s a biblical theme of God bringing them back together. Some
interpretations see a connection between Christ being \"the head of the
body\" (referring to humanity) and the idea of \"new heaven\" as the
head and \"new earth\" as the body united in Christ.

> **Isn\'t Eve already present in the beginning as she exists inside
> Adam yet to be born out from him?**

No.

Eve is a created being, just as Adam was a created being. This means
they have absolute beginnings. So no, Eve did not "exist" as Christ
existed before He took on flesh.
