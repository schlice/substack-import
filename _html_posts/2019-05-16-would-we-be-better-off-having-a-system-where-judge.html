**Would we be better off having a system where judges who are
knowledgeable about legal issues preside rather than civilian juries?**

A judge already is present in the courtroom and runs the proceedings. He
is indeed knowledgeable about legal issues.

However, a jury makes the important decision about whether or not the
prosecution met its burden of proof beyond a reasonable doubt. The
"reasonable person test" is employed, and that doesn't require legal
training to determine.

The right to a trial by jury is one of the reasons the colonies left
England in the first place. The Declaration of Independence accused King
George of \"depriving us in many cases, of the benefits of trial by
jury.\"

The Constitution has several places which protect the right of trial by
jury. Article III says that all trials shall be by jury. The 6th
Amendment protected the right to a jury even further, stating "\i

all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to a speedy
and public trial, by an impartial jury of the state and district wherein
the crime shall have been committed," and the 7th Amendment protected
the same right for civil cases.

Is a jury important? The Supreme Court
[said(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bde58c&url=https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/391/145/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="justia.com"}]{.qlink_container} of the matter:

> Those who wrote our constitutions knew from history and experience
> that it was necessary to protect against unfounded criminal charges
> brought to eliminate enemies and against judges too responsive to the
> voice of higher authority. The framers of the constitutions strove to
> create an independent judiciary but insisted upon further protection
> against arbitrary action. Providing an accused with the right trial by
> a jury of his peers gave him an inestimable safeguard against the
> corrupt or overzealous prosecutor and against the compliant, biased,
> or eccentric judge.

Trial by jury is one of the foundations of our legal system and should
never be tampered with.
