Can an emergency room turn you away for no insurance?

[[Wilk
Dedwylder](https://www.quora.com/profile/Wilk-Dedwylder)]{.qlink_container}
nailed this one. I just want to help clarify some of the process.

The process for the hospital to get paid happens after you are triaged,
not before. They will find out if you have insurance, and if you do not,
they will check to see if you are enrolled in any government assistance
programs. If you are not, they will help you get enrolled if you are
eligible. If you are ineligible for any assistance, you can still be
treated, but you must agree that you are responsible for the bill.

This analysis simply determines where the hospital sends the bill and
what level of continuing care the hospital will offer.

As Dr. D said, the hospital will triage and stabilize you regardless of
your ability to pay. That's the law in the United States (a law called
[EMTALA(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b573db&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergency_Medical_Treatment_and_Active_Labor_Act){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}).
