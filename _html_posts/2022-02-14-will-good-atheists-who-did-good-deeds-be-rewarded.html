First of all, atheists don't believe that God exists, so they're not
thinking about salvation at all. But let's assume for a second that
somehow an atheist who did good deeds died, and then discovered that
there was indeed a god, you're asking if this "good person" atheist
would somehow be rewarded with eternal life simply because they were a
good person.

If this were true, then this would mean that *you earn your own
salvation* by your own good deeds, and God is not necessary. I bet even
the atheist sees the flaw in this logic.

If it's God's afterlife, you kinda have to play by God's rules.

Will good atheists who did good deeds be rewarded in the afterlife even
