--- title: '"Can the Supreme Court overrule legislation?"' date: '2022-08-07' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

In a way, yes.

The Supreme Court's job is to determine *Constitutionality* of laws.
So if a law is passed, but that law violates the Constitution, the
Supreme Court can invalidate that law.

This is part of our system of checks and balances in our system of
government:

See that blue line on the far right: "declare laws unconstitutional"?
That's the one where the Supreme Court places a "check" on the power of
the legislature: they can't pass laws that are unconstitutional.
