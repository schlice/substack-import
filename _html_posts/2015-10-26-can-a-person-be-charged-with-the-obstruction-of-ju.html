--- title: '"Can a person be charged with the obstruction of justice if they confess to a murder they did not commit and are actually sentenced and later released?"' date: '2015-10-26' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: [If it can be proven that the innocent person specifically
confessed to the crime *in order to impede or delay* the investigation
into the real killer, the confessor can be charged with obstruction.\
\
Obstruction of justice is charged when a person deliberately, or through
omission, impedes the progress of a police investigation. Confessing to
a crime which one did not commit does not rise to the level of
obstruction unless the confession was done intentionally to forestall
the actual investigation into the actual criminal.]
