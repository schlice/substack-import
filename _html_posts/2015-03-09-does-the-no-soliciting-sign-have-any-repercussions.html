--- title: '"Does the no soliciting sign have any repercussions?"' date: '2015-03-09' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: [On a private residence, none whatsoever. It has to be
backed by law. On a private residence, it might simply indicate that a
solicitor would be dismissed out of hand, or have a door closed in their
face.\
\
If it appears at the entrance to a neighborhood, it could be that the
covenants of a covenant-controlled neighborhood prevent soliciting, and
the violation of that could mean a date in court and fines against the
company or individual.\
\
If it appears on the door of a business, the local laws in that area may
disallow solicitations in a local area, or in a particular business
zone. Businesses who solicit in those areas could face violations and
fines.]
