--- title: '"In the US Law, can someone be arrested before a trial?"' date: '2016-11-18' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

Yes, you are arrested and held for trial.

You must be charged within 72 hours of your arrest or you must be
released.

If you are tried and convicted, the time you spent in jail waiting for
trial usually counts toward your sentence. We call it "time served."
