Content: 

Marijuana is still a Schedule I drug, which still means that at a
Federal level, it is completely illegal. There are no exceptions.
However, some states have *decriminalized* its use, either for medical
or recreational use, which simply means the *state* will not pursue
charges against you.

How your employer treats its use is entirely up to them. Whether or not
they recognize your state medical exemption is, again, up to them.
You will have to check with USPS to see how they treat state medical
exemptions.

\

Original question:

If I live in Oregon, USA and I'm a federal employee (mail carrier) can I
legally use medical marijuana to treat my MS?
