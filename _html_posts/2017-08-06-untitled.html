Really? Alarmist much?

From the article YOU quoted:

> *No one disputes that the 1972 Clean Water Act allows federal agencies
> to regulate navigable rivers and lakes. Less certain is the status of
> some two million miles of headwaters and streams that flow only part
> of the year --- 60% of the river and stream miles in the lower 48
> states --- plus 20 million acres of wetlands that aren\'t directly
> connected to large waterways.*
>
> *Under the Obama interpretation, those waters are protected. Business
> groups and some Republican state officials --- including then-Oklahoma
> Attorney General Scott Pruitt, now Trump\'s EPA chief --- filed suit,
> contending the regulation gave federal officials too much authority
> over waters that don\'t cross state lines or have a clear link to
> waters that are covered.*
>
> *The regulation was in effect for a short period in 2015 before the
> appeals court issued a stay. Those legal challenges are still
> pending.*

So not only were the regulations which were corrected overly broad,
covering waterways which have no connection to navigable rivers and
lakes, and have no connection to manufacturing pollution, but those very
same regulations have been in limbo since a stay was issued by the
appeals court in 2015. So farmers were unable to use those waters for
their farms without Federal government approval.

Also, no one wants to see the regulations simply disappear. This is
simply a Federalist attitude (if the EPA isn't playing "Big Brother,"
states won't be able to care for their own waterways). This would simply
return control of about 60% of river and stream miles in the lower 48 to
states, especially for waterways which do not cross state lines and the
Federal government should not rightly have control over. That's called
"Federal overreach."
