What they really mean when they say "mandatory evacuations" is that
services will not be available during the evacuation period.

If you call the police, they will not come. If you call the fire
department, they will not come. If you call for an ambulance, you're not
getting one.

Officials declare a mandatory evacuation when they cannot safely provide
essential services in the evacuation zone. They want you to move to a
place where they can provide you with essential services.

So do you have to leave during a mandatory evacuation? Can you be
arrested if you stay? No. You can choose to "ride it out" if you want.
But the point is that if you do, you're on your own.

Original question:

Why do states like Florida pretend to order \'mandatory evacuations\'
when in fact they lack the authority to force people off their land?
