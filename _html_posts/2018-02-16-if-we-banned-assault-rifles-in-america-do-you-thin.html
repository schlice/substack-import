First of all, it's extremely expensive and difficult to own assault
rifles. The Army classifies an "assault rifle" as one which is
select-fire, which means it can fire one shot at a time, or multiple
shots with one trigger pull. The last time I used one of those, I was
Active Duty. Civilians have to have a special Federal permit, pay a lot
of money (since no "new" assault rifles can be registered to civilians),
and then follow oppressive state regulations on top of that. So we
already do have a *de facto* ban on assault rifles.

I think what you are trying to do is ban "assault weapons," which is a
made-up category of firearm classified by its cosmetic features. We had
an assault weapons ban for ten years until it expired. And we did fight
back: we proved that the ban did nothing to stop crime, and we allowed
the ban to expire.

So here's a fun exercise: which one of these weapons is an assault
weapon?

If you said B, according to the law, you'd be right. B is a weapon which
would have been classified as illegal under the Assault Weapons Ban.

But you want to know a little secret? They're the same weapon. This
is a Ruger 10/22. They both shoot 22LR, they both have a 10 round
capacity, they are both semi-automatic (which means one pull, one shot).
The difference? Cosmetic. One has a wood stock, the other has a black
composite stock. One has a vicious pistol grip and a vertical foregrip,
which makes bullets move faster I guess. One has a collapsible stock,
which makes everything so much more dangerous.

So wanna ban assault weapons like this again? Sure, fine, go ahead.
Certainly won't hurt me. I'll just use A instead of B, and I won't be
deterred in the slightest. Certainly won't stop any mass shootings. You
can just use A instead of B.

See, Democrats like to pass laws that feel good. Doesn't it feel
good to get those *evil-looking assault weapons* off of the streets?
Sure it does, but it doesn't actually help the situation you were trying
to help. Republicans are more concerned with passing laws that actually
do good. Pass *meaningful* laws, instead of passing laws for the
sake of passing laws.
