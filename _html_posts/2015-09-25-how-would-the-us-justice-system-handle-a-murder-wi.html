--- title: '"How would the US justice system handle a murder with two equally likely suspects?"' date: '2015-09-25' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: [They couldn\'t be tried separately or in parallel, because
you would have to charge both of them with the crime. Since it\'s clear
that only one person perpetrated the crime, the situation you have
described means that it\'s not time to charge either one, because you
either have evidence that implicates both of them, or you don\'t have
enough evidence to implicate one over the other.\
\
The police would be remanded to continue gathering
evidence.]
