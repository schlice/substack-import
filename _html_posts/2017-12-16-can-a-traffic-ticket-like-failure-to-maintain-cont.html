--- title: '"Can a traffic ticket like failure to maintain control be dismissed if an officer puts down the wrong date and time of accident in Iowa?"' date: '2017-12-16' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: 

In general, [[clerical errors (aka Scrivener\'s
Error](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=706c23&url=http://definitions.uslegal.com/c/clerical-error-scriveners-error-vitium-clerici/){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="uslegal.com"}]{.qlink_container}) do not automatically cause
a ticket to be dismissed.

If a cop gets actual factual data wrong on your ticket, like the wrong
make or model of car, or the wrong street, this would speak directly to
the validity of the ticket. Also, if the cop writes down the wrong name,
or the wrong birthday, you could also similarly argue that the ticket
was not actually addressing you, or your violation, in your car.

However, the wrong date is simply considered a clerical error, and even
in Iowa, wouldn't cause a ticket to be automatically dismissed.
