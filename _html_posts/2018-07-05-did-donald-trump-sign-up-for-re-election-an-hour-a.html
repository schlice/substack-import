**Is it true that Donald Trump signed up for re-election an hour after
his inauguration?**

According to the Washington Compost:

> On the same day that Donald Trump assumed his new office, he also
> qualified as an official candidate in the 2020 White House race,
> according to a
> [letter(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=98a750&url=http://docquery.fec.gov/dcdev/fectxt/1140262.txt){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="fec.gov"}]{.qlink_container} he submitted to the Federal
> Election Commission on Friday.
>
> Because Trump's campaign committee has been steadily raising money
> online since the election, he easily reached the \$5,000 threshold
> that requires filing an official statement of candidacy.
>
> The new president emphasized that the paperwork "does not constitute a
> formal announcement of my candidacy for the 2020
> election."\[1\](#zrZHf){#cite-zrZHf .citation_link}

Drudge leaked that the President was going to announce his candidacy in
February 2018, which would have made it officially the earliest
announcement for a president to announce a re-election campaign, but
there is no evidence that the president has officially or formally
announced his candidacy for 2020.
