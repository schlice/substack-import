> Some fat in your
> [liver(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=770901&url=http://www.webmd.com/digestive-disorders/picture-of-the-liver){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} is normal. But if it makes
> up more than 5%-10% of the organ\'s weight, you may have [[fatty liver
> disease](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=da2e70&url=http://www.webmd.com/hepatitis/fatty-liver-disease){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}. If you\'re a drinker,
> stop. That\'s one of the key causes of the condition.
>
> There are two main types of fatty liver disease:
>
> - Alcoholic liver disease (ALD)
> - Nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
>
> You can also get fatty liver disease during
> [pregnancy(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5b39bb&url=http://www.webmd.com/baby/default.htm){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}.
>
> Alcoholic Liver Disease (ALD)
>
> You can get alcoholic liver disease from drinking lots of
> [alcohol(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2bd3ef&url=http://www.webmd.com/mental-health/addiction/standard-alcoholic-drink){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}. It can even show up after
> a short period of heavy drinking.
>
> Genes that are passed down from your parents may also play a role in
> ALD. They can affect the chances that you become an
> [alcoholic(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e719a3&url=http://www.webmd.com/mental-health/addiction/understanding-alcohol-abuse-basics){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}. And they can also have an
> impact on the way your body breaks down the alcohol you drink.
>
> Other things that may affect your chance of getting ALD are:
>
> - [[Hepatitis
>   C](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=492702&url=http://www.webmd.com/hepatitis/hepc-guide/default.htm){.external_link
>   rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
>   onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
>   qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} (which can lead to
>   [inflammation(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e7be03&url=http://www.webmd.com/arthritis/about-inflammation){.external_link
>   rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
>   onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
>   qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} in your liver)
> - Too much iron in your body
> - Being
>   [obese(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=42cba3&url=http://www.webmd.com/diet/am-i-obese){.external_link
>   rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
>   onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
>   qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}
>
> Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)
>
> It\'s not clear what causes this type of fatty liver disease. It tends
> to run in families.
>
> It\'s also more likely to happen to those who are middle-aged and
> [overweight(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7eac09&url=http://www.webmd.com/diet/obesity/video/obesity-risks){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} or obese. People like that
> often have [[high
> cholesterol](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=631cc2&url=http://www.webmd.com/cholesterol-management/default.htm){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} and
> [diabetes(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c1a3f6&url=http://www.webmd.com/diabetes/default.htm){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} as well.
>
> Other causes are:
>
> - Medications
> - Viral
>   [*hepatitis*(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9fee73&url=http://www.webmd.com/hepatitis/default.htm){.external_link
>   rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
>   onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
>   qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}
> - Autoimmune or inherited liver disease
> - Fast weight loss
> - Malnutrition
>
> Some studies show that too much bacteria in your small intestine and
> other changes in the intestine may be linked to nonalcoholic fatty
> liver disease.
>
> Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy
>
> It\'s rare, but fat can build up in your liver when you\'re pregnant.
> This could be risky for both you and your
> [baby(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=815eb6&url=http://www.webmd.com/parenting/baby/default.htm){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container}. It could lead to liver or
> [kidney(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5ee64d&url=http://www.webmd.com/urinary-incontinence-oab/picture-of-the-kidneys){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} failure in either of you.
> It might also cause a serious infection or bleeding.
>
> No one fully understands why fatty liver happens during pregnancy, but
> hormones may play a role.
>
> Once you get a diagnosis, it\'s important that your
> [baby(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e832d5&url=http://www.webmd.com/parenting/baby/ss/slideshow-baby-milestones-first-year){.external_link
> rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
> onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
> qt-tooltip="webmd.com"}]{.qlink_container} gets delivered as soon as
> possible. Although you may need intensive care for several days, your
> liver often returns to normal in a few weeks.
