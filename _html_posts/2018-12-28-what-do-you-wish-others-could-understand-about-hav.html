**What do you wish others could understand about having multiple
sclerosis?**

***How you look on the outside does not match how you feel on the
inside.***

*"You have MS?* *But you look so good...**"*

UGH! If I hear that sentence again, I think I'll scream.

When you get MS, it doesn't matter what your symptoms are or how long
you have lived with the disease, it is guaranteed that you will hear
that phrase at least once, from some well-meaning person who only knows
MS because they know Richard Pryor or someone else who was in a
wheelchair or who had to use a cane.

Or they follow up that sentence with, "All of the guys I know who have
MS just look terrible and they never did very well, but not you! *You
look so good!*"

I always want to say, "So what, exactly, is a person with MS *supposed*
to look like?"

And if you are like me and you have issues with fatigue, it may be that
the walk through the hot parking lot will be the difference between
whether or not you can have dinner with your family that night, or that
you'll have to go to bed early because you're just too tired. Hanging a
handicapped parking tag and then walking in to the store will elicit
more than one dirty look from people who judge you because you don't
"look" handicapped.
