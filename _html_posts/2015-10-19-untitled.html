Content: [The Air Force eschewed the \"inside the boot\" method in
the late 90s because oftentimes, remains can be dismembered in an
aircraft crash. The boot seemed like a good place at the time, because
boots survived a fire (usually were the only article of clothing to
survive an aircraft fire). But more often that not, a violent crash left
a servicemember without his boots (and the feet that went along with
them), so putting the dog tags in the boots was forbidden.\
\
Aircrews are still required to wear both dog tags
today.]
