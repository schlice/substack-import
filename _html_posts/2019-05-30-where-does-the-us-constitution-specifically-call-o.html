**Where does the US Constitution specifically call out the
responsibility of Congress to oversee the Executive branch of
government?**

As with many things in the Constitution, it may not necessarily contain
those words, but it contains those ideas. We can also look at the
thoughts of the people who wrote the Constitution. A collection of
documents known as *The Federalist Papers* details what the framers of
the Constitution were thinking when they wrote what they did. Federalist
number 51 defines the principles we're discussing: *checks and
balances.*

Checks and balances has a full Constitutional tradition, even though
those words were never listed in the Constitution. Each one of the three
branches of government is *co-equal*; that is, no one branch is more
powerful than the others. The powers of each branch are carefully
defined, and in each case, the power to *oversee the other branches* is
also clearly defined. From our friends over at
[usconstitution.net(https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e5b5a8&url=https://www.usconstitution.net/consttop_cnb.html){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="usconstitution.net"}]{.qlink_container}, here's a listing of
the defined powers of each branch of government which serve as checks on
the other branches:

> Legislative Branch
>
> - Checks on the Executive
>   - Impeachment power (House)
>   - Trial of impeachments (Senate)
>   - Selection of the President (House) and Vice President (Senate) in
>     the case of no majority of electoral votes
>   - May override Presidential vetoes
>   - Senate approves departmental appointments
>   - Senate approves treaties and ambassadors
>   - Approval of replacement Vice President
>   - Power to declare war
>   - Power to enact taxes and allocate funds
>   - President must, from time-to-time, deliver a State of the Union
>     address
> - Checks on the Judiciary
>   - Senate approves federal judges
>   - Impeachment power (House)
>   - Trial of impeachments (Senate)
>   - Power to initiate constitutional amendments
>   - Power to set courts inferior to the Supreme Court
>   - Power to set jurisdiction of courts
>   - Power to alter the size of the Supreme Court
> - Checks on the Legislature (because it is bicameral, the Legislative
>   branch has a degree of self-checking)
>   - Bills must be passed by both houses of Congress
>   - House must originate revenue bills
>   - Neither house may adjourn for more than three days without the
>     consent of the other house
>   - All journals are to be published
>
> Executive Branch
>
> - Checks on the Legislature
>   - [[Veto
>     power](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bbe082&url=https://www.usconstitution.net/pres_veto.html){.external_link
>     rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
>     onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
>     qt-tooltip="usconstitution.net"}]{.qlink_container}
>   - Vice President is President of the Senate
>   - Commander in chief of the military
>   - Recess appointments
>   - Emergency calling into session of one or both houses of Congress
>   - May force adjournment when both houses cannot agree on adjournment
>   - Compensation cannot be diminished
> - Checks on the Judiciary
>   - Power to appoint judges
>   - Pardon power
> - Checks on the Executive *\by other than the Chief Executive\*
>   - Vice President and Cabinet can vote that the President is unable
>     to discharge his duties
>
> Judicial Branch
>
> - Checks on the Legislature
>   - Judicial review
>   - Seats are held on good behavior
>   - Compensation cannot be diminished
> - Checks on the Executive
>   - Judicial review
>   - Chief Justice sits as President of the Senate during presidential
>     impeachment\[1\](#Ippob){#cite-Ippob .citation_link}

The quote doesn't completely identify how the Legislature can oversee
the Executive. Our laws, as allowed by our Constitution, call for
[[Congressional
oversight](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=27dc8b&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congressional_oversight){.external_link
rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank"
onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"
qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. This power allows them
to review how Federal agencies are implementing their laws, and how they
may need to modify those laws.
