--- title: '"Why does attempted and successful murder have different sentences?"' date: '2015-03-07' tags: - uncategorized layout: post --- Quora Answer 





Answer









Content: [When it comes to the sentencing phase of a trial,
mitigating circumstances get to come into play. This is the time in the
trial that, faced with mitigating circumstances, a person\'s sentence
could be reduced because of them.\
\
The fact that a murderer, who was planning to murder, was able to stop
before he actually killed someone is absolutely taken into account when
the sentencing is being determined.]
