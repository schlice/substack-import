**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why does Colorado law allow multiple murder charges for the killing of one person under different theories?** It's more than just Colorado. Most states allow multiple murder charges under different murder theories, or different mental states which can comprise a murder. These separate mental states include such charges as intentional murder, murder through actions that one knows (or should know) will likely result in death, felony murder where a victim dies during the defendant\'s commission of a different felony, etc. The prosecutor will generally charge any mental state which might potentially apply as a separate count. If the defendant is found guilty of multiple counts, they will be merged together into one conviction for judgement and sentencing purposes.
