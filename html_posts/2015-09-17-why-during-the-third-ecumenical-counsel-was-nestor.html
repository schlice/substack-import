{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Your assertion that Nestorius was \"merely defending what scriptures taught\" was exactly what was at issue during the Council.\ \ During the Third Ecumenical Council at Ephesus (also known as the Council of Ephesus), Pope Celestine I and Roman Emperor Theodosius II found that Nestorius, in defending his view of the Immaculate Conception, was actually advancing the idea that the hypostatic union did not occur at birth, and Mary gave birth only to Christ, not to the divine *Logos*. He asserted that the *Logos* could not be \"born\" since it existed before Mary, and to say that the divine could be born would be to deny the nature of the divine. He also asserted that the *Logos* occupied the area normally occupied by the soul, which allowed Jesus to exist without the stain on the soul from the Fall.\ \ The Council found this to be a contradiction to the hypostatic union, and found Nestorius\' view to be heretical. The Council found that separating the divine from the human, and saying that the divine can\'t be born, would mean that Jesus was *less than* human because he was born without a soul.\ \ Source: [[Council of Ephesus](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8d1e25&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Ephesus){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}]{.rendered_qtext}
