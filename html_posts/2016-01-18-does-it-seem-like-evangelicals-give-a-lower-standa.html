**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, your article conflates the people they interviewed, who were of many varied faiths, not just Evangelicals, to official endorsement by the American Council of Evangelicals of Mr. Trump. This is simply not so. If you actually read the article and not just the headline, Questioner, you\'ll find that people of all faiths believe that while Mr. Trump may not necessarily be a man of God, he is almost universally referred to as someone who \"means well,\" and can \"get things done.\" Indeed, while politicians spend entire careers explaining how a vote in Congress actually \"got something done,\" Trump, for all of his New York brashness, has actually built things. He\'s built skyscrapers. He\'s built a thriving organization. And yes, he was born with a silver spoon in his mouth, and I daresay the characterization, \"he was born on third base and he thinks he hit a triple\" is accurate. That doesn\'t change the fact that he has actually achieved the things he\'s set out to achieve. He said he\'d build a world-class company, and he has done that. He said he was going to have one of the highest-rated reality shows on television, and he has done that. For all of his personal braggadocio, Trump *has actually shown that he can accomplish things.* And Republican voters look at that, and they then look at the America he is walking into: people are angry with eight years of failed Obama policy and unfulfilled promises. They are scared at a steadily weakening economy, and they are (rightly) afraid that a Syrian refugee will actually be a terrorist in disguise. They look at Trump and say, here is a man who may not speak eloquently, but he speaks honestly. He speaks from the heart. He has a genuine love for America (why else would he sink all this money from his own coffers into the race?), and most importantly, out of all of the candidates out there, he has the highest chance of actually accomplishing what he says he\'s going to do, because he has a *track record of success.* So when he says he\'s going to build a wall, why shouldn\'t we believe him? He\'s built skyscrapers. I bet he can build a wall. When he says he\'s going to fix America\'s trade deficit, restore the nation\'s economy, and return jobs to America, why shouldn\'t we believe him? He\'s run an immensely successful business. No one can doubt that. And based on the track record of some of our past presidents (ahem Bill Clinton), the fact that a president is a moral, upstanding Christian doesn\'t seem to be too high on most voters\' lists of requirements for a president. Clearly, voters are looking for honesty, and they believe that Trump has it in spades. He may not be the orator and skilled debater that Lincoln was, but his heart is in the right place, he has the best interest of the country at heart, and he has the ability to pull it off. That\'s why he is so popular right now.
