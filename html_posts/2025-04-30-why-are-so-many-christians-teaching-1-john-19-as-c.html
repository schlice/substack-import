Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They're confused by conditional forgiveness followers. So first, let's make sure we understand what we're addressing. Here's the verse in question: > *If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our > sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. \-- 1 John 1:9 ESV* When we discuss conditional forgiveness, this verse is the one discussed. Why? *It's the only one which talks about conditional forgiveness*. And that fact is important as we move forward in this discussion of conditional forgiveness. The main point: **the doctrine of conditional forgiveness is incompatible with the entire New Testament message, as well as the words of Jesus.** In fact, a great deal of the issue with the doctrine of conditional sin is the interpretation of the Bible. When you find a Bible verse which is apparently in contradiction with other verses in the Bible, you must stop and consider that you misunderstand at least one of the verses. In other words, it's not that the verses contradict each other, it's that our *understanding* of the verses contradict. So let's "zoom out" and see what the remainder of 1 John says about this subject: > *But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have > fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus his Son* > ***cleanses us from all sin****. \-- 1 John 1:7 ESV* > > *I am writing to you, little children, because* ***your sins are > forgiven*** *for his name's sake. \-- 1 John 2:12 ESV* Do these passages lead us to believe that Jesus' sacrificial work on the cross cleanses us from *some sin*, or from *all sin*? I think the Bible is wholly clear: Jesus' work on the cross cleanses us from **all** sin, not just sin we confess. So why the confusion? Perhaps the passage was directed at those who were not saved. Perhaps he was addressing those who needed to come to faith in Jesus, not those who already had. That said though, there's no denying that *everyone* needs Jesus to atone for our sins. Regardless, it's clear that Jesus' sacrifice cleanses us from ***all*** sin. Think about it. When we stand before the judgement seat, the Accuser will be there speaking against us. What will happen when the Accuser says, "Well, here's an example of a sin for which he did not repent!" What will the Advocate (Jesus) say? > *But if anyone does sin, we have an Advocate with the Father, Jesus > Christ the righteous. \-- 1 John 2:1 ESV* Will the Advocate say, "Oh yeah, I guess he didn't confess that sin, My Grace doesn't cover that one"? ***NO!*** He will say, "My blood paid for that sin." The Bible says "if anyone sins, we have an Advocate." It does not say, "if anyone sins *and confesses that sin*, we have an Advocate." All sin is covered. The Bible is exceedingly clear that there's no prerequisite to your sin being covered.
