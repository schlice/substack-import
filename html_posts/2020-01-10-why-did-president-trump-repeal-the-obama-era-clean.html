**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why did President Trump repeal the Obama-era clean water rule?** First of all, you're watching too much mainstream media. Second, the pre-Obama Clean Water Act is still in effect. Trump only rolled back Obama's changes to the Act. Obama's additions to the Clean Water Act took a good regulation and stretched it way too far, giving the EPA control over more than just water. It gave the Federal government control over vast amounts of dry land by adding terms such as "adjacent waters" and "tributaries" to Clean Water Act coverage. It allowed the EPA to control things like seasonal streams, farm irrigation ponds, roadside ditches and even "connective" dry lands that can turn into "shallow wetlands" when it rains. The government's position at the time said that tributaries and adjacent waters can flow downhill into larger bodies of water, and so the smaller adjacent waters must also be regulated. However, Obama's move constituted a massive power grab for the EPA, and great uncertainty for farmers and landowners over virtually any land which gets wet. Farmers, ranchers, real-estate developers and pretty much anyone who owned more than an acre of land had to be worried about violating the new rules because it was impossible to know if, for example, the presence of a pond, or even a seasonal pond, on that land wouldn't bring the EPA down on you. And the federal government is not shy about imposing multimillion-dollar fines on farmers just for plowing fields that they determine actually contained "seasonal wetlands." Ever since Obama made the changes, they were the subject of state-level lawsuits. Federal district courts stopped Obama's rules from taking effect in roughly half of the states in the US anyway. Trump reversed Obama's changes to the Clean Water Act, which were enacted by Executive Order, and so were reversed by Executive Order, returning some sanity to government.
