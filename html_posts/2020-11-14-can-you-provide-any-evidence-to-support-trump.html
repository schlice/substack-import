**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can you provide any evidence to support Trump's claim that there was cheating in the election? None of the authorities in our country can find any evidence of cheating, the courts are throwing out lawsuits, so where is this alleged cheating?** There is a pervasive media narrative which happens to be flat-out wrong: ***Evidence*** **is not** ***proof*.** Evidence *can* be proof, but it is not *always* proof. For example, there may be evidence that a crime has taken place, but upon investigation, proof of the allegation is sometimes not found. That doesn't negate the evidence. It also doesn't mean the allegation was incorrect, or wrong to bring. For example, there may be evidence that you killed your spouse. Your neighbors overheard you having a fight. You took a late-night fishing trip in your boat. And the next day, your spouse is gone, never to be seen again. That evidence is *circumstantial*, but it is still evidence. Is it *proof* of your guilt? Maybe. It's up to the judge to decide if it proves your guilt. Contrary to what the mainstream media is trying to feed you (which is disinformation), there is indeed evidence of voter fraud. That evidence must be investigated. If it is found to be lacking sufficiency, then the allegation is rejected. But the evidence is there. The investigation into the evidence follows, and if the allegation is proven, then the case moves forward. In at least a few cases, some of the allegations have merit, and their investigations are moving forward. In others, the allegations were found to be without merit and are tossed. A court would not allow a claim in the door without some evidence to merit its reason for existence. If there was no evidence at all, the case would have been tossed at the door. Perhaps the evidence was investigated and found to be insufficient to support the charge. In many cases, this is what happened. In some, there was sufficient evidence to move forward. If the case was allowed to be heard and there was no evidence, the defense would request *summary judgement* and would make the claim that the petitioner did not present sufficient evidence to meet the *evidentiary requirement* to bring the charge in the first place.
