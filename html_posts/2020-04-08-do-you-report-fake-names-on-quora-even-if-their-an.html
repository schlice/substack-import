Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you report fake names on Quora, even if their answers are very good?** Yep. 100% of the time, no exceptions. I find it *especially* irksome when someone who writes great answers can't be bothered to follow our community rules. When [[Nikki Grayestone](https://www.quora.com/profile/Nikki-Grayestone)]{.qlink_container} says that Quora is ultimately a collection of quality answers, what about the answers makes them quality? Besides the content and writing style, the only thing that distinguishes quality answers here from quality answers on *literally any other site on the Internet* is that **authors here personally represent and reinforce the quality of the answers they write** by stating specifically who they are. Remember the Orville Redenbacher commercials from the 70s? "You\'ll like it better or my name isn\'t Orville Redenbacher."[\[1\]](#zRtNT){#cite-zRtNT .citation_link} Even now, people stand behind the quality of their words, and their works, with their name. Quora expects us to do no less. And that is a massive brand differentiator for Quora. It's our job to help reinforce that.
