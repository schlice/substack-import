**Content:** [To expound on [[Matthew Bohrer](https://www.quora.com/profile/Matthew-Bohrer)]{.qlink_container}\'s answer, if you have a bench warrant out on you, you can be arrested anywhere in the country for that warrant. If you\'re not in the jurisdiction where the warrant was issued, you will be detained until the original warrant is pulled, and the original jurisdiction will decide whether or not they want you held for extradition back to the original jurisdiction. If they do want you held, the officer stopping you will have absolutely no choice but to hold you. If not, the complaining officer can let you go.\ \ The further away from the jurisdiction you get, the less chance you have of being extradited. If you get stopped in the jurisdiction where the warrant is active, you\'ll be arrested. If you get stopped in another jurisdiction within the state, there\'s a good chance you\'ll be transferred back to the warranting jurisdiction. If you\'re out of state, you may or may not be held for extradition.\ \ That said, I\'m not telling you to leave the jurisdiction. Understand that if you\'re far away from the original jurisdiction, and you know you have a warrant out for your arrest, they\'re going to wonder why you are so far away from the jurisdiction, because it kinda looks like you\'re running from your conviction, which is bad.\ \ If you don\'t have a criminal defense attorney, it\'s time to retain one and see if you can plead this thing out. You can\'t really live long in this country with any kind of warrant out on you. If it\'s a misdemeanor, you might be able to plead it out to something really small, so just get it done and move on. Running will just make it worse.]{.rendered_qtext}
