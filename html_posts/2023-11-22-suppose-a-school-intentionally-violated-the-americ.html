What would be their final punishment?"' date: '2023-11-22' tags: - veteran-benefits - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Americans with Disabilities Act is a law related to employment. So a school violating the Act is one which would be denying a teacher accommodations in employment, or rejecting an applicant to become a teacher due to a disability. Employers who knowingly violate the ADA can face the following consequences: - Fines: up to \$75,000 for the first violation, and up to \$150,000 for the second and subsequent violations. - Lawsuits. The Department of Justice may sue employers who fail to comply with ADA. (EDIT) You may be thinking of Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, also known as the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). This section of law says that schools must provide an equal education to all students, regardless of disability. Violations of this law could result in fines up to \$10,000 and compensatory education funded by the subject school.
