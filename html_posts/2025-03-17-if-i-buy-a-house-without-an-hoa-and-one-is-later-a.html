**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer, as with all legal answers, is "it depends." And what it depends on is **state law**. Most states **do** permit mandatory HOAs to be formed in neighborhoods where there was none before. However, the amount of consent that existing homeowners must provide varies. - In most states, **unanimous** consent is required to form a new, mandatory HOA (for example, here in NC, to form a mandatory HOA where there was none requires unanimous consent of the affected homeowners). - In some states, however, only a **supermajority** of homeowners is required (for example, TX law allows POAs to be formed with a supermajority of homeowners). However, there are usually exceptions which permit you (the current homeowner) to **opt-out**. For example, in TX, there is a one year right to opt-out of the newly formed HOA (of course, if you sell your house, the new owner would be automatically subject to the HOA). If you happen to be in a state which can form a mandatory HOA in your area without your permission, and you are being forced to participate against your will, we recommend not ignoring it, but **fighting** it. We strongly recommend retaining a real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law.
