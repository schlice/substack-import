--- title: '"Is it possible that some pastors and priests in Catholic, Protestant, and Orthodox churches identify as trinitarians publicly but privately embrace Modalism, Unitarianism, or Arianism, fearing repercussions and desiring to maintain their positions?"' date: '2023-06-06' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Sure, anything is possible. But it is highly unlikely. Living a lie is not mentally healthy for anyone, and eventually it will catch up to you. Being forced to live in cognitive dissonance, violating the very tenets of your own belief, just to keep your job, isn't good for anyone, let alone a priest or a pastor who is supposed to be guiding other people through the tenets of a faith in which they themselves do not believe. You're literally claiming that a priest or a pastor does not practice what they preach. While it's certainly possible, I find it unlikely.
