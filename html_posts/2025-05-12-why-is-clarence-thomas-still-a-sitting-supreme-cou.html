Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} He is still a sitting Supreme Court Justice because he has not been impeached. The Constitution is abundantly clear on this point. Supreme Court Justices are subject to impeachment. If there was proof, as you claim, that he "sold his vote for gifts," then that evidence should be presented to Congress so that they can begin Articles of Impeachment against him. To date, no such evidence has been presented to Congress, and Congress has not begun impeachment procedures against him. If you are aware of such evidence, you need to get it to your Congressional Representative so that they can begin impeachment proceedings.
