**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can you pray to hear from God for guidance, and then open up the Bible to a \"random\" page to receive His voice? Not for the future as would be a form of divination, but just to receive His voice in a more direct way. Or would that be false prophecy?** Let me answer you by asking a question: Who are you to tell God how you want to receive his guidance? *I want an answer, and I want it on God's letterhead, in Old English font, double-spaced, 12-point.* In case you missed the sarcasm, God doesn't work that way. You can pray for guidance. You can pray for wisdom. You can pray that God answers your prayers (if it is his will to do so). Separately, you can open up the Bible to a random section ***with an open heart and an open mind*** and let God's word speak to you. Before you do that, I would pray to have God do just that: open my heart and open my mind to teach me more about him. Ask God to give you wisdom and understanding, *and then open the Bible and read.* A good way to do that is what we call **SOAPing.** You pick a chapter from the Bible, and you read it. Alternatively, you can use any of the "X-day Bible Reading Plans" that are freely available on the Internet. Personally, I would highly recommend [[YouVersion](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=89cdcd&url=https://www.bible.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="bible.com"}]{.qlink_container}, available on the Web and on your favorite mobile device. Pray first for God to open your mind and your heart and teach you through your reading, then read the chapter. Next, do a SOAP analysis. As you can probably guess, SOAP is an acronym. It stands for **S**cripture, **O**bersvation, **A**pplication, and **P**rayer. Scripture means that you pick out a verse or two which stood out to you while you were reading. You then make an Observation about that verse. You Apply that passage to you, your life, or your circumstances, and then you Pray for God to help you in that endeavor. For context, I'll give you an example out of my SOAP diary (I keep it online for easy access on any device). This was a time when I was SOAPing through the Bible and I read John 1. Here's what I got: > **S**cripture > > There was a man sent from God whose name was John. He came as a > witness to testify concerning that light, so that through him all > might believe. He himself was not the light; he came only as a witness > to the light. (John‬ 1‬:6-8‬ NIV) > > **O**bservation > > I love how God sends \"the Light,\" but He also sends messengers to > testify about the light. I can only imagine that those messengers > appeared crazy to the people, especially when John said things like > "Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world! This is > the one I meant when I said, 'A man who comes after me has surpassed > me because he was before me.'\" (John‬ 1‬:29-30‬ NIV). What more can He > do to try to tell us the Good News? > > **A**pplication > > God sends me messages all the time. I need to do a better job at > receiving them. > > **P**rayer > > God, please help me to \"get it.\" Because I don\'t often get it. You > keep calling and I don\'t hear. Help me to know Your voice so I may > better separate You from the noise. SOAPing through the Bible is a great way to both read the Bible, and at the same time, put yourself in a frame of mind to receive the message that God is trying to deliver through it. Have you SOAPed today? Ha, a little Bible humor... But back to the question, asking for something and expecting an answer by opening the Bible is just another way of you demanding that God answer you, in the way you expect. We are in no position to demand anything from God. And God doesn't work that way.
