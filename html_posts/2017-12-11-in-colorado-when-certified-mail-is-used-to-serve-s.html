**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In Colorado, when certified mail is used to serve someone with a traffic ticket, do they have to sign for it to be considered served? What laws and/or case law govern this matter?** The only "traffic tickets" which can be served through certified mail are red light camera violations. Initially, these will come through regular mail, with an included option on how to waive your rights and pay the fine. But here's the trick: ***Before you are served, it's not a violation. And if you don't get a certified letter, you're still not served.*** There are three ways you can be officially served: - The citation is handed to you by a police officer or process server - The citation is left at your home with someone who is 18 years of age or older, - The citation is mailed to you as a certified letter, return receipt requested. Colorado Revised Statute 42-4-110.5 requires that, in the case of a traffic violation detected by an "automatic vehicle identification system" (like a traffic camera), the city must "serve the penalty assessment notice or summons and complaint for the alleged violation on the defendant no later than ninety days after the alleged violation occurred." [[There have been reports](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=22959a&url=http://kdvr.com/2017/09/13/drivers-can-legally-avoid-paying-red-light-camera-citations-in-colorado/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="kdvr.com"}]{.qlink_container} of city agents serving summons at 6 am in some cases, so be aware of that. The second notice could come as a certified letter. If you don't sign for the letter within 90 days of the alleged offense, the fine is no longer valid. **But if you do sign for the letter, you've been served,** and you are expected to pay. Failing to pay would subject you to civil punishments, not criminal (i.e. it will never add points to your driving record). You could get a negative hit on your credit, and your delinquent payment would be sent to a collection agency. \ *As you know, I am not a lawyer. This is not legal advice. If you follow this advice and end up in trouble, don't blame me.*
