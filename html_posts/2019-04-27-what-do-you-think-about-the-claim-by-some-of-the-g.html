Here's the context you left out (I also added it to the question for you): [[Tax overhaul raises taxes for some Gold Star families on survivor benefits](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=49db8a&url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/04/24/politics/gold-star-families-higher-taxes/index.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cnn.com"}]{.qlink_container} [[User-12209089141318703872](https://www.quora.com/profile/User-12209089141318703872)]{.qlink_container} is spot on: the problem is happening to Gold Star families who receive benefits from both the VA and the DoD. Legally, you can't receive both at the same time. What some Gold Star families would do is assign their DoD benefits directly to their children while they are minors (called the "child option") so that they can continue to receive full benefits from both the DoD and the VA. The new tax laws create a "kiddie tax" which taxes unearned income for children at a higher rate, up to 37%. This was done because some wealthy families were hiding money in their children\'s names. It is supposed to be a trust or estate tax, not a tax on death benefits received from the government, but it seems that children of Gold Star families were inadvertently swept up in the tax law, forced to pay the 37% rate, as well as back taxes. To be clear, it was **not** designed to impact Gold Star families. This was an unintended consequence, and the government is working to rectify this situation for Gold Star families.
