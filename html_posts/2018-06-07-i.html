Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How many gun-related mass murders have occurred in the US in the last 50 years that have been perpetrated by a person or people who followed gun laws?** So let's step back understanding that murder is illegal so no one who has committed a mass murder has actually followed all laws. Let's look at *up until the point of the mass murder*, whether or not all laws were followed. The news is very clear about these while they're being reported, because soon after you hear about a mass murder, the questions arise. Where did the shooter get the gun? Was it legal for the shooter to possess a gun? How did the shooter get the gun into a place where there shouldn't be guns? If we focus on school shootings, we can say that none of the shooters were following gun laws, as schools (currently) are gun-free zones, and private citizens, even fully licensed ones, cannot carry firearms into school zones. Therefore, every school shooter violated the law. **But the law did exactly what it was supposed to do.** Every one of the school shooters (who was alive following the incident) was prosecuted for violating the gun-free zone laws. And for murder too, but we're stepping back from that for the moment. *Wait, the law wasn't designed to stop shooters before they kill?* **NO, it was not.** In fact, the most shocking thing I think I can say is that **laws do not stop people from doing bad things if they choose.** I think people have a tremendous misconception about what laws are supposed to do. Laws are designed to punish those who break them. They are a *disincentive,* ***not*** *a preventative.* You would think that putting a law in place would prevent 100% of people from doing that particular bad thing, but it does not. It only prevents those who **choose** to follow that law from receiving that consequence. People can, in fact, choose to *not* follow that law, at which point the consequence kicks in. Notice that the law only prevented people who **chose** to follow it from receiving consequences. People who **chose** not to follow it received consequences. Therefore, the law actually didn't prevent bad behavior. It only prevented *the law-abiding* from receiving consequences from performing said bad behavior. People who chose to not follow the law received the consequences. **But they still performed the bad behavior before they received the consequences. So the law did not keep anyone safe. It only punished the law breaker** ***after the fact.*** *So if the law can't keep people safe, how do you stop a shooting before it happens?* **SECURITY.** Security prevents bad things from happening in the first place. Laws take the person *who has already performed a bad act* and gives them consequences. Laws have their effect on the back side of the act (after it happens); security has its effect on the front side of the act (before it happens). The challenge (should we have the courage to accept it) is making security *ubiquitous* and *unobtrusive* but *effective*. This is our challenge, and it will not be easy, but I am convinced that it is possible.
