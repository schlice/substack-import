**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'll assume you're in the United States. You don't "get released from a doctor's care." If you can no longer pay, you stop going. However, if you still need to go and you can't afford insurance, you find some programs which can help you. There are numerous Federal and State assistance programs for those who don't have the money to afford insurance. Medicaid, Medicare, the ACA Healthcare Marketplace, etc. all have options for people who cannot afford insurance. Alternatively, there may be some facilities which take charity patients. You would have to work that out with the facility themselves. Some publicly-funded institutions must take a certain amount of charity patients, and they usually do that by writing off the debts of those who cannot pay and who don't qualify for government assistance. So you may still be able to get the care you need, even if you can't afford to pay. Talk to your physical therapist about treatment options for those who are uninsured. They may even be able to give you some simple strengthening exercises that you can do on your own once you leave their care. Hope is never lost. It's only hopeless when we give up.
