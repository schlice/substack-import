**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I lower TPO antibodies when all other lab results are normal?** The short answer is: you don't, because it's likely that TPO antibodies are not your biggest problem. Thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibodies are a marker for the presence of autoimmune thyroid disease. Blood test results for TPO antibodies [[are positive in 95% of patients](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6516b3&url=https://www.mdedge.com/clinicianreviews/article/72387/endocrinology/thyroid-peroxidase-antibodies){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="mdedge.com"}]{.qlink_container} with chronic lymphocytic thyroiditis, also known as *Hashimoto's disease*, and in 50% to 80% of patients with Graves' disease. Hashimoto\'s disease often leads to an underactive thyroid gland, a condition known as *hypothyroidism*. Symptoms of an underactive thyroid include feeling tired, feeling cold when others do not, constipation, weight gain, and heavier-than-normal menstrual periods. Not all patients with Hashimoto's disease develop hypothyroidism. Graves\' disease is an immune system disorder that results in the overproduction of thyroid hormones, also known as *hyperthyroidism*. Although a number of disorders may result in hyperthyroidism, Graves\' disease is a common cause. Because thyroid hormones affect a number of different body systems, signs and symptoms associated with Graves\' disease can be wide ranging and significantly influence your overall well-being. Although Graves\' disease may affect anyone, it\'s more common among women and before the age of 40.
