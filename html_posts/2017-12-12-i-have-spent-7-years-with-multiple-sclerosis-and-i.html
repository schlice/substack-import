**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > ***I have spent 7 years with multiple sclerosis and I am all OK. Can I > expect the rest of my life to be the same?*** > > *I am having MS since March 2010 and today I complete 7 years. Till > now I have gone through 3 episodes, but currently I do not have any > physical or mental problem with grace of God. Can I expect my rest of > the life would go smoothly?* You didn't indicate whether or not you are on a disease-modifying drug (DMD), but you did indicate that you have regular disease activity (three relapses in seven years would likely be considered regular disease activity). I would assume that with this rate of relapse, you have relapsing-remitting MS (RRMS) and are not using one of the DMDs we have available. On average, for people who are not using a disease modifying drug, it takes 10 years for RRMS to progress to secondary-progressive MS (SPMS). So can I expect the rest of your life to go smoothly? Based on statistics and averages for people not on a DMD, I would have to say **no**. If you are not taking a DMD, I would say you're likely just a few years away from having a relapse that doesn't go away. If you're not taking a DMD, you need to start. Basically, all of the DMDs do the same thing: - slow the rate of relapses - reduce the severity of relapses - extend the amount of time between relapses Basically, the DMDs slow the progression of the disease. Nothing can completely stop the progression of the disease, but even when you are "all OK," the disease silently progresses in the background. It is fallacy to think that because you are currently symptom-free that your MS is not silently progressing. Perhaps it's time to talk to a neurologist about getting on a DMD.
