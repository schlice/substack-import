Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So since you cited "sin" in your question, this question only applies to religious believers. You can safely ignore all the answers from atheists, since if you don't believe in a higher power, there really is no universal right and wrong anyway, and no behavior is *verboten*. Sin can only be properly understood in the religious context, so your question only really applies to religious believers. Now, in the Christian religion, the Bible (Hebrews 13:4) is clear that sex needs to be maintained within the confines of marriage: > *Let marriage be held in honor among all, and let the marriage bed be > undefiled, for God will judge the sexually immoral and adulterous.* Clearly therefore, what is **acceptable** *within the context of marriage* is **unacceptable** *outside of the context of marriage*. Additionally, God lays out the purpose of sex, and it is in the context of marriage. For Christians, God designed sex to be something shared between a husband and a wife. For the Christian married couple, sex is about more than just sharing your bodies. It's a journey of intimacy. Since anything within the boundaries of marriage is acceptable, God leaves it up to the married couple to work through what things are acceptable *for them*. For example, some couples include oral sex or sex toys in their repertoire. For others, it may be a trigger for memories of past abuse or old pornography habits. So the same act can be loving for some couples and harmful for others. Working through those boundaries together is a great way for a couple to grow in love more deeply as a couple while seeking God's wisdom in everything.
