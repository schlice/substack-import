**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is Air Force One's cockpit door locked during flight?** Unless something has changed significantly, Air Force One's cockpit door is locked just the same as every other cockpit door on every other Air Force transport aircraft. The door remains closed and locked in flight, unless the flight crew is entering or exiting the cockpit. While the flight crew can come out of the cockpit, they can only go so far as the forward galley or the lav. Secret Service makes sure of that. Primarily this is because, as a general practice, the flight crew does not have the requisite clearance to interact directly with the President. If the President chooses to poke his head into the cockpit, that's his choice, and Secret Service must adapt accordingly. But the flight crew may not enter any of the areas where the President or his staff are located. To be fair, I flew on Air Force Two in the 90s, so like I said, something may have changed.
