**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Since I am not a militant vaxxer, nor am I anti-cruise, I feel like I can actually answer your question. The answer is: ***when they have taken enough financial losses to finally admit defeat and start returning life to normal.*** The CDC allowed the Conditional Sail Order (CSO) mandating 100% vaccinated passengers to expire on January 15, 2022. At that time, they ***recommended*** the cruise lines voluntarily follow the [[COIVD-19 Program for Cruise Ships](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ee704f&url=https://www.cdc.gov/quarantine/cruise/management/technical-instructions-for-cruise-ships.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cdc.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. *On January 15th,* all major cruise lines did opt in to the program. The program includes being labeled as a "good boy" on the CDC's list of [[Cruise Line Status](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f81c12&url=https://www.cdc.gov/quarantine/cruise/cruise-ship-color-status.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cdc.gov"}]{.qlink_container} if you have 95% of your passengers (excluding children) fully vaccinated, and being labeled as a "bad boy" if you didn't have 95% of your passengers and crew vaccinated.\* The major cruise lines, of course, opted in to the program by creating health and safety rules which demanded that all passengers and crew be fully vaccinated, up to date, and tested before boarding. Some people, especially those who had COVID-19 and recovered (and now that Omicron has literally swept the population, that number is growing by leaps and bounds), are simply disinterested in being vaccinated against a virus for which they already have immunity. So they won't sail, despite their love for cruising. And the cruise lines have been feeling the pinch of cruisers "opting out" of cruising. They have lost *billions* in revenue since the start of the pandemic. And they know full well that if they continue like this, it won't be long before their reserve coffers run dry and they're out of business. They built up massive war chests before the pandemic and they've been living off of those reserves, but they can't keep doing that forever. In other words, *they cannot keep operating like this and expect to stay in business.* At some point, some major cruise line will *be forced* to make the difficult decision to allow unvaccinated passengers on board. And it will be a *windfall* for that cruise line. They'll be booked solid once they make the announcement. There is definitely pent-up demand for cruising, but people want *normal* cruising where they can *relax*. They don't want this virus panic on the ship. They want to *get away* from virus panic. \* Of course, they didn't call them "good boy" and "bad boy." They called them the more politically-correct terms of "Highly Vaccinated" or "Not Highly Vaccinated." \ Original question: When will unvaccinated people be allowed to cruise again?
