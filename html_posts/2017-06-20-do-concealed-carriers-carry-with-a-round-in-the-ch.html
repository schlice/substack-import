Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Oh yes, every time. It's like American Express: "Don't Leave Home Without It." - It doesn't make the firearm any less safe. Most modern firearms have several internal safeties which prevent the gun from firing unless you pull the trigger. Our instructor in the military told us we could hammer nails with the back of the gun and it wouldn't fire. I wasn't about to test the theory, but I see where he's coming from. - When you need that firearm, you need it *yesterday.* You have zero time to waste on anything, including chambering a round. - When you draw your firearm, it's because you intend on using it. You don't draw it to play with it. You don't draw it to threaten someone. You draw it because you are planning on shooting someone. As in the previous bullet, you have zero time to waste on anything, because fractions of seconds count. - Also, it means you can carry one more round of ammunition. That's why you'll sometimes hear that a firearm can carry "X+1" rounds, which means the capacity of the magazine, plus one round in the chamber.
