--- title: "How are the police and military trained differently?" date: "2015-08-13" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [(Answer specific to the United States)\ \ They are each trained for their unique mission.\ \ The police are a law enforcement organization, and so they are very heavily trained in the laws which they are charged with enforcing. It is a very broad-based training, as a police officer works mostly autonomously and can respond to any call for service. They are trained in the use of all of the tools on their toolbelts, including the Taser, the baton, and of course, their sidearm. They are heavily trained in arrest control, vehicle operations, and firearms.\ \ The military is a force projection tool for the government, charged with supporting and defending the Constitution from all enemies. It is very compartmentalized, so their training is top-down, and specific to their job function. All recruits are given similar basic military training, which includes military decorum (how to salute, how to march, etc), physical conditioning, military history, and military rules and standards. Once they complete that training, they are assigned to a \"specialty\" which performs a specific military function. They receive specialized training in that function. For example, a military member assigned to Communications and Computer Systems will attend a special school to learn the basics of that job. Once they complete their \"tech school\" as it is called, they are assigned to an active unit. They then learn the specifics of working in that unit, usually through OJT (on-the-job training) which can be formal or informal.]{.rendered_qtext}
