**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can I use my homeowners insurance to pay the \$1,000 deductible for damage done to my car, parked in my assigned parking space in the condo building's parking lot? My auto insurance covered the damage.** Generally, homeowner's or renter's insurance cannot be used to pay deductibles. You may have separate gap coverage (i.e. AFLAC) which might cover deductibles, but in general, insurance can't be used to pay deductibles. The deductible is the amount you must pay before insurance will pay. You can always choose a lower deductible and higher monthly premiums, but the deductible is the way risk is shared between you and the insurance company. So the deductible is on you to pay. If your insurance reimburses you for the damage done to your car, they will simply deduct your deductible from what they reimburse, so in function, you don't necessarily have to "pay" your deductible out of your pocket in most cases. There are a small subset of cases where you may have to put that money down, but one way or another (either out of pocket or out of your reimbursement), you will pay your deductible.
