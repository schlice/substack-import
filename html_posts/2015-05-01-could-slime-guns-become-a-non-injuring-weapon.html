**Content:** [This sort of technology is currently in existence. We have \"net guns\" and \"bean bag guns\" that can be used without hurting someone.\ \ Some people won\'t be stopped by these technologies. And the use of one of these, in lieu of deadly force, will still leave the police and the public vulnerable to the use of deadly force.\ \ So the real question is: do you think that it is appropriate to use lethal force against someone who is a threat to a police officer and to the general public? Is there a line that you would draw below which you could \*not\* use lethal force? Would you universally say that Michael Brown should not have been shot simply because he was unarmed? Does \"unarmed\" equal \"not dangerous\" in your eyes?\ \ I might also add that a TASER is a less-than-lethal weapon, just as a slime gun is. Until the slime gun malfunctions, or a suspect chokes on the slime and dies, or the suspect slips in the slime and falls down and breaks his head open and dies. Does that make the slime gun a lethal weapon? No more than it makes a TASER a lethal weapon, and it still hurts.\ \ A slime gun is big and awkward (you need a big tank of slime to spray). When I can wear a slime gun on my belt like a TASER, I\'ll look at it as a possibility.]{.rendered_qtext}
