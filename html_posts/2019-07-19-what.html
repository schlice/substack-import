**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What\'s your reaction to the U.S. Air Force telling the Washington Post that the\"Facebook Storm Area 51\", cannot happen and that\" any attempt to illegally access military installations is dangerous\"?** The Air Force was telling the truth. You cannot storm Area 51. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-29a5605faaf97cece06f4f206e4d3695){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-29a5605faaf97cece06f4f206e4d3695" master_w="1280" master_h="960"} ::: :::: This is a sign from the outer border of Area 51. The little inset sign on the big one has little red words on the bottom which read: "Use of deadly force is authorized." It's true. I used to work at another Priority 1 non-nuclear facility: NORAD. We had the same signs. I carried the guns. The difference at Area 51 is that the actual facility is surrounded by *thousands of acres of desert terrain,* which is all owned by the government. Anyone even hanging around the outer border not in a common public area will incur a visit by the local sheriff. Anyone entering the outer border will be arrested. But what about the base, you say? The closest you can get to the base on public land is Tikaboo Peak, which is still 26 miles from the facility. You'll have a long way to Naruto run before you even make it to the fence. Same distance as a marathon, basically, but through the desert.
