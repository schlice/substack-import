--- title: '"Can the Commander-in-Chief be charged with dereliction of duty?"' date: '2020-03-30' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the Commander-in-Chief be charged with dereliction of duty?** No, because the President is not a member of the military. The Commander-in-Chief is not ***at*** the top of the military chain of command. He is ***above*** the military chain of command, ***outside*** the military chain of command, and is thus not subject to the UCMJ. There are already remedies listed in the Constitution for punishing a President for conduct which might be criminal (see *Impeachment*).
