Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should people that block emergency rooms, from people who need immediate treatment, be charged with murder in case of their death?** No. Murder is not an appropriate charge. A better charge would be involuntary manslaughter (or criminally negligent homicide, depending on your jurisdiction). This is a crime in which someone acts recklessly or negligently, and someone else dies as a result. Intentionally blocking access to an emergency room is both reckless and negligent. If someone dies because they couldn't access that emergency room, the blocker could be charged with involuntary manslaughter.
