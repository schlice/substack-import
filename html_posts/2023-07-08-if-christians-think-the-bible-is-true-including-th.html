I do find it fascinating that if you were to ask an Evolutionist or a Darwinist the same question, they would unequivocally tell you that all humans descended from primates. *So the atheists actually agree with the theists that humans all came from common ancestors.* :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-c976809032ec1d77d6838b8377327e0e){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-c976809032ec1d77d6838b8377327e0e" master_w="1280" master_h="960"} ::: :::: *"So then, how do you explain the different races?"* As a theist, I'm going to give you the most non-theistic answer: **evolution**. ***WHAT?????? THEISTS ADMIT THAT EVOLUTION HAPPENED?*** Oh yes. We've said it all along, because *we have seen it with our own eyes, and in our own lifetimes.* Specifically, it's called *microevolution*. We also commonly refer to it as *adaptation*. Small changes wherein a species adapts to its surroundings. For example, when bacteria are attacked by antibiotics, some survive because the parent bacteria possessed the genetic capacity to resist, or a rare biochemical mutation somehow helped it survive (we say "rare" because mutations are nearly always harmful). It passes that capacity or mutation on to its offspring. The evolutionist would say, "Yes, the bacteria *evolved."* We agree, they did. But in what way? The theist observes that throughout all of these mutations and generations, *the bacteria always remain bacteria.* They never become frogs or elephants. So categorizing the evolution (and calling it what it is, pointing out what it is not), is critical when we discuss evolution. *"But races are sooooo different! They must have evolved separately!"* Well actually, they're not. In actuality, there is only one race: the human race. The differences between the "races" are actually so small as to be trivial. The variations in DNA between individual humans is approximately 0.2%. Of this difference, only 6% of that is ethnic differences. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-8ccc59832d05889985996d3930f91720){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-8ccc59832d05889985996d3930f91720" master_w="397" master_h="251"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*ChristianAnswers.net*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5551dd&url=https://christiananswers.net/q-aig/race-definition.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="christiananswers.net"}]{.qlink_container}*)* The similarity between humans, even between humans of different races, is so great that, in practice, black people are often better tissue matches for white people than other white people. That's quite a telling data point. Humans aren't very different from each other. *"So back to the differences. How did they happen?"* So let's discuss our differences. There are four main ethnic groups of human beings, according to anthropologists: - Caucasoid (European or "white"), - Mongoloid (which includes the Chinese, Inuit or Eskimo, and Native Americans) - Negroid (black Africans), and - Australoid (the Australian Aborigines). Within those four main groups can exist subgroups. But even between the ethnic groups, everyone has all the same stuff. No one ethnic group has *something* that another ethnic group *lacks.* For example, everyone has melanin in their skin. Negroid people simply routinely express more than Caucasoid, and this is likely a function of where they primarily lived for thousands of years. Also, look at eyes. The typical almond-shaped Asian eye has exactly the same structural makeup as the traditional Caucasoid eye, but the Asian eye has a different amount of fat around it. They both have fat, but one has more, and the other has less. I posit that the differences between ethnic groups is far less than people think. Calling us different "races" just makes this confusion even stronger. \ Wanna hear the craziest modern-day hot take?
