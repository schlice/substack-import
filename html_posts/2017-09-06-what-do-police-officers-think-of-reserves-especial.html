--- title: "What do police officers think of reserves, especially those who have been through the academy and have been sworn?" date: "2017-09-06" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Every line cop I bump into who says they don't recognize me and I tell them I'm a Reservist has told me, "Wow, that's really awesome." "Thanks for what you do." "We really appreciate you guys." Or something similar. They thank us because we're the guys who come out to sit on a crime scene so they can get back to patrol. We're the guys who provide security for the County Fair so they don't have to. We're the guys doing the jobs that are "time sinks" that are serious time killers but require a sworn deputy to do. They know that if it wasn't for us, it would be them filling that position. We consider ourselves "force multipliers." We are there to back them up, relieve them when needed, and stand beside them if necessary. They know that, and as far as I can tell, they are grateful. I can understand some of the criticisms, but we don't "take" anyone's paid duty assignments. Those assignments are always offered to line cops first.\* If no one takes it (we always tend to have more requests for paid assignments than we can reasonably fill), they go to Reserves. The pay goes back into the Reserve budget except in extremely rare cases when a paid duty assignment *must* be filled and we can't even find a Reservist to fill it. The department has, on those occasions, authorized the pay to go directly to the Reservist just so the job gets filled. Usually it's something on a holiday when no one but the duty crew wants to be out, and I bet that in half of those cases, even they don't want to be there. These days, I think they recognize that the general public sees Reservists as filling a crucial gap, and I'm not talking about the manpower and money gaps (although we do that). We fill that "in-between" space between a cop and a civilian. We're your insurance agents (we have several), your car dealership owners (we have one), your tech support agents (again, several), military contractors, military retirees (there's a lot of that), etc. We're not full-time cops. We don't "live the life" of a cop. Therefore, in some respects, we provide an "outsiders" view of life as a cop, so the public knows there's people doing the job who have a civilian's perspective on the job, while still being full-fledged cops. The public respects that view, regardless of what they think of cops in general. \ \*There is one caveat to that. Here in Colorado Springs, our department has one big-ticket contract with 10ABW at USAFA to provide all of the security at Air Force home football games. With few exception, all of the deputies at the game are Reservists. And that contract is our bread-and-butter. Not to mention, it comprises a significant chunk of the 192 annual hours we are required to commit to the department. To be fair, pretty much everyone puts in more than that, but I had to mention the minimum requirement.
