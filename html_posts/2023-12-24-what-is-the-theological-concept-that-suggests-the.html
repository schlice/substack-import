Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The logic behind an inerrant Bible is not independent of the deity of Christ, as it is Christ who proclaims the inerrancy of the Bible. Basically, the logic goes like this: 1. Jesus claimed to be God, which is why He was crucified in the first place (blasphemy). 2. Jesus' claim to be God was miraculously confirmed by: 1. His fulfillment of many prophecies about Himself; 2. His sinless life and miraculous deeds; 3. His prediction and accomplishment of His resurrection. 3. Therefore, Jesus is God. 4. Whatever Jesus (who is God) teaches is true. 5. Jesus taught that the Bible is the inerrant Word of God. 6. Therefore, it is true that the Bible is the inerrant Word of God. Do we have any proof that point 5 is true (Jesus taught that the Bible is inerrant)? When the Sadducees tried to trap Jesus with a question, He said to them, "You are in error because you do not know the Scriptures or the power of God" (Matt. 22:29). The implication, of course, is that the Bible is inerrant. It wouldn't make any sense for Jesus to say, "You are in error because you don't know the Scriptures, which are also erroneous!" Therefore, Jesus taught that the Bible is inerrant. And if Jesus is God, and He said that the Bible is inerrant, then we know that it is.
