Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As always, the answer is found in the Governing Documents of the HOA in question. In most cases though, Governing Documents do not require HOAs to take a property owner to court before applying a lien to their property. They simply require a certain number of months of non-payment, or a certain minimum dollar amount of unpaid fees (sometimes both), and they can turn the account over to the attorney who will process the lien. \ Original question: Does an HOA have to take a property owner to court to put a lien on their property?
