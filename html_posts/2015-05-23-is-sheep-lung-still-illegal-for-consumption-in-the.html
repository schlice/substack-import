**Content:** [TL;DR: yes.\ \ There is, to this day, a ban on importing haggis into the US because it contains sheep lung, which is banned for import, as well as being banned for use in any food product for human consumption. The US has been considering reversing that ruling for several years, but it has yet to do so.\ \ While you can\'t *import* traditional Scottish haggis into the US, it can still be *produced* in the US (and this is the product sold in restaurants and in Scottish butcher shops), and is nearly identical to the authentic Scottish product, with two exceptions:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} 1. The USDA does not allow sheep lung to be used in any product for human consumption, so the haggis produced in the US does not have it. 2. The haggis produced in the US is not encased in a stomach, but in an artificial casing. Other than that, the haggis produced here is identical to the authentic Scottish product.\ \ McKean\'s is a company that produces \"authentic\" Scottish haggis in the US (following the two rules above). Their [[Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4a1c61&url=http://www.scottishhaggis.com/faqs.aspx){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="scottishhaggis.com"}]{.qlink_container} discuss the differences between the US product and its traditional Scottish roots.
