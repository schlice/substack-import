**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} ***Because that's not their job.*** Their job is to interpret what those fallible people wrote in the Constitution, and compare laws to that document. That's it. Their job is not to determine that the people who wrote the rules in the Constitution were *wrong* when they did it. And it's **certainly** not their job to decide cases accordingly. The Court decides cases based on how the Constitution is written, based on what the people who wrote it believed at the time they wrote it. If there is anyone who must accept the fallibility of the Founding Fathers, it is the American people. When they find that fallibility, it is up to the American people to amend the Constitution to correct any failings that they find.
