--- title: '"What is the difference between multiple sclerosis and MS-associated tremor ataxia syndrome (MTAS)? Is it possible to have both diseases at the same time?"' date: '2022-12-12' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Ultimately, there is no difference. Ataxia is an MS symptom, and it is the **lack of voluntary coordination of muscle movements**. In MS, this can present as clumsiness, unsteady gait, impaired eye and limb movements, and speech problems. In some cases, the lack of coordination appears as muscle tremors. So MS-associated ataxia is simply a symptom of MS. There is a different, yet related ataxia syndrome, and it is called **fragile X-associated tremor ataxia syndrome** (FXTAS). In FXTAS, there is also brain damage visible on an MRI, but it is specifically located in the area of the brain related to muscle movement (the cerebellum). FXTAS is a late-onset disease, usually presenting after age 50. The most common symptom of FXTAS is **intention tremor**, wherein a muscle you are trying to control trembles.
