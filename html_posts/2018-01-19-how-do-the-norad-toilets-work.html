**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do the NORAD toilets work?** If I had to try to understand this question, I would probably think that you're asking how is wastewater handled in NORAD. I'm assuming you're not asking how the toilets in the bathrooms work, because they are regular bathrooms with sinks and urinals and toilets and they all work like regular sinks and urinals and toilets. The short answer is that black water and gray water are pumped via an underground buried pipeline to nearby Fort Carson, and is sent to their sewer system for treatment. Drinking water comes from a natural spring which was discovered while digging out the facility. Clean spring water overflow is discharged either directly out the side of the mountain or is discharged into Fountain Creek, or is used to carry any remaining effluent into the Fort Carson pipeline. Here are the details on the system [[from the EPA](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cf8d0d&url=https://www.epa.gov/sites/production/files/2016-10/documents/co-0034762-cmafs-sob-pn-2016-b.pdf){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="epa.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. In this summary, CMAFS stands for Cheyenne Mountain Air Force Station, the actual name of the Cheyenne Mountain Complex. Interestingly, it took **40 years** to get the EPA to approve that "NPDES permit" which allowed us to discharge **untreated natural spring water** out of the side of the mountain. We literally discharged natural spring water overflow into the Fort Carson sewage pipeline for 40 years because we didn't have the permit to discharge out of the side of the mountain. Note that this is overflow from a 1.5 million gallon drinking water tank.
