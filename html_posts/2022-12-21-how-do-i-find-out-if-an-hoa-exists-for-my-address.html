--- title: "How do I find out if an HOA exists for my address in Florida?" date: "2022-12-21" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} When you bought your house, you would have signed the Governing Documents for the HOA in that big stack of papers you signed. Therefore, your deed would reflect that your home is part of a Homeowner's Association. You can check with your County Property Appraiser's office to retrieve a copy of your deed as filed with them, and it will indicate whether or not your home is part of an HOA. In fact, simply pulling the electronic records of your property on file with the County Appraiser's office should show clearly if the property is part of an HOA.
