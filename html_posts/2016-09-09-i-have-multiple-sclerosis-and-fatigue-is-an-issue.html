--- title: "I have multiple sclerosis and fatigue is an issue. How do I exercise regularly with such severe fatigue?" date: "2016-09-09" tags: ["health-autoimmune"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Basically, it's up to the individual. There will be some experimentation (i.e. trial and error) you will have to go through to find the right balance of exercise which produces minimal fatigue. The guideline is that if your fatigue lasts longer than **two hours** following your workout, your workout was probably too intense. Try to scale back next time, either a shorter time, lower frequency, or a less-intense workout. Use the Perceived Exertion scale to monitor your workouts. The PE scale is a 0--10 scale which helps you determine how hard you are working before, during, and after your workout. Warm-up and cool-down should be at a 1--2 RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion), and your exercise should be at a 3--5 RPE. Try to avoid exercising during the hottest part of the day. You might also consider something that automatically battles heat while you exercise, like aquatics. Aquatics can give you a great cardio workout while automatically reducing body heat, thus reducing fatigue. Finally, there are some lower-intensity workouts that people with MS may find extremely useful, like yoga and tai chi. These exercises can aid balance and coordination, also extremely beneficial for the MS patient.[\[1\]](#qwWZv){#cite-qwWZv .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-qwWZv){.citation_index} [[Benefits of an Exercise Program](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2b14d5&url=http://www.nationalmssociety.org/For-Professionals/Clinical-Care/Managing-MS/Intro-to-MS-for-Fitness-Professionals/Module-3){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container}
