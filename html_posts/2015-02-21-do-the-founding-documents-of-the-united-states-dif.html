--- title: "Do the founding documents of the United States differentiate between the states and the people?" date: "2015-02-21" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [This is a great question, and the assignment of rights to the states and to the people was done in the Bill of Rights, the amendments to the Constitution.\ \ The Constitution itself does not enumerate rights. It defines the limits of government. The tenth amendment specifically reaffirms this by saying that any powers not expressly enumerated to the federal government being to the states, or to the people.\ \ The use of the term \"the states\" indicates that a right is granted to state and local government, and not to the federal government. This part of the amendment has been used a number of times when the federal government has tried to force states to enforce federal law.\ \ The term \"the people\" was used three times in the Bill of Rights and it indicates an individual right given to each and every person in the country individually. For example, the Second Amendment says that \"the right of the people to keep and bear arms shall not be infringed.\" The courts reaffirmed this as an individual right, not a right gained by participating in a group activity.]{.rendered_qtext}
