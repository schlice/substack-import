**Content:** [Your first step would be to convince everyone that prison is not rehabilitative but punitive. In the United States, we have always held the belief that prison is supposed to be rehabilitative. Whether or not it is effective at being rehabilitative is not the question. The point is prison in the United States is supposed to be rehabilitative. Sending prisoners outside of the United States would not be considered rehabilitative, it would be considered punitive.\ \ Once you have completely changed the structure of the penal system in the United States to a punitive one instead of rehabilitative, then you can address the cost-benefits and the other benefits of sending prisoners outside of the United States.\ \ Though, if we change the entire prison system to a punitive one instead of a rehabilitative one, we could probably achieve greater cost savings by keeping our prisoners in the United States, but placing them all on a fenced in island in the middle of the ocean instead of shipping them by aircraft to a Third World country. Drop them all onto a prison island and let them fend for themselves. I can\'t remember off the top of my head what movie that\'s from, but if we\'re making our prison system punitive (in other words, our prison system is punishment instead of rehabilitative) then dropping them all onto a deserted island surrounded by 50 foot walls would be punishment enough. Hey, maybe we could get Trump to build the walls.]{.rendered_qtext}
