**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What does the law expect when an armed civilian requests proof from a potential police imposter beyond their equipment and their word that they are law enforcement?** "The law" is incredibly variable, as it is not Federal law (as they have no jurisdiction over individual police departments) and is most often not state law either. This means it's up to department policy as to whether and when an officer must show ID. Most departments do require their officers show some form of ID, but they also have wide latitude to deny the request if it will jeopardize safety or hamper police work (i.e. they are not required to stop what they're doing in order to show you their ID on demand). Whether or not the requester is armed makes zero difference in whether or not an officer must identify himself.
