**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My 13 year old is in the hospital after almost attempting suicide. He didn't want me to tell his siblings (10,7) why he's in the hospital. Should I tell them why?** Perhaps you can use this opportunity to make a deal with him. You'll promise to not tell his younger siblings why he's in the hospital if he agrees to go to therapy with you. You can simply tell the younger ones that he got hurt but he's going to be okay. He's going to have to go visit the doctor for a while afterwards, but it will all be fine. That's not technically a lie, but it's also not the whole truth either. At this stage in their young lives, their brains are not yet developed enough to understand the nuances of what is happening, so I would consider it parental prerogative to keep the details away from them. Save the whole truth for later, but you will have to tell them at some point. Just not yet. You probably should realize that kids, especially at this age, don't just decide one day to commit suicide. You're going to need to prepare yourself for a look in the mirror that I can guarantee you will at the very least be uncomfortable, and at worst, will be quite disturbing. But if you love your child (which I'm certain you do), you'll do the hard work needed to remedy the **underlying causes** that are driving your son to this line of action. There are underlying causes, and someone other than him is part of the problem. I know that's a lot to take in, especially while you have a child recovering from an apparent suicide attempt. But think about it: he'd rather end his life than reveal what is really going on. He must be so scared inside. This revelation should steel you to resolve that issue, sooner rather than later. Love him, *forgive him,* and **get him help.** I wish you all the best.
