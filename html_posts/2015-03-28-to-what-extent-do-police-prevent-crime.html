**Content:** [It\'s a function of numbers. There aren\'t nearly enough cops out there to be able to effectively catch criminals immediately after a crime has been done. We\'d need far more cops to be able to have that sort of saturation in the general population, and that sort of society turns more into a police state than I\'d like to see.\ \ Whether they catch the criminal 5 minutes after, or 24 hours after, the crime is not really relevant. So long as they catch the criminal, they have accomplished their stated duties. Why would the speed of apprehension affect a criminal? If a criminal knew he\'d be caught within 24 hours, would he still commit the crime?\ \ Sadly, this is the actual reason that crime persists: most criminals do not get caught. The very large majority of crimes go unsolved. It\'s true that if there is not a significant lead within the first 48 hours of a crime, the possibility of catching the criminal reduces by nearly 50%. So speed is definitely of the essence.\ \ Let me just add one more thing. Police are there to catch the criminal after the crime. That fact puts your well-being in ***your*** hands, not in the hands of the police. There will always be crime, there will always be criminals, because there will always be people, and people are people. But your well-being is up to you. You can choose to be a victim of crime, or you can fight back. The choice, and the consequences, are up to you. Don\'t be a bystander in your own life.]{.rendered_qtext}
