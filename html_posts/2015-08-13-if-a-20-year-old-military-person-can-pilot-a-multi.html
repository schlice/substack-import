--- title: "If a 20 year old military person can pilot a multi-billion dollar nuclear submarine or surface vessel, command a multi-million dollar tank or aircraft or lead a group of men into armed conflict, then why is it still illegal for them to drink alcohol in most of the United States?" date: "2015-08-13" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Because ***piloting*** a submarine does not take the same higher reasoning skills required to ***command*** the submarine. Air Force aircraft are piloted by officers, and they are older than 20. Men are not led by 20-year old enlistees; men are led by NCOs or officers who are most assuredly older than 20, and those groups are ***commanded*** by senior officers. So yes, the driver of the sub is a 20 year old enlisted person. The driver of the tank is a 20 year old enlisted person. But just because they are stick and wheel actuators doesn\'t mean they have free rein as to where those vehicles go. The ***commander*** of such a vehicle is an officer; the commander of the submarine is an O-5 or better, with 15+ years of military service, and well into his 30s, the commander of the tank is an O-3 or better, and he too is probably 25 or older, the age at which higher reasoning skills in the brain are said to have fully developed.]{.rendered_qtext}
