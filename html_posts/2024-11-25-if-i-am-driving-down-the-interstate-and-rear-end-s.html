--- title: "If I am driving down the interstate and rear end someone that has no tail lights, who would be at fault?" date: "2024-11-25" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal answers, the answer is "it depends." However, in all at-fault states in the US, the car in the back is always at fault in a rear-end accident. Your auto insurance usually covers the costs if you are found at fault in an accident. Auto insurance has multiple parts. **Liability insurance** pays the other party\'s injuries and property damage if you are found liable or "at fault." **Collision insurance** pays for *your* vehicle *regardless* of who caused the accident. But there is one more wrinkle in auto insurance: **no-fault states**. In a \"no-fault\" state, your own insurance will typically cover your medical bills *regardless* of who caused the accident, while in an \"at-fault\" state, the at-fault driver\'s insurance is primarily responsible for the other party\'s damages. In "at-fault" states (most US states are), if you are the car in back, your liability may be **limited** by the fact that the car in front had faulty tail lights. This would limit the amount your insurance company would have to pay. But the default position is that the car in back is responsible for the accident. You can help yourself by installing a dash cam. The dash cam video can show the missing tail lights (or any other mitigating factor in an accident) and may help you to avoid having your rates increase due to an accident which you would normally be at fault for. It can also help your insurance company negotiate a lower liability payment to the other company.
