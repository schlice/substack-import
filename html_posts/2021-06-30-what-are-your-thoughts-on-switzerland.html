**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Switzerland should do what's best for Switzerland. And in Switzerland's case, they want to show their faces. "In Switzerland, our tradition is that you show your face. That is a sign of our basic freedoms," Walter Wobmann, chairman of the referendum committee and a member of parliament for the Swiss People's Party, had said before the vote. Additionally, in Switzerland, almost no one wears a burqa and only around 30 women wear the niqab, the University of Lucerne estimates. Muslims make up 5% of the Swiss population of 8.6 million people. Think about that: 30 women out of a country of 8.6 million people. And almost no one wears a burqa.
