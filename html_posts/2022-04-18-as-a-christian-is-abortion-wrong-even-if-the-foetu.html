**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember that we're answering this as a Christian. And as a Christian, all abortion is wrong, regardless of the reason, because all life is created by God. God knew you before you were even conceived. He knew exactly what you would become. The Bible says that God formed you in the womb, stitched together the very fibre of your being. Voluntarily taking that life without justification violates the Sixth Commandment (you shall not murder). The only time abortion is not wrong is to save the mother's life. Other than that, abortion is morally wrong and violates God's law. What other crime allows you to kill the victim? I can't really think of any. You do realize that the baby is an innocent victim, correct? Why does it have to die? It's kinda illogical. Legally speaking (not as a Christian), I can see that a rape is not consensual, and therefore the woman did not consent to having her body be used to grow a baby for nine months. In that limited circumstance, I could see early-term abortion being permitted, even though I still believe killing babies is wrong. And that's about it for me. Late-term abortion should never be allowed. Allow the baby to come to term and give it up for adoption. Abortions out of nothing more than the mother's convenience should never be allowed. And why would abortion be allowed in the case of "stolen condom self-impregnation?" The woman wants a baby so badly that they would be willing to steal a condom and self-impregnate with its contents.
