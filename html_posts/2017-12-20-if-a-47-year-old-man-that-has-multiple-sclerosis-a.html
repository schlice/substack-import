**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a 47-year-old man that has multiple sclerosis, and smokes marijuana everyday several times a day and night while on several MS medications be a part of the reason that his symptoms are getting worse?** Medical cannabis is not a disease modifying drug. It does not slow down the progression of the disease, and it does not reduce the severity, duration, or frequency of MS relapses. What medical cannabis **does** do for MS patients is reduce neurological pain better than any prescription pain medicine on the market. For those MS patients who struggle with neurological pain (i.e. pain caused by faulty nerves, not by any physical condition), cannabis can be extremely helpful without all of the toxic side effects of today's high-test pain medicines like Oxycontin. I do question what "several MS medications" means. I assume it means a disease modifying drug and other meds to counter side effects, but I'm just taking a guess. If I were the physician, I'd focus on the particular disease modifying drug, as it's fairly clear that it is not working. It would be time to take a different tack.
