**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Question Details:* > > *I am a Service Connected Disabled Veteran with multiple deployments > to Iraq and Afghanistan. I am seeking a career in Law Enforcement and > am worried that my VA Disability Rating might be viewed negatively.* 1. Your medical records are protected health information (PHI) and cannot be accessed by anyone without your written consent. This falls under the federal HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) guidelines. No agency has the authority to override this law. Your VA disability rating is considered PHI. 2. The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) also protects you, as you are an American and you are disabled. ADA changed how employers are allowed to screen potential candidates so that persons with disabilities are not discriminated against. Your prospective employer is only allowed to list the physical functions required in the job and then they are allowed to ask you whether or not you can perform the physical functions listed, **with or without reasonable accommodations.** You only have to answer yes or no. The employer is allowed to perform physical testing to ensure you can meet those standards. So long as you pass the physical test, you can be hired. I have personal experience with this, as I too have a service-connected disability and I successfully completed the hiring process for my local Sheriff's Office. You can choose to disclose or not to disclose this information to your prospective employer; you are under no obligation to disclose that information. Your employer may not discriminate against you because you have a disability.
