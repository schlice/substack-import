--- title: "Do individuals labeled with disabilities get paid less?" date: "2016-05-20" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you are asking as a matter of law, the answer is no. Disabled people do not get paid less because they are "labeled" as disabled. If you are asking as a practical matter, then it's possible that a disabled person may not get the same job opportunities or the same promotion opportunities because they were labeled as disabled and some manager believes that the disabled person is somehow unworthy of the additional pay or position. This would also be something not based on any actual job performance. The job performance would be the same as their fully-abled counterparts, but somehow they would be paid less. This, by the way, in the United States, is highly illegal and opens the employer up to massive lawsuits for violating the Americans with Disabilities Act.
