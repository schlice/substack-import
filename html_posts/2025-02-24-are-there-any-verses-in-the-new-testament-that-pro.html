--- title: '"Are there any verses in the New Testament that promise material blessings to a follower of Jesus?"' date: '2025-02-24' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Before I answer your specific question, I need to explain what it is you are referring to. This is a movement known as **Prosperity Theology**. It is a belief that financial and physical well-being is a gift of God, and that faith and charitable donations will increase a believer's material wealth. **We believe that this is an unbiblical, quite possibly heretical, teaching.** There are a number of verses that believers in the prosperity gospel point to. I know you said New Testament, but believers also point to an Old Testament passage: > Bring the full tithe into the storehouse, that there may be food in my > house. And thereby put me to the test, says the LORD of hosts, if I > will not open the windows of heaven for you and pour down for you a > blessing until there is no more need. \-- Malachi 3:10 ESV But from the New Testament, we have the following: > The thief comes only to steal and kill and destroy. I came that they > may have life and have it abundantly. \-- John 10:10 ESV > > And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in > glory in Christ Jesus. \-- Phil 4:19 ESV > > Beloved, I wish above all things that thou mayest prosper and be in > health, even as thy soul prospereth. --- 3 John 1:2 KJV > > Therefore I tell you, whatever you ask in prayer, believe that you > have received it, and it will be yours. \-- Mark 11:24 ESV The idea of the prosperity gospel is foundationally unbiblical, as it promotes the idea of material blessings as being a sign of God's favor. It promotes the idolatry of money, and we believe that Jesus\' teachings indicate a focus away from material wealth, and towards spiritual blessings. Jesus spoke directly about this in the Sermon on the Mount: > Do not lay up for yourselves treasures on earth, where moth and rust > destroy and where thieves break in and steal, but lay up for > yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither moth nor rust destroys > and where thieves do not break in and steal. For where your treasure > is, there your heart will be also. \-- Matthew 6:19--21 ESV We believe that the "blessings" spoken of in the other passages refer to spiritual blessings, not material blessings. Prosperity theology teaches that God is a cosmic vending machine: put your prayers into the little coin slot, and financial prosperity comes out the little door at the bottom. Jesus specifically teaches against this.
