**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the Second Amendment protect the right of a convicted criminal after they are released, or are they considered to have surrendered that right?** The Second Amendment doesn't say anything about convicted criminals. Therefore, the Constitution leaves the details to the law and to the courts. Federal law says that convicted felons who lose certain civil rights (such as the right to vote and the right to hold public office) also lose the right to purchase and own a gun. State law is where the whole thing gets turned on its head. And all states are different. Some states allow non-violent felons to keep firearms. Some only follow federal law. And some states allow rights to be restored after a felon has served time. In the states that allow rights to be restored, if the state restores all rights (i.e. voting rights, serve on juries, gun rights, etc), then the federal ban is no longer applicable. However, if the state only restores gun rights but not other civil rights, then the federal ban remains in effect, and possession of a gun is still a federal offense.
