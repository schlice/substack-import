**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What is your opinion of the SCOTUS' 2nd Amendment ruling that the phrase regarding \"well-regulated militia\" can essentially be ignored as it relates to individual gun ownership?** I think "ignored" is the wrong word to use when it comes to the prefatory clause. The ruling in *DC v. Heller* clarified that the 2nd Amendment has two parts: - The first clause of the Second Amendment that references a "militia" is a ***prefatory clause***. > *A well-regulated Militia, being essential to the security of a free > State* The prefatory clause explains part of the reason we have the Amendment in the first place, *but it does not limit the operative clause of the Amendment*. Additionally, the term "militia" should not be read to limit gun ownership to those serving in the military, because at the time the term "militia" referred to all able-bodied men who were capable of being called to such service. - The second clause of the Second Amendment is known as the *operative clause.* > *The right of the people to keep and bear Arms shall not be > infringed.* The phrase "the right of the people" is used three times in the Bill of Rights, and each time it denotes a right reserved to individual citizens by virtue of their citizenship, not because of membership in a particular group (other than the group of citizens). > Because the text of the Amendment should be read in the manner that > gives greatest effect to the plain meaning it would have had at the > time it was written, the operative clause should be read to "guarantee > an individual right to possess and carry weapons in case of > confrontation."[\[1\]](#MyHLX){#cite-MyHLX .citation_link} **TL;DR:** So to answer your question specifically, we don't "ignore" the prefatory clause. However, the prefatory clause does not limit the operative clause. Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-MyHLX){.citation_index} [[District of Columbia v. Heller](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bf785c&url=https://www.oyez.org/cases/2007/07-290){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="oyez.org"}]{.qlink_container}
