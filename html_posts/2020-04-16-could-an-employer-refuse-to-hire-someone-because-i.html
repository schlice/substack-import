**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Could an employer refuse to hire someone because it believes that it would be unsafe for that individual to work with the machinery required to perform the essential functions of the job because of a disability?** *(US-specific answer)* Basically, yes, the employer can refuse to hire someone under those conditions. The employer would have to show that the person cannot perform the essential functions of the job safely, even with reasonable accommodations. Being able to perform the job safely is part of the essential functions of a job: not just doing it, but doing it safely.
