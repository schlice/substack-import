**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You will have to show them that there is benefit by showing that the chargers will eventually *generate revenue*. One way to do this is to open the chargers up to the public. While residents can charge for free, everyone else pays a fee. Over time, the chargers will eventually start to make money. But that's the problem. The HOA or the landlord has to have the capital up front to install the chargers, and depending on the type of charger, that can range anywhere from \$30K to \$200K. If the HOA or the landlord doesn't have that kind of disposable capital lying around, it's a hard sell to try to get them to impose a special assessment on homeowners or take out a business loan. TL;DR: Usually, the problem isn't that they don't see the value. It's that they don't have the capital, and installing EV chargers requires up-front capital investment.
