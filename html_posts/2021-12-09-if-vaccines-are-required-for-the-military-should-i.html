--- title: "If vaccines are required for the military, should individual soldiers and officers have the freedom to leave the service before their time if they wish to remain unvaccinated?" date: "2021-12-09" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, military members should not be allowed to break their service commitments if they don't want the vaccine. Members were fully aware of the types of exemptions for vaccines which were available at the time they enlisted, and none of those exemptions are changing now. Members are free to exercise their rights to use any of those exemptions if they so choose. Vaccines did not become mandatory until they were fully approved by the FDA, so there can no longer be an objection about an "unapproved" vaccine. Members who are not under any Active Duty Service Commitment (ADSC) are free to separate at any time. ADSCs are incurred upon certain events, such as the military paying for your college, or when you sign an enlistment contract. In other words, there is no reason that they should get an extra opportunity to breach their contract or lessen their ADSC due to this vaccine. \ Original question: If vaccines are required for the military, should individual soldiers and officers have the freedom to leave the service before their time if they wish to remain unvaccinated?
