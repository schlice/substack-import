**Content:** [*Original question: What is the evidentiary procedure to prove subjective intent?*\ \ There is no evidentiary procedure to prove subjective intent. There is only evidentiary procedure to prove objective intent.\ \ In contract law, subjective intent will only prevail over objective intent if that personal meaning was known to the other party. Hidden beliefs or reservations are usually not taken into account when the other party knew what the intent was.\ \ Usually, the objective intent (such as a signed contract) will prevail.\ \ *Updated question: What is the evidentiary procedure to prove subjective intent in Criminal Law?*\ \ The \"reasonable person\" standard is what is normally used in most court systems in the world to determine intent.\ \ See [[Intention (criminal law)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bc482d&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intention_%2528criminal_law%2529){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} for more information.]{.rendered_qtext}
