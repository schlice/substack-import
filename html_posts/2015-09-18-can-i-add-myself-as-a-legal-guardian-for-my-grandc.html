--- title: '"Can I add myself as a legal guardian for my grandchild?"' date: '2015-09-18' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [You stated that you want guardianship to be able \"to sign official papers and make some decisions.\"\ \ If that\'s the case, a power of attorney should be all you need. You can then perform whatever parental duties are authorized in the power of attorney documentation. You could sign as a guardian at the doctor\'s office, or sign the child into and out of day care or school. The power of attorney document is a powerful tool, and should provide you with everything you need.\ \ I think becoming a guardian ad litem is a very big step with very big consequences that you probably don\'t need to undertake.\ \ By the way, talking to a family practice attorney would have likely yielded the same information. Many of them here where I live will do a free initial consultation.\ \ And if you do want to move forward, I highly recommend you work through your family attorney.]{.rendered_qtext}
