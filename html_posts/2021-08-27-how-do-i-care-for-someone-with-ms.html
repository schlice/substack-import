Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." Every person with MS is different, and they all have different needs. They all have different "levels" of need as well. Some will need more hands-on assistance, while others will simply need emotional support and the love and care of a friend or family member. If instead, you are finding yourself overwhelmed and not knowing where to turn for help with caring for someone with MS who requires special care, there is help. Start with [[your local chapter of the National Multiple Sclerosis Society](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2ebc99&url=https://www.nationalmssociety.org/Chapters){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} (in the US), or the [[MS Society of the UK](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7adc24&url=https://www.mssociety.org.uk/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="mssociety.org.uk"}]{.qlink_container} (in the UK), or your country's MS Society. Reach out to them, and they can assist you with finding resources who can help you care for someone with MS. \ Original question: How do I care for someone with MS?
