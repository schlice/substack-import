**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} [[Elizabeth Costa Hobbins](https://www.quora.com/profile/Elizabeth-Costa-Hobbins)]{.qlink_container} is spot on. Untreated, RRMS transitions to SPMS within 10 years. We know that using the MS therapy drugs extends that time. We don't know exactly how much the drugs extend that time. Also considering that MS is usually diagnosed between ages 20 and 40, that would mean this woman is between 50 and 70 right now, which also means that she was able to hold off the SPMS for the majority of her childbearing years, meaning that quality of life during that time was pretty good. With new medicines being approved all the time, I think she's in great shape for the future.
