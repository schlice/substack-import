**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If someone is accused of rape, and he has a video where it shows that after sex, there were cuddling and even kissing goodbye which like nothing had happened, does that disprove the accusations in China and the USA?** Nothing *disproves* an allegation of rape short of the accuser saying that it really didn't happen. But the behavior you noted, which was purported to take place in the immediate aftermath of the alleged rape, would definitely throw into question the *credibility* of the accusation. In other words, those behaviors are not the behaviors of someone who did not *consent* to the contact taking place. And they are certainly not the behaviors normally undertaken when one has just been raped. Kissing and cuddling your rapist is not, on average, an accurate representation of normal after-rape conduct. Such video would certainly be used to raise what we in the United States would call *reasonable doubt* as to whether or not the accusations are credible.
