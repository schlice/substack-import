**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What do I do when my 4-year-old threatens to call the cops because I punished him with a timeout?** First of all, time outs are not punishment, and if you're using them as such, you are probably misusing them. But that's a discussion for another question. Secondly, you should never use the police as a crutch for parenting, and you should not allow your child to think that police are the answer to a parenting style with which they disagree. Where did he get the idea to call the police when he didn't like your parenting style? Have you ever said to the child, "You'd better listen to me or that police man over there is going to take you to jail." I certainly hope not. Police are not surrogate parents, and they are not there to be your parental enforcers. This only teaches children that police are to be feared. What if the child is lost and you call the police? If he fears the police, he'll hide from them when they try to find him, because he would feel as if he would get "in trouble" for being lost. Probably not the wisest thing to teach him. Finally, at some time in the future, you should take your child on a field trip to the police station. He needs a lesson on what police do.
