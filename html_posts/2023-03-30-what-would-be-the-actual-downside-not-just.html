**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Literally nothing.* Manufacturers will build a new style of semi automatic rifle that doesn't have the prohibited features of the AR pattern rifle (since that's all you'd be banning is prohibited cosmetic design, not functionality). Since the AR platform is so versatile, there are bzillions of attachments and add-ons to extend the functionality of this versatile platform, so other manufacturers will create adapter kits which will allow you to transition your previous accoutrements to the new rifle platform. And violent crime still won't care, because crime isn't caused by a rifle. Mass shootings will not decrease. Shooters will pivot to a new platform. Don't forget, the Nashville shooter had a Kel-Tec SUB2000 pistol along with a S&W M&P SHIELD 9mm pistol and a rifle manufactured by Lead Star called the Grunt, and it shoots .300 Blackout. It is an AR-pattern rifle, but again, that's just because it is the most popular and most versatile medium-range rifle pattern out there. So one out of three guns would be banned (and replaced by something else anyway) and nothing would change. This incessant focus on the AR pattern rifle because it is the most popular is silly. > Actual \"assault weapons\" with full auto features are already banned. No they're not. You need extra background checks, a bunch of extra steps, and *a whole helluva lot more money* to get them, but they're not banned. You can't possess them in a handful of states, but everywhere else you can have one if you can pass the background checks, jump through the government hoops, and cough up the artificially inflated prices for them.
