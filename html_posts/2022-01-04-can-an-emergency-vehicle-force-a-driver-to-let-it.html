**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes they can. I've seen it happen, as I was in the emergency vehicle doing the "forcing." Sometime in 1989, I was sitting in the passenger seat of a Chrysler K car, fully equipped with a "Kojack light" when we approached a red light, one car back from the intersection. A New York police detective was driving. We observed what looked like a traffic accident (with injuries) that had just taken place on the far corner of the intersection. The detective flipped a switch, and the Kojack light at my feet filled the footwell with red, rotating light. "Stick that thing on the roof, would you?" he said. I grabbed the light off of the floorboard and stuck it out onto the roof of the car. We then had to get to the accident, but there was a car in front of us at the red light. The detective pushed a momentary switch on the knee plate and the siren came on for each push. It made that sort of "woop woop" sound you hear sometimes from police vehicles. The implication for the driver ahead was "Please get out of my way so I can get to the scene of the accident in front of you." The car in front of us proceeded through the red light, past the accident, and drove off.
