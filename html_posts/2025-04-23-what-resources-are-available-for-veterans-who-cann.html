Being a veteran myself (and a 100% disabled veteran at that), I am intimately interested in this topic, so in just this one instance, I will make an exception. Several programs can help veterans afford homes, primarily through loans and grants. The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) offers various loan programs with favorable terms, including no down payment requirements and potentially lower interest rates. In fact, I have used my VA loan benefit each time I have purchased a home, and it is a great benefit. In most cases, VA loans get more favorable rates, and while you technically *can* buy a house with 0% down, I've only done that once, for my very first home purchase. Grants and down payment assistance from organizations like Soldier Next Door can also help with homeownership. Additionally, programs like the HUD-VASH program provide rental assistance and supportive services for homeless veterans.
