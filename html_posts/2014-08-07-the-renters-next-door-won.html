**Content:** [1. If you live in a neighborhood that is covenant-protected, go to your HOA. They are the best to handle this situation, since most covenant-controlled communities have regulations requiring homeowners to pick up after their dogs.\ \ 2. If you do not live in a community with a covenant, your first stop should be with the homeowner. They may be blissfully unaware of what their renters are doing, and they don\'t want any trouble. The owner of the property is on file with the city, so go to the city clerk\'s office to get that information and then write to the homeowner.\ \ 3. If that route fails, there are some legal remedies. Under common law and in most cities, this would be considered a public nuisance and is probably a low-level misdemeanor. In most municipalities, the law requires you to pick up after your dog, both on and off your property, and dispose of the waste properly. These laws are both for health and safety, as well as for public nuisance standards.\ \ 4. Almost every community I know of has Humane Society branches you can contact for assistance. They can help you get in touch with the right agencies to contact should it come to that.]{.rendered_qtext}
