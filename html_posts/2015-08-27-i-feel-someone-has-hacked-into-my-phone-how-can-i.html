**Content:** [It really depends on the phone.\ \ If it\'s an Apple, LG, Motorola or a Samsung phone in the US, it\'s exceedingly unlikely that your phone has been hacked. Unless you have jailbroken/rooted your phone, there isn\'t a way to access the multi-user \"shell\" that allows commands to be executed.\ \ If you are using a Google Nexus phone, the phone uses a version of Android that is much closer to \"stock\" Android as released by Google. There may be more unpatched security holes in stock Android; however, your phone gets updates more frequently. That said, you still only have serious issues if you have a rooted phone.\ \ Malware (a malicious app on your phone) may allow this, but again it\'s exceedingly unlikely that you have this on your phone, especially if you are not jailbroken/rooted.\ \ If your phone is jailbroken/rooted, the easiest way to remove hacker access is to unroot it/unjailbreak it.]{.rendered_qtext}
