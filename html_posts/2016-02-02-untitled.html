The only item I would add: *So an adaptation of the UDHR one would suppose?* The Universal Declaration of Human Rights is a document developed and owned by the United Nations. While it is binding on member nations, it does not impose any legal requirements for member nations to restructure their laws to match it. It is up to member nations to structure their laws and governmental practices to line up in accordance with its precepts. It is basically a \"guide\" for which member nations should strive. US law does not address every item in the UDHR because of the structure of the Constitution and the nature of a free society, but in practice, we meet most of them.
