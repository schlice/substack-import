**Content:** [Put simply, I have a *right*. I don\'t have a *privilege* which can be revoked (like a driver\'s license). I have a *right* to own a gun. Regardless of what you may think of the gun, I have a right to own one. I have an *individual* right to \"keep and bear\" one. A function of living in the United States as a citizen affords me that right.\ \ To address the horror you feel towards guns, that\'s simply because the gun is a purpose-built tool to kill. It is designed to kill. It does so effectively and efficiently.\ \ That fact alone does not mean that the gun has no place in a household or in society. That fact alone simply means that it is the best tool available for killing. Because it is. It minimizes collateral damage better than any other tool available; it is very precise. It kills whatever I\'m aiming at. As long as I operate it properly, it is one of the safest (for the operator) methods for killing we have. It\'s an extremely simple machine, as far as machines go. More tongue-in-cheek, it\'s the world\'s first \"point-and-click interface.\"\ \ But does the fact that it is a tool to kill make it unsuitable for a household?\ \ The question must follow: is killing ever needed? Or lawful?\ \ The answer is yes, killing is needed in certain scenarios, and even lawful. Killing is needed, under common law, for the purpose of self-defense, and the protection from tyranny. If you are being threatened with deadly force, you can meet that threat with deadly force as well. The gun is sometimes known as \"the great equalizer\" because it removes size and strength differences between people. A famous advertising campaign from Colt said, \"God made man; Samuel Colt made them equal.\" A small person can easily take down a very large man by using a gun; size and gender no longer factor in issues of self-defense.\ \ And while it is far less likely today, America was founded on the core belief that the people hold the ultimate power in the government. Government is \"of the people, by the people, and for the people.\" The people are the ultimate authority in the government. The people must also retain the right to overthrow the government should that ever become necessary. Because it\'s hard to do today, should we then say, \"Oh well, the government has grown too big and powerful to overthrow, therefore we should remove all guns from society\"? The logic does not follow, as this is a logically false conclusion to draw from the relevant facts.\ \ Finally, regulation. With every right comes an associated responsibility, and those responsibilities can, and should, and are, codified in law. We as a society have stated that felons should not have access to guns. We have also stated that people should not have access to automatic weapons. We have also stated that guns cannot be brought into sensitive areas, such as schools (although that\'s up for debate) and government buildings such as courthouses. Any time you buy a gun from a licensed gun dealer, you have to have a criminal background check performed. These laws all exist today. What further regulation would you propose that would substantially reduce crime without being increasingly onerous on the legal gun owner?]{.rendered_qtext}
