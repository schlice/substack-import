Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **Which weapons are not covered by the second amendment?** The Second Amendment does not specify weapon types. Laws would do that, not the Constitution. The Second Amendment specifies what types of laws government ***cannot*** write, namely any law that might infringe on a citizen's right to keep and bear arms. So to answer your question, no weapons are covered, nor are any weapons not covered, by the Second Amendment. Only government is affected by the Second Amendment, being specifically prevented from passing laws which might restrict a citizen from keeping or bearing arms. > **Am I guaranteed the right to own and use an RPG?** The answer is, as with all legal questions, it depends. Since the Second Amendment does not address any specific weapons platform, there is nothing in the Constitution which guarantees you the right to own any particular weapon. State law does that. And there are some pretty stringent state laws when it comes to certain weapons types (namely, anything covered in the National Firearms Act, colloquially called "NFA weapons," under which your RPG would qualify). Most states regulate, but not prohibit, NFA weapons. Many states require special permits to own them. Some states require permits from the chief of police or the sheriff where you live, and not all of those are "shall issue" permits. And in case you were wondering, using an RPG against a person is just as illegal as using a gun against a person. Murder is still illegal everywhere in the United States.
