**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How severe can HOA punishments become, considering you OWN the property?** The answer is "it depends." Don't forget, the HOA has an ownership interest in the entire neighborhood, even though you are the deeded owner on your house. Your Governing Documents determine just how bad it can get. In some cases, if there is something on the outside of your home that needs to be fixed, the HOA will hire contractors to fix it and then bill you for the work. In others, they will simply demand you fix it and will continually fine you until you do. In most HOAs, the remedy for the HOA is fines. However, if you don't pay your fines (or any work they may have done and billed to you), they can put a lien on your house. If you continue to not pay your fines, the HOA can foreclose on your lien to get their money (which means you no longer own your house).
