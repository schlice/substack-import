**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are Republicans glad they worked so hard to keep President Trump in office so that he could spectacularly bungle the government response to Coronavirus?** There are simply so many incorrect assumptions in this question that it's hard to keep up: 1. Republicans didn't have to lift a finger to keep Trump in office. Democrats are so spectacularly incompetent that they couldn't even impeach and remove from office when they had all the evidence right there in front of them. Democrats in the House can't find their own ass with two hands and a map. 2. Trump's quick action in closing the border to inbound persons from China was the single biggest factor in the slower than average rate of infection in the United States, so "spectacularly bungle the government response" is *just slightly* inaccurate. To be fair, he did make some mistakes, and he did misspeak (on more than one occasion) but overall the Federal response has been pretty good. He can do more, and should do more (and every expert has unanimously said so), but what he has done to date really can't be accurately characterized as a "spectacular bungle."
