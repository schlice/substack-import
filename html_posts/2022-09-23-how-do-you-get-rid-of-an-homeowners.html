**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you truly want to dissolve your HOA in Florida, you will need to consult with a real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law. Dissolving an HOA is possible, but it is not free, and it is not easy. For example, the HOA usually owns some property, even if it is just small areas of grass where the monolith (the sign at the entrance) lives. That property will need to be sold or gifted to someone. Utilities for water and electricity in the common areas will need to be terminated. Insurance policies for the common areas and for the Directors will need to be cancelled (and any associated fees paid). And that's just the stuff pertaining to the HOA itself. In order to dissolve a mandatory HOA, you will need signatures from 100% of the homeowners in the affected area. If there is just one holdout who likes what the HOA does for him, you can't dissolve it. The homeowners then need to get new deeds filed (which don't have an attached HOA) with their lienholders, assuming they mortgaged their home purchase. Ergo, more lawyers (read: not cheap) And even if you do dissolve the HOA, there is usually still a larger property owner's association (POA) which your HOA belonged to, and you'll still belong to that, and you can't dissolve that one. Normally you'd pay those dues once a year (or your HOA would pay that for you), but it still exists. \ Original question: How do you get rid of an Homeowners\' Association (HOA) in Florida?
