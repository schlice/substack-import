**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can someone hack my phone through my Gmail if it is opened on my phone?** **YES!!! If your Gmail account is open and accessible on your phone (and someone has access to it on your phone), they can reset ANYTHING!!!** Your Gmail account (if it is your primary email) is your **skeleton key** to every other account you own, and not just your phone. It's your bank accounts, your work accounts, your school accounts, your doctor's office account, *literally everything*. How? If I have access to your Gmail account, *I can reset any of your passwords.* PROTECT YOUR GMAIL ACCOUNT! If you don't have *two-factor authentication* turned on, do it. In fact, stop reading this answer and go enable it now. If you don't know how, seriously go look it up and enable it. Then if your Gmail is logged in to your phone, add a passcode to your phone. Set your phone to auto-lock after a certain time. Never leave your phone unattended. That's the quickest way to ruin your life in a big hurry. Let's assume you're thinking, "Can they hack my phone *just because Gmail is logged in on my phone?*" If that's the question you're asking, then no, just because Gmail is logged in on your phone doesn't mean your phone can be hacked. Someone has to be able to access your Gmail before your phone can be hacked. But if you have an Android phone, there is a lot you can do through your Google account to mess with your phone. So maybe not "hack," but how about "remote wipe?" That's no fun either.
