**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are school shootings responsible for CounterStrike?** School shootings: a (likely) unstable former student decides to get revenge on all of the people whom he feels "wronged" him in some way and takes a gun into a school and starts shooting anyone he sees, with absolutely no armed resistance whatsoever. It's a free-fire zone. [[*Counter-Strike: Global Offensive*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f45331&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} (affectionately known as CS:GO): An online first-person shooter multiplayer video game which pits a team of "terrorists" and "counter-terrorists" against each other in fictional cityscapes full of boxes, cars, buildings, windows, ramps and nary an innocent bystander anywhere to be seen (if you see a person, they're either on your team, or they're not). Both teams work to eliminate each other while working on other objectives (planting or defusing bombs, and defending or rescuing hostages). You communicate with your team and direct tactical movement, call out enemy positions when you see them, and are generally equally matched with your opponents in terms of available firepower (note to hardcore CS:GO players: I'm not talking about purchase ability and funds available based on round wins/losses; I'm talking about the difference between two \[generally\] balanced, armed teams vs one shooter and all unarmed victims). If you can make the stretch from school shootings to CS:GO, you've got quite an imagination.
