--- title: '"What are the pros and cons of unlocking my Galaxy S3?"' date: '2016-10-05' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Depends on what you mean by "unlocking." If you're talking about a "carrier lock" so you can use it on another network, there are no cons at all. If you're talking about "rooting" your device, there are the standard warnings about rooting your device. You make your device more unstable, you risk opening up security holes, and you also won't be able to upgrade your firmware automatically (only non-rooted phones get OTA upgrades). What you get in return is big though. You unlock the full capabilities of the hardware. You can do things in the hardware you never could do before. You have full control over the CPU and how it works. You have full control over all of your applications; when they run, when they die, etc. You can tweak your radio settings to get better signal strength. You can add utilities like f.lux and other hacks and tweaks to your phone to make your phone look and work the way you want it. You can remove system packages and carrier bloatware. You can schedule reboots, and tools like Tasker and Titanium Backup become infinitely more usable. Note that some of this can be done with apps, but that's a lot of disparate software packages to maintain for little individual functions.
