**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Donald Trump says it's his call to reopen the country. Does the constitution say otherwise?** Well yes, but not in the way you might think. The Constitution tells the various branches of government what they are allowed to do. This is a very limited set of things. The Constitution isn't very long. For hundreds of years, government has tried to grow outside of that boundary, but it finds itself constrained by the Constitution. So it's not that the Constitution "says otherwise." It's more that the Constitution doesn't say so. And if the Constitution doesn't grant the authority to any branch of Federal government, the authority belongs to the states. Trump is using that specific language not because he thinks he actually is king of the US, but because he is trying to put a very fine point on the fact that he wants to be the one to influence the states when they should start allowing businesses to re-open. He and his science team have set themselves in the position to provide the bulk of the technical understanding of the virus and its current impact. He also wants to be the one to say that it's time to start letting people get back to work, but he knows that the authority to allow businesses to re-open and people to go back to work lies in the states. But he also knows that **the people carry great sway in influencing their state governments**. And if the people start to hear that Trump says we should go back to work but state governors are not allowing businesses to re-open, people will riot. So ultimately, while Trump only holds advisory authority over the states, not actual Constitutional directional authority, he holds great sway over the minds of the people, and he will wield that to force the states to comply lest they face backlash, not only now, but in November. And you can rest assured that any challenger to an incumbent in November will use any failure to re-open their economy as a hammer come election time.
