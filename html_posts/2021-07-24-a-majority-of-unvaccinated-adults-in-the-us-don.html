**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The way we reach herd immunity when people will not vaccinate: The people who refuse to vaccinate will contract COVID-19, and they will either get better (and thus have natural immunity), or they will die and you won't need to worry about them infecting everyone around them. But everyone gets to choose their own path in life. Take the vaccine if you want to avoid COVID. Don't take the vaccine if you think you can survive being infected. But **ultimately, what you do is your choice.** It absolutely fascinates me that this pandemic has literally caused everyone to forget how to live in the United States of America. - Is it *really* medically necessary for everyone, regardless of age and prior infection status, to get the vaccine? - Shouldn't Americans have the freedom to decide if they want to get a shot that remains unapproved by the FDA, has no long term studies to indicate any possible health effects, and is known to cause serious complications in some? The public health apparatus in this country has failed us. It politicized the virus. It politicized the vaccine. It has destroyed trust in public health. Why didn't we just present the facts, present the hard evidence about for whom the vaccine would be beneficial, who might suffer the most by being infected, and then treat people like adults and allow them to make their own decisions? Instead, we have this push toward mandates and coercion which will further erode trust in public health. With this mad push toward forced vaccinations, you would think that there is some sort of grave risk of mortality from the disease (you know, like the Black Plague that literally killed half of Europe or something) which would necessitate everyone, young and old, getting vaccinated. But is that what the medical evidence shows? "There is a thousand-fold difference in the risk of mortality from the disease from the oldest to the youngest," says Dr. Jay Bhattacharya, professor of medicine at Stanford University. "Give people the information, the solid, good scientific information, let them make their own choice. And that is how public health gains trust. That's how public health becomes effective. **And that's how the pandemic actually ends**, not with coercion, but with good medicine conveyed by trustworthy people treating \[the public\] like adults as opposed to serfs." *But what about the people who have already been infected with COVID and survived? Don't they need the vaccine?* \"Natural immunity is quite effective at preventing you from reinfection,\" Bhattacharya said. \"100 million people \[in the U.S. have been\] infected, at least. And yet we have very few \[re\]infections. It protects you against the variants as well \... I read the scientific evidence around natural immunity \... If you had COVID and recovered from it, then you\'re protected against that just as much as you would be if you had the vaccine.\" Bhattacharya went on to express his puzzlement at why this is not being acknowledged by the leading public health officials. \"\[It\'s\] a very odd denial of natural immunity \... I don\'t understand it,\" he said. \"It\'s as if the only valid immunity is vaccine induced immunity \... If you\'ve had the virus and recovered, you have natural immunity. There are an enormous number of studies in prominent journals that have established this. I don\'t really understand why the public health community has not incorporated \[this\] into their thinking in a deep way.\" Bhattacharya also underscored another important aspect of the debate that doesn\'t seem to be getting any attention: why isn\'t the vaccine being distributed to those most in need? \"There are tens of millions of older people worldwide who have not had the vaccine,\" he noted. \"Why are we discussing vaccinating a population of people, younger people who face very low risk if they were to get infected, while still tens of millions of people worldwide have not had the protection of the vaccine? Just because they don\'t live in a rich country doesn\'t mean they don\'t deserve to have this kind of medical care available to them.\"[\[1\]](#SAqUr){#cite-SAqUr .citation_link} \ Original question: *A majority of unvaccinated adults in the U.S. don\'t plan on getting vaccinated.
