**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If someone purposefully tries to kill themselves by running into traffic, is the driver who hits them still charged with a crime?** Whether or not a crime is charged is up to the District Attorney in the city in which the incident occurs. If the evidence is clear that the "victim" was actually attempting to commit suicide (i.e. suicide note found online, other evidence of suicidal intent, etc), then it's likely that the DA wouldn't even file charges against the driver, unless the driver was also acting recklessly. Speeding to such a degree that you couldn't have stopped anyway could be charged as simple reckless driving. However, as to the death, the DA probably wouldn't charge the driver in relation to the death if clear evidence existed that this was a "suicide by car."
