**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, the found votes are not necessarily proof that fraud has taken place. They are, however, proof that mail-in voting is a medium rife for abuse, and a vivid demonstration that mail-in voting is not nearly as "safe" or "secure" as everyone has been saying it is. Do we have proof? Not really. We have never had widespread mail-in voting. Some states have all mail-in balloting, but if they believe their system is good, they're not going to be looking very closely for problems. Additionally, people claim that mail-in balloting is safe because it's always been safe, and that's not entirely accurate. We have had *absentee balloting* in the past, not widespread mail-in voting. And your mail-in voting system is only as secure as your requirements on voters. This is why we should go back to the original system, and limit absentee ballots to those who will physically be outside of the state on Election Day, or who are physically incapable of going to a polling place on Election Day. **TL;DR:** **mail-in voting makes your election less secure and more susceptible to malicious and accidental tampering.** \ Original question: **Are the \"found\" votes in MI, WI, and PA proof of the fraud involved in mail-in voting?**
