**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is an audio recording of a person slandering you admissible in a court of law if you decide to sue them?** The answer is "it depends." It depends on how you got the recording, and in which state the recording was made. In all states, at least one party to the conversation needs to have known that the conversation was being recorded. In some states, both parties to the conversation need to have known that the conversation was being recorded (these are called "two-party consent states"). So depending on how the recording was made and whether or not your state is a two party consent state will determine whether or not the recording can be introduced as evidence in a civil proceeding.
