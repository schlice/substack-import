**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Despite your misgivings about what is "wrong" or not, the bottom line is that we are a nation of laws. We are literally the only country in the world whose government is entirely founded on a piece of paper. No juntas, no oligarchs (despite the Leftists' claims), no religious leaders, no kings. Just a piece of paper and elections. As a nation, we live and die by those laws. If you don't like the laws, as Paul mentioned, you elect leaders who will change them. You lobby your elected leaders to get them to change them. But our nation of laws also has a law that says that the laws are applied to all people equally (see the 14th Amendment). So whether you think it's "fair" or not, if we have a law, it applies equally to everyone. It's not about being "fair," it's about being "just." Those are two totally separate concepts. Part of the problem you are citing is not necessarily a problem with the legal system, it's a problem with Congress. Congress has abrogated much of its responsibility under the Constitution by creating *bureaucracy*. So while we may not be a monarchy or an oligarchy, we are creating *bureaucratic despotism* in the United States. The EPA, for example, is an organization filled with civil servants. Unelected, unaccountable (to us) civil servants who create regulation which carries the force of law. We can be punished for violating EPA regulations. That means those regulations are just like laws passed by our Congresspeople. This is in violation of the Constitution, which clearly states in Article I that all laws are to be created by Congress, not by bureaucracy. But Congress has allowed this to happen. Not only did they allow it to happen, but they created the bureaucracy in the first place. If you really want to learn more about this, you should take Hillsdale College's free online course, [[**The Progressive Rejection of the Founding and the Rise of Bureaucratic Despotism**](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=02cabd&url=https://online.hillsdale.edu/page.aspx?pid%3D1185){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="hillsdale.edu"}]{.qlink_container}**.**
