--- title: '"Can an HOA (Homeowners Association) get sued or taken to court by one of their members without them knowing about it?"' date: '2023-01-26' tags: - policy-analysis - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Assuming that all goes according to plan, the HOA will be served with notice of lawsuit. So yes, they'll know. It is possible for the HOA to be sued and they not know about it. Let's assume that the service was done to the office of the HOA's Managing Agent, but the secretary at the front door accepted the service. The secretary failed to deliver the summons to the appropriate Managing Agent, who would notify the HOA's attorney. In other words, the notice of lawsuit got lost in the shuffle. If service happened correctly to a valid recipient and the correct people never found out about it, the court date would come and the HOA could have a default judgement issued against it. If the service **did not** happen correctly and the HOA doesn't show up, no judgement can be entered against them because they were not properly served. The case would probably be continued to another date, and the homeowner would have to try again to properly serve the HOA. Even if the HOA was improperly served, or not served at all, they can still show up and a trial can commence, even if service was incorrect.
