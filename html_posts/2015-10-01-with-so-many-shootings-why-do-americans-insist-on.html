**Content:** [Um, because it\'s not.\ \ We tried to ban things before in the United States. Surprisingly, people still got those things. For example, drugs are illegal in the United States. No one can possess them. Yet somehow, millions are addicted to drugs and we incarcerate over a million people a year for drugs and drug-related offenses.\ \ **Those drugs were 100% illegal. And people still managed to get them.**\ \ We also tried to ban alcohol in the United States in a period of time we call \"Prohibition.\" Yet people still had alcohol. Illegal bars called \"speakeasies\" opened up underground. Moonshiners distilled and trafficked their own grain alcohol (incidentally, the cars they souped up to outrun law enforcement would sometimes participate in races, leading to the creation of NASCAR).\ \ **Alcohol was 100% illegal. And people still managed to get it.**\ \ So what\'s the point here?\ \ ***Making something illegal will not stop people from getting it.***\ \ If you make guns illegal, you will simultaneously increase the black market for it and enable all crime, both organized and individual. It happened every time we tried to ban something. The black market for it skyrocketed, and all types of crime was increased.\ \ Also, the gun is a tool. It is the most efficient tool to use with which to kill individuals. Does that mean the gun should be banned? If there is never an authorized, valid reason to kill, then yes, guns should not be allowed, because no one has a valid reason to kill. However, the entire history of the United States, going back to the Common Law traditions on which our system of government was founded, includes \"universal\" rights which are endowed on all people. One of those rights is the right to self-defense, and another is defense against tyranny. I\'d not like to debate whether or not either of these rights exists or whether or not you believe they are valid. The simple fact is that our Constitution was built with them in mind, It\'s why we have a Second Amendment. To wit:\ \ ***The only way to remove guns from the United States is to repeal the Second Amendment.***\ \ There is no popular support for such a measure. Playing on emotion and trying to use tragic shootings as an impetus for Second Amendment repeal might work in Australia, but there is simply no support for such a measure here in the United States.\ \ Finally, statistics show that there is no practical benefit to such laws, as reviewing statistics from Australia and the UK, two locations often bandied about in the hunt for ways to impose gun control, reveals that their respective gun bans did not affect the trends in those nations of either homicides, or suicides, with or without guns. Zero effect. The overall trend in homicides was unaffected by the gun ban, and the overall trend in suicides was similarly unaffected by the gun ban.]{.rendered_qtext}
