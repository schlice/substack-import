**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First, let's get something out of the way right now. - The states certified their elections. - The Congress certified the results of the state elections. - The president was sworn in lawfully, and power was transferred. Therefore, **the president was Constitutionally elected.** Nothing about the process by which he assumed power was unconstitutional. THAT SAID, if these audits find that there was significant fraud in a state election, so significant that *it would have changed the outcome of that state's election,* we're going to take a very strong look at what failed in that state to allow an election to be corrupted in such a way. And the big question to the states from the Congressional subcommittees which do the investigations will be: *How could you possibly have certified an election with so much fraud taking place? What are you going to do to fix it?* Remember the 2000 election? Bush v. Gore? Two words: **hanging chads**. Florida got a *huge* black eye over that one, and they fixed their voting laws. 2020 was a very smooth election in Florida because of those voting law changes. States which changed their voting laws through emergency orders (due to COVID) instead of lawfully through legislative action are going to get a very stern look and a mandate to fix their voting laws before the next presidential election. Except that some states, in trying to do just that, are being disingenuously attacked for "limiting" voting rights, when the things that they are "limiting" were only put in place as emergency executive orders. Anyway, the only way that Biden leaves office is if he actually knew or participated in any of the fraud which took place in any of the states. If this evidence ever comes to light, he can be impeached and removed from office. If not, and it still comes to light that there was such significant state fraud that it could have changed the course of the election, then the Biden presidency will be indelibly marred by the stigma of being a president who was very likely not the people's true choice. This has been true of other presidents in history, and it would be true again. Any mandates he thought he had would be questioned even more strongly than they are now, and the Biden legacy will be reduced to a footnote in the pages of history. \ Original question: Is there a Constitutional, legal recourse to remove Joe Biden from the presidency IF it is proven through auditing processes, that he is not the legitimate winner of the 2020 election, since an illegitimate president is not Constitutional?
