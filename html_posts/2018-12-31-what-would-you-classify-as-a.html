**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What would you classify as a "modern" disease?** Type 2 Diabetes, which is most commonly a lifestyle disease caused specifically by our modern diets affecting certain at-risk people. We used to call it "adult-onset diabetes" since it generally only affected adults with poor nutritional habits, usually linked to excess sugar intake, sedentary lifestyle, and obesity. However, since "adult-onset diabetes" began affecting adolescents and teens, they really couldn't call it "adult-onset diabetes" any longer, so they changed it to "Type 1" and "Type 2" diabetes. Type 1 diabetes is diagnosed in children from a very young age through the 30s and is caused by an autoimmune response where the immune system destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. Type 2 diabetes is more closely tied to age, obesity, genetic predisposition, and a sedentary lifestyle, but the cause is not yet fully understood. While Type 2 diabetics do produce insulin, their bodies do not use it well. The only way to treat Type 1 diabetes is by supplementing insulin, since their pancreases do not produce it at all. Type 2 diabetes can usually be managed using some combination of diet, exercise, and medication. However, it is a progressive disease, and within five to 10 years, the Type 2 patient will likely also have to start supplementing insulin.
