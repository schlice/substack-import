**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(US answer only)* First of all, this is a poor argument. Ectopic pregnancies are not viable. The egg needs to implant in the uterus to be viable, not in the Fallopian tube. Additionally, a ruptured Fallopian tube can cause fatal internal hemorrhaging. Thus, the life of the mother is at risk. The odds of surviving an ectopic pregnancy (roughly 60 million to one) are outweighed by the risk to the mother for continuing the pregnancy (massive internal hemorrhaging due to ruptured Fallopian tubes). Thus, to save the mother's life, the pregnancy would be terminated. Every state which has a post-*Roe* abortion ban (either total or partial) has an exception to save the life of the mother. Medical procedures to save the life of the mother are never illegal, and that includes aborting a pregnancy to save the mother's life. They weren't even illegal *before Roe*. And they're still not illegal. \ Original question: Apart from abortion, which other way is the problem of ectopic pregnancy supposed to be solved?
