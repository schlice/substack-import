--- title: '"Can jurors be fined for not showing up to court or ignoring a summons for jury duty?"' date: '2022-11-18' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, most states will issue fines for jurors skipping out on jury duty. The possibility exists that you can also be cited for contempt of court under state law, or if you're called to a Federal jury, under 28 U.S.C. ยง 1966(g). Contempt of court charges for failing to show up in court could land you in jail for up to three days for a federal case. State laws differ, but you can face jail time. In most cases, jail is reserved for habitual jury summons ignorers. Here in Florida, it's estimated that around 3/4 of the people served with jury summonses will ignore them. Most of those people actually have legitimate exemptions from jury service and are excused. In Broward County, over a decade's time, only 33 people faced contempt charges for skipping jury duty, and only four were jailed. So it's highly unlikely that you'd go to jail. It's far more likely that you'll be fined. In Florida, you'll be fined \$100 for failing to show for jury duty.
