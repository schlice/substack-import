**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is yes you can. However, for an existing neighborhood, how you go about starting an HOA is crucial to understand what you'll end up with when you're done. The reason: there is a big difference between *voluntary* and *mandatory* HOAs. Mandatory HOAs are attached to the property. Generally, they exist before the property is sold for the first time, as they are established by the developer. You cannot choose whether or not you will belong to the HOA, because it is part of the property. If you buy the property, you're in the HOA. Voluntary HOAs are akin to club memberships or affinity groups. You can join and leave as you choose. You may receive benefits from such membership, such as clubhouse or pool access. If you're trying to create a mandatory HOA, you will probably need permission from 100% of the homeowners in the neighborhood. It can be done, you just need more permission. You will need assistance from a real estate attorney to file the paperwork and collect the legal signatures to modify the deeds of all of the homeowners to indicate the HOA. You can create a voluntary HOA with a much smaller percentage of homeowners, and it's much easier to implement. If you really are serious about going forward with establishing an HOA in your area, talk to a real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law.
