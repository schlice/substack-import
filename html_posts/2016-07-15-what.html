**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Any question like this gets answered primarily with "it depends." It depends on the load the person was shot with, the composition of that load, the power behind that load, distance away from the shooter the victim was, and where the shots landed. Yes, people shot multiple times can indeed survive. The chances are all dependent: - All rounds will cause more damage at closer range, as they still retain much of their forward velocity. Rounds will slow down as they fly (somewhat), so will necessarily be less able to inflict serious damage at greater distances. - Larger (heavier) rounds will cause more impact trauma than lighter rounds. - Fully-jacketed rounds will cause less trauma than unjacketed rounds. The metal jacket on a bullet will prevent the bullet from deforming quite as much as it hits soft tissue, so it will pass completely through soft tissue and out the other side (i.e. a "through and through"). Softer, unjacketed rounds will deform as they hit soft tissue and make a much larger wound cavity, which leads to more potential injury and more possibility for the victim to die. - Hollowpoint rounds will also deform more than fully-formed or jacketed rounds, again releasing more energy into the victim rather than retaining that energy in the bullet and passing clean through the body. This results in a larger wound cavity and a greater possibility that the victim will die. - Rounds which land on extremeties are less likely to cause death than rounds which land in the center mass of the victim. That said, anyone who can get shot in the arm with a .50 BMG and not die is a superhero. Besides the fact that the arm would be instantly vaporized and turn into nothing more than red mist, the impact trauma alone would kill you, even if you're shot in the arm or the leg. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6ceefcd6b4812f2059f716914f98b9b7){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6ceefcd6b4812f2059f716914f98b9b7" master_w="450" master_h="430"} ::: :::: The .50 BMG is on the far left.
