--- title: '"Do cops have the legal right to enter a locked private business if somebody accidentally called 911?"' date: '2015-06-18' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The police have the need to enter the building to ensure everyone is safe. The police can even claim exigent circumstances and forcibly enter into the building if they feel that someone\'s life may be in danger, if they say they heard someone screaming or they say they heard someone calling for help. Exigent circumstances allow the police to enter places that they normally would not, so it is used judiciously.\ \ In this case, an agent of the compay allowed them entrance. The doors opened, the agent of the company left, and the police walked in. No one stopped them. The police can rightly claim that an agent of the company opened the doors and did not stop them from entering. This, along with the obvious public safety concern of ensuring that the 911 call was actually an accident and not someone seriously in trouble, is enough legal basis for the police to enter the building.\ \ If the employee who opened the doors and did not stop the officers from entering is at fault according to company policy, the company is within its rights to terminate him.]{.rendered_qtext}
