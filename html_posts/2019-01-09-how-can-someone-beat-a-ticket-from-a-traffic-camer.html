**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can someone beat a ticket from a traffic camera?** A lot of people here will tell you "don't run red lights." While an obvious answer, it doesn't help you if you've already gotten a ticket in the mail. Legally speaking, there are lots of things you can do, but the problem is that laws for red light cameras and how they are implemented vary by state. You're going to have to research your local laws and see what you can do. For example, here in Colorado, we have a number of cities with red light cameras. Here, **you can choose to simply not pay the ticket.** State law says that the city has 90 days to serve you with the ticket, or it is invalid. And any city process server can serve you with the ticket; it does not have to be a police officer. And they don't have to serve the driver; any adult at the driver's home can be served with the ticket and it is valid. Here, you would have to try to avoid the process server for three months, and they work odd hours so they can catch an adult at home. Don't be surprised if someone rings your doorbell at 6:00 AM. They may also try to send the ticket via certified mail. If you don't sign for it within the 90 day window, the ticket is similarly invalid. *Once they know someone has received the ticket,* and you don't pay, the fine goes up. And while it doesn't add points to your license, if you fail to pay and they send it to collections, it can negatively impact your credit score. **If you can prove that it was not you driving the car**, you can also get out of the ticket, but you will have to prove it. For example, if a male owns the car and a female was driving it at the time, it may be as simple as sending them a copy of your driver's license so they can see that you were not the driver. Also law on the books here in Colorado: **red light cameras and speed vans must have posted signs warning drivers**. This is why you need to research your local laws. If you saw the camera flash but you didn't see a sign, you should drive around the block and check for signage. Take pictures of the intersection and take note of any signage that is missing. You could then use that information to dispute the ticket in court. You should ideally do that on the same day you see the camera flash so that you can say, "I saw the flash and drove around the block and took these pictures" so that you can show the lack of signage on the day of the incident. That's just an example of three things you can do in Colorado to get out of a red light camera violation, but again, these are highly dependent on Colorado law. There are others depending on the type of violation you receive (for example, if you didn't run the light but you are blocking the crosswalk, it will send you a smaller fine for that).
