**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **When Donald Trump commits an impeachable offence again, would Congress restart the impeachment proceedings or will they just resend the articles of impeachment to the Senate for a new trial?** You have several errors of fact in your question, which I presume are caused by your own biases. "**When** Donald Trump commits an impeachable offence again" is an inaccurate forward-looking statement. It should be "If," not "When." No one can see the future. You also said "When Donald Trump **commits an impeachable offence again**" which is inaccurate because no one is convinced that he already committed impeachable offenses. The Senate's party-line acquittal and the party-line House vote for impeachment proved this. Never has an impeachment taken place on a party-line vote. The Framers warned of this: impeachment becoming a party-line weapon. Democrats decided to jump the shark on impeachment anyway. This is where the uncertainty about actual impeachable offenses comes into play. Now that we've thoroughly dissected the bias inherent in your question, to answer the actual meat of your question, the Articles of Impeachment have been voted on and passed; the President has already been impeached. The Articles were sent to the Senate, and they voted to acquit the President and not remove him from office. In other words, while the House has the sole power to impeach, the Senate has the sole power to remove from office if they think the offenses sent by the House were severe enough to warrant removal. If new offenses are alleged by the House, they will have to start over from square one: new allegations, new investigations, new evidence, and a new vote for new Articles of Impeachment based on these new allegations. These new Articles will lead to a new Senate trial. And if it goes just as partisan as the first impeachment attempt, it will fail in exactly the same partisan way that this one did. And eventually, the people will grow weary of endless impeachments and will vote the rabble-rousers out of the House to allow for more level-headed persons to take over.
