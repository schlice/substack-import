**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can police arrest a suspicious looking man carrying two knives openly in a public place? What would happen to the man?** Let's break this down: *Can police arrest a suspicious looking man?* Arrest requires **probable cause**. This is a *reasonable belief* that a crime was committed or is about to be committed. So long as the police have a reasonable belief that this man committed a crime, they can arrest him and charge him with that crime. But "looking suspicious" isn't a crime. So only because he looks suspicious, he can't be arrested. *Carrying two knives openly in a public place*: Describe "openly." Does this mean openly on his belt in scabbards, or openly in his hands? The difference is huge, as most places that allow people to carry knives require that they be in sheaths. There are also blade length restrictions. Assuming he meets all of those, he would be perfectly fine. If, however, the knife blades were too long to carry, or if by "carrying openly" you meant "in his hands," there would probably be a bigger problem as the man would clearly be perceived as a threat and could be arrested.
