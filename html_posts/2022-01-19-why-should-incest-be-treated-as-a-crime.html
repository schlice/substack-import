Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Incest is treated as a crime because the state has a compelling interest in protecting children.** We already know that incest greatly increases the risk of birth defects in children. The problem with having sex with close relatives is that there is an astonishingly high chance that the child will be born with birth defects. > \[T\]he odds that a newborn child who is the product of brother-sister > or father-daughter incest will suffer an early death, a severe birth > defect, or some mental deficiently approaches 50 > percent.[\[1\]](#VqjQI){#cite-VqjQI .citation_link} Because the state has a vested interest in ensuring that children have as great a chance for success as we can give them, preventing children being born with birth defects is within the state's set of compelling interests. Since there is a high correlation between incestuous behavior and birth defects, incest is outlawed. > \ Original question: Why should incest be treated as a crime?
