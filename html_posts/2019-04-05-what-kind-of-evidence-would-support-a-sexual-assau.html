**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What kind of evidence would support a sexual assault case that will prove it was not consensual?** Any physical evidence or direct witness testimony that speaks to the fact that the contact was unwanted. These can include, but are not limited to: - defensive injuries (scratches, bruises, marks) consistent with a struggle in the orientation the victim claims he or she was in at the time - "rape kits" performed by an emergency room physician - witness testimony confirming that a struggle was directly heard. Hearsay is completely useless (i.e. I heard from so-and-so that she said no). - witness testimony immediately following the assault speaking to the state of mind of the victim, even if the crime was not immediately reported. Beyond direct physical evidence or direct witness testimony, the span of time between the alleged attack and any reporting made is also a factor. Did the victim go immediately (or reasonably quickly) to an emergency room or call the police, or did the victim wait 20 years before telling anyone?
