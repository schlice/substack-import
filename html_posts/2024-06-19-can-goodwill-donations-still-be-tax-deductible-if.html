--- title: '"Can Goodwill donations still be tax deductible if the items are not sold at their stores?"' date: '2024-06-19' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What Goodwill does with your donations does not determine whether or not donations are tax-deductible. If Goodwill is an approved organization for accepting charitable donations (and as of today, they are), then if you itemize your deductions, you can deduct what you donate to Goodwill. See [[IRS Publication 526, Charitable Contributions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b3794d&url=https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p526.pdf){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="irs.gov"}]{.qlink_container}, for more information. You should get a receipt from Goodwill, which you should retain for your records if you itemize your deductions.
