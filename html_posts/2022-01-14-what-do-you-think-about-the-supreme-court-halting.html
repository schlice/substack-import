**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is SCOTUS being reasonable. They found it *reasonable* for healthcare workers to be vaccinated. In their particular workplace setting, knowing what they will be exposed to, and knowing who they can potentially infect, it is *reasonable* for healthcare workers to be vaccinated. They found it *unreasonable* for the Occupational Safety and Health Administration to find that COVID-19 is a "workplace hazard" that they have the authority to regulate, across the board, for every industry, in every circumstance. Splitting the decision like this is honestly the most reasonable thing that SCOTUS could have done. \ Original question: What do you think about the Supreme Court halting the vaccine mandate for large businesses while leaving it in place for healthcare workers?
