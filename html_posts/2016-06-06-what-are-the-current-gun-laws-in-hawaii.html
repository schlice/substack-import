Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Hawaii has some reasonably tough gun laws. Here are some of the highlights: - Residents of Hawaii must get a permit for ***each individual firearm acquisition***. You must apply to the county chief of police before every firearm transaction and receive a permit before you can purchase or otherwise acquire a firearm. You must have training and qualifications which compare with what most other states have for concealed carry. - Every legally-owned firearm in Hawaii must be registered. - Safe storage requirements are in place. Every firearm must be safely stored when not in use. - Hawaii is a "may issue" state for concealed carry. - There is no open carry in the state. - Magazines have a 10-round limit. - Hawaii does have a Castle Doctrine and stand-your-ground laws.
