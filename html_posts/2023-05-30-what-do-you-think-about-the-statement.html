**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'm a theist and that's the stupidest statement I've heard yet today (but the day is still young). Atheists do not believe in God. However, I will argue that **atheism is a religion**. Just as they accuse theists, atheists believe ***with zero proof*** that there is no God. They cannot ***prove*** beyond a reasonable doubt that God does not exist, yet they have ***faith*** that He does not. So just like the theists, atheists have belief and faith. And just like theists, they have *dogma* (the inerrant nature of what they consider to be science) and *exclusivity* (you must believe as they do in order to join their club --- theists are not welcome). Even the courts have recognized that atheism is, in a certain sense, a religion, because it is covered by the Establishment Clause. The 7th Circuit said, "when a person sincerely holds beliefs dealing with issues of ultimate concern that for her occupy a place parallel to that filled by \... God in traditionally religious persons, those beliefs represent her religion." The Circuit said, therefore, the establishment clause is equally applicable to the nonreligious and even the anti-religious. "\[A\]theism may be considered, \[only\] in this special sense, a religion."[\[1\]](#auhiC){#cite-auhiC .citation_link} So while the statement in the question is clearly wrong, a statement which is at least an arguable one is, "**No one is more religious than an atheist.**" Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-auhiC){.citation_index} [[Atheism](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a7e1ba&url=https://www.mtsu.edu/first-amendment/article/1318/atheism){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="mtsu.edu"}]{.qlink_container}
