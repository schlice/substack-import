**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer, of course, is "it depends." Some cities have universal "No Turn on Red" laws within the city limits. If they do, it is usually posted as such. That said, there are a few conditions under which you cannot turn right on red: - Posted No Turn on Red or No Turns - Red Right Arrow If it's a solid red light, and you come to a complete stop, you can normally turn right on red. The "Right Turn Signal" signage is meant to indicate that the light in question will not follow the same pattern as the traffic light at the intersection, and should only be followed for right turns. Traffic at the intersection should not follow the "Right Turn Signal" indications.
