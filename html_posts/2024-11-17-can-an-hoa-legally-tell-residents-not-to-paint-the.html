**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer, as with all legal questions, is "it depends," and what it depends on is your Governing Documents. When it comes to paint, you can be required to submit your paint color for approval, especially if the color is changing from what it was originally painted. Even if you are painting the same color, you still need to get approval (although it should be pretty straightforward to say that you're repainting the same color). And yes, they can disapprove of your choice of color. Putting up Christmas decorations is a different story. I'm sure some HOAs don't allow decorations, but you could be crossing into First Amendment-protected territory if you disallow Christmas decorations, but you permit, say, Diwali decorations or Kwanzaa decorations or Ramadan decorations. You'd have to disallow ***all*** decorations outside of houses in order to stay legally okay. Most HOAs don't want to pick that fight when they disallow religious-based decorations, so they *regulate* decorations, not outright *ban* them. They may regulate the timeframe you can have them up, or how big they can be, or other details, but they probably won't outright *ban* them. Yes, the whole point of the Architecture Approval Authority (or whatever your HOA calls it) is to maintain a specific look of the neighborhood. It's literally written into your Governing Documents.
