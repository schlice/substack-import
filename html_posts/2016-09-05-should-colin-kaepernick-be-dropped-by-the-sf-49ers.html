--- title: "Should Colin Kaepernick be dropped by the SF 49ers because he refuses to stand for the national anthem?" date: "2016-09-05" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Of course he should not be dropped from the team ***because*** he exercised his right to stand or sit during the National Anthem. He would do well to realize that the men and women in uniform have fought and died to preserve his right to make that choice. His refusal to honor the nation because he believes in a totally false cause is disturbing, but is still his choice. Military members remember Voltaire's famous quote: "I may disagree with what you say, but I will defend to the death your right to say it." Military members do not take it as disrespect if someone chooses to make a political statement. In fact, that means the ideals we fought for are still in place, and that's a heartening feeling. The 49ers should be worried about losing something else: **fans**. People watch Monday Night Football (and now Sunday and Thursday) because they want to ***escape***. They want to get away from the turmoil of the political sphere for a few hours, and escape into a simpler world which involves nothing more than moving a ball. They want *entertainment.* When players choose to use that platform and *politicize* it, they risk alienating the very people who fund the leagues which pay him those hefty salaries to use his athletic talents. If you start losing your primary customers over your actions (regardless of how legal or proper they are), you run the risk that your continued employment may jeopardize the bottom line, and you **can** be dropped for that reason. If you start distracting too much from the game, you can indeed be fired for that. Kaepernick is a third-string quarterback, hardly the big draw that the first and second string QBs are. He can be cut without too much pain to the team. Making himself a distraction from ***the game*** does not help strengthen the case for continued employment.
