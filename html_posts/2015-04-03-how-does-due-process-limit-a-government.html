--- title: "How does due process limit a government\" date: "2015-04-03" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The [[Fifth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution](https://www.quora.com/topic/Fifth-Amendment-to-the-U-S-Constitution)]{.qlink_container} limits the power of the [[Federal Government of the United States](https://www.quora.com/topic/Federal-Government-of-the-United-States-1)]{.qlink_container} by saying that \"no person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law.\"\ \ The [[Due Process](https://www.quora.com/topic/Due-Process)]{.qlink_container} clause of the [[14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution](https://www.quora.com/topic/14th-Amendment-to-the-U-S-Constitution)]{.qlink_container} also states, \"\[N\]or shall any State deprive any person of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law\" The 14th Amendment restricts the states in addition to the federal government.]{.rendered_qtext}
