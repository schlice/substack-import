--- title: "If someone is bleeding profusely, would it be better to apply a pressure bandage to the wound, or apply stitches?" date: "2017-12-29" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In terms of first aid, apply a pressure bandage. Hopefully you have some form of clean trauma dressing available to you; if not, use whatever is the cleanest and whitest dressing you have available. Sometimes, this could be a piece of clothing. Back in the 80s, my dad (a retired New York police officer) used his white undershirt as a trauma dressing and saved a young man's life. Note that this is not recommended, but if you're saving a life, you do what you have to do, and you use whatever you have on hand. If you still cannot control the bleeding with a pressure bandage, and it is on an extremety, you may also want to apply a *restricting band* to the area in the direction of the heart between the heart and the injury. This will reduce (but not stop!) the blood flow to the affected area and will aid you in controlling the bleeding. Hopefully, help arrives before you have to make the fateful decision to apply a tourniquet. If you apply a tourniquet, DO NOT REMOVE IT! Only the doctor should remove a tourniquet. You should only apply a tourniquet to save a life, if blood loss is so severe that not applying a tourniquet would result in death. If you are interested in learning how to apply a tourniquet, I will allow you to Google it yourself. There are a number of details in applying a tourniquet, which is why I don't want to do it the injustice of going over them here. If you're this interested, you might think about taking a First Aid course from the Red Cross.
