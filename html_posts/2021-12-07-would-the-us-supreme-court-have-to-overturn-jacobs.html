**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Jacobson v. Massachusetts* was a case which established that the state can compel *private citizens* to get a vaccine under the state's police powers. Some states have already been requiring *businesses* to vaccinate all of their workers. This is a different style of mandate than was created by *Jacobson*. This is more of a workplace health and safety directive than a broad vaccine mandate for the entire state population, which is what was held to be Constitutional by *Jacobson.* So no, it's unlikely that SCOTUS would have to strike down *Jacobson* **** in order to overturn New York's private-sector mandate. But regardless, SCOTUS has not interfered with state-level mandates, either for or against, so it is unlikely that SCOTUS would interfere here either. They *have*, however, stepped in when the Federal government attempted to compel private companies to mandate vaccinations, as they do not have the authority to do so. \ Original question: Would the U.S. Supreme Court have to overturn Jacobson vs. Massachusetts to strike down New York City's recent vaccine mandate for most private-sector workers?
