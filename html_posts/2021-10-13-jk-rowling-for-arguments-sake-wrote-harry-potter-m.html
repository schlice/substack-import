**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Bible is a compilation of books and letters (66 of them) which were written by about 40 different authors over the span of about 1600 years. It was written by a wide variety of people: doctors, rabbis, tax collectors, fishermen. It was written in 13 different countries on three different continents. Yet there is **unity** in the message of the Bible. The themes of the writing are maintained across the years, the people, and the locations. And after all that space and time, the Bible does not contradict itself. It is only by divine inspiration that such a feat could have taken place. Compare this to the Qur'an. It was compiled by one person, Zaid bin Thabit, under the guidance of Mohammed's father-in-law, Abu-Bekr. Then, in A.D. 650, a group of Arab scholars produced a unified version and destroyed all variant copies to preserve the unity of the Qur'an. [\[1\]](#Hgewy){#cite-Hgewy .citation_link} In other words, the Bible was unified across the years, people, and continents, from the time of its writing. The Qur'an had unity forced upon it by human editors. That is just one way we know that the Bible, as a whole, is the inspired word of God. Some of the others: - Prophesy. God spoke to men telling them the things that he was going to make happen in the future. There are over 300 prophecies about the coming of Jesus, and these were all written long before he was born. They all came true in his life. - Archaeological records support what was written in the Bible. While people have tried to find archaeological evidence to disprove what was written in the Bible, to date they have failed. - Outside writings corroborate the stories told in the Bible. For example, a man named Titus Flavius Josephus was a Jewish historian who wrote during the 1st century AD. Josephus records some events which also correlate with what was written in the Bible. There are others as well, and they all back up what was stated by the Bible authors. - Scientific accuracy. Every scientific claim made in the Bible has been found to be true, despite the scientific knowledge of the age. For example, in Isaiah, it says "It is he who sits above the circle of the earth" (Isaiah 40:22 ESV). The word "circle" here translates to "globe" or "sphere." How did Isaiah know that the earth was a sphere, when all scientific knowledge at the time said the earth was flat? Job said, "He spreads out the northern skies over empty space; **he suspends the earth over nothing.**" (Job 26:7 NIV). How did Job know that the earth "hung in space" before modern astronomy? Divine inspiration. The Bible is an amazing book. It is so deep, that scholars can spend *lifetimes* studying it and still never completely plumb the depths of its knowledge and wisdom. Yet its stories are so simple, children can read the Bible and understand the major themes written within it. How can people create something so timeless and so complete, so complex and yet so understandable, at the times when these books were written? The only logical conclusion is divine inspiration. \ Original question: J.K. Rowling (for arguments sake) wrote Harry Potter & made billions through penning her gift of imagination.
