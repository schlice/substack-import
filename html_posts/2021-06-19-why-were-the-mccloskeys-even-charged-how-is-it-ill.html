In fact, there's lots of things that they did very wrong during the incident, and most of them involve *how* they were handling their weapons. Mrs. McCloskey pointed her weapon at people, with her finger on the trigger. This alone is a criminal assault. Mr. McCloskey, while maintaining good trigger discipline, also failed to keep his muzzle pointed in a safe direction until such time that he needed to bring his weapon to bear against a threat. The prosecutor, Mark said, "dropped all the felony charges, all the gun charges, and charged me with a crime that said I purposely placed other people in apprehension of imminent fear of physical injury." He was charged with a a Class C misdemeanor of fourth-degree assault. His wife pleaded guilty to a Class A misdemeanor of second-degree harassment. They will pay fines of \$2000 and \$750 for those offenses, and will avoid jail time. And in a political stunt that surprises no one (but was done to placate the fomenting masses who want the McCloskey's heads on pikes), they were also forced to relinquish the two firearms used in the assault. Mr. McCloskey called the fine the equivalent of "a parking ticket."
