--- title: "At what point do you need to reach a domestic partnership/pre-nuptial agreement, such as before even moving in with each other to the same residence?" date: "2015-07-20" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Prenuptial agreements can be entered into at any time before you are married. They come into force at the time of a divorce from marriage, so as long as you enter into the agreement at some time before you are married, it is a qualifying prenup. If you enter into an agreement after you are married, it is called a **postnuptial agreement**, but it carries the same weight, and still comes into force at the time a divorce is filed.\ \ Postnups are gaining in popularity, but as a way to save a marriage, not as a guide for an impending divorce.]{.rendered_qtext}
