**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If the American constitution was amended to give Cabinet secretaries and officials power to subpoena Congress, submit legislation to Congress, even vote on legislation, along with long, fixed terms, what might that look like?** It would look like a further erosion of a government by and of the people, and a further step towards a future of laws and policy being enacted by the unelected bureaucracy, and control by a federal dictatorship of hundreds of offices of unelected bureaucrats, further sliding America towards bureaucratic despotism. Cabinet secretaries are appointed positions by the President, not voted on by the people. At least in the original construction of the government, the House was elected by the people, so they had a voice when legislation was proposed. Remember the rallying cry around the Boston Tea Party? "No taxation without representation!" The House provided that representation, with legislation proposed and written by legislators directly elected by, and therefore directly accountable to, the people.
