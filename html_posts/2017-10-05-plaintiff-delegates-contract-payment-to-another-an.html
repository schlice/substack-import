--- title: "Plaintiff delegates contract payment to another and the defendant fails to perform. Can the plaintiff sue the defendant for non-performance? How was the plaintiff damaged if it was the other who paid?" date: "2017-10-05" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It depends on the terms of the delegation. If a contract has not been legally transferred to another party or amended to show the new payor, than the original signatory on the contract is still legally responsible for making the payment in whatever way that person chose to do so, and remains legally liable should the contract fail to be paid. Can the plaintiff sue the person who was supposed to pay? Sure, but you will have to show the agreement that was made to make payments on the contract, and how it was violated, and the agreement has to be something other than "Hey, you gonna pay this, right?"
