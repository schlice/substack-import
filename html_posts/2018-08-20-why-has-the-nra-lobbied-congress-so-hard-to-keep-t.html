**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why has the NRA lobbied Congress so hard to keep the CDC from investigating gun violence as a health matter?** Short answer: ***they didn't.*** Longer answer: In 1993, a CDC-funded research study by Arthur Kellerman found, among other things, that guns in the home were associated with an increased risk of homicide in the home. The NRA saw this report as unrelated to CDC's mission to prevent disease, and instead saw it as a veiled attempt at gun control through "research." Through their lobbying efforts, Congress passed the [[Dickey Amendment](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7fd880&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dickey_Amendment){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} as a rider on the 1996 Omnibus spending bill. The amendment simply says, "\[N\]one of the funds made available for injury prevention and control at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) may be used to advocate or promote gun control.\" That's it. Do as much research as you like, but you can't take a political position. Researchers, of course, saw this is a "restriction" on their work, and didn't do *any* research at all, making it appear that the NRA's lobbying efforts had in fact **prohibited** the CDC from doing any gun research at all. Democrats tried all throughout the Obama years to remove the Dickey Amendment from spending bills, but it stubbornly hung on. Obama directed the CDC to get back to work and do gun research. They funded a study in 2013, and did their own study in 2015. In 2018, when the Continuing Resolution was passed allowing for government operations to continue, a report accompanying the CR clarified that the CDC can, in fact, conduct research into gun violence, so long as they do not advocate or promote gun control.
