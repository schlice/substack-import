--- title: '"How can a private pool owned by an HOA employ a life guard without insurance and legal liability going through the roof?"' date: '2024-08-26' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In most HOA communities, a community pool is unguarded. Signs are posted at the gated entrance to the pool that swimming is at your own risk since there is no lifeguard on duty. The HOA maintains general liability insurance on all common areas within the HOA, and dues pay for that insurance. Neighborhoods which have pools will have liability insurance commensurate with the risk.
