**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Which country buys most iPhones?** Manufacturers' market share of smartphone sales in the United States is led by Apple and Samsung, with a market share of 46 percent and 25 percent respectively, as of the second quarter of 2020. Next to Apple and Samsung are LG and Motorola/Lenovo, with market shares of 12 and seven percent. Samsung had the largest share of the smartphone market in Central and Eastern Europe in the second quarter of 2020, followed by Xiaomi. However, Lenovo recorded the most significat increase in sales in that period. Apple only held a 10% market share in Central and Eastern Europe.[\[1\]](#lYcoS){#cite-lYcoS .citation_link} Samsung also led in Asia by 29%, but Apple has caught up to Xiaomi to hang on to second place with 16 and 14 percent respectively.[\[2\]](#QBRnO){#cite-QBRnO .citation_link} **TL;DR: The United States, far and away, buys more iPhones than any other country, with nearly 50% of the smartphone market dominated by Apple. Samsung comes in second place in the US, with a seemingly distant 1/4 of the market share there.** Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-lYcoS){.citation_index} [[Smartphone sales by region 2018-2020 \| Statista](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=56882a&url=https://www.statista.com/statistics/755388/global-smartphone-unit-sales-by-region/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="statista.com"}]{.qlink_container} [\[2\]](#cite-QBRnO){.citation_index} [[Mobile Vendor Market Share Asia \| StatCounter Global Stats](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2dac3d&url=https://gs.statcounter.com/vendor-market-share/mobile/asia){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="statcounter.com"}]{.qlink_container}
