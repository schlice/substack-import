{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Morals and ethics are relative. Unless we all share a common ultimate moral authority (like God), which we do not, then each person's moral authority is different, and therefore no one can be subject to the same set of moral standards. So rather than asking whether or not it is moral or ethical to do something, let's ask whether or not it is ***legal***, and whether or not it is ***appropriate*** for an able-bodied person to use a handicapped restroom stall. First off, there are no laws against able-bodied individuals using handicapped stalls in restrooms, as far as I know. This leaves us the question of appropriateness. Is it appropriate for an able-bodied person to use the handicapped stall in a restroom? The answer is yes. So long as there is not a wheelchair-bound individual waiting for that stall, and if there aren't any other usable stalls available, then it is appropriate to use the stall as you need. Do try to do your business promptly and vacate the stall in case a wheelchair-bound person is waiting, since that's pretty much the only stall they can use. Please keep your Instagram and Facebook surfing and your Flappy Bird playing to a minimum while you're in the handicapped stall.
