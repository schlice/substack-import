--- title: '"Could Roe v. Wade be reinstated?"' date: '2023-07-20' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not from the Supreme Court. That ship has sailed. Essentially, that's like asking if the Court would ever reinstate the 3/5 Compromise or Jim Crow. The Court has corrected a bad ruling, and barring something that has never happened in US history, we're not going back. Abortion is not a right in the Constitution, period. That error has been corrected. Maybe there will be a new ruling, with new facts and new circumstances, which legalizes abortion nationwide under a different Constitutional provision, but the court will never reinstate the ruling of *Roe*. But something *Roe-*like can be passed at the state level, and some states do indeed have similar *Roe-*like laws, in that they permit abortions. Or, a Constitutional amendment could be passed that makes the right to an abortion Constitutional.
