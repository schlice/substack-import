**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, I'm disappointed that we couldn't deport this specific alleged criminal alien. I'm not disappointed in the ruling. For reference, the case we are discussing is *Sessions v. Dimaya,* for which SCOTUS rendered a decision on April 17, 2018. Conservatives are thrilled that we have a strict constructionist on the bench. Gorsuch sided with the liberals today because they're right. The wording in the previous court's ruling regarding what constitutes a "violent felony" was unconstitutionally vague and was thrown out. So was this. The court is, remarkably, consistent. As a conservative, I want Congress to do its job and write good laws. The sad fact is that when Congress writes poor laws, no one benefits, and yes, criminals end up remaining in country because we can't correctly deport them. Clearly, we want criminal aliens deported. But in order to protect everyone, our laws must be iron-clad. Gorsuch did not vote on party lines; instead, he actually comported himself as a *jurist*, which is more than I can say for half the court.
