**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The consideration of ridiculousness is based on how well the belief comports with knowable, observable reality. If the belief flies in the face of what is knowable, repeatable and observable, then the belief can be considered ridiculous. For example, evolutionists believe that DNA just randomly self-assembled. However, knowable, observable reality tells us that messages (specified complexity) only come from intelligence. Observation and repetition tell us that unguided naturalistic processes never create specified complexity. So if someone believes (without evidence) that DNA just randomly evolved out of nothing, then we can rationally call that belief ridiculous. But if someone believes that God exists, then we can point to the fact that God specifically exists outside of the physical, so demanding physical evidence for an immaterial god is not rational. We use the evidence we do have to prove beyond a reasonable doubt that God exists (the evidence in nature, like DNA, and the truth which is written on to every human heart). So such a belief would not be considered to be ridiculous.
