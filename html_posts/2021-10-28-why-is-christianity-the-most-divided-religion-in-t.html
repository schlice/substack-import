**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Ultimately, even though the various Christian sects may disagree on the finer points of the faith, **the gospel** remains the same throughout any religion or sect which claims itself to be Christian. In fact, Christians are *allowed to have differences of opinion* on non-essential doctrine: > Now accept the one who is weak in faith, *but* not for *the purpose > of* passing judgment on his opinions. One person has faith that he may > eat all things, but he who is weak eats vegetables *only*. The one who > eats is not to regard with contempt the one who does not eat, and the > one who does not eat is not to judge the one who eats, for God has > accepted him. Who are you to judge the servant of another? To his own > master he stands or falls; and he will stand, for the Lord is able to > make him stand. > > One person regards one day above another, another regards every day > *alike*. Each person must be fully convinced in his own mind. He who > observes the day, observes it for the Lord, and he who eats, does so > for the Lord, for he gives thanks to God; and he who eats not, for the > Lord he does not eat, and gives thanks to God. For not one of us lives > for himself, and not one dies for himself --- Romans 14:1--7 NASB In other words, it is necessary for a church which claims to be Christian to believe that Jesus Christ is God, one of three persons in the Trinity, and that he died as a penal substitution for us and our sins. Really, the only thing we need to do in order to define whether or not a religion or sect is Christian is to determine who they say Jesus is. Yes, among Christian sects (which really exploded in the United States in the 19th century following the [[Second Great Awakening](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7a3720&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Great_Awakening){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}) there are still disagreements on certain details such as *sola scriptura* (only the Bible as the source of God's inerrant word) and *sola fides* (only faith saves you instead of faith plus works). But that alone doesn't make them less Christian. \ Original question: Why is Christianity the most divided religion in the world with diverse doctrines and believes that sometimes contrast, the only thing all denominations have in common is believing in Jesus?
