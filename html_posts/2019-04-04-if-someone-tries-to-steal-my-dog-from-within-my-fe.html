**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If someone tries to steal my dog from within my fenced yard, can I legally shoot the attempting thief?** I get the snark from the other answerers, but I can see a way in which you could misunderstand when to use deadly force. There is one circumstance in which you can use deadly force to stop someone fleeing: ***kidnapping***. In many jurisdictions, you can use any force, up to and including deadly force, to stop a kidnapping. But all states are different; be sure you review your state laws before you shoot at a kidnapper. If you own a gun, you should have already printed off and practically memorized the laws related to using your gun where you live. That said, your dog is not a person. Legally speaking, your dog is property, not people. Therefore, the person is committing a ***theft***, not a ***kidnapping***.
