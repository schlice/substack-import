**Content:** [You have an interesting story. I don\'t often hear of peoples\' MS stories starting with narcolepsy. Being genetic and running in your family, I would imagine that your narcolepsy is genuine, likely caused by a genetic mutation passed down your family lineage.\ \ The rest of the story, however, is intriguing. Your symptoms outside of the narcolepsy are definitely ones experienced by MS patients. The balance and coordination, memory and bladder issues are definitely MS-like. However, knowing your family history of genetic brain mutations, I would look very closely at the source of these other symptoms. MS is often called \"the Great Imitator\" since none of its symptoms are unique to it alone.\ \ MRI technology has come a long way in 10 years. So much so that the definition of clinical MS has changed so that a conclusive finding of clinical MS can be made with a neurological exam, a thorough vetting of all of the other potential causes for the symptoms, and a MRI scan showing all of the clinical signs of someone with MS.\ \ My suggestion for you would be to see your neurologist and run down this list with him. I would expect to see a full neurologic exam, a \"little white spot\" assay, which is basically a battery of blood tests to rule out other causes for white spots on an MRI, and the 1.5T high-field MRI exam, which should only take about 45 minutes \"in the tube\" if you have a good radiology crew. I have such a good crew here that by the time I have re-dressed and an ready to leave, my images are already on CD waiting for me. Of course, the radiologists report follows the next day. One way or another, you should be able to find the source of your symptoms. If you don\'t have a good neurologist, it is time to get one. You need someone who specializes in MS and related neurological diseases, or who is in a practice where such doctors also work, for easy consultation.\ \ Good luck to you, and if I can be of any more assistance, please let me know. As we say in the MS world, \"keep s\'myelin!\" :-)]{.rendered_qtext}
