--- title: '"Is there any liability for members of an HOA board if they make mistakes while making decisions for the association, even if their intentions were good?"' date: '2024-10-03' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In any corporation, while there is a fiduciary responsibility to do what's best for the corporation, there is no liability if you do your job, but just do it poorly. This holds true for the smallest HOA Board of Directors all the way up to the President of the United States. Liability attaches if you *intentionally* or *unintentionally fail to do your job as it is written.* But if you do your job and it doesn't turn out as you planned, or even if you did your job but you made poor decisions, that doesn't rise to the level of legal liability, unless your conduct was so egregious such that a reasonable person in the same situation would never have made the decisions you did. In almost every case, Board members on HOA Boards are covered by Director and Officer (D&O) insurance, providing liability coverage when you do your job but legal actions are raised against you.
