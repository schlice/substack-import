**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What precisely is he going to sue you for? > Only human beings, and not corporations or other organizations, have > rights of publicity and privacy interests that can be invaded by > misappropriation of name or likeness. Thus, only individuals can sue > for unlawful use of name or likeness, unless a human being has > transferred his or her rights to an organization. > [\[1\]](#MBNEV){#cite-MBNEV .citation_link} The business could try to sue you for defamation, but what you posted must be *untrue*, or the inference you made about what was clearly in the video must be false. But truth is always a defense to defamation claims of any kind. I'd say record what you saw and post it on Yelp or in a Google review and let the chips fall where they may. But make sure you have a good defense attorney lined up in case the business does try to sue you. Also, be aware that state laws may prohibit recording in businesses if you have been duly notified of the prohibition, so you could be in violation of a state law. Some states may require a posting at the door in order for the prohibition to be binding. Check your state laws before recording. \ Original question: Is it legal for me to record inside a restaurant? I wanted to expose the manager's awful behavior&his scams.
