**Content:** [In the United States, we no longer has \"dead or alive\" bounties. Bail enforcement is a legitimate business, and you either have to be licensed in the state or certified by the state in order to be a bail enforcement agent. While bail enforcement agents have more authority over their \"skips\" than police do, there are still rules and regulations that must be followed. When an accused signs a bail bond contract, he waives his Constitutional rights and agrees to be apprehended by a bail enforcement agent should he fail to appear. He doesn\'t have to be read Miranda warnings, he can be apprehended inside his house, which the bail enforcement agent can enter without a warrant, they can tap his phone without a warrant, etc.\ \ There\'s a great writeup on bail enforcement over at HowStuffWorks:\ \ [[How Bounty Hunting Works](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=95fbb6&url=http://people.howstuffworks.com/bounty-hunting.htm){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="howstuffworks.com"}]{.qlink_container}]{.rendered_qtext}
