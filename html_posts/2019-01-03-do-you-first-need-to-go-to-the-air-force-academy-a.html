**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you first need to go to the Air Force Academy and then apply to the Pararescue?** No. You do not need to be a USAFA graduate to enlist and become a PJ. You can enlist in the Air Force and go into the Pararescue career field. Here's some information about the Air Force Pararescue career field. [[U.S. Air Force - Career Detail - Pararescue](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ca23f6&url=https://www.airforce.com/careers/detail/pararescue?gclsrc%3Daw.ds%26gclid%3DCjwKCAiAgrfhBRA3EiwAnfF4tlKnT8m3amNgkJAmI_wpaNmMSgr1Hr63jqIByU4UjE7qGzjJBUMn3RoCN-sQAvD_BwE%26gclsrc%3Daw.ds){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="airforce.com"}]{.qlink_container} Here's the basic requirements to enlist in the US Air Force: - Be between 17 and 39 years of age. - Be a U.S. citizen or legal, permanent resident - Have a high school diploma, GED with at least 15 college credits, or GED You can become a PJ after graduating from the Academy, but the career paths are indeed different.
