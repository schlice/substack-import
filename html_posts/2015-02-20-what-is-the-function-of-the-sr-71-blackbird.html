**Content:** [The sharp edges are called \"chines\" and are part of the SR-71\'s design from the A-4 through A-6 models of the aircraft. The main reason that this was added was to reduce the radar cross section (RCS) of the aircraft. Besides the radar-absorbent material (RAM) which is painted over the entire aircraft, early studies in stealth technologies found that a shape with flattened, tapering sides would reflect most radar energy away from the beam\'s origin location.\ \ They did find that the chines added additional lift, acting as leading-edge extensions which also created turbulent (vs laminar) airflow over the wings at high angles-of-attack (AOA), which helped to prevent stalls. So besides creating a smaller RCS, it also helped the plane fly better.\ \ Modern aircraft may use foreplanes called \"canards\" instead of chines.]{.rendered_qtext}
