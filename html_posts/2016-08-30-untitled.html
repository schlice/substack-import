**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I can generally deconstruct the Young Earth theory using the Scopes trial. The Bible says that the earth was formed on the first, second, and third days, but the Bible also says the sun was set in the sky on the fourth day. Couldn't it be that those first three days lasted longer than 24 hours? There was no sun, no way to measure the day from the night, and there is no indication how long the day and the night lasted. So couldn't that first three days have taken 4 billion years? I personally don't see why not.
