--- title: "Under the Americans with Disabilities Act, what accommodations do businesses have to make for customers?" date: "2015-09-02" tags: ["veteran-benefits"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The presence or absence of security at the entrance to a building does not alter the requirements under ADA. Businesses must still provide access to disabled persons at any public accommodation.\ \ So the key to whether or not the business needs to provide accommodations to customers is whether or not the business is considered a \"public accommodation.\" If it is a business, and the business receives customers at their business location, it is likely that they fall under ADA guidelines and must provide accommodations. You will need to consult with the Disability office in your state to determine if the business is considered a public accommodation and is bound by ADA.\ \ That doesn\'t relieve the business from providing exactly the same accommodations to employees.]{.rendered_qtext}
