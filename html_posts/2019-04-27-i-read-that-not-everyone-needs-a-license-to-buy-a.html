**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **I read that not everyone needs a license to buy a gun, say hypothetically, a license was required, then who would benefit the most?** No one needs a license to buy a gun. Last time I checked, it was unconstitutional to force someone to get a license to exercise a constitutional right. Do you need a license to post on Quora (i.e. exercise your 1st Amendment rights)? Do you need a license to prevent you from having the police execute unlawful searches of your home? Do you need a license to exercise your Miranda rights? Similarly, you don't need a license to buy a gun. You need to pass a background check. Everyone who buys a gun from a licensed dealer needs to pass a background check. In most states, you can buy a gun from a private seller, and you don't need a background check. The article talks about the states in which you can buy a gun from a private seller and not need a background check (thanks goes out to HuffPo for giving criminals a ready resource for that). Now, in some alternate universe, if a license was required to buy a gun, who would benefit the most? The entity selling the licenses.
