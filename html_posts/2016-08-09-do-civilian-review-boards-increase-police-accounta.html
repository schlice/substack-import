--- title: '"Do civilian review boards increase police accountability?"' date: '2016-08-09' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Your question details actually answered your question. Civilians don't have their own review boards, but some departments do allow civilian volunteers to audit their internal reviews. The reviews don't change; they're exactly the same review that would be done normally. The civilians on the review board increase ***transparency***, or at the very least, the appearance of transparency to the general public. Knowing that civilians sit on the "internal" police review boards helps the public to feel like the police are being held accountable.
