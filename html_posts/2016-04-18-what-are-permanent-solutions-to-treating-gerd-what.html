**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First, there are other prescription medications that can be tried. But if you\'re tired of popping pills, or if you have GERD caused by a hiatal hernia, you are a potential candidate for laparoscopic anti-reflux surgery. This surgery is very non-invasive and has an excellent success rate. The actual surgery is called **fundoplication surgery** for Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD). During fundoplication surgery, the upper curve of the stomach (the fundus) is wrapped around the esophagus and sewn into place so that the lower portion of the esophagus passes through a small tunnel of stomach muscle. This prevents that \"straight-line\" from the stomach up the esophagus. Yes, we know it\'s not a straight line, but you get the point. Also, if your GERD is caused by a hiatal hernia, that will be repaired during the same surgery. There are risks. One of the biggest risks is bloating and gas buildup because the person cannot burp. The gas buildup may become more of a nuisance than the GERD was. Fundoplication surgery is not reversible, so the new symptoms would become permanent. So you may end up replacing a toothache with a headache, so to speak. There are, of course, the normal risks to surgery, related to infection, surgical complications, and anesthesia use. But that risk needs to be weighed against continuing to have to take medication for the rest of your life. Because when anti-reflux surgery is successful, it means a near-permanent cessation to GERD without medication.
