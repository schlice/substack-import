**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The logical fallacy you have committed is called a **false equivalency**, believing that somehow the two facts you stated are related. Let me explain where you went wrong. Gun company stock reacts positively to tragedies because Democrats always, without fail, immediately call for the banning of guns. When Democrats claim that guns are the problem in mass killings, people fear that some way, somehow, they will succeed in restricting the most popular rifle in America (the AR-15), and if they were contemplating buying one anyway, this pushes them over the edge. So gun company stock reacts positively to Democrats threatening to shutting down their sales. The NRA does not want to block the sale of guns because there is no reason to do so. The gun is not the culprit; the killer is. The killer passed every background check he was given, so adding more background checks wouldn't have stopped him. Banning guns from law-abiding citizens wouldn't have stopped him either; as a contractor for the government, he would still have had access to guns, just as the police would after a complete gun ban as well. The NRA is, rightly, opposed to punishing gun manufacturers because the gun is not the problem. So since the two facts you quoted are unrelated, your equivalency is also invalid.
