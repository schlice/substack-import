**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Bible tells us what it sounds like. It was recorded in Acts 2, after the apostles were given the gift of the Holy Spirit. The apostles shared the gospel with the crowds, speaking to them in their own languages. The crowds were amazed: "We hear them declaring the wonders of God in our own tongues!" (Acts 2:11). The Greek word translated "tongues" literally means "languages." Therefore, the gift of tongues is speaking in a language the speaker has never learned in order to minister to someone who does speak that language. Imagine this: a room full of people from all over the world. Spanish, French, German, Russian, Korean, Japanese, Polish, Nordic people, all represented in the room. Then one person gets up and starts speaking. ***But everyone, from every country, could understand what he was saying, in his own native language.*** **This** is what "speaking in tongues" looks like. It means speaking in languages that the speaker never learned to communicate God's Word to people who do not speak their language. Anything else that you may have heard of being related to speaking in tongues is nonsense and is unbiblical. Incoherent babble is exactly that: *incoherent*. Speaking in tongues was speaking in a language that everyone could understand. If you hear something and you don't understand it, you are not hearing speaking in tongues.
