**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **The ACLU lawyers report they\'re unable to locate parents to 545 children, who are the reminder of the Trump border policy. Could the US incur sanctions from other countries for this practice?** Other countries could sanction the United States for whatever reason they choose, or for no reason at all. *But why would they?* The rest of the world wants to trade with us. We get far less benefit from trading with other countries than they get from trading with us. Any limitations those countries place on us hurts *them* more than it hurts *us*. And in other news that everyone knows but no one acknowledges, people will *buy* or *steal* children in other countries because they believe it will get them preferential treatment in the US immigration system. News flash: it doesn't. So the "parent" gets deported, and you've got a "Dreamer" left in the country.[\[1\]](#lvcXN){#cite-lvcXN .citation_link} Other news flash that's not really news: *that's exactly what those actual parents wanted.* If you believe with all your heart that your child will have a better life in the United States than they will in their home country, you'll do anything to get them in, including paying child mules. So for many of those 545 children, their biological parents don't care that there is no parent there for them. They want the foster care system to take them in, because it will still be a better life for them than anything they could have had at home. We can't deport them because we don't know where to send them. Without a parent to claim them, no country will accept them. And yes, it's true, some of those 545 were just misplaced. In any system run by humans, human error is bound to occur. But those children are **all** taken care of. They're not left by the side of the road or "abandoned" or locked in cages or anything else that nonsensical Trump haters will tell you. They join the approximately 500,000 children across the nation in our foster care system.
