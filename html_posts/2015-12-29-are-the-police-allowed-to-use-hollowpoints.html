--- title: '"Are the police allowed to use hollowpoints?"' date: '2015-12-29' tags: - environment - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, as far as I know, most law enforcement agencies still use hollow point ammunition. Here\'s why: Hollowpoint ammunition deforms much more than \"jacketed\" bullets (lead with a jacket of usually copper) when striking soft targets. This does several things: it imparts more of the kinetic energy of the bullet into the target, and prevents the bullet from continuing on through the target and hitting anyone behind the target (known as \"overpenetrating\"); the bullet stops in the intended target. The downside is that it does make a larger wound (which is presumably why the Hague Convention of 1899 banned it) but it is a safer bullet for bystanders, and more effective against a single target. Most agencies I know of use JHP (jacketed hollow points) which are essentially FMJ (full metal jacket) rounds with a hole drilled in the tip.
