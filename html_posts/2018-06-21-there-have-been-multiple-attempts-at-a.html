**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **There have been multiple attempts at a \"good\" immigration policy. In the end there is always outrage (some times more than others). Crowdsourcing here... what would make an actual good immigration policy that is both fair and compassionate?** To be honest [[Ollie](https://www.quora.com/profile/Ollie-Townsend-III)]{.qlink_container}, you don't want what's fair. Because what's fair has to be fair for everyone. "Fair and compassionate" do not make great immigration policies. They make great "open border" policies, and that's the exact opposite of an immigration policy. A good immigration policy is inherently "unfair" because not everyone gets to come in. That is the textbook definition of "unfair." "Fairness" really means being fair to the immigrant, **and** being fair to the American citizens, either born or naturalized. You have to balance both of their needs. To that end, a good comprehensive immigration policy would include **all** of the following: - **Get rid of multiculturalism.** This is another sham of a program foisted upon the American people by Progressives, and it is ruining this country, creating more division instead of more unity. We must re-establish the fact that the United States is a *sovereign nation*, we have a *national identity*, and we have our own view of how to live in the world, different from that of other countries, ***and that's not a bad thing!*** We need to return to the "Great American Melting Pot" concept of naturalization. Require immigrants to learn English. Require immigrants to pass a basic primary school US history test. Require immigrants to swear allegiance to the United States and its Constitution. In case you're unsure of what the "Great American Melting Pot" is, those from my generation learned a lot about the country from *Schoolhouse Rock*. Heck, I memorized the Preamble to the Constitution solely from Schoolhouse Rock. Anyway, here's "The Great American Melting Pot:" [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=01b861&url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v%3D5ZQl6XBo64M){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="youtube.com"}]{.qlink_container} - **Fix the immigration system**. If it takes decades to become a citizen, people are going to look to other means to enter the country. We must reduce the amount of time it takes to complete the immigration process. Part of this is communication with other countries, and their willingness to hand over information about their citizens who want to emigrate. If they don't do it within certain timeframes, we should be cutting off immigration from that country, plain and simple. Let immigrants argue with their host countries about sending information faster. But one way or another, we must shorten the amount of time it takes to become a citizen. - **Fix the border**. We must do everything we can to eliminate illegal immigration. This includes preventing illegal aliens crossing into the country across the southern border. - **Keep better track of visa holders.** The other big source of illegal immigrants is people who overstay their visas. We need to do a better job of integrating those systems which track visa holders with our law enforcement systems and other systems so that we can catch them quicker and repatriate them appropriately. - **End birthright citizenship**. The practice of birthright citizenship is not part of the Constitution; it was established by law. This should be overturned. You should only become a born citizen if you are born to citizens. Being born to illegal aliens in-country should not entitle you to automatic citizenship. - **End businesses hiring illegals.** If the system to track illegals is broken, fix it. We should be able to identify whether or not a person is legally in this country. This takes the incentive away from someone wanting to illegally stay in this country and being able to find work. If you're an employer and you're paying an illegal under the table, we shut down your business, period. Come down hard on employers hiring illegals. - And on that note, **END SANCTUARY CITIES.** Anyone at any level of government harboring known illegals should be dealt with harshly. I have no problem withholding Federal funding for cities which refuse to follow the law. This is yet another incentive for illegals to come into the country: a great place to stay where they know they won't be deported. It's like another country inside of the US. This practice must end, and it must end quickly.
