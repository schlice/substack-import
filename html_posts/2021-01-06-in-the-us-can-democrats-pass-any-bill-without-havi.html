**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In the US, can Democrats pass any bill without having a majority in the Senate?** Yes! A majority in the Senate is not required to pass a bill. You, as a Democrat Senator, must reach consensus with your Republican counterparts, and a bill can certainly be crafted that would pass both Republican and Democrat scrutiny. You need a majority in the Senate (to go along with the majority in the House) to pass legislation that Republicans, by and large, *do not agree with*. In other words, in order to *advance your agenda*, you need a majority. Then, a party-line vote will fall your way. You don't need a majority to pass any bill.
