**Content:** [As I understand it, there is currently no disease model for multiple sclerosis in zebrafish. They do use zebrafish to study the normal myelination process, as well as to test remyelination therapies. While they use zebrafish to study remyelination, they cannot use the same fish to study demyelination. The zebrafish has a very strong and adaptive immune system.\ \ Zebrafish are cheaper to work with than mice, but mice can have an MS-like disease induced in them, called [[experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis (EAE)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=518c15&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experimental_autoimmune_encephalomyelitis){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. This allows scientists to study demyelination processes and treatments to slow this process.]{.rendered_qtext}
