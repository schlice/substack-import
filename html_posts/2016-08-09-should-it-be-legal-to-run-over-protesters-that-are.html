--- title: "Should it be legal to run over protesters that are blocking a highway?" date: "2016-08-09" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: no. Longer answer: Your car is a **lethal weapon**. It can kill people. Before you can run someone over, you have to prove that there was a threat to your life or the lives of the people in your car. Most definitely, if the protestors start smashing your car windows with rocks or tools, by all means defend yourself and your family with whatever means you have at your disposal (which in this case simply means lifting your foot off the brake). What ***should*** be happening is police should be getting tougher on protestsors who block roadways. This is ***patently illegal***. You cannot block a roadway and call it a protest. In fact, you must have a permit to hold a protest on a public street so the road can be closed and traffic re-routed. Additionally, if the police tell you to leave, you must leave or you can be arrested for this alone. This is not happening because right now, police chiefs are *afraid*. I get that. But when do we stop being afraid and when do we wake up to the fact that these are not protestors, they are **bullies**? I'd say call in the Fire Department and let them practice putting out fires on these protestors. A few seconds of being blasted by the truck-mounted water cannon will clear them from the highway pretty quickly. It won't hurt them, and if they get hurt and try to sue, they're going to have a hard time explaining why they were standing in the middle of a highway, illegally, attempting to protest without a permit.
