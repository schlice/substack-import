--- title: '"What is the difference between the Air National Guard and the Air Force Reserve?"' date: '2015-09-22' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There is actually a **very big difference** between the Air National Guard and the Air Force Reserve. The difference is not in the job they do, since they both fly, fight, and win. The difference is who they work for.\ \ The Air Force Reserve is the Reserve branch for the United States Air Force. They work for the **Commander-in-Chief**. These reservists are in different mission categories which allow them to \"drill\" one weekend a month, and a few weeks over the summer with their active units. There are some Reserve units that are full-time, which does make things confusing.\ \ The Air National Guard is actually not a part of the active US Air Force. The National Guard (all branches) works for the **Governor** of the state in which they serve. The Air National Guard defends the residents of that state.\ \ When I was in the USAFR, I was an Individual Mobility Augmentee (IMA), which means that I filled in for an active-duty billet when that person was deployed. If the person for whom I was covering were to be deployed, I would be brought onto temporary active duty to fill that slot full time until that person returned. This is done in critical-manned billets that have to have a person filling that slot at all times. I still trained with my unit, but I would have been brought to active duty had my billet needed backfill.]{.rendered_qtext}
