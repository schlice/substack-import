--- title: "Is it considered unethical to quit a job when facing termination without the option of receiving unemployment benefits?" date: "2024-11-14" tags: ["policy-analysis"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. If you're facing termination without the option of receiving unemployment benefits, then you are being terminated ***for cause***. This means you have done something seriously wrong, something which violated the terms of your employment agreement. Stealing, misconduct, poor performance, or some other violation of company policy are all examples of terminations for cause. Termination without cause means that you didn't do anything wrong, but you were terminated anyway. Company restructuring which also eliminated your position, company downsizing, or an economic downturn can all result in termination without cause. Termination for cause generally makes you ineligible for unemployment benefits. Similarly, quitting generally makes you ineligible for unemployment benefits. Generally, you can only receive unemployment benefits if you are terminated without cause. With all this in mind, if you quit in lieu of being fired, it's not unethical. You're being fired anyway because of something you did wrong. Quitting in lieu of being fired doesn't change the ethical calculation at all, other than to publicly admit that you knew you did something wrong and you quit before the company could get around to firing you. You will still be listed as quitting in lieu of termination for cause, if anyone asks your former employer whether or not you left on "good terms."
