**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What can you do if you don\'t want to sign a mandatory arbitration clause in a cellphone or other utility contract (requiring disputes be handled this way)?** You don't sign the contract. **Is there an alternative besides not signing up for the service?** Of course. There are always other carriers, right? Especially for utilities in your community, right? That would be normally true, if they all didn't do it. And they all do. Until the FCC gets off of its collective bums and forces carriers to no longer include forced arbitration clauses in their contracts, this practice will continue. But in some cases, courts have looked disfavorably at those clauses, finding that they unreasonably restricted consumers' right to get viable relief. In the Cingular Wireless case of 2007, Washington State allowed customers to file a class action lawsuit because it found that the forced arbitration clause was "grossly unfair and blocked the customers from obtaining meaningful relief."
