**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the United States, you can sue anyone for anything. The question becomes whether or not you might succeed in your suit. You can sue a school district for emotional distress. You can sue if the school district was responsible for victimizing students or staff. You can also sue if the school district was at fault or did nothing to prevent emotional and physical injuries. In most states, you can file a lawsuit against a private school within two years from the injury\'s date. You can file a lawsuit against a public school within three years.
