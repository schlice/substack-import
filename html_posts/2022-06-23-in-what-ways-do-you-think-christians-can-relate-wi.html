--- title: "In what ways do you think Christians can relate with Muslims?" date: "2022-06-23" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Christians and Muslims are both human. They both live on Earth. They have rights as human beings. Oh, did you mean relating in terms of religion? Well, they both believe that their religion is right and the other guy's religion is wrong. They each believe that they will receive salvation and the other guy will not. So they can both relate in that way. They both teach the existence of angels and demons. But in terms of their *religions*, they are pretty much diametrically opposed. Christianity believes things that Islam does not. For example, Christianity believes that Christ died and was resurrected three days later. Islam believes that Christ never died. Islam believes that Christ was a prophet (a good one), and not deity. But as C.S. Lewis points out, if Christ was not deity, then he was a really *bad* prophet, because he claimed to be God. If he wasn't what he claimed to be, then he was either crazy, or a liar. Hardly a good prophet. We should all accept and respect people who have different beliefs than we do. But we should not accept the insane notion that all religious beliefs are true. Because contradictory religious beliefs exist, and mutually exclusive beliefs cannot all be true, we should not pretend that they are. In fact, it could be dangerous to do so. If Christianity is true, then it's dangerous to your eternal destiny to not be Christian. Similarly, if Islam is true, then it's dangerous to your eternal destiny to not be Muslim. \ Original question: In what ways do you think Christians can relate with Muslims?
