**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the United States, no. A president is limited to two terms in office under the 22nd Amendment, a total of eight years. These eight years do not have to run concurrently, but a single president is limited to eight years. [[Franklin D. Roosevelt](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c6f2c0&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} was the only president to serve more than two terms. The 22nd Amendment was ratified in 1951.
