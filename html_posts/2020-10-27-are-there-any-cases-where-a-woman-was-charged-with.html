**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are there any cases where a woman was charged with neglect for specifically refusing to breastfeed a baby?** No. Examples of physical neglect are when a child is not fed repeatedly or for a long period, adequate health care is not provided by the parents, the child does not have appropriate clothing and footwear, or even when parents drive intoxicated with a drug or substance that endangers the child\'s safety. [\[1\]](#tBhny){#cite-tBhny .citation_link} Nothing in the neglect standard *requires* a child to be breastfed. While breastfeeding within the first two years of life is *encouraged* due to its positive influence on both mother and child,[\[2\]](#Ynavy){#cite-Ynavy .citation_link} it is not *required* to breastfeed a child. In order to not be neglectful, you simply must *feed* the child. Infant formula is plentiful, easily accessible, and provides a nutritionally complete substitute for breast milk.
