**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are businesses with \"Mandatory Face Mask\" policies violating Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act ("ADA")?** In general, no. Businesses are required to provide **reasonable** accommodations to persons with disabilities. Most businesses have determined that allowing anyone to enter without a face mask is an unreasonable risk to everyone else in the store. Instead the business provides *other, more reasonable* accommodations, which don't include coming into the store without a mask: curbside delivery, car-side delivery, free home delivery, etc. Most businesses are extremely willing to work with anyone who cannot wear a mask, so long as they don't come in to the store. Additionally, it is generally accepted that a person who cannot wear a face mask can instead wear a face shield and it will provide reasonable protection against their droplets. Not all businesses allow this workaround, though. So long as the business provides reasonable accommodations, they do not have to provide ***every*** accommodation requested by the disabled person.
