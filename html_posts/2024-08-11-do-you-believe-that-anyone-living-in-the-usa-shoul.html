--- title: '"Do you believe that anyone living in the USA should be able to vote or should that be restricted to only US citizens? If the latter, should ID be mandatory to enforce this?"' date: '2024-08-11' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Voting should be restricted to US citizens.** **ID should be mandatory to enforce this.** \ This shouldn't even be a question. The people who are citizens of the United States should be the people who determine the direction and leadership of the United States, not just someone visiting on hoiday, or someone on a student visa, or someone who may have crossed the border illegally, or someone awaiting an asylum hearing, or any other classification of person lawfully or unlawfully present in the United States. The only people who should vote are **citizens**. It's one of the privileges of being a citizen. \ Since people seem to not care about our laws, then unfortunately we must resort to voter registration and identification to prove that you're at the right voting place, and you are registered to vote, and you are the person you claim you are. Government identification isn't a big lift. You require government identification for so many different things in life in the United States that it's actually challenging to live *without* it. And yes, it is absolutely true that some people do not have state issued ID cards or driver's licenses. But that number is *vanishingly rare*. I could set up a help booth and literally give assistance to anyone who doesn't have an ID card, and I guarantee I could make it happen one way or another. There are so many provisions to get ID cards to people that there is virtually no way that it can't be done. The trope that requiring ID is discriminatory has absolutely no basis in reality.
