**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why I\'m I going to hell if I don\'t worship Jesus Christ?** The answer is a little more nuanced than "if you don't worship Jesus, you're going to hell." It requires some definitions, particularly a definition of the word "worship" as well as the word "hell." Christians generally define a point on the spectrum of belief in order to say that a person has achieved salvation. The most often quoted biblical reference is John 3:16, but there are many others: > *For God so loved the world that He gave his one and only Son, so that > whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life.* This, among others, establishes that "point" which Christians believe is the point between salvation and "not salvation:" belief that Jesus Christ is the son of God and that he died on the cross to forgive our sins. The Bible is very clear on this point. A little later on in John (14:2--6), Jesus says the following: > *"My Father's house has many rooms; if that were not so, would I have > told you that I am going there to prepare a place for you? And if I go > and prepare a place for you, I will come back and take you to be with > me that you also may be where I am. You know the way to the place > where I am going."* > > *Thomas said to him, "Lord, we don't know where you are going, so how > can we know the way?"* > > *Jesus answered, "****I am the way and the truth and the life. No one > comes to the Father except through me****."* It's clear that Jesus says that the way to salvation is through Him; more specifically, belief in Him. Therefore, "worship" means "believing that Jesus Christ is your personal Savior who died on the cross to atone for your sins." Christianity is the only major religion which believes that it is grace alone which saves you; good works are incidental to the grace received. You cannot save yourself. There is nothing you can "do" which will *earn* you a place in God's eternal kingdom. As Christians, we know that we are sinners and "fall short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23). It is *only* through the grace of God that you are saved, and that salvation comes specifically from believing that Jesus Christ died on the cross to pay for your sin. When you believe that the blood of Christ has washed you clean, when you believe that Christ died to make all things right between you and God, it is then that you have your salvation. The definition of "hell," in most contexts, is not a fiery pit of damnation which smells of fire and brimstone, but an eternal separation from the love of God; an eternal rejection of the gift that Jesus so freely offered you. He wants nothing in return for this gift. He has given you the gift of salvation; all you need to do is *accept* that gift.
