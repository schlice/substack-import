--- title: "How do I request special assistance at airport security for my disability?" date: "2022-06-27" tags: ["veteran-benefits"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} That depends on your disability, and what special assistance you may require. First thing you should do is to contact the TSA directly through their **TSA Cares** program: - 1-855-787-2227 (Federal Relay 711) Weekdays: 8 a.m. to 11 p.m. ET - [[TSA-ContactCenter@tsa.dhs.gov](mailto:TSA-ContactCenter@tsa.dhs.gov){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"}]{.qlink_container} Weekends/Holidays: 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. ET Make sure you contact the TSA no earlier than **72 hours before you travel** to request the assistance of a **Passenger Support Specialist** at the checkpoint. That person can assist you with your specific needs. If available, the airport will direct you to a special TSA Disability Screening line. You can also download a [[TSA Disability Notification Card](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a533ba&url=https://www.tsa.gov/sites/default/files/disability_notification_card_508.pdf){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="tsa.gov"}]{.qlink_container} which you can present to TSA agents at the beginning of your screening to help get you the assistance you need during your screening. \ Original question: How do I request special assistance at airport security for my disability?
