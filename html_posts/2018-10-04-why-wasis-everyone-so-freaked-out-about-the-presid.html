**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why was/is everyone so freaked out about the presidential alert?** It's a general freakout about Trump. You know that Trump *loves* Twitter. You also know that no other president in history has talked as plainly and as openly directly to the American people through a social media platform as Trump has. Therefore, some people worry that the Presidential Alert would become "Trump's personal Twitter Line" directly to people that they can't turn off. Not only is this ridiculous (he does just fine with Twitter), but it's untrue. The Presidential Alert is one of three categories of messages which the government can send through the Wireless Emergency Alert system, part of the nation's Emergency Alert System (which used to be called the Emergency Broadcast System). It preempts all other communications, and the WEA system itself can preempt other communications on your device to get through. But, the system can only be actually activated as a "last-ditch" communication method if the President can't get to the media. Additionally, its use is controlled by three different groups: FEMA, the FCC, and NOAA. Therefore, so long as Trump has access to Twitter, you're not going to be seeing the WEA become Trump's personal text message service to your phone.
