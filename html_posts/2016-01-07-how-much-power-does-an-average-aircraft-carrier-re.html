--- title: "How much power does an average aircraft carrier require?" date: "2016-01-07" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} An aircraft carrier requires very large amounts of power. Specifically, they require enough power to drive the steam catapults to launch the aircraft, drive the steam turbines which drive the propellers, and to drive the steam-powered generators to produce all of the electricity for the entire vessel. Since the US Navy has the lion\'s share of the world\'s aircraft carriers, we\'ll use US aircraft carriers as the example. All currently-operating US aircraft carriers have PWR (pressurized water reactor) nuclear power plants as the source of all of their power. In the larger submarines and surface ships, the reactors produce in excess of 500 MWt (thermal megawatts), and most US surface ships are powered by two or more of these reactors. US submarines are usually powered by one.
