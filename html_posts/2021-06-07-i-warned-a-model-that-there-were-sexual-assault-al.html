--- title: '"I warned a model that there were sexual assault allegations made against the photographer she was working with. I shared the stories other models shared about the abuse. I never said he was a rapist. Could I be sued for slander?"' date: '2021-06-07' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, you can be sued for slander. The question is, can he collect damages. In order for the photographer to collect damages from you, he has to prove that your statement: - was false, - affected his business, trade or profession, and - implies he committed a crime, or leads to the conclusion that he has a loathsome disease (such as a sexually transmitted disease). But here's the trick: **the absolute defense to slander or libel charges is the truth.** If, in fact, the photographer had sexual assault allegations made against him, and those allegations can be proven, then the statement you made was not false. Slander (spoken) or libel (printed) require **knowingly passing on a false statement**. If the statement is provably true, then slander does not apply. Slander also only applies when the statement is a false statement *of fact*. If you are passing on your opinion, such as if you told the model that you believed the photographer was unprofessional, or you didn't like working with him, these are not slanderous as they are statements of opinion, not of fact. But because you said that the photographer had a sexual assault allegation made against him (which is a statement of fact), this part does not apply.
