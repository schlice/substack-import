--- title: '"What are some ways that an atheist can contribute positively to a religious society?"' date: '2023-09-16' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} - Be a good human. Treat everyone with respect, despite the fact that some of those religious people wrongly don't treat you with respect. It is a sad fact of modern religious practice that many religious people overlook the person and see the sin (remember, they see atheism as a sin). - Along those same lines, don't denigrate the whole because of the actions of a few. Those few will definitely sour you to the whole group of theists as a whole. The religion itself is not to blame; people's understanding of the religion is what causes that distaste. And I can understand it: even being a religious person, I see their actions and it is disappointing to me too. - Don't get frustrated when the religious try to convert you. They will, but they are quite ham-handed about the whole thing, because most of them have never had any formalized training on evangelism. They do what they think is right without any knowledge, so please be patient with them. Their intentions are noble even if their actions are...questionable. - Along the same "be a good human" lines, participate when you can. If there are volunteer efforts ongoing (run by the religious), volunteer if you can. You don't have to be religious to demonstrate what it means to be a good person.
