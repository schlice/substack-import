**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Nope. I mean he can try, which he may do for publicity, but it won't win. Calling someone a name is not against the law. It's protected speech under the First Amendment. Defamation of character has a pretty high bar to meet before it can really be seriously looked at. She would have to have: - Made a false statement. The statement must actually be false; truth is always a defense to defamation. She's got this one nailed; I doubt she has details of a crime when prosecutors for years have been unable to find them. And if she does know something and didn't tell anyone, she's going to be looked at pretty skeptically. - Broadcast that statement. Clearly, if this question is being asked, she's done that. - Acted with actual malice. Because Trump is a public figure, the standards for defamation are higher. It would have to be proved that she acted with *actual malice,* meaning that she knew the statement was false or that she acted with reckless disregard of the truth. - Damaged his reputation. This false statement would have to be shown to have been harmful to Trump's reputation. But given Trump's...colorful...reputation, I doubt AOC could even move the needle on that front. So while he *can* sue her, he won't, because he won't waste his time on the likes of her, and even if he did, there's no way in The Hot Placeâ„¢ that he'd win.
