**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} We will see illegal abortions increase. But how *unsafe* exactly they are is definitely in question. The short answer: *abortion has become safer over the last 60 years, and it's not because it was legalized.* Abortion has become safer due to advances in antibiotics and medical practices. The *myth* of "back-alley, coat-hanger abortions" was exactly that, a myth, promulgated by the people trying the hardest to demonize illegal abortions. Stanley Henshaw, who from 1979 to 2013 researched abortion statistics at the Guttmacher Institute, said the following: > In the 1960s, the officially recorded number of deaths from illegal > induced abortion was under 300 per year. While there were undoubtedly > other unreported abortion deaths, it is unlikely that the actual > number was over 1,000. The figure of 5,000 to 10,000 is reasonable for > the 1930s, when there were probably more abortions and less effective > treatment of complications. In my opinion, if *Roe v. Wade* were > overturned, women would turn to relatively safe medications that can > be purchased over the Internet. There would be some deaths but > probably not as many as there were in the > 1960s.[\[1\]](#utHqd){#cite-utHqd .citation_link} The safety of abortions is because of advances in medicine, not because of legalization. We will still see illegal abortions happen, but they will likely be by pill, not by coat-hanger. We will still see some deaths, but not even near 300 per year.
