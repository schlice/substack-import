**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the US, this would be considered spam, which is **opt-in**. You can only send commercial email to people who have opted-in to receiving email from you. As long as the people to whom you are sending email have already consented to being on your list, you are fine to email them according to the frequency you informed them of when they joined your list. If, however, you have a random list of email addresses, you cannot email them. In the US, e-mail is covered by the [[CAN-SPAM Act](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=09ce7d&url=https://www.ftc.gov/tips-advice/business-center/guidance/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ftc.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. I would strongly suggest you read it over carefully before you send any email.
