**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It doesn\'t seem as if anyone answered the question from a purely legal (US) perspective, and despite all the good-natured who-I-might-morally-desire-to-help-first answers, it really falls down like this: - I have no legal duty to render aid. I\'m not a doctor. I\'m not an EMT. Yes I\'m trained as a first responder, but I'm only protected by Good Samaritan laws, and we know those are not foolproof. If I treat the rider, and I hurt him in the process, Good Sam laws or not, I'm likely going to be the target of a negligence lawsuit. Doesn't sound like the perfect recipe for a happy day to me. - The motorbike damaged my property (my dog). Dogs are not people, they are property under the law. My property, which **is** my responsibility, was damaged. Therefore, I'm going to tend to my damaged property (my dog) while I take pictures of the bike and its license plate so that I can prepare to file suit to recoup the costs to repair my damaged property (i.e. my vet bills caused by the rider, whether through accident or negligence), and maybe, if I have time, as I'm taking my dog to the vet, I'll call 911 to report that a motorbike hit something and the rider fell off and probably needs an ambulance.
