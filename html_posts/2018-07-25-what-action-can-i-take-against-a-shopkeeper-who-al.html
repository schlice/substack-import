**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What action can I take against a shopkeeper who allows people to drink in front of his shop, and also serves snacks to those who drink? The police officers might be helping him in running his shop.** You didn't mention where this is happening. Since I know the laws in the US, I'll address this as if it is happening in the US. In order to be able to do something about it, you have to be an *aggrieved party*. In other words, there must be some *demonstrable harm* caused by what's going on. For example, you own the neighboring shop, and the actions are causing you to lose business. If you're not experiencing some demonstrable harm from the action, it's unlikely that your suit would go anywhere as you do not have *standing* to bring a suit. Secondly, you have to ensure that you're targeting the right person. Unless the shopkeeper is responsible for enforcing laws on the street in front of his shop, he probably is not the right person to address. You should be talking to the city, asking them why they are not enforcing the laws.
