--- title: "Does Aubagio, a multiple sclerosis medication, cause tooth loss?" date: "2022-10-11" tags: ["health-autoimmune"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is that it hasn't been fully investigated. There may be a relationship between taking Aubagio and some periodontal conditions which may have pre-existed in some patients. This is unclear at the moment. But the hypothesis today is that the action of teriflunomide allows certain types of infections to flourish. This is not uncommon in MS drugs. There are indeed MS drugs which suppress the immune system in a very specific way which allows some infections and viral activity to flourish (I'm thinking of Tysabri and the JC virus). So it is not outside of the realm of possibility that teriflunomide allows certain endodontic bacterial infections to flourish, specifically ones that cause pulpitis, and allows other endodontic infections and periapical reactions to occur unchecked, which can be followed by teeth loss. It is imperative, therefore, that anyone starting or taking Aubagio stay current on their dental health. Checkups and cleanings every six months should be the minimum. Any endodontic issues should be dealt with right away, or the risk of tooth loss may be accelerated. Always practice good oral hygiene, as it is posited by the NIH that "\[p\]oor oral hygiene is common in MS patients."[\[1\]](#wEHsF){#cite-wEHsF .citation_link} I'm not sure where they came up with that statistic, but they said it.
