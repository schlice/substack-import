**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can I convince my HOA to allow me to make an extension to my residence driveway to accommodate for a wheelchair for my wife? They denied my request.** Let them know that Federal law already allows you to extend your driveway to make it accessible for a wheelchair and an HOA rule preventing this is in violation of Federal law. You were simply advising the HOA that you were making these changes, not seeking their approval. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act, and the Architectural Barriers Act all mandate accessibility standards for those in wheelchairs. Local ordinances (including HOA architectural rules) cannot limit what Federal law allows. They can make it *more* accessible, not *less.* That said, there are some changes they *can* mandate. For example, they can tell you that if you have two entrances to your house (a garage entrance and a front entrance) you have to put the ramp on the garage entrance. That would be a restriction they can make since you have two front-of-house entrances. Unless they can show that your driveway is already accessible enough for a wheelchair, they can't deny your change. If your driveway, for example, is wide enough for two cars, they can deny your request and say that your driveway is already wide enough to accommodate one car and a wheelchair load/unload zone; you don't have to be accommodated in order to fit two cars side by side as well as loading/unloading space for a chair. However if you only have a single-car driveway, you can extend it to add a loading/unloading pad for the wheelchair.
