**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As far as I can find, Trump never said that he would lose. What he *has* done is backed off of his usual rhetoric of Biden being a "low-IQ individual" who is "the worst, most incompetent, and most corrupt president" in the nation's history. Last I heard, when he was on the *All In* podcast, he did soften his rhetoric on Biden. "I assume he's going to be somebody that will be a worthy debater," he said. "I don't want to underestimate him.\" "Maybe I'm better off losing the debate,\" Trump commented in an interview with *Real America's Voice* earlier this month. "I'll make sure he stays. I'll lose the debate on purpose, maybe I'll do something like that." He also commented during the *All In* podcast about Biden's debate with Paul Ryan in the run-up to the 2012 presidential election: "Well, all I can say is this: I watched him with Paul Ryan, and he destroyed Paul Ryan," said Trump. "So I'm not underestimating him. I'm not underestimating him. It is what it is. We'll see what happens." The rhetoric may be toned down, but the advice is correct. He's walking into the lion's den, into a debate moderated by the anti-Trump network CNN. He rightly posited, "I'll be debating three people instead of one half of a person," at a rally in Racine, Wisconsin last week. Legacy media sources read this situation as Trump setting up excuses in the event he does poorly. But this may very well be because some concerns have been raised by some in his party that he has set the bar so low for Biden that he is sure to exceed it. And it underscores the stakes for both men in a race that has appeared largely static for months.
