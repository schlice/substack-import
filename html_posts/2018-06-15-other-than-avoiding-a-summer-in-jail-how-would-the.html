**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Other than avoiding a summer in jail, how would the effects of the President pardoning Paul Manafort now in June 2018 be different than those of pardoning him after his pending trials a few months later?** As others have stated, the massive difference is whether or not Manafort can be compelled to testify against Trump. If Trump were to pardon Manafort now, Mueller could compel him to testify against Trump, and Manafort would have no 5th Amendment protections against self-incrimination, because he was officially pardoned and cannot be convicted, even if he incriminates himself in his testimony. Since this would be, definitionally, *bad* for Trump, he won't do it. He'll wait for the trial and any possible convictions, and *then* he would consider a Presidential pardon. Most certainly not before.
