**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it morally wrong for a traffic cop to have a quota to fill every month?** I'm reasonably sure that department policies regarding how many tickets you have to write has little to do with morals. Quotas are a bad idea. Officers shouldn't be judged against a pre-set number of tickets, but should be judged on the quality, as well as the quantity, of tickets written. By quantity here, it means that if an officer is supposed to write traffic tickets, a very low quantity could be an indication that he's not doing his job effectively and requires further review. Similarly, a very high number of tickets may also indicate an overzealous traffic officer who maybe needs to settle back a bit. This is why workers have supervisors: to keep them on the right track. A pre-set number you have to "hit" is a poor way to motivate someone to write tickets, because it leads to poor tickets. The only time that any sort of measurement of quantity should ever come into play is for specialized enforcement units, such as a traffic detail specifically deployed to a problem area.
