--- title: '"Why did the US government allegedly forcefeed captives at Guantanamo Bay?"' date: '2015-10-17' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Under the Geneva Convention, which we are holding to as far as treatment of prisoners (even though the combatants do not fall under the legally recognized criteria for inclusion), say that prisoners of war must be provided full medical care while they are prisoners.\ \ Widespread hunger strikes have taken place at Gitmo, and when the chief medical examiner determines that the body has fallen below healthy body mass, he can order force feeding through a nasal tube. Many of the prisoners accepted the force feeding, while others needed to be sedated and/or restrained as they would induce vomiting to attempt to remove the forced nutrition.\ \ Doctors cannot, in good conscience, \"let them die.\" We don\'t allow anyone else in the world to starve themselves to the point of death; we intervene and provide them with mental health counseling. However, when counseling fails, and the person still refuses to eat, doctors can intervene.\ \ Some have called force-feeding \"torture,\" alleging that the military made the process unnecessarily painful and humiliating, a charge which the United States vigorously denies. The US believes that failing to intervene when a person becomes dangerously malnourished is torture, and we may have to forcefully intervene to save that life.]{.rendered_qtext}
