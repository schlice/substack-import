**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you have to time to go to the gym at USAFA,** Yes. While your time is limited and you are indeed very busy, there is some time in your schedule that you can go to the gym. Generally, that's going to be in the afternoon, after classes and after any participatory sports (intramurals or intercollegiate, you'll do one or the other) you may have on that day, until you either go to dinner, or between dinner and Academic Call to Quarters (ACQ). ACQ (or Ac Call) is at 2000, or 8:00 pm. It lasts for three hours every night, and should be used for study time. If you are on Academic Probation, you *must* use that time for academics. If you are not on Academic Probation, the time is, somewhat, more flexible, although you get to structure that time as you see fit. You must be in the Cadet Area, but you can be down at the gym (or in my case sometimes, the ice arena). Summer hours are more limited, but cadets still have eight hours a day (from roughly 11:00 am until 7:00 pm six days a week) to use the gym. **and is it as good as a commercial gym?** It's not as good as a commercial gym. It's **way better.** The gym facilities at the Air Force Academy are *world-class.* In fact, beginning in 2012, the cadet gym got a 50,000 square foot upgrade. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-b854fdd588f52ba053563a0bbd35156e){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-b854fdd588f52ba053563a0bbd35156e" master_w="3600" master_h="2400"} ::: :::: The gym now boasts some of the finest workout equipment available, including a cybex room, climbing walls, cardio equipment, physical fitness testing room, and a brand-new weight room. The Air Force spent \$9.5 million on the upgrade, which was desperately needed, as the facility hadn't been upgraded since it was installed in the late 50s to service no more than 2,200 male cadets. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-47f2a6fcf5de854e81e9306ff167abce){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-47f2a6fcf5de854e81e9306ff167abce" master_w="3600" master_h="2400"} ::: :::: The new facilities include upgraded locker room facilities, and a new swimming pool area and fencing area improvements. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-044b46ba6e60343a7c5d06413e832a67){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-044b46ba6e60343a7c5d06413e832a67" master_w="3600" master_h="2400"} ::: :::: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-9fd3914cb014b823d13f48fbcf43c545){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-9fd3914cb014b823d13f48fbcf43c545" master_w="1000" master_h="638"} ::: :::: The upgrades to the gym included donations from commercial corporations (along with the Academy's traditional food budget) to include a wellness area which includes snacks and sports and nutrition drinks, appropriately dubbed "Falcon Fuel." In mid-2014, the NCAA ruled that student athletes may consume "unlimited" snacks and meals in conjunction with their athletic participation. To that end, cadets have full access to these stations, to provide appropriate nutrition during and after their workouts. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6030d85cde82bf8d532de300fe16e4f8){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6030d85cde82bf8d532de300fe16e4f8" master_w="1250" master_h="600"} ::: :::: (Images courtesy [[USAFA Public Affairs](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3dc48e&url=https://www.usafa.af.mil/USAFANews/Article/428549/cadet-fitness-center/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="af.mil"}]{.qlink_container})
