--- title: '"My neighbor put up a fence and hangs towels on my side of the fence. So I have a ton of stuff hanging on the side of my property over her fence. Is this illegal?"' date: '2021-06-16' tags: - law layout: post ---

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The correct answer, as with the answer to all legal questions, is "it depends." If the fence is precisely on the property line, straddling both properties, then when your neighbor hangs things on the fence and in to your yard, this is technically a tort trespass (not criminal trespassing). If the fence actually is located within the neighbor's property, then hanging things on the fence technically is not a trespass, as the action takes place completely within the neighbor's property. Therefore, your first step is to call your city or county and request a survey to determine the actual location of the property line. If the fence actually is on the property line, and if this trespass truly bothers you, you should start by asking your neighbor to not do that any more. Let them know that your side of the fence is actually your property and you don't want items hanging into your yard. Your next step should be to retain the services of a tort attorney in your area. Let the attorney send them an official cease-and-desist letter, citing the trespass, and what may happen if they don't stop, which will be you suing them for trespass. Applying treatments to your side of the fence which will damage their towels is probably not advisable. Instead of focusing on the problem, perhaps you could assist with some solutions. Perhaps you can help by offering your neighbor some suggestions on hanging towel racks which they could hang on their side of the fence. If you were really motivated to end this practice, you might consider chipping in to help them purchase these racks, or assist them with putting them up. It benefits you as well as them.
