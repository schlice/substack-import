**Content:** [Like the other officers said, very much depends on the orientation when the grab happens, but in all cases is considered life threatening.\ \ When I stand facing someone, I usually have my hands folded in front of me. What you usually don\'t see is my right forearm resting on the grip of my duty weapon to detect a potential grab. Were someone to try to grab my weapon at that point, they\'d end up with a mouthful of elbow and an arrest.\ \ If I were in my car, as Officer Wilson was, I can\'t say I\'d act much differently than he did. I am about Officer Wilson\'s size, so I would consider such an assault a life-threatening one and world likely respond using lethal force.]{.rendered_qtext}
