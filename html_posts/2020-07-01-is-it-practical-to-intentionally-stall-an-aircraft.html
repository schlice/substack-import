**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it practical to intentionally stall an aircraft to execute a fast, controlled descent?** It doesn't fit what you're trying to do. You want a practical method by which you can execute a fast, **controlled** descent. A nose-high stall does not meet this criteria, because for at least a part of it, it is uncontrolled. Granted, you regain control pretty quickly once you start getting airflow over the wings again, but it begins with an altitude-gaining maneuver before airflow over the wings ceases, and that's like going south to go north. if the point is a fast, controlled descent, your best bet is a simultaneous *reduction* of throttle and forward pressure on the control yoke.
