Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the US, there is no paperwork required for someone to be able to drive your car. If you give them permission, they can do so, and so long as they hold a valid driver's license for that type of vehicle, they are even covered by your car insurance¹. However, if the driver does not have a valid driver's license, or if the driver does not have permission to drive your car, your car insurance will likely not cover any accidents or damage. \ ¹ Your mileage may vary (no pun intended). Most insurance companies do cover drivers who you have authorized and who have valid licenses. But there are some exceptions: for example, if the vehicle is being used for business, your personal auto policy won't cover accidents.
