**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Many pro-life people make exceptions for the mother's life but what about medical procedures or medication that the mother needs but could harm the fetus? Ie. chemotherapy for a cancer that isn't guaranteed to be but could be fatal.** We seem to be missing what *is* and *is not* a choice between the life of the baby and the life of the mother. It sounds like the medical procedures the mother needs can probably be delayed until the baby is born. If they can, then it *is not* a choice between the life of the mother and the life of the baby. If they cannot, then it *is*. Doctors take the Hippocratic Oath which begins "First, do no harm." That means no harm to anyone, including the baby. They would only risk the health of the baby if the mother's life was at stake.
