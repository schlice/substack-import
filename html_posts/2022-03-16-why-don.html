**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Ah, an easy question. And there are a few really good answers: **1. Because he claimed he was God.** If he was actually *not* God, and was actually a magician, then he was either a liar, or he was insane. Not really someone worth believing in. A magician has no ability to save my eternal soul. **2. Magicians perform sleight-of-hand, not miracles.** A miracle, as defined by Oxford, is "a surprising and welcome event that is not explicable by natural or scientific laws and is therefore considered to be the work of a divine agency." Sleight-of-hand may *appear* to be miraculous, but it actually can be explained by natural or scientific laws. Miracles, by definition, cannot be explained by science. So if a "miracle" has a scientific explanation, it's not a miracle. For those reasons, it doesn't make sense that he *is not* God. \ Original question: Why don\'t Christians believe Jesus was a magician, as weird though it seems, it is not as big a leap as he was God?
