**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is yes, you are. If it is your biological child, you are responsible for paying child support. Let's take this hypothetical a step further. What if you (man) are raped (by a woman) at gunpoint and she gets pregnant --- do you still have to pay child support? The answer to this is also yes. Remember that child support is not about punishing anyone --- that's what criminal and/or civil charges are for. Child support falls outside of the realm of the courts as it is mandated by the state in order to care for the child. Child support isn't about punishing the man or enriching the woman --- it's about caring for the child. The state has a compelling interest in caring for the child, so they mandate that the two biological entities who created the child pay for that child's upbringing. The only time this is different is if you donated sperm to a sperm bank. Any children created out of that sperm are not associated with you, and you do not owe child support for those children. \ Original question: If a woman stole my semen and pumped it inside her and made a baby, am I still liable to pay for child support?
