**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are police officers legally allowed to lie when you ask them if they\'re a cop? Does this rule change in undercover situations?** This question comes up frequently, but I can't seem to find the old questions. So let me just quickly answer: 1. Police are allowed to lie to you. You are allowed to lie to the police (unless you're talking to a Federal agent, where lying to them is a crime, or if you're lying to aid a criminal). Police are under no legal obligation to tell you the truth. 2. Since police are legally allowed to lie, they are under no obligation to tell you they're undercover if they're undercover.
