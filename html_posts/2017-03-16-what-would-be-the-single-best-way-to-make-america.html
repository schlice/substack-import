**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *What would be the single best way to make America get rid of arms > from civillians \[sic\]?* I added "Hypothetical Scenarios" to your list of topics, because this will **never happen**. You need a Constitutional amendment to overturn rights enumerated in the Bill of Rights (several depending on how you want to hunt down all these guns), and a clear majority of the American people voting for it. Not to mention the vast majority of states have similar rights in their Constitutions, and they are independent, so states would also have to choose to alter their own Constitutions. Since you will never get all of this to happen, it's most definitely a hypothetical. The design of the United States is one that encourages *individual* responsibility for one's own safety. Understanding this, to get rid of civilian ownership of guns, you would have to start by making it so that someone else is responsible for your safety. To wit: you need a police officer for every citizen. So instead of the roughly 800,000 to 1,000,000 police officers we have nationwide now, you would need **150 times** more police officers (approximately 150 million officers). You would then have to assign an officer to every man, woman, and child in the country (the remaining 150 million people), to be their personal bodyguards. In this way, the citizenry would not be responsible for their own safety, and would not need firearms. Maybe someday when our personal robotic assistants (think of the movie *I, Robot* starring Will Smith) can also be our personal bodyguards, we can remove the right of citizens to be armed. Until that day, and until you ***fundamentally change the United States,*** firearms will always be a part of the very foundation and fabric of this country. If you remove a brick from the foundation, the rest of the structure is immeasurably weakened. Firearms are a fundamental part of the foundation of the United States. They cannot be so easily removed without irreparably damaging the entire nation.
