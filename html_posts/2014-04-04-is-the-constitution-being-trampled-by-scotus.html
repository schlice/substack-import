**Content:** [Your question really boils down to what you believe the job of SCOTUS is, and HOW they are supposed to interpret the Constitution.\ \ Some people believe that the Constitution should be interpreted as it is written, drawing from the Framers the context around how to interpret. These people are called \"strict constructioninsts.\" Others believe that the SCOTUS should exercise broad latitude in interpretation, drawing context from modern society so that they can be larger influencers in society. These people are called \"broad constructionists,\" \"loose constructionists,\" or \"liberal constructionists.\" The very first \"construction\" that was either \"strict\" or \"loose\" was the interpretation of the \"Necessary and Proper\" clause (also called the Elastic Clause) of the Constitution:\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} > The Congress shall have Power \... To make all Laws which shall be > necessary and proper for carrying into Execution the foregoing Powers, > and all other Powers vested by this Constitution in the Government of > the United States, or in any Department or Officer thereof. \ Almost immediately after the Constitution was ratified, the Founding Fathers split into these two separate groups (so this is not a new debate by any stretch of the imagination). The strict constructionists were led by Jefferson, the loose constructionists were led by Hamilton. Jefferson believed that only the strict construction of the Necessary and Proper clause would keep federal government small and allow the people the largest access to liberty. Hamilton believed that a much more powerful federal government and much less powerful state governments could propel the country to much greater and faster economic development. Only the broadest, loosest reading of the Necessary and Proper clause would allow Congress to create the laws needed to fulfill Hamilton\'s view of how government should work.\ \ Jefferson\'s Democrat-Republicans and Hamilton\'s Federalists became the nations first two political parties. They have been replaced by Republicans (strict constructionists) and Democrats (loose constructionists). This argument between Jefferson and Hamilton over the Necessary and Proper clause became the first major political dispute in our nation\'s history.\ \ So if you believe that SCOTUS should take a strict interpretation of the Constitution and they are not (or if you are a loose constructionist and SCOTUS is taking a strict view), then they are \"trampling the Constitution.\"
