**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If the police bang on your door and you let them in but they don\'t have a warrant do they then have the right to search because you voluntarily let them in?** The answer, as with all legal answers, is "it depends." Without a search warrant, if you simply allow them in the front door without granting them permission to search, the police cannot "touch" or "move" anything inside of your house in order to find evidence. They can only look with their eyes at the interior spaces of your house. That said, if you left your crack pipe on the coffee table (in "plain sight," the basis for the "plain sight" exception to the warrant requirement), the police can take the pipe into evidence. Additionally, the presence of the pipe provides probable cause to apply for a full search warrant to find the crack cocaine which goes in the pipe. Police can call that evidence in pretty quickly and use it as the basis for a search warrant. Now, if the police come and bang on your door and you let them in and they ask you if they can search your house *and you grant them permission to search*, this obviates the need for a warrant, because you granted permission to search. Note that the homeowner does not need to be the grantor; anyone in the house over 18 years old can grant permission to search the house.
