**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What do you think of the Supreme Court ruling against Virginia's House of delegates for their racial gerrymandering of state districts to benefit Republicans in elections?** First of all, your take on the racial gerrymandering "to benefit Republicans" flies in the face of traditional Democrat talking points. It's simply an incorrect assumption, which is how most of the drive-by media is painting this decision. The maps that the Virginia House of Delegates were arguing to keep were gerrymandered along racial lines, to ensure that at least 55% of every district was black. If it were only to benefit Republicans, the current logic goes, then it should have been drawn to minimize the black vote, no? Because isn't that what evil Republicans do? They want the black man in chains again, right? Regardless, SCOTUS already ruled in 2015 that race cannot be the main factor in drawing state district boundaries. Lawyers for the Virginia House argued that by making sure that each legislative district had at least 55% black voters, the state was protecting their voting power. The Virginia House appealed, and SCOTUS simply ruled, this time, that they did not have standing over the state AG. \"The House, we hold, lacks authority to displace Virginia\'s Attorney General as representative of the state,\" stated Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg in the majority opinion. \"We further hold that the House, as a single chamber of a bicameral legislature, has no standing to appeal the invalidation of the redistricting plan separately from the state of which it is a part.\" Virginia law gives the authority and responsibility to represent the state in court \"exclusively\" to the attorney general. RBG goes on to state that the Virginia House won't be injured by allowing the new district maps to stand, because the entire Virginia Assembly, not just the House, is elected using those maps. The new maps have already been used in Virginia's state primaries. So, my take on this? Fine, if you want your districts to *not* take racial makeups into account, then certain districts are going to lean very heavy towards one race, and certain districts are not, and that race's voting power will be diminished in those districts. I guess you can't make the claim of voter suppression by redistricting in Virginia, because the Democrats ensured that you can suppress the black vote using redistricting.
