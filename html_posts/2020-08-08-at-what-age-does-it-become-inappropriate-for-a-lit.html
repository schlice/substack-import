If men can do it, so can women. However, it's not that simple. We have one other problem. Like it or not, agree with it or not, in the United States (and in many other countries as well), the female breast is not just viewed as a device which feeds babies. The female breast is viewed as a sex object. Clearly, there will be much haranguing about why this is, and whether or not this should be in the first place. **But women are just as guilty of objectifying the breast as men are of enjoying the look of objectified breasts.** You can't simultaneously objectify your own breasts (and unless you dress like an 1800's school teacher, you do) and at the same time declare "Free the nipple!" It's an incongruent position. If it is at all a dual-use organ, it must fall under the same rules as any other sex object. So what age is it inappropriate for a little girl to run around topless? Experts say that children begin to distinguish gender differences around age 3 or 4, and not long after that, they usually begin to feel modesty.[\[1\]](#MWVlP){#cite-MWVlP .citation_link} So if there is an age when children should start being clothed in public, it's around 3 or 4. Additionally, societal norms play a large role in determining when you should no longer allow children to spend time naked. In the United States, people have a sense that the body is very private. The sexualization of nudity, **and a perfectly reasonable fear of pedophiles taking an unhealthy interest in a nude child,** is yet another reason why children should not run around naked. Experts also agree that parents would be wise to teach their children that different situations call for different behaviors. It's not at all inappropriate to teach your children to consider the feelings of others before they act. So while it may be reasonable to let a child run around naked *at home*, when guests come over, it is reasonable to teach your child that consideration of the feelings of the guest should dictate that clothes go back on. A similar attitude should prevail in public. You can disagree until the cows come home whether or not this *should* *be*.
