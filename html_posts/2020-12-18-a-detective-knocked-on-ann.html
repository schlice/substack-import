**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **A detective knocked on Ann's apartment and asked Ann for a statement about an acquaintance. Ann said she'll get back to them and took their card. It's been 5 days. Are they going to come back and talk to Ann or did it blow over?** As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." It depends on how badly the police need her testimony, and how much their case hinges on learning what she knows. It also hinges on how closely Ann is tied to the actions of this acquaintance. Are they asking about something that Ann and the acquaintance may have done together, or something that the acquaintance did and that Ann may have direct knowledge of? If so, the police may be back, and this time, they may ask Ann to come down to the station and give a statement. If, however, they merely know that Ann and her acquaintance are just friends, and they are hoping that the acquaintance told Ann something, then not getting Ann's testimony may not bother them too badly. To that end, if the acquaintance didn't tell Ann anything, she should tell the police as much so that they do leave her alone. They may be hanging on to some false hope that Ann can help them make their case if they don't hear anything from her. Ann would remain on the list of possible corroborating sources, and if they start running into dead ends, they may try to run down those possible sources.
