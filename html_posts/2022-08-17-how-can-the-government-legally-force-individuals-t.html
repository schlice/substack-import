**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is an **excellent** question! When we mandated the vaccine, we forced people to do something that only benefited them, not anyone else. Sure, at a base level, getting vaccinated may have contributed to fewer hospitalizations, so it may have reduced some of the load on the hospitals, but by the time mass vaccination was rolling out, hospitalizations were already on the decline. **So it only makes sense** to continue the logic. I mean, if we forced people to do something for their own good *once*, why can't we *do it again?* There are **SOOOOOO MANY** areas in which we can force people to do the most healthy things: - A nationwide moratorium on all sugar, in all forms. Sorry baking community, but your stuff is unhealthy. Learn how to use natural products like fruit juices, stevia and honey to sweeten things. Processed sugar will go the way of the internal combustion engine. We can literally cut the rate of type-2 diabetes in half with one law. - Literally shut down every fast-food restaurant in the country (with a few carefully selected exceptions, and even those would have their menus and food offerings closely scrutinized). Any unhealthy eating outside of the home causes a drain on the healthcare system. - Every restaurant in America will have to have their menu approved by the government, and any restaurant which cannot modify its menu to meet strict health guidelines will be immediately and unceremoniously closed down. - Then, to ensure that people are actually eating correctly, we can install cameras in their kitchens so we can see what they are feeding their children, and we can send CDC goons immediately to their houses if they try to feed anything unhealthy. It is for our good, after all, because unhealthy people are a drain on the healthcare system. - Everyone gets a government-issued Fitbit and you must wear it and it must connect back to the government. You must get 10,000 steps a day and you must raise your heart rate to workout levels three times a week. If you fail to do this, the government carts you off to "health camp" where you will work directly with government trainers for a week-long boot camp where you will learn the finer points of push-ups. And the benefits don't stop there! This will literally spur new technology: - In-body calorie sensors to detect your caloric intake and warn you (and the government) when calories in exceed calories burned - In-body sensors which determine your level of exercise and notify the government if you don't get enough There's just so many things we can do, my mind is racing! I can't write them down fast enough! \ For the uninitiated, the preceding was *sarcasm*. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-7112f861d15399ba1d75a959c4460098){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-7112f861d15399ba1d75a959c4460098" master_w="677" master_h="307"} ::: :::: As I was writing, I literally felt like what I imagine Orwell felt like when writing *1984*. Again, for everyone else, there is literally **no way in Hades** that we would ever let any of that stuff happen, not here in the United States. It literally only happened during COVID because it was so new and so unknown. But not anymore. We know what COVID is. We know what it does. We also know what the "vaccines" do and more importantly, what they *don't.* But we went along with it during the pandemic because...it was a pandemic (or the closest thing we've had to one in decades). In short, **you can't mandate someone be healthy.** At best, you can provide "incentives" for people to become healthier. Perhaps you can tax sugary drinks at a higher level than non-sugar drinks...oh wait, we already do this. Perhaps you can move forward, and give tax breaks when you complete fitness programs. Actually, most employers already do this, because it saves them money when their employees are healthier. And it makes them even more money when their employees are working and not out sick. My employer even buys my Fitbit. Maybe the government could think about tax breaks for fast food companies which improve their menus; lower sodium, sugar, empty carbs and fat, and increase minerals and vitamins, fiber, and greens. But *forcing* people to be healthy is the first step towards *totalitarianism*, and we will vigorously defend against that. \ (EDIT) I read through my answer, and I heard you screaming at your monitor. You were saying, "Yes, but how do we control the spiraling costs of healthcare? Can't we reduce the drains on our healthcare system?" Yes we can, but those drains are not *patients*. The vast majority of those drains come directly from *government intervention*, **exactly the kind of intervention you wish to impose on the public.** The next largest drain on the healthcare system comes from **healthcare insurance companies,** which don't actually offer health *insurance*, but *pre-paid healthcare plans*. Fixing that would greatly reduce the drain on our healthcare system. \ Original question: How can the government legally force individuals to be healthy in America and not eat like pigs, we forced a vaccine on people, why not health and fitness for those who are a drain on the healthcare system?
