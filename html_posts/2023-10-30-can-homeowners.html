**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer, as with all legal answers, is "it depends." And what it depends on are the rules you have in your Governing Documents. If your HOA can dictate what kind of car you are allowed to have on the property, if you have a disallowed car on the property, you can be fined, even if it is not directly visible from the street. For example, many of my corner lot neighbors have fully fenced-in back yards, and gates wide enough to fit a car which can be entered from the side street. If you were to park a prohibited car there (not visible, not in violation of city ordinance) the HOA can still fine you. The Governing Documents are essentially a contract between you and the HOA.
