**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should "The Notorious RBG" (a.ka. Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg) and her anti-Trump bias be allowed to participate if The Schiff Show somehow ends up at the Supreme Court?** Ignoring the clear bias in your own question (you might think about removing the plank from your own eye before you worry about the splinter in your brother's eye), I'm curious how you think an impeachment will end up at the Supreme Court. Impeachment is a political process, not a legal one. The Supreme Court has already determined that it has no role in impeachments as they are purely political, not judicial. No one in the House or the Senate can ask the courts to weigh in on the results of the impeachment, as it is entirely up to the Congress to prosecute an impeachment. There may be questions of law which rise to the level of the Supreme Court; in Nixon's impeachment, there were questions about executive privilege over his taped recordings of Oval Office conversations, and that question regarding subpoenas *was* a legal question. But SCOTUS was not involved with the *actual* impeachment or Senate trial in Nixon's case; they addressed the narrow question of legality of the subpoenas and whether or not the White House was obligated to produce the information. While the Supreme Court can judge specific legal questions, in this case the Democrats in the House chose to ignore the courts and run the impeachment on their own. They issued subpoenas, the White House exerted executive privilege, and the House let it drop. They did not pursue the subpoenas at all. They just ignored conducting a full and complete investigation in favor of expediency.
