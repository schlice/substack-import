**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do private Catholic schools have to follow state laws about what has to be taught** In the United States, students must be taught to a certain level. This level is determine by standardized testing, not by "what is taught." States largely allow districts to develop their curricula, and to teach it as they see fit, so long as student achievement on standardized testing remains high. Private schools are generally not bound by any curriculum rules which might be set by a school district, although their students must still meet a minimum average on standardized testing. (EDIT) Thanks to [[John Milhaven](https://www.quora.com/profile/John-Milhaven)]{.qlink_container} for pointing out that I forgot my usual legal tagline: every state is different. Even though many states can operate similarly, all states can administer their education programs exactly as they choose, within Federal law, which does not specify that a school must teach any one specific thing.
