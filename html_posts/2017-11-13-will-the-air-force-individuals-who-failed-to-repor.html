**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: **no.** Reason: **they were not obligated to do so.** In actuality, he got a Bad Conduct Discharge, and the military is not mandated to report those. It's *optional*, so they did nothing wrong. They are only *required* to report Dishonorable Discharges into NICS. It's optional because the two legal systems have different burdens of proof and different standards of evidence (i.e. in the military system, you are guilty until proven innocent in a court-martial). Therefore, not all discharges are reported to the civilian judicial system, and only Dishonorable Discharges *must* be reported and entered into NICS. If you want change, you would have to re-try every person who gets court-martialed and gets a Bad Conduct Discharge in the civilian legal system after they get out of military prison. It's not double jeopardy to do so, but since the standards of evidence and burden of proof are different between the two systems, they must be re-tried in a civilian court before you can hold them to the same domestic violence punishments as you would a non-military defendant.
