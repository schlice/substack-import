--- title: '"Should homeowners have the power to stop their neighbors from building a new home because they do not like the idea?"' date: '2024-06-10' tags: - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. **In general**, homeowners should not be able to stop other homeowners from building new homes. The calculus changes when the homes are all within an area controlled by an HOA. In that case, it is the Governing Documents which control whether or not a homeowner can build a new home. Approval for the home is up to the Architectural Review Committee (a committee under the HOA), and if they do not approve it because its design or colors or layout does not comport with the rest of the community, then this level of control is acceptable. Beyond the example of being within an HOA, if you are a homeowner, you get to do whatever is allowed by your local government's zoning rules. So there's always someone who will control what you do on your property. Unless you have an HOA though, that won't be your neighbors.
