Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the U.S. federal government have the power to create plebiscites?** Can the government create a plebiscite? Yes they can. Can they create a plebiscite for anything they like? **No**. For example, can the federal government create a plebiscite to elect the next President? **No.** Presidential elections are governed by the Constitution. Can they *use* a plebiscite to determine national opinion? Sure, but they still are required to follow the Constitutional process to elect the President. The plebiscite cannot elect the President. In short, in any case where a voting method is **prescribed** by the Constitution, the Federal government cannot overrule that prescribed method with a plebiscite.
