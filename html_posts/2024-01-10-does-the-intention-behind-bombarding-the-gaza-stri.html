**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So first of all, this comparison comes from the Gaza Media Office, so you should already take it with a grain of salt. That office reported on January 4th, 2024, that Israel had dropped explosives with a *total weight* across everything dropped of over 65,000 tons, which "is more than the weight and power of three nuclear bomb dropped on the Japanese city of Hiroshima (during World War II).\" Since the comparison to Hiroshima was intended to be inflammatory rather than informative, it can be pretty much ignored. Besides, it's a false comparison. Totaling up the weight of all the bombs across an entire campaign and comparing it to a single bomb dropped on one city is a pointless comparison designed only to inflame readers. Next, they claimed that about \"two-thirds of the bombs and missiles \... are unguided and imprecise, commonly known as dumb bombs.\" They claimed that the use of such bombs indicates \"the deliberate targeting of indiscriminate and unjustified killing by the occupation, a clear and explicit violation of international law and various international conventions.\" Understand what they're trying to tell you here: the use of conventional munitions violates international law and "various international conventions." It's interesting because the United Nations Office for Disarmament Affairs actually has a Convention on "Certain Conventional Weapons," which is actually the shortened title for "The Convention on Prohibitions or Restrictions on the Use of Certain Conventional Weapons Which May Be Deemed to Be Excessively Injurious or to Have Indiscriminate Effects." Aha! This is exactly what Hamas is talking about! Except it doesn't ban dumb bombs. There are four main categories of weapons considered to be excessively injurious or to have indiscriminate effects: - Protocol I: Prohibits the use of weapons that employ fragments not detectable in the human body by X-ray - Protocol II: Regulates the use of landmines, booby-traps and similar devices - Protocol III: Limits the use of incendiary weapons - Protocol IV: Prohibits the use of laser weapons specifically designed to cause permanent blindness As you can see, conventional munitions are not called out as a type of weapon which produces "excessive injury or which have indiscriminate effects." Okay, but can't traditional, conventional weapons be used in a way which causes "excessive injury" or used "indiscriminately?" Yes they can, but this isn't what the Gaza Media Office is alleging. They are alleging that the use of dumb bombs is violative of international treaties, and clearly it is not. Regardless, let's be generous and look at whether or not Israel's use of conventional bombs is "excessive" or "indiscriminate." Even in late December, US President Joe Biden accused Israel of using "indiscriminate" bombing. But is it really true? Believe it or not, Politico summed up the truth: > Whether it is to protect the lives of Israeli soldiers, accelerate the > end of the war or send a message to Hamas that it will either release > hostages or face certain death, Israel's willingness to liberalize the > rules of engagement in Gaza isn't surprising. This doesn't mean, > however, that the bombing campaign or its tactics are illegal, > punitive or malicious. And to call it indiscriminate fails to grasp > the extensive lengths gone to to minimize civilian casualties, > especially considering Hamas' "human shield" policy. > > While Israeli battlefield tactics may run counter to strategic war > aims, calling its bombing campaign indiscriminate is a > mischaracterization of both the IDF's conduct and its > intentions.[\[1\]](#qJKCH){#cite-qJKCH .citation_link} The IDF is a professional fighting force. They use extensive planning to minimize civilian casualties and achieve the greatest military successes. Hamas is also keenly aware that they have no chance of defeating Israel on the battlefield. For them, "shaping public opinion in Israel, around the world and especially in America is their strongest weapon --- and the West's greatest vulnerability. Losing a battalion of the al-Qassim Brigade is a small price to pay for Hamas if worldwide media can film the prisoners surrendering, stripped naked and humiliated." This is why Hamas is so aggressive in using human shields and deploying weapons in homes and hospitals. Every IDF military victory they can paint as a human rights travesty (which is eagerly and blindly accepted by Western media) is a "win" for Hamas. In other words, Hamas' only hope of victory is in the court of public opinion, delivered through half-truths and outright subterfuge.
