--- title: "Can the Supreme Court overrule legislation?" date: "2022-08-07" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In a way, yes. The Supreme Court's job is to determine ***Constitutionality*** of laws. So if a law is passed, but that law violates the Constitution, the Supreme Court can **invalidate** that law. This is part of our system of **checks and balances** in our system of government: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-3f6b1704619af812065ad073a6f5d6bd){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-3f6b1704619af812065ad073a6f5d6bd" master_w="1130" master_h="834"} ::: :::: See that blue line on the far right: "declare laws unconstitutional"? That's the one where the Supreme Court places a "check" on the power of the legislature: they can't pass laws that are unconstitutional.
