**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The two parts of the question are incongruous. **What are some examples of an unlawful order from a police officer?** My esteemed colleagues have already provided information about what is a lawful and unlawful order, I don\'t think I need to re-hash it. **Or do the police have the right to make you do what they want?** The two parts of your question are not related. If the order is lawful, it\'s likely that he wants you to obey it. It sounds like you\'re asking if the police have the right to tell you to do anything that they please at any time, and you simply have to follow it because it\'s an order from a police officer. So if a police officer said to you \"strip naked right here right now,\" do you think that order would be enforceable? If you had just been exposed to a deadly neurotoxin that was seeping through your clothes and if it made contact with your skin it would instantly kill you, it\'s possible that such an order would be lawful. But an officer doesn\'t have to tell you at that moment what the source for his order is. So how are you to know? You might ask, \"Why, was I exposed to a deadly neurotoxin that is now seeping its way through my clothes and if it touches my ski\.....\" and then you die because you took too long trying to figure out why a police officer would order you to do such a thing.
