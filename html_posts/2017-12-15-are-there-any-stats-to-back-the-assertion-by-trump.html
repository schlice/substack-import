--- title: "Are there any stats to back the assertion by Trump that a police officer was assaulted every 10 minutes in the US in 2016?" date: "2017-12-15" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} According to the FBI, [[57,180 officers were victims of line-of-duty assaults](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4aa103&url=https://www.fbi.gov/news/pressrel/press-releases/fbi-releases-2016-statistics-for-law-enforcement-officers-killed-and-assaulted-in-the-line-of-duty){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="fbi.gov"}]{.qlink_container} in 2016. This means, on average: - 156.6 assaults a day, or - 6.5 assaults an hour. Six assaults an hour means one assault every 10 minutes.
