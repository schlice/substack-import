**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the government suspend the constitution & violate human rights during wartime?** *(US answer)* In short, no. The Constitution is never suspended, and the power of martial law is actually laid out in the Constitution: > *The Privilege of the Writ of Habeas Corpus shall not be suspended, > unless when in Cases of Rebellion or Invasion the public Safety may > require it.* *Habeas corpus* is the right to a hearing when being lawfully imprisoned. More broadly, it is the oversight by the judiciary of law enforcement. The imposition of martial law suspends habeas corpus, and allows law enforcement to imprison people without due process in order to quell insurrections or invasions. Martial law is also governed by several notable Supreme Court decisions, as well as the *Posse Comitatus Act*, which prevents the military from enforcing civilian laws without Congressional approval. The Constitution remains in full force and effect even during periods of martial law. Additionally, human rights in general are not defined in the Constitution, unless you're referring to the Bill of Rights as "human rights." Human rights existed before the Constitution, and would still exist even if "the Constitution was suspended."
