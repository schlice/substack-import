**Content:** [Whether or not someone chooses to do something is not necessarily something with which you should be concerned. If they choose the disease and its potential side effects over the vaccine for the disease, so be it. I would think about child endangerment charges, but IANAL (I am not a lawmaker). Many of these diseases can cause permanent disability, or even death, so in my humble opinion, the cure is not worse than the disease, and the risk of the vaccine is much lower than the risk of the actual disease itself.\ \ That said, however, the recent measles outbreak has shown us that our vaccines are not 100% effective. In other words, a person who chooses to not be vaccinated and contracts the disease can actually infect people who have already been vaccinated because the vaccine was not 100% effective. In the most recent outbreak of measles, we have seen this happen: people who have been vaccinated have contracted the disease anyway.\ \ We know that vaccines are not 100% effective, so the way to eradicate a disease is to vaccinate the entire population to limit the spread of the disease, and any exposures of the disease will eventually burn out with limited casualties\ \ When people choose to not vaccinate, they not only endanger the person who is not vaccinated (usually a child), but they also risk a virus outbreak, since these diseases are highly contagious and vaccines are not ever 100% effective.]{.rendered_qtext}
