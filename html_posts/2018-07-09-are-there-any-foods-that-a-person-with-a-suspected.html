**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are there any foods that a person with a suspected MS should avoid?** Let's go past the "healthy eating" information that we all know. You're asking if any foods make it more likely or less likely to get the disease, or for the disease to progress. The short answer is no. **There is no diet that will either "cure" MS, or will "make your actual disease advance more quickly or slowly."** However, there is ample anecdotal evidence that *diet can help to make symptoms less severe.* See the Wahls Protocol and the Swank MS Diet for more information. The caveat is that while symptoms may be less severe, the disease continues to progress. The only thing you can do to slow down the disease itself is to get on one of the approved MS drugs. Full stop. There is also a growing body of research which suggests that aggressively treating suspected MS is crucial to long-term reduction in disability. Aggressive treatment means getting on the MS drugs *sooner rather than later*. Rather than waiting for suspected MS to develop into full-blown MS, starting the drugs early limits the earliest disease activity, which prevents long-term damage from MS. This research recently appeared in the journal *Neurology* and it was issued by the American Academy of Neurology which published new guidelines for MS treatment, including a new series of recommendations for how and when a neurologist should prescribe MS drugs. In summary: foods won't make a difference in whether or not suspected MS will turn into full MS.
