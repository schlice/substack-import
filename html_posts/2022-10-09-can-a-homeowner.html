**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is yes. The longer answer is that the HOA must put a *lien* on the house for the amount of the unpaid dues, fees, fines, or anything else the homeowner owes. Once the lien is attached, the homeowner no longer deals with the HOA, but with the HOA's attorney, and the attorney's fees also get added to what the homeowner owes. The attorney *may* work out a payment plan with the delinquent homeowner. Note that I said *may*, they are under no obligation to do so. Once the amount of the unpaid money reaches a specific level (varies by state), the attorney (as proxy for the HOA) can foreclose on the lien, sell the property and collect their unpaid money from the proceeds. \ Original question: Can a homeowner\'s association (HOA) foreclose on a property if the owner has not paid HOA fees or fines, and the property is paid off?
