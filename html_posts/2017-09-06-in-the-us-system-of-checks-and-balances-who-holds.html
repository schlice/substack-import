--- title: "In the US System of checks and balances, who holds the judicial system accountable?" date: "2017-09-06" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If the judicial branch declares a law to be unconstitutional, the legislative branch can re-write the law to either clarify the wording which made it unconstitutional, or they can exclude the unconstitutional provisions and pass the law again. Alternatively, they can propose Constitutional amendments to change a portion of the Constitution used to rule a law as unconstitutional, or they can propose Constitutional amendments to overturn a judicial decision. Finally, they approve federal judge appointments made by the executive. The executive branch checks the judicial branch by appointing all federal judges.
