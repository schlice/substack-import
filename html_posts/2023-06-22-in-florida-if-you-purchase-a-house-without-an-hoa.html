--- title: '"In Florida if you purchase a house without an HOA and years later a HOA is organized what is my obligation to the HOA?"' date: '2023-06-22' tags: - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you already own your home, any HOA formed will be a *voluntary* HOA.ยน You are under no obligation to join the HOA. The only time you become part of a mandatory HOA is if it is part of your purchase agreement. Mandatory HOAs can only be attached to properties if 100% of the landowners agree. Developers can create mandatory HOAs because they own all of the properties during the development phase. If an HOA is formed later on, it is voluntary. You are not obligated to join. That said, you won't get any of the benefits of joining. Perhaps the HOA decided to build a fitness center in the neighborhood on a piece of property owned by someone. The HOA could allow you access to that fitness center. That's just an example of what a voluntary HOA could do. But even if the voluntary HOA performs services for you, you are not obligated to join it. \ ยน *As mentioned, if a community gets 100% approval to create a mandatory HOA, it can be done, but in order to become mandatory and binding on all current and future homeowners, it must be done with 100% approval.*
