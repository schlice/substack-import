--- title: "Under what circumstances is it illegal to tell a lie?" date: "2016-01-27" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} When you are stopped by the police, it is illegal to lie about your identity. You can lie to the police at any other time you choose (at your own peril, of course), but you may not lie about your identity. This is a separate charge in and of itself (obstruction of justice, or obstruction of the duties of a peace officer, or barring justice, depending on jurisdiction), but you can be jailed until the police can establish your identity. The reason for this falls under [[Scott Welch](https://www.quora.com/profile/Scott-Welch)]{.qlink_container}\'s answer, that it is illegal to lie to a government official. For the police, this clause only applies to your identity.
