{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > Question details: > > If Patient A has cancer and a rare blood type. Patient A\'s sibling is > a perfect match, but refuses to donate. Is there any legal action > Patient A can take to compel them to donate? None whatsoever. Donation is voluntary. Heck, registration in the bone marrow registry is voluntary. If the sibling is not registered, you cannot compel them to register either. That said, there is a level of anonymity between donor and patient. If they are utilizing the registry, the patient and donor remain anonymous until long after the procedure is completed.
