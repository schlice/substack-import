--- title: '"Do FBI and police officers take their guns home with them?"' date: '2016-10-24' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} State and local police officers are usually issued a weapon which they take home with them. In contrast, military police **do not** take their duty weapons home with them. Military police officers have a weapons card which they bring to work with them. They arrive at the beginning of their shift and present the weapons card to the armory. The card indicates which weapons the member is qualified to carry. The armorer provides the empty weapon and appropriately loaded magazines to the service member, leaving the weapons card in the slot from which the weapon was retrieved. The weapon is loaded using a **clearing barrel** in the presence of a **clearing barrel official** (CBO) to ensure the weapon is loaded and unloaded safely. The weapon is given to the member with the slide locked open and the safety on. The barrel of the weapon is inserted into the clearing barrel, the magazine is inserted, the slide is released, and the safety is turned off. This leaves the weapon in **condition 2**, which means round in the chamber, hammer down, safety off. The weapon is inserted into the holster and the thumb break is closed. At the end of the shift, the procedure is reversed, and the weapons card is returned to the member. Interesting side note: military police are trained to never draw their weapons from their holsters unless they intend to fire them. Under normal circumstances, the weapon leaves the holster exactly one time: when taking it out to clear it before returning it to the armorer. The "ready position" is hand on the backstrap, thumb break open. When we train and do exercises, we use exactly the same ready position, including opening the thumb break.
