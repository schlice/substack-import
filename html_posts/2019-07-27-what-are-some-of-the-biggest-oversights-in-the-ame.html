**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What are some of the biggest oversights in the Americans with Disabilities Act?** The ADA hamstrings businesses in favor of ease of access for the disabled. The law says that someone merely needs to claim to be disabled and businesses must offer accommodations, no questions asked. So places of public accommodation, like restaurants and amusement parks, are forced to provide those accommodations to anyone who requests them. This has led to widespread abuse of the system by the non-disabled. Businesses are forced to limit the mechanisms by which accommodations are provided, and what is actually provided, to the bare minimum required to make the facility accessible, which does nothing to help the truly disabled. Some balance in the law would have been nice.
