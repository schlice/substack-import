--- title: '"Is the human right to life axiomatic or is there a basis to it?"' date: '2017-10-31' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the United States (and this is only applicable to the United States), our Declaration of Independence actually spells this out fairly clearly (emphasis mine): *We hold these truths to be* ***self-evident****: that all Men are created Equal; that they are* ***endowed by their Creator*** *with certain* ***unalienable*** *Rights; that among these are the right to Life, Liberty, and the Pursuit of Happiness.* Let's review the words I highlighted: - self-evident: This word means the same as axiomatic. - endowed by their Creator: This phrase means that man did not bestow these rights on their fellow Man. These rights did not come from Man, so they cannot be removed by Man. - unalienable: This word means that there is no way that anyone can remove this right from a person, not even by the person themselves. Therefore, you couldn't give up your own right to life, even if you willingly chose to. Therefore, in the United States, the right to life is based on our belief that such a right was bestowed by our Creator.
