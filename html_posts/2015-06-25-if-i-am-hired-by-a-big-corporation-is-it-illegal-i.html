--- title: "If I am hired by a big corporation, is it illegal if I quit because someone else tries to recruit me and I want to join the new firm?" date: "2015-06-25" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [It\'s not illegal, but many companies make you sign a \"non-compete\" clause when you join them, which requires that if you want to hire on with certain companies (which are direct competitors of big corporation), you will have to wait a certain amount of time, generally six months to one year, before you can join the new firm. Check with the HR department at big corporation to see if you signed one of these when joining, and what companies it applies to.\ \ If you have access to certain IP (intellectual property) at big corporation, you may have had to sign additional contracts when gaining access to that property which may limit your ability to move to new companies. As [[Dan Robinson](https://www.quora.com/profile/Dan-Robinson-9)]{.qlink_container} said, slavery was abolished in the US, but companies are allowed to protect their intellectual property.]{.rendered_qtext}
