--- title: "What does it mean when something is proven beyond all possible doubt? Can the existence of God be proven in this way?" date: "2024-06-18" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Most knowledge we possess is gained through **induction**. Induction is the process by which we take specific observations and use them to come up with a general conclusion. We all do this, every single day. For example, you go to sit in a chair. Will that chair hold your weight? You don't know, but you have some specific observations: - The screws all seem to be in place. - The chair appears to be sturdy. - Historically, chairs designed with this material in this configuration have held your weight. So while you can never know with 100% certainty whether or not that chair will hold your weight, you can know *beyond a reasonable doubt* (not beyond all doubt) that the chair will hold your weight when you sit in it, and you rely on that knowledge. *But you can't be 100% sure! How can you sit in the chair without being 100% sure?* **Faith**. You have faith that the knowledge you do have indicates the general conclusion that the chair is safe to sit in. **Faith bridges the gap between the undeniable and the unknowable.** The exception to the induction rule is called the *perfect induction* and it happens when all possible specific observations are known. For example, I can say "My right hand contains nothing but pennies." You can examine all the coins in my right hand and you can make the general conclusion that the knowledge gained through induction is 100% true. So there is one time where you can say that something is true beyond *all* doubt. However, in the vast majority of cases, knowledge gained through induction is never 100% true beyond *all* doubt. \ The case of the existence of God follows the same rules of inductive reasoning. It is true beyond reasonable doubt, but not beyond all doubt. We can never know with 100% certainty that God exists, because we cannot know all the answers to make a 100% certain conclusion. Some things we will never know while we are alive on this earth. No one observed the Big Bang. No one saw what existed before the Big Bang. No one has the ability to traverse past the end of the universe. There are certain pieces of information we will never be able to access. Therefore, we can never, while we are alive, know with 100% certainty if God exists. We can only say that with the rational and logical evidence we do have, we are certain *beyond a reasonable doubt*, but not beyond all doubt. There is abundant evidence to show that God exists, but it's not direct evidence of God Himself. For example, the scientific evidence overwhelmingly confirms that the universe exploded into being out of nothing. Either someone created something out of nothing (the theistic view), or no one created something out of nothing (the atheistic view). Which view is more reasonable? The theistic view. Which view requires more faith? The atheistic view. Also, the simplest life form contains the information-equivalent of 1,000 encyclopedias. Theists believe only an intelligent being can create a life form containing the equivalent of 1,000 encyclopedias. Atheists believe non-intelligent natural forces can do it. Theists have evidence to support their conclusion (scientific evidence based on observation and repetition). Since atheists don't have any such evidence, their belief requires a lot more faith. So not only does the belief in the existence of God have evidence, the belief that God does not exist requires a lot more faith.
