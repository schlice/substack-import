**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Although we don't know the exact origin of the salute, it is generally accepted that it originated in medieval times, when people who approached a knight would raise their empty weapon hand to show they were unarmed. Knights would use their sword hand to lift their visors on their helmet armor to show another knight that they were friendly. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-397fabbc9dfb97c745aba9f146e645b7){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-397fabbc9dfb97c745aba9f146e645b7" master_w="572" master_h="381"} ::: :::: This evolved into a military courtesy, again generally accepted as seen first in the British army, where the person rendering the salute uses an upturned palm, as if lifting a visor. Originally, the custom stemmed from grabbing the brim of the hat and tipping it off of the head as a sign of respect, but the British Army was concerned about the wear and tear this caused to the headgear, so the salute simply became applying the hand to the tip of the brim, with the palm facing out as if lifting a visor. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-b1097b7180f0eb40cca85153d339f26d){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-b1097b7180f0eb40cca85153d339f26d" master_w="3000" master_h="1907"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy Sgt Andy Malthouse ABIPP/MOD, OGL,* [[*45151321.jpg*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ac9490&url=https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid%3D26896182){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikimedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}*)* This image of the British Royal Armed Forces is significant, because the American salute stemmed from the British Royal Navy salute. Notice the difference here; this difference originally stemmed from the fact that naval ships were sealed with tar and pitch; it was considered exceptionally bad form to show a dirty palm to someone if rendering a sign of respect, so the salute was turned to the palm-down orientation to conceal any blackened palms. It is generally accepted that the American salute has its origins in the British Royal Navy salute. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-d9e144ba284154d58b4d6a1ef4dcdd44){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-d9e144ba284154d58b4d6a1ef4dcdd44" master_w="3032" master_h="2100"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy MSgt Scott T.
