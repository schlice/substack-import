**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Ah, this is the difference between **pantheism** and **panentheism**. Look carefully, those are not spelling errors. **Pantheism** is the belief that the material and the divine are one. A pantheist would say "God is everything." God is the tree. God is the air. God is the rock. To a pantheist, God is in all things because God *is* all things. **Panentheism** is the belief that the divine permeates every part of the material world but is not any of those things. They believe the divine is the "spirit of the universe," but the universe is not the divine, even though it is dependent on the universe. To a panentheist, God cannot exist apart from a material universe. So panentheism would also say that God is in all things, even though God *is not* all things.
