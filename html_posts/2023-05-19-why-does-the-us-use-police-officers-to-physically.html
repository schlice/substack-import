**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In many jurisdictions in the United States, while speed cameras may be legal under state law, they are not used for general traffic monitoring. They are used in very specific situations, such as school zones (which are universally 20 mph) and some states will use them in construction zones. States vary on the legality of automated speed enforcement systems. **18 states and the District of Columbia** have passed laws that permit the use of speed cameras. **9 states** have passed laws that prohibit the use of speed cameras. But even in the states where it is legally permitted, it is generally seen as government overreach when they are used for completely autonomous speed monitoring and ticketing. Unlike in the UK, the US was founded on a healthy distrust of government. Government only has the authority which it has been given in the Constitution and nothing more. Americans, as a group, are generally very wary of government overstepping its Constitutional bounds. We are completely disinterested in having government tell us what is "for our own good."
