**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why is free speech the hill most conservatives want to die on when nearly everyone is granted this? Is the issue more so on consequences or criticism from free speech?** You said that nearly everyone is granted this. Less than half of the country does not believe this. [[40% of the country believes that "hate speech" should be regulated.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=76975d&url=https://www.cato.org/survey-reports/state-free-speech-tolerance-america){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cato.org"}]{.qlink_container} The problem: *"hate speech" is free speech.* The First Amendment exists to protect speech you don't like. If everyone spoke only things you like to hear, there would be no need for the First Amendment in the first place. The fact that speech exists which you don't like is an indication that it needs to be protected if we're going to protect freedom of speech at all. The issue is indeed about consequences or criticism from free speech. - There should not be limits on what words you can say (unless we're talking about causing imminent harm or inciting a riot or speaking falsehoods). - Similarly, **there should not be limits on** ***consequences*** **from your speech.** For example, you can say whatever you believe about whatever topic you like. However, if your employer finds that your words are incongruent with their corporate philosophy, your free speech may cause you to lose your job. This is all acceptable, and is a natural consequence of free speech. Putting limits on what words you can say *before you say it*, or trying to divine what someone was *thinking* when interpreting the words they spoke, is the exact opposite of free speech.
