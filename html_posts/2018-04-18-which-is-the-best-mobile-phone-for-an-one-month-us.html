**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Which is the best mobile phone for an one-month use for a salesman and sell it afterwards? (I need high quality camera and high battery capacity)** *(In case you're wondering, any time you ask a "which is the best" question, it's all opinion. I'm trying to base my answer on independent reviews from around the Internet, but in the end, it's still just opinion. Your mileage may vary)* In the United States mobile market, you're not really going to find an amazing phone with a fantastic camera **and** amazing battery life. You're either going to get incredible battery capacity and a mediocre camera, or one of the best cameras on the market and so-so battery. I would suggest going for the latter. And if that's the case, you want the **Pixel 2** from Google. While it does not possess the best *physical* camera hardware available in a mobile phone today (right now, that goes to either the iPhone X or the Galaxy S9+), the software which runs the Pixel's camera produces the best quality photos, every time, in every environment, **hands down**. You simply cannot take a bad photo with that phone. There's one of two reasons you buy a Pixel 2: you're a hardcore Android fanboy, or you want the best camera experience in town. There's no two ways about it. If you want the camera which will take the best pictures every time, you get a Pixel 2. Plus, since carriers don't directly sell them, you can get it from Google and the resale market is really solid. On the downside, battery life is so-so. But you can get external batteries and battery cases of all kinds. You can't really get good external cameras. So the trade off is not that bad in my book.
