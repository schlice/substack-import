**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Pro-life and pro-choice people have one major difference in philosophy: **The pro-life movement believes that the life of a human being** ***deserves to be protected at any stage of development.*** **The pro-choice movement believes that the life of a human being** ***has differential value based on temporal circumstances.*** Saying something like "a fetus is not a person" or saying "a fetus is not alive" are silly, and have already been disproven by science. Those things they say are just cover to allow them the mental justification to take a human life. Everything else is just fodder. I mean, some things are definitely issues, like "pro life people don't care about the baby after it is born" and while that may or may not be true (we can certainly debate that), it has no bearing on whether or not it is morally acceptable to kill a fetus. You can absolutely make those arguments (and I expect the comments to light up with people telling me that I just want women barefoot and pregnant in the kitchen, that I believe women are nothing more than babymaking machines, "no uterus, no opinion", etc), but all of that is ancillary. This is the real, unvarnished truth about each movement. Because pro-choicers don't value life in its earliest stages in the same way or to the same degree, they find it morally acceptable to kill it. Since pro-lifers value life equally at all stages of its development, we believe that the fetus has rights, and one of those rights is the right to life.
