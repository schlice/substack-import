Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The question is whether or not the victim posed a threat. If the victim wasn\'t a threat to anyone, there\'s nothing that will prevent the officer from being charged with murder. No different than anyone else who walks into a shopping mall and shoots someone to death in front of everyone. The officer would have to show that the person was an imminent threat to the people around him.\ \ There may be a defense to that charge, such as temporary insanity. In the absence of a threat to the officer\'s life, or the lives of others, the officer simply lost touch with reality and saw the victim as a three-headed space monster. The officer would be committed to a psychiatric hospital and would lose his job.]{.rendered_qtext}
