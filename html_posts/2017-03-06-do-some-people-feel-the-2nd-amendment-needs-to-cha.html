**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Some people do feel this way. However, the logic does not follow their reasoning. Their reasoning is based on ***emotion***, not ***logic.*** Put simply, they are wrong. Looking at the situation logically produces a different set of results. At the time of the founding, there were indeed repeating rifles. There were breechloading rifles. They were not vastly underpowered as compared to modern-day rifles. Granted, modern technology and manufacturing processes have made the firearm more accurate, easier to maintain, and easier to use, but "more lethal?" I can't see that modern firearms are "more lethal" because they are more accurate or easier to maintain. More accurate rifles still require a decent shooter to put the round on target. Rifles at the time of the founding also required a decent shooter to operate the rifle. Not much different than today. Rifles then and now still go boom, and the recipient becomes just as dead. They aren't "deader" today than they were at the time of the founding. The founders absolutely could have imagined more accurate rifles, or higher capacity repeating rifles, because they already had that technology, so envisioning improvements to that technology would not have been unheard of. As for some people who feel that the 2nd Amendment should change because technology has changed, what other amendments should we change because technology has changed? Perhaps the 1st Amendment? While firearms may have improved but are basically the same technology that was available at the nation's founding, the tools we use for speech have *vastly* changed, and the Founders had no way of knowing we'd have anything like radio, television, computers, or the Internet. Instant dissemination of information was **witchcraft** back then. The Founders *never* could have reasonably assumed we'd have such means of communication when they wrote the 1st Amendment. If any amendment needs to change because technology has changed, it's the 1st Amendment, not the 2nd.
