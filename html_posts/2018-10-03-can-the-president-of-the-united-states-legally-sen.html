**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the president of the United States, legally, send everyone any message he wants using the Emergency Alert System?** In general, no. The Emergency Alert System has been designed to be used in the event of national emergency. Broadcasters work in cooperation with FEMA, the FCC and NOAA to broadcast messages related to national or regional emergencies. The important point to remember: **the EAS is a "last-ditch" method to get the word out if the President is unable to get to the media.** For example, during the September 11, 2001 attacks against the United States, the government opted not to use the EAS because the media was fully engaged and was adequately getting the word out. In short, so long as the President still has access to Twitter, it's unlikely he'd be able to use the EAS.
