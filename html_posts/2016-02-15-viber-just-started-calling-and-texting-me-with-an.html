**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} [[Talmon Marco](https://www.quora.com/profile/Talmon-Marco)]{.qlink_container} really answered the question, but I\'ll add my \$0.02 anyway. Viber is a pretty cool service. It allows you to share free calls, SMS, and pictures with anyone, and it\'s cross-platform. You might [[check it out](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=35590b&url=http://www.viber.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="viber.com"}]{.qlink_container}. When you sign up, you enter your phone number. The system will then send you a SMS message with a confirmation code you must enter back in the site. This closes the loop, ensuring the number is correct, and ensuring someone is not trying to sign you up for something without your knowledge. It\'s a security feature. Looks like in this case, someone was trying to sign themselves up and they mis-typed their phone number. The mistype ended up being your number. It is **safe to ignore** those messages. When the person discovers that he is not getting his SMS message, he will check his information and discover his error.
