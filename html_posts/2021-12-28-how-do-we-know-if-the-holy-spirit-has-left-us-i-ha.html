**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What a great question, thanks for asking! So, let's first clear up some misconceptions you clearly have: - The Spirit will never "leave you." As the Spirit is God, and God has stated plainly that he will never leave us or forsake us, we can presume that the Spirit will never leave us. - That said, it is possible to get ourselves into a state of mind where we convince ourselves that we have done something "beyond redemption." You said that you "messed up way beyond repentance," and this just isn't a thing. **There is nothing you can do that can separate you from the love of God**. - The LORD is compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in love. He will not always accuse, nor will he harbor his anger forever; he does not treat us as our sins deserve or repay us according to our iniquities. For as high as the heavens are above the earth, so great is his love for those who fear him; as far as the east is from the west, so far has he removed our transgressions from us. --- Psalm 103:8--12 NIV Once you realize that nothing can separate you from the love of God, you can begin to repent of your sin. I'm certain that whatever you did was not directly in conscious countervention of God's commands. i'm sure you didn't think to yourself, "I know what the right thing is, but I'm going to do the wrong thing anyway." You made a mistake. Mistakes are a patently human thing to make. God understands this. He understands we make mistakes. Now, it's time to do what he commands: "Go, and sin no more." It's time to "go," or move on with your life, don't get hung up on this, "and sin no more," or **repent** for what you have done. Turn away from your sin and your sinful desire. Make a conscious effort to avoid that in the future. Then know that you will feel the presence of the Spirit again. Remember, God never left you. He was there with you the whole time. You just got yourself into such a state that you couldn't hear his voice or feel his presence. \ Original question: How do we know if the holy spirit has left us? I have a feeling I messed up way beyond repentance. because I haven't felt the spirit in me in 6 months no matter what I do or pray.
