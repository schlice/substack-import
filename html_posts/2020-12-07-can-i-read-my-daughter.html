**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can I read my daughter\'s iPhone texts with an appleid email and phone number but no password and no device access?** No. In short, you either need the password, or a court order. You may even own the account, and your daughter's phone may be a line on it. You might think that you could just go to your carrier and demand an output of all of the SMS messages that went to that line, as you own the account. However, your cell phone provider has an obligation to protect the privacy of the other participant in the conversation, and you were not a party to that conversation. Therefore, in order to retrieve text messages from your cell phone carrier, you must obtain a court order.
