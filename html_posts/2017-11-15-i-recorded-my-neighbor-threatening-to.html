**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} [[Sharon Talbot](https://www.quora.com/profile/Sharon-Talbot-3)]{.qlink_container} is spot on. Threatening bodily harm is not always a crime, but in the states where it is, it is called different things. It's sometimes called "criminal threatening" or "intimidation." If intimidation or criminal threatening exists in your state, call the police. If there is no intimidation or criminal threatening statute in your state, file for a restraining order, and then file a civil lawsuit for assault and intimidation. Assault in tort is different from "battery" in tort, which is actual physical harm. The "assault" is the words, the "battery" is the action.
