--- title: "If a tree on your property damages a neighboring property, what legal liabilities do you face as the tree\" date: "2023-02-16" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In most states, the condition of the tree before it fell and whether or not you knew that it was in that condition will determine liability. In short, if the tree was alive and healthy, you are not responsible if it falls (i.e. during a hurricane) and damages your neighbor's property. But if the tree was dead, you'd be liable for any damages it caused by falling over onto your neighbor's property. Additionally, if your tree was a hazard because it was dead or dying or its limbs were broken *and you were notified about the issue* ***and you did nothing***, not only are you liable, but your homeowner's insurance won't even cover you because you were aware of the hazard and did not address it.
