--- title: '"Is it normal for American tank/APC columns to be driving down American highways with troops all over them?"' date: '2015-12-18' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. While the US Interstate system has military priorities built into it all over the place, during peacetime operations, tanks and APCs are transported, usually, by rail. When they must be transported over the road, they are loaded onto flatbeds and travel as a long convoy. They are normally not operated on their own during peacetime operations stateside.
