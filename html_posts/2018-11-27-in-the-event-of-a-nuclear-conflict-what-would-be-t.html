**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In the event of a nuclear conflict, what would be the instructions for the Air Force One, if it was known that major bases were taken out?** First of all, missile flight time is not instantaneous. It would take about 30 minutes for a missile to go airborne and then to impact within the United States. So we do have a few minutes to react. In the event of missile launch threatening the United States, the President would go airborne from wherever he is. If he is at the White House, he, the First Family, and key staffers from the White House would be evacuated by helicopter to Joint Base Andrews. From there, they would board Air Force One and would take off. Where and when they land would be entirely up to them. The rest of the cabinet and those in the line of succession would be evacuated to [[Site R](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=246c1a&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raven_Rock_Mountain_Complex){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. For reference, on September 11th, 2001, the President went airborne, and the Vice President went to Site R. NORAD staff would relocate from Peterson Air Force Base up the road into the Cheyenne Mountain Complex.
