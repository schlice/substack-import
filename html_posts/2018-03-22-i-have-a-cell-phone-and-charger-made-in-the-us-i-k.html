--- title: '"I have a cell phone and charger made in the US. I know when people travel to India they buy an Indian SIM card, but how do they charge their phones without a special transformer adapted to Indian voltage and plugs/outlets?"' date: '2018-03-22' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In my last trip to India, I used both a step-down transformer as well as a plug adapter. Indian power runs at 220V (as does most of Europe), and India uses EU-style outlet prongs. Charging your phone may be as simple as just using a plug adapter if you don't need to step the voltage down to 110. It is up to you to check your charging "brick" to see if it accepts 220V. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-4787b4d18bd185f6af4bd9fedb1f37e0){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-4787b4d18bd185f6af4bd9fedb1f37e0" master_w="3024" master_h="4032"} ::: :::: You'll notice my US Samsung charging adapter accepts "100--240V, 50--60Hz", That means I can plug this directly into the wall in India, and the voltage will be accepted. I'll just need a plug adapter to convert my US prongs to fit India's outlet prongs. This is my favorite plug adapter: [[Amazon.com: Kensington International Grounded 3 Prong Travel Adapter, Black](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d4217f&url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B071FYG49B){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} Remember though, this is not a transformer. This will simply convert the prongs between devices. It will still deliver 220V to your device, so your device must be able to accept 220V to use this alone. My laptop's power supply also accepted 220, so I was able to plug it directly into the wall as well with the plug adapter. I use a travel power strip when I travel, like this one: [[Amazon.com: FosPower 3-Outlet Mini Power Strip](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5abf80&url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B06XDJHXVB){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} I'll plug this into the plug adapter, and then the devices which accept 220 can get plugged into the strip. Very few of the devices I bring with me internationally can't accept 220, but **if your device only accepts 110V,** you will have to plug into a **step-down transformer** in order to convert the voltage to 110V. This is the one I use: [[Amazon.com: Simran SMF-200 Deluxe 200 Watts Step Down Voltage Converter](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2724e5&url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000W9DJ1Q){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} This device will step the voltage down from 220 to 110 if you have devices which do not accept more than 110V. My electric razor and my electric toothbrush did not accept 220, but the hotel I was staying in conveniently had a US 110 plug near the bathroom sink for electric devices like toothbrushes and razors.
