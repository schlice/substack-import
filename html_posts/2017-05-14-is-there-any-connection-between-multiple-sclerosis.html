--- title: '"Is there any connection between multiple sclerosis and achalasia (not just dysphagia)?"' date: '2017-05-14' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are indeed connections between both diseases, however slight. The primary site of nerve damage in achalasia patients is the **esophageal myenteric plexus** at the lower esophageal sphincter (LES). Some studies, however, have indicated that nerve damage further back (towards the brain) could be the culprit, and the nerves at the LES were actually intact. When the dorsal motor nucleus of the vagus nerve is altered, achalasia symptoms may be present, and the vagus nerve can be altered by multiple sclerosis.[\[1\]](#PqjAD){#cite-PqjAD .citation_link} To date, it has not been proven. There is also a connection between achalaisa and multiple sclerosis in that they have both been positively linked to autoimmune responses.[\[2\]](#AahaT){#cite-AahaT .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-PqjAD){.citation_index} [[Could a central origin be responsible for achalasia ?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=28af4f&url=http://www.hon.ch/OESO/books/Vol_4_Prim_Motility/Articles/ART091.HTML){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="hon.ch"}]{.qlink_container} [\[2\]](#cite-AahaT){.citation_index} [[Mysterious esophagus disease is autoimmune after all](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ffdfd6&url=https://nieuws.kuleuven.be/en/content/2014/mysterious-esophagus-disease-is-autoimmune-after-all){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="kuleuven.be"} ]{.qlink_container}
