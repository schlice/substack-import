**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'm going to answer these out of order. It will make sense later. Trust me. > **That is, the fact that they sin is the result of a curse placed on > humanity because of SOMEONE ELSE'S SIN.** Adam's sin *changed humanity*. It changed us by *separating us from God.* Jesus reunited us with God. That's the 30,000 foot view. Let's talk details though. Before the first sin, Adam and Eve walked with God in the Garden. They could talk to God directly. They were as close to God as you could get. After the fall, humanity was separated from God. Because Adam's choices had introduced sin into the human condition, our bodies became susceptible to disease and early death because of it. We were always free to choose good or evil. But now we get to choose good or evil in sinful bodies which cannot help but to sin. And because of our sin condition, most of us sin without even thinking about it, in our thoughts and in our words, in what we have done, and what we have failed to do. We are all sinners. Each and every one of us have fallen short of God's glory. If God were nothing but completely just, He would be well within His rights to reject us all from His Kingdom and send us to the torment of eternal separation from Him. But He has offered us an alternative. Instead of leaving us to this fate, He sent His son to pay for our sin in our place. Jesus died on the cross, accepting onto Himself the sins of the world: past, present, and future. His sacrifice paid for all of our sin. His blood washed us clean. All we have to do is repent of our sin and accept Jesus as our savior. He built the system this way so we have the freedom to choose to love Him or reject Him without violating our free will. If you think about it, it's actually a pretty ingenious system. The TLDR here is that you can *blame* God for the way this is, or you can *love* Him for who He is and what He has done. In other words, you can be ***bitter***, or you can be ***better.*** Be better. > **If Christ truly paid for the sins of the world, then why must we > continue to worship a God who condemns innocent people to Hell?** Is anyone truly innocent? As we have seen, we are all sinners. No one is truly innocent.ยน Also, God has written His truth on to everyone's hearts, and His invisible hand can be seen in all of His creation, in nature. In this way, everyone knows about God. But people reject what they know is true. This is what sends people to hell, not God. In other words, *people willingly separate themselves from God by rejecting what they know in their hearts to be true.* It is only the people who have accepted Christ as their savior who benefit from His salvific work on the cross. \ ยน Children and the mentally handicapped are unable to consciously reject God, and so they are not held responsible for that inability to reject Him.
