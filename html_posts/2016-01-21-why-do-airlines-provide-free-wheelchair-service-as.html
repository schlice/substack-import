**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, the **airline** does not provide wheelchair service. The **airport** does. It\'s an important distinction. The service that runs wheelchairs around the airport is provided by the airport; the airline only indirectly funds the service through landing fees. Secondly, ADA makes all disability services easy to abuse. All I have to do is ***claim*** I have a disability. I don\'t have to ***prove*** I have a disability. This isn\'t limited to airport wheelchairs (think \"assistance animals\" akin to glamour girls with purse pups). There are several practical reasons why **airports** provide wheelchair service. 1\. The infamous and troublesome Americans with Disabilities Act. ADA requirements say that disabled persons need to have the same access to facilities that non-disabled persons do. Therefore, to get disabled persons through the airport expediently, wheelchair service is provided. 2\. Wheelchairs owned and operated by the airport can go into and out of the security checkpoint without a tremendous amount of security difficulty. 3\. Disabled persons must transfer from a traditional wheelchair (in the airport) to an aircraft wheelchair (in the plane) because it fits down the aircraft aisle.
