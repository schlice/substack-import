**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} These answers are all correct, to a degree. The bottom line is that you will be handcuffed whether or not you agree. In general, it works this way: 1. I ask you to do something 2. I tell you to do something 3. I make you do something. The "do something" can be anything from putting your hands behind your back to moving to a particular location or anything else which I may need you to do. If I am placing you under arrest, you will be handcuffed. So I will first *ask* you to place your hands behind your back. If you refuse, I will then *tell* you to place your hands behind your back. If you still refuse, I will *make* you put your hands behind your back, which will usually involve some form of control technique such as a wrist lock (which incidentally also involves some pain), at which point I will apply handcuffs anyway. So not only did your polite refusal get you nowhere in terms of not getting handcuffed, now you're handcuffed and in pain. The only time I would not handcuff you behind your back is if you are physically incapable of putting your hands behind your back (perhaps because of an injury or a defect related to your arm), and I would belt you and cuff you wherever I was able. Bottom line: if you're riding in the back of the car, you're handcuffed. The short answer: don't refuse the order of a police officer who is performing his duty. You won't get far.
