**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you can prove that the State's motivation in restricting abortion is to remove the woman's control over her body, then the State can use the same line of reasoning (removing control over his body) to sterilize a man. But as of yet, no State (nor does the draft SCOTUS opinion) state as their primary reason for repealing *Roe* that the motivation in restricting abortion is to "remove the woman's control over her own body." In my opinion, women absolutely have control over their own bodies, but only to a certain extent. In the case of abortion, it's not a control issue. It's an equal rights issue. And if we are to be consistent regarding equal rights, then we must consider the baby in the womb to have at least access to the same rights. So it's much less about control, and much more about equal rights. \ Original question: A question for Pro-Lifers.
