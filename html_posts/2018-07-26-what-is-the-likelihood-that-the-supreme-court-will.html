**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What is the likelihood that the Supreme Court will grant certiorari and then uphold Indiana\'s ultrasound abortion law, overturning the lower courts\' decision?** Unlikely, even with a majority textualist SCOTUS. The Indiana law places an undue burden on women seeking an abortion. The courts recognize this, and correctly block the law. That's the only thing at issue. It's not a pro-life vs. pro-choice issue at all. It's purely an undue burden issue, which could block some women from being legally able to get an abortion when abortion would otherwise be legal. If a woman couldn't afford a trip to the nearest OB-GYN which could provide the procedure, or afford the procedure on top of the abortion, it could be a barrier to abortion, which would currently be unconstitutional.
