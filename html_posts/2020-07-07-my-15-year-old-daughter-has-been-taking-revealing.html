**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My 15-year-old daughter has been taking revealing photos of herself and they're showing up on my phone from iCloud. How do I address this to her and should I be worried?** By the way, this question (or variations of it) [[has](https://www.quora.com/I-found-inappropriate-photos-on-my-15-year-old-daughter-s-Facebook-account-What-should-I-do)]{.qlink_container} [[been](https://www.quora.com/My-friend-s-daughter-has-been-posting-photos-up-on-social-media-like-half-naked-photos-shes-only-14-years-old-what-can-he-do)]{.qlink_container} [[asked](https://www.quora.com/I-found-my-daughters-Instagram-and-saw-very-revealing-pictures-of-her-What-should-I-do)]{.qlink_container} [[many](https://www.quora.com/I-caught-my-13-yr-old-daughter-exposing-herself-naked-on-an-online-video-chatting-platform-How-should-I-react)]{.qlink_container} [[times](https://www.quora.com/I-found-inappropriate-photos-on-my-14-year-old-daughter-s-phone-What-should-I-do)]{.qlink_container} on Quora. I know their search sucks, but try. First, should you be worried? It depends on how revealing the revealing photos are. Child pornography is defined as "visual images of sexually explicit conduct involving a minor (someone under 18)." The legal definition of "sexually explicit conduct" does not require that an image depict a child engaging in sexual activity. A picture of a naked child may constitute illegal child pornography if it is sufficiently sexually suggestive. Additionally, the age of consent for sexual activity in a given state is irrelevant; any depiction of a minor under 18 years of age engaging in sexually explicit conduct is illegal. In short, if the revealing photos involve nudity, she could face charges for distribution of child pornography, a Federal crime if someone in another state gets hold of the pictures. You're talking [[15 to 30 in Federal prison](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2dbd77&url=https://www.justice.gov/criminal-ceos/citizens-guide-us-federal-law-child-pornography){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="justice.gov"}]{.qlink_container} for a child pornography charge. If she's interested in spending the prime of her life in prison, this is a good way to get there. Even if the images are just underwear pictures, or "side-boob" pictures, or other "toeing the line" kind of pictures, and they technically won't land you in jail, once you post a picture on the Internet, it will stay there forever. Future employers do scan social media profiles and do reject applicants who they determine don't meet their standards. Eerily creepy or not, that's what they do. It will be an interesting conversation with her own children why these pictures exist on the Internet. I know this all sounds very "doom and gloom," but as a parent, this is the one thing I absolutely took a hard line on with my kids. They would have instantly lost all access to electronics or the Internet had they done any of that. They knew my rule: no dick pics, period. No "send nudes," period. If they wanted to pay for their own devices and risk the jail time, that's on them, but on my devices that I pay for, that stuff does not happen, period. That kind of thing just didn't become a priority for them, because I restricted it. Don't worry, they found plenty of other things to get them in trouble. Just not this.
