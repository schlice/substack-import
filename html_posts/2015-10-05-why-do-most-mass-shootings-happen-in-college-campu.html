Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} - Most college-campus shootings are perpetrated by college-campus students - Most college-campus shooters have a ***perceived injustice*** which has taken place, and over many years, have developed a perception that life is not worth living\ - Most college-campus shooters have spent years developing their plan of revenge against those who have wronged them - Most college-campus shooters have watched other college-campus shooters get their revenge and watched their perceived aggressors get their comeuppance, and have idolized or even emulated their \"bravery\"\ - Most college-campus shooters know that most colleges are fairly devoid of armed resistance, if not through policy than through developed distaste of guns by people who frequent college campuses, so their plans for revenge will go off without too much of a hitch - Most college-campus shooters know that this is their \"last hurrah\" so they want to go out with a bang (no pun intended) and they want to take as many of their perceived aggressors with them before they go, so most college campus shooters will only stop shooting when they are done, or when they are confronted with armed resistance, at which point they end it through suicide or \"suicide-by-cop\".
