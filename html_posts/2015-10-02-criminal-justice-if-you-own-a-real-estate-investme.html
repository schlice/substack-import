--- title: "Criminal Justice: If you own a real estate investment property and a murder occurs at one of your properties, what happens with your home?" date: "2015-10-02" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [In most neighborhoods, cities, and states, the owner of the property (as far as the property is concerned) is still held responsible for the conduct of tenants and guests in the property.\ \ This does not mean that the owner is responsible legally if someone commits a murder in their house. What this means is that if a tenant violates local neighborhood covenants, damages the property, etc., the owner is still liable for that violation, even though his tenant committed it. Therefore, any repairs/refurbishment that is required are the responsibility of the property owner. Generally, owners will charge \"deposits\" which are used to cover some of the costs should the owner discover damage in the property which must be repaired. Deposits generally cost one month\'s rent, and are provided by the tenant up-front. The owner will retain the deposit once the tenant leaves to cover damages.\ \ Under the terms of the lease agreement, the property owner must, on his own, seek redress for all costs related to the tenant\'s behavior directly from the tenant, as the tenant\'s use of the house is only covered in a lease agreement. The owner is still responsible for the house, and as I mentioned, is responsible for what the tenants of that house do in it. The owner will have to file civil suit to reclaim the costs of repairing the property, as well as reclaiming the lost revenue while the home was being repaired.]{.rendered_qtext}
