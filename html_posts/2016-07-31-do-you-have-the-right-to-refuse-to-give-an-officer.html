--- title: "Do you have the right to refuse to give an officer your license and registration?" date: "2016-07-31" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A2A *When you are the driver*: no, you do not have the right to refuse to give the police your license or registration. You have a license which is issued by the state. Your license is not a **right**. Your driving **privileges** were granted by the state, and the state can revoke your license at any time. Part of the privilege of driving on state-owned roads involves agreeing to the state's rules. You must have insurance on your car; it's required. Most states require you to carry proof of insurance in your car at all times. Most states require you to carry a copy of your registration information in your car, and when you are the driver, you are required to carry your license on you. State law also says that the police can stop your car, and you must stop. Finally, you are required to present your license, your proof of insurance, and your registration card at any time when a police officer asks you to do so. If you fail to do this, you can be arrested for driving without a license. You can get that charge dismissed in court, but you have to go to court to show them you have a license. Big waste of time. *When you are a passenger*, you are not required to carry identification on you. The only piece of information you are required to give to the police when you are asked is your real name. If you fail to do that, you can be arrested until the police verify your identity. Beyond that, you do not have to talk to the police if you don't want to.
