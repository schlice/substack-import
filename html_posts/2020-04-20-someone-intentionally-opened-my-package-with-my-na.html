**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Someone intentionally opened my package, with my name on it, and used the content. Is this illegal?** On multiple levels, yes. This is literally the best definition of the *Federal* crime of mail theft: > Because the United States Postal Service is a federal agency, > mailboxes are considered federal property, and mail theft is charged > as a federal offense. If you are charged with mail theft, you could > face up to five years in federal prison and fines of up to \$250,000. > It is also a crime to injure, deface or destroy any mail deposited in > a mailbox. For each act of vandalism, you could be imprisoned for up > to three years and fined up to \$250,000. Depending on the manner in > which the mail theft is committed, you could face other criminal > charges, such as assault, and breaking and entering. If personal > identifying information, such as names, dates of birth, addresses, > telephone numbers, tax I.D. numbers, Social Security numbers and > driver's license numbers, was stolen you could also face identity > theft charges.[\[1\]](#HlzrF){#cite-HlzrF .citation_link} In addition to the Federal charge, most states have also criminalized mail theft. Some states (like California) charge it *more harshly* than even Federal law does; check with your state laws to see what the punishment could be for mail theft.
