Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So if I understand the question, you\'re saying that police officers are only allowed to exceed the speed limit by 10% if their sirens are not on. The statement, as written, does not match the law. Unless you can show me a state law which restricts the speed of emergency vehicles, I will have to say that your question is not based in fact. You might be confusing this with *military police*, who are not allowed to exceed the speed limit by more than 10 mph, even when running code 3 (full lights and siren). And even that rule is somewhat ignored depending on the circumstances. This restriction is part of that unit\'s Operating Instructions for the facility at which they are assigned. There is no similar restriction for police emergency vehicles. Emergency vehicles are not subject to speed limits. Period. That said, does that mean an officer has the legal authority to act recklessly? That he can place civilian lives at risk by driving unsafely? Certainly not. Usually, department policy still requires things like clearing intersections before proceeding through them, and using speeds commensurate with the surrounding environment (city center vs open highway, etc) If you ever see an officer driving in a manner which you believe to be reckless, take the time to fill out a complaint with the officer\'s department.
