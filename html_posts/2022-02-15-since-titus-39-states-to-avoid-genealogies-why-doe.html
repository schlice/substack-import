{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > But avoid foolish controversies and genealogies and arguments and > quarrels about the law, because these are unprofitable and useless. > --- Titus 3:9 NIV The word "genealogies" in Titus doesn't refer to people making family trees. It refers to a genealogical practice which was going on in Paul's time: **using lineage to create false authority**. During Paul's time, there were many apostate teachers who would recite their lineage to give their teaching more credibility. For the same reason, other apostate teachers would manufacture a lineage out of whole cloth. This practice caused a great deal of controversy among the Jews and the Gentiles, and this was the practice that Paul was warning against, not necessarily creating a family tree or investigating who your ancestors were. It's okay to have pride in your family tree. But you shouldn't use your genealogy to attempt to make yourself look better or more important. \ Original question: Since Titus 3:9 states to avoid genealogies, why does the Bible have numerous genealogies in it?
