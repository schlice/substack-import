Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." There are several situations under which the police can search without probable cause and without consent: - Plain sight. If contraband is visible in plain sight (and the "plain smell" test falls under the same plain sight exception), the vehicle can be searched and no other probable cause or consent is needed. - Search incident to arrest. If you are being arrested, you and your car can be searched. \ Original question: Is it illegal for an officer to search your car without probable cause or consent from the owner of the vehicle in Texas?
