--- title: '"Can a police officer pull you over only because he smells marijuana?"' date: '2017-08-03' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I think *stopping a vehicle* ***because*** *an officer smelled marijuana as you drove by* would be a pretty thin reason to establish reasonable suspicion. It would have to be, like, *clouds* coming out of your car as you rolled by an officer standing outside of his car. I mean, it's theoretically possible that an officer could have smelled pot as *you* drove by, but I find that highly suspect. I would think my lawyer would be all over that one in court.
