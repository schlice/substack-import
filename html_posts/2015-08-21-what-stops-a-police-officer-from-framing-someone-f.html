**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Additional Comments:* > > *It seems like if a police officer doesn\'t like you, he can do > anything he wants with no repercussions. He wants to put you away in > prison for a while? He confiscates some crack from someone else and > slips it in your pocket. Boom. His word against yours, which the cops > always win. He wants you dead? He find you in an area with no > witnesses, shoots you dead, and puts a gun in your hands.* > > *Is there actually anything preventing this?* You don\'t have to be a cop to do any of the things you said. Bad people do this all the time, and far more often than cops. The only thing that prevents people from doing the things that you mentioned is a conscience. Lose that conscience, and everything in life becomes an exercise in moral relativity. But if you're asking if there are anything preventing a police officer from doing any of this, the answer is that there is nothing specific. The same laws that prevent anyone from doing these things also prevent police officers from doing these things. Basically, police officers are not unique in this regard. What makes police officers different here is that they have a great deal of experience with these types of cases. They know how to "game the system" and do the worst to you with the least likely backlash against themselves. And it is true that the qualities which make a person a good police officer also make a person a good criminal. The only difference between the two is the moral strength of the person in question. In other words, there is nothing structurally in place to prevent a police officer specifically from doing any of these things. It's up to police departments to hire people with good strong moral character to prevent these types of abuses from taking place.
