Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [My doctor can take my blood because I know that HIPAA and doctor-patient confidentiality means that he can\'t use it in any other way other than to benefit my health. And he can\'t tell anyone about what he finds; what I disclose is up to me, not my doctor.\ \ I don\'t trust my insurance company as far as I can throw them. If they find a loophole in HIPAA, they will use it to benefit their bottom line. I have no doubt of that. Obamacare actually makes it harder for insurance companies to make money, so I can only see this getting worse, not better. I can\'t see a release statement being so air-tight that they can only use your blood to determine if you are a smoker and nothing else.\ \ I\'d be wary of this, for sure.\ \ You are part of a \"risk pool\" where the risk of you contracting a certain disease and causing an increase in what they pay out is spread across an entire pool of people. Granted, they are doing everything they can to minimize risk, but they can\'t deny you coverage. They can only pass the costs on to you. And especially with the government now increasing taxes on insurance companies instead of you, insurance companies are going to do everything and anything to satisfy that bottom line. They are going to be looking for things that they can use to charge you more. Having your blood gives them a palette, a smorgasbord of things they can use to charge you more.]{.rendered_qtext}
