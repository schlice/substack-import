**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does water imbalance, whatever that may mean, accompany the onset of a MS relapse? My friend says that is the first sign that he\'ll need another infusion, soon.** Water imbalance is another word for fluid imbalance or dehydration. [[A study](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b16fd9&url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5156578/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} of 50 women found the following: > Fatigue is a prevalent and functionally disabling symptom for > individuals living with multiple sclerosis (MS) which is poorly > understood and multifactorial in etiology. Bladder dysfunction is > another common MS symptom which limits social engagement and quality > of life. To manage bladder issues, individuals with MS tend to limit > their fluid intake, which may contribute to a low-hydration (LoH) > state and fatigue. > > \[...\] > > LoH status was more common in MS subjects with bladder dysfunction. > Statistically significant differences in self-reported Fatigue > Performance Scale were observed between HiH and LoH subjects (*p* = > 0.022). USG was significantly correlated with fatigue as measured by > the MS Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS) score (*r* = 0.328, *p* = 0.020). So it is possible that being dehydrated can trigger the onset of certain symptoms, such as fatigue, and perhaps can increase stress due to self-limiting of fluids. All of these factors together could trigger a relapse.
