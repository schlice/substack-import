**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Recognize that you are not there to *perform*. At least for the congregation. **You perform for an audience of one.** The best church musicians lead the congregation to worship and *enhance* their worship. They don't stand out on their own. Their gifts and talents glorify God. The best church musicians are the ones who display a heart of worship for the congregation to mirror. They don't stand out on their own. I can see whether or not I'm successful in that endeavor when I watch the congregation during a set. Their level of engagement and energy is a direct reflection of yours. Well, actually that's not completely accurate. It's said in worship music that an engaged congregation will only be about 80% of your energy. So you really have to be energetic and on fire to lead your congregation in fulfilling worship. \ Original question: What do the best church musicians do that set them apart from the rest?
