**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} An article was posted in USA Today in September of 2014 about this very scenario. [[Statutory rape victim forced to pay child support](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=86c6e3&url=http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2014/09/02/statutory-rape-victim-child-support/14953965/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="usatoday.com"}]{.qlink_container} The upshot of the article is a few things: 1. Child support is not for the rapist, nor is it punitive damages for a rape victim. It is intended to ensure a child is cared for. 2. Most states will come after the other parent for child support, regardless of how the pregnancy happened. 3. The only exceptions to this occur if the petitioning parent is guilty of sexual assault, or sexual assault with a minor, and not all states have this exemption.
