**Content:** [*While we rush people out of cars on fire, people ARE held responsible for injuries resulting when they touch injured people.*\ \ Actually, this is not true. All 50 states have what they call \"Good Samaritan\" laws that indemnify most individuals when assisting at the scene of an accident. A small number of states do not indemnify civilians, but most do. There are peculiarities that vary from state to state, but they all have some form of the law. So if you rushed in to an accident scene and tried your best to help but you actually ended up causing a secondary injury, the Good Samaritan laws in your area (assuming you have appropriate ones \-- we do) would protect you from any civil or criminal penalties, assuming you were not acting recklessly (dragging a person by a clearly broken arm, etc), or if you have training, acting outside of the bounds of your training.]{.rendered_qtext}
