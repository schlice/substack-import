**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If I overhear co-workers having a conversation about sexual content on another co-worker\'s Instagram (not in her presence) , does this count as sexual harassment? She had explicit Boudoir photography.** In general, such talk violates company harassment guidelines, and is by itself reportable directly to HR. Just by you overhearing their discussion, they have committed the harassing behavior. If you were an exceedingly nice person (and assuming your workplace also explicitly bans this behavior), you could approach them and advise them that such talk is unacceptable under HR rules and they need to stop. You are under no obligation to do so, especially if they are making an uncomfortable work environment for you. If you do, and they do not stop, don\'t bother with any other intervention, go straight to HR. They will be out the door by the end of the week. In this day and age, companies don\'t take kindly to harassment in the workplace. It\'s as close to zero tolerance as you can get. Companies don\'t want the liability or the bad press of harassment lawsuits, so they nip that in the bud right quick. Oh, and tell the other girl to clean up her IG, as that too can get her fired. People have been fired for less. Note that not all companies share the same policy on workplace harassment, so YMMV. Check with HR if you have questions. My employer has a very strong workplace harassment policy, and we fight hard to make a safe, inclusive workplace for everyone.
