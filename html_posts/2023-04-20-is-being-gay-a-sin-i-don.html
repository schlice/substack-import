**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The problem is that people have a hard time distinguishing between *temptation* and *sinful acts*. In general, temptation to sin is not sinful, but sinful acts are. In other words, having a homosexual temptation is not sinful. Allowing that temptation to go further into homosexual thoughts or homosexual sex is sinful. Just look at King David in the Bible. Or if you don't want to read the story of David and Bathsheba, you can watch the old movie starring Cary Grant. Same difference. Basically, David was up on his palace rooftop one night when he saw Bathsheba in a nearby home taking a bath. > It happened, late one afternoon, when David arose from his couch and > was walking on the roof of the king's house, that he saw from the roof > a woman bathing; and the woman was very beautiful. - 2 Samuel 11:2 ESV David saw her accidentally; that was temptation. **But his sin occurred when he didn't look away**. He kept looking, and he thought that she "was very beautiful." His sin in thought led him to sin in action. The accidental view wasn't a sin. **Allowing his mind to continue the thought was.** The Bible does indeed say that homosexual sex is sinful. The Bible is replete with admonitions against homosexual behavior (all quotes from NIV): > Do not have sexual relations with a man as one does with a woman; that > is detestable. --- Leviticus 18:22 > > If a man has sexual relations with a man as one does with a woman, > both of them have done what is detestable.
