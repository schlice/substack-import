**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Where\'s the best pizza in Colorado Springs?** You're asking for an opinion. My opinion of what is the best pizza will most certainly differ from yours. If you like real New York-style pizza, you can't go wrong with [[Borriello Brothers](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=170bbe&url=http://www.borriellobrothers.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="borriellobrothers.com"}]{.qlink_container}. But understand what New York style pizza is. It started out in New York City in the early 1900s where Italian immigrant *pizzaiolos* (pizza makers) were forced to use lower-temperature coal-fired pizza ovens where hardwoods were scarce. Old Italian pizza ovens burned much hotter and made firm crusts; New York pizza is cooked at a lower temperature, resulting in a softer crust. It's floppy; it generally has to be folded in half so that you can get it into your mouth. And if it's cooked with real cheese (most is), you'll find that the oils from the cheese will drip out into your hand from the fold. Yep, that's New York style pizza. If you're more interested in Italian-style pizza (also known as Neapolitan pizza) and a simpler ingredient set, check out [[Duca's Neapolitan Pizza](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=915d22&url=http://ducaspizza.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ducaspizza.com"}]{.qlink_container}. It's cooked in real wood-burning ovens at around 800--900 degrees, fully cooking a pizza in about 90 seconds. It's been certified by the **Associazione Vera Pizza Napoletana** which registers approved suppliers of "Vera Pizza Napoletana" --- the 'true Neapolitan pizza.' The designation is only given to pizzerias that met strict requirements that respect the tradition and the art of Neapolitan pizza making. If you're looking for the eclectic in pizza-making and perhaps some tradition in Colorado Springs, you should visit [[Fargo's Pizza.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fff650&url=http://www.fargospizza.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="fargospizza.com"}]{.qlink_container} It's very much an experience as well as relatively decent pizza. Notable is the use of large corn meal on the bottom of their crusts. It's odd, but oddly satisfying. They have a huge pizza menu as well as a very large salad bar, an arcade, and Victorian period costuming and theming. It's definitely something to see. Also worth mentioning: - [[Marcos Pizza](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e2da3d&url=https://www.marcos.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="marcos.com"}]{.qlink_container} - family owned and operated here in town, and they make very good pizza - [[Blackjack Pizza](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3772fb&url=https://blackjackpizza.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="blackjackpizza.com"}]{.qlink_container} - a Colorado Springs institution And some of the smaller, up-and-coming, but no less good, chains: - [[Blaze Pizza](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7ec99e&url=http://blazepizza.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="blazepizza.com"}]{.qlink_container} - [[MOD Pizza](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f3bf3a&url=https://modpizza.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="modpizza.com"}]{.qlink_container} Hope this helps.
