**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What is an HOA?** An HOA is a "Homeowner's Association." It is a not-for-profit corporation (IRS section 501(c)(4)) which manages a common interest company. The mission statement of the company generally includes keeping home values high by controlling the exterior appearance of the homes under its purview. There are two types of HOAs: **mandatory** and **voluntary.** Mandatory HOAs are created before the houses are first sold, and are included as part of the deed to the property. When you purchase the property, you agree to abide by the rules of the HOA that is already in place. You cannot "opt out" of a mandatory HOA. They **control** the exterior of your home, and can tell you whether or not you can or can't make certain changes to your home. They can also fine you, place a lien on your home if you don't pay, and foreclose on that lien to get their money. Voluntary HOAs run much the same way as mandatory HOAs. They are also not-for-profit corporations which help to pay for some neighborhood amenities, but they are, as the name implies, voluntary. They don't **control** the outside of your house; they rely on whatever local government zoning is in place (and they help you stay in compliance with them, even though they don't provide the local control). They may also offer voluntary benefits, like club memberships. **Can your neighbors create one without the board of directors?** Since an HOA is a 501(c)(4) not-for-profit **corporation,** it has to conform with all of the requirements of having a corporation, such as appointing a Board of Directors. But even if your neighbors *create* one, it would only be voluntary. Mandatory HOAs can only be implemented before any of the houses are sold. You can convert a voluntary HOA to a mandatory HOA, but you must have permission of 100% of the homeowners. Even one dissenting homeowner would mean that you can't create a mandatory HOA in that community.
