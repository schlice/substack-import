**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you're charged with a crime, is it always better to plead guilty even if you are actually innocent?** *Why would you voluntarily go to jail when you didn't do it?* First of all, if you're charged with a crime, get a lawyer. Then, depending on your case, do what your lawyer tells you to do. The only time you would be pleading guilty is if you are involved in a plea bargain (i.e. you agree to plead guilty in order to receive an agreed-upon lighter sentence so that you can avoid a trial). But on its own, pleading guilty to a crime comes with a whole host of ramifications which follow that pleading. You will be labeled a "felon," and many of your Constitutional rights will be stripped from you. It will become nearly impossible to get a good job, or to do simple things like apply for a loan. Want to rent an apartment, or financing for a car? Good luck. Filling out any official document becomes quite difficult. Second, simply pleading guilty when you are not guilty is a perversion of the justice system, and doesn't serve anyone's best interest: the state, the accuser, nor the defendant. Some people may do this anyway because they're "covering" for someone else --- a family member, a child, etc. They choose to "take the punishment" for someone else, so they claim they did it. When a prosecutor gets an unsolicited, unexpected guilty plea, he's always suspicious of these alternate reasons for such a plea. That's noble, but it doesn't help anyone get to the bottom of the case. Yes, someone goes to jail, but a) it's not the right person, and b) we never find out the real reason for the crime. There are things which can be done if the offender is a minor, or if there are extenuating circumstances. Ideally, we want the *right* person to go to jail, not just *a* person.
