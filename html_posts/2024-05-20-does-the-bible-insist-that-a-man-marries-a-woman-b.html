**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, that's a cultural norm, not a Biblical command. The Bible says that sex is to only take place between a married man and the wife to whom he is married. The sex outside of marriage was already a sin, so we're already well past following God's rules on this one. A man marrying the woman he's knocked up is a man taking responsibility for his actions, and committing to raise the child in a common household with the child's mother. Despite the current society's elevation and laudation of single motherhood, the statistics do not lie: children have ***the best possible chance for a good outcome in life*** when they are raised by their biological parents in a common household.[\[1\]](#HarIJ){#cite-HarIJ .citation_link}[\[2\]](#rqeYd){#cite-rqeYd .citation_link} This well-known-but-not-frequently-discussed statistic morphed into a societal norm, which is often expressed in polite company this way: *Well, are you going to do the right thing and marry her?* "Doing the right thing" means owning up to your mistakes (and the implication here is that impregnating a girl outside of marriage is a mistake, even without the religious context) and doing what's *best* for the woman and the baby. The idea is that in the traditional context of "being a man," fixing your own mistakes is at the top of the list.
