--- title: "If cops see something suspicious outside of your home, can they search without probable cause?" date: "2022-08-07" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer, as with all legal answers, is "it depends." But if your "something suspicious" doesn't rise to the level of probable cause, they cannot search inside the home, and certainly not without a warrant. There are some exceptions to the need to get a warrant. Here's a few examples: - Is the "something suspicious" they see accompanied by sounds from inside the home which would indicate to a reasonable person that someone was hurt or injured inside and needed medical assistance or was otherwise in trouble? - Is someone in the house on parole? - Did someone in the house give permission to search? In other words, are there "exigent circumstances" that would lead a reasonable person to believe that a crime was taking place, or was about to take place, or someone was hurt, or a fugitive was about to escape, or evidence was about to be destroyed? If so (and if the officer can articulate that suspicion in court), then he can search without a warrant. But all of those things amount to "probable cause" in some way. There must always be probable cause, either through exigency, or a warrant. A police officer cannot search a home without probable cause if he wants any of the evidence to be admissible in court. Also don't forget, he doesn't have to tell you what his probable cause is. He only has to tell the judge. \ Original question: If cops see something suspicious outside of your home, can they search without probable cause?
