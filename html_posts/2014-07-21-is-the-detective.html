The most important one:\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} > **CLUE FIVE:** He mentions that it\'s the detectives curse to have the > solution right under your nose, but to be paying attention to the > wrong clues. \ The detective is never fortunate enough to have the solution right under his nose. In the case of my big Italian schnoz, if it was there, I likely would miss it too.\ \ From what I know of actual detectives (I was raised by one), the clues aren\'t elusive. They are right there, staring you plainly in the face, not hiding under your nose (regardless of how big it may be). It is the **[facts]{.underline}**, on the other hand, that take a great deal of hard work to find, and often are still elusive. I have witnessed investigations that have gone on for months and have produced nothing. I have witnessed others that have gone on for months and finally revealed something which the detective could use to find and close in on the criminal and eventually arrest (and hold) the same criminal.\ \ The Detective\'s Curse, therefore, in my opinion, is knowing exactly who did it and how they did it, but being unable to prove it with hard evidence, and having \"the establishment\" try to thwart your efforts at every turn.
