**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is smoking cigarettes like another master in your life such as serving both God and money? Is it a sin? Something a Christian should do and which God approves of? Is it wrong for a Christian to smoke? Looking to make God happy** There's a lot to unpack here. Let's take it a little bit at a time. **Is smoking cigarettes like another master in your life such as serving both God and money?** It can be. The problem with having two masters is that only one of them is worthy of being master. So for example, if your smoking is interfering with your ability to be a good Christian, a good spouse, a good parent, a good friend, then it's certainly possible that smoking could be another master in your life. **Is it a sin? Something a Christian should do and which God approves of?** No, smoking is not sinful, in the traditional way we think of sins. It's a habit, and like all habits, some are less dangerous than others. It's a dangerous habit. Nicotine is not an intoxicant (and the Bible definitely talks about drunkenness), but too much smoking can shorten your life, and that damaging your body this way is selfish to others. It can cause you to sin, such as if you lie to others about your smoking, or quitting smoking. **Is it wrong for a Christian to smoke? Looking to make God happy** God is happy when you love him and you trust him. He's happy when you give your life to him and you let him drive, so to speak. He's happy when you bring your troubles to him, even though he already knows your troubles and just wants to hear it from you. So instead of talking to us about making him happy, talk to him. Tell him that you want to do what's right and you want to please him with your actions, and let him tell you what you should do, and then do that.
