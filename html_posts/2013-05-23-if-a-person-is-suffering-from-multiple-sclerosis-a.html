Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [As always, the true answer is \"it depends.\" The patient and his/her doctor will need to evaluate the specific circumstances in order to determine the best course of treatment. It depends on the age and general overall and neurological health of the patient, the degree of advancement of the MS, as well as the type of MS the patient has. If the person is older and the MS is not exceedingly advanced, assuming the patient has RRMS, the patient and his/her doctor may choose to do nothing and simply allow the disease to progress, and deal with the relapses as they occur. Medication is certainly an option; however, in most cases, there isn\'t a surgery that is helpful.]{.rendered_qtext} ***Disclaimer:*** ***This answer is not professional medical advice****, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
