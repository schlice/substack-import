--- title: '"Hello Pro lifers. Lets say you have a 16 year old daughter who had consensual sex and got pregnant. Do you believe a federal or state government in the state you live in should get to decide if she is allowed to get an abortion?"' date: '2024-09-01' tags: - environment - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You asked what I believe, and I'm not sure that's really what you wanted to hear, but here goes. The answer to your question: **no**, I don't believe that a government should decide if she is allowed to get an abortion. She should not be allowed to get an abortion, period, and it should be beyond the reach of government to authorize such a thing. I don't believe a federal or state government has the right to take a life without due process of law (it's in the Constitution, it's in the 5th and 14th Amendments). And unless there has been a trial and the baby has been found guilty of a capital offense and has been given the death penalty, we should not be **permitted** to take its life. I teach my children to take responsibility for their actions. They are well aware of the purpose of sex, and that is to create babies. Yes it's fun, but it is fun *because* it encourages humans to do it more, in order to...wait for it...make babies. Physiologically, it is fun so that humans do it to make more humans. So yes, my 16-year-old daughter would be homeschooled for a year (in the middle of high school) and would have that baby and then likely give it up for adoption. Look, I understand that abortion is a very volatile issue. And I understand that women have bodily autonomy, and that this autonomy is very important. But what we have with abortion, in my view, is a ***conflict of rights*** issue, and it is a conflict between the right of the woman to bodily autonomy and the right of the baby to live. Scientifically, the baby is a living human. We know it is human because it has two human parents and it produces human proteins (not frog or carrot proteins). We know it is alive because it is growing and consuming energy. **I believe that all living humans have the right to live, and to not have their life unjustly taken from them.** I can't think of a way to *consistently* define equal human rights that excludes fetuses but also includes every kind of born person. I *also* can't think of a *consistent* reason to allow someone's right to bodily autonomy to trump someone else's right to live. And again, I do think that women have bodily autonomy, and while I agree that the right to bodily autonomy is important, I don't think that the right to bodily autonomy gives anyone the right to kill another human. Take the case of conjoined twins, where separating them means that only one will live. Does the right to bodily autonomy of the one twin give them the right to kill the other twin? Which twin's right to bodily autonomy trumps the other twin's right to life? Society has lots of conflicts of rights, and I do think that the woman's right to bodily autonomy is valuable and important. But in this particular case of this particular conflict of rights, I think the baby's right to life outweighs the mother's right to bodily autonomy.
