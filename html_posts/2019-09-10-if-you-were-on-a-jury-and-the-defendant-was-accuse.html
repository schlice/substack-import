Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember that the trial phase is to determine if the defendant did what he was accused of doing. That\'s it. Did the prosecutor prove, beyond a reasonable doubt, that a crime had been committed. That\'s what the jury determines. Your personal opinion as to whether or not this should actually be a crime, or if you were the DA whether or not you would have pursued charges, is all irrelevant. If you can\'t decide the case on its merits, without second guessing the intent of the law or it\'s application, be sure to tell the judge before the trial starts so you can be sent home. Where leniency is warranted is in sentencing. That\'s where we say things like it didn\'t create a nuisance, his work was as good or better than the city, etc. That\'s where he is able to walk away with a slap on the wrist. I hope you can see the difference.
