**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The salient part in the Fifth Amendment is bolded: > No person shall be held to answer for a capital, or otherwise infamous > crime, unless on a presentment or indictment of a Grand Jury, except > in cases arising in the land or naval forces, or in the Militia, when > in actual service in time of War or public danger; nor shall any > person be subject for the same offence to be twice put in jeopardy of > life or limb; **nor shall be compelled in any criminal case to be a > witness against himself,** nor be deprived of life, liberty, or > property, without due process of law; nor shall private property be > taken for public use, without just compensation. The 5th Amendment simply says that you cannot be forced, in a criminal case, to be a witness against yourself. So if, in answering a question, the answer would implicate you criminally, you are not required to answer that question. It doesn't matter the subject of the question. If the answer would implicate you, then you are not required to answer it. Now, prosecutors could ***cut a deal*** with him, wherein he would answer the question with a **guarantee** that he would not be indicted for any criminal behavior of his which his testimony may or may not reveal. If they really want to "nail Trump," they'd offer him that deal. If they're not offering him that deal, it's because they're not going after Trump.
