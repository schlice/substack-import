--- title: "Are all sidewalks installed in public rights of way in the United States required to meet ADA requirements for grade, etc?" date: "2019-04-30" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} All new construction and modifications to existing construction is required to meet ADA rules for sidewalks. The slope for all landings must be between a minimum of 1% for drainage and a maximum of 2% for pedestrian stability in all directions. A minimum 5 ft. x 5 ft. landing must be provided wherever two sidewalks cross, or a turn may be required. ADA ramp specifications require a 1:12 ramp slope ratio which equals 4.8 degrees slope or one foot of ramp for each inch of rise. For instance, a 30 inch rise requires a 30 foot ramp. ADA guidelines require a minimum 5\' x 5\' flat, unobstructed area at the top and bottom of the ramp.
