Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Benign MS is not a form of MS. It is a term used to describe **Relapsing-Remitting MS** (RRMS) with periods of relapse characterized by very mild symptoms, and extended periods of remission (in the span of years). You can really only say that you have benign MS if you've gone at least 15 years with no symptoms, and you have little to no disability related to MS. **Benign MS does not mean that you do not have MS**. This simply means that your *symptoms* are mild to non-existent. MRIs still show disease progress, even if the progress does not produce symptoms. Additionally, benign MS can become active at any time. Most neurologists shy away from this term because it only describes *past experience,* not future potential. And it confers a false sense of security which may lead patients to forego needed treatment.
