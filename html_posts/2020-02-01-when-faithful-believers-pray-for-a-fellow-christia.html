--- title: "When faithful believers pray for a fellow Christian to be healed of cancer, and that fellow Christian suffers terribly and dies despite such prayer, what are the faithful to think? How does a Christian make sense of unanswered prayers for healing?" date: "2020-02-01" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So in essence, you are asking if prayer is even worth it, because sometimes we don\'t get what we asked for, is that it? Have you ever asked for something as a child that you didn\'t get? Perhaps a special toy, or a certain food. Why didn\'t you get that thing? In most cases, it was because your parent determined that you would ultimately be better off without it, or maybe because they were saving that money for something else, or maybe because it wasn\'t as great as you thought it was. In any case, someone with more knowledge, wisdom and foresight, made a decision on your behalf, despite your insistence that you really wanted it and that it would definitely enhance your life. While an imperfect analogy, it\'s close enough for our purposes. We do not know what God has planned for us. We do not know what God has planned for any of us. We don\'t know how His choices fit into His plan. We may never know during this lifetime. Only when we see Him face to face will we ever get a small glimpse of how all the pieces for together. But now? We simply don\'t know. What we do know for sure is that He has promised that our lives will be used to further His kingdom and to bring others in as well. He has promised that we will be with Him. And we know that he has always kept His promises, despite our inability to see the big picture. So we may ask for healing, but we do so by saying *if it is your will, heal this person.* And that\'s the key. It may not be His will that this person is healed. Prayer is not like a vending machine, where you put the money in and you get your desired product. Prayer is a pleading of the heart, a heart He already knows. He knows your pain, and he feels it as acutely as you do. But He has plans for you. He has plans for all of us. Faith, therefore, is our ability to trust that He has a plan, and that He will keep His promises.
