**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In 2013 25,000 federal workers successfully sued for damages because they were forced to work without pay. Will 2018-19 workers do the same? Do they have a similar case?** *If* a government worker was **forced** to work without pay (i.e. they were in a *non-essential* job and they were told that they had to work or they'd lose their job, or they were in an *essential job* and were told that they had to work without pay), then they should again sue for damages, and will again likely win.
