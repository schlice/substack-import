--- title: '"If Trump pardoned General Flynn, Counsel Mueller could still subpoena him to testify. If Flynn refuses to testify, Mueller could hold him in contempt. If Trump pardons him for the contempt, would that constitute obstruction of justice?"' date: '2018-01-05' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Since the President has the Constitutional authority to issue pardons, they cannot be considered obstruction of justice. However, Congress could consider them an abuse of power and choose to impeach because of them, although they'd have a very tough hill to climb to explain how a president using his Constitutionally-enumerated powers amounts to an impeachable offense.
