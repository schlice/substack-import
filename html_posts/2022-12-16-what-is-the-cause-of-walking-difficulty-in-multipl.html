--- title: '"What is the cause of walking difficulty in multiple sclerosis and how can it be treated?"' date: '2022-12-16' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Gait problems are one of the most common movement symptoms of MS. Walking trouble can be caused by a number of factors: - Weakness. Muscle weakness occurs either through disuse, or nerve damage. Weakness in the leg muscles can lead to changes in normal walking stride, which can lead to pain, making walking even more challenging. - Spasticity. Muscle tightness or twitching can affect walking. - Balance. Balance problems typically produce a swaying, "drunk-like" walking pattern called *ataxia*. - Sensory problems. Some people with MS have trouble feeling their feet on the floor, leading to challenges walking. This is also known as *sensory ataxia*. - Fatigue. Fatigue, or *MS Lassitude*, can induce walking problems when it happens. MS walking difficulties are usually most effectively treated through physical therapy. You can also use appropriate assistive devices, and there are some medications which can be used to treat either the underlying symptoms above, or one specific medication (Ampyra) which can treat walking speed. Ampyra, by the way, is the first medication developed specifically to treat a symptom of MS. If you are having difficulty walking due to MS, please talk to your neurologist or your primary care doctor.
