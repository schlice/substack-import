**Content:** [Also, the plane is still \"flying\" even after it touches down. The control surfaces still work after the wheels have touched down, so the pilot uses the control yoke to keep the aircraft in the correct attitude until the airspeed/ground speed has reduced enough that the wings no longer produce lift.\ \ In lighter aircraft (gliders, for example), the wings produce so much lift that in a decent headwind (like 10 knots), they continue to produce lift even after the aircraft has come to a complete stop. We could balance a glider on its one wheel after it had come to a complete stop just using the ailerons.]{.rendered_qtext}
