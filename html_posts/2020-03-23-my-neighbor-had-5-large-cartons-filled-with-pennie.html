Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My neighbor had 5 large cartons filled with pennies. His pickup conked and hauled pennies to a car dealership and bought a truck, but the dealership refused to accept the legal tender. Why?** Most businesses don't have large coin counters expecting people to bring in pennies as currency. While pennies are indeed legal tender, Federal law does not compel any business to accept them. The business can indeed ask you to change the pennies into larger denomination currency at a nearby bank just as easily as they can ask you to change a \$100 bill into smaller denomination bills.
