**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you get \"swatted\", the 911 call that generated the SWAT response was unlawful. Does that mean you have the right to resist an unlawful arrest?** We have said it many times on Quora. Only a judge can determine if an arrest is unlawful. Judges are involved *way, way after* the actual arrest takes place. An arrest is *presumed to be lawful* if the police officer is acting under color of law, and he has probable cause to effect an arrest. That makes the arrest, *de facto*, lawful, until a judge says that it is otherwise. And even if the SWATting occurred, the arrest would still be lawful because the police only have to meet the probable cause standard at the time that the arrest was made. Using that standard, the arrest itself was still lawful, but the state would not press charges since you were the victim of an unlawful 911 call. Therefore, since only a judge can determine if an arrest is unlawful, you have no right, under law, to resist the actions of the police. Do what they tell you, and "tell it to the judge."
