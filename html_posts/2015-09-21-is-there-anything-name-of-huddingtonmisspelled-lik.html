**Content:** [Yes, you\'re thinking of [[Huntington\'s disease (HD)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3524eb&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huntington's_disease){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, which is a genetic, degenerative neurological disorder which can develop any time in a person\'s life, but will generally reduce the lifespan of the affected person to about 20 years following the initial diagnosis. The patient will eventually die of pneumonia, heart failure, or other complications.\ \ This disease is known as the quintessential family disease, because each child of a parent with HD has a 50/50 chance of developing the disease.\ \ HD is often described as having ALS, Parkinson\'s and Alzheimer\'s \-- simultaneously. Early stage HD usually involves mood and cognition. Middle stage HD involves more of the movement disorder: inability to maintain normal movement and involuntary movements, known as \"chorea\". Late stage HD involves an inability to perform most gross motor functions, and the patient depends entirely upon caregivers for support. The patient usually cannot speak, but is still cognizant and recognizes friends and family.]{.rendered_qtext}
