**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I see where you're going here. Just know that Quora is not a debate site. I'm not here to debate you about the US invasion of Iraq. If you want to ask about the legality of the US invasion of Iraq in 2003, then ask that specific question. I will delete any further attempts to quibble with my answer, but because I'm feeling rather litigious today, I'll give you an answer. The legality of the war was questioned from day 1. Any healthy democracy should question the motives of its leaders. Ultimately though, under national law, we approved the war. Congress authorized the war under the [[Iraq Resolution](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0af21a&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joint_Resolution_to_Authorize_the_Use_of_United_States_Armed_Forces_Against_Iraq){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, otherwise known as the "Joint Resolution to Authorize the Use of United States Armed Forces Against Iraq." This authorized the war under United States law. Under international law, the case is far more fuzzy. The US and other member states used the argument that Iraq was in violation of UN Resolutions 660 and 678 when they invaded Kuwait in 1990. The US also used Resolution 1441 as a legal standard to invade Iraq, when they failed to fully submit to weapons inspections and failed to fully comply with UN weapons inspection protocols. While Resolution 1441 did not legally allow individual member states to act without Security Council authorization, Resolution 678 allowed individual member states to work with the government of Kuwait to enforce Resolution 660 with regard to Kuwaiti sovereignty. So while it's sketchy, the legal justification is there. Let me give you a related example here in the US. When Obamacare was found to be legal, the Supreme Court said that you have to make all reasonable attempts to verify that a law is constitutional. The law's authors said that Obamacare imposed a "fee" if you didn't comply; they said very plainly it was not a tax. The Supreme Court said, basically, it is levied by Congress, it is collected by the IRS, it is collected as part of your taxes, so it's a tax, and since Congress is authorized to raise taxes, it's legal. Regardless of what they said (it's a fee, not a tax), it's a tax, and that makes it legal. Using that same justification, while the wording of the invasion may have been incorrect, and the primary reason for invasion may have been the incorrect primary reason, if there is justification **at all** under international law, we must therefore submit that the invasion is legal and authorized.
