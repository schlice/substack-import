--- title: "Is it possible to be charged with both assault and battery?" date: "2022-09-14" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Assault and battery are two separate offenses. But you don't get "charged" with assault and battery, you get "sued" for assault and battery. Criminal assault includes both the assault (the threat of physical harm) and the battery (the actual physical harm). "Assault" and "battery" (in the jurisdictions which have these torts) are *civil* crimes, and so you can be sued separately for assault, or for battery, or for both together (which is where the term "assault and battery" comes from). Remember, these crimes are state-level crimes, and they are not defined equally across all of the states. This definition is only applicable to the states which have assault and battery as separate torts, and don't call their criminal assault crime "assault and battery."
