**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} China has taken on a very strict "Covid-zero" policy to try to eradicate the virus. Because there is a not-zero chance that your cat or dog can transmit the virus to a human (even though that risk is really, really small), once the pets were found to be Covid-positive, they were euthanized. The US doesn't recommend euthanizing Covid-positive pets. They instead recommend the more commonsense approach of isolating the pets in a room and wearing gloves when handling or interacting with them, until they test negative. You know, kinda like you do with Covid-positive *people*.
