--- title: "What are the statistics of people in the US having used their firearms to actually protect themselves during an attack?" date: "2016-06-16" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [A2A\ \ It is *estimated* that there are between 1 and 2 million defensive gun uses in the US each year.\ \ Why is it only *estimated*?\ \ Most of these defensive gun uses are **never reported**, because many of them occur ***without a single shot being fired***. The mere **presence** of a firearm turns a victim into an opponent, and many criminals want to encounter victims, not opponents, so they turn tail and run away.\ \ And even if they did report the incident to the police, how would *they* track it? Departments track statistics differently, and some do not even track \"non-crime\" incidents. For those that do, categorization is all over the board. Statistics become unreliable.\ \ For that reason, it is nearly impossible to come up with useful, reliable statistics about defensive gun uses in the United States.]{.rendered_qtext}
