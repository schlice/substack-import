--- title: '"Is it worth the time and resources to publicize U.S. mass shootings in the media? No policy changes seem possible. Should we just collectively learn to shrug and ignore these stories?"' date: '2015-10-02' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There is always a point to publicizing mass shootings.\ \ The problem is, most people *miss the point.* The President is a big reason most people miss the point.\ \ Even before they finished searching the campus for bombs, before they could ascertain exactly what had happened, the President was calling for increased gun control.\ \ Missed the point.\ \ I think most people intuitively know that *the shooting wasn\'t caused by guns.* So how can you hope to *fix* the issue when you\'re not addressing the *cause*?\ \ But so many people tend to focus on the gun. It\'s easy to do that, isn\'t it? It\'s easy to focus on the gun, because it\'s *tangible*. It\'s a real thing you can hold and presumably regulate.\ \ People, on the other hand, are *messy.* It\'s decidedly harder to fix *people* than it is to fix *guns.* And no one wants to admit that drugs can\'t help every mentally ill person, nor that deinstitutionalization wasn\'t a rousing success for everyone. As is often the case, a one-size-fits-all solution is never the answer, as there are always some for whom the answer doesn\'t fit.\ \ If we actually use incidents like these to highlight the failings of the mental health system in the country, we could start making positive changes to that system.\ \ Until then, incidents like these will only be ratings fodder for the drive-by media.]{.rendered_qtext}
