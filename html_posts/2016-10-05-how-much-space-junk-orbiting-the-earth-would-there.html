**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I always hesitate to add anything to an answer that [[Robert Frost](https://www.quora.com/profile/Robert-Frost-1)]{.qlink_container} has already answered, because it's hard to add any credibility after him, but I do have one relevant piece of information. I worked in NORAD. Why, perchance, is that relevant? As part of NORAD's Space Control mission, the Space crew maintains the SCAT (that's pronounced "S - Cat", not "scat") The SCAT is the "space catalog" which contains tens of thousands of pieces of cataloged space junk down to the size of a baseball. We regularly maintain the SCAT by ensuring the pieces of space junk are in their correct orbits and we know where they are (i.e. their orbits have not changed significantly, and have not decayed). We have the SCAT in a big database which can actually be represented graphically (not to scale, of course). It's actually quite beautiful when represented that way. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-5850a4f8fbb9bea725aa2013088a5675){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-5850a4f8fbb9bea725aa2013088a5675" master_w="998" master_h="471"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*ComSpOC*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d1b42a&url=https://comspoc.com/spacebook/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="comspoc.com"}]{.qlink_container}*)* When the Shuttle was flying (when I was there, it was), we would insert the shuttle's actual flight path into the database, draw a 10 meter "box" around the shuttle, and fly it forward in time, about 36 hours forward to be precise. We would then have the computer tell us if there was to be any "convergence" between the box and any debris in the SCAT. If there was, we'd call whoever was controlling that Shuttle mission and advise them of the convergence. I know that our input has prompted several course corrections during the course of the Shuttle program. I would not be surprised in the least if we do the same thing with the ISS today.
