Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **No.** God will accept you **right now, as you are.** Broken, battered, and at the end of our rope, not perfect and shiny. > But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still > sinners, Christ died for us.\ > --- Romans 5:8 NIV Jesus didn't come to heal the *well*, he came to heal the *sick*. And all you have to do is **repent** and **believe**. It's as simple as ABC: - **A**dmit to God (and to yourself) that you are a sinner and without him you are lost. - **B**elieve that Christ died as a penal substitute for your sins - **C**onfess that Jesus Christ is your Lord and savior, and **C**ommit to turn away from your old life of sin. **AFTER** you are saved, God will begin the long work of helping you clean up your act. It's a lifelong process called *sanctification*. With each passing day, the old ways will fade away, and you'll learn a new way to live. It takes effort, but keep it up, because God will never, ever give up on you. \ Original question: Do I have to clean up my act before God can forgive me?
