**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you were on the jury, how would you have voted in the case of the off-duty Dallas police officer shooting her upstairs neighbor?** Exactly as the jury did. There's not a lot of wiggle room here. She claimed that the Castle Doctrine applied even though she made a "mistake of fact" and entered the wrong apartment. She truly believed she was in her own, and that belief is what drove her desire for self-defense. However, the Texas Castle Doctrine law is very clear. > (c)â€‚A person **who has a right to be present** at the location where > the deadly force is used, who has not provoked the person against whom > the deadly force is used, and who is not engaged in criminal activity > at the time the deadly force is used is not required to retreat before > using deadly force as described by this section. She did not have "a right to be present" in the other person's home, so there is no application of the Texas Castle Doctrine, according to plain language of the statute. Therefore, Castle Doctrine theory did not apply, so a determination of whether or not she was justified in shooting took place, and they found quite clearly that a reasonable person would not have feared for their life in the same situation. Guilty, although if I had a choice, it would have been manslaughter, not murder. Murder is a killing with malice and forethought, and this was more an unjustifiable homicide than a murder. I would have called it manslaughter, not murder, but this is Texas, so...
