**Content:** [Neither is wonderful, and I can\'t speak to the Norway case, but two things about Columbine:\ \ 1. It was Columbine that forever changed the way we handle what we now call \"active shooter\" scenarios. Up until that point, we handled them like hostage scenarios, assuming the shooter actually wants to live and actually doesn\'t want to kill hostages. Things are very much different now. Active shooters want to kill as many people as they possibly can before they get killed or take their own life. They don\'t expect to walk away from the incident. It\'s a \"blaze of glory\" scenario now.\ \ 2. Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold took their own lives; the police did not shoot them.]{.rendered_qtext}
