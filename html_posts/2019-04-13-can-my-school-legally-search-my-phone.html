**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can my school legally take my phone and search it in USA?** The answer, as is the answer to any legal question, is "it depends." Schools generally have more leeway than law enforcement when it comes to confiscating and searching anything on school grounds, including lockers, backpacks, and yes, mobile devices. But of course, these policies all vary by state. In general, a school can remove anything from a student which is disruptive to the learning environment. So can the school take your phone? Yes, they can. Whether or not they can *search* your phone varies wildly by state. Some states have legislated this issue. For example, California students have as many rights as anyone on the street when it comes to privacy and devices. For students there, searches require warrants or legitimate emergencies. Other states have made it clear that students have no expectations of privacy when it comes to their devices on school grounds. Because of the lack of clarity and the patchwork of laws and school policies, it's important for everyone --- parents, students, teachers, and administrators --- to seek clarity on the rules that apply to them. It's best to do that *before* an incident occurs, as it will avoid a lot of unnecessary headaches and confusion.
