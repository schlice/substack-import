Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Apples and cars. The comparison between the US and Sweden is totally unhelpful. US police officers do not train for only 19 weeks. US police officers only receive, on average (as all departments across the country are different), about 21 weeks of classroom instruction *before they are sworn and* can go out on patrol. But that doesn't mean *solo* patrol taking calls for service. This starts a multi-month period of ***on-the-job training*** (OJT) which is overseen by a Field Training Officer (FTO). The rookie must ride as part of a 2-man car until they have reached a certain level of proficiency. Usually, field training under one or more FTOs takes about six months, but this can vary from person to person, and from department to department. And we should recall that people in the US want to *cut* funding to police departments, not increase it, so they are necessarily calling for that training to be *reduced* due to budget cuts, not *increased*. \ Original question: Should cops in the United States have to train for three years before they start working as a cop just like they do in Sweden, or should the US keep the current 19-week training before becoming a cop?
