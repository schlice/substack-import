**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why are President Trump's supporters attacking the Virginia restaurant that refused service to Sarah Huckabee Sanders?** Businesses can refuse to serve anyone for any non-protected reason, as long as they are *consistent* with their behavior. As long as they deny service to *every* government employee, they are legally okay. But denying service to *one* government employee and not *all* of them is inconsistent, and is probably something the law would refer to as *discrimination*. The Civil Rights Act made discrimination illegal. Just as you can't discriminate against a person simply because you don't like what they're wearing (unless you don't allow that article of clothing in your establishment at all, regardless of who is wearing it), you can't discriminate against a person simply because you don't like them personally, or you don't like what they do for a living. While she may have a good case for a discrimination lawsuit, she probably will just drop it. And in a free-market economy, people have the right to protest and boycott the restaurant for its treatment. That's how we should be dealing with these types of issues: with our wallets.
