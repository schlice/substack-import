**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **I received a ticket in Missouri from a red light camera. It included photos of my truck, my plates, and my friend in the driver seat. Since my friend was operating my vehicle am I obligated to incriminate him if I go to court to fight the ticket?** No. You are only obligated to prove that you were not the driver. You are not obligated to incriminate the actual driver; you are only obligated to prove that the driver was not you. And you are only obligated to prove that you were not the driver because Missouri local laws surrounding red light cameras are all over the place. [[https://www.bleyevanslaw.com/blo\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=732324&url=https://www.bleyevanslaw.com/blog/missouri-strikes-down-red-light-camera-ordinances/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="bleyevanslaw.com"}]{.qlink_container} > In [[*Sarah Tupper v. City of St. > Louis*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6350a2&url=https://www.courts.mo.gov/file.jsp?id%3D90869){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="mo.gov"}]{.qlink_container}, the Court found that St. > Louis' red-light camera ordinance was unconstitutional because it > shifted the burden of proof to the defendant, typically the owner of > the vehicle, requiring them to prove that they were not the driver of > the car at the time that it ran the light, as opposed to requiring the > prosecutor to prove that the defendant was the driver. So whichever city or town gave you the red light ticket has an unconstitutional system in place, because it has shifted the burden of proof to you, requiring you to go to court and prove you were not the driver of the car at the time it ran the red light. If you want to ***truly*** fight this, get yourself an attorney and make the claim that the locality where you got the ticket has an unconstitutional red light camera ordinance in accordance with *Sarah Tupper v.
