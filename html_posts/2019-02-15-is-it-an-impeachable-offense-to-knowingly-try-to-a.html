**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it an impeachable offense to knowingly try to artificially create a National Emergency, even if there is a process to stop you?** There really isn't a definition of what constitutes an impeachable offense. The Constitution says "high crimes and misdemeanors," but that's not a specific legal definition. When Gerald R. Ford was in the House, he said that "an impeachable offense is whatever a majority of the House of Representatives considers it to be at a given moment in history." Therefore, if a majority of the House believes that the President is abusing the national emergency power, they can indeed file articles of impeachment.
