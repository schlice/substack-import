**Content:** [Like other posters have stated, thank you for your rational, reasoned approach to your question.\ \ I\'m going to take a slightly different tack than the other respondents who discussed what it would take to change the current law in the US to disallow personal gun ownership.\ \ Your specific question asked \"why weapons are so important to people in USA.\"\ \ The answer to that specific question is found in US history and its founding. It was individual gun ownership that founded the country. It was one of the things that the colonists were escaping from in England. And it was one of the very first rights specifically enumerated in the amendments to the US Constitution because it was so fundamental to the freedoms the founders wanted to enshrine.\ \ The Constitution is a definition of what government is allowed to do. The Constitution is supposed to limit overreach, and to keep government to a manageable size by defining the limits of governmental power. The Statists in the Continental Congress at the time were not satisfied that the Constitution would be able to limit governmental overreach (turns out they were right), even though the Constitution defined specifically what the government was allowed to do, and turned all other rights over to the states and the people. So they wrote a number of Amendments to the Constitution to specifically define rights of the people that were to be enshrined and never curtailed. Some of those rights:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - The right to speak against the government if you choose (you would have been arrested in England for speaking out against the King\'s policies) - The right to practice whatever religion you wanted, or to practice no religion at all (the King demanded the State religion be the Church of England). - The right to the sanctity of one\'s own home (the King quartered soldiers in peoples\' homes in peacetime). - The right to keep and bear arms (following the colonists\' successful uprising against the King\'s soldiers, the colonists wanted to ensure that no one would ever be denied the right to keep and bear firearms in defense of their property or their country). The nation was founded on the belief that an armed population could never be oppressed by government. And the colonists\' inherent distrust of government ensured that these principles were ingrained in the psyche, the very character, of the nation from the very beginning.\ \ Americans believe that the following of these principles upon which the nation was founded will ensure her continued survival.\ \ To uproot that would mean uprooting the very fabric that founded the nation.
