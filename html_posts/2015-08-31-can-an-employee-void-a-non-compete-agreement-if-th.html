--- title: '"Can an employee void a non-compete agreement if their company cannot enforce a drug free workplace?"' date: '2015-08-31' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [I\'m a little late to the party, but since I got the A2A, I don\'t see how you can justify breaking your non-compete.\ \ First of all, the company just got acquired.\ \ Second, just because the company was hired by a company with a Colorado headquarters says absolutely nothing about their legal ability to maintain a drug-free workforce. Since the statute says that an interstate company must be compliant with both Federal and state laws, companies that do business in Colorado can still legally enforce a drug-free workplace. Whether or not they do is also their business.\ \ However, whether or not they do has no bearing on the enforceability of a non-compete clause. The two are completely separate provisions of employment.]{.rendered_qtext}
