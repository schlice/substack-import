But the longer we can keep a driver in his/her seat, the more the possibility arises that the driver could potentially regain control of that vehicle.\ \ What about the equivalent of a PIT? The car would still be operable, but the driver could be bounced out of his seat.\ \ Anyway, I agree that it\'s really unlikely, but it is possible. I think that arguing that it is really unlikely that such an accident scenario could actually happen is not a good argument for repealing laws requiring driver safety restraints. You stand a better chance limiting the seat belt requirement to speeds over 50 miles per hour, where you can show that at those speeds, such an accident is likely to result in deaths whereas below those speeds, you\'re more likely that the seat belt will injure the person rather than save them.]{.rendered_qtext}
