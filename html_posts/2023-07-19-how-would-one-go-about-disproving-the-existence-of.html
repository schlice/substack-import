--- title: "How would one go about disproving the existence of God using mathematics and/or logic (without resorting to faith)?" date: "2023-07-19" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Philosopher and scholar Mortimer Adler said, "\"An affirmative existential proposition can be proved, but a negative existential proposition\--one that denies the existence of something\--cannot be proved.\" In other words, you can prove the existence of something, but you can't prove that something does not exist. Therefore, there is no mathematical or logical proof you can provide which would show that God does not exist. At best, what exists is logical arguments against the logical proofs which argue that God does exist.
