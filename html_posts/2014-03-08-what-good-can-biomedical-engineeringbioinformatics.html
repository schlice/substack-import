**Content:** [Neural engineering (or neuroengineering) is the sub-class of biomedical engineering that focuses on using engineering to enhance or repair neural systems. These people work at the interface between the body and the machine. Two applications come to mind right away:\ \ Biocompatible prostheses: prostheses that respond directly to neural input. It doesn\'t have to be an artificial limb, either. Imagine putting on an exosuit (think a Spandex bodysuit with filaments running through it) and having it conduct the signals from the brain to the body because the nerves that are supposed to perform that function are shot. You would be able to walk again, or grab things correctly. This would be a coup for MS patients. It\'s also something that spinal-cord injury patients would like to see as well.\ \ Neural repair: one of the problems that MS patients face today is that nerves don\'t readily repair themselves. Even if the plaques are removed, the damaged nerves remain. Many times, especially in the brain, the function that was lost can be \"re-learned\" using other neural pathways. But in the spine especially, nerve tissue does not readily re-grow. Finding a way to influence nerve cells to regenerate (perhaps by bio-engineering adult stem cells) could mean that the body\'s own ability to heal itself could be enhanced and tuned to fix the damage caused by MS.]{.rendered_qtext}
