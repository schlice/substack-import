Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, gun owners should not be able to overrule elections, and if that's what you got after you read that article, you should think about some critical reasoning classes at your local community college. There are plenty, generally offered by the philosophy department. Check them out. The ultimate purpose of the Second Amendment is to ensure that no government ever disarms the populace. The first step towards dictatorship is for a government to disarm the people. A disarmed populace can be controlled by force. The framers were worried that a disarmed populace in combination with a standing army which could be used against them would be two of the three legs of the chair upon which sat the next dictator of the United States. The framers allowed the standing army they were so worried about, but ensured that as a compromise, that the people could never be disarmed. In this way, government could never overrule an election by force. Consequently, we have had peaceful transitions of government for over 200 years.
