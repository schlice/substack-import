**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember that military rifle rounds are jacketed, meaning under most circumstances, they do not deform when striking human flesh. In many cases, the bullet will go \"through and through,\" make a clean puncture wound, effectively removing that soldier from the fight, but increasing (as much as possible) the chance that the soldier can be patched up and still live. This is a type of weapon that is acceptable in war (insofar as any weapon of war is \"acceptable\"). Laser weapons cause permanent blindness. Weapons which don\'t simply remove a soldier from the fight, but even in the best case leave a soldier permanently blind, are not considered to be valid weapons of war. An alternative would be a bright enough strobe light that could temporarily blind a troop but leave no permanent damage like a laser. We have such a weapon, it is called the [[Dazzler.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c4bccb&url=https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dazzler_(weapon)){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}
