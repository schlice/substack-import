**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **When driving in traffic and someone behind you is following closely, like they are in your trunk, what is your reaction?** The correct answer is to increase the distance between you and the car in front of you. This gives you more reaction time to slow down gradually should traffic in front of you come to a rapid stop. If you fail to leave extra space in front of you, you may end up having to brake hard, and the guy behind you is very likely going to end up actually in your trunk. The douche-y answer is to slow down to exactly the speed limit, or maybe five miles an hour or so under the speed limit. Nothing says I have to do the speed limit, and if I'm not being a hazard, oh well. I've had angry drivers go on to the shoulder to get around me when I do that.
