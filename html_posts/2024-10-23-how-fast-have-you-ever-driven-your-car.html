**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In 1993 I got a 1989 Ford Thunderbird SC as a gift. Image courtesy* [[*Greg Gjerdingen from Willmar, USA, CC BY 2.0*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cff51a&url=https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="creativecommons.org"}]{.qlink_container}*, via Wikimedia Commons).* This car had the supercharged and intercooled version of the Essex 3.8L OHV V-6. Mine had the M5R2 5-speed gearbox, and was *Motor Trend's Car of the Year* for 1989. I was home on leave at the time (late November 1992), and so we decided to take the car out for a speed test. We went down to Ocean Parkway, which runs the length of the barrier beaches along Long Island's south shore. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-8f1e0a77ef31f4d971467d6e04a7bb26){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-8f1e0a77ef31f4d971467d6e04a7bb26" master_w="313" master_h="374"} ::: :::: *(Image from* [[*Wikipedia*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=88544f&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_Parkway_(Long_Island)){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}*)* As you can see, it's pretty straight, pretty flat, and in the cold of November, no one was expected to be on that stretch of road. So we took the car down there, and opened it up. At the same time, I was testing a used secondhand radar detector that clipped over your rear view mirror. When we were pulled over, the officer said he clocked us at 147 mph. The radar detector didn't even blink. The officer even took his handheld radar gun out of his car and blasted it directly at our rear view mirror, and we got nothing. Oh well. I guess that detector was secondhand for a reason.
