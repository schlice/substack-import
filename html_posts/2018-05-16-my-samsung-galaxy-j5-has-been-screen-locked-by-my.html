**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **My Samsung Galaxy J5 has been screen-locked by my younger brother. > He downloaded a lock screen app on CH-Play, set a password and > doesn\'t remember it. How can I delete the app and get my phone back > to normal?** You will need to boot into recovery mode and wipe your phone. Please follow [[this guide](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=62d288&url=https://www.digitaltrends.com/mobile/use-recovery-mode-android/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="digitaltrends.com"}]{.qlink_container} to boot your device into recovery mode. You will then select the **Wipe Data/Factory Reset** option from the recovery menu. Use the volume up/volume down buttons to move to the option, and press the power button to select. ***PLEASE NOTE: Doing this will wipe all of your programs and data, and will return the device to factory new settings. You will have to restore your settings from your last Google backup and your apps will need to be re-installed (this will happen when you log back in to your Google account). The third-party app will NOT be re-installed.*** ***AGAIN: THIS IS A DATA DESTRUCTIVE OPERATION! YOUR DEVICE WILL BE WIPED WHEN YOU DO THIS!*** Okay, now that the big warnings are done, understand that this will wipe and re-install your device from the recovery partition. You will need to log back in to your Google account when you re-start the initialization process because of FRP (Factory Reset Protection), so be aware of that. When you re-initialize the phone, it will ask you to restore from a recent Google backup, so your apps and settings will be restored. Any personal data you had on the device *may not* have been backed up in either your Google or Samsung accounts, so be prepared for that. Good luck!
