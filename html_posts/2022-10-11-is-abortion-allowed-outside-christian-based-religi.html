**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes. 1. The [[Unitarian Universalist Church](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=707ed0&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitarian_Universalism){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} specifically allows abortion. In 1978, the Unitarian Universalist Association passed a resolution that declared, \"\...\[the\] right to choice on contraception and abortion are important aspects of the right of privacy, respect for human life, and freedom of conscience of women and their families\".[\[1\]](#EJkaR){#cite-EJkaR .citation_link} 2. While there is no single stance on abortion in the [[Buddhist](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ef4518&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism_and_abortion){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} faith, it is generally looked upon negatively. 3. The [[Sikh](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=28a63d&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikh){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} faith is similar: no specific stance, but generally regarded negatively. Nearly all other non-Christian faiths condemn abortion where the life of the mother is not at risk. I should also note that there are some Christian denominations which are more accepting of abortion. Some denominations like Methodists, Episcopals, Presbyterians, the United Church of Christ, and the Evangelical Lutheran Church of America (among others) are "more permissive" of abortion. Additionally, there are not-insignificant minorities of some Christian denominations which disagree with their denomination's position on abortion. In short, not all Christian denominations are anti-abortion, and not all people who call themselves Christian believe abortion is murder. \ Original question: Is abortion allowed outside Christian-based religious affiliation?
