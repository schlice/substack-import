**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Samsung's Galaxy Fold may be premature, but I still see a foldable iPhone in our future. Do you think so?** It depends on how well the Galaxy Fold does. Since Samsung released their foldable first, Apple will wait to see what happens. Unfortunately with the Galaxy Fold, it's not ready for consumers. First, its release price is \$1980. This is not a consumer device. It's a device for people who are either die-hard Samsung fans, or people who buy things like Supreme or \$600 sneakers with a rapper's name on them. Second, the device is intended to be a foldable tablet, not a device that you use primarily as a phone and then occasionally pop open to use as a tablet. Consumers aren't really interested in foldable tablets just yet. It does do some things surprisingly well, but its high price tag and its lack of appeal to the broader consumer market are things that will make this release of the Galaxy Fold something that is not yet ready for broad consumer adoption. And Apple isn't going to go anywhere near a foldable device until they know that there's a solid market for it. \ What Samsung ***did*** do right was to release a trio of Galaxy S10 devices that exceed Apple in both price and quality. The Galaxy S10e starts at \$749.99, the Galaxy S10 starts at \$899.99, and the Galaxy S10+ starts at \$999.99, and you get 128 GB of storage on each of those. Compared to Apple's lineup, the iPhone XR is also \$749.99, but only comes with 64GB of storage. The iPhone XS and XS Max start at \$999 and \$1,099 respectively, but also only come with 64GB of storage. So just based on price alone, Samsung's offerings appear to win on price. But that would only remain true if Samsung's hardware was at least on par, if not better, than Apple's. And from an objective comparison, Samsung's hardware beats Apple's hardware, hands down. The Snapdragon 855 is faster and more efficient than the A12 Bionic chip Apple is running. Now arguably, as Apple's iOS is custom-designed to run on Apple's A12 chip, iOS still runs more efficiently than Android on a Snapdragon. But it still suffers from thermal throttling that the Snapdragon does not. The Snapdragon hardware is simply a better mobile processor. Samsung's displays beat Apple's displays, hands down. Why would I say that? Apple displays are sourced from --- Samsung. So of course, Samsung is going to produce better display technology for themselves than they are going to provide to Apple. They have other features as well. The new Samsung devices are also sporting under-glass ultrasonic fingerprint readers. This makes them quicker, they don't require visible bright lights to read, and they are more secure, when compared to any other security technology. The new Samsung devices have larger batteries than any other flagship device. Finally, they also sport a feature called Wireless PowerShare, which is essentially Qi charging in reverse. This allows them to charge small Qi-enabled devices, like the Samsung Galaxy Buds, which sport wireless charging in the case.
