**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "God-fearing" is a phrase which usually means understanding the awesome power of the Creator of the Universe, the being who literally put the breath that you breathe into your lungs and has the power to wipe you from existence such that no one would ever know you were there. It is understanding and respecting the power of the Almighty. It means living in awe, respect, obedience, and submission to God's authority. Fear has at its roots both awe and terror. So it's understandable how some people could read "God-fearing" and think that you are to be afraid of God. People are afraid of things all the time, like snakes and spiders, and they are afraid of those things because they can *hurt them.* But this is unjustified with God, as He would never hurt you. *But He's all-powerful! He might send me to hell!* Actually no, He doesn't send anyone to hell. **You send yourself to hell** by disobeying God. He doesn't condemn you to punishment. You condemn yourself to punishment by refusing to repent of your sin. God simply refuses to abide sinners. And if you haven't taken care of business and repented of your sin, God is going to remove you from His presence and send you away. That's not God punishing you. That's you punishing yourself. It's not just a word game. The punishment is all in your hands. It's not like you could be humming along thinking you're saved and then God smites you. That's only true in Hollywood. In reality, whether or not you are saved is totally up to you.
