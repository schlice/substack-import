**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a law was proposed that all child molesters be shot when convicted of their first offense, who would likely oppose such a law and why?** People who are wrongly convicted of molesting a child. It does happen. And apparently, most of the false allegations are not made by the child. They are made by adults who care for children, and the large majority of those are part of court battles over child custody during divorce proceedings. > Findings of multiple studies performed between 1987 and 1995 suggested > that the rate of false allegations ranged from a low of 6% to a high > of 35% of reported child sexual abuse > cases.[\[1\]](#WGXsJ){#cite-WGXsJ .citation_link} Some legal systems lend themselves handily to people abusing the system. In the UK for example, large payouts to alleged victims without proof of the allegations required means that more people will be encouraged to abuse the system.
