--- title: "Florida Governor Ron DeSantis recently signed a Florida bill requiring that state public schools allow children of all faiths a moment of silence or prayer. How will this work with separation of church and state?" date: "2022-04-25" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "A moment of silence" has always been permitted. A moment of silence is not tied to a religion. Students *can* pray if they are so inclined. Students who do not pray can merely remain in silence for a moment. There is no imposition of religion at all in a moment of silence. What has *not* been permitted is the school *leading children in prayer*. Schools have tested this in the past, and they have lost in court. \ What is most disappointing is that the legislature of a state feels that they only way it can compel its own Department of Education to allow students their religion-neutral moment of silence is to write a law.
