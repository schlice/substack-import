**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should people be allowed to walk around naked?** I just answered a different question (but the fundamentals are the same) regarding when it is no longer appropriate to allow little girls to run around topless. [[Bill Stein\'s answer to At what age does it become inappropriate for a little girl to run around topless?](https://www.quora.com/At-what-age-does-it-become-inappropriate-for-a-little-girl-to-run-around-topless/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} So why can't people walk around naked? Well, depending on what part of the world you live in, maybe you can. But at least part of the reason is related to climate. Humans do not have thick coats of fur or layers of blubber to keep us warm. Granted, we are warm-blooded so we don't rely on the environment to heat us, but our skin is definitely not designed to be exposed to bone-chilling cold. Our skin can freeze and our ability to regulate our own body temperature is compromised when it is exposed to the elements. But the question was not "can people walk around naked." The question was "should people be allowed to walk around naked." And the answer, *in current society*, is **no.** In today's society, there is a thing called *modesty* and *propriety*. Whether or not you believe those things *should* exist is certainly a topic for debate. Regardless, those things *do* exist, and in determining whether or not we should allow people to walk around naked all the time, we must take society as it *is*, rather than how we *wish it would be.* In today's society, there is also a thing called *evil*. There are people called *predators* who are literally itching for you to get your proposal approved and start walking around naked. Combine your public nudity with the laws which say that I can take pictures and video of anyone in public, and you've got yourselves the makings of a world-class pervert factory. I can't imagine that such a move wouldn't have a non-trivial affect on the rate of crimes against the person, if you know what I mean. I shouldn't have to spell it out. Oh, for heaven's sake, rapes and sexual assaults will skyrocket. C'mon, work with me here. If you want to talk about the creation of an *ideal society*, we can certainly talk about whether or not clothing should exist. We can also talk about whether or not certain articles of clothing should be mandatory. But in today's society, with today's issues, we simply can't support the idea that anyone can walk around naked at any time. Americans are indeed private about their bodies. It's not appropriate to call it "prudish," it's more appropriate to call it "private." Today's society sexualizes nudity, and women use their, er, assets to their advantage when they choose clothing. To suddenly declare that "no longer a thing" isn't really possible.
