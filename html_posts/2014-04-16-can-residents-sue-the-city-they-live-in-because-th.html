--- title: "Can residents sue the city they live in because they have not installed a proper stormwater runoff canal directly adjacent to their property and it is now or will be causing foundation issues in the future?" date: "2014-04-16" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The short answer is yes, you can file a suit. You would have a simple tort claim for \"trespassing\" and the case could be made that the government\'s inaction created the condition which allowed the trespass onto your property. You\'re going to have a tough time coming up with a suggested remedy, because there hasn\'t been any damage\...yet.\ \ This would be the time to petition the city to get the problem addressed sooner rather than later, because if damage does occur, you will have to pursue legal action. Make sure you include the city engineering department as well as the city legal department. Don\'t be surprised if they try to bully you and say it\'s your fault or whatever; it\'s not. You are responsible for your property, they for theirs. And if their property is in a condition that is causing a trespass on to your property, they are liable for that condition.]{.rendered_qtext}
