**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > Question details: > > Is there a regulation that allows or forbids a person to use lavatory > before taking off? > > Any safety consequences if someone uses it before taking off? The question is not one of using or not using the lavatory. The lav is completely functional and can be used at any time before, during, or after the flight. The question is whether or not you are obeying lighted signs, placards, and crewmember instructions. I'm sure you've heard it a million times during the briefing: US federal law requires compliance with lighted signs (i.e. the seat belt sign), placards (i.e. non-lighted permanent signs), and crewmember instructions. If the seat belt sign is off and there is time, passengers may use the lavatory before take-off. However, once the seat belt sign is on, passengers are required to remain in their seats with their seat belts securely fastened low and tight across their laps. This is a critical portion of the flight, during taxi and take off. The most jarring forces are sent into the aircraft both during landing and during take-off. Flight crews are not "flying stewards" or "flying stewardesses." They are primarily there for your safety. The laws in place back them up. It's critical that all passengers follow the instructions of the crew as well as obeying the lighted signs. If you are up when the light comes on, you are required to return to your seat. If you're in your seat when the light comes on, you're required to stay there. Failing to do so is a violation of federal law.
