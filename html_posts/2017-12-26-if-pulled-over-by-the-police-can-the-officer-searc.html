--- title: "If pulled over by the police, can the officer search the car without the driver\" date: "2017-12-26" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Simply "not being the owner of the vehicle" by itself does not provide reasonable suspicion that a crime has been committed, or that a crime will be committed. It certainly does not alleviate the requirement for having probable cause or obtaining permission to search the vehicle. Remember though, if an officer demands you exit the vehicle and then proceeds to search without a warrant, feel free to protest by saying, "Just reminding you that I did not consent to a search," and then leave it at that. The officer can continue searching if he so chooses. You will fight this later on, not while he's searching. You let your lawyer handle it at that point. Also remember that the officer is not required to explain his basis for search to **you**, he's required to explain his basis for search to **the court.** If he fails to do this to the legal standard and the search is ruled unlawful, anything found during the search is inadmissible in court.
