--- title: '"Is it possible to believe in objective moral truths without God(s)?"' date: '2017-11-10' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The external force need not be a god, but objective moral truth cannot exist without an external force, be it cooperation between people to hold to a certain moral standard, or reliance on an external standard. Otherwise, you only have morals that you infer (i.e. **subjective**), and my morals may not match yours. For example, I may say that "survival of the fittest" is my objective moral truth, and I may kill you to achieve it. There is no objective moral truth that all sentient beings naturally arrive upon without some external collaboration to achieve it. HOWEVER, if there is an *imprinted* morality, how did it get there? Morality cannot develop from nature, as the cold, immoral universe cannot impart morality onto any of its creations (see the Law of Causation). Arguing such implies that you can get something from nothing, which is a postulation long since abandoned.
