**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The representation numbers, according to the Constitution, are determined based on the population of (or the fact that you are in) a state. DC is not a state, it is a special district. So there is no Constitutional authority to give Congressional representation to the district. There are a few ways to address this: - Legislation - Constitutional amendment - Return the District to the state of Maryland - Make DC the 51st state While these have all been proposed, they have all faced challenges, both political and Constitutional. But *taxation without representation?* Those people live in a **not-state**. Only states have representation in Congress. Personally, I think the *best* argument is to return most of DC (all of the *residential areas*) to Maryland, and reduce DC down to the areas that only encompass government buildings. In this way, 95% of the population of the district would end up in a state, and have voting representation in Congress. Those who chose to continue living in the areas immediately around the major government buildings would have to accept the fact that they live in a **not-state** and keep living as they are. Or they can move down the street and get their representation. \ Original question: Isn't disallowing the District of Columbia a voting member of the House and representation in the Senate considered taxation without representation?
