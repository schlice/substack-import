**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *In regards to Trump\'s tweets about banning the trans community from > serving, would that violate the 14th Amendment?* > > *Also, is Trump just trying to create more chaos to distract from the > Russian investigation?* No, and no. The 14th Amendment prevents the government from applying laws in an unequal way. It says nothing about how the federal government (and the military) can spend its money. If the military chooses to not spend \$3.7 billion over 10 years on gender reassignment surgery, hormone therapy, and the cost of being a person down for a year while they recover and go through their Real Life Experience therapy, they can make that choice. Also, since transgenderism is not a protected class under the Civil Rights Act, the military (as an employer) can discriminate against transgendered individuals if it can show clear reasons why doing so furthers their mission, vision, and values. As to Russia, the media has bought in to the whole Russia thing; they have no choice but to ride that ship for all it's worth, and go down with it if need be. They will play the Russia story daily to keep it fresh in your minds. Despite the fact that there's nothing there of any real substance. At. All. I mean, seriously. Don't you find it odd that we switched from "interfering in the election" to "collusion" which isn't even a thing? What does that even mean? Does that mean "supporting a candidate?" The United States does that, and more, all the time, and we have done it for many, many years. How then is Russia supporting Trump (even if they did, which I doubt) any different? So every political donor is "colluding" to get that candidate elected? Remember, we've gone away from "interfering" and we're simply on to "colluding." The circus continues...
