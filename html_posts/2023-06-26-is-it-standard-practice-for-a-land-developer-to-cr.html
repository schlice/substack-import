--- title: '"Is it standard practice for a land developer to create an HOA and maintain complete voting control (keeping more votes than the lot owners combined) over the CC&R"' date: '2023-06-26' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Generally, no. But it varies by state. What a developer is able to write into the Declaration of Covenants is absolutely and ultimately controlled by state law. In our Covenants in our neighborhood in Florida, the developer remains a Class B voting member of the Association until such time as the conditions in state law are met. Among those conditions in state law is that the developer loses his membership three months after 90% of the lots have been conveyed to members.
