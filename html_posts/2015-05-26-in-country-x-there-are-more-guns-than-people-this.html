**Content:** [If you were trying to portray the United States as \"Country X,\" your maths are wrong. There are estimated to be somewhere between 270 million and 310 million guns in the US, and the population of the US is roughly 319 million. So there are likely fewer guns in the US than there are people. Even though they are close, your portrayal is inaccurate.\ \ With all those guns in the United States, I would expect to see every single person carrying one, and yet I don\'t. I hardly see anyone carrying them openly. Is that because they\'re all concealing firearms on their person? Not so, as only 4.8% of the population holds a concealed carry permit.\ \ So all these numbers say is that people in \"Country X\" like to own guns.\ \ If I told you that the number of hamburgers consumed in \"Country X\" was 166 times greater than its population, what would that say about \"Country X?\" In the United States, we consume roughly 50 billion hamburgers annually. All that really says is the residents of \"Country X\" like to buy hamburgers. And presumably eat them, but we don\'t really know that for sure.]{.rendered_qtext}
