HSCT stands for [[hematopoietic stem cell transplantation](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9a8f9f&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hematopoietic_stem_cell_transplantation){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. [[It](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9a8f9f&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hematopoietic_stem_cell_transplantation){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} is a procedure which uses your own induced pluripotent stem cells to "reboot\" your immune system. They harvest your stem cells, induce pluripotency, and reproduce them. Then they use chemotherapy to destroy your bone marrow. Once it is all gone, they re-introduce your stem cells and re-grow your bone marrow, thus restarting your immune system from scratch. It is a dangerous procedure (one has died already), and there are indeed many risks, but so far, the small study group has seen tremendous success, with 70% coming completely off of medication with no further indication of MS. Larger trials are just beginning now, but it shows promise. Good luck, and keep us posted on the book.
