Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Ultimately, the law says that she has to be able to consent. If she is not able to consent, it\'s rape.\ \ His state of mind is not at issue. Since in most jurisdictions, rape is a crime that a man commits against a woman, the committing of the crime turns on whether or not the woman has consented, and continues to consent, during sex. She must be lucid enough to consent to sex. If she cannot consent, it is rape.\ \ Whether or not she pursues rape charges is a different story.\ \ And yes, in some jurisdictions, the man can say that he did not consent and that he was raped against his will.]{.rendered_qtext}
