**Content:** [It\'s a slippery-slope argument that holds no water. Incest and LGBTQ lifestyles have absolutely zero in common.\ \ Now, replace \"incestuous\" with \"polygamous,\" and the argument isn\'t quite as slippery slope as you may think.\ \ [[Judge finalizes order finding polygamy law unconstitutional; victory for \'Sister Wives\' - Religion News Service](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0441a9&url=http://www.religionnews.com/2014/08/28/judge-finalizes-order-finding-polygamy-law-unconstitutional-victory-sister-wives/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="religionnews.com"}]{.qlink_container}\ \ The logic goes, \"if we open the marriage door to one type of marriage which is not traditionally part of marriage, that door is open, and other types of marriage which were also not part of traditional marriage can also be considered.\"\ \ An interesting thought piece in the *Daily Beast* (yes, their articles are often thought-provoking, and before you start asking why a conservative libertarian is reading the *Daily Beast*, there\'s a lot that I read that doesn\'t jibe with what I believe, because I purposefully challenge my beliefs daily. If my beliefs don\'t win, I don\'t deserve to believe them) posited this thought:\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} > \[W\]hile it's mildly understandable that some see these conversations > as conceptually linked---"If we're changing the marriage laws to > include gay couples, how else might we change them?"---polygamy > doesn't inherently flow from gay marriage. If anything, what polygamy > does flow from is a general opening up of options.\ > \ > We increasingly allow Americans to define their own families for > themselves while removing coercive public policy and judgmental social > norms. And this idea, which is at the heart of everything from > increasing access to birth control to the striking down > anti-miscegenation laws to so much more, is exactly what conservative > religious extremists have always opposed.\ > \ > There are interesting arguments to be made for legalizing polygamy, > from protecting children from secretive non-consensual > multiple-marriage situations to how being "feminist" actually means > not protecting women from these marriages but letting them choose for > themselves. \[1\]\ \ \[1\] [[Is Polygamy the Next Gay Marriage?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e9bebb&url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/09/12/is-polygamy-the-next-gay-marriage.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="thedailybeast.com"}]{.qlink_container}\ \ This link, that gay marriage equals a general opening up of options, is where the current polygamist thoughts towards legalization are headed.\ \ It\'s also not too much of a stretch to assume that by legalizing gay marriage, we are de-coupling the law from its association with \"people who can make babies\" and associating it (more rightly) to \"people who can raise babies.\" The government only licenses marriages because it has a compelling interest in children, and seeing them raised in the best possible environment. Since there are many familial configurations that will accomplish that goal, tying benefits to only one of those configurations is unnecessary and doesn\'t follow the 14th Amendment. Polygamous relationships also meet the standard of \"people who can raise babies\" and should be treated no differently under the law than gay couples. You can start by removing the restriction that says that one man can only be married to one woman at a time, and vice-versa.
