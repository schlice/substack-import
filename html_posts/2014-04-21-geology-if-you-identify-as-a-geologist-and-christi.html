**Content:** [I would absolutely suggest looking up *Inherit the Wind*, which was a play based on the famous [[Scopes Trial](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=70f914&url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scopes_Trial){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. The seminal part of the trial was when Clarence Darrow, an acknowledged agnostic, called the prosecutor, Col. William Jennings Bryan, to the stand, and proceeded to \"demonstrate\" how it was possible that evolution and the Biblical account could actually be related.\ \ For example, the Genesis story of the creation of the earth talks about how long it took God to form each part of the earth. In particular, he asked about the first day when God separated the waters in the heavens from the waters on the earth, and then the second day when he formed dry land. He asked if those were typical 24-hour days (the Bible, of course, does not specify). He asked if it was possible that they were 25 hours, since the sun, in the biblical account, was created on the fourth day. As he said, there was no sun, no way to count the hours, so isn\'t it possible that the creation of the earth took exactly the same geological amount of time that we can measure through carbon dating?]{.rendered_qtext}
