Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Generally, a crime is committed somewhere that is covered by laws. Sometimes, crimes are committed between jurisdictions, such as in international waters, or international airspace. In such a circumstance, the countries with jurisdiction will decide how to prosecute the case. International corporations still maintain a headquarters somewhere, and are bound by the laws of some country.
