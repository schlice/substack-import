**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How does the shutting down the asylum claims and deploying the military to the border solve the illegal immigration problem?** It doesn't "solve" the illegal immigration problem. It helps the US focus efforts elsewhere. First of all, the US didn't "shut down" asylum claims. The US said, "If you want to make a claim of asylum, you have to do it at a border checkpoint, or you have to be on an active or expired (within five years) visa. In other words, you either have to present yourself to CBP at a border crossing, or you must be in the country legally. You can no longer be in the country illegally and apply for asylum. If you are found in the country illegally, you will be deported. You can come back to a border crossing to make your asylum claim. Adding military personnel to supplement CBP also helped the issues at the border. It didn't "solve" anything. Nothing will be "solved" until both sides come to the table and ***HONESTLY*** discuss illegal immigration. Neither side wants to honestly discuss the issue. Until that happens, the problem of illegal immigration isn't going to be solved.
