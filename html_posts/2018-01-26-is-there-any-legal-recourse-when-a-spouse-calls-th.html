**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there any legal recourse when a spouse calls their work place multiple times and gets them fired?** The only way something could be done is if the spouse lied about something, and it was that lie that cost you your job. This is called slander. Remember though, it must have been a lie, and the lie is what needs to have cost you your job. The truth is always a defense against slander. And you are only able to recover damages from the spouse, you can't get your job back. In concert with the slander lawsuit (if you actually plan on suing your spouse), I would also follow [[Gareth Greetham](https://www.quora.com/profile/Gareth-Greetham)]{.qlink_container}'s advice and get a divorce. If you don't, it's really difficult to sue your spouse when you are married, as his finances are your finances. So it's like you're suing yourself. So get the divorce first, then sue for slander.
