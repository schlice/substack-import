**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Could Trump nuke London if he wanted to?** The correct answer is yes. While there is a two-person rule when it comes to actually "turning the key" to launch the missile, preventing one person from being able to actually target and fire a missile, there is no two-person rule when it comes to the one person issuing the order.[\[1\]](#KnSVC){#cite-KnSVC .citation_link} The President, as Commander-in-Chief, has the ***sole*** authority to issue the order to launch a nuclear strike. And specifically, to answer the question, the President can indeed order a direct nuclear assault, even if the United States is not under direct nuclear attack. There may be mechanisms to slow down, or possibly to stop, the decision to authorize a nuclear strike, but they are highly classified. There is more collaboration than you might be aware of when the President decides to issue an order to the military. The Joint Chiefs of Staff, for example, always act in an advisory role, and would counsel the President before he issued that order. However, the president has ***unilateral*** authority as Commander-in-Chief to order that nuclear weapons be used for any reason at any time.[\[2\]](#qKRTc){#cite-qKRTc .citation_link} This may seem scary. You might even truly believe that this is incorrect. However, it is not. As you can see in the associated links I provided, this is a question which has not only been hotly debated over the years, that debate actually validates this point of view. The question asked specifically if Trump could nuke London if he wanted to, and since the ***sole, unilateral decision authority to use nuclear weapons belongs to the President, the correct answer is yes, he can***. Will he? Of course not. The UK is our ally, and while Trump is certainly brash and braggadocios, full of himself and probably quite the pig when it comes to women, he's not stupid as many have claimed. He most certainly would not take such an unhinged action. **But the question didn't ask whether or not he** ***would*. The question asked whether or not he** ***could*.** And the answer is yes, he can.
