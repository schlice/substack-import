**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There's a lot of fearmongering going on right now. It would make sense to look at the facts. The Reedy Creek Improvement District is not exempt from property taxes. Disney is the largest taxpayer in Florida, paying upwards of \$300 million in property taxes to Orange and Osceola counties, and another \$250 million in other state taxes. But dissolving the District would revert control of the area back to Orange and Osceola counties, including paying for fire, medical, and 911 services. OCSO provides law enforcement in the District, but in a contract deal. Dissolving the District would mean that OCSO would provide the same service, but with no additional revenue to do it. County residents could be faced with a tax increase in order to pay for those services. Disney, as a county business, would be forced to pay higher taxes, just like all the other residents of Orange and Osceola counties. But the move wouldn't be neutral for Disney, either. Along with the increased taxes that Disney will be forced to pay, Disney allows construction in the District at the pleasure of the Walt Disney Corporation. If the District is dissolved, things like construction permits, zoning applications, and ecological impact fees would all revert to the respective counties, hamstringing Disney's ability to rapidly grow. However, these special districts (there are six of them in Florida which were created before Florida's Constitution was ratified) are granted to *corporations*, and these days, Florida generally looks askance at special improvement districts which were created before the State Constitution and are not necessarily in line with it, but were grandfathered in, being granted to for-profit corporations. In doing this now, legislators in Florida are sending a message to Burbank: RCID exists at the pleasure of the State of Florida, and it is by no means immovable. State Rep. Randy Fine (R.-Melbourne Beach) is a sponsor of the bill, HB3C, which eliminates the Reedy Creek Improvement District. "Disney is a guest in Florida," he [[said Monday in a tweet](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8fa71a&url=https://twitter.com/VoteRandyFine/status/1516417533825454083?s%3D20%26t%3DF0mV1S3erBQRx1kVbfP4fA){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="twitter.com"}]{.qlink_container}. "Today, we remind them." ***So what's likely to happen?*** If RCID is dissolved, it's very likely that Disney and Orange and Osceola counties will re-negotiate a new special improvement district agreement that allows Disney to keep its control, but one which is in keeping with the State Constitution, and (if I was a betting man) one that keeps Disney's corporate front-office in Burbank out of the political business of the state of Florida (at least publicly). Saying that you'll protect your LGBT employees is one thing. Saying that you'll fight to overturn a duly-enacted Florida state law is quite another. (EDIT) It appears the Florida Legislature has passed the bill which will dissolve RCID, and it goes to the Governor's desk for signature. HB3C says "Notwithstanding s. 189.072(2), any independent special district established by a special act prior to the date of ratification of the Florida Constitution on November 5, 1968, and which was not reestablished, re-ratified, or otherwise reconstituted by a special act or general law after November 5, 1968, is dissolved effective June 1, 2023. An independent special district affected by this subsection may be reestablished on or after June 1, 2023, pursuant to the requirements and limitations of this chapter." At the same time, Disney has vowed to file a lawsuit against the state, claiming that Florida is violating the company's First Amendment right to free speech by the move, and that it violates FS 189.072, which shows that they're literally grasping at straws. Looks like Disney is going to have to renegotiate the terms of RCID.
