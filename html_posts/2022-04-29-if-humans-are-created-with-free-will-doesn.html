**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} An excellent question! We can partially glean the answer from what free will will look like in the future kingdom that believers will inherit. Clearly, evil will be defeated, and we will have no desire for sin. Therefore, in heaven, our free will will be limited by the inability to choose what is evil. We will only be able to choose that which is true, noble, right, pure, lovely, and admirable (ref Phillipians 4:8). Also, in order to answer the question you posit about free will, we have to come to an understanding about what it is. In my opinion, free will consists of three elements: - agency over oneself - at least two choices freely available to pick from - control over our own actions If you have these three, you have free will. God knowing which choice you will make doesn't influence your decision in that moment. You still have complete agency over your thoughts and control over your actions, so you still have free will. God may know what you're going to do, but you still have the freedom to choose in the moment. It's a function of being creatures who are bound by time. So now to answer the question. You said that if humans were created with free will, then that must mean that we were meant to exist in a realm with evil. Knowing what we know now, we can see that this is not true. God created Adam and Eve with free will (they chose to eat the apple), but without a desire for sin, as sin did not enter the world until they made their choice. Without the tempting of the serpent, Eve would not have been coerced into eating the apple, because she had no *desire* for sin. It was the serpent who planted the seeds of doubt and question into Eve's mind. Before the serpent, Adam and Eve existed nearly exactly as we will in heaven, *except that they had an extra choice, provided by the serpent, a choice we will not have in heaven.* So knowing this, we at least know that humans can function with full free will *in an environment without evil.* And knowing this, you can see that we were created with the *ability* to live in an environment filled with sin, but without the actual sin. Our free will allows us to function in that environment, even though it wasn't part of the original design. \ Original question: If humans are created with free will, doesn\'t that suggest that we were meant to exist in a realm between good and evil? Had we not the knowledge of evil, what other could we choose from? (referring to the forbidden fruit).
