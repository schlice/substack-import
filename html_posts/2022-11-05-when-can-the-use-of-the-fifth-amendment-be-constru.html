--- title: '"When can the use of the fifth amendment be construed as an admission of guilt?"' date: '2022-11-05' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In criminal cases, you are allowed to "plead the Fifth" and your silence cannot be used against you. In civil cases however, such as divorce cases or cases over orders of protection, you can still claim your Fifth Amendment right if necessary, but the judge or the jury is allowed to assume that by "pleading the Fifth," your answer would have been bad for you. This is called an *adverse inference*. So while pleading the Fifth doesn't necessarily mean that you are guilty, and doing so never amounts to an admission of guilt, in civil cases, pleading the Fifth creates an adverse inference, which may not help your case.
