**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there a way that the president could effectively make marijuana legal with an executive order or similar?** The answer to the question is absolutely yes. Marijuana is illegal at the Federal level because of a law called the Controlled Substances Act. Congress passed this law, but it is the Drug Enforcement Agency (DEA), which works for the Executive Branch, that oversees the Drug Schedules. These are the lists of drugs which are illegal under Federal law (which, as some other answerers have pointed out, is a gross Federal overreach, but that is the answer to another question). The DEA is an Executive-branch agency. Their boss is the President. Since the DEA controls which drugs are on which schedule, and the DEA works for the President, the President can issue an Executive Order directing the DEA to move marijuana to whatever schedule he tells them to. For example, he could have it moved to Schedule IV, so it would share a danger level with drugs like Xanax, Kionopin, Halcion, and Valium. Or he could, at least, move it to Schedule II, where it would still be considered highly addictive (which is debatable), with a high potential for abuse, but has accepted medical uses. Drugs like cocaine and opium live on Schedule II. He could also use an Executive Order to order the DOJ to use as much "prosecutorial discretion" as they wanted, in order to minimize prosecution of marijuana crimes, effectively telling them not to prosecute those cases. Which is exactly what President Obama did during his administration. This signaled to willing states that if they chose to change their own laws, the Federal government wouldn't get in the way. Enter Colorado, who decided to make the first fully government-approved marketplace for marijuana. They are still living through the unintended consequences of that decision, but that too is an answer to another question. Many other states either decriminalized possession of small quantities of marijuana, or allowed medical use only.
