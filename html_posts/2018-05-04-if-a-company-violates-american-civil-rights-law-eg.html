**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a company violates American Civil Rights law (e.g. ADA) but the victim cannot pay for a lawyer, can the violator be prosecuted still?** There are attorneys who will provide legal services at low or no cost (i.e. *pro bono*) for disability rights cases. Check the pro bono websites ([[here](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=1f68df&url=https://www.probono.net/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="probono.net"}]{.qlink_container} is an example) for attorneys who might be able to assist you. You might also check with the advocacy group for your particular disability. For example, I have MS, so I would check with the Colorado-Wyoming chapter of the National MS Society. They can also put you in touch with legal aid resources who can assist you. Or, as [[Colleen Jones](https://www.quora.com/profile/Colleen-Jones-49)]{.qlink_container} said, you can file a complaint yourself with the DOJ.
