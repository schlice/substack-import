**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is, of course, \"it depends,\" or (actually very appropriately), \"YMMV\" (your mileage may vary). And these comparisons are to doing nothing (i.e. walking fewer than 6,000 steps per day which is technically considered \"sedentary\"). If you take a ***brisk*** walk for one hour a day, you will likely exceed 10,000 steps. This is the goal to shoot for: a brisk walk, breaking the 10,000 step mark. What will 10,000 steps a day do for me? > A 2011 > [[study](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=15d6d9&url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3169444/){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} published in the > *International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity* > found walking 10,000 steps a day had a significant impact on body fat > and waist and hip measurements when compared to walking 6,000 steps a > day. Women who averaged more than 10,000 steps were 4 to 6 inches > narrower in their waist and hip measurements and had 40 percent less > body fat than subjects who averaged less than 6,000 steps. > > Doing 10,000 steps per day not only helps with weight management but > it can also help reduce blood pressure in women. A 2000 > [[study](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=393917&url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11131268){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} published in the journal > *Hypertension Research* found walking 10,000 steps per day or more is > effective in lowering blood pressure, increasing exercise capacity, > and even reducing sympathetic nerve activity in hypersensitive > patients --- regardless of exercise intensity or duration. > Participants\' blood pressure dropped after just 24 weeks of the > 10,000 steps per day program. \[1\] You may have to work up to 10,000 steps. For example, if you walk at about 3 miles per hour, this means that walking for 20 minutes will take you one mile. 10,000 steps is about 5 miles. So at 3 miles per hour, you\'d have to walk for 1 hour and 40 minutes to achieve that goal. So start small, and increase your speed gradually so that you\'re walking faster, closer to 4 miles per hour. Combined with the general walking we do during the day, you can easily achieve 10,000 steps a day. Pedometers that track your step count for the day can be more expensive (like a Fitbit) or really really cheap (like a small step-counting pedometer you clip to your waistband, usually for between \$1 and \$3), but clipping it on or putting it on in the morning and wearing it all day will help you achieve that 10,000 step goal. *Remember to consult with your doctor before you start* ***any*** *exercise program.* \ \[1\] [[Can Anyone Really Walk 10K Steps Every Single Day?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2a851a&url=http://www.medicaldaily.com/walk-walk-10k-steps-day-challenge-promotes-weight-loss-and-fitness-it-realistic-343268){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="medicaldaily.com"}]{.qlink_container} ***Disclaimer:*** ***This answer is not professional medical advice****, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
