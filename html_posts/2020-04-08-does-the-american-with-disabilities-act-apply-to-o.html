**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the American With Disabilities Act apply to OCD?** The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) does not specify the actual conditions which it applies to. It simply defines what constitutes a disability. It states that a person with a disability is "a person who has a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activity." This includes people who have a record of such an impairment, even if they do not currently have a disability. It also includes individuals who do not have a disability but are regarded as having a disability. Therefore, if OCD "substantially limits one or more major life activity," it would be covered. "Major life activities include, but are not limited to, caring for oneself, performing manual tasks, seeing, hearing, eating, sleeping, walking, standing, lifting, bending, speaking, breathing, learning, reading, concentrating, thinking, communicating, and working."[\[1\]](#tvUDN){#cite-tvUDN .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-tvUDN){.citation_index} [[The ADA Amendments Act of 2008 Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c2ae6d&url=https://www.dol.gov/agencies/ofccp/faqs/americans-with-disabilities-act-amendments){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="dol.gov"}]{.qlink_container}
