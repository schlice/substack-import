**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What steps can a student in the sophomore year take that would help him/her get into USAFA?** I'll assume this is you, for the sake of making a question easier to answer. First thing you should know is that you're at the perfect time to start thinking about what you have to do to get into USAFA. And the first thing you should do is to visit the [[Academy Admissions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60e33c&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} website. There, you'll find a few important things: - [[Find your ALO](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3baf43&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/outreach-programs/find-your-admissions-liaison-officer/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container}: An ALO (Admissions Liaison Officer) is an Academy grad and Air Force officer whose job it is to assist you throughout your admissions process. Your ALO will help you select appropriate high school classes so that you can be competitive, make sure you're engaging in team sports, help you with your admissions packet, assist with the nomination process, and generally just encourage you in the right direction to give you the best chance for success. Finding your ALO and contacting that person is critical. I would not wait to do that. - The Admission Tracker App: The app (available for [[iPhone](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9a269e&url=http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/united-states-air-force-academy/id427133480?mt%3D8){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container} and [[Android](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e32eee&url=https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id%3Dcom.u360mobile.airforceacademy%26feature%3Dsearch_result%23?t%3DW251bGwsMSwyLDEsImNvbS51MzYwbW9iaWxlLmFpcmZvcmNlYWNhZGVteSJd){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container}) provides a checklist of steps that you can follow to assist you along your admissions journey. Download that and start tracking your own checklist. On the website, you'll also find all sorts of information relating to your admissions packet, namely what things the Academy is looking for in the ideal cadet. I'll briefly review them here, but the website goes into excruciating detail about every step of the process. The [[steps you need to follow](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=80e2b6&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/the-application-process/application-steps){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} are outlined on the website, but let's talk about what you can be doing right now, as a sophomore, to give yourself the best chance to succeed: - **Get good grades**. Academics makes up the largest portion (50%) of your entrant score. Getting the best grades you can get is critical. Now, you don't have to take all kinds of AP or Honors classes (but if you do well in them, it won't hurt). If you do score 4 or 5 on an AP class, you can actually use it as transfer credit which will apply to your core academic requirements. While AP or Honors classes are not necessary, you do have to have excellent academic scores, and you need to score well on the standardized testing (i.e. SAT or ACT). To be academically competitive for an appointment to the Academy, we recommend completion of the following high school courses: - four years of English (with a college preparatory class in writing) - four years of math (strong background in geometry, algebra, trigonometry and pre-calculus) - four years of science (lab-based and should include biology, chemistry and physics) - three years of social studies (to include a course in U.S. History) - two years of a modern foreign language - one year of computer study - **Participate in team sports.** The large majority of cadets participated in some form of organized team sport while in high school. The Academy isn't looking to see *how many* team sports you participated in. What they are looking for is "sustained participation and leadership in a few activities." Most cadets also lettered in their team sport. Doesn't mean that you can't get in without a varsity letter, but if you consider that most cadets lettered in their varsity sport, earning a varsity letter is a step in the right direction. - **Participate in non-athletic extracurricular activities.** Again, as I mentioned, sustained participation and leadership in a few activities (such as a team sport and other non-athletic activities) makes you a strong candidate. The Academy only looks at activities you participated in starting in your sophomore year, so again, this time is perfect for you. Each one of these things on your admissions packet is weighted and scored. These scores combined form your overall rank, and your packet is ranked alongside all of the other packets that are submitted for that class year. Normally, the Academy receives approximately 10,000 packets per class year. The best thing you can do right now is to focus on your grades, and make sure you are doing everything you can to keep your GPA high. If that means before or after school tutoring or help from your teachers, do it. If it means doing extra credit assignments, do it. Do whatever you can to keep your grades up. This is the time when stupid mistakes can derail your chances, so don't do that. If you have other questions, please feel free to ask.
