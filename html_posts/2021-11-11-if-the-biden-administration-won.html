**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As we have witnessed in a variety of SCOTUS cases over the years, not only can you not compel the federal government to enforce the law, you can't complain when the federal government makes an immigration law you don't agree with, and you cannot usurp a power given to the federal government and enforce it yourself. Immigration is an Executive function. The Executive has broad authority to control who comes across the border, and has the authority to protect national security with that power. The border can be as porous or as solid as the Executive chooses it to be, and the states do not have a say in that decision. Right or wrong, for better or worse, that's how Congress set it up. Governor DeSantis is frustrated with the federal government's apparent inaction on their own border policy. I share his frustration. If the federal government wants to allow anyone and everyone into the country, they should have the testicular fortitude to modify immigration law to allow for that.
