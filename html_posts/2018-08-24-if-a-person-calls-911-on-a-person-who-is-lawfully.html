**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a person calls 911 on a person who is lawfully carrying a gun in the open, why isn't he/she arrested for abusing an emergency line and taking officers away from a possible real emergency?** The situation you're describing is not classified as false reporting. False reporting (at least in Colorado) would consist of reporting an event which does not exist. [[Swatting](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=19e73b&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swatting){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} would be an example of false reporting using the 911 system. However, calling 911 and reporting a person with a gun is not false reporting. The police can absolutely come and check it out and see what the person is up to, and then perhaps advise the caller that it is legal for people to openly carry firearms in that jurisdiction.
