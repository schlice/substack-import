--- title: '"Do all people with MS (multiple sclerosis) have lesions on their brain?"' date: '2022-12-12' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. Some people have lesions on their spinal cord. Lesions will indeed be present somewhere. In about 5 percent of cases, a person may initially develop MS symptoms before lesions are present, but within a short timeframe, those lesions will appear. The longer a person goes with *symptoms* but no *lesions* should indicate to the neurologist that they should be looking for other conditions which mimic MS but are not MS. There are, of course, other tests to add to the body of evidence pointing to MS (visual evoked potential, CSF sample, etc). The MRI alone does not determine whether or not a person has MS, but the brain lesions are a hallmark symptom of the disease.
