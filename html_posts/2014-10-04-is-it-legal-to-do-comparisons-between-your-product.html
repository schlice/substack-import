--- title: "Is it legal to do comparisons between your product and other publicly available products without their company\" date: "2014-10-04" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [US Copyright law allows for product name, likeness, and logo use for comparative advertising; this is covered under fair use. Copyright statement must be included (X is a registered trademark of Y, all rights reserved)\ \ Be careful that you represent the product correctly, libel can still attach, and is the most common complaint raised in comparative advertising (you misrepresented the product).]{.rendered_qtext}
