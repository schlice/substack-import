--- title: "Can a President be impeached and removed for not committing high crimes of some sort?" date: "2019-04-22" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can a president be impeached for acts that are not criminal?** Yes. Remember that impeachment is a *political* remedy, while indictment for crimes is a *judicial* remedy. When President Gerald Ford was in Congress, he uttered these famous words: "\[A\]n impeachable offense is whatever a majority of the House of Representatives considers it to be at a given moment in history." That said, most Congresses have defined impeachable offenses as anything which could be prosecuted as a crime. That's not to say that this is a rule, but it is precedent. However, to answer the question, the House can impeach a president for any reason they deem is impeachable.
