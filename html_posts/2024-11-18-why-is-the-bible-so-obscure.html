**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *It's not a defect, it's a feature.* So let's say you're God. You need to deliver Your Wordâ„¢ to your people in a way that is enduring and easy to replicate and translate, but you must also respect the free will you gave to people. You therefore must make sure that what you reveal is not *too* convincing. If it's so clear as to be absolutely unambiguous, then no one will ever be able to deny it, and you won't have *believers*, but *sheep*. So what do you do? **You inspire human writers to write everything in a book.** You inspire early humans to write prophesy, and then you bring that prophesy true. You inspire writers to write it all down. Books are great because they are easily copied and easily translated. We have enough manuscripts to ensure that the copies are accurate, and these writers were personal enough to convince anyone willing to listen with an open mind that they were telling the truth. But it's "just a book" written by "humans" so it's easy enough to discount if you don't want to believe it. There's enough evidence in the Bible to convince anyone willing to believe, but there's enough ambiguity so as not to coerce the unwilling. So people are free to either believe or not, and their free will is never violated.
