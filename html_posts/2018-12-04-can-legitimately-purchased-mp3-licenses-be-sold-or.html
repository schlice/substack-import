**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can legitimately purchased mp3 licenses be sold or otherwise transferred to somebody else?** It used to be that you could re-sell your CDs and records legally. This was called the *first sale* doctrine. Otherwise, donating your CDs or records, or selling them at a garage sale, would be illegal. The first sale doctrine says that you have the right to "sell or otherwise dispose" of your media without seeking the permission of the copyright owner. But what about digital media? The courts have said that the first sale doctrine does not apply to digital media. When you sell your hard media, there is still only one copy of that media. When you sell your digital media, a flawless perfect duplicate copy is created on the purchaser's computer. That copy, said the courts, is an unauthorized reproduction. Traditional media had an inbuilt limiting factor slowing down reproductions: copies degrade with each passing copy. However, digital copies do not suffer from this limitation: each copy is an exact duplicate, which retains 100% of its quality. There is an opportunity for digital retailers and content creators to create a marketplace for reselling copies of music, and Amazon and Google are working towards that. But today, no such technology exists. So for now, reselling digital licenses and transferring the media to someone else is illegal.
