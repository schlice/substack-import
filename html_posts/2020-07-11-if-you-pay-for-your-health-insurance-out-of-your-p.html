Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you pay for your health insurance out of your paycheck, then why does your employer have any right telling you what services/medicine you can get?** Because you don\'t pay for all of your health plan. In fact, you only pay for a very small portion of the cost of your health plan. Your employer pays for the bulk of your plan. Your monthly cost plus your copays and your coinsurance costs still don\'t add up to even more than half of the total cost of your insurance plan. Your employer pays for most of it, which is why it is offered as a benefit to you. To that end, they can determine what services they will cover. To be clear, no one is telling you what services or medicine you can get. If you can get it, you can get any service or medicine (some require prescriptions and some require referrals). They are only telling you which ones they will help pay for. You are free to get any other service on your own, and pay the full retail cost out of your own pocket.
