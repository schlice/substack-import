--- title: "Is a school liable for wrongful death if they allegedly ignored/failed to adequately address bullying, and the student committed suicide?" date: "2015-03-08" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [This is obviously a very hot issue these days, as bullying has led to a number of suicide attempts as well as a few successful ones.\ \ In common law, a person is not required to act when a crime is being committed. Failure to act is not a crime. Notable exceptions are police officers and doctors who are required to act by law.\ \ Criminally speaking, the school did not violate any criminal laws because, as others have said, the school did not force the student to commit suicide. The crime of assisted suicide is the one we are talking about, and the school did not aid the student in his suicide by providing materials and/or instructions.\ \ However, I think a civil case could be made if you could show a pattern of willful ignorance and failure by the school to properly address bullying. However, it certainly won\'t be an easy case. It\'s been 16 years since the Columbine High School shooting; the dialog about bullying has been happening for some time now, so making such a case would be an uphill battle at best. National bully prevention programs have been implemented for many years, so I really doubt that a school would not have such a program in place, and/or would be aware of bullying and willfully fail to act (i.e. analyze the situation, know that bullying is taking place, and then willfully choose to allow the bullying to continue).\ \ So I think making a case for wrongful death against the school would be a really tough case to make.]{.rendered_qtext}
