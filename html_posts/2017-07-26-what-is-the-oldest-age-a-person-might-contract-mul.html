Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I had a PA at one clinic who developed and was diagnosed with MS in his late 60s. It was relapsing-remitting, so they simply decided not to treat it since the benefit would be minimal. But to answer the question, there is no upper age limit at which someone can be diagnosed with MS. Additionally, there is no lower age limit. Pediatric MS is a thing as well. These outer boundaries are rare, but they do exist.
