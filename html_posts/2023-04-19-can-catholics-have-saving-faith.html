--- title: '"Can Catholics have saving faith?"' date: '2023-04-19' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Absolutely! Repent and believe, even Catholics do this. However, they miss the mark when they focus too heavily on "eating the bread" and "drinking the cup." They can certainly perform those rituals, but the thought that eating the bread and drinking the cup is what saves you is decidedly unbiblical, regardless of how Catholics twist themselves into a pretzel to try to justify it, even in light of the definitive exegetical evidence against it. No disrespect to my Catholic brethren, but growing up Catholic gives me a pretty unique perspective. Additionally, holding that the Magisterium and Church Traditions are sources of God's word is also decidedly unbiblical, with similar exegetical evidence against it. So Catholics can indeed have saving faith, so long as they don't focus on the trappings of the church to have it.
