**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Jesus did tell a few people to quit their jobs and follow Him. He needed disciples, and they couldn't rightly keep their jobs and follow Him at the same time, so that kinda makes sense. But He also told at least one guy that he needed to sell all his worldly goods. Here's the passage: > Just then a man came up to Jesus and asked, "Teacher, what good thing > must I do to get eternal life?" > > "Why do you ask me about what is good?" Jesus replied. You have to sell all your possessions and you will have treasure in heaven!* While I don't doubt that you will indeed have treasure in heaven if you sell all your possessions, this was not a command from Jesus to us. We already have the Great Commandments: Love the Lord your God, and love your neighbor. Then what's the story with this whole "sell all your possessions" thing? See, *Jesus knew the rich man's heart before he spoke a word.* Jesus knew the rich man loved his money more than Him. So Jesus told him to do the one thing that He knew the man would not do: part with the one true love of his life; his money. The rich man went away sad, because he realized that in order to gain heaven, he would have to give up the love of money. We are told in the Bible that the love of money is the root of all evil. And this rich man loved money, so he was ill-equipped to enter heaven. Jesus knew that this rich man would not "come and follow me" while he still had his money, because he loved it too much. Can you *have* money and not *love* money? Certainly. You can know that money makes life easier, but it doesn't solve problems, and it doesn't take away sin. Only Jesus can do that. Only Jesus is the way to heaven, not money. Money doesn't get you to heaven. That's why Jesus turns to the disciples and tells them that the rich person (one obsessed with the love of money) will never make it to heaven. It would be easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to go to heaven, Jesus said. While it is possible for rich people to go to heaven, it is not easy. Jesus basically says that *all* of his followers must abandon the various forms of wealth in order to follow Him: money, property, security, family, prestige, or popularity. But Jesus also assures them that *"The things that are impossible with people are possible with God"* (Luke 18:27 ESV). But what we **gain** far outpaces anything we **lose**. And that's the point. Everything in this life is fleeting, but what we gain in exchange is *far greater* than anything we give up here.
