The *source* of the impairment is irrelevant, whether it be from alcohol, prescription medication, sleeping pills, NyQuilâ„¢, or anything else you ingest that may impair motor function. **yet I can\'t find any proof that it impairs driving?** The proof is in the pudding, as they say. A charge of impaired driving starts with a law enforcement officer observing behavior which may indicate a driver who is under the influence of a substance which impairs motor function. The officer stops the vehicle and performs some roadside tests which demonstrate the driver's ability to function. This *body of evidence* is then used to make a determination as to whether or not the driver is impaired. The truth is we don't have to know exactly what substance is impairing the driver. We don't have to know how much of the substance was ingested. There doesn't need to be a Breathalyzer test to determine how much THC is present in the bloodstream. Impaired driving is a traffic infraction wherein the driver is impaired by an ingested substance which so affects motor function that driving a car becomes unsafe. It is a *subjective* charge made by the officer. **TL;DR: The substance itself doesn't determine whether or not you can be charged with driving under the influence. The driver's motor functions do.**
