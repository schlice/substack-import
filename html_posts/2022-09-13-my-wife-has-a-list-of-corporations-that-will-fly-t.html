**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Since you asked this question in the Christian Corner, I'm going to give you a Biblical answer. And the answer is more related to where you allow your money to be spent. Your money is not yours. It's God's. We Christians know this. God gives us the resources we need to live our lives. We are merely stewards of the resources that God has given us. So tell me: are you being a good steward of God's resources if you knowingly allow it to be given to an organization which will use those resources to facilitate the murder of babies? I don't doubt that It will be hard. You'll have to give up your iPhones. In my case, I'll have to forego renewing my Annual Pass to Walt Disney World, despite the fact that from all signs, Disney is not going to sell Annual Passes in their current form any more, so once I let it go, it's most likely gone for good. But I also know that there are plenty of other companies whose health plans include travel to "centers of excellence" or locations for specialized healthcare if it is not available near you. For example, my company's health plan allows for travel reimbursement in these cases, for all covered health services. So if I, for example, get cancer, I can travel to a cancer treatment center and recoup some of my travel expenses through my health care plan. But what constitutes a "covered health service" varies from company to company, and it's not all black-and-white, and it's not all out in the open. So it becomes very difficult to really know whether your money will actually be facilitating abortions. However, for the "clear-cut" cases (like where companies have literally issued press releases declaring that they will pay for employees to travel to states which allow the killing of babies), we can choose to spend our money elsewhere. \ That said, is there a *limit* to how much we must disrupt our lives? For example, if Visa said that they will pay for their employees to travel to abortion clinics, do I have to cancel all of my Visa cards because of this? If my bank supported this policy, should I change banks? If my *employer* supported this policy, should I quit? And what about *taxes*? What if the government successfully repeals the Hyde Amendment and actual tax dollars are forced to be paid to abortion providers like Planned Parenthood to pay for abortions? Can I refuse to pay taxes because my money will go towards paying for abortions? I struggle with these questions, and I'd love to hear your thoughts in the comments. But I know that God is sovereign, He is just, and He is righteous. Notice that nowhere in the Bible does it say that God is *fair*. So I know that He is loving and righteous, but sometimes following Him isn't fair, or fun, or painless. I will have to be uncomfortable. I will have to be inconvenienced. I may have to even endure suffering at times. But all of it is according to His will and His good plan for us. In my opinion, where companies *applaud* evil, I will make the most inconveniencing efforts to distance my resources from that company. But you will have to follow the dictates of your conscience and the pull of the Spirit on your heart. In the end, we will all account for our actions in this life, whether we have been washed clean by the blood of Christ or not. I pray that God will guide our hearts to make decisions regarding stewardship of His resources that will be pleasing to Him and will advance the Kingdom
