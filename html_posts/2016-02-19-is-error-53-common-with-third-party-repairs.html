Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, as you know the issue occurs when particular components which are integrated into Apple\'s security model, such as the fingerprint sensor or touchscreen, are replaced by third-party repairers. However, after much public pressure over forcing users to repair their devices through Apple rather than third-party maintainers, Apple released [[a new version](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=09bffd&url=https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT205628){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container} of iOS 9.2.1 over iTunes if your device has been hit by Error 53. This version will not automatically be pushed to all users; you can only get it if your device has been affected by an Error 53. Good luck!
