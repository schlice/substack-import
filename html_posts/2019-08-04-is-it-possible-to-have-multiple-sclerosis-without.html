**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it possible to have multiple sclerosis without lesions on scans?** The answer is, while extremely rare, you can have MS without a positive MRI. The MRI is a great tool, and the results of the scan will absolutely add to the body of evidence which indicates MS. Under the updated McDonald criteria, one symptom and a positive MRI can indicate MS. However, two symptoms separated by space and time, along with other evidence, can still indicate MS. That other evidence does not need to include a positive MRI. This is why you need a neurologist to diagnose MS; it is as much an art as it is a science. There is no one test that can indicate MS.
