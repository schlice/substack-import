**Content:** [Thanks for the A2A, and great question.\ \ Two things:\ 1. \"most dope busts follow random vehicular felony stops\" is not necessarily an accurate depiction of most dope busts, as it were. Vehicular drug enforcement is accomplished through several measures, one of which is random stops. In many circumstances, police know who they are looking for, and they stop the appropriate vehicle with the appropriate occupants in the appropriate area.\ \ 2. Self-driving cars must still have a licensed driver in them, and driver override of an automated system is always possible. So police can approach and light up an auto-drive car, and the driver will be required to take over and stop the car.]{.rendered_qtext}
