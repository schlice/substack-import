**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What can you do to increase your chances of surviving a mass shooting?** Like [[Chris Knox](https://www.quora.com/profile/Chris-Knox-4)]{.qlink_container}, I used to advocate the Run, Hide, Fight method. Today, I advocate the "Move! Escape or Attack" method. It's essentially an incremental improvement on Run, Hide, Fight (RHF) which allows you to be more flexible in an active shooter situation. - **Move!** When people are not used to high-stress situations, they can freeze up. Instead of saying anything else, we say "Move!" to try to break that vapor-lock. Moving to cover or concealment is the first step. Get out of the line of fire, get to a place where you can stop and analyze the situation. Moving first to an area of cover or concealment is appropriate. - **Escape or Attack**. The problem with RHF was that "Fight" was listed only as a last resort, after hiding failed and your position was breached by the attacker. This fails to allow for any flexibility. You only hide if you can't run. And you only fight if you can't hide or your cover is blown. Escape denotes thoughtful movement towards an exit (and non-traditional exits must be considered, i.e. windows, fire doors, loading docks, or service areas not normally accessible to the public), whereas Run can be blind running, and it may not lead you to an exit. Run may not be purposeful, while Escape is a direct action leading to an exit.\ \ Attack is listed as equivalent to Escape, since it's not only applicable if Escape fails. Attack gives you an emotional advantage over Fight, which can be defensive in nature. Attack is a deliberate forceful proactive action. It's appropriate to orient a person into an aggressive posture to immediately counter a threat.
