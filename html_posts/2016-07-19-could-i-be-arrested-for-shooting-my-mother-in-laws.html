--- title: "Could I be arrested for shooting my mother-in-laws aggressive dog?" date: "2016-07-19" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Animal cruelty charges can result if you knowingly kill an animal without cause. Since you had a pretty easily substantiated reason (defense of a child), you likely won't be charged with animal cruelty. At worst, you could be charged with criminal mischief, or criminal destruction of property. Destruction of property ranges from a misdemeanor to a felony depending on the dollar amount of the property destroyed. However, again, you have a defense: you were defending the life of a child. You *may* be charged with other violations, depending on your jurisdiction. You may be in an area where discharge of firearms is prohibited (such as within most city limits), and if you are a person prohibited from owning a firearm, you would also be charged for those infractions. Your defense may be lessened if you knew the dog was aggressive and you allowed your child to remain in its presence. So first and foremost, don't allow your child to be near the dog. Don't allow your child to be in the same house as the dog. If this upsets your mother-in-law, perhaps she should get rid of the dog, or kennel it while your child is present. Can you be arrested? Yes. Would you have a fairly solid defense that a defense attorney should be able to work with? Also yes. But don't take my word for it. You should talk to an attorney licensed in your jurisdiction before you take action on any advice.
