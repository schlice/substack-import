**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} My best friend at USAFA was prior enlisted at Plattsburg AFB in upstate New York. He worked on avionics and automatic flight controls (I have no idea why I remember that) on the F-111. Several flavors of F-111s were flown out of Plattsburg, but this story involves the Ravens (EF-111As) that had the large electronic countermeasures (ECM) pod at the tip of the vertical stab: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-2204e886a055785b90f6733d9b962a62){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-2204e886a055785b90f6733d9b962a62" master_w="300" master_h="202"} ::: :::: Realize that this is early to mid 80s when this happened: no cell phones, and TV was still broadcast OTA (over-the-air). The story is told that a right-seater in the Raven had a house that was roughly on final approach for one of the runways at Plattsburg. Apparently, he would let his wife know that he made it home by quickly powering on and powering off the ECM pod when he was over his house. This would cause all the TVs in the neighborhood to briefly "fuzz out." It was also entirely outside of regulation. Eventually, other people in the neighborhood complained to the base that their aircraft were interfering with their TVs. A little research, matching up the incidents to flight plans, and the culprit was quickly discovered and disciplined.
