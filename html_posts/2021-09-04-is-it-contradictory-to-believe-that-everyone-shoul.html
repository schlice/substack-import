--- title: '"Is it contradictory to believe that everyone should be Covid-vaccinated, and also believe that everyone should be able to choose freely NOT to be vaccinated?"' date: '2021-09-04' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not at all. You can believe that vaccination is the right choice for most people, but still believe that people have the right to choose for themselves what is best. The big kicker: **simply believing that vaccination is the right choice for most people does not mean that you can force people to get vaccinated.** **It also does not mean that you have the right to exclude those people from society, or deny them essential services.** This is where the majority of people fall down when they are comparing the ability of a person to decide for themselves whether or not vaccination is appropriate. *If you truly believe that people have the right to choose for themselves, then you have no right to exclude them from any facet of society because of their choice.* Don\'t get me wrong, businesses still have the right to determine which patrons come into their establishment. And if the business (except in Florida) wants to restrict patrons in their business to people who are vaccinated, they can do that. But you can\'t deny hospital beds to the unvaccinated. You can\'t deny ambulance service to the unvaccinated. You can\'t deny any taxpayer-funded service from the unvaccinated.
