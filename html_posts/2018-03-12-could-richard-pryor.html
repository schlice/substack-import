**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Could Richard Pryor\'s long time substance abuse have contributed to his multiple sclerosis diagnosis in 1986?** The short answer is "we don't know." Could my smoking in my early 20s have contributed to my own MS diagnosis in 2005? We don't know. We do know that there are factors which we can point to that do contribute to making a person more or less likely to develop MS: sex, ethnicity, geography of childhood, family history, and exposure to an as-of-yet unidentified infectious trigger. However, drug abuse (or smoking) has not been identified as having a clear correlation to development of MS.
