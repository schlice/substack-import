**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Will senators who have stated they will not be impartial in the impeachment trial of the president, technically be in legal jeopardy after taking an oath of impartiality?** Impeachment is a political process, not a legal one. There is nothing involving the law, or the courts, related to an impeachment proceeding. There is no legal jeopardy for anyone for following or not following Senate rules. Senators who violate their oaths in the Senate can be investigated by the Senate Ethics Committee and can be reprimanded. Recall that the oath (written by the Senate) says that they will do fair and impartial justice under the Constitution and the law. While Senators can never be completely impartial, they can at least approach whatever they are presented with with objectivity. Senators are elected officials and they work for the people who elected them. To that end, they are never impartial, so the oath is a little disingenuous in that regard. The Senate should think about changing the verbiage of that oath.
