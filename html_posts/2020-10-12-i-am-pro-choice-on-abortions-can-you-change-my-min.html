**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Nope. I have little to no hope of changing your mind. You have already declared yourself "pro-choice," so you've already determined that the convenience of the mother trumps the life of the baby. Maybe you've got a pocketful of "Buts" that keeps you on the pro-choice side: **But what about rape?** This is the only place that I will give a little ground, because the woman was an *unwilling participant* in the act creating a child. Any argument which discusses responsibility cannot include a rape victim. But I'll only give a little ground. Why? *The baby is a victim, too.* Since when do we kill innocent third-parties when someone else was the victim of a crime? Does that really sound like the appropriate answer? I may not think it is, but I am willing to consider reasonable solutions (i.e. at the conclusion of the police investigation which initially establishes that the woman was not lying about being raped, she is given an abortifactent). **But what if the woman did not consent to carrying a baby? What if she used protection?** No ground given here. The woman consented to carrying a baby the moment she allowed a penis to enter her vagina. The woman was a *willing participant* in the very act that creates children. Let's say I stick my hand in a blast furnace, but I'm wearing a fireproof glove. Can I still get burned? Of course I can. The glove could fail. I could be wearing it incorrectly. Regardless of why, I knew that such was the possibility the moment I stuck my gloved hand into the blast furnace. Believing that a condom-wrapped penis is a 100% guarantee against accidental impregnation is clearly a mistake; it's even clearly written on the box. Claiming that you did not consent to carrying a child because of it is plain, unadulterated ignorance. Birth control never has been 100% effective. There is always the possibility that you can get pregnant, even while using birth control. **But what about healthcare? Isn't abortion health care?** No, it is not. Abortion is very, very rarely part of normal women's health care. And even if it was required to save the life of the mother, it would be permitted. No one would ever seek to override a doctor who said that it was either the mom or the baby, but only one would live. But those incidents are shockingly rare. Modern health care (which includes obstetric care) means that giving birth is very, very safe. I won't get into the corner cases, because corner cases always exist, but I also won't allow all abortions simply because corner cases exist. **But you just want to control women! You want to keep them barefoot and pregnant in the kitchen!** Um, no I don't. I'm really not interested in controlling women. I'm simply interested in saving lives, and to me, it appears that pro-choice advocates are simply ignoring the fact that pregnancy involves ***two lives***, the life of the mother, and the life of the baby.
