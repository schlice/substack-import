Driving slow is bad but hitting the brakes and slowing down even more is even worse."' date: '2022-12-04' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} When you tailgate, you limit your own reaction time to the car in front of you. Consequently, the car in front of you cannot slow down quickly, or you'll run into them. To combat this, the car in front of you must follow the car in front of *him* at a greater distance, so that the car in front of you has more time to decelerate more gradually, so that you have more opportunity to stop before you hit him. If the car in front of you has to hit his brakes hard, you'll end up in his lap, and no one wants that. So you tailgating forces the driver in front of you to slow down to give him more reaction time to slow down more gradually so you don't hit him. Or he's just trying to brake-check you as a method to send a clear message: *get off my ass.*
