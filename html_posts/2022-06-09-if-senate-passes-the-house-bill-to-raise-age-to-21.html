**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember that this is only for commercial purchases of rifles. Military members are issued rifles by the military to use in combat, so this bill would not apply to them. But *logically* and *morally*, the bill makes no sense when you factor in military members. Why is it *logical* that a 20 year old is not mature enough to buy a rifle, but that same person is mature enough to be *issued* a government-purchased rifle and go off to war to defend the country? The logic simply doesn't hold. Really, what this bill says is that *unless you join the military and we can personally evaluate you, we don't trust that you're mature enough to own a rifle.* In other words, *because a tiny fraction of people buy rifles and kill people with them, we will take away the ability of anyone to purchase a rifle.* But killers never follow laws anyway. Would this have stopped Sandy Hook? Nope, because the killer didn't buy the rifle. He broke into the safe storage, killed his mother, and then went to the school to kill Kindergarteners. So we stop an even smaller percentage of killers from legally obtaining a firearm, but in the process we have stopped large swaths of innocent, law-abiding Americans (who can join the military and vote) from purchasing rifles themselves. We keep chipping away at the Second Amendment piece by piece, because we keep allowing the little corner cases to be restricted.
