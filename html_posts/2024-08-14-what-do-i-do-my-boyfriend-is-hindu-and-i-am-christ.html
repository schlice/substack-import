**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *He answered, "Have you not read that he who created them from the > beginning made them male and female, and said, 'Therefore a man shall > leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and the two > shall become one flesh'?" \-- Matthew 19:4--5 ESV* You *leave* your respective families and create *your own* family. Your marriage is not about them, it's about you. Nevertheless, which wedding you choose, and which family you upset, is less important that which **religion** you choose. And ultimately, since you are Christian, you have undoubtedly heard about being "unequally yoked," correct? :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-3ca716ced0f4f6da422fde958df62854){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-3ca716ced0f4f6da422fde958df62854" master_w="470" master_h="274"} ::: :::: A **yoke** is a wooden structure used to hook two oxen together. This way they can pull a load twice as heavy as one ox could pull. If one ox is significantly stronger than the other, or one ox starts pulling in the wrong direction, they are considered *unequally yoked*, and they don't make much progress. Paul makes the same comparison in the Bible: > *Do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers. For what partnership has > righteousness with lawlessness? Or what fellowship has light with > darkness? \-- 2 Cor 6:14 ESV* Now I'm not saying that one of you is good and the other is bad, or that one of you is righteous and the other is lawless. But the point still remains. If you marry someone from another religion, you two won't be "pulling in the same direction," so to speak. Uniting a Christian with a non-Christian is going to make for a very difficult marriage. So one of you is going to have to change religions. And knowing what you know about how God has saved you, are you really planning on giving that up? Is he planning on giving up his faith? **TL;DR:** I'm not worried about your marriage arrangements or whether or not you upset your respective families. I'm worried about your *soul*. So is God.
