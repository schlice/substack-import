**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It's a good question. And the answer really has nothing to do with Mitch McConnell or Congress. **Democrats got complacent with** ***Roe.*** The problems with *Roe* started long before Mich McConnell refused to allow the nomination of Merrick Garland to the Supreme Court to come to the Senate floor. They watched silently as President George W. Bush added both Chief Justice John Roberts and Justice Samuel Alito to the Court, both of whom already made no bones about wanting to place limits on abortion. They watched silently as President Obama delivered a [[commencement speech](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=611a60&url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/18/us/politics/18obama.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nytimes.com"}]{.qlink_container} to Notre Dame saying that he would be working to reduce the number of abortions in the country. And, of course, in 2016, part of the reason that Hillary Clinton lost the election to Donald Trump was that they weren't worried about *Roe*; so comfortable were they with *Roe's* resilience that even though abortion was an issue at the ballot box, supporters of nationwide access to abortion never thought it could be overturned. So why did they "cave so easily" to Mitch McConnell? Besides the fact that there was nothing they could do about it, they didn't press harder or turn it into a campaign issue because the never in a million years thought *Roe* would be overturned. Until it was.
