Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can Trump adjourn Congress?** Yes, although the President is assuming that the Constitutional authority applies in this circumstance when it may not. The Constitution says the President \"may, on extraordinary Occasions, convene both Houses, or either of them, and in Case of Disagreement between them, with Respect to the Time of Adjournment, he may adjourn them to such Time as he shall think proper\" (Article II, Section 3). However, SCOTUS has never ruled on that clause, since it has never been invoked before. There is some disagreement as to what constitutes "disagreement" with respect to the time of adjournment and whether or not he can only adjourn sessions which he has convened because of "extraordinary Occasions."
