**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do police investigate missing parcels that say delivered and aren\'t received when there is no security video or any proof. Also if the sender files the report would the receiver get a knock on the door by a investigating officer to get more info?** *(US Answer Only)* Police do not investigate missing parcels, because missing parcels is not a crime. If you are alleging mail theft or "porch piracy," even if you have proof, it's highly unlikely for the police to be able to catch the perpetrator, so it's again highly unlikely that the police would investigate at all. The only investigation that *might* be done would be accomplished by the delivery service (UPS, FedEx, or UPS if you used a trackable delivery feature). They could tell you if they delivered the package, and where. Amazon, now entering the "last mile" delivery game, has its system set up so that the delivery person takes a picture of the parcel on the doorstep, and that picture is included in Amazon's delivery report, thus proving that they dropped the package on your doorstep. Porch piracy would therefore need to be investigated by the police, if they will even investigate.
