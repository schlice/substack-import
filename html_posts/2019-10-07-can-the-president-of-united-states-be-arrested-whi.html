**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the President of United States be arrested while in charge?** Practically, no the President cannot be arrested while in office. He is surrounded by Secret Service and is under surveillance 24x7. It would be a tremendous challenge to arrest the President. Constitutionally, it's much more clear that the President cannot be arrested. Article II, Section 1 of the Constitution is very clear: "The executive power shall be vested in a President of the United States of America." This means that **the President** ***is*** **the Executive**. Not the President and the Vice President. Not the President and the Attorney General. Not the President and the Director of the FBI. The President only. This was a deliberate decision on the part of the Framers, and it was done in part to balance the Executive and check the power of the Judiciary and the Legislature. To that end, **all Executive authority flows from the President**. The authority of any prosecutor to issue an indictment, the power of any sheriff to attempt an arrest, all flows directly from the power of the executive, which is exclusively vested in the President. It's the same reason that a deputy cannot arrest the sheriff: it's the sheriff who deputized the deputy. He could instantly revoke that deputization, and the deputy would have no authority. Therefore, the President cannot be indicted or arrested while in office for several reasons: 1. The President *is* the source for all executive authority. Since he granted the authority, it is plainly obvious that he will not allow himself to be indicted while in office. 2. Arresting the President would have serious functional ramifications on the government. In order to maintain smooth functioning of the government, you cannot remove a key officer of the government and expect it to continue to run smoothly. So does this mean the President is above the law? Of course not. The Constitution included a provision to deal with Presidential lawlessness: *impeachment*. You can impeach a president and remove him from office, and then you can feel free to indict and/or arrest him.
