**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I think you missed what I was saying, because I absolutely agree with you. Point a), I'm not so sure that a lack of funding within law enforcement is the issue. Perhaps a shortfall of funding within BATFE (whose jurisdiction FFLs fall under) is indeed the issue. Point b), we should most definitely make those arrests, and Point c), we should actually immediately revoke that FFL from that dealer. Even in cases where the FFL has found to be in the wrong, very rarely is the license actually revoked. This is a travesty. And Point d) would be moot if we could get BATFE to do their jobs and revoke FFLs from dealers who actually do break the law. If they can no longer sell firearms because they're not playing by the rules, we would indeed put a big dent in the illegal arms trade. It would, of course, transition to other forms of illegal arms trade outside of the errant illegally-operating FFLs, but when you're faced with a dam with a million holes, you gotta start plugging those holes somewhere.
