**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I regularly bounce between multiple versions of the Bible. I use ESV, NIV, and the 1995 version of the NASB. And my favorite app to do that is [[YouVersion](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6ecbdf&url=https://app.bible.com/app-android){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="bible.com"}]{.qlink_container}. Some of the things I love about it: - Read yourself, or listen to an audio version of any chapter or verse you happen to be reading. - Easily switch between multiple Bible versions while reading - Highlight important verses, and save your highlights across YouVersion apps - Add Bookmarks, Highlights, and public or private Notes to any verse in the Bible - Share Bible verses on social media - Create Bible Images, which are shareable Bible art using stock photos or your own pictures - Built-in community Friends features help you experience the Bible with others - Offline versions allow you to download many of the Bible versions directly to your device. By the way, all of these features are available in all versions of YouVersion, such as the [[iOS](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=637399&url=https://app.bible.com/app-ios){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="bible.com"}]{.qlink_container} version, or the [[Kindle](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5b4fa7&url=http://www.amazon.com/YouVersion-Bible/dp/B004MC8CA2){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} version.
