--- title: '"If your god is all knowing and all powerful, why do you pray? Do you think your god needs your information, advice, or will change its plans because it realizes that you have better ideas than it does?"' date: '2024-07-23' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} God's mind is infinite, and God does not dwell in spacetime, like we do. In order for God to "change His mind," He would have to exist in one state of mind "before" He changed His mind (which already, He can't do), and then He would have to learn of something He didn't know before (which He also can't do because He knows all) and then He would have to use that "new information" to do something differently. So God doesn't "change His mind." But God does take our free choices into consideration when He made plans for how everything would unfold for all eternity. This is challenging concept to wrap our brains around, because we are constrained by life inside of spacetime. But let's try a few scenarios to see if we can understand it somewhat (if not completely): Let's say that Dave's wife has cancer. **Dave prays fervently for his wife to be healed** of the cancer, and Dave gets his whole church to do the same. Dave knows that God may not choose to heal his wife, but he demonstrates his faith in God and recognizes God's power to heal his wife. He requests that God would heal his wife and extend her life if God is willing to do so. **Let's say that God chooses to heal Dave's wife in response to the prayer** of Dave and the others in his church. Now, **on the one hand,** God eternally knew that He was going to allow Dave's wife to have cancer and **He eternally knew that He would heal Dave's wife**. **But, on the other hand,** we must not forget that **God also eternally knew that Dave would freely choose to pray fervently for the healing of his wife, and God eternally knew that He would respond to that prayer**. In this way, God allowed for Dave to make the free choice to pray fervently for the healing of his wife, which He already knew would happen, and knowing that it would happen, God already planned for all of eternity to heal Dave's wife in response to that free-choice prayer. So in a way, God already knew that Dave would pray to heal his wife, and so He took those prayers into consideration when He determined what would happen to this universe and everything in it from all of eternity. Now, **let's say that God eternally knew that Dave would** ***not*** **pray for his wife to be healed**. If Dave and the others chose not to pray for his wife because they believed that prayer would do no good because God's plans are already fixed, then God would have eternally known that, and He would have taken that into account in His plans for how the world would unfold. In this case, God's eternal plan may, or may not, have included healing Dave's wife at all. But, as it is, **God eternally knew that Dave** ***would*** **pray for his wife, and so (from eternity past) God took this into account in His plans**. So, even though God eternally knows what He is going to do about everything (unlike humans who decide things as we go), He also eternally knows what we humans will freely choose to do at our moment of choice (including our prayers), and He incorporates our free choices into His eternal plans for us. However, like we already mentioned, our prayers do not cause God to "change His mind." God's decision to answer Dave's prayer was already made, and the course of action God took was also already eternally chosen, based on Dave's decision (at the moment of choice) to pray fervently for the healing of his wife. So God's actions are known from all eternity, but our decisions are made on the fly, and we don't know what we will choose at that moment. But God, being omnipotent, sees all spacetime as "now." The past is now. The present is now. The future is now. When God made His plans for how everything would turn out, He already took into consideration what we were going to freely choose, and He used those free choices to determine how the universe would unfold, which He already knew for all eternity.
