**Content:** [First, for the specifics, read my previous answer on this:\ \ [[Bill Stein\'s answer to Can a man with multiple sclerosis have a normal sex life?](https://www.quora.com/Can-a-man-with-multiple-sclerosis-have-a-normal-sex-life/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container}\ \ Next, don\'t avoid sex just because you have MS. First of all, you\'ll never know whether or not you actually are having an issue with sexual dysfunction if you avoid sex.\ \ Second, it\'s not something to fear; it\'s something to work around. There are drugs readily available to help with sexual dysfunction (Viagra, Cialis, etc), so don\'t worry, even if you do start to have sexual dysfunction caused by MS, you can work around it.\ \ Also, you don\'t want this to become psychosomatic; i.e. you get so worried about not being able to perform that you actually start to perform poorly because of your fear. In other words, your fear of performing poorly actually causes you to perform poorly, not any disease activity.\ \ Finally, don\'t forget to talk to your doctor about any symptoms you are experiencing, regardless of whether or not you may be embarrassed because of them. If you are not open and honest with your doctor, you won\'t be getting the best care you possibly can.\ \ Good luck, hang in there!
