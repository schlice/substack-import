--- title: "Why did the Supreme Court hold that parents were entitled to opt their children out of school lessons that could violate their religious beliefs?" date: "2025-06-27" tags: ["policy-analysis"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Why? It's simple. The parents argued that without an opt-out provision, their First Amendment religious freedoms were violated. Writing for the court majority, Justice Samuel Alito agreed, saying parents challenging the board\'s introduction of the \"LGBTQ+-inclusive\" storybooks, along with its decision to withhold opt-outs, are allowed to excuse their children from the classes related to the books while the litigation proceeds. Alito wrote, \"The parents are likely to succeed on their claim that the Board\'s policies unconstitutionally burden their religious exercise.\" The school board, for its part, noted that it did initially allow opt-outs from specific lesson plans, but it removed that ability when it became too onerous and disruptive to manage all of the opt-outs. The board argued that while it is easy enough to facilitate single-class opt-outs, as the school district provides for sex education, it is much more challenging to take children from the classroom every time that a book mentions same sex parents or gay and lesbian kids. To that end, parents are now able to excuse their children from those classes related to those books while the lawsuit regarding opt-outs continues.
