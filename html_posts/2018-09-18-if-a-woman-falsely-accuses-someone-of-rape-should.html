**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a woman falsely accuses someone of rape should there be strong punishment if she really believes her version of history?** The problem is your word choice. "Falsely" simply implies that she is wrong. She could be wrong because she has faulty memory (but she strongly believes that what she remembers is the truth). Or, she could be wrong because she is *deliberately* speaking untruths in order to harm her alleged offender. In the first case, she should not be punished. In the second case, she ***should*** be punished (strongly, as you worded it), since in today's society, the accusation carries far more weight than the actual factual findings in a legal sense. Therefore, accusations should never be made lightly, and they should **absolutely never** be made maliciously, or with deliberate lies. Deliberately lying about sexual assault *should* be a criminal charge, not just what the civil slander laws (and its attendant penalties) can recover.
