--- title: '"Which accomplishments of President Obama will be overturned if Trump becomes president?"' date: '2016-05-23' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The only items that the President can summarily overturn are anything that a previous President has enacted via Executive Order. Therefore, anything in that list which was enacted by Executive Order can be rescinded by Executive Order. Everything else was done through Congress, who actually writes law. The new President can *recommend*, even *strongly recommend* creation and passage of a particular law, but if Congress is disinclined to do so, it doesn't get done. Any Executive Orders which were passed because "Congress failed to act" should be rescinded. If Congress failed to act, it's because Congress lacked the political will to act (which also means the people did not provide the requisite support to act, either). The President overrides both the Constitution and the will of the people when he acts unilaterally because "Congress failed to act." A failure to act is an action in and of itself. Congress is deliberate in its inaction, just as it is deliberate in its action. Failure to act doesn't mean they ran out of time, or they couldn't figure it out. Failure to act means they **chose** not to act. That is a deliberate choice. And since Obamacare passed with **no Republican votes whatsoever** and only passed because Democrats use a Parliamentary trick reserved for budget negotiations (which Congress has also failed to do for years) called *reconciliation,* don't be surprised if Republicans repeal Obamacare using reconciliation as well. The only hope of such a tactic working is with a Republican president.
