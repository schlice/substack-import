**Content:** [We have already answered the second part of your question in great depth\ \ [[Why do American police always shoot to kill?](https://www.quora.com/Why-do-American-police-always-shoot-to-kill)]{.qlink_container}\ [[Are police officers taught/trained to \"shoot to kill\" or to \"shoot to incapacitate\"?](https://www.quora.com/Are-police-officers-taught-trained-to-shoot-to-kill-or-to-shoot-to-incapacitate)]{.qlink_container}\ \ The distilled version:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - A gun is deadly force. If you resort to your gun, it\'s because you are facing a situation in which lethal force is authorized. - Police don\'t \"shoot to kill.\" Police \"shoot to stop the act.\" While it\'s true that death sometimes happens when people are shot, understand that death is not the goal. For example, if police shoot a person and he lives, did the officer fail to do his job? Not at all. Police shoot to stop the perpetrator from doing something which can cause great harm to the officer or the public.\ - A shot in the leg is not always non-fatal. You can hit the femoral artery which will cause death before the ambulance arrives through *exsanguination*, or \"bleeding out.\" \ We have more guns per person in the US than in any other country in the world. Having an unarmed police force makes no logical sense.
