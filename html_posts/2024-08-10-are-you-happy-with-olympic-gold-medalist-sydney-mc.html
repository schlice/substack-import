Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What's to be not happy about? She is a woman of faith. She is under no compunction to keep her faith under a basket while she's representing her country. Unless you've forgotten, the ability to freely practice your religion is in the Bill of Rights. So if she wants to credit everything that she does to God's grace, what difference does that make to you? The United States is a country where you are free to practice whatever religion you choose, or no religion at all. But that also brings a *responsibility* to allow those who **do** wish to practice their religion to do so without your interference. Remember, you don't have the right to be *free from religion*. The Constitution doesn't recognize such a right. But it *does* recognize the right of every individual to practice their religion as they please, even on the world stage. And that is a beautiful thing for the world to see.
