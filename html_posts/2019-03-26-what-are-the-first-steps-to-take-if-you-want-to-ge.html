--- title: "What are the first steps to take if you want to get rid of a neighborhood Home owners Association (HOA)?" date: "2019-03-26" tags: ["law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What are the first steps to take if you want to get rid of a neighborhood Home owners Association (HOA)?** You will first need to find out what is necessary in your area to dissolve an HOA. It will require a certain percentage of the community to sign their approval of the dissolution, and you will have to meet that minimum standard. For many communities, it's 3/4 of the homeowners; for others, it's 100%. You will then need to ascertain a list of everything that the HOA pays for, and all real property that the HOA owns. Usually, the things the HOA pays for are associated with the real property the HOA owns. Common areas can be donated to non-profit organizations, who would then take over the ongoing maintenance of those areas. For example, a playground may be taken over by a non-profit organization, who would then be responsible for paying for things like watering the grass, paying for the general liability insurance on the area, upkeep of equipment, etc. If the HOA owns roads, the roads would revert back to city/county ownership, and the city would have to agree to annex that property. This part could take some time. Your attorney can help you with all of that. On that note, you will do all of this with the assistance of a licensed real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law. This attorney will also need to be paid, along with the filing fees to file the HOA dissolution documents with the city/county. The place to start, therefore, is a comprehensive survey of all property owners in the community, since you will need all of their help to dissolve an HOA. Good luck.
