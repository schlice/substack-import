**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is **yes.** In religion, we call it a [[**theophany**](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4d11b9&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theophany){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}**.** A theophany is a physical manifestation of God, and it doesn't have to occur just in prayer. Theophanies are documented in the Bible. Some of them are God appearing as: - A burning bush - A pillar of smoke - A cloud One of the intrinsic characteristics of a theophany is its *temporariness*. It is not a permanent manifestation, but only a temporary one. There is a really great story of Jacob in Genesis wrestling with a man who was actually a theophany: > *Then Jacob was left alone; and a Man wrestled with him until the > breaking of day. Now when He saw that He did not prevail against him, > He touched the socket of his hip; and the socket of Jacob's hip was > out of joint as He wrestled with him. \[...\] And He said, "Your name > shall no longer be called Jacob, but Israel; for you have struggled > with God and with men, and have prevailed."* > > *Then Jacob asked, saying, "Tell me Your name, I pray."* > > *And He said, "Why is it that you ask about My name?" And He blessed > him there. So Jacob called the name of the place Peniel: "For I have > seen God face to face, and my life is preserved." --- Genesis > 32:24--25, 28--30* So is it possible for someone to physically see or feel God when praying? In Christianity at least, the answer is yes, it is possible. \ But what about today? If we're asking about modern-day theophanies, I think we're asking the wrong question, because the fulfilled promise of Jesus changes the game. Our friends at [[Christianity.com](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=dd40fe&url=http://Christianity.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="christianity.com"}]{.qlink_container} summarized it way better than I could: > *As Christians, the question we must ask ourselves is not "How can I > experience a theophany?" but rather "How can I* ***be*** *a > theophany?" After all, if a theophany is a manifestation of God's > presence in the world, then this is precisely the call that Christians > have. Our lives are to herald the presence of Jesus. Jesus calls us to > this very way of life when he says "let your light shine before others > that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven" > (*[[*Matthew > 5:16*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=96cf39&url=http://www.christianity.com/bible/search/?ver%3Dniv%26q%3Dmatthew%2B5:16){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="christianity.com"}]{.qlink_container}*). We are made in > the image of God and empowered by the Holy Spirit for this purpose. > Thus, Christians today are the very places through which Christ is > revealed to the world.*[\[1\]](#AJeHU){#cite-AJeHU .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-AJeHU){.citation_index} [[What Is a Theophany in the Bible?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2cb017&url=https://www.christianity.com/wiki/christian-terms/what-is-a-theophany-in-the-bible.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="christianity.com"}]{.qlink_container}
