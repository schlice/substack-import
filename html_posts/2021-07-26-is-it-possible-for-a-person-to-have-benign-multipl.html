**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: yes. Longer answer: Benign MS is characterized by a single symptom or a group of symptoms followed by a very long period of no disease activity whatsoever. You can't really say that you have benign MS unless you've gone at least **fifteen years** with virtually no visible symptoms, and little to no disability. Bear in mind: - A diagnosis of benign MS does not mean that you will always be symptom-free. A *history* of no disease activity does not guarantee a *future* of no disease activity. - MRIs can still show that the disease is actually progressing but not producing any visible symptoms. - You can still have some issues like MS Lassitude (fatigue) or memory issues even if you don't have any motor function-related symptoms (weakness, balance, bowel/bladder, etc). \ Original question: Is it possible for a person to have benign multiple sclerosis for 27 years with no change to their walking?
