**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is the US Supreme Court too Catholic?** LOL Seriously, I just got finished laughing. On the other hand, is that a bad thing? I mean, I don't even think the Catholic Church is *Catholic enough!* In any case, the question asks a factually flawed question. Supreme Court justices are not selected based on their religion; it is specifically prohibited in the Constitution. Article VI, Paragraph 3 (emphasis mine): > *The Senators and Representatives before mentioned, and the Members of > the several State Legislatures, and all executive and judicial > Officers, both of the United States and of the several States, shall > be bound by Oath or Affirmation, to support this Constitution;* ***but > no religious Test shall ever be required as a Qualification to any > Office or public Trust under the United States.*** So the SCOTUS can never be *too Catholic*, since no religious test is authorized.
