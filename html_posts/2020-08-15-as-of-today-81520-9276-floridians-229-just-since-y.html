**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Florida resident here, so I actually have some skin in the game. Yes, we have had deaths. But compared to the number of deaths (per 100,000) in other areas of the country, Florida doesn't even move the needle. Florida is 25th in the country in terms of deaths per 100K residents. New York City is first, with 278.7 (NY as a state has 78.9). Florida has 23.4. The list of states with more deaths per 100K residents is pretty extensive, and includes NJ, CT, MA, RI, DC, NY, LA, MI, IL, MD, PA, DE, MS, IN, AZ, CO, GA, NH, MN, NM, OH, AL, IA, and VA.[\[1\]](#IiXOM){#cite-IiXOM .citation_link} I bet you're surprised to hear that. That's because you're not being told the whole story. You're being told every day that there are X-thousand number of new cases in Florida, breaking all previous one-day records. Without additional context, however, this only tells you the pervasiveness and the effectiveness of our testing. Are these people symptomatic? Were they hospitalized? Not even close. You're also being told that "doing more testing" is not a valid reason for seeing more cases. Really? As of this week, we're now testing anyone who walks in the door and says they want to be tested. Just two weeks ago, you had to be symptomatic and over 65, even at our drive-up testing locations. Today, anyone can drive in and get tested. And we're finding lots of people who are asymptomatic. And with the record numbers of people reporting that they are infected, our low death rates are a testament to the fact that Florida took steps to protect the most vulnerable among us: the elderly. As you know, Florida has whole cities of nothing but retirees (i.e The Villages). We put strict rules in place to protect those communities. For example, unlike New York, we don't return COVID-positive seniors back to their homes. We set up twelve COVID-positive assisted living facilities around the state; that's where seniors go when they test positive, not back to their communities to infect others. You're also being told that ICUs are filling up. Again, data without context, just to scare you. Are those all COVID patients? Actually, only around 15% of ICU patients are COVID cases. The rest? Remember when people were deferring hospital visits and surgeries because they were afraid to go to the hospital? Now they're coming in, and their conditions are worse than before. So (surprise, surprise) they have to go to the ICU for care. So ICU capacity is down because we're seeing more ICU patients for lots of reasons, some only tangentially related to the pandemic. Also, hospitals have put plans in place to essentially double their ICU capacity if they need to. They don't need to, yet. But you're still being told that there is no ICU space. Data without context. You're being told that there are all of these counties in Florida that have no ICU capacity. A grain of truth in a sea of misinformation. Many rural counties in Florida have no ICU capacity, period. Their critical cases must be shipped to a larger city nearby for intensive care. They've never had ICU capacity. It does appear that this information is selectively and creatively being reported, just to scare you. Don't let the media win. Don't let them scare you! The number of people who are reporting as positive does not equal the number of people showing symptoms.
