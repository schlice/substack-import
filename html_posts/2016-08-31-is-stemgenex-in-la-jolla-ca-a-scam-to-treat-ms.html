**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} To add to [[Me](https://www.quora.com/profile/Me-9043)]{.qlink_container}'s answer, I think you would be hard-pressed to categorize such a business as a *scam*. What it may be, more than a scam, is a very expensive, very unproven, treatment option. Some people have found great success with stem cell treatment. Some stem cell treatments (for example, treatment which completely rebuilds your immune system using stem cells, after destroying it with chemotherapy) are very dangerous and should only be used when there are **zero** other options for treating your disease. Stem cell treatments have not been proven to be effective. They haven't been recognized by any governing body as a treatment which does anything it claims to do. That doesn't mean it may not work. It just means that it may not work with any regularity, or with any predictability. We also don't know the *permanence* of such treatment. Does it wear off in a year? Five years? Are there any undesirable side effects? Has anyone who has received this treatment had his/her disease return, ever? And most importantly for any treatment, how *safe* is it? What's the risk? Can it kill you? Basically, is the treatment worse than the disease? Until we know the answers to these questions, stem cell treatment is still in its young, development phase. Not yet ready, IMHO, for prime time.
