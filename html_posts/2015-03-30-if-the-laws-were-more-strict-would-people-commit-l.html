**Content:** [Limiting the discussion to capital offenses as you asked, I would say that the strictness of the law has little to do with whether or not people will actually commit the crime. In some states, capital offenses are punishable by death, and I\'m not sure we can get much stricter than that. Yet, people still commit murder in those states.\ \ What tends to be the aggravating factor is the fact that people can get away with it so easily. It\'s a known fact that most criminals do not get caught. So if I\'m a \"smart criminal\" (an oxymoron if I ever heard one), I\'d do the crime and NOT get caught.
