**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **For any reason at all: if a company that produces bulletproof materials offers to completely bulletproof your truck/car for free, would you accept the offer, even if you don't live in an area that's full of violence and prone to theft and vandalism?** No. First of all, while it would be *cool* to have a totally bulletproof vehicle, there are a few disadvantages. - Bulletproofing, while it would be free, is not *light*. It's heavy. In fact, it's *really* heavy. Adding an extra half ton to the vehicle in bulletproofing will mess with lots of things in the vehicle: performance, acceleration/braking, gas mileage, premature wear on components of the car, etc. - While it might make a cool novelty car, I don't buy cars that just sit in my driveway as novelties. I'm not that rich. Every car I own is a working vehicle, even if it's just a daily driver. I can't afford to fully bulletproof a car and then have to drive it around every day. It actually makes the driving experience that much more dangerous. The President's bulletproof limousine is built on a *truck* frame, not a stretched car frame. My car, built on a *car* frame, can't handle the added stresses and will eventually fail, leaving me without a car. **TL;DR:** While it would be cool, and free, it's unnecessary and not good for my car.
