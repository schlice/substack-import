**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A no confidence vote would simply remove the person from office. Trump would go back to Manhattan and would resume CEO duties for the Trump Corporation. If Trump himself was removed from office under such a vote (if we had a mechanism to do that), the 25th Amendment (and its subsequent definitions) would kick in, and the Vice President would become President for Trump's remaining term. We don't have a "no-confidence" vote because we are fairly confident through over 250 years of precedent that we can peacefully vote someone out of office when the next election rolls around. The people have decided that four years is enough time to give someone to either generate confidence, or prove that they have none. Precedent has shown us that the only necessity would be to make someone a "one-term President."
