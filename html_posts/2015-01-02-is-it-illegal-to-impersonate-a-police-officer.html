--- title: '"Is it illegal to impersonate a police officer?"' date: '2015-01-02' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You are protected by the First Amendment when you dress up in a police uniform. The minute you try to a) gain special favors, or b) try to perform the duties of a police officer, you are impersonating a police officer and can go to jail. Same holds true of military impersonators. People dress up in military uniforms all the time, and while we in the military think that\'s disgraceful, if we believe in the Constitution, they have a right to do it. The minute they walk into a Starbucks and try to get a 10% military discount on their latte, they are impersonating a military member to attempt to receive illicit gain, and are in violation of law in all states (it's called ***theft of service***). The point at which the impersonation moves from protected free speech to illegality is when the impersonator attempts to extract some gain from the impersonation, or attempts to perform the duties of the impersonated.
