**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Without automatic weapons in the hands of a shooter, how could the Las Vegas deaths have happened and 17 children get murdered in mass killings?** Maybe I'm not understanding the question, but it seems like you're asking how people can kill if they don't have guns. You do realize that guns make killing ***easier***. Guns don't make killing ***possible.*** Here's a few examples of other items which are not guns which have been used to kill: - Trucks. [[2002 Bali bombings](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=425bbd&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2002_Bali_bombings){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} - Fire. [[Cases of arson-homicide in Australia almost double in 20 years: study](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d3aca3&url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-07-03/arson-homicides-on-the-rise-in-australia-institute-criminology/6592448){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="abc.net.au"}]{.qlink_container} - Arson is the tool *du jour* in Australia. Incidentally, you'll notice that arson homicides have doubled since the Australians instituted a gun ban, which just lends credence to my stance that if you take guns away, killers will simply change their killing tools - Knives. [[A knife attack every 4 minutes; 130,000 per year - but ministers still insist crime rates are falling](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5598da&url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-1036154/A-knife-attack-4-minutes-130-000-year--ministers-insist-crime-rates-falling.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="dailymail.co.uk"}]{.qlink_container} - Knives are the preferred killing tool in the UK. There, where guns are also banned, roughly 350 people **per day** are victims of knife attacks. - Airplanes. [[September 11 attacks](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=1c9366&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/September_11_attacks){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} Any one of these methods could have been used with varying degrees of success in Las Vegas and in Parkland, FL. Some methods may have caused fewer deaths, others may have caused far more deaths. This is the reason that focusing on the tool is the wrong idea. I mean, let's make sure we're not sloppy with the tool. I think we should raise the minimum age to buy a long gun to 21 (not possess, just buy, and then keep a record of transfer to someone under 21). But beyond things like that, let's make sure we focus on the killer, not the tool they use. You do remember that murder is illegal too, right? Yet these killers, regardless of the tool, continue to murder, despite the presence or absence of the tool.
