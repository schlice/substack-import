**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You have to explain what "harassment" is. In Florida, harassment has a specific legal definition. > Fl. Stat. 784.048 Stalking; definitions; penalties.--- (1) As used in > this section, the term: (a) "Harass" means **to engage in a course of > conduct directed at a specific person which causes substantial > emotional distress to that person and serves no legitimate purpose**. So while the HOA fulfilling its duty may cause you substantial emotional distress, in order to qualify as harassment, what the HOA is doing must serve no legitimate purpose. If the HOA can claim that their actions are in furtherance of the execution of their duties, and those duties are found to be lawful, it's unlikely you will prevail in a harassment lawsuit. But if you really believe you have a case for harassment, contact a real estate attorney in your jurisdiction who specializes in HOA law.
