**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because religious beliefs are *naturally* exclusive. If I believe that Islam is true, then I therefore believe that anything that is not Islam *is not true*. If I believe that Christianity is true, then I believe that anything that is not Christianity is not true. This directly contradicts the concept of "tolerance" and "pluralism" promoted in today's society, because *society sees "tolerance" as more than just "coexisting."* Society's definition of "tolerance" today means not just treating those with different ideas civilly and respectfully. "Tolerance" today means ***affirming their ideas are valid*,** which traditional religious people (Christians especially) cannot do without denying their own beliefs. For example, if you believe that the Bible says that homosexual behavior is sinful, you can't at the same time agree that homosexual behavior is not sinful. **TL;DR:** Religion is a problem because religion teaches that absolute truth exists and can be known. Religion is now becoming a problem because society's *belief about truth* is changing, and religion is not. I gave a little more context about truth in another answer I wrote today: [[Bill Stein\'s answer to I can't stand the phrase "speak my truth", can you give suggestions for anything else we can say for that concept?](/I-can-t-stand-the-phrase-speak-my-truth-can-you-give-suggestions-for-anything-else-we-can-say-for-that-concept/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} So understand this was a secular answer, but this is especially poignant when applied to Christianity. The postmodern secularists (some of whom answered that question) don't have to address the intrinsic contradictions in their views. They **reject** the idea of absolute truth and the Law of Noncontradiction. They just flit along their merry way moralizing to everyone about "new Tolerance." These "tolerance peddlers" are exposed as frauds when you realize that they don't practice what they preach, especially when it comes to Christians. They certainly don't "tolerate" that Jesus is the only way to salvation, as this would invalidate their ideas about tolerance. To them, our insistence that absolute truth exists and that Christ is the author of truth is so beyond the pale as to *disqualify Christians from receiving the tolerance of others.* To justify their positions, they use pithy (but logically incongruent) statements such as, "we cannot tolerate the intolerant." They say these with a straight face, not even realizing the glaring contradiction in their logic. But when Christians claim that there is one true religion, this doesn't mean that they are intolerant or disrespectful of others or their beliefs. Modern culture has been intentionally confused by these distinctions, so much so that they feel comfortable enough in their confusion to paint the Christian as intolerant of those who believe differently. However, we should realize that Christianity is not the only religion which makes truth claims. All major religions make such claims, which belies their assertion that all religions essentially believe the same thing, but through different views. So much for pluralism, eh?
