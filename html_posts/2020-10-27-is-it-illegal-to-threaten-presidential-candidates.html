**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it illegal to threaten presidential candidates online?** While intricacies always exist, the short answer is an emphatic **yes**. Reference ***18 U.S. Code § 879: Threats against former Presidents and certain other persons*** > (a)Whoever knowingly and willfully threatens to kill, kidnap, or > inflict bodily harm upon--- > > \(1\) a former President or a member of the immediate family of a > former President; > > \(2\) a member of the immediate family of the President, the[ > [](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=75b2f4&url=https://www.law.cornell.edu/definitions/uscode.php?width%3D840%26height%3D800%26iframe%3Dtrue%26def_id%3D18-USC-586235682-206521002%26term_occur%3D999%26term_src%3Dtitle:18:part:I:chapter:41:section:879){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cornell.edu"} > ]{.qlink_container}President-elect[[,](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=75b2f4&url=https://www.law.cornell.edu/definitions/uscode.php?width%3D840%26height%3D800%26iframe%3Dtrue%26def_id%3D18-USC-586235682-206521002%26term_occur%3D999%26term_src%3Dtitle:18:part:I:chapter:41:section:879){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="cornell.edu"}]{.qlink_container} the Vice President, or > the Vice President-elect; > > \(3\) a major candidate for the office of President or Vice President, > or a member of the immediate family of such candidate; or > > \(4\) a person protected by the Secret Service under section > 3056(a)(6); > > shall be fined under this title or imprisoned not more than 5 years, > or both. I'll skip the definition of "immediate family" and I'll skip the definition of paragraph 4 since those weren't asked for. But the definition in paragraph 3 of "a major candidate" is defined in Title 18 § 3056(a)(7) as "those individuals identified as such by the Secretary of Homeland Security after consultation with an advisory committee consisting of the Speaker of the House of Representatives, the minority leader of the House of Representatives, the majority and minority leaders of the Senate, and one additional member selected by the other members of the committee." In other words, once Congress recognizes that a person is a major candidate and identifies them to the Secretary of Homeland Security, they are covered under this section. Their spouses are also covered within 120 days of the election as well. The definition of "threaten" which matters in this context is threatening to "kill, kidnap, or inflict bodily harm." The medium is irrelevant. Threats anywhere are taken seriously.
