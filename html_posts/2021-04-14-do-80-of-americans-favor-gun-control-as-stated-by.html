**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do 80% of Americans favor gun control as stated by the White House press secretary?** Remember the old phrase: *There's lies, damned lies, and statistics.* So sure, 80% of Americans favor "gun control" depending on how you ask them the question. Most questions are related to background checks, and yes, most Americans favor requiring background checks on all gun transfers. When asked just this question, they usually respond yes. It's easy to say "yes, I want that" but it's less easy to understand *how* you go about implementing that (which is difficult), and *how to pay for it* (as it will be very expensive). So while we may "want" that, it certainly doesn't mean we want to do everything possible in order to pay for it. And no universal background check system has ever been implemented in a way in which anyone would refer to as "smart." They simply state that **all gun transfers require a background check, period.** But what does that functionally mean? If I want to gift a gun to my wife, I have to get a background check done on her. If I want to buy my 18 year old son a plinking rifle, I have to get a background check on him. Laws in the four states which have them have been reported to have no better than a 5% compliance rate because of how unworkable they are in practice. And are we really catching any criminals here? Are we really preventing gun crime? Or are we really inconveniencing lawful gun owners more than stopping crime? And ***absolutely none of that has anything to do with what we really feel about "gun control," whatever that means.*** So it's easy to distort statistics to make them say whatever you want.
