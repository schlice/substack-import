--- title: '"Is the United States Government a terrorist organization?"' date: '2015-10-06' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [A terrorist organization uses fear to meet political ends. Terrorists will usually use illegal, sensational acts of violence to frighten (terror) people into making political change. They will almost always take responsibility for their crimes, and indicate that such crimes will continue until their demands are met.\ \ The United States is engaged in a legal conflict known as the Global War on Terror (the GWOT). As the article rightly states, the US military uses every possible shred of evidence it can find, as is gathered by the CIA, the NSA, and other intelligence-gathering organizations. Most of the people killed in drone strikes are indeed militants.\ \ But on occasion, even the best intelligence can be wrong. The hospital in Kunduz was one such accident. Sometimes these things can be attributed to the \"fog of war.\" The US was responding to an Afghan call for an airstrike, and it\'s still unsure whether or not we targeted the facility that the Afghanis requested we target. If militants had taken up position within the hospital, the hospital becomes a valid target under the Geneva Convention. We don\'t know whether or not this is completely true. Doctors Without Borders claims that all people who were killed were either hospital workers or patients. But this claim has not yet been fully substantiated. The Afghanis have not stated yet which building they indicated for strike and whether or not there was a reason to target a hospital if they did, in fact, request an airstrike on the hospital. This is still subject to investigation.\ \ You do have to rely on your intelligence to execute an operation, and it is true that sometimes, there are mistakes made. The White House has said, through its spokesman, Josh Earnest, \"There is no country in the world and no military in the world that goes to greater lengths and places a higher premium on avoiding civilian casualties than the United States Department of Defense.\"\ \ Calling the United States a \"terrorist organization\" would mean that we routinely and **knowingly** engage in illegal acts of violence for the sole reason of enacting political ends. You would have to have information stating that the US ***knew*** that the hospital did not contain any militants, that it was not a valid target under the Geneva Convention, and the military commanders made a decision to destroy that hospital anyway, in order to effect political change. Then, you will have to show that the US ***admitted*** to killing all of the civilians, and then that the US ***demanded*** a particular political end or more civilians in non-combat areas would be intentionally targeted.\ \ Since this is not happening and is not likely to ever happen, you cannot call the United States a terrorist organization and be speaking from any platform of fact.]{.rendered_qtext}
