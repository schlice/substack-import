**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If I see a bear in my backyard and I\'m between it and my house, what should I do?** Go inside. Black bears are not prone to attacking you without being provoked. That doesn't mean they're like puppies, but they certainly are much less aggressive than grizzlies. Just walk backwards, keep your eyes near the bear (I suggest looking at his feet and not locking eyes with him) until you get to your house and go inside. They should leave you alone. Grizzlies are much more dangerous, but follow the same basic rules: make yourself look big (if you're wearing a coat, open it up and hold it open like a cape), walk backwards, don't make eye contact (look at the legs or feet), and slowly but methodically make your way back to your house and go inside. Bears with cubs are a whole other ballgame. All momma bears are going to be protective of their cubs, so if there are cubs in the mix, you definitely want to back up slowly, not make any threatening moves, but keep backing up until you get into your house.
