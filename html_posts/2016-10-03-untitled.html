**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is actually not true. In fact, if you as a private citizen were threatened with death or grave bodily harm, you too have the right to defend yourself with force. There are two differences: - As a citizen, you can only **match** the force presented to you. The police are allowed to use higher levels of force to compel compliance. - The police have an additional duty to safeguard the general public; the private citizen has no such duty. But if that attacker is engaging you, you have the same right to defend yourself as any police officer does.
