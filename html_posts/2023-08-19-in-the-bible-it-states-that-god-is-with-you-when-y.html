**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I take it you've never heard "Footprints," have you? > One night I dreamed a dream.\ > As I was walking along the beach with my Lord.\ > Across the dark sky flashed scenes from my life.\ > For each scene, I noticed two sets of footprints in the sand,\ > One belonging to me and one to my Lord. > > After the last scene of my life flashed before me,\ > I looked back at the footprints in the sand.\ > I noticed that at many times along the path of my life,\ > especially at the very lowest and saddest times,\ > there was only one set of footprints. > > This really troubled me, so I asked the Lord about it.\ > \"Lord, you said once I decided to follow you,\ > You\'d walk with me all the way.\ > But I noticed that during the saddest and most troublesome times of my > life,\ > there was only one set of footprints.\ > I don\'t understand why, when I needed You the most, You would leave > me.\" > > He whispered, \"My precious child, I love you and will never leave > you\ > Never, ever, during your trials and testings.\ > When you saw only one set of footprints,\ > It was then that I carried you.\" --- Author Unknown God is always with you, whether you willingly walk beside Him, or whether your path tends to stray away from His. He will always be there, gently guiding you back on to His path for your life. And what I've noticed through my handful of decades walking this walk is that if you are obedient and you allow Him to guide your path, **your walk becomes significantly easier.** The times in my life where I chose to "make my own decisions" and "go it alone" without God's guidance and wisdom have been the most challenging times in my life. I'm not saying that all of our challenges are self-inflicted. What I am saying is that if you let God lead you, He will lead you down a path which will ultimately benefit you. > "For I know the plans I have for you," declares the Lord,\ > "plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and > a future." --- Jeremiah 29:11 Even God's plans may be a bit rocky, but if we persevere, His plans will lead us to fullness. I think of it as a tandem bike riding down a wooded path. We all tend to want to be in the front, driving the bike, and we tend to want to put God on the back. When we do this, we may initially prosper, but we may end up going off of the path God has planned for us. Until one day, we finally decide that we want God to lead the course of our life, so we pull over and we say, "Okay God, you drive." He looks at you and says, "Sit down, shut up, and hold on." Then He gets on the front and starts going, *and suddenly veers the bike off of the path and into the trees!* And you're flying down a hill, bumping and bouncing like crazy, and you're literally doing the best you can to hold on. But then, you emerge, and you break out into the most picturesque meadow you have ever seen in your entire life (think postcard-style meadow). And if you hadn't trusted God to drive, and endured the bumps along the way, you never would have found this majestic place. Our walk with God is similar. If you let Him lead, you certainly will not go down the path you intended to go on. Your journey will be filled with bumps and bounces. It will sometimes feel chaotic. **But you have to trust God to bring you through it**. And when you do, it will be like nothing you could ever have imagined.
