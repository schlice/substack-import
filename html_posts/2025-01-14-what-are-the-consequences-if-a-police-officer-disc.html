--- title: '"What are the consequences if a police officer discovers that your car is not insured, even though it is registered and tagged correctly?"' date: '2025-01-14' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This kind of thing happens all the time. You had insurance when you registered your car, but for some reason, your car no longer appears on your insurance. Maybe it's because you changed insurance carriers. Maybe it's because you updated your policy and the car fell off for some reason. It happens. In most states, if you're caught without insurance on your car, you can expect some or all of the following consequences: **Penalties** - **Fines**: You may face a fine of up to \$5,000, depending on the state and how many times you\'ve been caught. - **License suspension**: Your driver\'s license may be suspended. - **Vehicle impoundment**: Your vehicle may be impounded. - **Jail time**: Driving without insurance can be a misdemeanor or felony (depending on your state), and you may face jail time. - **SR-22**: Your insurance company may require you to file an SR-22, which can make it harder to get car insurance in the future. **Liability** - If you\'re at fault in an accident, you may be required to pay for the other party\'s medical expenses and vehicle damage. - If your car is totaled, you may be responsible for the remaining balance on the loan. **Future insurance rates** - Your future car insurance rates may be higher if you\'ve been caught driving without insurance.
