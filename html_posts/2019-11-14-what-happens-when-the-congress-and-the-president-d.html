Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What happens when the Congress and the president do not like a Supreme Court ruling that interprets the meaning of the Constitution?** There are really only three things to do: nothing, re-write the law, or re-write the Constitution. 1. **Nothing.** They can understand that the Supreme Court isn't the last word because they're always right; the Supreme Court is always right because it has the last word. 2. **Re-write the law.** They can write a new law which accomplishes what they wanted to accomplish while staying Constitutional. Perhaps they can remove the unconstitutional portion of the law. Perhaps they can re-word the law to respect the Constitution. 3. **Re-write the Constitution.** They can pass an Amendment to the Constitution and submit it to the States for ratification. If the Amendment is ratified, then they can re-pass the original legislation which is no longer conflicting with the amended Constitution. Admittedly, this would be the most difficult path.
