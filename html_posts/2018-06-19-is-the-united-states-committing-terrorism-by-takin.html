**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is the United States committing terrorism by taking children from their families with no plan for reuniting them?** Nope. The United States is enforcing the laws as written by Congress. Terrorism is a lawless act by its very nature, so the United States is certainly not engaging in terrorism. The goal of separating parents from their children is not to incite terror or fear, so again, the US is not engaging in terrorism. That's an example of *media sensationalism.* It certainly caught your eye, didn't it? Ha, gotcha. The law is very clear: illegal immigrant children (accompanied by their parents or not) cannot be incarcerated for more than 20 days. If their illegal immigrant parents are going to be incarcerated for more than 20 days, the law is clear: the children must be transferred. People also fail to remember that *within this country, each and every day, children are taken from their parents*. This happens to American citizens! Every day! Parents who are arrested have their children taken away each and every day in America, and in far greater numbers than what is happening at the border. But the one thing in common between American citizens and illegal immigrants who have lost custody of their children: **they all broke the law**. They all ended up in jail, and your kids can't stay with you in jail. So until we establish "family jails" where the whole family can stay together in jail, we have no choice but to remove those children.
