**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You've heard already about "attractive nuisance" statutes which require that you take reasonable steps to secure your nuisance, be it a swimming pool or a trampoline. It's important to note that you can successfully defend against a liability lawsuit if you have actually taken those reasonable steps to secure your trampoline. If you erected the appropriate 6-foot privacy fence around your yard, and you installed appropriate warning signs, whatever is required by your locality, and they were all in good repair, you stand a good chance of being able to prevail in such a suit. The court will look at what a reasonable person would do in order to secure a yard with a trampoline, as well as local ordinances for securing "attractive nuisances," and your liability will be reduced based on how far you went to do so.
