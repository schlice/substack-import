**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Roe v Wade. Before you rush to the comments, note that I'm not saying this because of a personal distaste for killing children or a particular lack of empathy for women. I'm only saying this because this decision is **bad law**. Not its outcome, but its method of implementation. This was a terribly sourced decision. I would have much rather seen a decision based on the 14th Amendment and due process and equal protection, instead of creating a right to privacy out of some cobbled-together "emanations and penumbras" to create a right which doesn't actually exist. And I would have much rather seen the Court actually place an age limit on when a fetus becomes a person. Saying that a fetus is not a person at 39 weeks, 29 days, 23 hours and 59 seconds, but then once it moves two feet out of the birth canal, it's a person? It's medically, scientifically, philosophically, and morally unsustainable, but there it is. I would personally set that date at 20 weeks. But that's my opinion, considering all of the medical, scientific, philosophical and moral evidence I can piece together about how developed a fetus is at 20 weeks. I'd say no abortions after 20 weeks except in cases of incest or to save the life of the mother. Yes, that means if you were raped and you choose to carry the baby for 20 weeks, you can carry the baby for another 20 weeks and then give it up for adoption. Violations of that law are visited on the doctor/hospital, not the mother. No doctor in his right mind would risk his medical practice by knowingly aborting a fetus older than 20 weeks. Does this mean that some women would turn to back-alley abortions? Yep. Does that risk the woman's life? Yep. Does she make that decision willingly? Yep, and if she's not, if she's being coerced into a back-alley abortion, that's a violation of a different law and should be handled accordingly. (EDIT, 25 Jun 2022) This aged well.
