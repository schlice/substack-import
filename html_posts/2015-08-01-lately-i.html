**Content:** [Let\'s play a hypothetical game. Let\'s say you could communicate with a baby in the womb. How would you describe the things it is going to see when it is born? Would you say, \"Hey, baby, there is this stuff called air, and it smells wonderul, except when it doesn\'t, and there are these things called trees, and they\'re fun to climb on, but I know you don\'t understand fun, or climbing, but it\'ll all make sense when you get out here. And don\'t get me started on chili dogs.\"\ \ Could you imagine how difficult it would be to try to explain concepts like this to a baby in the womb? The baby would literally have no understanding of anything you\'re talking about. Similarly, Isaiah 55:9 explains God\'s dilemma when trying to explain concepts like Heaven to us: \"As the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than your thoughts.\" God\'s mind is so, so far greater than ours that we can\'t even begin to hope to understand His ways.\ \ So with that understanding, how can we possibly know what God is thinking? How can we even hope to know what His plans for us are? He is so much higher in thinking than we are that we can\'t even comprehend the plans he has.\ \ Who are we to say that God is unfair? By whose standards? Ours? Are we in charge here? Are we to be so presumptuous that we say, \"God, you are totally unfair, I want you to fix it!\" The thought borders on the blasphemous.\ \ We don\'t run the show, God does. We\'re not in charge here, He is. We can never hope to understand His plans until we see Him face to face and He enlightens us.\ \ The best we can do is to pray for more understanding. It reminds me of when Kennedy quoted Phillips Brooks and he said, \"Don\'t pray for an easy life, pray to be better men.\" Don\'t get angry with God because you don\'t understand His plan; you can never hope to. Pray for more tolerance and understanding of His ways.]{.rendered_qtext}
