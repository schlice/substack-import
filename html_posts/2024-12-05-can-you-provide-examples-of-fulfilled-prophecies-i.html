Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Sure! First, there are two categories of prophecies in the Bible: **fulfilled prophecies** which refer to predictions that have already come to pass, primarily relating to the life and ministry of Jesus, and **yet-to-be-fulfilled prophecies** which are predictions about future events, often focused on the second coming of Christ, the end times, and the establishment of God\'s kingdom on Earth. **Examples of fulfilled prophecies:** - **Messianic prophecies:** Details about Jesus\' birth, life, death, and resurrection, like being born in Bethlehem, entering Jerusalem on a donkey, being betrayed for 30 pieces of silver, and being crucified with pierced hands and feet. - **Prophecies concerning the destruction of Jerusalem:** Predictions about the Roman siege and destruction of Jerusalem in AD 70. - **Prophecies about the rise and fall of empires:** Predictions about the rise and decline of various ancient kingdoms as detailed in the book of Daniel. **Examples of prophecies yet to be fulfilled:** - **The second coming of Christ:** Jesus\' physical return to Earth to establish his kingdom. - **The rapture of the church:** The sudden taking up of believers into heaven before the tribulation - **The great tribulation:** A period of intense suffering and persecution before the second coming - **The millennial reign of Christ:** Jesus ruling on Earth for a thousand years.
