**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it illegal to gift a lottery ticket to a minor? If an adult buys a lottery ticket or scratch-off and gives it to a minor, would that be illegal? The adult buys and redeems the ticket on behalf of the minor.** The answer is that it varies by state. However, **in all cases**, an adult must purchase the tickets. There are no states that allow minors to purchase lottery tickets. For lottery tickets, there are both in-state and multi-state lottery tickets. The state rules for lottery tickets vary (some allow tickets to be gifted to minors, some do not). Many states are very strict about scratch games, with ad campaigns in that state reminding residents that scratch tickets are "not child's play" and that scratch tickets cannot be gifted to minors. The reason why there are such strong restrictions (in most states) about gifting lottery tickets to minors is that there is not insignificant evidence to suggest that kids who received scratch tickets or lottery tickets as gifts tended to gamble earlier in life, which is a possible risk factor for more severe gambling disorders later. > \"We know that playing the lottery at a young age is inappropriate and > can increase the potential for problem gambling later in life,\" said > McGill professor Jeffrey Derevensky, co-director of the International > Centre for Youth Gambling Problems and High-Risk Behaviors at McGill > University. Therefore, while it seems innocuous, research suggests that playing lottery games could potentially lead youth down the path to more severe disorders later in life. To that end, I would suggest a different gift for a child than scratch tickets.
