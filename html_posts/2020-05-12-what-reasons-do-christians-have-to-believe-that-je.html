**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What reasons do Christians have to believe that Jesus is both fully human and fully divine?** The answer about anything that a Christian believes should always be "The Bible" or it's not the right answer. So, to add a finer point to the question: **What reasons \[as recorded in the Bible\] do Christians have to believe that Jesus is both fully human and fully divine?** The Bible teaches that Jesus' divine nature was united with his physical nature - [[1 Timothy 3:16 - NIV](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=46a949&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search%3D1%2BTim%2B3%253A16%26version%3DNIV){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container} - [[Hebrews 2:11-14 - NIV](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=feef83&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search%3DHeb%2B2%253A11-14%26version%3DNIV){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container} The Bible also teaches that the two natures are united in only one divine person: - [[Romans 1:3-4 - NIV](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bf73f3&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search%3DRomans%2B1%253A3-4%26version%3DNIV){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container} - [[Galatians 4:4-5 - NIV](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5a029e&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search%3DGalatians%2B4%253A4-5%26version%3DNIV){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container} - [[Philippians 2:6-11 - NIV](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f43a6b&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search%3DPhil%2B2%253A6-11%26version%3DNIV){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container} > The Gospels portray Jesus as the omnipotent Creator, even while also > admitting that as a man he was limited in power. As God, Jesus was > able to control the behavior of the wind as well as the biting of fish > (Luke 8:23-25; John 21:5-6). As a man, he could do nothing on his own > (John 5:19). As God, his essence was and is unchangeable (Heb. 13:8; > cf. Ps. 102:26-27; Mal. 3:6; James 1:17). As a man, he had to grow and > be made perfect (Heb. 2:10).[\[1\]](#tEnkv){#cite-tEnkv > .citation_link} Some argue that the hypostatic union is a creation of man, or instituted to resolve the differences between the camps that thought Jesus was either fully human or fully divine, or something in between (like when you mix red and orange you get yellow). While the actual hypostatic union is documented well in the Bible, the most theologically and historically sound definition, however, was created at the Council of Chalcedon in 451 AD. It is also where the term "hypostatic union" was coined. > Therefore, following the holy fathers, we all with one accord teach > men to acknowledge one and the same Son, our Lord Jesus Christ, at > once complete in Godhead and complete in manhood, truly God and truly > man, consisting also of a reasonable soul and body; of one substance > with the Father as regards his Godhead, and at the same time of one > substance with us as regards his manhood; like us in all respects, > apart from sin; as regards his Godhead, begotten of the Father before > the ages, but yet as regards his manhood begotten, for us men and for > our salvation, of Mary the Virgin, the God-bearer; one and the same > Christ, Son, Lord, Only-begotten, recognized in two natures, without > confusion, without change, without division, without separation; the > distinction of natures being in no way annulled by the union, but > rather the characteristics of each nature being preserved and coming > together to form one person and subsistence, not as parted or > separated into two persons, but one and the same Son and Only-begotten > God the Word, Lord Jesus Christ; even as the prophets from earliest > times spoke of him, and our Lord Jesus Christ himself taught us, and > the creed of the fathers has handed down to > us.[\[2\]](#zrAZs){#cite-zrAZs .citation_link} In short, Christ was both fully human and fully divine at the same time. The human did not constrain the divine, and the divine did not alter the human.
