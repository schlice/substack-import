--- title: '"Can you resist a citizen arrest?"' date: '2018-07-26' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can you resist a citizen arrest?** Sure you can! You can resist *any* arrest! Although I'd advise you to not resist the arrest of a peace officer. Not only does a peace officer in uniform acting under color of law have a presumption of lawful action, they have *numbers* that you don't have. They can bring overwhelming force in a very short amount of time. In short, you won't win. Regardless of who is making the arrest, what happens later depends entirely on **whether or not the arrest had merit.** *If it did*, then you'd still be arrested for the original charge, and you'd also have a resisting arrest charge added. *If it did not*, you would be filing charges against the person who arrested you for unlawful detention, and if it was a citizen's arrest, you'd be adding assault to that list as well. If they moved you from the location of the incident, you'd also be adding kidnapping.
