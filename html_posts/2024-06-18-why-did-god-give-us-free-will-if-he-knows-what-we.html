Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **Why did God give us free will if he knows what we will choose?** The free will isn't for Him. The free will is for **you*.*** Just because He can see what you chose even though you haven't gotten there yet doesn't mean that the free will itself isn't important. Free will permits you to be accountable for your own decisions > **Will those who make the wrong choice be punished even if they did > not want to make that choice?** This makes no logical sense. If you didn't want to make the choice, then why did you make the choice? You have free will. You could have freely chosen the other choice. Hardly anyone chooses something they did not want to choose. You chose the desire of your heart at the moment of choice. You desired that more than anything else, and that's what you chose. Nearly everyone chooses in this way. Therefore, since you are free to make any choice at the moment of choosing, the choice you make indicates what your greatest desire is at that moment. **TLDR: We are all responsible for the choices we freely make**
