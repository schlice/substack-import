--- title: '"Can a police officer and/or sergeant face charges for writing a police report and lying about the true facts from what witnesses and the victim reported to police?"' date: '2017-09-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Charges? First, the only charge I can think of is "false reporting," and it is unclear whether or not this applies to the police. Second, you're going to have a tough row to hoe to prove that the police knew facts other than what they entered into the report. Third, why would they lie? There's no real reason I can think of that they would want to deliberately falsify a report that they submit. Falsifying a report can only hurt them, as it would be more in violation of department policy than law, which means that they can be administratively punished (or potentially lose their jobs) if the falsification is found to be deliberate for some reason.
