{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are several items that police officers use frequently. Not all devices are used by all departments, but most police departments generally use one form or another of these devices. In almost all cases, officers must be trained and certified on each device they carry on their duty belts in order to lawfully carry them. **TASER** :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-f25631b8459cff86a6993d3e7e5574e1){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-f25631b8459cff86a6993d3e7e5574e1" master_w="600" master_h="400"} ::: :::: The [[TASER](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7af561&url=https://www.axon.com/products/taser-x26p){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="axon.com"}]{.qlink_container} is probably the tool you hear about most frequently these days. It is in a class of devices known as a "pain compliance device" even though beyond the initial pain, it really functions by disrupting nerve signals between the spinal cord and the muscles, essentially "overloading" the nerves with electrical impulses, and rendering the person incapable of moving. TASER is an acronym invented by the device's inventor and his favorite comic book character, Tom Swift. TASER stands for "Thomas A. Swift's Electronic Rifle." **PEPPER SPRAY** :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-ef94fce7c422f5e7cdef674a30980708){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-ef94fce7c422f5e7cdef674a30980708" master_w="602" master_h="732"} ::: :::: [[Pepper spray](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5b3bfd&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pepper_spray){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} is basically the concentrated oils from hot peppers. In chemical terms, it is called ***oleoresin capsicum*** or OC for short. It causes inflammation of mucus membranes in the eyes, nose, and sinus cavities, producing pain and mucus discharge, and rendering an attacker incapacitated for a short time. **BATON** :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-0cc2b3ab791dc21f92f7eb91cd060ce6){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-0cc2b3ab791dc21f92f7eb91cd060ce6" master_w="400" master_h="172"} ::: :::: The baton is an impact weapon which can be used for striking an attacker, as well as in combination with holds to incapacitate an attacker. It is collapsible, which makes it more convenient to carry. Most commonly, the baton comes in 16″ and 21″ variants, with a rubberized or padded handle and steel construction. Batons can also be equipped with a pointed end-cap which can be used to break glass. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-3b6410130aea46f025bd14c418e84de7){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-3b6410130aea46f025bd14c418e84de7" master_w="184" master_h="273"} ::: :::: Without the end cap, officers will usually break glass by striking it with the business end of the extended baton.
