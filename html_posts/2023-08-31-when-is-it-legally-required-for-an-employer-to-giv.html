--- title: "When is it legally required for an employer to give notice of firing before actually terminating someone" date: "2023-08-31" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You said "employment contract." That's a very specific legal term. That term means the employer and the employee have signed a contract specifying a length of time of employment. Employment contracts will have specific termination requirements, or they may have none at all, depending on the character of the termination. The requirement for giving notice will be spelled out in the employment contract depending on the character of the termination. In other words, if it is a termination "with cause" (because you broke a term of the contract), it will have one specific set of rules regarding notice, and if it is a termination "without cause" (when you didn't break a term of the contract), it will have another specific set of rules.
