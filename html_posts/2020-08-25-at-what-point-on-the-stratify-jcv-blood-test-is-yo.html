**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **At what point on the Stratify JCV blood test is your level too high to start Tysabri?** The answer is that there is no specific point at which Tysabri is contraindicated. The JCV test simply determines whether or not the patient has or has not been exposed to the JC Virus. At readings of \<0.20, the test is Negative. At readings of 0.20-0.40, the test is Indeterminate. And at readings of \>0.40, the test is Positive. Your neurologist will balance this finding with the benefits of Tysabri, understanding that it carries a risk of PML. Also understand that for those who are JC positive, the risk of PML increases over time taking Tysabri; it is not static. I know of plenty of people who were JC positive who took Tysabri. They had to get off of it sooner than those who were JC negative. Ultimately, you and your neurologist must weigh the risk of Tysabri versus the reward: the medicine with the best efficacy of any MS therapy drug today, period. It is well-tolerated, and only needs to be infused once every 28 days, so it is also convenient. Everyone's situation is different. Some people don't have any other good choices because other medicines may be contraindicated. Taking Tysabri while JC positive is not impossible. It simply must be done very carefully and fully understanding the risks.
