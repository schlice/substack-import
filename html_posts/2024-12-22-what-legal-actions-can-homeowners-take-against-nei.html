--- title: "What legal actions can homeowners take against neighbors who discharge firearms near residential areas?" date: "2024-12-22" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The correct answer, as with all legal questions, is "it depends." If you live in a city or a county, there are usually laws which prohibit the discharge of firearms within that city limits. So it doesn't matter what you're shooting at, generally discharging a firearm is a crime, usually a misdemeanor. This is purely a safety thing, because people live in such close proximity that you can't have people randomly shooting firearms, because even if you're not aiming at someone, the population density simply means that you could *potentially* hit someone. If you live in unincorporated land, or in rural areas, there are usually no laws against discharging firearms. So long as someone is not shooting *at you*, then there really isn't much you can do about the discharge of firearms. If they are doing it late at night, there are usually still noise ordinances, so perhaps law enforcement could still get involved. So check your local laws and see if there are any provisions against discharge of firearms. If there are, contact your local law enforcement and let them know about the neighbors who are unlawfully discharging firearms. But again, be sure it is illegal where you live before you contact the police.
