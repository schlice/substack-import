**Content:** [The headphones that are included with your phone are trying to strike a balance between \"decent earbuds\" and \"the least expensive option we can bundle in with the phone.\"\ \ These particular headphones use a noise reduction technique called \"noise-isolation,\" as opposed to the more sophisticated \"noise-cancelling\" method.\ \ Noise-isolating headphones aim to block the entire ear canal and fill it with rubber. Your headphones pictured use silicone rubber earpieces to close out the entire ear canal to isolate the ear canal. That way, there isn\'t any foamy earpad that allows sound to get in, and you get a good fit with the rubber buds fully enclosing the ear canal. These work best when pressed into the outer part of the ear canal.\ \ Noise-cancelling headphones are different. They use external microphones that listen to waveforms that exist outside the headphones, and they broadcast an equal but opposite waveform into the audio path, which effectively cancels out the external noise. Noise-cancelling earbuds are identified by external battery packs, an on-off switch, and usually an indicator light of some sort, as well as microphones near the buds themselves (to listen to the outside noise). Prices vary depending on additional features. They still use the small rubber earpieces which sit just inside the ear canal, so they still are noise-isolating, but these are far less likely to fall out of your ears. As a matter of fact, once you seat these, really the only way to get them off is to take them off.
