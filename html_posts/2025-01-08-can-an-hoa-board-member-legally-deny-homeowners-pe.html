--- title: "Can an HOA board member legally deny homeowners permission to sell their property if they owe money or have violations?" date: "2025-01-08" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In concept, yes. Not in actual practice. An HOA board member, acting alone, cannot deny a homeowner permission to sell their property. However, the HOA (as an entity) will report to title company whether or not the seller is "in good standing" with the HOA (i.e. their account is current and there are no past due balances on the account). In many states, you cannot complete the sale of a house if the owner is in arrears with the HOA. Please note that this varies by state. In many states, you cannot process the sale of your home until the HOA reports that your account is settled, but in nearly all cases, the HOA will provide a Statement of Account to the title company which clearly shows that the HOA is due money to be paid out of the proceeds of the sale. The title company will ensure that the account is returned to good standing before any proceeds are paid to the seller.
