**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If Donald Trump is guilty of obstructing justice, what are the chances that VP Pence and Speaker Ryan were not complicit? If Trump is impeached, is it likely that Pence and Ryan can be immediately impeached as well?** What's with the multiple questions? Let's start with the first one because that leads to the second. **If Donald Trump is guilty of obstructing justice, what are the chances that VP Pence and Speaker Ryan were not complicit?** Donald Trump is not guilty of obstructing justice. Trump was exercising his power as the head of the Executive Branch under Article II. *This action cannot be illegal*, because it is a power directly granted to the President in the Constitution. If that action violates a law, then the law is unconstitutional. The Constitution is not at fault, the law is. So, was the President guilty of the crime of obstructing justice by firing Comey? In short, **no**, he was not. Did Trump fire Comey to stop the investigation? Also, **no** he did not. You do know that there is more than one person that works at the FBI, right? If Trump wanted to stop the investigation, as the person in charge of the Executive Branch, *he could have directed the FBI to stop the investigation.* But he did not do this. If he did, it would have been seen as highly suspect, and perhaps then, he could have been impeached on an abuse of power charge. As it stands though, there still isn't an impeachable offense here, and his actions so far don't amount to anything other than discharging his duties under the Constitution. **If Trump is impeached, is it likely that Pence and Ryan can be immediately impeached as well?** No, cases would have to be brought against each individual for individual contributions.
