Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Can* you carry a gun like that? Sure you can. All you have to do is stick it there, and you can carry it. It may not be lawful in your jurisdiction to carry a firearm in this manner, but not every jurisdiction has carry laws dictating how you must carry a firearm. *Should* you do this? **Absolutely not**. Modern firearms are designed to only discharge when the trigger is pulled. Therefore, to prevent accidental discharge, you must protect the trigger while the firearm is being carried. ***This means you have to carry that firearm in a holster.*** This is the only way to safely carry a loaded firearm, if it's not in your hand under positive control. If you fail to do this, you risk accidental discharge. Here's just a few stories of recent self-inflicted gunshot wounds caused by improper carry of a firearm: [[Police: Man mishandles handgun, accidentally shoots himself in leg at Colorado Springs 7-Eleven](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=33c9fd&url=http://gazette.com/police-man-mishandles-handgun-accidentally-shoots-himself-in-leg-at-colorado-springs-7-eleven/article/1592584){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="gazette.com"}]{.qlink_container} [[Man accidentally shoots himself in Colo. Springs gas station restroom](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cea4b0&url=http://www.kjct8.com/content/news/381297021.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="kjct8.com"}]{.qlink_container} [[Police: man accidentally shot self in leg after crash](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bcc5c5&url=http://gazette.com/police-man-accidentally-shot-self-in-leg-after-crash/article/1561568){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="gazette.com"}]{.qlink_container} There are various scenarios here, but the root cause of all of these accidental shootings is improper carry of a firearm. The moral of the story: never believe anything you see on television, Don't tuck a firearm into your waistband, ever. Always carry a firearm in a holster. If you want to tuck the firearm inside your waistband, you need an "inside-the-waistband" holster (otherwise known as an IWB holster). Don't be lazy and stick it in a pocket or just shove it in your pants. It's not like cell phones which went from waistband holsters to pockets because it's cool. It's definitely not cool.
