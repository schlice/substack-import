**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In San Francisco, California, when you are an hourly employee and you are off work, is it legal for your employer to send you text messages and ask you work related questions? If not, how do you report them?** In any state in the country, the Fair Labor Standards Act prevents employees from working off the clock. It is illegal for your employer to make you work (and work is anything you do for the company, including text messages) when you're not on the clock. Some of the suggestions mentioned here are good ones, such as when your employer sends you a text message, responding immediately back to your employer with, "I'm on the clock, right?" If it's email, set an OOO every time you clock out that says that you are off work and you will respond once you're back on work. Remember that you can file with the US Department of Labor to recoup all the back pay for all of the hours your employer made you work off the clock. If it is significant, I would definitely file to get that money back.
