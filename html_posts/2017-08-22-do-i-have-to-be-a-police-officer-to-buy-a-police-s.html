--- title: "Do I have to be a police officer to buy a police shirt? I want to buy this shirt off of a uniform site for a costume (not a police costume) and I wanted to make sure it was okay for me to buy it." date: "2017-08-22" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You may absolutely wear a police uniform and dress like a police officer. You can buy police uniform shirts. There are some items that are sold online which require your identification as an active police officer. In that case, you need to provide the company documentation on department letterhead indicating that you are an active officer in good standing in their department before you can purchase any of those restricted items. Basically, if you can't buy it, the company will tell you. They won't sell you something you're not allowed to buy. That said, you may not act in a way such as to make people think you are a police officer, and you may not attempt to exercise any police authority. I do like [[Dave Spinks](https://www.quora.com/profile/Dave-Spinks-2)]{.qlink_container}' idea of adding a comedy element to your costume, such as a banana in the holster, a paper badge, or something similar, so as to avoid any confusion. That said, in most jurisdictions, it is not required that you distinguish your uniform appreciably from a police uniform; it is your actions that determine whether or not you can be charged with impersonating a peace officer. As always, check your local laws, YMMV, and I am not a lawyer. (Interestingly, we're getting a 5.11 retail store in northern Colorado Springs, and I'm totally stoked)
