**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do Protestant priests bless people like a Catholic Priest will?** First of all, Protestants in general do not have "priests." They follow the Bible much more closely in this regard, and they have positions as you'd find in 1 Timothy, such as "elders" and "deacons." Deacons in Protestant churches would be closer to Pastors today. Catholics have the Magisterium, which includes priests, bishops, cardinals, and the Pope. Anyway, more accurately asked, **do Protestant pastors bless people?** Kinda. Protestant pastors follow the traditions in the Bible and will perform the "[[laying on of hands](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6fb1a7&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_laying_on_of_hands){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}" (Acts 6:5--6) in order to bless people who are leaving on missions, or who are undertaking some other spiritual calling. The laying on of hands also tends to be a more community-oriented function; Protestants tend to do this as a community rather than by themselves. They certainly don't bless people with the regularity that Catholic priests would bless people.
