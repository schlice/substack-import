--- title: "Who is responsible if my friend gets into an accident with my uninsured car?" date: "2019-03-21" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Who is responsible if my friend gets into an accident with my uninsured car?** You are. All of it. You're responsible for any damage your car caused to anything or anyone else, and you're responsible for paying for your friend's injuries. Of course, your car is damaged, and you'll probably have to get that fixed (which you'll have to pay for as well). You are 100% responsible for all of those costs, because you own the car. Oh, and by the way, you'll also be responsible for the fines you'll face for having an uninsured vehicle on the road, which violates state law in nearly every state. At a minimum, you are required to carry liability insurance for injuries and property damage. Insurance which covers the actual car itself is not usually required. In some states, you can pay a fee instead of carrying insurance. And in states like Mississippi and New Hampshire, you can put down a cash bond in lieu of carrying liability insurance. In all other states, liability insurance is mandatory. If you think your friend should be responsible for some of those costs, you'll have to file a lawsuit to recoup your costs. For example, if your friend was driving recklessly, you'll have to sue him to get your money back, after you pay for all the damage he caused.
