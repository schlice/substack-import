Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the Department of Justice have the power to remove Trump by invoking the 25th Amendment?** No. The Department of Justice works for the President. They do not have any authority under the 25th Amendment. The entire purpose of the 25th Amendment is to define how the Vice President takes control from the President. It allows the Vice President to *assume* the authority of the President when he is incapacitated to such a degree that he cannot *cede* control to someone else, and it allows the President to *cede* authority to the Vice President if he cannot fulfill his duty. That's why there is all of that complex language in Section 4 about the President telling Congress that he *is* actually fit to resume duty, and there being Congressional hearings to determine if that is the case. The DOJ, which works for the President, is not involved in the process.
