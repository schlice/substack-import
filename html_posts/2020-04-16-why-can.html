**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why can\'t President Trump impose a national lockdown like India?** Wow, a lot of answers, a lot of speculation, a healthy dose of TDS (which always appears when Trump's name is in a question) and still no one has actually touched on the right answer: **The President can only do what the Constitution allows him to do.** There is no power granted to the Executive branch which allows the President to issue an order that all states must follow. Period. The President could declare a "national emergency," which he has done before, but all this does is activate certain provisions of other Federal laws. Unless there is a Federal law which allows the President to issue binding orders to the states in cases of national emergencies, and I'm not aware of one if it does exist, the the President simply doesn't have the authority to issue a "national lockdown." The Constitution exists to define and **limit** the power of the Federal government. Any power or authority not expressly granted to the Federal government belongs to the states, and to the people, respectively. Simply put: **the President can't impose a national lockdown because the Constitution hasn't authorized him to do it.**
