**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In addition to the other good answers here, the TOS likely also says that Microsoft can change your gamer tag at their sole discretion at any time. Secondly, you would have to show some harm was caused by Microsoft changing your gamer tag. For example, if you\'re losing money because your gamer tag was changed, you can challenge their ruling. The TOS probably specifies that you must use binding arbitration, not the courts, for such actions (most TOS\'s do; I don\'t know for Microsoft\'s TOS, you will have to research it). Good luck.
