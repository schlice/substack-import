**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} 1. Today's Democrats support measures which **feel** good. Today's Republicans support measures which **do** good. Any Democrat who is seen as doing something which "feels" good or "feels" right is going to get votes. Republicans don't support such measures because they don't **do** any good. They don't make people safer. They don't reduce crime. They don't keep weapons out of the hands of criminals. They only restrict the rights of law-abiding citizens. 2. There's a great deal of confusion (which they actually caused) around "assault weapons." They have banned "scary-looking" guns and their attachments, which have absolutely zero bearing on their function. Democrats are attempting to sow that confusion, and then capitalize on it. If they can get people confused enough about what an assault weapon is, they can gin up enough support to sneak some bans through. So while they can't wholesale ban firearms (which is actually their ultimate goal), they can attempt to chip away at gun rights until there are simply so many regulations that they are functionally banned. This is why the NRA and their Institute for Legislative Action as well as many other grassroots organizations must keep fighting. If they back off, Democrats will be able to get their foot in the door and get nonsense legislation passed, which is the beginning of the end for the right. They know they've lost the battle with handguns, so they have switched to assault weapons, attempting to capitalize on that confusion.
