**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My lease states \"The landlord may enter the apartment to provide services, inspect, repair, improve or show it.\" Is that technically saying they can enter whenever they want without prior notice?** There is generally more in the lease than just that. Generally, the lease document will go on to describe when and how the landlord gains entrance into the apartment, as in something like *the landlord will contact the tenant and provide a minimum of 24 hours notice of entry for any purpose* or wording like that. Keep reading your lease and see if you can find the language which discusses when they will enter. See if there's a tenant's rights group in your area or legal aid which may be able to help you review the lease. If worse comes to worse, talk to your landlord. They're usually not jerks (though sometimes, everyone can be a jerk. Just ask my wife...). Tell them that you have no problem if they come in whenever they need to in order to do all of those things, but you'd like just a little bit of advance notice and can they do that. I suspect they'll tell you their current procedure and maybe can work with you to ensure that you get advance notice before they come in.
