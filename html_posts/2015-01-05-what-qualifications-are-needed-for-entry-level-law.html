**Content:** [In addition to [[Rick Bruno](https://www.quora.com/profile/Rick-Bruno-1)]{.qlink_container}\'s answer, our department requires an excellent driving record, a full medical exam, and in place of the polygraph, we use a [[Computer Voice Stress Analysis](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5a56b9&url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice_stress_analysis){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} (CVSA) as part of the background check. But it\'s essentially the same for most departments:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - Colorado has a state-authored peace officer entrance exam, which all peace officer candidates must pass. It includes math and verbal skills, along with some logic and reasoning. It requires no prior police training to pass. - Candidates then move on to Oral Boards. These boards are conducted by the individual departments. The board members are looking for leadership ability, ability to speak in stressful situations, and problem-solving skills (part of Oral Boards is individual, and part is group. In the group evaluation, they are looking for someone to take charge of the group, direct the discussion, and deliver the verdict intelligently). - If the candidate passes both written and oral exams, the application process starts, which includes all the items Rick already mentioned, along with the medical, physical, psych, and CVSA testing.
