**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, every state is different, and the specifics about what is covered under each state's specific driver's licenses varies from state to state. However, most non-commercial driver's licenses are valid for passenger vehicles up to 15 passengers (including the driver) with Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) of 26,000 pounds (11,793 kg) or less, which would include most cars, trucks, and RVs, as well as two or three-wheeled motor vehicles with smaller than 50cc engines (like mopeds or small scooters). If you want to drive anything larger, or anything that holds more than 15 passengers, you will need an *endorsement* on your license. This usually involves a separate test (possibly a driving test as well), and a separate fee for each endorsement. \ Original question: What vehicles are Americans allowed to drive with their car licence?
