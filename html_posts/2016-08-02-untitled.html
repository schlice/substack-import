**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Detention legally occurs when the person being questioned "feels like" they can't leave. No specific words have to be said. You simply must "feel like" you can't leave and that counts as detention. If you are ordered to stay, you are being detained. You must be read your rights before any statements you make are legally admissible in court. So if the officer tells you to stay and then questions you, feel free to tell him you killed your girlfriend, because it's not admissible if he didn't read you your rights. But how often are you killing your girlfriend? And how often are you told to stay and then asked questions which might implicate you later on? Yeah, hardly ever. You're more likely to be bitten by a shark, twice, than have that happen to you.
