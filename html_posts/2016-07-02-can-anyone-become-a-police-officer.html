--- title: '"Can anyone become a police officer?"' date: '2016-07-02' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A2A Pretty much anyone can join the department beyond the classes you noted. If you've ever been convicted of a felony, you're out. If you're not a US citizen, you're out. But other than that, if you're over 18 and are a high school graduate or have your GED, you can apply. In most cases you must also possess a driver's license and you must also have an almost clean driving record. All department hiring practices are different, but most require you to have certain physical skills (be able to lift a minimum amount of weight, have a minimum level of aerobic fitness, etc), and pass numerous other physical, psychological, and educational tests. In our department, you start with a written test. You then move on to oral boards, where you make a few extemporaneous presentations in front of command staff. If you pass those two, you move into background checks, physical tests, psychological tests (both paper and interview), and a CVSA (Computer Voice Stress Analysis, similar to a polygraph). If you pass all of these, you are offered a slot in the upcoming class. State law requires a complete application from soup to nuts for each class. So when the department decides to do a class, it opens up testing about four months before the class is scheduled to start. By the time the class is set to begin, they have developed a class list for that class.
