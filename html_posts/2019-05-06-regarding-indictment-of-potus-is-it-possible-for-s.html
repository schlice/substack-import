**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Regarding indictment of POTUS, is it possible for SCOTUS to rule that it\'s possible to indict a sitting President AND that the statute of limitations is tolled for the length of the presidency, so long as the DOJ waives its right to indict?** No, these are not both simultaneously possible. If SCOTUS rules that it is possible to indict a sitting president, then the statute of limitations cannot be tolled, because the president can be indicted. If SCOTUS rules that it is **not** possible to indict a sitting president, then the statute of limitations *will be* tolled, since indictment is not possible. A SCOTUS decision would not be made based on implied or promised conduct by the Department of Justice, which works for the President. A SCOTUS decision is made based on interpretation of the Constitution. SCOTUS would only rule on whether or not you can indict a sitting president. And they would only do so if the President is indicted, and his attorneys appeal the decision on the grounds that you cannot Constitutionally indict the President.
