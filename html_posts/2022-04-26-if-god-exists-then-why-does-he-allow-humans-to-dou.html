--- title: '"If God exists then why does he allow humans to doubt Him?"' date: '2022-04-26' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because God has given us the greatest gift of all (besides his son, which was a pretty great gift): *free will*. Free will is that ability to question everything, even the existence of God. The ability to make choices, *even the ability to reject truth*, is what makes us moral creatures, and allows us to choose our own destiny. God has given us the choice to either accept or reject him. And in order to make sure that our choice is **truly free**, he has placed us in a world filled with evidence of his existence, *but without his direct presence*, which would be so overwhelming and powerful as to override our free will and force us to believe in him. In other words, *God has given us enough evidence to convince anyone willing to believe, but has left enough ambiguity so as to not compel the unwilling.* In this way, God gives us the choice to love him or reject him, without violating our free will. Because if we would truly love him, that love must be freely given; it cannot be coerced. **TL;DR: If God didn't allow us to doubt him, we wouldn't truly have free will.** \ Original question: If God exists then why does he allow humans to doubt Him?
