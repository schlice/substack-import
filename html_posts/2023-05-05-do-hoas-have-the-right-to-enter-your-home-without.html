--- title: "Do HOAs have the right to enter your home without a warrant?" date: "2023-05-05" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Most likely no. While it is true that most covenants give HOA Board members and Officers the authority to enter *property* when it is necessary to protect the community or neighboring property, in the vast majority of cases the *inside of the home* is off-limits. We would frequently enter property when there was something like a sprinkler system failure and water was spraying everywhere and running into neighbors' yards; we'd be able to get to the shutoff vale to stop the chaos until the homeowner returned home and was able to fix the broken pipe. Presumably, there *could* be a clause in the Governing Documents that allowed an HOA Board member or Officer to enter a home in the event of exigent circumstances which might lead to damage of neighboring property, but I have never heard of it and I highly doubt any HOAs have it. This could be something I could see happening in a Condo Association, but in a single-family HOA, unlikely.
