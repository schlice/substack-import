--- title: "Do police officers ever struggle with becoming bitter towards members of the public?" date: "2015-09-14" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Some officers do struggle with this. Can you blame them? They deal with the worst of the worst in society, every single day, ***so you don\'t have to.*** They do this willingly. Most of these people are harmless, but some are hardened criminals who would just as soon kill a cop than go back to jail (again). Statistics vary as to just how many officers are affected in this way, as medical records are confidential, and most offers are less-than-willing to talk about such shortcomings with anyone, much less statistic-takers.\ \ There are programs that officers can avail themselves of to assist them with dealing with these types of emotional issues. Our department has an on-staff psychiatrist (who is an awesome lady, by the way) who handles all the \"required\" interactions, such as use of force incidents where a suspect dies, and the \"non-required\" interactions, where officers can talk to her when they start feeling the stress and strain of the job. There are other work/life programs that are also offered by the department.]{.rendered_qtext}
