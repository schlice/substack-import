--- title: '"How do you identify someone with a hidden disability?"' date: '2016-06-06' tags: - veteran-benefits layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They tell you "I have a hidden disability." Other than that, the point is that the disability is "hidden," which means it doesn't produce any visible symptoms. Which generally produces the response, "Wow, ***but you look so good..."*** The dichotomy that then goes on in the mind of such a disabled person is, "It's strange that I **look so good on the outside,** when I **feel so bad on the inside.**" The short answer is that you cannot identify someone with a hidden disability unless they tell you.
