Read up on [[asymmetric warfare](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3c3bcc&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asymmetric_warfare){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. Tell the goat herders in Afghanistan that they don't stand a chance against the US military, while they're systematically killing our troops with nothing more than old unexploded ordnance and a burner cell phone. 2. We may not *win* against the US military...wait, I need to re-think this. We have somewhere north of **100 million** gun owners in the United States. Our military [[stands at](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=84ccf5&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces%23Personnel){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} approximately 1.3 million, with another 800,000 in reserves, bringing the total to just about 2 million. Therefore, as a fighting force, we outnumber the US military 50 to 1. And there's a good chance that a majority of those military members will never turn their backs on the Constitution and will never turn their guns on US citizens. 3. If you're reading bullet 2 and thinking, "Yeah, but the US military is the best in the world, and we're just a bunch of rank amateurs," you should read up on another war where a bunch of rank amateurs defeated the best military in the world. The Revolutionary War. We did it once... 4. But even if we didn't "win," that win would be **very costly** for the US military, in terms of dollars and lives, rivaling the last Civil War. There won't be much of a country to lord over once this war is done.
