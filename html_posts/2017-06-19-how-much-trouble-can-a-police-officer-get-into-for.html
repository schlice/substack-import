Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Pulling anyone over when you don't have reasonable suspicion that they have violated a law is in itself a violation of department policy. There is no police department I am aware of that allows you to stop a vehicle without at a minimum reasonable suspicion that they have committed a violation. How much trouble can an officer get into? He does have the authority to stop cars, so what he did was not criminal. But he almost certainly violated department policy, which could get him reprimanded, suspended, or at worst, fired.
