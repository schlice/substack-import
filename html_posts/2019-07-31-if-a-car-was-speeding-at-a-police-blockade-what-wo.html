**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} While I agree with [[Brian Parramore](https://www.quora.com/profile/Brian-Parramore)]{.qlink_container} that any centerfire rifle fired multiple times at the radiator will disable the vehicle, the question was asked about which gun would be the ***best*** gun to slow it down or stop it. And the ***best*** gun the police have access to would be this: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-48735fbf68ee465f65df45a888458f4d){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-48735fbf68ee465f65df45a888458f4d" master_w="1000" master_h="1000"} ::: :::: The Barrett M107. This behemoth fires the similarly behemoth 50BMG round: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6ceefcd6b4812f2059f716914f98b9b7){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6ceefcd6b4812f2059f716914f98b9b7" master_w="450" master_h="430"} ::: :::: (Photo courtesy [[Wikimedia](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=767e93&url=https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Rifle_cartridge_comparison.jpg){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikimedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, public domain) The bullet on the far right is a .22 Long Rifle (LR). The next bullet to the left is the 5.56 NATO, which is fired by most centerfire rifles such as the M4 or the M-16. And all the way on the far left of the photo is the .50 BMG, fired by the Barrett. The 5.56 travels at a maximum of around 3,100 ft/sec and imparts around 1,371 ft/lbs of force. The .50 BMG, on the other hand, at its heaviest 800 grain Barnes configuration, flies at a relatively slow 2,895 ft/sec, but imparts a *whopping* 14,895 ft/lbs of force on target. So will a 5.56 do the job? Yes, probably with more than one shot, but it will stop the car. But will a Barrett do the job better? In all respects, yes.
