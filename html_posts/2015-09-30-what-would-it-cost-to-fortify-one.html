**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} When my dad was a cop, they raided a drug dealer\'s home. This was during the early 80s. This drug dealer had fortified his home so no one unauthorized could make it in. First, he had fenced in the yard and filled it with attack dogs. He then constructed a concrete \"man-trap\" inside the front door and replaced his entry doors with steel security doors. Within the man-trap was a small teller window from which they could transact their business. To conduct the raid, first an officer in plain running clothes jogged by the house and tossed raw chickens stuffed with sleeping pills over the fence. Within 20 minutes, the dogs were neutralized as a threat. Then, the main raid force moved in. They took the large department tow truck (which can tow tractor-trailers and Sherman tanks) and drove it through the chain-link fence and through the picture window (essentially, they drove it through the front of the house). The security thug in the living room literally pooed himself when the truck came in. The fenders around the front tires of the tow were diamond-pattern stainless steel, and they were stair-stepped. The raiding group simply climbed up the fenders and into the house. I tell you this story because if the police want into your house and they have a warrant (even a \"no-knock\" warrant like this one), they will get in. And if they have a warrant, then a judge has signed off on the evidence presented and agreed that illegal drug activity was indeed taking place inside that home. Remember that the evidence has to be really solid before a judge is going to sign off on a \"no-knock\" warrant, and there has to be real, conclusive evidence that knocking on the door and announcing the warrant will result in lives lost and evidence destroyed. These warrants are not issued lightly. What I\'m trying to tell you is that the only people who need to protect themselves against no-knock warrants are criminals. So to clarify what I said before and to answer the specific question: *How much would it cost to fortify your home against a no-knock warrant?* It can't be done. If the police want to get in, they will. There is no amount of money that will keep the police out if they choose to get in.
