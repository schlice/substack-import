**Content:** [The Independent Payment Advisory Panel (or Independent Payment Advisory Board, or IPAB) is an independent panel which is responsible for keeping Medicare costs down. Sounds innocent enough. The rub is how they plan on doing so.\ \ The former chief of Medicare [[has said](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=df8c6b&url=http://dailycaller.com/2010/05/27/death-panels-were-an-overblown-claim-until-now/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="dailycaller.com"}]{.qlink_container} that it\'s cheaper to let people die (or in more technical terms, to simply provide palliative care) than to treat them with traditional treatments. Obamacare therefore invented this board, which is truly independent. No Congressional oversight, and no input from doctors either. This board essentially gets to make the decisions as to what doctors you can see, what treatments you can get, and whether or not you can get care at all.\ \ Sounds like a death panel to me. If it walks like a duck\...\ \ [[Forbes](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=378bfa&url=http://www.forbes.com/sites/sallypipes/2015/06/15/two-more-pieces-of-obamacare-may-be-on-their-way-out/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="forbes.com"}]{.qlink_container} says, \"If Medicare spending exceeds limits set by law, then IPAB can impose its own set of cost controls\... Once IPAB settles on its cuts, lawmakers must either offer an alternative plan that cuts the same level of spending or muster a super-majority to block the Board\'s cuts from taking effect.\" IPAB independently decides what care to deliver, and unless Congress can muster a super-majority (ha ha), it\'s unlikely that anyone can stop it.\ \ Both sides of the aisle are pushing to abolish IPAB. Many groups which are in favor of the ACA are pushing for IPAB\'s disbandment. In committee alone, the repeal motion had [[20 Democratic co-sponsors.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e1d75b&url=http://thehill.com/policy/healthcare/245109-house-gops-focus-on-obamacare-excludes-court-case){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="thehill.com"}]{.qlink_container}\ \ As the opposition to this law pointed out before its inception, the cost to take care of sick people, which is the bulk of people using the coverage, is too high for the program to bear. Already, insurance providers are pushing for rate hikes in the state Exchanges, in some states as high as 51%. [[Only Maine](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b65418&url=http://www.wsj.com/articles/health-insurers-seek-hefty-rate-boosts-1432244042){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wsj.com"}]{.qlink_container} has not sought to increase rates. Wonder what they\'re doing right up there\...]{.rendered_qtext}
