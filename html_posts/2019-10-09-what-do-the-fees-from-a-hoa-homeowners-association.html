--- title: "What do the fees from a HOA homeowners association fee really cover? Are they worth it?" date: "2019-10-09" tags: ["law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The last HOA I ran had far fewer amenities. We didn't have a park, a pool, or a playground. But we still had plenty of fees to cover: - Water for the common areas, and landscaping for the same. This was our biggest expense; water in Colorado Springs is not cheap. - Snow removal on the walking paths and common area sidewalks - We contract for neighborhood trash service; it saves money and adds convenience for homeowners. - General liability insurance on the common areas - Administrative fees paid to our managing agent (the lowest expense we had). - Legal fees paid to our attorney. And that's about it. We have money available for common area wall repair, fence repair, and other maintenance and upkeep. And maybe an end of summer barbecue in one of the cul-de-sacs.
