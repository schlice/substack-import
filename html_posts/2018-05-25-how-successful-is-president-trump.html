**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How successful is President Trump\'s Tax Cut bill given that most companies are spending more in buying back stocks than in investing in capital or labor?** I'm not sure you and I define *success* in the same way. The tax plan has arguably put money back in the pockets of every working-class American in this country. By lowering the Federal income tax, companies withhold less from your paycheck, which means more money in your pocket. This also means more money for companies to use (since their effective tax rates are higher than the individual worker). They can do lots of things with that money, including returning some of it directly to employees in the form of bonuses or pay increases, capital investments, R&D investments, business expansion, or simply strengthening the bottom line of the business by reducing debt by buying back stocks. The simple fact is that money is *moving*. Under the Obama-era economic uncertainty, businesses didn't invest. They just held on to their capital reserves. They knew they had improvements to make, but with such a volatile and uncertain market, no one wanted to spend the money. The market loves certainty. And under Trump's tax bill. it is certain that the economy will grow. And when companies see positive movement in the economy, they loosen the reins on their capital reserves, and they start spending. The more companies spend, the better the health of the economy. **TL;DR: Stipulating that companies must spend on labor or capital improvements is too narrow a measurement to determine whether or not the tax plan is successful.**
