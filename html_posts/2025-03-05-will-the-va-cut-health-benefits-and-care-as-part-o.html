--- title: "Will the VA cut health benefits and care as part of its plan to cut 80,000 jobs?" date: "2025-03-05" tags: ["veteran-benefits"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **No. Existing benefits will not be affected. No veterans will lose benefits due to these cuts.** Trump cut 1400 jobs, out of almost 500,000 employees at the VA. The 1400 who were cut by Trump were provisional employees who had been there less than two years. The announcement today of 80,000 employees to be cut was made by the VA, not Trump or Musk. These cuts will return the VA to its 2019 staffing levels of about 400,000 employees. These 80,000 employees were brought onboard during the Biden administration, part of which were added under the 2022 PACT Act, which expanded VA coverage to veterans who were disabled due to exposure to toxic burn pits. The VA Chief of Staff, Christopher Syrek, sent out a memo this morning instructing top-level staff to prepare for an agency-wide reorganization in August to "resize and tailor the workforce to the mission and revised structure." It also calls for agency officials to work with the White House's Department of Government Efficiency to "move out aggressively, while taking a pragmatic and disciplined approach" to the Trump administration's goals. It is estimated that roughly 3.5 million veterans are now covered after the PACT Act made exposure to toxic chemicals from burn pits, Agent Orange, and other chemicals "presumptive service-connected." This makes it easier for veterans who were exposed to such chemicals and who now suffer medical conditions due to that exposure to get compensation and care.
