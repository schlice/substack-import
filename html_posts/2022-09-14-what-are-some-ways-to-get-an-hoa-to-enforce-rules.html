Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: you don't. The HOA is free to enforce some rules and not enforce others. So long as they enforce equally, they can *not* enforce a particular rule and that's perfectly legal. Most Covenants contain a statement that says that the Association ***may*** enforce the provisions of the covenants. That word "may" is a legal term which does not require action. That word would be "shall." The word "may" means that the Board has enforcement discretion. Check your Governing Documents to see what your Board needs to do in any one case. Our docs also state that an owner may "bring an action for specific performance, declaratory decree or injunction." This means that you can sue the Board in order to force them to enforce the provisions. So the only way you can "force" the Board to enforce the rules is to sue the Board. Otherwise, in most cases, the Board is under no obligation to enforce any one specific rule. However, this is all dependent solely on what your HOA's Governing Documents say, as well as prevailing state law, so you must always check those to see what your Board must do and what they are not legally compelled to do. \ Original question: What are some ways to get an HOA to enforce rules about noisy dogs in the neighborhood?
