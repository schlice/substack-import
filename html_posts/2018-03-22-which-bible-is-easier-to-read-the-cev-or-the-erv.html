**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Which Bible is easier to read, the CEV or the ERV?** CEV. The Contemporary English Version (CEV) was designed to allow for anyone who reads contemporary English to read it and understand it. It was based on studies into how people read and understand English, and the translation was done with that understanding. It is designed to be read and understood by people without stumbling in speech, and by people who have little to no understanding of "bible" words. It therefore achieves its goal of being an easy-to-read bible translation. The Easy-Read Bible (ERV) originally started out as the English Version for the Deaf (EVD). Deaf people sometimes have difficulty reading English, because sign language is their first language. The EVD was designed specifically for them, with simpler vocabulary and shorter sentences. In 2004, the EVD was translated into the ERV (the EVD was left as-is and is still available). They expanded some of the vocabulary in the translation, but being based off of a version designed for the deaf, it still suffers from the same shortcomings that a translation of a version originally designed for the deaf would suffer. Its original sources are good, but its translation could suffer because of its origins. And while it is indeed easy to read, it suffers slightly over the CEV.[\[1\]](#svcmB){#cite-svcmB .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-svcmB){.citation_index} [[218 online Bibles in 72 languages, in text and audio format.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=764f11&url=https://www.biblegateway.com/versions/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="biblegateway.com"}]{.qlink_container}
