**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Would anyone in the Justice Dept have the authority to stop an indictment from the Mueller probe grand jury?** There's a lot of non-starters that have to happen before the Justice Department would even have the opportunity to stop an indictment. First, the Special Counsel would have to convene a grand jury. The only reason they would do this is to see if charges are warranted. However, it's fairly Constitutionally certain that you can't indict a sitting president, so the purpose of such a grand jury is dubious at best. Yes, I know, no one has ever tried to indict a sitting president, but if you look at it from a Constitutional standpoint, then it's highly likely that you cannot indict a sitting president. So let's assume for a moment that the Special Counsel's office *does* convene a grand jury, and they file an indictment. Trump's attorneys would most definitely object to the indictment, stating that a sitting president cannot be indicted, and must have a Constitutional remedy applied (impeachment, removal from office, etc). The Justice Department is not really in the loop if the Special Counsel's office convenes a grand jury, and they indict.
