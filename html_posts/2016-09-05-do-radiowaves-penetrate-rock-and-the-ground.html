--- title: "Do radiowaves penetrate rock and the ground?" date: "2016-09-05" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Radio waves can traverse through the ground. Assuming they are powerful enough and at the appropriate frequency, they certainly can. For example, we have a device known as ***ground-penetrating radar*** which can see through soil. Radio waves of sufficient power and at the appropriately low frequency can penetrate soil to fairly impressive depths. Communicating through soil? Probably not. Those types of radio signals are at a much higher frequency and probably won't get very far through any dense material, soil included. Most radio waves cannot penetrate solid rock. In general, for a radio to work correctly (a traditional radio we are familiar with), you need ***line of sight*** between the sender and the receiver.
