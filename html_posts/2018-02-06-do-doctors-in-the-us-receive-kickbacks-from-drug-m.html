**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do doctors in the US receive kickbacks from drug makers to prescribe their medicines? If so, how much do they receive? My Dr. is very particular on costly medicines which require pre-authorization and it makes me feel suspicious.** Doctors do not get paid for prescribing certain medications. In general, doctors don't receive any money from drug manufacturers at all. My neurologist was on a product advisory board for a few Multiple Sclerosis drugs, and he was paid for that time. You may or may not know that those drugs are **very** expensive. Without insurance, those drugs would be prohibitively costly (to the tune of \~\$10,000 for a 30-day supply). I often wondered about the same issue, especially because of the advisory boards, so without pretense or accusation, I asked him about it. Interestingly, he had already recommended that I **not** take the drug that his advisory board had researched. He welcomed the question, understood from whence it came, and was glad to share what he received, when he received it, and he showed me how it was the efficacy of the drug and how it fit my particular disease profile, and not his time on any advisory boards, which prompted his decision to recommend one drug over another.
