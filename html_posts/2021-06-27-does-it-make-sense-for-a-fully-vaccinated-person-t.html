**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You're asking a question about "what makes sense" and that answer varies from person to person. Some fully-vaccinated people are so concerned about "showing care for others" that they still wear masks in public just to show that they are not a risk to others. Some got vaccinated but believe that fully-vaccinated people can still carry the disease and spread it to others, so they choose to wear a mask to forestall that possibility. So does it make sense? That's for each individual to decide for themselves. I think masks will stick around for quite a while, as we normalize the ability for each and every person to decide for themselves what is best for them. \ Original question: Does it make sense for a fully vaccinated person to wear a mask in public indoor settings like supermarkets (I don\'t ask for an answer from a legal standpoint)?
