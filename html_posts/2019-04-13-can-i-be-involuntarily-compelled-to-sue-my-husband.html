**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can I be involuntarily compelled to sue my husband who is also a victim of the actual defendant and if so, would that in itself be a claim for which relief could be granted?** No, for multiple reasons. 1. You can't be compelled to sue your lawful spouse. First of all, at common law, you can't sue your spouse because legally, a married couple is considered a single entity. This is referred to in common law as "spousal immunity." While some states have laws against suing your spouse, most states only carve out exceptions where spousal privilege doesn't apply. 2. You can't be compelled to sue anyone. Whether or not you sue is up to you. There is no legal mechanism that can compel you to file a lawsuit against someone, and I can't find any relevant case law where someone was compelled to sue someone else against their will.
