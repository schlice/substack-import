Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is the very definition of "omnipotent," which means "all-powerful." That means only he, unlike any of us, can do things that we believe are "impossible." For us, the Law of Causation applies. For us, Einstein's Theory of General Relativity applies. For us, the idea of spontaneous generation has been abandoned decades ago. Not so for God. As God exists *outside* of the universe and is not bound by its constraints, God can, quite literally, do anything. Just remember: if you ask a question that begins with "Can God..." just know that the answer is always **Yes, God can.** \ Original question: What did God make the universe out of when there was nothing but him and nothing?
