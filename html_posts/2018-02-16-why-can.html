**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why is the US government reluctant to implement gun restrictions (if gun control has failed)?** The government is not *reluctant* to implement gun restrictions. It is *prohibited* from doing so by the Second Amendment which, in part, reads: > *...the right of the People to keep and bear Arms shall not be > infringed.* So while they may *want* to implement restrictions, they cannot. That doesn't mean they don't try, though. And that's only *Federal* restrictions. Many *states* still have repressive gun laws which haven't been challenged or repealed yet, even though the Second Amendment has been incorporated to the states with the *Heller v. DC* and *MacDonald v. Chicago* Supreme Court cases.
