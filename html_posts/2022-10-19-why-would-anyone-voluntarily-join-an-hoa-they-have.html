--- title: '"Why would anyone voluntarily join an HOA? They have a notoriously bad reputation."' date: '2022-10-19' tags: - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Do you always do or not do things based on their reputation alone? Sure, there are indeed some problem HOAs out there. They've been thoroughly mocked and derided sufficiently in the movies and on TV that people recognize them and largely have corrected them. Primarily, people don't like HOAs because 1. they don't like to be told what to do and not do with their own homes, and 2. the HOA is empowered, by you, to provide consequences for your actions, and people don't like consequences for their actions. Secondarily, the HOA is run by *people*. People are fallible. People sometimes have different ideas about how to address issues. Some people are just so weird when it comes to whether or not the HOA should issue a fine. Some people never want to levy fines, and others are all too quick to jump right to fines. Having smart leadership, and a good property manager, are essential, and it is a rare combination when an HOA has both. In general, HOAs are beneficial, and help to keep neighborhoods looking good. They also maintain the common areas that all homeowners in the neighborhood enjoy. \ Some HOAs are, indeed, voluntary, and you only join them if you want the benefits they are offering (like club membership, etc). Other HOAs are mandatory, and you automatically join them when you buy a house in a particular neighborhood, as the HOA is attached to the house.
