**Content:** [Any time a decision like this is made, it is done so in the public interest. So ask yourself why the public would want someone convicted of a crime to have a permanent record. There are several reasons why we would want this.\ \ - While past behavior is no indication of future actions, it is possible that in the future, an investigation may need access to the wealth of information that was previously collected during a criminal investigation.\ - There is an entire field of research devoted to looking at statistics and inferring patterns based on those statistics. We learn a great deal of information from this field of study, called epidemiology. It is most often used in healthcare, when we are unsure of the cause of the disease. We can look at ancillary statistics about the disease, and we can narrow our focus towards the cause of the disease using those statistics. The same can hold true for criminal behavior, and maintaining records can help us narrow down societal causes of aberrant behavior, in the hopes that we can make positive changes to reduce violence.]{.rendered_qtext}
