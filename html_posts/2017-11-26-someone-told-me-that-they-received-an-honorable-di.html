--- title: '"Someone told me that they received an honorable discharge from the military before they served their term? How is that possible?"' date: '2017-11-26' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Without knowing exactly what the discharge code was, we will never know why the person was discharged before their period of enlistment was completed. But think about it: You sign up for three years, and the military agrees, because they want to keep you around for three years. Then, they let you go after two years. **WHY IN GOD'S NAME WOULD THEY DO THAT?** Perhaps because *they realized they didn't want to keep you?* The military has to agree to release you, and it has to be for a valid reason. Whatever that valid reason is, they let you go without requiring you to serve your entire contracted period of enlistment. If they wanted to keep you, you think they would let you go without completing your term so easily? Just to show you how many possible reasons for discharge exist, here is a more or less complete list of [[Military Separation Codes](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=52404c&url=https://www.thebalance.com/military-separation-codes-3356946){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="thebalance.com"}]{.qlink_container}.
