Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [While you might like to set some conditions that nations must follow, nations are independent. They don\'t answer to any \"world government\". You can certainly write a treatise in the United Nations for member states to use as guidelines, but there is no one organization that can direct nations\' laws.\ \ A nation can determine on its own whether or not it feels that pre-emptive military action is appropriate. The rest of the nations in the world can judge for themselves whether or not they think that this action was justified and can make policies to deal with that nation accordingly. So nations who act unilaterally and unprovoked not only face the wrath of their own citizens, but the rest of the world.]{.rendered_qtext}
