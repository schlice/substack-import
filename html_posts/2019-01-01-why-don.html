**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why don\'t we see many bumper stickers on car bumpers anymore?** Stickers ruin plastic. It used to be that bumpers were made of steel with a chrome coating. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-f50802412e9abce4e9445c53ebcd9860){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-f50802412e9abce4e9445c53ebcd9860" master_w="2463" master_h="1672"} ::: :::: *(Chrome bumper on a 1970 AMC Ambassador SST. Source:* [[*Wikimedia*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=311513&url=https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:1970_AMC_Ambassador_SST_hardtop_yellow-black_K-t.jpg){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikimedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}*. CC 0)* Stickers were easy to apply, and easy to remove. You could easily and safely use solvents to remove any sticky residue from the place where the sticker was, without causing *too much* damage to the bumper itself. Nowadays, most bumpers are made of plastic covering the impact absorbing material inside. Putting stickers on plastic is not advised. The plastic is usually painted, and if you've ever removed a sticker from a painted surface, you've probably removed some of the paint along with it. Therefore, placing bumper stickers on modern bumpers is not advised. Most people these days will apply stickers to the glass, and not the bumper. Stickers have moved away from the "bumper sticker" shape (since we don't have to fit them on a narrow bumper) and have taken more freeform shapes: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-c6958b073422fe8d9f73ea6cbc19d3a6){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-c6958b073422fe8d9f73ea6cbc19d3a6" master_w="1600" master_h="1067"} ::: :::: *(Skrillex sticker on glass: Source:* [[*Wikimedia*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=012a81&url=https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Skrillex_carsticker.jpg){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikimedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}*, by* [[*55Laney69*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e4e7b0&url=https://www.flickr.com/people/42875184@N08){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="flickr.com"}]{.qlink_container}*. CC BY 2.0)* It's actually easier to remove stickers from glass without damaging the material underneath. So this new method is better for the car's finish than even bumper stickers were.
