--- title: '"What would happen if a top military official refused to carry out a military order from the president of the United States?"' date: '2017-07-17' tags: - policy-analysis - retirement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Whether it's a top general or a Second Lieutenant, an officer refusing to follow an order is a serious charge. You'd better have a damn good reason for refusing to follow an order from your commander, and the only good reason would be that the order violates a law. It may violate rules of engagement, it may even go so far as to violate the Law of Armed Combat for the engagement in which you are currently operating. But regardless, you never see active refusal to follow orders unless there is a violation of law in the order. Generally, what would happen is that the senior officer would find someone else to carry out the order (if they can) and then will file Article 15 proceedings against the officer for failure to follow a direct order. The order will be reviewed, and if the order is found to be compliant with law, the officer will be court-martialed and likely dishonorably discharged. So if you're going to refuse to follow an order, you better be right. Specifically for the President and his service chiefs, he would probably fire them (i.e. reassign or retire) and get someone else who would follow orders.
