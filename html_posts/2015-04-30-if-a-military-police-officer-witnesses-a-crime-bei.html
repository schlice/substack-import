**Content:** [So there are two questions here; let\'s answer them one at a time.\ \ *1. If a military police officer witnesses a crime being committed by a civilian, can he or she arrest that civilian?*\ The answer, as always, is \"it depends.\" If the military police officer is on a military installation stateside, he can detain the civilian and contact local authorities to come pick up the criminal. This is because stateside, military installations are under \"concurrent jurisdiction;\" the base is both a military installation and a part of a city or county. If the military police officer is not on a military installation, he has the same \"citizen\'s arrest\" powers that anyone has, except that he may be armed under the provisions of the Law Enforcement Officer\'s Safety Act (LEOSA).\ \ *2. If a member of the military commits a crime and there are no MPs around, like deserting in a combat zone, can superior officers detain/arrest them until there are MPs around?*\ Yes, though it\'s unlikely. Normally, a superior officer would order the military member to report to the MPs, and he can be escorted to the MPs by an NCO. He does have the authority to detain a deserter, but like I said, it would be challenging in a war zone.\ \ If a military member commits a crime under the Geneva Convention or he violates a local law, the superior officer can order the military member be detained and transported to the MPs.]{.rendered_qtext}
