**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are their any legal ramifications for using pepper spray on someone?** Of course there are! However, most of the time that pepper spray is used on someone (which constitutes a simple assault), you have an *affirmative defense.* An affirmative defense is one in which you *affirm* that you committed the offense, but you did so because of a generally accepted reason (i.e. you used pepper spray in self-defense). If you were not using pepper spray to defend yourself, then you could be seen as an aggressor and you could be charged with assault.
