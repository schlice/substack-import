**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Some* government buildings cannot be photographed. Those buildings will be clearly marked, and police will indeed tell you that you can't photograph those buildings. Generally, you also can't access those buildings (i.e. they will be fenced off), but this is not always the case. If an officer tells you that you can't photograph a building that you believe you are allowed to photograph, please feel free to file a complaint with that officer's department, or head to your nearest ACLU office to file a First Amendment violation lawsuit. If you don't stand up to officers who act inappropriately, the behavior will never be corrected, and you have no reason to complain when it continues.
