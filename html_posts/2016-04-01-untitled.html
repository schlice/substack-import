**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I think you misunderstand the basis for when you can fire a weapon. The level of scrutiny is \"fear for one\'s life.\" If you fear for your life, you can use deadly force. The police officer has a different level of scrutiny. The police officer is not only charged with protecting his own life, but he is also charged with protecting the lives of the general public. So while a civilian is only authorized to defend himself, an officer has to make a judgment call, in a split second, as to whether or not this person might continue on and harm others. If the answer to that question *might* be yes, the officer has a **duty** to stop that person. The law allows officers to stop that person using whatever means are necessary. If you don\'t believe that officers should have the authority to stop criminals using whatever means necessary, you should lobby your legislators to change the law in your state and remove that authority from the police.
