Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is a penitent bound by the seal of confession?** Not according to the canon of the Catholic Church. But it wouldn't make sense that the penitent would have to be bound by a confessional seal; it's most likely that he doesn't want that kind of stuff getting out, anyway. The point of the seal of confession is that when the penitent confesses to the confessor (always a priest), that priest can never reveal what he has heard. The canon law of the Roman Catholic church states clearly, \"The sacramental seal is inviolable; therefore it is absolutely forbidden for a confessor to betray in any way a penitent in words or in any manner and for any reason.\" Additionally, anyone else who overhears the confession *is* also bound by the confessional seal. But if the penitent chooses to tell someone else his sins, there is no such seal forbidding that. So a penitent can confess in the confessional to committing a crime, and then can later confess to the police without violating church law. For more serious offenses, a priest may even advise a penitent to "turn himself in" and doing so would not violate the seal of confession. The priest, however, can only advise the penitent to do so; he may not divulge anything heard in confession to anyone, including authorities.
