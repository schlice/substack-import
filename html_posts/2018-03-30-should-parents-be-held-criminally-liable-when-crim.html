--- title: '"Should parents be held criminally liable when crimes are committed by their children with their guns?"' date: '2018-03-30' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I\'m going to buck the trend, I guess. **No one should ever be held criminally responsible other than the criminal, and anyone else who commits the crime with them, for committing a crime.** If your area has safe storage laws, parents can be held responsible if they fail to follow those. Parents might also be held liable for a neglect charge, or something similar. But holding a party criminally responsible when they did not participate in, know about, or otherwise knowingly facilitate the crime in question? No. If you want such a law, first you have to pass safe storage laws (and deal with the Constitutional issues around that) and then make it a crime to violate that law. Then you can charge them with reckless endangerment or something like that, too.
