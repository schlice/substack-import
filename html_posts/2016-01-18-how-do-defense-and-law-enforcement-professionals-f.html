**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I am reminded of the following quote: *When seconds count, the police are only minutes away.* The simple fact remains: **the police are not in the business of protecting you** ***from*** **crime.** The police are specifically \"law enforcement.\" That means they enforce laws. In order to do that, someone must *violate* the law, and the police will then *enforce* that law. This implies that the police duty actually occurs *post-violation*. In other words, *the crime has already happened by the time the police are engaged.* Under their actual mandate, the police do not get involved until the crime has already occurred. Which begs the question: *If the police are not supposed to be protecting me, whose job is it to protect me?* Unless you\'re rich enough to hire your own bodyguards, the job of the safety and security of you and your family falls on **you** (more specifically, the head of the household). Under the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution, this country was created as a *free society*. We have certain unalienable rights, among these are life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness. Some of the other inalienable rights we have which were endowed by our Creator are a natural right to self-defense. We have the right to be secure in our persons. We also have the right to be secure in our homes (and most legislatures in the country have recognized this by instituting some form of [[Castle doctrine](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=1588d1&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castle_doctrine){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} in their laws). Since we have the right to self-defense, we have the right to defend ourselves from all forms of tyranny: governmental tyranny, tyranny by armed thugs, or by anyone who wishes to deprive us of our fundamental rights to life, liberty, or property. This is one of the reasons that the Second Amendment was so important, that it was made number two. As Senator Rubio mentioned during the debate, they didn\'t place the amendments in a hat and draw them out randomly. Their ordering was very deliberate. The Second Amendment is second only to our right to speek freely against the government (in word or in press), to peaceably assemble, and to practice our religions in peace. Immediately following that, we ensured that we had the right to be secure in our persons by ensuring that everyone always retained the right to defend themselves against all forms of tyranny. If Senator Rubio is doing **his job** as the head of his household, he will be buying a gun to defend them against any and all threats to his safety, and the safety of his family, be it from ISIS or from the meth-mouth down the street looking to steal something he can fence for cash for more meth and not caring who he hurts along the way.
