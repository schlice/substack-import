Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} While laws are different between states, the general rule is that your charge is based on the laws that were in place at the time you were charged and convicted. Since you were convicted as a felon, you are still a felon. Some states allow citizens to seek expungement for certain non-violent felonies. It would be best to seek the advice of a criminal defense attorney to see what your options are in this case.
