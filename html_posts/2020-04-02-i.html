**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **I'm wanting to join the Air Force and wondering if anyone knows how to. When you get your pilot slot, how do you get the B-2 Spirit Bomber as your aircraft?** Since I not only joined the Air Force, but I served my time and got out, I think I qualify as "knowing how to." First, let's address how to join if you want to be a pilot. In order to be a pilot, you must be a commissioned officer. Commissioned officers come from three commissioning sources: the Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC), Officer Training School (OTS), or the US Air Force Academy (USAFA). ROTC is a program you attend while in college. You gain your military training while you are pursuing your undergraduate degree. When you graduate, you get your Bachelor's degree and a commission in the Air Force. OTS is a program for those who already have their Bachelor's degrees. It is an intensive three month program which takes people and trains them to become Air Force officers. Graduates from OTS are commissioned into the Air Force. The US Air Force Academy in Colorado Springs, Colorado is one of our nation's service academies. It requires acceptance into the school as well as a nomination by a Congressman, the Vice-President, or the President. By far, the option chosen by most people (simply based on volume) is ROTC. I would definitely look into ROTC. Here's a great website to get you started: [[Home \| U.S. Air Force ROTC](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6dcace&url=https://www.afrotc.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="afrotc.com"}]{.qlink_container} Even if you think you want bombers now, thinking about which airframe you want to fly is putting the cart WAY before the horse. Worry about becoming a commissioned officer first, and then worry about getting into pilot training. Lean on your advisers and instructors for guidance on what you need to do in order to maximize your chances for being selected.
