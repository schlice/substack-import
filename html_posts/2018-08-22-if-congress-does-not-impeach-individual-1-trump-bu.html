Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If Congress does not impeach Individual 1 (Trump), but the states find enough evidence there were crimes committed, can he still be indicted after he leaves office?** If the state has standing (i.e. a crime was committed in that state), the state can most certainly file charges against Trump after he leaves office. **What could that mean for Trump, his businesses and his pardoned associates?** For Trump and his businesses: he defends himself in exactly the same way as any other business owner charged with a crime. For *his pardoned associates:* they are not subject to those charges, as they have already been pardoned for any crimes they may have committed.
