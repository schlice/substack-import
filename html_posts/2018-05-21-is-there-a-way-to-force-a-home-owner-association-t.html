Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there a way to force a home owner association to prove that they are equally giving people warnings over the same things?** You can request all of the HOA's documentation on the notices they have issued to homeowners, as well as meeting notes for all Board meetings, but finding out whether or not they are providing equal warnings will be difficult, if there is no record of how the HOA receives notices of violations. Secondly, the inequality must be deliberate. If a Board member receives a notification of a violation directly from a homeowner and then forgets and does not communicate that to the rest of the Board to send a notice, is that inequality in warnings? In our HOA, we have a centralized violation system that records reports of violations and records what notices were sent and when. The notices are auto-generated, so they are all recorded and available for homeowner inspection. We advise our homeowners to report violations directly to the Managing Agent who administers that system so that the violation can be adequately recorded. We also advise them that they can talk to anyone, including Board members, but we recommend that reports of violations go to the Managing Agent. Email suffices as a way to record interactions with any of our Board members or our Managing Agent. If an HOA Board is not being forthcoming with their records, you may have to involve a real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law.
