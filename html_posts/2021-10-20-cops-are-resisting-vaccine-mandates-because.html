Despite most of the answerers who believe that cops are not being vaccinated because they think themselves "above the law" or because they "are simply too stupid to be vaccinated," those really are not the reasons why cops are not getting vaccinated. Cops, by their nature, and reinforced through the work they do, are highly skeptical of everything. Also as a function of the work that they do, they know their rights. When a suspect says, "you're violating my rights!" in most cases they are not. And also in most cases, if they are violating your rights, they know it. To that end, most cops who are not getting the vaccine either had COVID already and have recovered and feel they don't need the shot, or are simply skeptical about the vaccines and believe they have the right to control what they put in their own bodies. And yes, while they enforce the laws written by the government, they are still skeptical of the government's motivations. This doesn't lead them to blindly follow government edicts, but actually leads to (sometimes unhealthy levels of) skepticism. And they question the motives behind a vaccine mandate. While they are getting vaccinated, albeit at lower numbers than the general population, their unions are absolutely against vaccine mandates. Only in the largest departments, which can absorb the loss of officers who will resign before getting the jab, are vaccine mandates even being pursued. Most departments are actually following the CDC recommendation and are offering the "either-or" path forward: either the vaccine, or weekly COVID testing. Cops look at this and wonder why the government doesn't follow its own CDC recommendations. So it is quite possible in this case that cops are actually "too smart for their own good." \ Original question: Cops are resisting vaccine mandates because "The govt. can't tell me what to do".
