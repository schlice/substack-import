--- title: '"Why did the Senate inquiry fail to require private hospitals to provide abortion care as a condition of receiving public funding?"' date: '2023-09-05' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because a Senate inquiry is not how you change law. The Hyde Amendment is still the law of the land, and has been re-enacted every year since 1976. It is not a permanent law, but rather a temporary "rider" to the Congressional appropriations bill. Congress has the opportunity to lift the Hyde Amendment each year, but they have added it to the appropriations bill every year. The Hyde Amendment prohibits federal Medicaid funding for abortion services. The only exceptions are when continuing the pregnancy will endanger the patient\'s life, or when the pregnancy results from rape or incest. These circumstances are considered medically necessary health services.
