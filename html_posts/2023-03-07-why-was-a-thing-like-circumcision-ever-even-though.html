**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Circumcision began as a religious practice, and still continues today as such. But it is performed by non-religious people as a matter of health and for social reasons. Circumcision was practiced among Egyptians and Jews historically, with the earliest records of the practice from Egyptian temple paintings and wall drawings from 2300 BC. These days, it is practiced non-religiously for both health and social reasons. On the health front, it was discovered around 2007 that circumcision greatly reduces a man's risk of acquiring HIV.
