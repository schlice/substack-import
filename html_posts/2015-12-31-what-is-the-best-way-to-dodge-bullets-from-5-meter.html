**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Additional question details:* > > *Just curious. If someone suddenly starts shooting at me, should I > run, tuck and roll, do front flips or back flips, which way will make > it more likely for the person to miss when he or she shoots?* You can do whatever the heck you want to do to aid your belief that you can actually get out of the way of a bullet. Especially a bullet fired at such a close range, there is nothing you can do to avoid being hit. As *Mythbusters* [[discovered](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4f120a&url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v%3D2qY0DA7DG9s){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="youtube.com"}]{.qlink_container}, it is theoretically possible to evade a bullet if you are a great distance away from the gun (a minimum of 500 yards, or roughly 457 meters), you can see the gun firing (by the time you hear the report, you\'re dead, so you have to see the muzzle flash), and you move the correct direction out of the way, assuming you are prepared to jump out of its way because you are staring at the shooter and anticipating a shot. Practically, not everyone is a crack shot. At 5 meters away (\~15 feet), you\'d really have to be blind or have never fired a gun ever to miss. But further away, it\'s likely that many shots with a handgun will miss. If someone starts shooting at you, the best thing you can do is find the shortest distance to **cover** and run there. Doing silly things like tucking, rolling, or flipping, will not get you to cover in the fastest way possible. Therefore, if someone starts shooting, keep low and run for cover.
