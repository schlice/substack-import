--- title: "Is it possible to sue for slander if the statements made are considered to be a joke?" date: "2023-02-06" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "it was only a joke" is never a defense against defamation. Slander is spoken defamation while libel is printed or written defamation. There are four elements to defamation: - The defamatory statement, which is made claiming to be fact - Publication or transmission of the statement to a third party - Fault amounting to at least negligence, and - Damages, or some harm caused to the reputation of the person against whom the statement was made. In order to prevail in a defamation case, you need all four. And if the "joke" actually caused damages, suing for defamation is still possible.
