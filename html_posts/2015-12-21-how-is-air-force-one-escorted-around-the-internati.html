--- title: '"How is Air Force One escorted around the international airspace? Does it have any alleged offensive capabilities?"' date: '2015-12-21' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Air Force One is not routinely escorted by fighter jets while it is flying. Yes, it is closely monitored by Air Traffic Control and by the US military (through NORAD) during its flight, but it does not routinely receive fighter escort, especially over the continental United States. It has happened in the past, however. When President Nixon was on his way to Syria for a scheduled appearance in 1974, Syrian fighter jets intercepted Air Force One, which took evasive action. As far as I am aware, Air Force One does not have any offensive capabilities. It does have both ECM and flare/chaff packages which can be deployed, but it would likely call for fighter assistance should it run into trouble. This happened on 9/11, when President George W. Bush left Sarasota, Florida. A credible threat was received and AF1 called for fighter escort.
