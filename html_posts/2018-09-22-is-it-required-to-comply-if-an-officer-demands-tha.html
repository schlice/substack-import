**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it required to comply if an officer demands that you step out of your car at a traffic stop? What is the purpose of this command?** The answer is yes; if an officer demands you exit the vehicle, you must comply. If you fail to comply, you may find you end up with a shattered driver's side window, a cut seat belt and various bruises from being dragged out of your vehicle, but if you have to be dragged out of your vehicle, you're going to jail, not just getting a ticket and going on about your way. > In [[Pennsylvania v. Mimms, 434 U.S. 106 > (1977)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=215a5b&url=https://scholar.google.com/scholar_case?as_sdt%3D4%252C33%26case%3D16533225265380952768%26hl%3Den%26q%3Dpolice%2Bmay%2Brequest%2Ba%2Bsuspect%2Bto%2Bstep%2Bout%2Bof%2Btheir%2Bvehicle%2Bduring%2Ba%2Blawful%2Btraffic%2Bstop){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container}, the Supreme Court of the > United States held that where a driver commits a traffic offense and > is subsequently pulled over by the police, the police may order the > driver (and any occupants in the vehicle) to step out of the vehicle > at *any*time - no questions asked. The rationale for this decision is > best articulated by the New York Court of Appeals in [[People v. > Robinson, 74 N.Y.2d 773, 774 > (1989)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a95d16&url=https://scholar.google.com/scholar_case?as_sdt%3D4%252C33%26case%3D11492072550395741286%26hl%3Den%26q%3Dpolice%2Bmay%2Brequest%2Ba%2Bsuspect%2Bto%2Bstep%2Bout%2Bof%2Btheir%2Bvehicle%2Bduring%2Ba%2Blawful%2Btraffic%2Bstop){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container}, which held that > constitutional rights are not violated where \"a driver is directed to > step out briefly from a lawfully stopped and detained vehicle because > the inherent and inordinate danger to investigating police officers in > completing their authorized official responsibilities in such > circumstances justifies that precautionary action.\" > > In layman\'s terms, what all of this means, is that courts have sided > with police officers and their ambiguous fears about their safety as > compared to any constitutional rights that you may have to be free > from police officers\' often times humiliating requests to step out of > your car for seemingly no reason.[\[1\]](#YMWat){#cite-YMWat > .citation_link} The purpose, clearly, is not necessary to articulate. It could be because you're acting cagey. It could be because you appear to be acting unsafely. It could be because the officer thinks you may have been drinking and wants to test you. It could be because he thinks you may be a flight risk and it's safer to just take you out of the car. It could be for *any reason at all*, but courts have clearly stated that the police have the right to remove you from your vehicle at any time for any reason at a traffic stop.
