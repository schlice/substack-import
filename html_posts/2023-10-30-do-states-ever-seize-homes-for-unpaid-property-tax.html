--- title: "Do states ever seize homes for unpaid property taxes?" date: "2023-10-30" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. Property taxes are levied by counties and cities, not states. So states do not seize homes for unpaid property taxes, but county and city governments do, all the bloody time. Also, if you don't pay your income tax, the IRS can put a lien on your house, and then foreclose on the lien to be paid. But the IRS has to wait in line behind the bank, the local government, and the HOA. Once they get paid, then the IRS can get paid.
