**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What makes a sign ADA compliant?** ADA has many requirements for signage, but there are a few rules of thumb to follow when it comes to signs. **Size and Space Requirements** For 5/8 inch letters (the most common size), you need to allow 2 inches of height per line of text, including the Braille translation below. For larger text sizes, allow 2 times the height of the characters, plus 1 inch per line of Braille. This is a general guideline; actual space needed may be more or less depending on the text. This handy chart can help with lettering: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-368957940d301dbdc193e40c4fff9936){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-368957940d301dbdc193e40c4fff9936" master_w="390" master_h="149"} ::: :::: Other spacing requirements: - Pictograms: You need a 6 inch high area for a pictogram. There are no rules regarding the actual height of the pictogram, but 4 to 4.5 inches is a good starting point. - Braille: You need to leave a minimum of 3/8 inch clearance on all sides of Braille lettering. **Text** Text should be in simple, sans serif fonts in bold weight. Do not use script, italics, or other hard-to-read text decorations. All text must be UPPERCASE. Lowercase lettering is only allowed on visual-only signs, such as pictures, directories, or direction signs.[\[1\]](#LFFYR){#cite-LFFYR .citation_link} The kind folks over at Accent Signage Systems (not a sponsor) have a great [[quick reference PDF](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f90c7e&url=https://www.accentsignage.com/wp-content/uploads/ADA-Quick-Reference.pdf){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="accentsignage.com"}]{.qlink_container} detailing the ADA rules for signage.
