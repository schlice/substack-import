--- title: "We have a rule at home: No mobile phones at the dining table. My 6-year-old daughter played on the phone during dinner, so I denied her dinner. Did I do anything wrong?" date: "2015-03-04" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [To be specific, you didn\'t do anything wrong legally. She ate at least once that day, and courts have ruled that \"sending a child to bed without dinner\" is not \"cruel and unusual punishment\" assuming the child has eaten at least one other meal that day and the pattern is not continuous (i.e. daily).\ \ However, as a parenting philosophy, it doesn\'t make sense to deny food because the child isn\'t following the phone rules. If the rule is \"no phones at the table,\" the violation of that rule results in the phone being taken away for a period of time, not the dinner.\ \ You might even try to forestall the issue entirely by placing one of these near (but not at) the dinner table\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-4f5a95e8e188185a8c42658964e6d952){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-4f5a95e8e188185a8c42658964e6d952" master_w="300" master_h="259"} ::: :::: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-be6563d95eabde58c0f357ba0d5cd03b){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-be6563d95eabde58c0f357ba0d5cd03b" master_w="640" master_h="440"} ::: :::: All phones must be checked in to the phone stand before dinner, no one may respond to their phones, and as an added bonus, the phones charge while you eat!\ \ Additionally, you can say that eating at the table is a privilege which can be removed; i.e. the child can be made to eat in his room by himself and not with the family. But in all cases, the most appropriate punishment is the removal of the phone, not the removal of the meal.
