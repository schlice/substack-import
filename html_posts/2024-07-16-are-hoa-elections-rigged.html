--- title: "Are HOA elections rigged?" date: "2024-07-16" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." Each HOA's elections are governed by their Governing Documents: the Declaration of Covenants, the Articles of Incorporation, and the Bylaws for the corporation, as well as any Policies, Procedures, Rules and Regulations (PPR&R) which may have been written and approved by the Board. The ability to which an HOA's election can be "rigged" is a function of the specific rules for the election process in the Governing Documents, as well as rules which may be in place for HOA elections in your state. But *in general*, it's not easy to rig an HOA election. Usually, the process by which the election takes place has some minimal security in place. A disinterested third party (usually the management company) will administer the election, collect the responses, and ensure there is only one vote per household.
