**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What actions will the House take on persons refusing to testify?** Behind closed doors, they've been talking about having the Sergeant-at-Arms detain people who violate subcommittee subpoenas. But this seems a little far-fetched, even for them. Ultimately, they will attempt to hold individuals who defy subpoenas in contempt of Congress. This really means nothing unless they attempt to enforce those subpoenas, usually through the DOJ and the US Attorney's Office (normally, the office in DC is the one used for this sort of enforcement). It's at this point that they will start to face some of the legal challenges that this issue is certain to bring, starting with the fact that the legitimacy of the subpoenas is questionable at best, and at worst, is rife with legal deficiencies. If the subpoenas are not legally enforceable because the underlying investigation is unconstitutional, the US Attorney's Office will be unable to pursue any enforcement of the subpoenas. At the very least, issuance of subpoenas, being held in contempt of Congress and attempted enforcement actions will bring the question of the legality of the whole "subpoena about impeachment" issue to the fore. And to the courts, where those questions of law will be decided.
