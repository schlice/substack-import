**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} 1. I don't understand what "can't be rebuilt" means. You can always raze the rubble and build a totally new building where the old one stood. Thankfully, HOAs have insurance on their buildings, just like you do on your house. If the property is destroyed, insurance will pay most of the cost to rebuild it. Your HOA may need to do a special assessment to raise money to furnish it again, but insurance will cover most of the cost. 2. What happens to the HOA? Nothing. The HOA still exists even if the clubhouse gets destroyed. The bylaws don't burn; they're on file with the county government. Don't worry, your HOA will be fine. They will survive a fire. \ Original question: What happens to a homeowners\' association (HOA) if the building is destroyed by a fire and can\'t be rebuilt?
