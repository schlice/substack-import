**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What can the President do with the Presidential Alert function on my phone other than show messages?** It seems that people have the false notion that the President can use WEA in the same way he uses Twitter. Let's dispel some myths, shall we? 1. "The President" by himself cannot activate the Emergency Alert System (EAS) and its component system, the Wireless Emergency Alert (WEA) network.\ \ The network is activated in conjunction with the FCC, FEMA, and NOAA, and can only be used as a "last-ditch" communication measure when the President cannot reach the media, during a national emergency.\ \ For example, the system was not engaged during the last national emergency, September 11th, 2001, because the agencies determined that the media was fully conveying all relevant information without the need to activate EAS or WEA. 2. WEA, despite claims to the contrary, cannot control your phone. It can't activate your microphone. It can't access your location. It can't activate any functionality that E911 can. Essentially, it's nothing more than a text message. Your phone receives it. It displays the message. That's about the extent of it.
