**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > How many times is baptism mentioned in the Bible? The word \"baptism\" is mentioned **22 times** in the New Testament. The verb form, \"baptize\", is mentioned 10 times, and the action form, \"baptized\", is mentioned 60 times. > What is the purpose of baptism according to the Bible? Baptism is a symbolic gesture, both for your own personal journey and as a sign to the rest of the world that you believe in the death and resurrection of Jesus. Remember: *baptism does not save you*. *Baptism is what saved people do.* The Bible commands believers to be baptized. Jesus tells us to do it as part of what we call the Great Commission: > *Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the > name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching > them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you > always, to the very end of the age. --- Matthew 28:19--20 NIV* So at the very least, we are baptized because Christ commanded it. Beyond that, we believe that Christ followers are "buried with Him" through baptism, and if we are united with Him in death, we will "certainly also be united with him in a resurrection like his." > *Or don't you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus > were baptized into his death? We were therefore buried with him > through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised > from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new > life.* > > *For if we have been united with him in a death like his, we will > certainly also be united with him in a resurrection like his. For we > know that our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled > by sin might be done away with, that we should no longer be slaves to > sin---because anyone who has died has been set free from sin.* > > *Now if we died with Christ, we believe that we will also live with > him. For we know that since Christ was raised from the dead, he cannot > die again; death no longer has mastery over him. The death he died, he > died to sin once for all; but the life he lives, he lives to God.* > > *In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in > Christ Jesus. --- Romans 6:3--11 NIV* Baptism is usually considered the *beginning* of a personal faith journey for a Christian.
