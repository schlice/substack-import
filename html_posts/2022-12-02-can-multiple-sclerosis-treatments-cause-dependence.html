--- title: "Can multiple sclerosis treatments cause dependence on the medication?" date: "2022-12-02" tags: ["health-autoimmune"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you are referring to "dependence" along the same lines as "chemical dependence," which has symptoms such as: - Tolerance to or need for increased amounts of the drug to get an effect - Withdrawal symptoms that happen if you decrease or stop using the drug that you find difficult to cut down or quit - Spending a lot of time to get, use, and recover from the effects of using drugs - Withdrawal from social and recreational activities - Continued use of the drug even though you are aware of the physical, psychological, and family or social problems that are caused by your ongoing drug abuse then the answer is no. However, as other answerers have pointed out, we MS patients are nonetheless *dependent* on our drugs to keep the progression of the disease at bay. There is no cure for MS, and the best we can hope to do is to slow it down. Basically, we'll keep taking the medications until, as my doctor put it, we're "too old to care." I'd say that, in some capacity, that qualifies as *dependence*. \ ***Disclaimer:*** *This answer is not professional medical advice, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
