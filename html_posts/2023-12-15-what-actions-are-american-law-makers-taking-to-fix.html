--- title: '"What actions are American law makers taking to fix the miscarriage of justice that allows public indecency in the form of breastfeeding in public?"' date: '2023-12-15' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} ***Absolutely nothing.*** - All fifty states have laws which allow breastfeeding in any public or private location. - Thirty-one states specifically exclude breastfeeding from public indecency laws. - Thirty states have laws related to breastfeeding in the workplace. - Twenty-two states exempt breastfeeding mothers from jury duty or allow their duty to be postponed. - Four states have endorsed or encouraged the development of a breastfeeding awareness or education campaign. It seems that American lawmakers are *doubling down* on breastfeeding, rather than considering it public indecency. \ It would only be a miscarriage of justice if the justice system came to an *unjust* conclusion. So is it *unjust* that we consider breastfeeding to not be public indecency? The answer comes down to how society feels about breastfeeding, and how society feels that laws should convenience those who take offense at seeing such things. Countries do this differently. In Australia, laws encourage mothers to be considerate of those around them who might see them feeding their babies, while in the US, those encouragements don't exist. The law encourages mothers to breastfeed and not feel uncomfortable about doing so.
