**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, if you are in fear for your life, you can use any force, including deadly force, to preserve your life. The problem with a flashbang is that they are indiscriminate, and they are not 100% harmless to people around them. You risk injuring yourself and the other people inside your home, not to mention that a flashbang doesn't work like it does in a video game; it does grenade-level damage to your property. It's going to cost you so much more to throw a flashbang than it's worth. Actual military and LE flashbangs are considered "destructive devices" and are not available on the commercial market. However, there are civilian versions of flashbangs which consist of a charge in a cardboard canister, and these are essentially classified as "pyrotechnic devices." They are less destructive and quieter than the real thing (125 db vs 175 db) but would still be pretty convincing in a home defense scenario.
