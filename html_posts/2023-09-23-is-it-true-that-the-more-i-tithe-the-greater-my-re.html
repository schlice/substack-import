Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the *vast* majority of cases, no. You cannot *buy* favor with God. And you only need to read Mark 12:41--44 in order to recognize this. > Jesus sat down opposite the place where the offerings were put and > watched the crowd putting their money into the temple treasury. Many > rich people threw in large amounts. But a poor widow came and put in > two very small copper coins, worth only a few cents. > > Calling his disciples to him, Jesus said, "Truly I tell you, this poor > widow has put more into the treasury than all the others. They all > gave out of their wealth; but she, out of her poverty, put in > everything---all she had to live on." Jesus was watching not only their offerings, but their *hearts*. He could see not only what they gave, but *why* they gave it. The text says "Many rich people threw in large amounts." Today, we often see celebrities donate large sums of money to charities in an effort to *virtue signal* to people; to telegraph their supposed kindness or benevolence. Do we "clang our coins" while we donate, so that others may notice? Do we donate for the praise of man, or for the glory of God? The poor woman was praised for donating her two coins not because of the amount, but because she gave out of *faith.* Our challenge is the same. We are challenged to not only to give from what we have in abundance, but to give what we have out of *faith*. The other thing to note is that Jesus is not saying it's bad to have wealth. But in this story, as well as with the story of the rich young man who was told to give away his money (Matthew 19:16--30), our wealth can "get in the way" of our discipleship and our walk with Christ. We must also be cautious of this, and not allow money to get in our way of our faith walk, as it can very easily become the "god" in our lives.
