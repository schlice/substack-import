**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Question Details:* > > *My friend said he was using a soundboard to call up a store, and the > soundboard was saying all of these aggressive insults to the employee > that answered the phone. He said that it sounded like the guy who > answered figured that the call wasn\'t serious and was playing along, > but then when my friend clicked on a phrase that said \"If I knew > where you were I would smoke you\" and then the employee said that he > was calling the police.* > > *My friend was using Google Voice to place the call and the employee > got his GV number. Do you think police will bother to trace it back to > him and arrest him?* If the called party chooses to press charges, then yes, your friend can be arrested for verbalizing a threat. It wouldn't stick, of course, because a crime requires the unlawful act (the *actus reus)* and the state of mind in which you actually wanted to do the crime (the criminal mind, or the *mens rea*). Realistically, I don\'t think he will be arrested. I think it\'s pretty straightforward that this was nothing more than a prank call, and there was no malicious intent on the part of the caller. However, if the called party wants to press charges, the caller can be arrested.
