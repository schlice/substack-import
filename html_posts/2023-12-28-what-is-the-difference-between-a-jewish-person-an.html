**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Depends on how you define the word "Zionist." These days, Zionism is an ideology that supports \"the development and protection of the State of Israel,\"[\[1\]](#WwwNy){#cite-WwwNy .citation_link}or someone who advocates for an independent Jewish state where Jews can live safely. Its original meaning, though, was the advocacy of establishing Jewish control of Jerusalem, which is currently in the territory controlled by Hamas. Zionism is used derisively today as advocation of the destruction of the Arabs in the region. Not all Jews live in Israel. There is a significant population of Jews in the United States as well. Jews exist in many countries around the world, not just Israel. But to many religious Jews, Israel is "the promised land." However, you don't have to be a religious Jew to want to see a country where Jews can live in safety and freedom. Many people, especially Palestinian Arabs, don't see a difference between "Jew" and "Zionist." The very presence of this question shows just how much people misunderstand the difference between "Jew" and "Zionist." Most Jews don't even live in Israel. And not all Jewish Israelis are "settlers" or "occupiers" who want to take over more and more Palestinian land. Some openly advocate for the "two-state solution" where Israel and Palestine live side by side in peace. **TL;DR:** Not everyone who is Jewish advocates for the destruction of the Palestinian state, and not everyone who argues for the destruction of the Palestinian state is Jewish. Judaism and Zionism are not the same.
