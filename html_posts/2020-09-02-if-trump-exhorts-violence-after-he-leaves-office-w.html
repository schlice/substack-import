**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If Trump exhorts violence after he leaves office, will he be open to federal prosecution?** If by "exhorts violence" you specifically mean "inciting violence," then any American citizen can be charged with this, even a former President. However, that's a pretty high bar to meet. The standard the courts use is called "imminent lawless action." This standard was developed in a Supreme Court case called *Brandenberg v. Ohio*, and has come to be known as the "Brandenberg test." Under the imminent lawless action test, speech is not protected by the First Amendment if the speaker intends to incite violence that is both imminent and likely. Imminent violence means violence in the very near future, or at least a specified time. The imminence piece has been tested several times, and making speeches calling for violence at some unspecified future time does not amount to imminence, and therefore does not fall outside of First Amendment protection. Likelihood has also been tested. Simply calling for violence is protected by the First Amendment, It has to be "likely" that violence would be triggered by the speech before it might lose that protection.
