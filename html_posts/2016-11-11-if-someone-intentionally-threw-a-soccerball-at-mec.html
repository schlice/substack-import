**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Look, Nicholas, I know that the United States is a very litigious society. But c'mon, man, really? Someone threw a soccer ball at you and you want to sue? [[https://youtu.be/4PLvdmifDSk?t=1\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=082d59&url=https://youtu.be/4PLvdmifDSk?t%3D1m40s){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="youtu.be"}]{.qlink_container} Applying what Dr. Malcom said to your situation, you're so preoccupied with whether or not you could, you didn't stop to think if you **should**. *Can* you sue? Of course you can. *Should* you sue? In my opinion, no. You can sue for assault and battery in civil court. You might get some money for pain and suffering, if you can prove that you have suffered because of the attack. But you've got an uphill battle in front of you. You may have also been doing something illegal too. Did you know that using vulgarity in a school zone may also be illegal in your jurisdiction? You would now have the crime you committed up against a civil infraction your opponent committed, and your misdemeanor trumps his civil infraction. So you ought to be careful wielding the lawsuit stick. You may get some blowback onto yourself going that route. My advice to both of you: **grow up.** You're not little kids anymore. It's time to brush it off, admit your own culpability, accept it for what it is, and **move on**.
