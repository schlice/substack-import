**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is the impeachment trial unconstitutional or are Republicans misleading the American public on the issue? It seems that the only party that can declare something unconstitutional is the Supreme Court - not me, you, a Congressman or the entire Senate.** The Constitution is very clear. The purpose of impeachment is removal from office. Only secondarily is its purpose to vote to not allow someone to ever hold political office ever again. The Article of Impeachment, the one that the Senate will be trying, calls for removal from office as the prime remedy. Except that Trump has already been removed from office. The primary objective, therefore, of the impeachment article, as written by the House, has already been met. There is a concept in law called *mootness* wherein when you have already achieved your goal, your case is dismissed. Second, impeachments are designed for the President, the Vice President, and other Federal office holders. Trump, however, is not a Federal office holder. He is a *former* Federal office holder. He is a civilian. The Constitution has a very specific provision against Congress passing *Bills of Attainder* which are Congressional bills which apply punishments to specific civilians without the protections of a judicial trial. It's the equivalent of the Legislative branch assuming powers of the Judicial branch, and this is patently unconstitutional. This trial would, in fact, apply a punishment to a specific civilian, without the protection of a judicial trial. This makes this proceeding the processing of a Bill of Attainder, not an impeachment trial, as the person "on trial" does not qualify for a trial in the Senate. It is for those specific reasons that the trial is unconstitutional. Republicans can say that it is unconstitutional, but it would only be after the Senate actually imposed a punishment that Trump could bring the case to Federal court and argue that the Senate trial was, in fact, unconstitutional, and then the courts could decide. Regardless of the constitutionality of the action, the Senate has, by the narrowest of margins, voted itself the power to conduct the trial. But rest assured, the vote to convict will fall along similar party lines. There's no way you'll get the 2/3 majority you need to convict, or to ban the President from ever holding office again. So Trump will have been twice impeached and twice acquitted, and he will declare himself "totally innocent" and he will have this kabuki theater of a Senate trial to hold up as proof.
