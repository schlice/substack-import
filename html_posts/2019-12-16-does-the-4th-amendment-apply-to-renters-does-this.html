**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the 4th Amendment apply to renters? Does this only apply to home owners?** The 4th Amendment protects citizens, keeping them secure from unreasonable search in their "persons, houses, papers, and effects." Your "house" is your domicile, wherever you live, be it a rented apartment, townhome, condo, or single family home. Own or rent, the 4th Amendment applies. Clearly if you rent, there are certain things your landlord has the right to do, such as access your apartment for maintenance with the proper notification. But that doesn't constitute a government agent performing an unreasonable search.
