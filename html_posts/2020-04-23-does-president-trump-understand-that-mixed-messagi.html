**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does President Trump understand that mixed messaging between himself and medical experts hurts the country by creating confusion?** I understand that you may be confused by mixed messaging. However, I didn't elect "medical experts" to run the country. Medical experts, if they had their way, would keep us in lockdown for the next 18 months until a vaccine is distributed. Then, they'd identify everyone who got a vaccine with some sort of identification to prove that you're safe to leave your home. Thankfully, medical experts don't run the country. Trump takes advice from medical experts and creates policy which **balances** the need for medical action with the needs of the economy, which can also kill just as efficiently as the virus. Numbers released just today show that **27 million people** have filed for unemployment benefits since the start of the pandemic. Think about that. **27,000,000 people** can no longer buy food for their kids or afford their houses. And there is a significant number of those families who already had difficulty feeding their kids to begin with, many of them with school food programs being that child's primary meal of the day. So now, not only are they not in school being fed by a school food program, but their caregivers have no means to make money to even potentially buy food. Millions more children, beyond those who were starving before, will be starving now. The UN has already warned that hundreds of thousands of them may die. [[U.N. warns economic downturn could kill hundreds of thousands of children in 2020](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e1245d&url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-children-un/u-n-warns-economic-downturn-could-kill-hundreds-of-thousands-of-children-in-2020-idUSKBN21Y2X7?utm_source%3Dquora%26utm_medium%3Dreferral){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="reuters.com"}]{.qlink_container} And all of that because maybe 100,000 may die. 27,000,000 impoverished because maybe 100,000 may die. Do those numbers add up to you? If they do, then it's probably because you don't understand what "flattening the curve" was all about. Flattening the curve was never about preventing you from getting sick in the first place. Flattening the curve was about making sure that we don't overwhelm the medical system. This also means that yes, people will get sick. Yes, some people will die. But you cannot prevent that. Even with aggressive measures taken (the most aggressive we have the ability to take in this country), people will still get sick and people will die. This isn't an argument of lives vs money. It's an argument of lives vs lives. It always has been. If we don't start allowing people to go back to work, and if we don't start allowing people to earn money again, we will be facing a second pandemic, and it has nothing to do with a virus. Instead, our strategy should be one of allowing the young, healthy people to go back to work, protecting the most at-risk among us, monitoring disease outbreak, making changes where needed, and getting the country back to work again. The medical professionals would never stand for that. It's good to hear what the medical experts say. But then it's important to hear what the President says, which is what happens when you remember who was elected to actually run the country.
