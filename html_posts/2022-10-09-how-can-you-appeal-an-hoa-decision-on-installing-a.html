--- title: '"How can you appeal an HOA decision on installing a fence or other structure that is not allowed by the CC&Rs?"' date: '2022-10-09' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If there is an appeals process (and there does not need to be one), it will be outlined in your Governing Documents. Unfortunately, if the HOA says you can't install a fence, you can't install a fence. There really isn't anyone to whom you can appeal. Now, if the HOA isn't following their own rules, or they denied your fence application unfairly (i.e. they allowed your neighbor's fence that is exactly the same, and disallowed yours), you'll have to get a lawyer and file a lawsuit against the HOA (in the case of my example, for selective enforcement). But as far as the things that the HOA has control over go, the HOA has the last word. \ Original question: How can you appeal an HOA decision on installing a fence or other structure that is not allowed by the CC&Rs?
