**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **As a pro-lifer, what do you think about a rape victim that wants to have an abortion?** As a pro-lifer, it is literally the only time I would be willing to even discuss the possibility of abortion, or when the life of the mother is threatened by a continued pregnancy. But we are discussing rape right now, so let's stick with that. Here are a few points to consider: 1. Yes, the rape victim (we're assuming it's a woman) is a victim here. ***But so is the baby***. Does it make much logical sense to take someone else's life because you are the victim of a crime? That baby is as much of a victim as the woman. And the only thing that allows you to kill the baby is its location. Let's say (as sick and twisted as this is) that the rapist brought his 6 month old daughter with him while he raped that woman. Would it still be okay to kill that 6 month old girl because a woman was raped? Of course not. So why is it okay to kill one victim because another person was a victim too? 2. The reason why I will compromise my own principles in the case of rape is because first of all, I know that only about 1% of abortions are a result of rape (and not every woman who is raped chooses to abort her baby; it's around 50% of women who are impregnated during a rape who choose to terminate their pregnancies). In order to get an abortion then, a woman must report a rape. The police must investigate, and they must conclude, at the very least, that she's not lying. So this has the dual effect of correcting the under-reporting of rape, and saving lots and lots of lives. I'm willing to compromise my own principles and save 99% of babies instead of holding fast to my principles and not saving any. 3. I fully understand that if we even entertain the idea of restricting elective abortions, we must massively change the adoption infrastructure in this country. We must make it *dead simple* to both give up a baby for adoption, and also for parents to adopt a baby. The amount of red tape to adopt an American baby is ludicrous. Parents know they have a better chance to actually adopt a baby if they go overseas to do so, to China or Russia or the Philippines. This must end. However, we still must ensure that we are not allowing people to merely adopt children so that they can benefit from tax breaks and other federal assistance. I want adoption to be safe and easy, and not become a cash cow to unscrupulous foster parents. We absolutely can do both, and we can do it much faster than we do now. It will take great efforts on the part of the federal government, and a whole lot of cash, and we are currently short on both of those today.
