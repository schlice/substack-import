**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What\'s a common medical condition that most people don\'t realize they have?** Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism. Either too little or too much hormone produced by the thyroid can cause a myriad of symptoms which are not well understood. Additionally, what most western doctors test for regarding thyroid function (TSH) is not nearly adequate enough to diagnose thyroid issues, and they just end up chasing symptoms. Consequently, many with thyroid dysfunctions go undiagnosed. According to the [[American Thyroid Association](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a86c5c&url=https://www.thyroid.org/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="thyroid.org"}]{.qlink_container}, up to 20 million Americans are suffering from some form of thyroid dysfunction, and up to **60%** of those with thyroid disease **have no idea that they have it.** The causes of thyroid disease are largely unknown. Here's some other disturbing facts about thyroid disease: - One woman in eight will develop a thyroid disorder during her lifetime. - Undiagnosed thyroid disease may put patients at risk for some serious conditions, such as cardiovascular diseases, osteoporosis and infertility. - Pregnant women with undiagnosed or inadequately treated hypothyroidism have an increased risk of miscarriage, preterm delivery, and severe developmental problems in their children. Most thyroid diseases are life-long conditions, but can be managed with medical attention. That little butterfly-shaped gland in your throat is far more important than people give it credit for.
