**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If I own the building where I work and I am fired, can I legally evict my employer for that reason?** If the lease you signed (as the building owner) contains a clause which states that you can evict your tenant if they fire you, then you can. You can evict a tenant for violating a term of the lease. If you can show the court a provision of the lease they violated, you can begin eviction proceedings. If not, then perhaps you simply won't lease to them again once their current lease is up. You are under no obligation to *renew* their lease, but if they haven't otherwise violated the lease by firing you, you can't evict them.
