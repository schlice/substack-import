**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What will happen if the House comes to a different opinion on the Mueller reports, evidence?** Once the (majority of the) report is released, the House is free to do whatever it feels is necessary, including re-investigating everything that was already investigated. It would be unwise for them to do so. As reported by the Attorney General, the Special Counsel's office "...employed 19 lawyers who were assisted by a team of approximately 40 FBI agents, intelligence analysts, forensic accountants, and other professional staff. The Special Counsel issued more than 2,800 subpoenas, executed nearly 500 search warrants, obtained more than 230 orders for communication records, issued almost 50 orders authorizing use of pen registers, made 13 requests to foreign governments for evidence, and interviewed approximately 500 witnesses."[\[1\]](#JEnKT){#cite-JEnKT .citation_link} And after all of that evidence, the Special Counsel found that there was no Russian collusion. The Special Counsel did not render a decision on the question of obstruction of justice, and instead forwarded all of the information they gathered to the Attorney General. After his careful review, AG William Barr determined that there was not enough evidence that proved beyond a reasonable doubt that the President had obstructed justice, and so has decided not to impanel a grand jury. He made this decision without consideration as to the Constitutional question of whether or not you can even indict a sitting president. If the House proceeds with investigating again what was already investigated, those (including the President) who called the Special Counsel's investigation a "witch hunt" and "sour grapes" for his 2016 election victory will certainly conclude nothing better about this, and they would be justified in doing so. Nevertheless, the House is certainly free to do this (and Rep. Schiff has repeatedly asserted that he will be doing so, this information notwithstanding). *If they do*, and *if they come to a different conclusion*, they are free to file Articles of Impeachment for a vote of the full House. If the Articles of Impeachment pass the House, the Senate will hold a trial to determine whether or not the President should be removed from office.
