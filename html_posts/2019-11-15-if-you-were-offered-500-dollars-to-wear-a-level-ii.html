**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you were offered 500 dollars to wear a level III bulletproof vest and let someone empty a snub nosed 38 special revolver into you at short range for testing, would you volunteer?** No way. Not because I'm afraid of getting shot, but because if you believe the vest needs to be on a *person* instead of an *analog* in order to have a valid test, then your test is obviously flawed. Placing the vest on a human analog will provide exactly the same force feedback on the obverse side of the vest as a human chest would. Some human analogs actually have human skeletons and human organs inside of them to more accurately reproduce the force feedback that would result from a trauma to that analog. In other words, there is *simply no amount of money* that would necessitate shooting at a real person **for testing**. And even if there were an amount of money that would convince someone to allow themselves to be shot in a vest, \$500 is most certainly not it. You're missing quite a few zeros.
