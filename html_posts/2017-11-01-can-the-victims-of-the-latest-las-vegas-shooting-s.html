--- title: '"Can the victims of the latest Las Vegas shooting sue the National Rifle Association for the lax regulations on owning guns in the US?"' date: '2017-11-01' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: **no**. The NRA does not write regulations for owning guns in the United States. If you want to complain to someone for "lax regulations" (which I find absolutely hysterical), complain to Congress. They write the laws, not an affinity group of gun owners who also happens to have a lobbying arm (NRA Institute for Legislative Action). Any citizen or group, including the NRA, is entitled to express their opinions about what government does, does not, should, or should not do. This is the express purpose of the First Amendment. Should I try to sue Everytown, USA when someone can't buy a gun because regulations are too tight and then a robber kills them? Everytown, USA is no more responsible for what a robber does than the NRA is responsible for what the Las Vegas shooter did.
