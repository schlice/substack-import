--- title: "Hypothetical Scenario: If a police officer is attacking you and has a serious chance of killing you, do you have the legal right to defend yourself?" date: "2016-03-10" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Legally,* there are no circumstances in which you have the legal authority to resist a police officer in the performance of his duties. In fact, doing so gives the officer *more* authority, not *less*, to continue his assault. However, since we\'re talking about a very unlikely hypothetical, I think the assault would reach a point where you would have no choice but to defend yourself. The difference comes in the punishments. If the officer was legally carrying out his authority but just used excessive force, he might lose his job. If you defend yourself against a police officer, you are resisting arrest, and quite possibly assaulting a peace officer, and you\'re going to jail *and* you\'re a convicted felon.
