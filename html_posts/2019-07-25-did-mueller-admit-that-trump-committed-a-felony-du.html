**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Did Mueller admit that Trump committed a felony during the recent hearing?** Initially it seemed that way, but Mueller corrected that statement that made it seem like he did. There were two hearings; one before the House Judiciary Committee, and one before the House Intelligence Committee. In his first set of testimony to the Judiciary Committee, the following exchange took place: > LIEU: And I'd like to ask you the reason, again, that you did not > indict Donald Trump is because of OLC opinion stating that you cannot > indict a sitting president, correct? > > MUELLER: That is correct. Later that same day, Mueller testified again to the Intelligence Committee, and in his opening remarks, he said the following: > MUELLER: Now before we go to questions, I want to add one correction > to my testimony this morning. I want to go back to one thing that was > said this morning by Mr. Lieu who said, and I quote, \"You didn\'t > charge the President because of the OLC opinion." That is not the > correct way to say it. As we say in the report, and as I said at the > opening, we did not reach a determination as to whether the President > committed a crime. **Therefore, the reason that Mueller did not charge Trump with a crime is because he did not reach a determination whether or not Trump committed a crime.**
