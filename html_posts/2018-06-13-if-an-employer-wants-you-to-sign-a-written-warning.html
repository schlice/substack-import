**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If an employer wants you to sign a written warning but you disagree with it, should you sign it?** Generally in the US, signing a warning is only an acknowledgement that you received the warning. It is not an acceptance or agreement with the contents of the warning. Generally, the warning document also explains that your signature only verifies receipt of the warning, it does not imply agreement or acknowledgement of any wrongdoing. If the warning does not explicitly state the above, you can always write "SIGNED UNDER PROTEST" above your signature and then sign it, or you could refuse to sign it. Refusal to sign will be noted on the form by the person delivering the warning (usually your supervisor) and will be yet another red mark against you (the first one being the one triggering the warning in the first place). You might therefore consider refusal to acknowledge the warning as "strike two" which makes your future with that employer seem less bright.
