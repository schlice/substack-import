**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **No.** Rights are exactly that. They are the opposite of **privileges** in that they do not require action of any kind in order to gain them. **Rights** are granted based on membership in a group. Human rights are granted because of membership in a particular group: the human race. Therefore, human rights are universal to all humans simply by *being human*. **Privileges**, on the other hand, are *granted* by an entity, be that a government or a company (i.e. the privilege to drive granted by the state, or the privilege to enter a club based on membership). You absolutely can be required to pay something or own something in order to be granted a privilege.
