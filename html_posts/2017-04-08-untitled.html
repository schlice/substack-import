Yes, it is true that both the addict and the user/abuser both begin in the same place: a poor choice. Everyone makes poor choices in their lifetime. It happens. And on a side note, your absolutist opinions about addicts are quite disturbing, and pretty closed minded. The problem comes **after** that first poor decision. In the brain of the addict, the system which controls choice is broken. It causes addicts to *inappropriately* assign more importance to the drug than would normally be assigned. The drug becomes more important than sleep, food, sex, hygiene, etc. in the primitive portion of the brain. Being an addict is not a choice. It is a disease *of choice.* Addicts should not be treated like criminals. They should be treated like patients.
