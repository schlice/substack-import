**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is a really tough question. In truth, there is nothing *proven* to put MS in reverse, yet. Anecdotally, there are many examples of people doing just that, either with a combination of drugs and diet, or even diet alone. Just take a look at [[Dr. Terry Wahls](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=172475&url=https://terrywahls.com/about/about-terry-wahls/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="terrywahls.com"}]{.qlink_container}, who overcame her own MS through a very strict diet called [[the Wahls Protocol](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d5f415&url=https://terrywahls.com/the-wahls-protocol/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="terrywahls.com"}]{.qlink_container}. And there are some therapies which promise to stop or even reverse the disease. One such promising therapy is called [[HSCT](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=118598&url=https://www.nationalmssociety.org/Research/Research-News-Progress/Stem-Cells-in-MS/Bone-Marrow-Stem-Cell-Transplant-%25E2%2580%2593-HSCT){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container}. Now, I must caution you: **we do not advocate stopping your MS therapy drugs to go on a diet.** We know that diet and exercise can help you feel better, have more energy, and generally live a better life with MS, but **using diet alone to control your MS has not yet been proven to be effective**. Maybe someday it will be, and Dr. Wahls and others like her will be vindicated, but today, there is no diet out there (and there are several specifically targeted for MS) that have been proven to control the disease process. And as far as HSCT is concerned, it is still being evaluated for its benefit in stopping MS, as it is not 100% effective in all cases, and the protocol comes with *significant* risk. The treatment has already been approved in the US, but not for MS. There is growing evidence that HSCT is not for everyone (as I said, it's not 100% effective in stopping the disease), and research today is focusing on which MS patients might benefit the most from the treatment. Initially, it appears to be very effective for a certain subset of patients. So for now, our advice remains the same today as it did ten years ago: **eat right, exercise, and take an approved MS therapy drug.** This gives you the best chance to control the disease, reduce the duration and severity of relapses, and to live the best life you can with MS. \ Original question: What is the best way to put multiple sclerosis in reverse or remission?
