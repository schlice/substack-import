**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You have a few inaccuracies in here which are causing your confusion. 1\. You said that because only Jesus was without sin, it implies that humanity is "inherently flawed." **But this is incorrect.** Humanity was created with the *potential* to sin. This potential, which God absolutely adores and promotes and protects, is called **free will**. Free will, if we are truly free, means that we have the *free choice* to pursue good or evil, with zero consequences in this life. So in this life, I can choose to *kiss* a puppy or *kill* a puppy, and my life continues on without interruption (human consequences notwithstanding). God allows me to choose good or to choose evil. He desires for us to choose good, but He allows us to choose evil, because that is the very nature of free will. 2\. God does not condemn anyone to hell, not even the flawed. **People choose hell on their own.** Those who make a conscious decision to reject God will be eternally separated from Him, which is a torment beyond any I can think of. As in point 1, those who choose evil choose hell as well. That's their choice. Their free will separates them from God.
