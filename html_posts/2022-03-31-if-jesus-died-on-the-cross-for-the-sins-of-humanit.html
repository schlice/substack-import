**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They already do. It doesn't cost you anything to gain your salvation. Jesus is literally standing there, holding a gift in front of you. ***All you have to do is take it***. Even when someone offers you a gift, *you still have to take it*. There is some action required on your part to accept the gift Jesus gave you. But, to your point, it's *free*. It literally costs you nothing. To accept the gift, you have to do two things: **repent** and **believe**. **Repentance** is a "turning away" from our sins. It's recognizing what you've done, turning away from those actions, and asking Jesus to forgive you. Then, you need to **believe** that Jesus is your savior, who died on the cross to take the punishment for your sins, that you deserve. He did this *willingly*, no one made him do it. *But shouldn't everyone just "get" the free pass? Why do they have to do something?* God gave us something else when he created us: free will. We have the freedom to either accept his gift, or reject it. He will never force you, for love can only be given freely, it cannot be coerced. He left enough evidence in this world of his existence for anyone willing to believe in him, but he also left enough ambiguity so as to not compel the unwilling. Understand that this wasn't a mistake; he did this intentionally. You must accept him freely. *You must be willing to reach out and take the gift.* It will not be forced on you; you will not get the gift without willingly accepting it. \ To be fair though, there might be a cost to accepting your salvation. Because once you're saved, your life will change. The old ways of thinking will fade away. Desires for sinful things will fade. Your desires will change; your thoughts will change; your actions will change. Depending on how you lived before, the things you spent your time on, the friends you spent your time with, and the thoughts you spent your day thinking, may change. In short, it is possible that *your entire life* may change. This is the impetus for the phrase: *salvation is free, but it could cost you your life.* But considering the eternal consequences, it is literally *the best gift that anyone has ever given to anyone else in the history of the world,* and it's yours. For free. \ Original question: If Jesus died on the cross for the sins of humanity, wouldn\'t everyone have a free pass into heaven?
