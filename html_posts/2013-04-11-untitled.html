**Content:** [You would have higher-pressure air in your lungs compared to the air pressure outside your lungs. When the plane depressurizes, you make a grunting sound, kinda like getting hit in the gut with a basketball but without the pain. You exhale without exhaling. Remember that your lungs work like balloons and they draw in air when your diaphragm moves down increasing the size of the chest cavity, causing a negative pressure in the lungs and causing air to enter and inflate the lungs. So the ability to breathe is very dependent on the ambient air pressure and an equalization of pressure between the air in your lungs and the air outside of your body.\ \ If you rapidly decrease the pressure of the surrounding air, your lungs now contain higher pressure air than your surroundings, and will be wanting to escape, involuntarily if necessary. You\'d have to hold your breath and grab your nose in order to keep that air in, and you\'d then risk other injury, such as a ruptured eardrum.]{.rendered_qtext}
