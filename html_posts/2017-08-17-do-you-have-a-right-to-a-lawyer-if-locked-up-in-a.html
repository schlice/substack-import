--- title: '"Do you have a right to a lawyer if locked up in a police cell?"' date: '2017-08-17' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. You have the right to an attorney if you are questioned by the police. You don't have to answer any questions, and you can have a lawyer present when and if you do. But just being in a cell with no questions being asked of you? No, that's not when your lawyer needs to see you.
