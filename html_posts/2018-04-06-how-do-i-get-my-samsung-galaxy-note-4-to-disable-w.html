**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I get my Samsung Galaxy Note 4 to disable waking the screen when I receive notifications from apps? I want the light to turn on, but not the whole screen.** There are several ways you can prevent the screen from turning on when you receive notifications. Try them all out and see what works best for you. Note that I'm not going to give you all the breadcrumbs through Settings because the breadcrumbs are different depending on Android version. You should be able to search Settings to find what you're looking for. Or search Google for the feature and your Android version and you'll find the breadcrumb trail to get you there. 1. The setting you're really looking for is called *Ambient Display* settings and it's usually under Settings \> Display. You can turn that off, and then Android won't wake the display when you get a notification. 2. You can modify how notifications for each app appear under Settings \> Notifications. You can enable/disable notifications for a specific app, or make them appear "silently" which means they will still show up in the notifications list, but they won't pop a notification onto the screen. 3. More obscure would be to change how Gmail presents you with notifications (i.e. use the Priority Inbox) or how Email presents you with notifications (i.e. use the VIP setting), but these are a little more difficult to configure, and they are highly dependent on how you use email, and they do nothing for other alerts like Snapchat or SMS texts.
