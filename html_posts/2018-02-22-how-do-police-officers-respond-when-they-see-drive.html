**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do police officers respond when they see drivers flash their headlights to warn other drivers that there is a cop ahead? Is it illegal to do so?** [[Roger Curtiss](https://www.quora.com/profile/Roger-Curtiss-1)]{.qlink_container} has the right answer here. "Flashing your lights" by **turning them on and off** *may be illegal in some states* as some states have a statute which requires motorists to have headlights on continuously during periods of reduced visibility or darkness. So if you're flashing your lights in this way during a period of reduced visibility or darkness, you could be cited under that law. "Flashing your lights" by using the "flash to pass" function where pulling the turn signal stalk momentarily turns on your high beams is likely not illegal in most states. Plus as Roger mentioned, part of the goal of police monitoring speed is to "encourage" compliance with the speed limit. Drivers warning other drivers of police activity is no different than using Waze and marking police locations, or (back in the day) using CB radio and calling out a "smokey" when you saw one. Any one of these activities accomplishes that goal, so honest police organizations (who are not trying to make revenue off of speeding tickets) should acknowledge this.
