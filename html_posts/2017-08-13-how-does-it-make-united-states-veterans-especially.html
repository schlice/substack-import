Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *I disapprove of what you say, but I will defend to the death your > right to say it.\ > -Evelyn Beatrice Hall* Dissent and differences of opinion are healthy and normal in a well-functioning society. The ability to express your differing opinions is a function of a free society. Unfortunately, the political climate which has been fostered over the last eight years and capped off by the election of a contentious figure has ignited the passions of those who were already easily swayed by such dramas. So normal differences of opinion morph into violence at the drop of a hat. I don't feel sad that a protest and a counter-protest took place. I feel proud that I live in a place where such things are legal and that the protesters are not immediately hauled off to jail for daring to express an opinion. That's a sign of a well-functioning free society. I **do** feel sad that a protest and a counter-protest so easily devolved into violence. That's a sign that we have allowed our passions to take over for our logic.
