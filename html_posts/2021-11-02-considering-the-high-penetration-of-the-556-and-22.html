{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The weapon or ammunition you use does not change Rule 4 of the Big 4 Gun Safety Rules: **Know your target and what is behind your target**. Yes, something which classically over-penetrates may not be the best choice for an apartment dweller, but I won\'t deny them that choice. Rule 4 still stands though, if that\'s what they choose. Don\'t forget that a pistol can still over-penetrate, so it\'s not like pistol users can ignore Rule 4. A single family home may be different though. It might be easier to ensure nothing is behind your target. Plus, there is a lot to be said for accuracy in a neighborhood. I want to hit what I am shooting at, and I have the best chance at that using a rifle. \ Original question: Considering the high penetration of the 5.56 and 223, why would someone use an AR15 for self defense in a neighborhood or apartment? I would personally use a shotgun or pistol.
