--- title: '"If Democrats take back the House in 2018, what will likely happen as a result?"' date: '2018-02-05' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Several things will happen: 1. Nothing of any importance will pass Congress. The House will send bills to the Senate, and the Senate will ignore them. 1. In concert, the President will have a great deal of cause to harangle Congress for not "getting anything done." 2. The House will, most likely, impeach the President. The Senate won't remove him from office. If Clinton can lie to Congress and not be removed from office, Trump won't be removed from office for "obstruction of justice" which legally can't be a crime that he can commit.\* These two are pretty solid predictions. Remember that the House only needs a simple majority to impeach, but the Senate needs 2/3 to remove from office. \* Obstruction of justice is a crime. The President is in charge of the Executive branch, and is therefore responsible for personnel actions. Therefore, **even if** he maliciously fired Comey with the specific intent to stop the investigation into himself (which it can be argued that he did not), he cannot be convicted of obstruction of justice for performing a Constitutionally-authorized job function. That said, he can still be impeached for obstruction of justice, since impeachment is a political function, not a legal one.
