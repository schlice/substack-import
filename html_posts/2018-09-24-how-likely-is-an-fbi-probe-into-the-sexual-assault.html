**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How likely is an FBI probe into the sexual assault allegations against Brett Kavanaugh?** Extremely unlikely. First of all, the Senate Judiciary Committee has no authority to direct the FBI to investigate the allegations. The FBI does not work for Congress, and Congress does not have the authority to direct the FBI to do any independent investigations. If Congress learns of a Federal crime, they can indeed refer the evidence of that crime to the FBI for investigation, but there is no evidence that a Federal crime took place here. Secondly, the FBI does *background investigations* on Supreme Court nominees, **at the behest and the direction of the nominator.** In other words, the FBI works for the White House. It has already conducted multiple background investigations on Judge Kavanaugh and it has presented that information to the White House. The only way that the FBI is even allowed to investigate these allegations is if the White House decides to re-open the latest background investigation on Judge Kavanaugh and to direct them to investigate this new allegation. The White House has already stated its reluctance to re-open the investigation, as it is virtually impossible to determine without any evidence and with spotty recollection of over 36 years ago whether or not the allegations are credible. Now, before you hit the comments to trash me for calling Dr. Ford a liar, I can tell you that she most likely did not lie. However, her memory is likely faulty, and several pieces of evidence show this to be the case. Therefore, unless the White House has a change of heart, there will be no FBI investigation into the allegations against Judge Kavanaugh.
