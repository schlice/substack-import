**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'm a Christian and I really despise these "did you know" questions. **OF COURSE THEY KNOW THAT THEY CAN'T PROVE THAT GOD DOES NOT EXIST.** But in exactly the same way and using exactly the same tools, **we also cannot prove that God exists.** Of course, the reason for that is, in that scenario, the tools we both are trying to use are wrong. Science can never prove or disprove the existence of God, because God exists outside of what science can detect. It's like trying to use an X-ray machine to find a plastic spoon. Science is simply the wrong tool. We can prove that God exists using logic and reason. This is evidence gained through *inductive* reasoning, and as such, can never be 100% certain. Atheists, however, reject these proofs as lacking, despite the fact that we can prove God exists beyond what we would consider reasonable doubt. The difference between our two camps is what we both consider *reasonable.* **And that, dear questioner, is** ***by design.*** > See, God designed us with **free will**. And the beauty of free will > in God's plan is that if you're not willing to accept Christianity, > then you're free to reject it. This freedom to make choices---even the > freedom to reject truth---is what makes us moral creatures and enables > each of us to choose our own destiny. > > This really hits at the heart of why we exist at all, and why God > might not be as forthcoming in revealing Himself to us as you would > like. > > For if the Bible is true, then God has provided each of us with the > opportunity to make an eternal choice to either accept Him or reject > Him. And in order to ensure that our choice is truly free, He puts us > in an environment that is filled with evidence of His existence, but > without his *direct* presence---a presence so powerful that it could > overwhelm our freedom and thus negate our ability to reject Him. > > In other words, **God has provided enough evidence in this life to > convince anyone** ***willing to believe*, yet He has also left some > ambiguity so as not to compel the** ***unwilling*.** > > In this way, God gives us the opportunity either to love Him or to > reject Him without violating our freedom. In fact, the purpose of this > life is to make that choice freely and without coercion. For love, by > definition, must be freely given. It cannot be > coerced.[\[1\]](#zFRwD){#cite-zFRwD .citation_link} **TL;DR: Yes, atheists know full well that they can't prove God doesn't exist.
