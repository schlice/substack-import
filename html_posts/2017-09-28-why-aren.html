**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are several reasons. 1. Bandwidth. That's a lot of image traffic flowing over wireless networks. 2. Manpower. Monitoring video feeds is very manpower intensive. 3. Return on investment. Very few situations, if any at all, that require immediate assistance are *not* reported by the officer. The officers usually do a very good job letting dispatchers know that they need help. So monitoring dashcams live doesn't get help to them any faster.
