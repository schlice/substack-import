--- title: '"Can damages be claimed for having had the identity of your biological parents revealed by third parties -especially the media-?"' date: '2015-11-13' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [You can claim whatever you want at whatever time you want. The question is, does your case have any legs? Is there any merit to what you\'re asking?\ \ And to determine that, you first have to show some harm.\ \ If there is some demonstrable harm caused by the adopted person learning the identity of his/her biological parents, and it was a fact that was supposed to remain private, you may have a case for pain and suffering.\ \ I think a better tack may be to look at privacy laws surrounding adoptions. Revelation of birth parents is tightly controlled, and is only ever allowed if both parties separately agree through a neutral third party (usually the adoption agency). So there may be some breach of privacy laws you may be able to use. The damages for pain and suffering can be tacked on to the privacy case, and you won\'t have to show harm for cause at that point.\ \ I would have the adopted person consult with an attorney in his/her local area to find out whether or not there is a case to pursue.]{.rendered_qtext}
