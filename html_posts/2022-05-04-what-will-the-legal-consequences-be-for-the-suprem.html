Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Probably none. The drafts are not classified like information at the DoD, and previous leakers have been prosecuted for leaking classified information. If the leaker was an employee in one of the Justice's offices, the person *could potentially* be charged with theft of government property if they weren't authorized to have it. If the leaker was a clerk (themselves credentialed lawyers), they would probably be disbarred. Regardless, whoever the person was (a clerk or another employee in one of the Justice's offices), they will lose their jobs and will likely never work in law again. Breaching trust is not looked upon kindly by the profession. Interestingly, if the leaker was actually one of the Justices, there really is no "crime" other than breach of SCOTUS protocol. The only real punishment they could face would be impeachment and removal from the Court.
