--- title: '"Is the new Real identification law requirements going to affect all of the United States, or just California?"' date: '2019-02-11' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The [[Real ID Act](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=789bf1&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_ID_Act){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} is far from new. The provisions which affect the states were originally supposed to go into effect in 2008, but it was postponed for **five years** to get more buy-in from the states. The Act only affects the states insofar as the Federal government has set minimum standards that states must follow in order for their state identifications to be accepted by the Federal government for official purposes. Official purposes include things like boarding commercial aircraft, and gaining access to nuclear power plants and Federal buildings.
