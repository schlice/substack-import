--- title: "Can a cop search me without probable cause or reasonable suspicion?" date: "2022-07-19" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer to the question is no. The longer answer is no, but it's hard to know whether or not they have probable cause or reasonable suspicion because they don't have to tell you. In the landmark case *Terry v. Ohio*, the court allowed the police to do a cursory pat-down of a person in order to check for weapons to ensure their own safety while they continued asking questions. This cursory pat-down is called a *Terry* *stop*, and it only requires reasonable suspicion that a crime was committed or is going to be committed. It also only permits a quick pat-down for weapons, not a complete search. If the police have probable cause to arrest you, they can do a complete search. But here's the catch: **they are under no obligation to tell you why they have reasonable suspicion or probable cause.** Once you're arrested and you go to your arraignment hearing, the judge asks the police officer to detail his or her reasonable suspicion and/or probable cause. *That is where the police officer must articulate the reasonable suspicion and/or probable cause*. They don't have to tell you, they only have to tell the judge. That information will be in the arrest report, but the judge can question the officer on it if he or she chooses. If the officer did not have reasonable suspicion to conduct a *Terry* stop, anything found during that stop is suppressed and can't be used as evidence. If the officer did not have probable cause to effect an arrest, the arrest is suppressed and you are released from custody. You and your lawyer will work through all of those details. So if you're in a situation where the police may have unlawfully searched you, make sure you have a good criminal defense attorney and tell him or her everything. \ Original question: Can a cop search me without probable cause or reasonable suspicion?
