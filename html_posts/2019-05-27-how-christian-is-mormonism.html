**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I got into a disagreement with [[Collin Campbell](https://www.quora.com/profile/Collin-Campbell-20)]{.qlink_container} about this very topic on [[the War Elephant Discord](https://warelephant.quora.com/The-Discord-is-Live)]{.qlink_container}. I was very forceful in my argument, and I was, in fact, a big fat jerk. Collin, again, I apologize. I still feel bad about that exchange. I do, however, want to address the meat of my argument. How Christian is Mormonism? First, we must define our terms. What does it mean to be "Christian?" More importantly, who's to say? Me? A Mormon? Anyone? Can anyone make a definition of Christianity and it would stick? We have to have some universally-accepted standard to define what Christianity is, and is not. Thankfully, we do. In general, all Christians believe in the infallibility of the Bible. They all, to a person, believe that the Bible is the inspired word of God. They also believe that the Bible never contradicts itself. Therefore, if your religion has a belief, or a book, or a practice which is not in line with the teachings of the Bible, then it is right to question whether or not that belief or book or practice is "Christian." Mormonism teaches that god is an exalted man from another world who has a goddess wife who, together, produce spirit children, and that we ourselves have the potential to become gods of our own worlds. Not only are none of these beliefs in the Bible, but they are most decidedly violative of several core tenets of Christianity: - There is only one God.[\[1\]](#ptnrh){#cite-ptnrh .citation_link}[\[2\]](#FIJvg){#cite-FIJvg .citation_link}[\[3\]](#jVvnV){#cite-jVvnV .citation_link}[\[4\]](#HwtmV){#cite-HwtmV .citation_link} - The Trinity is one God in three persons (this would require a complete answer of its own, so I'm not going to provide references on it, but there are some really wonderful readings about the Trinity out there). - Jesus is one member of the Trinity, and He is divine.[\[5\]](#ysLzx){#cite-ysLzx .citation_link}[\[6\]](#qDCAu){#cite-qDCAu .citation_link} - Forgiveness of sins is by grace alone. No good works are required.[\[7\]](#UFmBg){#cite-UFmBg .citation_link}[\[8\]](#lFWjM){#cite-lFWjM .citation_link} - Jesus physically rose from the dead and was assumed into Heaven.[\[9\]](#trgxI){#cite-trgxI .citation_link}[\[10\]](#Kyric){#cite-Kyric .citation_link} Mormonism denies that there is only one God, and also denies that forgiveness of sins is through grace alone. Mormonism justifies the Book of Mormon's inconsistency with the Bible by stating that the Bible is the inspired word of God *insofar as it has been translated correctly*. That's a convenient excuse, but it is wholly unsupported by fact. Here's the facts. The Bible was written primarily by 40 different authors in three languages (Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek). It took approximately 1600 years to write, and is internally consistent throughout. The Bible is 98% textually pure. Through all the copying of the Biblical manuscripts of the entire Bible, only 2% has any question about it. Nothing in all of the ancient writings of the entire world approaches the accuracy of the Biblical documents. The 2% that is in question does not affect doctrine. The areas of interest are called *variants*, and they consist mainly in variations of wording and spelling. The New Testament (NT) has over 5,000 supporting Greek manuscripts existing today with another 20,000 manuscripts in other languages. Some of the manuscript evidence dates to within 100 years of the original writing. There is less than a 2% textual variation in the NT manuscripts. No work which has been "so poorly translated" has so much supporting documentation proving that it, and its translations, are textually consistent.
