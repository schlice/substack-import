**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Air Force Academy graduates have done it all. Almost every civilian job conceivable. We have everything from Members of Congress to scientists, lawyers, and doctors. Most recently, this doctor: [[Dallas surgeon: \'This killing, it has to stop\'](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fb27b6&url=http://www.cnn.com/2016/07/11/us/emotional-dallas-dr-williams/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cnn.com"}]{.qlink_container} Dr. Brian H. Williams is a '91 grad from USAFA. I am proud and honored to say I served with him. We have had Rhodes Scholars and astronauts and government officials at almost every level of government. We even have sports coaches of NBA teams, such as the San Antonio Spurs: [[Gregg Popovich](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=1f38ba&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregg_Popovich){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} Gregg is a 1970 graduate. We have had Olympians, All-American athletes, and even a few NFL players came from our ranks [[Chad Hennings](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3b9811&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad_Hennings){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} Chad is a 1988 grad. Basically, what I'm saying is that graduating from USAFA doesn't prepare you for ***a*** job. It prepares you for ***any*** job. Because if you have what it takes to graduate from the Academy, you have what it takes to do ***anything you want with your life.***
