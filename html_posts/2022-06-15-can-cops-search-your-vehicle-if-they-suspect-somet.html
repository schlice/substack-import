--- title: "Can cops search your vehicle if they suspect something illegal is inside it?" date: "2022-06-15" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is *maybe*. And it depends on what you mean by "suspect." Why do they suspect something illegal is in it? Their suspicion must amount to what we call "probable cause." When it comes to anything besides cars, probable cause must be the basis for obtaining a search warrant. But there is an exception for cars. The police can search your car without a warrant if they have probable cause to believe that evidence or contraband is in the vehicle. Cars are excepted because there is "a lower expectation of privacy because of the regulations under which they operate. Also, the ease of mobility creates an inherent exigency to prevent the removal of evidence and contraband."[\[1\]](#SlscF){#cite-SlscF .citation_link} However, and any time a police officer wants to search a car, he must have **probable cause**. Probable cause is "a reasonable amount of suspicion, supported by circumstances sufficiently strong to justify a prudent and cautious person\'s belief that certain facts are probably true\"[\[2\]](#PYYrA){#cite-PYYrA .citation_link} So **if a police officer** **orders you to get out of the car so they can search**, allow them to do so and then tell your lawyer. **But if a police officer** ***asks*** **if he can search your vehicle, he does not have probable cause.** If he had probable cause, he'd yank you out of the car and search it. If he's asking you for permission, he does not have probable cause. You can politely tell him to go pound sand and to get a warrant. \ Original question: Can cops search your vehicle if they suspect something illegal is inside it?
