**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **When you block someone\'s number, do you have to keep it in your phone?** The answer is "it depends." Some carriers include a block list in their carrier system which integrates with your phone, so that when you block a number in your phone, they add it to your carrier's block list. Different software (iOS vs Android) handles this in different ways, so it's not useful to go through that here. The best advice I can give you if you want to pursue this for your phone and your carrier is to contact your carrier's technical support department and ask them how it works. It's so different per device and carrier combination that you will be best served by working with your carrier. Phone handsets do have block lists built in, so if you use that feature, the number will stay "in your phone" but in a protected area for blocked numbers. It's not a full-fledged contact entry, it's just a special list off to the side of numbers that you have chosen to block. Regardless of how it is blocked, If you get a call or a text from a number in your blocked list, it won't ring and the text message will not appear in your texts.
