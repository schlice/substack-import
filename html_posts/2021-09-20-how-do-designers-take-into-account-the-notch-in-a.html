Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In addition to the other answers, the ejection of a shell does not involve the movement of enough mass to make a difference against the inertia imparted by the firearm itself and your holding of it. Therefore, the kinetic energy of the shell casing would be pushing against both the mass of the firearm and your mass. Therefore, the action of ejecting a cartridge should not impart enough kinetic energy to cause a large enough motion in the firearm (or you) to need to worry about. \ Original question: How do designers take into account the notch in a handgun where spent shells are ejected, as it relates to throwing the barrel offline during recoil?
