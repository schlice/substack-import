--- title: "What is the difference between a high end and a flagship?" date: "2017-01-03" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "High-end" describes the technology, and "flagship" describes a marketing strategy. "High-end" devices have some of the newest technology in them, and generally some of the priciest. Most postpaid customers will be provided with high-end devices. Devices which are not "high-end" devices may have older, previous-generation, or lower-quality technology in them, built for a lower price-point. MVNO devices or pre-paid devices often fall into this "not-high-end" category. "Flagship" devices showcase a carrier's technology offerings. When a carrier decides on a "flagship" device, they devote marketing dollars (advertising, signage, commercials, etc) to the sale of that device. Note that a "flagship" device will virtually always be a "high-end" device, but not all "high-end" devices are a carrier's "flagship" device. Carriers may carry a number of "high-end" devices, but generally only one from each manufacturer will be the "flagship" device. Example: Samsung invested heavily in making the Note 7 the "flagship" device for their GALAXY line of phones. While the GALAXY S7 is still a high-end device, the Note 7 was the flagship device. Until it started exploding.
