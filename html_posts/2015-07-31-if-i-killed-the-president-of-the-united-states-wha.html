Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Killing the President is murder, just as killing your next-door neighbor is murder. In most jurisdictions, killing the President may not be considered capital murder or first-degree murder. Generally, first-degree murder is charged when you kill:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - A police officer, firefighter, or paramedic - A child - During the commission of another violent felony, such as burglary, sexual assault, kidnapping, etc (known as the \"felony-murder rule\")\ - Multiple times in a single incident\ - As a murder-for-hire However, because of the nature of the victim, a first-degree murder charge may be sought.\ \ Capital murder or first-degree murder generally carries a life sentence without the possibility of parole, or the death penalty, depending on the state.
