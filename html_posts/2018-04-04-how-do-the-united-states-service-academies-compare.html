**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do the United States service academies compare to Ivy League schools academically?** Interestingly, US News and World Reports does an annual assessment and ranking of public undergraduate liberal arts colleges. The *Military Times* newspaper discussed this year's rankings with an emphasis on our military service academies: > The Naval Academy maintained service-school bragging rights in the > annual U.S. News [[Best > Colleges](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a932a9&url=http://www.usnews.com/education/best-colleges/articles/us-news-ranks-best-colleges?int%3Da14709%26int%3Da14709){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="usnews.com"}]{.qlink_container} rankings released Tuesday, > taking top honors among public national liberal arts colleges. > > The U.S. Military Academy finished second and the Air Force Academy > completed the podium sweep for the 2017 edition. The schools ranked > tied for 12th, tied for 19th and tied for 32nd, respectively, among > all national liberal arts colleges \-- a category that rules out > larger, research-oriented schools like most public universities and > Ivy League stalwarts. > > The 1-2-3 order of finish was the [[same as last > year](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f666a1&url=https://www.armytimes.com/story/military/2015/09/09/navy-beats-army-us-news-college-rankings/71951714/){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="armytimes.com"}]{.qlink_container}, when Navy ranked ninth > overall, Army took 22nd and Air Force was > 29th.[\[1\]](#ykpru){#cite-ykpru .citation_link} So compared to the Ivy Leagues, military academies simply don't compare, because they don't offer higher degrees than Bachelor's degrees. But considering the magnitude of the additional training received at a military academy, and the fact that besides the military academies, no school ranked ahead of USAFA in the list has an annual tuition under \$47,900 (service academy cadets are paid for by US tax dollars), I think the ranking is not only respectable, but actually quite good for what we pay.
