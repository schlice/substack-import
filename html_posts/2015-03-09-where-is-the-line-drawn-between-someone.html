**Content:** [You are not allowed to lie about someone in a way that causes harm to the person that you lied about. That is the definition of slander. If you lie about someone and they lose their job because of it, that is actionable slander.\ \ If the statements you say are actually true and you can prove it, you have every right to say them. But if they are lies (called oral defamation) that causes harm to the person being defamed, it is considered slander, and you can face civil punishments.\ \ Some slanderous statements are actionable *per se*, because the harm is obvious, like saying that someone has a bad disease, or that someone can\'t perform their job, or that someone committed a crime.\ \ (EDIT) Thanks to [[Catherine Beale](https://www.quora.com/profile/Catherine-Beale)]{.qlink_container} for catching my Latin error which actually changed the text of what I was going for.
