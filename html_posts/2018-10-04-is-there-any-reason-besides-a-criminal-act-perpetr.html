**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there any reason (besides a criminal act perpetrated while sitting on the bench) that a Supreme Court Justice would be removed? Has anyone ever been removed for cause from the Supreme Court?** Let's start with the second question first. Only one US Supreme Court justice has ever been impeached: Samuel Chase in 1804. Justice Chase was acquitted by the Senate. Therefore, the answer is no, no Justice has ever been removed from the Supreme Court through the impeachment process. The Constitution says that Supreme Court justices "shall hold their offices during good behavior." This language, along with the "high crimes and misdemeanors" language used for impeachment of Presidents, is intentionally vague. It allows for interpretation by Congress, even though the "accepted" definition of an impeachable offense is something that you could be convicted of in court. However, President (then Representative) Gerald R. Ford said in the House on 15 Apr 1970, in comments surrounding possible impeachment of Justice William O. Douglas: "\[A\]n impeachable offense is whatever a majority of the House of Representatives considers \[it\] to be at a given moment in history; conviction results from whatever offense or offenses two-thirds of the other body considers to be sufficiently serious to require removal of the accused from office." So to answer the first question: A Supreme Court Justice may be impeached if the House deems that the Justice has committed an impeachable offense. That Justice can then be removed from office if 2/3 of the Senate believes that the offense was sufficiently serious as to require his or her removal from office.
