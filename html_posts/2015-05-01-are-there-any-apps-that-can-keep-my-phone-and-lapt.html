**Content:** [It certainly depends on what you mean by \"keeping my phone and laptop in sync.\"\ \ If you\'re looking at syncing data files between your phone and your laptop, check out [[Syncplicity](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2ce5d2&url=http://www.syncplicity.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="syncplicity.com"}]{.qlink_container}. It is 100% cross-platform, so it will work anywhere.\ \ If you are looking to sync calendar, contacts, and emails between phone and laptop, use the Google infrastructure to do it (i.e. Google Calendar, Google Contacts, Gmail, etc). If you use Google Drive, you can also then keep files synced too. If you have an Android-based phone, these sync features with the Google infrastructure are baked into the operating system. iPhone can certainly sync with Google as well, but Android has a leg up there since you don\'t have to install anything new to do it.]{.rendered_qtext}
