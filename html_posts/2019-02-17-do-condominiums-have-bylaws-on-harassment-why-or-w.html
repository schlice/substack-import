**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do condominiums have bylaws on harassment? why or why not?** Because bylaws and rules generally must follow a purpose, and that purpose is generally twofold: - Set the conditions so that one person's actions doesn't negatively impact the property value of someone else - Set up rules for use and maintenance of common property. Bylaws and rules are generally not re-writes of state or Federal law. Harassment is a blanket term which involves both criminal and civil activities. Some states define a criminal harassment law, but most states have civil harassment penalties. Harassment is not something which needs to be addressed in order to meet the property value or common property goals for which bylaws or rules and regulations are written. **TL;DR: Harassment is not within the scope of a Condo Association or HOA to address; it is for lawyers (or the police) to address. If you suspect harassment, get a lawyer. If your state has a criminal harassment law, call the police.**
