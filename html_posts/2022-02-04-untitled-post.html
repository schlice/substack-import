**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The municipal judge did what he was supposed to do, in cases where children want abortions without parental consent, and appeal to the court. Florida makes them prove they are intelligent enough to understand their choice. The judge evaluated everything the law told him to evaluate, and that included intelligence. She claimed she was a B student but her grades showed she had a 2.0 GPA (which, in case you don't know letter grades, is not a B). The judge said because of that, she was either lying, or stupid. Or, in judge-speak, "she either has a lack of intelligence, or a lack of credibility." She won on appeal, because the local court judge did not allow her to clarify her statement. Yes, she had a 2.0 GPA, but she was now making B's and was hoping to pull up her GPA. She acknowledged she was not ready to be a mother. She also researched, and found she did not want to carry the baby to term to give it up for adoption, and she also stated she wanted to consult with her doctor (which also requires parental consent, or a court order). Hope that helps to clear it up a little. \ Original question: \"either a lack of intelligence or credibility\", is why a judge denied a 17 yr old an abortion without parental consent. This is in Florida. Would a lack of those attributes be a good reason to become a parent at any age? She did appeal and won.
