**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(Despite what the "world" thinks it knows, this answer is specific to the US only)* **Because "crime" is a pretty big bucket, and it contains more than just murder.** And the reason that these numbers are changing is because of *the effects of the events surrounding COVID-19.* In 2020, the murder rate (the number of murders per 100,000 people) rose sharply, by almost 30%. Assaults were up as well, by almost 10%. And the increases were even across both red and blue states. The number of murders was also up: > Relative to 2019, the number of murders jumped by more than 30 percent > in the largest cities and by 20 percent in places designated by the > FBI as "suburban" --- cities with fewer than 50,000 inhabitants that > are within a Metropolitan Statistical Area. Murders rose by comparable > levels in rural areas too --- an important fact that is only now > beginning to receive press attention.[\[1\]](#lsDxe){#cite-lsDxe > .citation_link} However, other types of crime were *down*. Property crimes reached a record *low* in 2020. There could be many reasons for this, but lockdowns and people losing their jobs (thus spending more time at home) could be a contributing factor. None of this changes the fact that *overall crime has been declining for the past decade.* **TL;DR:** ¿Por qué no los dos? Overall crime is indeed down over the last decade, while murders are up almost 30% in 2020.
