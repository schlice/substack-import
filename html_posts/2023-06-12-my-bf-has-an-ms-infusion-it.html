**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The infusion room is generally pretty quiet, filled with a bunch of La-Z-Boy (or equivalent) reclining armchairs. They're big, comfy chairs that the patient will sit in while they are being infused. The nurse will set you both up in the room, and she'll check you in, and then she'll place a central line into (usually) the veins on the back of one of his hands. Then she'll go off and retrieve the medication from secure storage (it's expensive and can only be administered to the people who are prescribed to get it). When she returns, she'll hang the bag on the infuser, type in the infusion rate, and start the infusion. And that's about as exciting as it gets. You can read, talk (quietly, there are usually others in the infusion room receiving infusions as well), eat, or otherwise just relax. When it's done, they'll usually make you wait for a short time to observe for any reactions, but then you're off. The medication will determine exactly how long the infusion will take. It won't be more than a few hours. It's a pretty underwhelming experience, to be honest.
