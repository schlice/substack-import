**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Be wary of the logical fallacy called "hasty generalization." You're taking what *some* Christian parents do and you are generalizing and assuming that *all* Christian parents do this, when you have no evidence of this. I will absolutely agree with you that *some* Christian parents do this, because they themselves never question the Bible. There is an entire discipline within religion called ***apologetics.*** Apologetics is the practice of defending religious beliefs through discourse and systematic argumentation. It\'s also a branch of Christian theology that studies and defends Christianity (commonly referred to as Christian Apologetics, for obvious reasons). Apologetics involves using historical, evidential, and reasonable facts to defend Christian theology. For example, apologetics might consider if the Bible is trustworthy, if God exists, and how to know if Jesus died. In my own apologetics studies, I have examined each of these topics. Not every Christian studies apologetics, because **doing so is not necessary for salvation.** Perhaps you think it to be intellectually lazy, and you are entitled to that opinion. However, the truth that apologetics are not required for salvation doesn't change based on your opinion.
