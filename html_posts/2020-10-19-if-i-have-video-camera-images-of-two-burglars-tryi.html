**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If I have video camera images of two burglars trying to break into my house, will the police do anything even though they were not successful in invading my house?** A crime was committed. It was an attempted burglary, which is a crime in nearly every jurisdiction in the United States. However, it is highly unlikely that the police will investigate the incident much beyond taking your statement and your camera footage. Without some concrete evidence proving the identity of the criminals, it is highly unlikely that the police can do much. **Are camera images deemed invasion of privacy?** No. There is no expectation of privacy on the outside of your home. Your security cameras are capturing areas on your property, and you as the property owner control the privacy of those areas. The persons who enter those areas are bound by your rules of privacy, except in areas on your property in which there is a reasonable expectation of privacy, like your bedroom or your bathroom.
