**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is called a *faithless elector*. What happens to you if you are a faithless elector is up to the state. If your state has faithless elector laws, you would be subject to the penalties for violating those laws. In 16 states, if you don't vote the way the state told you to vote, your vote gets cancelled and you get replaced. Five of those states impose a penalty on you for doing so. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-4290bc09f34c743369e041dafe090156){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-4290bc09f34c743369e041dafe090156" master_w="841" master_h="607"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*fairvote.org*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bad881&url=https://www.fairvote.org/faithless_elector_state_laws?gclid%3DCjwKCAjwtcCVBhA0EiwAT1fY75-_rq0-Z16J9BCwfowHTDqbUrDKIJ7cOqufgAVJ-x_RYZnMmzrrPRoCq9kQAvD_BwE){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="fairvote.org"}]{.qlink_container}*)* Under the [[Uniform Faithful Presidential Electors Act](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e8921e&url=https://www.uniformlaws.org/committees/community-home?CommunityKey%3D6b56b4c1-5004-48a5-add2-0c410cce587d){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="uniformlaws.org"}]{.qlink_container}, electors pledge to vote for a candidate, and a mechanism exists to replace them if they don't. \ Original question: What happens if any of the 538 electors in the US Electoral College decide they don\'t want to cast their vote for the candidate who has received the majority vote in their district?
