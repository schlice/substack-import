Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This whole Russia thing has been blown so far out of proportion, that it's bordering on the ridiculous. Let's look at **facts** and ignore hyperbole. - **What was done, if anything**? If anything at all was done, someone in the RNC (not likely Trump or Pence themseleves) colluded with Russia \*somehow\* and Russian hackers exposed e-mails from a private organization (the DNC) and gave them to WikiLeaks. The RNC staffer may have known that the Russians were attempting to hack the DNC. The staffer may have asked the Russians to get hackers to hack the DNC and give the details to WikiLeaks. The staffer may have even paid for those services. - **Is this a crime?** It depends on how the information was obtained. We're assuming that the RNC didn't actually gain access to the data themselves, but simply asked the Russians to do it for them. The level of collusion is also unknown. So the crime, if any, was "conspiracy to commit computer fraud." The level of evidence required to prove this crime is quite high, and I'm certain that the FBI does not possess such evidence. - **Does this rise to the level of "high crimes and misdemeanors" specified in the Constitution for impeachment?** It does not. First of all, it's highly unlikely that Trump or Pence were even personally involved, even if it did happen. Second, there is no affirmative "duty to report" a crime, so by not saying anything, *even if they knew,* they still did noting wrong. - **Did anyone "hack the election?"** This is just media hyperbole. Was anyone claiming that voting machines were tampered with by the Russians? No. Was anyone claiming that the Russians participated in voter suppression? No. Was anyone claiming that election results, vote counts, or ballot tallies were tampered with? No. There was no "election tampering" as we know it. There was definitely an information campaign which included WikiLeaks leaking e-mails from the DNC, a private organization. No government tampering is even alleged. So was the election "hacked?" Not even close. In fact, both organizations engage in information campaigns, sometimes including leaked information, just usually not leaked to WikiLeaks. It is for these reasons that I don't see how a charge rising to the level of "high crimes and misdemeanors" could even be levied against the President or Vice President.
