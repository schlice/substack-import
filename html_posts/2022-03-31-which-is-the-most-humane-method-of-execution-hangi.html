**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} [[Asphyxiation with inert gas.](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=641d13&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inert_gas_asphyxiation){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} We're not talking about breathing in toxic gases. Breathing in inert gas "tricks" the body into thinking that it is still breathing air, but the oxygen content of the air decreases. When humans feel *suffocation*, it's due to a rise in **carbon dioxide**. But simply breathing in inert gas (which is usually odorless and tasteless) instead of oxygen will cause *asphyxiation* (death from lack of oxygen) without feeling the traumatic effects of suffocation. The victim will simply get sleepy and will lose consciousness, eventually asphyxiating. Loss of consciousness in an oxygen-deprived environment typically occurs within one minute.
