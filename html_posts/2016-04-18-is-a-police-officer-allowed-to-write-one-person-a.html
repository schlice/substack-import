--- title: '"Is a police officer allowed to write one person a parking ticket and not another person in the same place?"' date: '2016-04-18' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} He is allowed to do so (as he has the authority to make judgment calls about whether or not a violation should be written), but it\'s not wise. He could get himself into trouble because of what we call *selective enforcement*. This is a defense which is often raised by a driver who claims the officer ignored others around him who were also violating the same law. To win with a "selective enforcement" defense, however, you have to take a huge ­additional step and show that the officer had a specific and improper ­motive to pick on you. Unless you can prove (not just allege) that the officer was unfairly targeting you alone, you won\'t be able to get by with a \"selective enforcement\" defense in traffic court. The reason is, regardless of what the officer did or did not do with other motorists, you were actually violating the statute in question, and the officer did observe you violating the statute. So the presence or absence of tickets given to other motorists does not change what happened in your particular case.
