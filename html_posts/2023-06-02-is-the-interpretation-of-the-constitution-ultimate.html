--- title: '"Is the interpretation of the constitution ultimately a matter of judges balancing their duties? Would SCOTUS overturn a gun law that actually prevented deaths without disproportionately inconveniencing lawful gun owners?"' date: '2023-06-02' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Constitution is interpreted dispassionately. Or, at least, it is supposed to be. The *effect* of laws is something that *lawmakers* take into consideration. The *Constitutionality* of laws is the only thing addressed by SCOTUS. So to answer the big question: > Would SCOTUS overturn a gun law that actually prevented deaths without > disproportionately inconveniencing lawful gun owners? The answer is **yes, if the law was unconstitutional.** *How* that elimination of that law played out is irrelevant to the decision. In other words, "preventing deaths" is not a valid reason to leave an unconstitutional law in place, just as "retaining a woman's access to legal abortions" is not a valid reason to leave an unconstitutional decision like *Roe* in place. If lawmakers want to continue to prevent deaths or retain access to legal abortion, they can write a new law which does not violate the Constitution.
