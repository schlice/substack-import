**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **When I was 13 years old, one of my close relative sexually abused me multiple times, then he grew old and supportive to me and my family while I was broke, now that my situation has changed, should I Sue him or forgive him?** That's not an "either/or." You can both forgive him **and** if grounds to do so exist (and you are still legally permitted after so much time), you can sue him. Start by checking with a personal injury lawyer in your area to see if you still have standing to sue. But start with forgiveness. You should not be harboring guilt, nor should you be harboring ill will. Vengeance belongs to God. Let Him worry about it. You need to move on with your life. That doesn't mean you forget what he did and welcome him back into your home and your life, but it does mean that you remove the anger and bitterness from your heart. Absolutely forgive him. Seek out survivor groups if you are sincere in your desire for forgiveness; they can help you navigate your feelings and reach a place where you're able to forgive. After you've done so, ask yourself if you've suffered so much that you think that exacting a monetary penalty is appropriate. If you do think so, then perhaps a personal injury lawsuit is appropriate. However, it does sound like a fairly significant amount of time has passed, and in most states, you don't have more than two to five years to file a personal injury lawsuit. Remember, in most states, you don't need actual, physical "injury" to file a lawsuit for assault and battery.
