Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not really. Most of the benefits of a pardon are conferred to someone who is alive to have their rights restored. It's possible that some rights of the next of kin may be restored with a pardon, but it's unlikely. Posthumous pardons are mostly symbolic gestures. \ Original question: Does a posthumous pardon have any real legal effect?
