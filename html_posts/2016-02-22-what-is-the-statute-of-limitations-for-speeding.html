--- title: "What is the statute of limitations for speeding?" date: "2016-02-22" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Varies by state, but in most states: - Speeding is not a \"prosecutable\" offense, as it is a moving violation, not even a misdemeanor. Most moving violations are punished with fines and \"points\" against your license. Accumulate enough points, and you can lose your license. Be aware, there are a few moving violations which are misdemeanors, such as reckless driving or reckless endangerment. Depending on what is involved, those might rise to the level of a felony. - In most states, moving violations must be \"observed by a peace officer\" in order to be issued. Which is why red-light cameras have required law changes in many places in order to come into existence. In some cases, one peace officer can observe the offense, and a second peace officer can issue the ticket with the first peace officer as the complaining official.
