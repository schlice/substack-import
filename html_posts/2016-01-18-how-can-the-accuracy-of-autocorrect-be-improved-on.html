Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Phones have to do more than substitute words that might be similar to your misspelled or unrecgonized word. They need to do grammatical matching (i.e. does the word I\'m about to substitute make grammatical sense in this sentence). Modern-day word processors do grammatical checking all the time; there is no reason that a suitably-powered processor on a phone (which we are rapidly approaching) could also check grammar as well as spelling. The convergence of those two is what produces a better spell check, and therefore, auto-correct.
