**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} ***NOTE: This is an answer about suicide in the realm of Christianity. It is not meant to discuss any one situation, nor is it meant to judge anyone who has committed suicide, or who is contemplating committing suicide.*** ***If you fall into the last category, and you are considering suicide, please seek help now. Please find the box titled "There is Help" and reach out to someone. In the US, please call 988 or 1-800-273-8255, the national hotline. Or get yourself to a hospital or call 911. Or go alert someone in your home, apartment, or workplace, or wherever you are.*** ***Do whatever it takes to get help.*** \ **TL;DR:** As far as Christians are concerned, what happens to them depends on whether they have accepted Christ as their savior while they were still alive. To be sure, Christians view suicide as a sin. God, as our Creator, is the only one who gets to decide when and how a person should die. Psalm 31 says, "My times are in your hands" (Psalm 31:15). God is the giver of life. He gives, and He takes away (Job 1:21). Therefore, ending your own life goes against God's plan for our life, and is therefore sinful. But here's the important part: while we believe suicide is a sin, it's just like any other sin we commit on a daily basis, with the notable exception that we don't get the opportunity to repent of this particular sin before we die. And if suicide is a sin like every other sin, then **committing the sin of suicide does not determine a person's eternal destiny.** Our eternal destiny rests solely on God's grace. Those who trust in Jesus Christ are fully forgiven of every sin, and they receive eternal life; those who reject Him remain condemned (John 3:16--18, 36; Ephesians 2:1--10). And once we have been saved, we do not lose our salvation because we have committed a sin. We face judgement for our sins, but we are still saved if we were saved before.
