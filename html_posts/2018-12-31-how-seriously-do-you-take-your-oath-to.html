**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How seriously do you take your oath to "support and defend the constitution of the United States; against all enemies foreign and domestic?** I take my oath so seriously that while I have been relieved of my post on Active Duty, I have never been relieved of my oath. As far as I am concerned, I've not been relieved of that particular duty. I will continue to support and defend the Constitution, and I am still prepared to give my life in its defense. **For officers, what does "without mental reservation, or purpose of evasion" mean to you?** The full sentence says, "I take this obligation freely, without any mental reservation or purpose of evasion." Therefore, mental reservation or purpose of evasion are modifiers to the first part of the sentence. To that end, "mental reservation" means I'm committed to my oath (which may be why I take it so seriously) and I do it consciously, without any doubts about what I'm doing, and "purpose of evasion" means exactly what it says: I take the obligation because I want to, not because I'm trying to evade some other responsibility.
