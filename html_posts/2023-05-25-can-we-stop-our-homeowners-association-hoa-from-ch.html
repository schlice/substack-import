--- title: "Can we stop our Homeowners Association (HOA) from charging us late fees if the board was in violation of their own bylaws and failed to have an annual meeting, as required by state law?" date: "2023-05-25" tags: ["law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, the two are unrelated. You still have to pay late fees if you are late on a dues or a fine payment. If the HOA failed to *even attempt* to have an annual meeting, you can sue the HOA for violating state law. Usually though, they don't "fail" to have a meeting, because the bylaws usually state that they have to "attempt" to have a meeting, which means they have to send out meeting notices and proxy forms to homeowners, who have the option of participating or not. In many cases, they don't. If the Board doesn't have enough attendance (both in-person and by proxy) to lawfully hold an annual meeting with the percentage of homeowners required in the bylaws, then they cannot hold an annual meeting. Whether or not they try again, and how many times they try, is also dependent on your individual governing documents. So it could happen that no annual meeting takes place, but it is perfectly legal.
