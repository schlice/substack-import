**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should the requirement of a national identification card be viewed more as a violation of privacy or states rights or neither?** It's not a violation of privacy. The Federal government can create an identification card to identify you as an American citizen. It's also not a violation of states' rights. The Federal government is not forcing the states to use the ID card for anything. Which raises the question: **why have a national ID card at all?** What purpose would it serve? It wouldn't be used for elections, since those are run by the states, and ID requirements vary. It wouldn't be used for international identification; we have passports for that. And we already have a passport card, which works like a passport book in and around most places in North America you might reach by car or passenger ship. So what would be the purpose? Unless you can come up with a good reason to have a national ID card, then it just seems like a big waste of time and money.
