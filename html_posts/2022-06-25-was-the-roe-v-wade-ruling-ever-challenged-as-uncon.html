**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, there isn't anything covered by law that is exclusively a state's right issue, because the Federal government can still pass laws which apply to all 50 states. They didn't pass an abortion law because there is no political will to do so, and the only laws proposed only appeal directly to one side of the political debate, and so they did not move forward. Since no one in Congress is willing to compromise, the law did not pass. The ruling in *Dobbs* corrected a mistake by the Court. *Roe* was decided on the basis of a made-up right to privacy, which does not exist in the Constitution. This is why that case was overturned. Essentially, the Court determined that an issue like this should be handled by the individual states, because they know that Congress doesn't have the requisite authority to enact such a law. This doesn't stop Congress from trying. It wouldn't stop them from passing a law binding on all of the states, but they would have to do so under some Constitutional authority. I'm curious which Constitutional authority they would attempt to cite in order to pass abortion laws for all 50 states. The Commerce Clause?
