**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the Americans with Disabilities Act prevent an employer from being fired if their Tourette syndrome causes them to use profanity on the job?** The Americans with Disabilities Act prevents employers from terminating employees specifically because they have a disability, Instead, it requires employers to provide *reasonable accommodations* to employees with disabilities. It does not require that the employer change his or her business in an *unreasonable manner*. For example, it does not require that the employer spend inordinate amounts of money to accommodate the employee. It does not require the employer to change business practices or change how they conduct business. In this case, the employer could rightly be concerned if the person with Tourette's Syndrome were in a customer-facing role. Profanity in a customer-facing position would not be ideal. If this business did not have any positions that this person could do which were not customer facing, the employer could probably not find a position this person could perform, and could reasonably terminate that employee. If the employer had a non customer-facing role in which they could place the employee for which he is qualified, he could place the employee in that role. **TL;DR: Under certain conditions, the employee can be fired and it would not violate the ADA.**
