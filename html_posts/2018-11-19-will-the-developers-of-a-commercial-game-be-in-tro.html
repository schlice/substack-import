**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Will the developers of a commercial game be in trouble if an artist suddenly changes the license of the music used in the game from something like CC-BY to a non-commercial license when the game has already been released?** CC licenses are non-revocable. Once a content creator publishes content under CC, licensees can use that content based on the terms of that agreement for the duration of that license. You can certainly stop distributing **new** content under the CC license at any time, but once you publish content using the CC license, you cannot retroactively go back and re-license that existing content, or change the terms of the license agreement on content already released under CC. Anyone who has a copy of that CC-licensed content can continue to use it based on the terms of that license. As a content creator, therefore, you should think carefully before distributing your content under CC.[\[1\]](#TCUCK){#cite-TCUCK .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-TCUCK){.citation_index} [[Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=587b41&url=https://creativecommons.org/faq/%23what-if-i-change-my-mind-about-using-a-cc-license){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="creativecommons.org"}]{.qlink_container}
