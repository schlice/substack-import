**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I know most of the answers already said this, but I really feel like I need to add my voice to this discussion. There are several big (really big) reasons why you need to have either a fenced-in yard or a protective pool cover, and none of them have anything to do with your kids. They help your kids incidentally, but primarily those things are there for **your legal protection.** A pool is known as an "attractive nuisance." In other words, pools, like trampolines and big piles of dirt, attract kids. Like moths to a flame, kids are naturally drawn to "attractive nuisances." And some of those things can really hurt them, or even kill them if they're not careful. When you have an attractive nuisance on your property, **the onus is on you** to protect that nuisance from the kids (primarily, but others too) who may seek to take advantage of that thing without your consent and without adult supervision. Without this, you could be liable if someone hurts themselves or dies when you were not watching your pool. In fact, most states won't even certify your pool for use unless you either have a fence or a locking gate around your pool. You should check with your city or county's building permit department to see what the laws are where you live. Also, you should check your homeowner's insurance policy. Most will recommend you expand your homeowner's insurance to include what is known as an "umbrella policy" to protect you against lawsuits from people who may injure themselves while using your pool, and of course, you can't get any homeowner's insurance (in most cases) unless your pool is protected. As far as your own toddler is concerned, Florida has that covered.
