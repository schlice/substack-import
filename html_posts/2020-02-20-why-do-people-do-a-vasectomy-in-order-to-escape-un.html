**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do people do a vasectomy in order to escape unwanted children and abortions?** I made a decision to not risk exposing further children to the spectre of Multiple Sclerosis. Now, I'm fully aware that the risk isn't very high. The average person in the United States has about a 1 in 750 (.1%) chance of developing MS. For first-degree relatives of a person with MS, such as children, siblings or non-identical twins, the risk rises to approximately 1 in 100 to 1 in 40 (2.5-5%).[\[1\]](#fpiZx){#cite-fpiZx .citation_link} I have already, unknowingly, subjected three children to the higher risk of developing MS. My youngest child was born in 2002. I was diagnosed in 2005. The risk of exposing my children to an increased risk of MS was something that we chose to avoid.
