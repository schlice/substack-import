**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Exceedingly unlikely. See, these confirmation hearings are no longer about *Senators* learning anything about the nominee. They may have already met with her in private. They have teams of researchers looking into everything from her family life to her legislative history. They don't *need* these hearings. These hearings are much more about *the American people* learning something about the nominee, and also possibly about the Senator. They are also a stage where potential Presidential candidates can get a few sound bites recorded (see Booker, Cory and Harris, Kamala, during the Brett Kavanaugh confirmation hearings). These hearings are nothing more than a glorified dog-and-pony show. Judge Barrett needs only get in there, say that she won't talk about how she will potentially rule on any particular issue, talk about her values, talk about her judicial record, and that's it. She should avoid other entrapment-style questions like the plague. And then Senators are going to mostly vote along party lines, with few exceptions (if our RINOs are feeling spry, they may vote against the candidate, but that's not enough to stop this train from rolling), and she will be confirmed. \ Original question: ***Is there anything Amy Coney Barrett might say in her SCOTUS confirmation hearings that would change any senator\'s mind about how to vote?***
