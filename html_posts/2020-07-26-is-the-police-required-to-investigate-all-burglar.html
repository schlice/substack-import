**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is the police required to investigate all burglar alarms or are there some neighborhoods where they don\'t have to respond to them?** The police are under no specific legal requirement to do anything, except to enforce the law. What does "enforce the law" mean? That means if a person breaks a law, the police ensure that whatever the punishment is that is listed in that law, the person is brought to a judge to see if they will be given that punishment. That's it. That's what the police are required to do, by law. They do all the rest when they have time, when there aren't shootings and drunk drivers and rioters that they have to deal with. They fill their time answering *calls for service*. And with the number of false positives with burglar alarms, the priority for unverified burglar alarms is really low, if they get to it at all. Note that priorities in some departments are different. Some departments may classify some alarms differently.
