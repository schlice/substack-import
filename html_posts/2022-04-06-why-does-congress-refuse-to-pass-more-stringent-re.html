**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The First Amendment. The line between protecting people from cyber harassment and infringing on free speech rights is very thin and not well defined. Americans are as passionate about reining in cyber harassment as they are about protecting their First Amendment rights. This line is a very difficult one to navigate, and the government is not the right organization to do it, thanks to the Bill of Rights. In other words, **the government cannot really do much more to stop cyber harassment than they are already doing without running afoul of the First Amendment.** It is really up to industry to fix this issue, not government. \ Original question: Why does Congress refuse to pass more stringent regulation to protect Americans from cyber-harassment?
