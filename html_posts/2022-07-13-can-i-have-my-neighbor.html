**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It depends on your neighborhood. For the sake of discussion, I'll also assume we're talking about an HOA consisting of single-family homes, not a condo. If you live in a gated community, then your community owns everything inside the gates, including the roads. Your HOA can boot and tow cars for violating parking rules. If you do not live in a gated community, your HOA does not own the roads. Your local government (city, county, etc) owns the roads. The HOA cannot boot or tow cars unless they are parked on HOA property (so, for example, if you have a clubhouse owned by the HOA, cars can be towed from that parking lot). The only remedy for parking violations in a non-gated community is fines. But that becomes a rather expensive proposition, because you first must prove the car belongs to a resident or a guest of a resident, which means the HOA has to pay to run the plates. If the car can't be linked to a resident, then the money to run the plates is wasted. The HOA can only enforce fines against homeowners, residents and their guests, and if you can't positively ID the owner of the car as one of those, you don't know who to fine. In short, in a non-gated community, parking rules are extremely difficult to enforce. Your best bet is to contact the local government if the parked car is in violation of city or county parking laws (i.e. parked in a No Parking Zone). \ Original question: Can I have my neighbor\'s vehicle towed if they are violating the neighborhood parking rules and restrictions in my community?
