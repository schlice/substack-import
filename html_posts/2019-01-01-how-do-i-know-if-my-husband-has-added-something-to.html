**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I know if my husband has added something to my phone where he can see what I\'m doing?** There are two basic ways to detect and remove spyware from your phone: 1. Run an antispyware application 2. Wipe your phone and reinstall everything. **Antispyware**: There is antispyware for your phone, and it can detect whether or not spyware is installed. - If you're an Apple iPhone user, install [[Certo iPhone](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=539f7d&url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/certo-mobile-security/id1274429896?mt%3D8){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container}. - If you're an Android user, install [[Incognito](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8450a2&url=https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id%3Dcom.dvtech.incognito%26hl%3Den_US){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container}. Running one of these antispyware apps will tell you if there is any spyware on your phone, and help you remove them. It will also tell you whether or not your phone was rooted/jailbroken without your knowledge. **Wipe your phone:** Admittedly, this is kinda the "using a sledgehammer to drive in a finishing nail" sort of approach to uninstalling offensive applications, but once you do, you can be sure there is no monitoring software on your phone. In order to ensure that *only* apps you choose are installed on your phone, you have to *wipe your phone.* I know, that sounds pretty harsh, and in truth, it is a pretty ham-handed way to clean up your phone. But two things will definitely happen: 1. your phone will run really well, and 2. you'll be sure that there is no spyware on your phone. For an iPhone: 1. First, make sure you have a good backup, either in your iCloud or in iTunes. You will need that backup to restore your apps and data. 2. Navigate to **Settings** \> **General** \> **Reset** 3. Tap on **Erase All Content and Settings.** 4. Tap on **Erase** **iPhone**. 5. Tap on **Erase** **iPhone** again to confirm. Your iPhone will wipe, and your installed applications from your last backup will be restored. Be sure to check that entire list of applications and don't install any apps you are unsure of. For an Android phone: 1. First, make sure you have good current backups in your Google Play account. 2. In current versions of Android, go to **Settings \> General Management \> Reset**. 3. Click **Factory Data Reset.** 4. You will have to Confirm your choice and then enter your PIN in order to proceed. Your Android device will reset, wipe and re-install its base OS. It will re-initialize as if it was a new phone. Once you log back in to your Google account, it will ask to restore your apps and settings. In that list of apps, make sure they are all apps you want to re-install and are not spyware apps.
