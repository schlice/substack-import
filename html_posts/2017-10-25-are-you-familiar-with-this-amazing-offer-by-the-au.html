**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Ah, you've fallen into a very common trap. When you follow this policy, you become a **permanent resident alien**. This means you are allowed to live and work in Australia for as long as you like. **But you are not a citizen. Therefore, this is not an "immigration policy" since you are not "immigrating" to Australia. You simply remain in the country as a permanent resident alien.** We have a similar program in the United States, commonly referred to as a "green card." It constitutes permanent residency, but not citizenship. Non-citizenship carries some weighty caveats. For example, if you commit a crime while you are in a foreign country (i.e. a country that is not the one which correlates to your citizenship), you can be **deported** as part of your punishment. You may have come to the country on a green card (or a 189 visa in Australia), but that doesn\'t make you a ***citizen****.*
