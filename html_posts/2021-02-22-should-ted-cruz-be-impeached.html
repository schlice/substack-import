--- title: '"Should Ted Cruz be impeached?"' date: '2021-02-22' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Since senators are not appointed under the Appointments Clause, they cannot be impeached. However, if the state of Texas feels that Ted Cruz needs to leave his position, the state of Texas may hold a recall vote to remove him from office. If they otherwise do not remove him, or if they do not have a mechanism for recall of effected officials under Texas law, the next best bet for Texas voters is to vote him out at the next election. And as others have pointed out, the individual chamber can expel a member with a 2/3 vote.
