--- title: "Why are fighter jets louder than jumbo jets?" date: "2016-05-26" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Different engines. Fighter jets use turbojet engines. Commercial aircraft used to use them as well, but they have all switched to turbofan engines, which provide large amounts of thrust at vast fuel savings. This is a cutaway view of a turbofan: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-3528ac14d811f3ebd5054b99dcbac40e){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-3528ac14d811f3ebd5054b99dcbac40e" master_w="2000" master_h="1323"} ::: :::: There is a great deal of bypass air that goes around the actual jet engine portion, which quiets the noise significantly. That's why they are called "high-bypass turbofan engines." Fighter jets use turbojet engines (just the interior portion of the turbofan): :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-568f9106c7450e388e774297f8878c4c){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-568f9106c7450e388e774297f8878c4c" master_w="475" master_h="292"} ::: :::: Many fighter jets today use "low-bypass" turbofans, which are basically turbojets with small amounts of bypass air which cools the combustion chamber and provides extra oxygen for the afterburner. There is little to no noise suppression on military jets
