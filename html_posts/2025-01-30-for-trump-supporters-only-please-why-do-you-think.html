**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So you have two questions here: why is the Constitution included in the *God Bless The USA Bible*, and why does the link to the Constitution not appear on the Trump White House website? \ Here's the answer to the first question: ***Why do you think Trump has the Constitution included in his \"God Bless the USA Bible"?*** Let me just start by saying, as a Christian, I take issue with the "God Bless the USA Bible," also known as the "Trump Bible." The Bible wasn't commissioned or written by President Trump, and he didn't have any editorial control over what was placed into it. It was only *endorsed* by President Trump. It was also endorsed by singer/songwriter Lee Greenwood (who wrote the song "God Bless The USA"). But neither of them decided what was to go into it. For brevity's sake (and because I'm lazy and don't want to type out its proper name every time) we'll just call it "The Trump Bible." The Trump Bible is a leather-bound Bible with an embossed American flag on the front, and it contains the King James Version of the Bible, along with the Founding Documents (the Declaration of Independence, the Constitution, the Pledge of Allegiance) and the lyrics to the song "God Bless The USA" in Lee Greenwood's own handwriting. It was supposed to contain the New International Version of the Bible, but Zondervan (the publisher of the NIV) withdrew their version of the Bible after public criticism. Instead, the KJV was included because it is already part of the Public Domain and is not under copyright restrictions. I take issue with the Bible being lumped together with our nation's founding documents. Doing so tends to equate (at some level) our founding documents with the Bible. While our nation was indeed founded on a Judeo-Christian heritage, that does not put our founding documents on par with the Bible. They are certainly great documents, *but they don't hold a candle to the Bible.* Putting them together in a single book tends to both improperly elevate our founding documents, and water down the Bible. The Bible should stand *alone* as the inspired Word of God, as the founding documents, while pretty great in their own right, are not the inspired word of God. \ Second question: ***\[Why was\] the Constitution removed from the White House web page on \[Trump's\] first day in office?*** So first mistake: the Biden White House website did not have a copy of the entire Constitution on it. It had [[a page describing](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a2b36a&url=https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/about-the-white-house/our-government/the-constitution/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="archives.gov"}]{.qlink_container} the Constitution, and then[ [a link](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=22ee61&url=https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="archives.gov"} ]{.qlink_container}over to the National Archives which has more information (and photos and history and the actual text) about the Constitution. Second mistake: The Trump White House team did not intentionally remove references to the Constitution from the website. **The new Administration builds a completely new website from scratch, and the old administration's version of the White House website is** ***archived*.** Those archives stay online, and [[you](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=65c09c&url=https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="archives.gov"} ]{.qlink_container}can [[go](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6d8588&url=https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="archives.gov"} ]{.qlink_container}visit [[them](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6ef8ae&url=https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="archives.gov"} ]{.qlink_container}any time. Trump White House Website 2.0 is still under construction, and is very thin currently. It is constantly being updated. Currently it only has very pertinent information about the early days of the Trump 2.0 administration, and a lot of the historical information (like information about our government) is still under construction. Lack of a link to the Constitution does not mean the administration does not care about the Constitution, or doesn't retain any fidelity to it. It simply means the site is still under construction, and we are still very early in the new administration.
