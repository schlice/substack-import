--- title: '"How easy and/or hard would it be for the police to track a crytophone?"' date: '2016-02-03' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you\'re talking about physically locating a phone, rather easy. The cryptophone still has a phone number, and still connects and registers with a cell tower. Therefore, with the proper warrants, the police can get the location of that handset to within a few city blocks of that tower. If you\'re moving, triangulation can actually get them even more precise. If you\'re talking about the police\'s ability to read your text messages or listen in on your phone conversations on your cryptophone (as long as you are communicating with someone else who also has a cryptophone), then even if the police have a wiretap warrant, they won\'t hear anything but gibberish, and your SMS messages will be nothing but unreadable text. Your SMS messages and your voice will all be encrypted end-to-end between handsets. If you\'re using third-party services from your cryptophone, you may still be able to be monitored through those services, so caution is in order. You will need to use the handset\'s secure mail application, and the receiving party will need to do the same, when using email.
