**Content:** [To date, there is very limited research showing a benefit for MS patients. There have been some studies done overseas regarding marijuana\'s effects on pain in MS patients.\ \ Marijuana\'s active ingredient (THC) attaches to cannabinoid receptors on nerve cells, which has a positive effect on neurological pain. It is said to reduce pain better than analgesics or other man-made pain meds.\ \ Regardless, there is no conclusive research which shows the long-term impacts of marijuana use, nor is the any safety of efficacy data on its use.
