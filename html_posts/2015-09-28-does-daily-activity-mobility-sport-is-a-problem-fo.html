**Content:** [For the majority of MS patients, problems with movement are not quite as common as some other symptoms like fatigue. Known as \"MS Lassitude,\" fatigue hits about 80% of people with MS. Movement issues can actually be exacerbated with fatigue.\ \ Walking (gait) issues are the most frequently reported movement issues with MS. There are several factors that contribute to gait issues, including weakness, spasticity, loss of balance, sensory deficit and fatigue.\ \ Walking issues can be helped by physical therapy, assistive therapy (such as electromuscular stimulation to help combat foot drop) and medications such as Ampyra. Ampyra was the first drug designed specifically to treat a symptom of MS.]{.rendered_qtext}
