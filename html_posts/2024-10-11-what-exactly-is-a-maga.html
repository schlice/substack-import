**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As a Christian, I know that we are all sinners. We have all fallen short of the glory of God (Romans 3:23). I am no better or worse than Trump in that regard. You said that "\[Trump\] is not a good man." But *no one is!* No one is righteous, not one (Romans 3:10). Yes, he is a sinner. Yes, he has character flaws. Yes, I would beseech him to seek repentance and forgiveness. But whether or not he does that is between him and his Savior. While I would encourage him to do so, I cannot force him to do so. So it wouldn't be right to judge whether or not I should vote for him based on his sins alone, because using that metric, I can't vote for anyone. I could only vote for Jesus, because He is the only person who was ever sinless. So if I can't use their level of righteousness to determine whether or not to vote for any one candidate, what do I use? I use their potential for positive results for the country. Whose policies will best promote the common good? As a Christian, we should first turn to the Bible for answers. > But seek the welfare of the city where I have sent you into exile, and > pray to the LORD on its behalf, for in its welfare you will find your > welfare. \-- Jeremiah 29:7 ESV So it seems that the Bible agrees that we should vote in a way in which we justifiably believe will promote the common good. I personally follow the "Least Bad Option" strategy. Since it is quite possible that either candidate will provide some "common good," and neither candidate is perfect, I tend to vote for the candidate who is the "lesser of two evils," which suggests that both candidates might be detrimental to the common good. Nevertheless, I can still vote in good conscience for the less harmful candidate when I am convinced that they possess the necessary qualifications and suitability for the office, and is the most likely to provide good outcomes for the country. So yes, as a Christian, I can safely vote for Trump, knowing that his presidency is the most likely to provide good outcomes for the largest majority of the country.
