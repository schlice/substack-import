--- title: "If somebody works as a federal contractor, has the appropriate clearance, a signed non-disclosure agreement and a need to know, would that person end up in prison for sharing classified information (on accident or on purpose) with uncleared persons?" date: "2019-04-27" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If somebody works as a federal contractor does having a security clearance mean that they cannot talk about what they are working with and if they do they will end up in prison?** The answer is "it depends." Not all parts of every federal contract are classified. In fact, it is very infrequently that an *entire* federal contract is fully, 100% classified. It is true that *parts* of some federal contracts involve classified material and classified projects. But very rarely is an entire federal contract 100% classified. It is true that some federal contractors who work on some federal contracts won't be able to discuss certain portions of their work. However, simply possessing a security clearance does not mean they can't talk about the entirety of what they do.
