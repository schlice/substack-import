--- title: '"Are you legally allowed to shoot a police officer to prevent him from killing an unarmed man?"' date: '2016-10-02' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **No.** There's really not much more to say than no, but if you want some level of explanation, here's some: 1. **You have no idea why the police officer is doing what he is doing.** Police have the legal authority to enforce the law, and to ensure the safety of the general public. When a police officer uses (or threatens to use) deadly force, it is because he is attempting to stop a threat to life or a threat to cause grave bodily harm. What is "grave bodily harm?" Here's an example: if someone punches you in the face so hard that he shatters your orbital socket, you could be blinded in that eye. That vision may return, or it may not. Nevertheless, this is considered "grave bodily harm" and any citizen, not just police, has the right to defend themselves against such harm. The police have the additional burden of defending society in general against that harm, and allowing such a person to continue constitutes an exigent threat against the public at large. You may not know this. You may only see a police officer and an unarmed man. 2. As we have said numerous times here, **unarmed does not mean "not dangerous."** Unarmed people can inflict the "grave bodily harm" we spoke of in the previous bullet. For example, if a person is mentally unstable, he may cause great bodily harm, without thought for his own well-being, and doesn't need any weapon to do that. Also, if a person is under the influence of drugs, not only can he cause bodily harm, but depending on the drug, he could be entirely immune to pain control techniques, such as Tasers. 3. Finally, **you have no authority to insert yourself into the police's job.** If you think you can do it better, feel free to pick up an application at the police station and fill it out. Don't think you can become Joe or Jane Vigilante and expect to get off. If you shoot a cop, **you** will be the focus of the investigation, not the person who the officer had in his own sights. Also, consider this: if the police are going to use deadly force, they have to be able to reasonably articulate an imminent threat to themselves or to the public. Whether or not their opinion is "reasonable" is a matter of fact which is determined by a court. Not you, not the public, not YouTubers. A court. If you size up a situation and determine that the officer is not reasonable in their belief that there is an imminent threat to the public, you are abrogating a duty of the court onto yourself, which in and of itself is not lawful. You have no legal authority to make decisions that are supposed to be made by a court, and you will be charged as an aggressor. So if you see something like this happening, what should you do? I know it seems like the "in" thing to do these days, but if you're there, and you have your phone handy (and who doesn't), **take a video**. Turn the video over to the local media when you're done. They will be sure to use it to boost their ratings. Or, if you're more interested in seeing justice done than seeing your video on the local news, turn your video over to the District Attorney's office and let them investigate the police. Then put it on YouTube.
