**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it illegal for US companies to disobey Trump\'s orders to immediately start looking for an alternative to China, including bringing their companies home and making their products in the US? Can the Supreme Court overturn Trump\'s orders?** Anyone who has taken basic Civics classes already knows that Trump's tweets aren't an "order." The Executive Branch does not have the authority to "order" businesses to do anything. The Executive Branch executes existing law. Trump likes to use words, but not all of his words are grounded in law. We get that. He's a businessman, not a lawyer or a politician. However, if there is a law that Trump is going to start enforcing (where they failed to enforce it before), then maybe that's something he can "order." But Trump sending out a few tweets does not constitute an "order" that businesses "must follow." But if I was an American company, I wouldn't read Trump's tweets as an "order." I'd read them as a "strong suggestion." As in, "If I were you, I'd start looking for an alternative to working in China." Reading between the lines, I'd read: "Because there are going to be some significant changes related to the things I can control: tariffs. And those changes will have a significant impact on your company, if you continue to choose to work in or with China." It's true, Shenzen set itself up to provide this sort of cheap labor and parts, and American businesses have taken advantage of it, but the rampant IP theft and potential security issues leaves Shenzen a temptress which might just be too costly with which to climb into bed. Or stay there. As a company, I may not change anything *today*. But I also would have put an entire team together to examine my whole business model, find out what parts of it are dependent on manufacturing in China or materials sourced from China, and look for alternatives. I hear India is ramping up to meet the challenge. In fact, some companies are already doing this (i.e. [[Apple is moving some of its manufacturing to India](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=469e12&url=https://appleinsider.com/articles/19/04/15/foxconn-confirms-that-mass-production-of-the-iphone-will-begin-in-india-soon){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="appleinsider.com"}]{.qlink_container}). If China continues to be so flippant with US patent law, I would be looking elsewhere anyway.
