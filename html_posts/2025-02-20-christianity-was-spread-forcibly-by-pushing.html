**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, not at all. The passage in Luke is a historical account of Jesus discussing the kingdom of God. By using a parable, Jesus is explaining that He can't take His throne now, he has to die, be resurrected, ascend into heaven, and then wait until the time is right for Him to return (Luke 24:50--53; Revelation 19:11--16). When Christianity was spread by force during the time between 400 AD and 1500 AD, it was spread for a different reason. This was during the time that the Latin Church insisted on universal Christianity. It was perceived, at the time, that "religious aliens," or unbelievers, were "contaminating" Christianity, and there was widespread intolerance by church and state authorities, mobs, and vigilante groups. \ In short, it wasn't any Bible passage that caused people to spread Christianity by force. It was **sin**. It was the sin of pride, and its resulting intolerance by people.
