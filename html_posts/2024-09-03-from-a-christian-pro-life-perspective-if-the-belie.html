**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This question is an example of the logical fallacy known as a **false equivalence**. > A false equivalence or false equivalency is an informal fallacy in > which an equivalence is drawn between two subjects based on flawed or > false reasoning. This fallacy is categorized as a fallacy of > inconsistency. Colloquially, a false equivalence is often called > \"comparing apples and oranges.\"[\[1\]](#TUyzk){#cite-TUyzk > .citation_link} Comparing Jesus' sinless life to a fetus in the womb is incorrect. Jesus was created sinless; fetuses in the womb are created with original (imputed) sin. Secondly, pro-choice people do not care that fetuses are not guilty of any crime deserving of the death penalty, because *they believe the life of the fetus is less valuable than the life of the mother*, full stop. Therefore, they readily and easily discard the life of the fetus in accordance with the whims and wishes of the mother. The issue in abortion is not one of **sin**. The issue in abortion is one of **equal rights.** Pro-life people believe in equal rights; pro-choice people do not.
