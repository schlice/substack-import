{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Several reasons that it was an acceptable weapon:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} 1. It\'s the weapon we had. It may not have been the weapon we wanted, but it was the weapon we had. 2. It was a strategic weapon, not a tactical one. When you use strategic weapons like bombs, you know that there is collateral damage. 3. Scientists knew that the results of the weapon would be devastating, but they didn\'t know exactly how devastating until they dropped it.\ 4. The decision to use it was fueled by many different things, but I can probably bet that Pearl Harbor was still fresh in Americans\' collective minds. I\'d also bet that if we were fighting a country and not a terrorist organization when 9/11 hit, there would have been calls to launch nuclear strikes against that country. I\'m reminded of the words of the orator John Stuart Mill:\ > War is an ugly thing, but not the ugliest of things. The decayed and > degraded state of moral and patriotic feeling which thinks that > nothing is worth war is much worse. The person who has nothing for > which he is willing to fight, nothing which is more important than his > own personal safety, is a miserable creature, and has no chance of > being free unless made and kept so by the exertions of better men than > himself. \ It was a hard thing to do, but ultimately I think it ended the war with fewer Japanese lives lost than the alternative would have (an Allied invasion of Japan).
