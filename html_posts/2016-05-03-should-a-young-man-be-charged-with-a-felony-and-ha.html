**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *If* he is convicted of indecent exposure (which is seems likely that he will be), he will be required to register on the sex offender registry. *Will* he be? That\'s up to the court to decide. Where I live, indecent exposure involves exposure of the genitals **with the intent** \"to arouse or to satisfy the sexual desires of anyone.\" The \"anyone\" could be himself, a known viewer, or an unknown viewer. Our law is written with an intent clause. Here, it would be up to the prosecution to prove that there was intent to cause sexual arousal. However, the defendant is in Arizona. There, indecent exposure is listed as: \"Intentionally exposing your private parts (genitals or anus, or a female exposing the areola or nipple of her breast) and another person is present. The defendant must be reckless about the fact that the other person may be offended.\" This young man\'s conduct clearly meets this standard. The fact remains that no one caught the offensive picture and it was published. He was reckless in allowing the picture to be taken and to be used. He made no effort to inform anyone of his prank and allowed the picture to be used, which was reckless. Under Arizona law, this would be considered indecent exposure.
