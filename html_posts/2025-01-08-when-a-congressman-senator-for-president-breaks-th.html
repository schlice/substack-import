Should they be given a dishonorable discharge from government the same way a soldier would be dishonorably discharged?"' date: '2025-01-08' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Congress gets to set the rules of conduct for their own members. And they do have both conduct rules and an ethics committee. But what did this particular congressman do that you believe "breaks their oath" to "protect and uphold the Constitution?" And anyone can "give a congressman a dishonorable discharge." We have the power to do that. It's called **the voting booth**. If you don't like how a congressman is acting, or what he is doing, you have the power to remove him from office. The trick, of course, is convincing enough of your fellow constituents that he also needs to not be re-elected.
