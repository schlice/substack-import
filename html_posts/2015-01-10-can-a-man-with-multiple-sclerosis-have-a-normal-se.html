--- title: '"Can a man with multiple sclerosis have a normal sex life?"' date: '2015-01-10' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Short answer: yes.\ \ Longer answer: It depends on whether or not there is any sexual dysfunction associated with his MS. The [[National MS Society](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cb8467&url=http://nmss.org){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nmss.org"}]{.qlink_container} says that in a recent study, 63% of men have reported some level of sexual dysfunction. Since sexual arousal begins in the central nervous system, damage along these pathways can cause dysfunction in arousal and orgasm. Other MS symptoms such as fatigue and spasticity can affect sex life, as well as self-esteem and mood changes.\ \ There are medications and medical devices that can be used to assist men with sexual dysfunction, such as Viagra, Cialis, Levitra, or the MUSE suppository. There are also implants available that can help as well.\ \ MS does not affect fertility, or the ability to transmit or receive STDs. Men with MS have to take the same basic precautions against unplanned pregnancy and STDs as anyone else.]{.rendered_qtext}
