**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you think there will be lawsuits on the basis of constitutionality if President Trump\'s \"Food Box\" program is made into law?** No, there would not be a Constitutional challenge if they decide to float a proposal to cut SNAP funding (not eliminate, but cut) and replace some of it with "food boxes." So far as I can tell, there is no Constitutional requirement to have a SNAP program at all, so changing it to give less money for food and instead actually give food should not cause a Constitutional issue. I'm sure the 9th Circus can find some Constitutional argument for trying to block Executive action (again). Maybe food boxes somehow violate the 3rd Amendment or something. Because food boxes is just like quartering soldiers...or something. Who knows. If there is a wacky equivalence to be found, you can bet on the 9th being the stalwarts to find it. Meh. There are many concerns with the actual proposal (not the Constitutionality of it) of course, such as people with dietary restrictions, and people who are homeless and can't receive a box. It is those concerns that would probably render such a program a non-starter. They should instead revamp the SNAP program and be MUCH more strict on where SNAP funds can be used, and on what products SNAP funds can be spent. You would think that the *actual name of the program*, the Supplemental Nutritional Assistance Program would render it self-limiting, but I guess they thought that it was somehow discriminatory to try to tell people on what they could spend nutritional assistance dollars. I personally find it fascinating how much money from SNAP gets spent in the Port of Miami. They must have a special 3-night SNAP Cruise that I'm unaware of. But Constitutionally? It should not be a problem.
