**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can we pass a constitutional amendment that would allow states to leave the United States of America?** People love to throw around the term "Constitutional crisis" like it's confetti. However, this is exactly the type of action that would, in actuality, create a Constitutional crisis. While it is possible to craft and ratify an Amendment to the Constitution which would permit a State to secede from the Union, depending on what the requirements for secession look like, the Amendment could cause another Civil War should a state attempt to secede under the authority of that Amendment. In the United States, the Supreme Court ruled in *Texas v. White* that unilateral secession is unconstitutional, but that an "indissoluble" union *could be dissolved* by one of two methods: revolution, or *consent of the states*. Therefore, an Amendment is really not necessary. A state can secede from the Union if it receives the consent of **all of the states**. If even one state says no, then the seceding state may not leave. The reason? When a state became part of the Union, it was clearly stated that this action would be perpetual and indissoluble. The state gained all of the rights of the group, and shouldered all of the responsibilities of being a member of the group. When it comes to decisions related to the group, the whole of the group must now be in agreement. For that reason, a state can declare its intent to secede from the Union, but it can only actually do so with the full consent of all of the member states, since they all belong to the same Union. They all joined with the same admonition, and under the same rules. They didn't become separate states part of a loose coalition, but instead became part of an indissoluble union. They all expected that. Each member state has to agree to "lose" the seceding state. They must be unanimous. And there are logistical problems. What happens to Federal territories within the state? Do they become foreign embassies? How does the nation get compensated for the loss of National Parks? Or all the Federal dollars we provided in assistance to the state? The tendrils of interconnection between the state and all of the others would be difficult to sort. **TL;DR:** At best, an Amendment could say that a state can declare its intention to leave, but then *all of this* would be up to every other state to approve before it could be completed.
