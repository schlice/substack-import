**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What federal crimes is it permissible for the US president to commit while in office and not be indicted while in office? If any, what are the applicable laws?** You have some mixing of issues here. The President is not *immune* from laws; he's not above the law. He's still an American citizen. That said, the President cannot be *indicted* **while he is in office**. He has to be out of office before he can be indicted. Therefore, if a president commits a "high crime or misdemeanor," the Constitutional remedy would be impeachment, removal from office, and *then* indictment for his crime.
