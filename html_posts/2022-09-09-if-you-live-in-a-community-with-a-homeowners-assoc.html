--- title: '"If you live in a community with a Homeowners Association (HOA), is it legal to repaint your home without approval from the HOA first?"' date: '2022-09-09' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." Check your Covenants, Conditions, Rules & Regulations (CCR&R) documents for your HOA. All HOAs have them, and they are on file with your local government if you can't find them. Your HOA may even have a separate Architecture Review group which may have its own guidelines published. In MOST cases, *if you repaint with* ***the same color that was previously approved***, this is not considered an exterior "change." This is considered "standard maintenance" which you are required to do, and does not require additional approval. Note that some HOAs still require pre-approval, even if you are repainting with the same color. Check your Rules and Regulations document to be sure. In ALL cases, *if you are going to* ***change the color of your house****,* this IS considered to be an exterior "change," and your HOA will need to approve your change before you make it. And since I can't stress this point enough, **before beginning any work on the exterior of your home**, check your HOA Governing Documents and be sure you understand the requirements. Ask your Architecture Review group or your HOA Board members if you have questions. \ *Unless you live in my neighborhood, I am not a Director on your HOA Board.
