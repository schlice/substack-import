--- title: '"Do local police have the means to spy on text messages?"' date: '2018-12-30' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do local police have the means to spy on text messages?** First of all, no one can "spy" on text messages. SMS is highly regulated, far more so than messages within an app. SMS is regulated just like phone calls: you need a warrant to get them. In order to "spy" on text messages, the police must go to the wireless carrier, who can provide copies of SMS messages. The police can only do this with a warrant. There is no technology that allows you to "spy" on a text message. SMS does not work that way; it's not like "tapping an analog phone line" and listening in. What *can* happen is that someone can install spyware on your phone. One of the most well-known of these apps is called **mSpy**. It can allow remote access to the GPS location, SMS messages, and communications through nearly any other communications app (i.e. WhatsApp, SnapChat, etc). But this involves physical access to your phone and someone installing an app. It can't be done remotely. Additionally, to track anything beyond phone calls and SMS, the phone itself must be rooted (Android) or jailbroken (iOS).
