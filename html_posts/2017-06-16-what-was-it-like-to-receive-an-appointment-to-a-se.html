Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Each Member of Congress can have up to 5 appointed cadets in any one of the service academies. Once one of them leaves for any reason, the slots re-open and that Congressman can appoint more cadets. In the year that I applied, my Congressman had two slots. There were roughly 100 applicants. Which meant that I had a roughly 2% chance of getting an appointment. But I approached it in exactly the same way I approached everything while going through the Academy entrance process. There were forms, essays, and interviews. Interviews were conducted by a panel of people at the Congressman's office. I got my nomination from [[Congressman Norman F. Lent](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9c0d08&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_F._Lent){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, who was our representative at the time (New York's 5th Congressional District). He announced his selections for appointment in a press conference at his office on Long Island, which was also pretty cool. I don't know how difficult it was for that team to make its choice. They did have roughly 100 candidates to choose from. Was I a good candidate by a little? A lot? I think that information may be lost to the ages. I hope that answers your question. If there was something more specific that you wanted to ask, please do.
