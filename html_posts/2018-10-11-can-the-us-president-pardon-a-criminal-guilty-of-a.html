**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the U.S. president pardon a criminal guilty of a any federal crime?** Yes. **Can the president pardon any federal crime?** Yes. The President's pardon power comes from the Constitution, Article II, Section 2. Under it, the President \"shall have power to grant reprieves and pardons for offenses against the United States, except in cases of impeachment.\" The courts have interpreted this provision of the Constitution to mean that the President can issue pardons, conditional pardons, commutations of sentence, conditional commutations of sentence, remissions of fines and forfeitures, [[respites](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=05850a&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Respite_(law)){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, and [[amnesties](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=78cfec&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amnesty){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}.
