**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} People keep asking this question, but the answer does not change. You are looking to prove an immaterial God through the use of the material sciences, and that simply does not make sense. You cannot prove the existence of an immaterial God through the use of the material sciences. That does not mean that we cannot prove the existence of God; we can. We simply do not use the **material sciences**. We use other sciences, like **forensic science,** which allows us to understand causes in the past by examining the effects today. Forensic science is as valuable an investigation technique as material science and peer-reviewed journals are, and it is no less valuable or meaningful. Science, at its very root, is a search for causes. This is the basis for the Law of Causality, which is the fundamental principle of science. Without the Law of Causality, science is impossible. In fact, Francis Bacon (the father of modern science) said, "True knowledge is knowledge by causes." In other words, science is a search for causes. That's what scientists do---they try to discover what caused what. The problem that you're probably running into is that because we cannot use the material, empirical sciences to prove an immaterial God, materialists reject God out-of-hand. But that's illogical, because there are plenty of things which we can correctly say exist even though they cannot be explained directly by material science. Materialists assert that only materials exist, but materials don't have morality. How much does hate weigh? Is there an atom for love? What's the chemical composition of the murder molecule? These questions are meaningless because physical particles are not responsible for morality. If materials are solely responsible for morality, then Hitler had no real moral responsibility for what he did---he just had bad molecules. This is nonsense, and everyone knows it. Human thoughts and transcendent moral laws are not material things any more than the laws of logic and mathematics are material things. They are immaterial entities that cannot be weighed or physically measured. As a result, they can't be explained in material terms by natural selection or any other atheistic means, and they can't be analyzed using the empirical sciences. But they exist nonetheless. Reliance on the material sciences while excluding all else is the folly of the naturalists. That's why this hyper-focus on "show me peer-reviewed studies which prove God's existence" are nonsense; they are literally illogical.
