**Content:** [No, the arrest in and of itself is not a violation of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. You do not have a right to be free from arrest for crimes which the State believes you have committed. You have a right to be treated fairly and humanely while you are incarcerated by the State. You have the right to food, clean water, and medical care. You have the right to humane living conditions.\ \ If the State makes a law that says that simply being a \"thief in law\" is illegal, not actually performing some other criminal act, then the act of arresting you for being a thief in law is not a violation. It is a law, and the State is carrying out the law. However, if the State treats you inhumanely while you are incarcerated, that is a violation of the Universal Declaration.]{.rendered_qtext}
