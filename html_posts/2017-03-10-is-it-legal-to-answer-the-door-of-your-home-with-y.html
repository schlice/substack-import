--- title: "Is it legal to answer the door of your home with your gun drawn and pointed at whoever is on the other side of it?" date: "2017-03-10" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is that it is illegal to point a gun at someone who you do not intend to shoot. There are two crimes here: the criminal law at question is called **brandishing a weapon**, and it attaches any time you hold a weapon in a threatening manner, such that it can be brought to bear against another person. The tort crime is **assault**, which is differentiated from the crime of assault in that the tort assault can be verbal or non-physical (or in this case, a simple assault with a weapon), and in tort law, a battery is the physical execution of that assault. A criminal assault involves physical attack. To be legally compliant, you can answer the door with a holstered pistol. You can answer the door with a slung shotgun or rifle (assuming it is not slung across the chest and your hand is not resting near the trigger). Anything else could be considered brandishing and is a crime.
