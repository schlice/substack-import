Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [You are absolutely correct. The problem we have in this country is how we treat our mentally ill. Do you know where the three largest mental institutions in this country are? Riker\'s Island, the LA County Jail, and the Cook County Jail. The problem is that we treat our mentally ill as criminals. This is what our state mental programs have devolved into.\ \ However, controlling the sale and acquisition of guns is not a terrible thing, either. A gun is something that is specifically designed to kill people. No other item allowed for distribution to the general public has this specific use. Therefore, it is right to ensure that only qualified owners who are not criminals or mentally unstable may actually possess such a tool.\ \ There are definitely items on both sides of the debate that need to be addressed. Sensible restrictions on who can possess a firearm are necessary to protect the citizenry. However, understanding that the law is only applicable to those who follow it, in order to have an ordered society, we need to criminalize only the criminal behavior. Our current laws do nothing to further that goal; they only criminalize law-abiding citizens\' behaviors. When laws criminalize non-criminal behavior, they are less likely to be followed by the non-criminal citizenry, making the laws patently useless. We must have laws that make it unlawful for the mentally unstable to possess firearms, but we must do so in a way that is wholly non-invasive and protects the rights of the remainder of society to keep and bear arms. This is definitely a difficult task, because of our treatment of the mentally ill in today\'s society. In order to keep guns out of the hands of the mentally ill, we have to do a better job of identifying, and helping, the mentally ill.]{.rendered_qtext}
