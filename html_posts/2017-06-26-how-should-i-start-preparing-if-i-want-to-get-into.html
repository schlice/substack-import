--- title: '"How should I start preparing if I want to get into the United States Air Force Academy?"' date: '2017-06-26' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'm going to repeat what others have said, and add a little more. - Get the best grades you can get. Work hard at school. If you can take AP or college-level classes, do that. It shows your ability to handle the academic rigor of college. - Get into an ACT or SAT prep class. I know it's a little early to be thinking about those classes, but they help. Anything to help boost your test score is helpful, and those prep classes do help. - Make sure you are participating in team-level sports at your high school. If you're not, start. About 95% of applicants who were accepted into the Academy have participated in team sports. About 80% of those had earned varsity letters in their sport. Those numbers tell you that team sports are very important in the selection process. - Make sure you are participating in volunteer activities outside of your school. Volunteering with your church or in your community is another important aspect of your portfolio. - Use the [[Air Force Academy Admissions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60e33c&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} website. Get to know that site. Read all sections of the site, even if you don't think they pertain to you. Use the Admissions Tracker on the site to track the things you need to do. Use the Admissions Liaison Officer (ALO) Locator to find your ALO and reach out to him/her. It's never too early to start that conversation. - Research commissioning sources. Your local Congressman is usually the place to go with the best chance of success. Research what your Congressman requires for a commission. You can discuss that with your ALO as well. - Ask questions! If something is unclear, ask about it before it becomes an issue. Good luck!
