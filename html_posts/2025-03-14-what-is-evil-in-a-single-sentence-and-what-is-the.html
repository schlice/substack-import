--- title: '"What is evil in a single sentence, and what is the primordial origin of evil?"' date: '2025-03-14' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In a single sentence, evil is the opposite of good. Evil is not a *created thing*. Atheists love to say things like "Why did God create evil?" He didn't *create* evil. Evil, in that context, is the **absence** of good. It's like a hole. You don't *create* a hole. You *remove* something, and the void that is left is a hole. Evil is a hole created by the removal of good. God is good, so the removal of God is the "hole" which is the definition of evil. God is love, so the absence of love in a person is un-Godlike, and is therefore evil. That absence of love manifests itself as unloving behavior. The same can be said for God's other gifts: mercy, patience, justice, etc. The absence of those qualities in a person constitutes evil. That evil then manifests itself in behavior that is unmerciful, unjust, impatient, etc. The source of evil? It's *us*. We were given free will. We were given the opportunity to choose good or to choose evil. With Satan's help, we chose evil. When Adam sinned, mankind fell. Our bodies were now sinful, and our world was fallen. We live in that fallen, sinful world today. Our sinful world allows us to make sinful choices. We are held accountable for our own sinful choices, and our choices alone.
