--- title: "What can I do against police harassment?" date: "2016-12-23" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you ever believe that police conduct is improper, it is up to you to report that behavior. You have several options. 1. You can go down to the local police department in question and file a complaint. Be aware that you will have to identify yourself and give real personal information (name, address, phone number, SSN in some jurisdictions, etc), and provide a written affidavit. Investigators will question you. It seems a bit intimidating, but it is necessary. 2. If you don't get anywhere with the local department, your best next step is to go to your local media. Most local TV stations have a "Call for Action" hotline. Call them, tell them your story. They will make some phone calls on your behalf to the department in question. If your complaint didn't get any traction, the local media sniffing around for a story may prod them into action. You will either hear something being done, or you will clearly hear why the department believes nothing needs to be done.
