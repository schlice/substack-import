**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should people who get caught the 2nd time being under the influence of drinking, and drugs, behind the wheel of a vehicle, be banned from driving for 20 years, and have to take the bus, train, or walk?'** State laws are pretty good, albeit somewhat lenient, when it comes to license revocation. There's a [[pretty good website](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=262c2e&url=https://www.dui-usa.drinkdriving.org/dui_dwi_state_laws.php){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="drinkdriving.org"}]{.qlink_container} which summarizes all the penalties, by state, for DUI charges. There's also fines and jail time, which similarly all vary by state. If states think it's a problem, states can crack down harder. Even in Nanny-State New York, you have to hit your fifth DWI before your license is permanently revoked. California's rules are surprisingly lighter than New York's, with only the fourth and subsequent DUIs even being felonies, but if you get three or more DUIs within 10 years, you're eligible for a 10 year revocation. Ultimately though, if states think DUI is a problem, states can change the penalties.
