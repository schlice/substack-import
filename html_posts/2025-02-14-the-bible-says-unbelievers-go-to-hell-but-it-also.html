**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **The Bible says unbelievers go to hell but it also says every knee > will eventually confess to god and that he is all loving?** All three of those things can be true at the same time, because none of them are in conflict: - Unbelievers will go to hell. Unbelievers are people who know the truth and actively reject it. - Every knee will bow, and every tongue will confess that Christ is Lord (Phillipians 2:10--11). Even those who did not believe will eventually believe. But if they do so unwillingly, they should not expect leniency. - God is indeed infinitely loving. But He is also infinitely just, and He can be both at the same time. His commands are for our good (Jeremiah 29:11). It is perfectly loving to say that if you willingly violate His commands, you receive the due results of your actions. It would be patently unloving to have rules in place that you don't enforce. > **So why do evangelicals still think they have a right to decide if > people go to hell or not?** "Evangelicals" do not decide if people go to hell or not. That's entirely up to God. That said, we can (and we are commanded to) point out things that believers might be doing which is sinful. In fact, it would be *unloving* to **not** point out sin in others for which they may not have repented. > **Don't unbelievers have a chance?** People who are willfully ignorant have already had their shot. Indeed, the Bible says that everyone knows about God through simply viewing His creation, as well as what is written on their hearts > *For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine > nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the > world, in the things that have been made. So they are without excuse. > \-- Romans 1:20 ESV* \ Original question: **The Bible says unbelievers go to hell but it also says every knee will eventually confess to god and that he is all loving? So why do evangelicals still think they have a right to decide if people go to hell or not? Don't unbelievers have a chance?**
