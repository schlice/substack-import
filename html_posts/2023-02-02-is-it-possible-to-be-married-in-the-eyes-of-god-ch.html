**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I think the question we would need to answer is **why**. If you intend to be married and to stay together, why not have a ceremony? Sure, there may be situations where a ceremony is either impractical or some other legal or social situation keeps people from actually getting married. I would strongly suggest you fix that issue first, and then get married. The truth of the matter is that you make a vow *not only* before God, but you make that vow *in front of family and friends*. Why are family and friends important to be present at a wedding? If you have only made a private commitment but not a public one, it can appear as an ungodly relationship to others. > The wedding ceremony served many purposes, including letting people > know who was married to whom, avoiding appearances of evil and > accusations of adultery (2 Sam. 3:7 and 8), maintaining proper order > and behavior in society, providing a time for the relatives to get to > know each other (which was very important since many marriages were > really about power and economics--1 Kings 3:1), and providing memories > that would be a blessing throughout the marriage, especially for the > bride (Jer. 2:32). These things are still important in our modern > society[\[1\]](#nfzPv){#cite-nfzPv .citation_link} People also reject Christianity because they think Christians are hypocrites, but the marriage ceremony lets everyone know publicly of your intent to live together as husband and wife, and that you are also honoring God's instructions on the sanctity of the marriage union between a man and his wife. There are several other reasons I can think of: - **Accountability**. While I don't doubt your sincerity, it is certainly true that couples who are not formally married can walk away from that vow much more easily. A couple who is not formally married has an "easy out" and they usually take it. - **Legal issues**. If you are not legally married, there are issues related to government functions which may not be resolved, such as taxes, spousal privilege with relation to healthcare decisions, and transfer of real property. - **Generational issues**. There could be some legal issues regarding generativity (determining the "family tree" of children) if you don't have a legal marriage in place. It is for these reasons that I think private ceremonies are unwise.
