Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [I have deep convictions that conflict with my own emotions, as well as others\' beliefs.\ \ The one that gets me in the most trouble: I believe that gay people should be allowed to marry. I believe that gay people should find a church that believes the way they do and get married. I also believe that states should be allowed to decide for themselves whether or not they want to recognize gay marriage, and whether or not they want to issue marriage licenses to gay couples. Finally, I believe that the Federal government (not state governments), if they are going to allow states to behave this way, should not tie any benefits whatsoever to marital status. Whether or not states do is up to states. Where people choose to live can then be based on the state that is most accepting of them.\ \ That\'s a tough one when you truly believe that gay people should be allowed to marry, but you also truly believe that states should have the right to choose whether or not they accept that marriage. It isn\'t fair to tie any benefits to marital status, especially at the Federal level if the states are going to decide for themselves. Federal benefits must be decoupled (no pun intended) from marital status.]{.rendered_qtext}
