--- title: "I want to make an app that allows the top 10 music of that day to be downloaded free. So, is it illegal to download top songs?" date: "2016-01-11" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No one truly downloads songs for free. Someone always pays for the music. Unlike what you may have read or heard somewhere, if you\'re downloading songs for free and no one is paying for the music, you are *stealing.* Unless the author has released the music royalty-free, someone is supposed to be paying for that music. Therefore, for you to make an app that allows people to download songs for free, you have to work out who pays for the music, because clearly you don\'t want the consumer to foot the bill. If you are hosting the music, you must pay royalties every time the song is downloaded. Maybe you can show them an ad before the download starts. That way, you can pay the royalties with the ad revenue. Good luck making any money that way though. If, however, you are just pointing to songs hosted somewhere else, it is up to the hosting provider to pay the royalties however they choose to do so. If you point to songs you know are illegally hosted, or you know the provider is not paying royalties to stream the music which is not released royalty-free, you can face fines as well.
