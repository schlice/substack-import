**Content:** [Your answer is not a rant. It\'s a valid concern about the structure of the Federal government. Remember what I said in my answer: **the People choose Congress, the States choose the President.** The current system ensures some level of equality throughout the states, so that states with small landmasses are not dwarfed in their power by states with large landmasses, and states with small populations are not dwarfed by states with larger populations. Allowing a popular vote for President makes the election a popularity contest, and doesn\'t take the best interest of the state into consideration. You would think that voters take their states\' interests into consideration, but most often they do not.\ \ You speak in terms of percentages of votes, but you don\'t speak in terms of percentages of eligible voters who actually vote. When our voter turnout is such that one out of every five eligible voters actually casts a vote, I don\'t think it\'s right that a popular vote determines the winner. In that case, it really is a popularity contest, and the more popular person will win, because people will actually make the effort to vote for him, whereas if they are not thrilled with him in general, they won\'t. That\'s not democracy. That\'s representative government, where the disinterested are forced to live with the choices of others.]{.rendered_qtext}
