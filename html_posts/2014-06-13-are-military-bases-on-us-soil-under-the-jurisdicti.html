--- title: '"Are military bases on US soil under the jurisdiction of the police?"' date: '2014-06-13' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Since the base is located on soil within a city, county, and state, those entities have jurisdiction on the base. It\'s called concurrent jurisdiction, and it means that the local police as well as the military police, both have jurisdiction. However, the local police usually defer to the military police on matters which occur on a military base.\ \ Legally, because of concurrent jurisdiction, since both the state and the Federal government have jurisdiction, someone committing a crime on a base can be charged twice, once by the base and once by the locals. He can be convicted twice, and serve two separate sentences, and this is not covered by the \"double jeopardy\" rule where you can\'t be convicted for the same crime twice. In this case, you can. It\'s the only loophole in the \"double jeopardy\" rule, but it would only be exercised when something really serious happened.]{.rendered_qtext}
