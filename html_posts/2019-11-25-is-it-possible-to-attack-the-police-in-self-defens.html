**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it possible to attack the police in self-defense?** First of all, you can never "attack" in self-defense. So no, attacking in self-defense is never lawful. That's attack, never self-defense. Secondly, while it is physically possible to defend yourself from a police officer, that's not really the question. The question is whether or not you will be charged for doing so. In most cases, police officers act under "color of law." That means that if they are doing their jobs (something you also really can't tell at the scene), they are presumed to be acting lawfully unless a court says otherwise. And that's the real rub. You can't tell whether or not a police officer is acting lawfully until a court determines it. It is only a court that can make that determination, not you, not your sidewalk lawyer, not the Internet. Just a court. So an officer acting under color of law is presumed to be acting lawfully until the courts says otherwise, so you "resisting" or "defending yourself" is unlawful.
