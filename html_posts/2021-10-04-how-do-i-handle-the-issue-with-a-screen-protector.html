Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} 1. Don't use one. I know you're worried about your screen scratching, but the Samsung phones with in-display fingerprint sensors use Gorilla Glass, which doesn't start scratching until they encounter something on the Mohs hardness scale at level 6 (with deeper groves at level 7)\*. Anyway, only put your phone in your pocket, and keep your pockets clean, and you should be just fine. 2. Use a PIN or passcode instead. An 8-digit passcode is just as secure as your fingerprint. 3. Set up face detection. While you can do this, it is notoriously unreliable, and it is far too insecure as it does not use 3D face mapping like Apple's FaceID or Google's dual infrared cameras making a 3D representation of your face. This means you can hold up a picture of yourself and it will unlock your phone. So while you *can* do this, I recommend that you do not. \ Original question: How do I handle the issue with a screen protector interfering with the fingerprint sensor on a Samsung phone? \* Hopefully, some of you get that reference.
