Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As always with legal questions, the answer is "it depends." If the Governing Documents have rules regarding appropriate sitting attire for sitting in your own backyard, and they can enforce that rule without violating your rights as a homeowner, then yes, they can. But most do not. Perhaps in your neighborhood, you can't have privacy fences (my neighborhood in Colorado did not permit privacy fences other than in very limited circumstances, and even where they were allowed, it was not at the yard boundaries). In that case, you are visible from your neighbor's properties, and it might be considered reasonable for the HOA to create rules dictating appropriate attire for your backyard, so that everyone can enjoy their yards. In some cases, the HOA cannot regulate anything in the back yard that is not visible from the street or from a neighbor's yard. With some exceptions, Florida state statute does not allow HOAs to regulate what you do in your backyard if it is not visible from the street or from a neighbor's yard. So any time you have a question about a particular HOA, check that HOA's Governing Documents, because it says very clearly what the HOA can and cannot regulate.
