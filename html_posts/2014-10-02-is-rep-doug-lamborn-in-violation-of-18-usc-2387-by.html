**Content:** [If the member of the military was within his legal rights to resign, he can resign for whatever reason he\'d like. Even being told by a sitting Congressman to resign. All perfectly legal.\ \ The law expressly forbids inciting someone to perform acts that are illegal, like insubordination or mutiny. Resigning when one has the free will to do so is not illegal, regardless of the reason the person gives to resign.\ \ In actuality, if you don\'t have any service commitments, you don\'t really give a reason for leaving AD, you just leave. Your DD-214 simply states \"completion of active duty service commitment\" as the reason for termination of active duty.]{.rendered_qtext}
