**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **As a renter, if the police come into my home without a warrant and without my permission, do I have the same rights as the homeowner? Can my landlord give them permission against my wishes?** Tenants enjoy the same privacy rights inside their rental units as homeowners do inside their homes. A landlord can only grant police entry without your permission if the police have a valid search warrant or if there is a bona fide police emergency. Otherwise, the landlord is bound by the normal landlord-tenant rules which state that a landlord must give written notice before he enters a rented property to perform maintenance without the tenant being present. If the landlord unlawfully granted entry to the police into your apartment without a warrant and evidence was collected, the evidence would be inadmissible and anything resulting from the evidence would be similarly inadmissible.
