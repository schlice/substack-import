**Content:** [You\'re right, in most cases, you\'ll be able to go about your day without ever needing your permit.\ \ But then that begs the question: why are you carrying a gun in the first place? Is it like a pendant that you keep inside your shirt? Only you know it\'s there? You\'ll never show it to anyone? No one will see it unless they see you remove your shirt?\ \ If, on the other hand, you carry a gun because you are preparing for the worst, being ready in case you may need it, then you may run into trouble. Because once you do use it, or even take it out (brandish it), you now face your own investigation into why you had concealed a weapon in the first place. You will be investigated each and every time you brandish and/or use your weapon, even if you were completely legal in your action. Even if you potentially stopped a mass shooting with your weapon, you would still face weapons charges for unlawfully concealing a weapon. The only way you *might* avoid prosecution is if public opinion was so far in your favor that the DA declined to press charges. Other than that, if you ever hope to actually ***use*** your weapon for more than just a lump in your waistband, in doing so, you subject yourself to prosecution.\ \ It\'s a hollow victory when you save your life or the life of another, only to be prosecuted on weapons charges.]{.rendered_qtext}
