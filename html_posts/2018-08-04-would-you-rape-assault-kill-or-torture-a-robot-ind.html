**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Would you rape, assault, kill, or torture a robot indulgently since it does not have rights?** Ah, so I see you've been watching [[*Westworld*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9ef183&url=https://www.hbo.com/westworld){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="hbo.com"}]{.qlink_container} then, have you? I'm on to Season 2. *Westworld* is a series on HBO which delves into this very question. It explores a future fantasy theme park, where "hosts" (robots) exist in an Old West re-creation. "Guests" can do whatever they like with the "hosts," including raping, assaulting, and "killing" the robots (who go offline when they are shot), only to have their memories wiped and be reset for the next round of guests. The guests cannot be killed by the weapons in the park, so they can live out their fantasies, as either a "white knight" or a "black hat." When one character (William, played by [[Jimmi Simpson](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ee20e1&url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0801051/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="imdb.com"}]{.qlink_container}) enters the park for the first time, he is actually confronted with the physical choice in color of hat, not too subtly offering him the choice to either be a "good guy" or a "bad guy." If you haven't seen the show, I won't ruin it for you. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-4300d8bdaf67f45129d36fcc729fcfee){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-4300d8bdaf67f45129d36fcc729fcfee" master_w="767" master_h="432"} ::: :::: At any rate, the show explores the idea that you *can* do these things with robots because they are specifically *not* human. The show also explores when "life" begins and what it means to be alive. *Can* you violate the *rights* of a non-sentient creature? Does a robot have rights? Legally, since a robot is not "alive," you cannot violate its rights, even if it is an expensive, complex, humanoid robot. It cannot be raped or tortured, because those are crimes against *people*. You can no more rape a robot than you can rape a bar stool. Now, to the question. Would ***I*** rape or torture or kill a robot simply because it does not have rights? I don't think so. It's not "fun" for me to rape or torture or kill, and I wouldn't role-play it with a robot simply because I could get away with it because it's not illegal. That's like saying that the only reason I don't rape, torture or kill today is because it's illegal. Like the only thing stopping me from doing it to a *human* is the law. Thankfully, my moral compass is slightly more evolved than that. I'm certain that there are people out there who would, and who are only deterred by the existence of the law. But I am not one of them.
