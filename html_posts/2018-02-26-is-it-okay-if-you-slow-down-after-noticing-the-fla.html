--- title: "Is it okay if you slow down after noticing the flashing school zone light?" date: "2018-02-26" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It sounds like you're asking whether or not it's okay if you missed the light but then slowed down as soon as you noticed the light. Perhaps you're asking whether or not it's okay to *start* slowing down after you pass the light. In either case, according to most vehicle and traffic laws in the United States, you are required to slow down *before* you enter the school zone. Once you enter the school zone, you should already be at the prescribed speed (usually 20 mph). Whether or not you will be ticketed is up to the officer stopping you. If he saw that you were slowing down, he might give you a warning, but school zones are the number one area where "children vs car" accidents occur, and cracking down on speeding in those areas is a top priority for law enforcement. I personally wouldn't expect much leniency, even if you did finally notice and start slowing down.
