**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If I'm a job seeker, why would you *not* tell me the salary range that your job pays? I may be looking at the totally wrong job, and I may spend weeks progressing through your interview process until I find out that your position doesn't pay me nearly what I'm worth, or more specifically, what I need to make. It only *benefits* a company to provide the starting salary range up-front so that you only get candidates who are willing and able to accept that salary to do that work. Please explain how disclosing a good-faith starting salary harms an employer. So far, the only complaint posed by recruiter groups is one of First Amendment reasoning, not that it harms the business, just that the recruiter has the right to not disclose the starting salary. \ Original question: Colorado has a law stating employers have to publish salary ranges, so some companies will not hire in Colorado for remote positions. What are some other state laws that harm business?
