Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > If Jesus was the son of God (or a prophet of God), why on Earth did he > not write down the word of God? Because it was already written down, thousands of years before He got there. Jesus, being a Jew, read from the Hebrew Bible. He actually came to fulfill the prophesies which were already written. After He died and was resurrected, others wrote about what He did. Clearly, He wouldn't be writing about His own life. Others did that for Him. > If you answer that he was illiterate, would that not reflect badly on > his own divine power? The *hypostatic union* describes the person of Jesus: *fully human, and fully God*, at the same time. He isn't some sort of superhuman, and He isn't some sort of demi-God. He is fully human and fully divine. But His divinity did not influence His humanity, and His humanity did not influence His divinity. There are examples throughout the Bible of both His humanity and His divinity, and neither were limited or influenced by the other.
