--- title: "Can a landlord make rules about what religion you can practice on their property?" date: "2024-10-17" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." In the ***vast*** majority of cases, the answer is absolutely no. The First Amendment and the Supremacy Clause makes certain of that. No law shall be made which abridges the free exercise of religion. But since there are always exceptions, here's one. While landlords cannot discriminate based on religion, they can implement reasonable rules regarding property use that *might impact* religious practices, **as long as these rules are not specifically targeting a particular religion** and are applied equally to all tenants. So you can't bypass the landlord's rules just because you're practicing religion, so long as the rule applies to all tenants regardless of whether or not they are practicing religion.
