**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you were able to legally purchase a fully automatic weapon such as an M16 or submachine gun, would you, and why?** Able by law or able by finances? Because there answer to the first question is that everyone is able to buy a fully automatic weapon. Some states have restrictions, and there are additional licensing steps you need to go through, but they are legal to own. The National Firearms Act limits fully automatic firearms for sale to the general public. No fully automatic weapon manufactured after May 19, 1986 can be registered to a civilian. To that end, the answer to the second question: finances. All fully automatic firearms made before May 19, 1986 have pretty much been registered by this point, and the market has been artificially limited. So let's go back to Supply and Demand 101: when demand goes up but supply is constant, price goes up. To that end, prices for transferable automatic weapons are very high. Where a new fully automatic M-16 might cost a police department about \$500, the limited supply makes the price for a transferable one well over \$10,000, and that doesn't count the licensing fees and the costs for the background investigations in order to be able to be licensed to own one. So to answer your full question (now that you know the background): if I had a spare \$10K, no I would not be spending it on a fully automatic firearm. I'm not a collector, and I have no desire to put myself through the nonsense licensing and registering that is required to own one. I am quite happy with my semi-auto rifles.
