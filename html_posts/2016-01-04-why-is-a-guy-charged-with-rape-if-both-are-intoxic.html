Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Gender bias. In the vast majority of cases, it is the male who is the aggressor and the female who is the victim. So charge the male, and in the vast majority of cases, you\'re going to get it right. But just \"charging\" someone with rape doesn\'t guarantee a \"conviction.\" And when both parties are intoxicated, then there is enough reasonable doubt that can be generated about what was consented to and what was not that it would be very challenging to actually get a conviction when both parties were admittedly intoxicated. Best advice: Don\'t have drunk sex. Too many legal issues.
