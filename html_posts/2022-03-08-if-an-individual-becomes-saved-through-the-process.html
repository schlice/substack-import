--- title: "If an individual becomes saved through the process of sanctification, are they to be set apart from the world but also to be a part as well to transform lives?" date: "2022-03-08" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Individuals are not saved through the process of sanctification. Individuals are saved through the process of **salvation**. What precedes salvation is a process called **regeneration.** Regeneration is a sovereign work of God which prepares us for salvation. *Regeneration precedes salvation because salvation is by faith, and dead men cannot have faith. God, therefore, has mercy and imparts life.* As for the second part of your question, yes, the saved are "set apart" from the world. In other words, you are *in* the world, but you are not *of* the world. You can be a light to the world (a conduit for Christ's light) while not being *of* the world, or belonging to the world. **This will cause you pain**. The world will reject you. The world will hate you. It's not easy to be a Christian. *Salvation is free, but it will cost you your life.* But take heart, for Christ has overcome the world. > "I have told you these things, so that in me you may have peace. In > this world you will have trouble. But take heart!
