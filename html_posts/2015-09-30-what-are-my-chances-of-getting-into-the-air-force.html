**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Question details:* > > *How can I get in?* > > *I am currently in 11th grade, 4.29 GPA, cadet in the Civil Air > Patrol, member of my school\'s Spanish honor society and the national > honor society, I have a varsity letter from my school\'s academic > team, and am taking about 7-8 AP classes throughout high school. I am > also taking a 4-year aerospace elective career track. I tutor kids > around 3 days a week in math, too. The only thing I lack is physical > stuff, which I will be working on. I am a girl, by the way.* The \"physical stuff\" is important. This is why only 52% of cadets who enter the Academy are in the top 10% of their respective high school classes. I\'m assuming by your statistics that you\'re in the top 10% of your class. You\'ve got everything you need already, except for the \"physical stuff.\" Take [[Tom Farrier](https://www.quora.com/profile/Tom-Farrier)]{.qlink_container}\'s advice and get into the Air Force Academy recruiting site. The Academy will be looking for athletic participation, usually in team sports. The \"physical stuff\" is very important, as females are expected to meet physical standards in the military as well as males. The actual numbers vary slightly between the sexes, but the standards are there for both. The website talks about what you will need to do to prepare for the Academy\'s physical requirements. You should be participating in one or more of these things: > - Rigorous team sports (ex. Soccer or Football) > - Strenuous individual sports (Swimming or Wrestling) > - An individual fitness program including both distance running and > conditioning exercises --- upper-body strength, running speed and > endurance should be your objectives You will have a [[Candidate Fitness Assessment](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=3b86bc&url=http://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/the-application-process/fitness-assessment/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} that you will have to pass before you can get into the Academy. And it is very physically demanding; don\'t forget that the Cadet Area at the Academy is at an altitude of 7258\' above sea level. If you\'re like me, coming from sea level, that transition is really tough. It\'s doable, but it\'s tough. Finally, remember that you\'re not applying for \"college\" so much as you\'re applying to become an Air Force officer. This is a completely different type of college: you\'re signing up to become one of the future leaders of the finest Air Force in the entire world. It is definitely the greatest challenge you will ever undertake: you will push yourself beyond what you think is possible. If that sounds a little scary, it\'s because it is. Challenges are sometimes scary. The leaders of character we need in the military are the ones who will push themselves beyond what they think they can achieve to learn what they really can achieve. Remember what Mark Twain said: \"Courage is resistance to fear, mastery of fear, not absence of fear.\" Keep fighting, and good luck!
