**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Considering the fact that your personality is, in part, made up of your past experiences, and considering that MS causes memory and cognition issues, it is conceivable that the disease itself can change your personality, as it will change what you remember about yourself. It's a challenge, and many patients with MS do change, not just because of physical changes in the brain, but because of many factors, including side effects from the MS medications, side effects from the medications taken to control the side effects of the MS medications, and general psychological changes related to having a lifelong chronic disease like MS. I know, because this happened to me. I may not be "critical, angry, paranoid and cruel," but my personality has changed, and it is hard for my loved ones to understand why or how. I wish I could go back to being "who I was," for their sakes, but I'm afraid I don't know who that was. I feel exactly the same, in my own mind. Which, I guess, is the problem, isn't it? If I don't know I've changed, and if I don't know how I've changed, it would be very hard for me to change back. It would be like becoming someone new. For me, I feel like it's hard enough being who I am, let alone trying to become someone else so that I can be more like someone I used to be, when I don't feel any different in the first place. Why can't they just accept that this is who I am now? Why do they want to make me someone I'm not? See the problem? This disease wears you down. Physically, mentally, emotionally, in every way possible. It doesn't kill you, but after 24 years of having to deal with it, I could understand how it could so wear you down that you'd want to be dead. Your family wants to help you, but in doing so they make you feel more like a cripple, so you really just want them to stop or leave, so you subconsciously try to force them away by being bitter. This is where some therapy for both the patient and the caregiver can be very helpful. The patients need to understand that the caregivers are only trying to help, and the caregivers need to understand that too much helping actually hurts. The local [[MS Society chapters](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=28f09c&url=http://www.nationalmssociety.org/Chapters){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} can help find individual and group therapy which may be helpful.
