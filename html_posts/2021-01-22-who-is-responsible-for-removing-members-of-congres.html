**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Who is responsible for removing members of Congress under Amendment 14 article 3? Is it up to each house if Congress or is an independent panel set up to hear and decide the outcome?** The 14th Amendment was one of the Reconstruction amendments. Everything in there has an eye toward the Civil War and the Confederacy. Any discussion about "insurrection" were well-understood: section 3 was designed to prevent former US military members, as well as federal and state representatives, who served the Confederacy from serving in public office unless that "disability" was removed by a 2/3 vote of each house of Congress. It is up to Congress to determine internally whether or not a member is ineligible to serve under Section 3. The most likely remedy is *expulsion*, which can be accomplished for any reason with a 2/3 vote of that chamber. Expulsion is prescribed in Article I Section 5: \"Each House \[of Congress\] may determine the Rules of its proceedings, punish its members for disorderly behavior, and, with the concurrence of two-thirds, expel a member.\"
