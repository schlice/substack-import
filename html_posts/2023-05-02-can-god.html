**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It depends on what you mean by freedom. So let's start by defining freedom, which I can probably presume you mean "free will." The applicable definition of "free will" is that a person has the power to make the ultimate decision. So can God be sovereign over all (including our free will) and yet we still have free will? The answer is **yes. Because if it is anything other than yes, then we do not have free will.** Think about it this way. If I have a gun and I am pointing it at you, I literally have the power to take your life. I have *sovereignty* over your life at that very moment. But I choose not to do so, because I don't want you dead (and I don't want to go to jail for murder), so I don't. I have the power, but I do not exercise it. Similarly God is indeed sovereign over all (including our free will). He does exercise some influence (in a process we call *regeneration*), but He does not exercise complete or ultimate influence, because He doesn't want to. He truly desires for us to be free and to make the choice to love Him freely. That means that the *ultimate* decision is ours. > "But there are some of you who do not believe." (For Jesus knew from > the beginning who those were who did not believe, and who it was who > would betray him.) And he said, "This is why I told you that no one > can come to me unless it is granted him by the Father." --- John > 6:64--65 ESV God does exercise His will in granting new hearts to those who He chooses. God *regenerates* them, giving them hearts which will love God. But the choice to follow Jesus is still ours, *even if God allows us to love Him.* Therefore, just because God has the power over our free will doesn't mean we don't have free will. It would only mean we don't have free will if God exerts His control over our self-determination, which He does not do. \ But more importantly, and this probably gets at the very heart of this question, *if God has sovereignty over our free will,* ***are we still accountable for our choices?*** And that answer is assuredly **yes**. We have free will. We can make our own choices, and we are indeed responsible over our choices, and we will account for them. We will, as Jesus says, "give account for every careless word" (Matthew 12:36). Indeed, we will give an account, according to Romans 14:12, of all of our preferences and choices and behaviors. Each of us will give an account of himself to God.
