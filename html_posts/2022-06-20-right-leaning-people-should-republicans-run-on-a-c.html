**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Politically speaking,* in today's climate, a complete pro-life stance is unworkable. The public feeling for rape victims precludes "forcing" them to continue their unwanted pregnancy, so the platform actually ends up being "pro-life with a few exceptions." *However, if you frame it correctly,* the pro-life position is a workable platform. To date, no one has done so. To frame it correctly, you must look at the argument from an **equal rights** view. If you are to claim that everyone has equal rights, you must logically include unborn babies in that group. A baby has as much right to life as its mother. The mother's rights and the baby's rights are equal. No one's rights "trump" anyone else's rights. The rights compare equally, in all cases. Rights always compare equally in every other case of rights as they are listed in the Bill of Rights, why suddenly in this case does a person not have the right to life because of an intersection with someone else's rights? This means that **bodily autonomy has limits**. And the limit of the mother's bodily autonomy is at the fetus. The mother's right to bodily autonomy does not reduce the baby's right to life. The mother using her body to care for and grow the baby does not limit the baby's right to continued existence. The fact that the mother may not have wanted to end up pregnant does not limit the baby's right to life. But of course, this is the point that the pro-choice people trot out the edge cases, like the 13-year old rape victim who is now pregnant. But you can't use the edge to ignore the middle. While I certainly feel for the 13-year old, I also feel for the baby, who didn't ask to be in this situation either, but here we are. But we're not ready for that discussion yet. From a practical standpoint, "pro-life with a few exceptions" is the appropriate platform to run on, and it is the stance that the majority of state laws are implementing, in advance of the overturning of *Roe*. Abortion on demand is mostly illegal (some states allow it early in the pregnancy) but past a certain point, abortion becomes illegal unless there is a reported rape, or when the physical life of the mother is at risk.
