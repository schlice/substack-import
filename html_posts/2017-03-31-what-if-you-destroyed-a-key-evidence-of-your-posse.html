--- title: '"What if you destroyed a key evidence of your possession after the opposing party find out its existence but before a court orders you to turn over?"' date: '2017-03-31' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In most jurisdictions, the notice that a claim has been filed also includes directions about not destroying any evidence, even if that evidence has not been requested through discovery yet. From the moment the claim has been made, and you have been notified about it, the evidence is protected. If you destroyed the evidence before you were notified of a claim, you might be protected, but only if you had no knowledge that a claim was forthcoming. If you knew that a claim was being processed, and you destroyed evidence with that knowledge, and the opposing party is able to prove that you knew the claim was being processed, the destruction of evidence can cause you to lose your case.
