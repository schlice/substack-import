**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Hebrew Bible, also known as the Old Testament, is a collection of sacred books that developed over about a thousand years. The oldest texts are thought to date back to the 10th or 11th centuries BC. The Torah, the first five books of the Bible, were likely finalized and canonized during the Babylonian Exile in the 6th--5th century BC. The entire Hebrew Bible was complete by about 100 AD. The earliest examples of written Paleo-Hebrew date back to the 10th century BC. The Khirbet Qeiyafa ostracon, which was found in a dig at the ancient fortress city near Beit Shemesh, is the earliest Hebrew writing previously found, dating back to 1000 BC. But you talk about "the oldest evidence we have of god" when these writings are not "evidence" of God. They are the oldest writings we have about God, but they are not "evidence" of God.
