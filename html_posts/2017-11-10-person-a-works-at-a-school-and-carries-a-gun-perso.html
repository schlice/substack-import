--- title: "Person A works at a school and carries a gun. Person B also carries a gun and kills people. Person A shoots person B and kills them in self defence. Will Person A be charged anyways for having a gun inside the school? What will be the punishment?" date: "2017-11-10" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is **it depends.** It would have to be determined if Person A was lawfully carrying a gun on school property. Most school districts only allow armed people who are not the police in the school if they are registered security guards with the district. Some school districts allow *teachers* to volunteer to take the training and become registered security guards. This allows them to legally carry a gun on school property. These same teachers also have to apply and be granted a concealed carry permit from the local police department. This is all **on their own time, with their own funds.** They are not paid to carry a firearm, nor are they paid to be a security guard. Assuming this is the case (or assuming this particular school district allows properly registered and armed teachers on school property), Person A (who works at a school and carries a gun lawfully) would not likely be charged with any crime. **In any other case**, Person A would not likely be permitted to carry a gun onto school grounds, *even if they have a concealed carry permit from the local police or Sheriff*. If Person A shoots Person B in self defense, they will probably not face any criminal charges, but they may face charges for unlawfully concealing a firearm on school property.
