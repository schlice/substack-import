**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is one of those items you truly forget you have with you until TSA finds it. At one time I received one of these gimmicky folding credit card knives that looked substantially like this as a free gift: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-7a86ccfe8fb4ed2d79d08a61bdf40d61){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-7a86ccfe8fb4ed2d79d08a61bdf40d61" master_w="480" master_h="360"} ::: :::: The wallet I had it in was one of these metallic wallets: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-7a0a0fc3f775ddbdd9c4425755de6b47){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-7a0a0fc3f775ddbdd9c4425755de6b47" master_w="350" master_h="318"} ::: :::: By the way, these wallets are great if you, like me, sit on your leather wallet and often break credit cards because the wallet bends. Long story short, I ping going through the magnetometer. I recognize my wallet is still on me, so thinking my wallet pinged the machine, I send it through the X-ray, at which time the metal blade of the knife becomes fully visible on the screen. It was the knife blade that pinged the magnetometer, not the aluminum wallet. Anyway, I told them to throw it away, because it would have cost me more to send it home than I paid for it (which was nothing).
