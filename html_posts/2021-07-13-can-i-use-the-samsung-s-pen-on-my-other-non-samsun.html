[[Bill Stein\'s answer to Is the Samsung S Pen compatible with any tablet?](/Is-the-Samsung-S-Pen-compatible-with-any-tablet/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} The Samsung S Pen is an *inductive* stylus. It communicates with the Samsung Note phone when the device is brought close to the screen. The Note series (and the S21 series of phones) contain an extra Wacom layer which supports the inductive stylus. The electromagnetic Wacom layer in the screen *induces* a current in the S Pen, and they begin to communicate. This is why the stylus works without it touching the screen. It knows where the tip of the stylus is, even when you hover it over the screen and don't touch it. Other non-Samsung Android phones use *capacitive touch* screens only (Note phones also use capacitive touch, but the stylus uses inductive communications). In a capacitive touch setup, the screen is given an electrostatic charge. Your finger (or the stylus) changes the electrostatic field in the screen when you touch it, and the device registers that change as a touch, or a swipe. **TL:DR:** No. The S Pen only works on S Pen-enabled devices. You need a capacitive stylus to work on capacitive touch screens. \ Original question: Can I use the Samsung S Pen on my other non-Samsung Android phone?
