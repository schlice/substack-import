--- title: '"What guns are legal in all fifty states out of the box, with minimal difficulty, and outside of special circumstances?"' date: '2017-03-17' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It's not so much the gun as it is the state that you're in which matters. There is no gun that is simply universally accepted in every state without any hassle. It's up to the gun owner to know the laws of the state into which they are entering and to be prepared to have their weapons confiscated until such time as they get themselves properly registered in that state. In many states, only state residents may register weapons. Guests are often not allowed to register weapons (where weapon registry is mandated by the state), and the process to get registered (if you're even allowed to do it as a non-resident) may take longer than the amount of time you're going to be in the state.
