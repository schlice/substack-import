**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: **Trump is wrong, but so is Kaepernick.** Longer answer: Trump is wrong. This country welcomes free speech, even if it's speech you don't agree with. That includes both Kaepernick's speech and Trump's speech as well. If you allow Kaepernick to express his views, you have to allow Trump to do the same. However, the US is the most inclusive, most welcoming country in the world. No other country is quite as easy to become a citizen as ours is. We (used to) welcome dissenting speech, and people who question and think generally seek out those with whom they disagree to engage in healthy, thought-provoking debate. We have become so sensitive in this country, we can no longer engage in debate because we fear upsetting someone. We have done our children a disservice by teaching them that they have the right to never be offended. Nevertheless, Trump, in trying to spin up his base, is actually portraying the wrong message to the nation, and to the world. I'm sorry Trumpers, but he's wrong on this one. Kaepernick is wrong as well. 1. While he has the right to express his opinion, it is disrespectful and based on lies. 2. Also, the NFL is wrong in allowing this nonsense to go on during a football game. Let's address point 1. Kaepernick is sitting during the National Anthem to protest police treatment of blacks in this country. Groups like the ACLU, Black Lives Matter, and the Obama administration parrot this narrative. ***EXCEPT IT'S COMPLETELY WRONG***. The chief conceit of the Black Lives Matter movement is that the most dangerous thing in the life of a young black man is a racist police officer. This is a **lie**. To be clear, this is not a factual error, or a misstatement. A **lie** is *intentionally misrepresenting the truth to gain some advantage or express an opinion that would not otherwise be served by the truth.* That is what we have here. If you do not believe that Black Lives Matter is lying to you, take literally five minutes out of your day and get the facts: [[https://youtu.be/UQCQFH5wOJo](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=deeb13&url=https://youtu.be/UQCQFH5wOJo){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"}]{.qlink_container} As you can see, there is no government agency in this country that is more dedicated to the lives of black people than the police. When a 911 call comes in, the operator does not ask the color of the person's skin before dispatching officers. Officers go towards the gunshots, regardless of who fired them. The whole foundation of the Black Lives Matter movement is **a lie**. And Kaepernick is wasting his stage protesting *that?* This is, indeed, sad. If you want to protest something, protest the fact that illegal immigrants *do* take jobs from honest American citizens, because employers get away with hiring them, and then underpaying them because they don't take taxes out of their pay. The government does nothing about this. Protest the fact that Americans step on and burn the flag as a form of "protest" and "free speech." "If you want a symbolic gesture, don\'t *burn* the flag; *wash* it."[\[1\]](#xezzw){#cite-xezzw .citation_link} In other words, *be the change you want to see in the world. Protests are great, but get off your butt and* ***do something about it.*** William McRaven, Chancellor of the University of Texas System (a former Navy SEAL) sent the following regarding Kaepernick's actions (emphasis mine): > Honoring the flag does not imply that the republic for which it stands > is perfect. Far from it, honoring the flag is our collective > commitment that we will constantly attempt to get better as a nation, > to improve as a people, and to use the freedoms we have been given to > make the earth a better place. > > I spent 37 years defending freedom of speech and freedom of > expression. Nothing is more important to this democracy. Nothing! > However, while no one should be compelled to stand, **they should > recognize that by sitting in protest to the flag they are > disrespecting everyone who sacrificed to make this country what it is > today --- as imperfect as it might be.** > > Those that believe the flag represents oppression should remember all > the Americans who fought to eliminate bigotry, racism, sexism, > imperialism, communism, and terrorism. The flag rode with the Buffalo > Soldiers of the 9th, 10th, 24th and 25th Calvary and Infantry > Regiments. It was carried by the suffragists down the streets of New > York City. It flew with the Tuskegee Airmen of WWII. It was planted in > the fields where Cesar Chavez spoke. It marched with Martin Luther > King Jr. It rocketed into space on the shoulder patches of women, > gays, Hispanic, Asian and African American astronauts. Today it waves > high over the White House. **It is a flag for everyone, of every > color, of every race, of every creed, and of every orientation, but > the privilege of living under this flag does not come without cost. > Nor should it come without respect.** > > The nation and everything it strives for is embodied in the American > flag. We strive to be more inclusive. We strive to be more > understanding. We strive to fix the problems that plague our society. > > But in striving to do so, we must have a common bond; some symbol that > reminds us of our past struggles and propels us to a brighter, more > enlightened future.[\[2\]](#nrRbP){#cite-nrRbP .citation_link} Sitting to protest the flag disrespects everyone who has ever fought for the ideals for which it stands. Sitting to protest ideas which are wrong just makes you look stupid as well as disrespectful. No one is saying you don't have the right to do it. We're just saying that doing so is not without consequence. And speaking of allowing you to do it, let's talk about the NFL. People tune in to the NFL because they want to watch ***football***. They tune in to Monday Night Football (and now Sunday and Thursday) because they want to be *entertained*. They want to escape from the stresses of the day and relax watching a non-political sporting match. They really don't want to see political protests during a football game. Yes, players are entitled to their free speech, **but there are consequences for every action you take**. I've taught my kids that since they were very little. Some consequences are good (get good grades and maybe get to go out for ice cream), and some are bad (get bad grades and lose screen time). But there are always consequences for every action. Just because you make political speech does not shield you from consequences. I know plenty of people who will no longer watch football because of this nonsense, and I am included in that bunch. I'll watch *theScore* on my phone and get score updates over Twitter. I don't need to watch even more political drama on TV. The NFL, by allowing these charades to go on *during a football game,* risks alienating the **very people** who pay the salaries that allow people like Kaepernick to have the stage to make those protests. In other words, **the NFL risks losing customers over this**.
