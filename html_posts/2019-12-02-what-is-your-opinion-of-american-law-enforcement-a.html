**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What is your opinion of American law enforcement agencies asking for proof of citizenship?** As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends," and today, it depends on who you're talking about when you say "American law enforcement agencies." American law enforcement agencies *meaning police who work for a state* should not ask for proof of citizenship. It's not the state's job to be the arbiter of US citizenship. That's a Federal government function; the Courts have said as much. American law enforcement agencies *meaning Federal law enforcement agents like the FBI or DHS* **can** and **should** ask for proof of citizenship, if it is reasonably in question, especially if citizenship is at issue in whatever they are doing.
