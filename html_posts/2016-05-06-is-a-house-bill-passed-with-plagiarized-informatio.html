--- title: '"Is a House Bill passed with plagiarized information from an unpublished work valid?"' date: '2016-05-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The source of the information in legislation does not invalidate the bill. The information from the book may have been used as \"reference material\" and should be cited appropriately, but there are really no copyright issues here since the US Government does not publish works for sale, it writes laws. I would hope that a simple letter to the Office of the Law Revision Counsel would correct any oversight in failing to properly attribute the quote, especially during positive law codification if the bill ever becomes law and is added to US Code.
