--- title: "What is the connection between atheism and immorality? Do atheists tend to be more or less moral than people of other belief systems?" date: "2023-09-20" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The problem you're running into is a fundamental misunderstanding of morality, and by whose standards do you judge how moral someone is. Morality for all people is something which is developed throughout your childhood and upbringing, for theists and atheists alike. Your morals are shaped throughout your childhood and into your adult life. The difference between the theist and the atheist is **the source of that morality**. - For the atheist, the source of that morality is the **shared moral goals of society.** - For the theist, the source of that morality is **that religion's deity and the teachings of that religion.** The problem arises when atheists attempt to interpret the shared moral goals of society. The big stuff, clearly, is well understood in society. Don't murder, don't kick old ladies in the shins, etc. All societies all over the world share these moral imperatives (which raises an interesting question in and of itself, but we'll ignore that for now). But as we get down into the weeds, we start to see variation, not only between societies, but between people themselves. How high do we value virtue? To what extent is philanthropy noble? When do I have to stop to help that person, or when can I go on about my merry way? These are most definitely variable when you try to interpret society's position and then impart it on to your children. But make no mistake: **to each individual, those actions are 100% moral.** But if the ***source*** of your morality is not solid, unchanging, and timeless, then the morals you may develop over your lifetime may not match the morals of others in society. This is the problem when you do not have an unchanging source of morals. Your morals, essentially, become ***self-referential***. So are atheists, by default, less moral than people of other belief systems? No, not at all. They believe they are acting 100% in accordance with their morals, and they are. *The disconnect is in comparing their morals with the morals of others.*
