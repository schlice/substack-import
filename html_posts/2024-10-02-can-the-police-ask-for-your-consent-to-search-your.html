--- title: "Can the police ask for your consent to search your vehicle if they already have probable cause?" date: "2024-10-02" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They *can*, but they *won't*. They'll only ask for your consent if they don't have probable cause to search. If you consent, it's an immediate waiver of your 4th Amendment rights, and the police can search with impunity. If they have probable cause, they will ask you to step out of the vehicle, and then they will conduct their search. Probable cause may be, among other things, "evidence in plain sight," such as seeing drug paraphernalia, or the smell of marijuana (the "plain smell" test is a subset of the "plain sight" test). The police can search your vehicle with nothing more than probable cause in 49 states (in the state of Washington, the police must use their probable cause to obtain a warrant).
