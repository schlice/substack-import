**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not at all. What it ***will*** do is to signal to the Democrats that they can stop playing this silly game of pretending that Republicans are actually ***relevant.*** Democrats consider Republicans to be the "Washington Generals." Remember them? They're the team that plays the Harlem Globetrotters, always destined to lose, just there to be the straight-man to the Globetrotters' antics, but they still get to "play" and they still get a paycheck. If Hillary wins, it will be confirmation that they are the **only** party that matters, and it's time to quit pretending we have a two-party system. America's Anchorman, Rush Limbaugh, was discussing this very thing on his program on September 7th. He was reading from an article over on the Claremont Institute website titled "[[The Flight 93 Election](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6e2441&url=http://www.claremont.org/crb/basicpage/the-flight-93-election/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="claremont.org"}]{.qlink_container}." It's an absolutely brilliant piece, but very long. It is critical of conservative "Never Trump"ers. Nevertheless, this is an excerpt from Rush's show where he is reading a section of the article discussing what a Hillary election will signal to the Democrat Party.
