--- title: "Can I become a pilot wearing Ortho-K lenses?" date: "2017-08-23" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The question is about becoming a pilot. I will address private pilots and orthokeratology (Ortho-K), since it is a non-starter for military service. Any condition corrected by Ortho-K is [[disqualifying for military service](https://www.quora.com/Is-eyesight-required-for-the-US-Army)]{.qlink_container}. As far as the FAA is concerned, they have the following to say about Ortho-K: > Orthokeratology (Ortho-K) is the use of rigid gas-permeable contact > lenses, normally worn only during sleep, to improve vision through > reshaping of the cornea. It is used as an alternative to eyeglasses, > refractive surgery, or for those who prefer not to wear contact lenses > while awake. The correction is not permanent and visual acuity can > regress while not wearing the Ortho-K lenses. There is no reasonable > or reliable way to determine standards for the entire period the > lenses are removed. > > Therefore, to be found qualified, applicants who use Ortho-K lenses > **must meet the applicable vision standard while wearing the Ortho-K > lenses AND must wear the Ortho-K lenses while piloting aircraft**. The > limitation **must use Ortho-K lenses while performing pilot duties** > must be placed on the medical certificate. [\[1\]](#PeGvq){#cite-PeGvq > .citation_link} **TL;DR: You can become a pilot using Ortho-K lenses. The caveat: While Ortho-K lenses are supposed to be worn at night, you will have to be able to meet the vision standards while wearing Ortho-K lenses, AND you will have to wear your lenses while you fly.** Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-PeGvq){.citation_index} [[Guide for Aviation Medical Examiners](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e86ab1&url=https://www.faa.gov/about/office_org/headquarters_offices/avs/offices/aam/ame/guide/app_process/exam_tech/et/31-34/ok/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="faa.gov"}]{.qlink_container}
