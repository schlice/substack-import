--- title: "In the US, is it legal for a pedestrian to walk along the side of a two-lane road (not a highway)?" date: "2016-01-27" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In most jurisdictions, the \"side of the road\" is not technically the road. It is called the \"shoulder.\" Legally, bicycles and other unlicensed vehicles (i.e. ATV\'s) cannot use the sidewalk, as it is exclusively reserved for pedestrians. Those vehicles must use the shoulder, as far off the road as is practical. Sometimes the shoulder is marked with a solid white line, :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-890092af2acadca8258406e0d4e5c717){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-890092af2acadca8258406e0d4e5c717" master_w="3648" master_h="2736"} ::: :::: sometimes it is not. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6c798466b351c3d139bd84029528cc70){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6c798466b351c3d139bd84029528cc70" master_w="520" master_h="315"} ::: :::: Sometimes the shoulder is wide enough to fit a whole vehicle (in the case of a disabled vehicle), sometimes it is barely wide enough to fit a bicycle and rider. When the sidewalk is impassable, but the shoulder is available (or if there is no sidewalk), pedestrians can use the shoulder along with bicyclists, assuming it is safe to do so.
