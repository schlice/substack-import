**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should we have a second version of the constitution for private companies to protect the employees?** No. The Constitution is not a document related to protecting employees. The Constitution is designed to define what government is, what government is authorized to do (in the first part), and what government is not authorized to do (in the first ten Amendments, called the Bill of Rights). If you want to protect employees, you pass laws. If you think that there are not enough laws defining how private companies are required to protect employees, then you probably need to re-take your Business 101 class. But by all means, if you believe that new laws are needed, lobby your Congresspeople to get those laws written. Or better yet, find an affinity group who shares your views. There's power in numbers.
