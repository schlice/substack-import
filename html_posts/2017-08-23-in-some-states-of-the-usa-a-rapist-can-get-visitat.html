--- title: "In some states of the USA, a rapist can get visitation rights to a child he fathered through his crime. Why does this law exist? Can you explain the origins of this law?" date: "2017-08-23" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the eyes of the court, a child needs a family. The rapist (for better or worse) is the father of the child. Regardless of how he came to be the father, he is. Since he is the biological father, he retains his right to visitation with his child, because ultimately, it's not the "sensibilities" of the adults which are at issue, it is the needs of a child which are at issue. The father can request visitation and it can be granted. Conditions can be applied, and the mother would likely demand such conditions (such as supervision during visitation, etc).
