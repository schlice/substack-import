--- title: "How is it legal for the police to lie to me when it is illegal for me to lie to them? Does this violate the 14th Amendment?" date: "2017-12-21" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The only time you must be truthful with the police is when you are providing your name and/or otherwise identifying yourself. Almost all states require you to be truthful in that situation. Other than that, you are under no legal obligation to be truthful with the police. Whether or not it is a *good idea* to lie is a whole other story.
