--- title: "Why do you think God taught us to hate?" date: "2023-10-10" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} While He didn't "teach" us to hate, He created us with the capacity to both love and hate. This shouldn't be surprising. We naturally hate the things which destroy what we love. And if we are made in the image of God, then God too can hate. But the difference is that God's hate is *perfect*, while our hate can easily lead us to sin, as we cannot hate without ill will. We cannot love with a perfect love, nor can we hate with a perfect hatred. But God can both love and hate perfectly, because He is God. God can hate without sinful intent. He can hate the sinner in a perfectly holy way and still lovingly forgive the sinner at the moment of repentance and faith (Malachi 1:3; Revelation 2:6; 2 Peter 3:9). It might seem a contradiction that a God who is love can also hate. Yet that's exactly what the Bible says is true: God is love (1 John 4:8), and God hates (Hosea 9:15). God's nature is love (He always does what is best for others) and He hates that which is contrary to His nature (He hates that which is contrary to love).
