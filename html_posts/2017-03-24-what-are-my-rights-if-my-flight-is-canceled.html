--- title: '"What are my rights if my flight is canceled?"' date: '2017-03-24' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In general, the contract of carriage requires the company get you to where you are going. And that's about it. They try to get you there at the agreed-upon time, but that's not always feasible because of unforeseen circumstances. Some of these circumstances (i.e. things that are the airline's fault) entitle you to a refund. Some (especially weather-based ones) do not. Airlines have different policies depending on what caused the delay. But even if the airline puts you on a **bus** and drives you to your destination, you have completed the contract of carriage. I have had this happen to me. I live in Colorado Springs, and on occasion, I will have a connecting flight between Colorado Springs and Denver (about 75 miles between the airports). One time, the Colorado Springs Airport was completely closed due to weather. The airline decided to put us all on a bus and drive us to the Colorado Springs Airport. We actually still ended up in the terminal, still picked up our luggage at baggage claim, but our transport was a bus. There is no requirement in the contract of carriage that you will actually be transported by aircraft. The only requirement is that the carrier gets you where they said they were going to take you.
