**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What happens if a person with mental disabilities is caught sexually abusing children over the internet?** Well, you can't sexually assault someone you can't touch, but you can commit online sexual harassment. The problem, of course, is that the law hasn't fully caught up with the technology. Usually enforcement begins with the platform on which the abuse is taking place, but if law enforcement is involved, then it proceeds outside of the jurisdiction of the provider. The mental disability of the suspect can be a mitigating factor, but it does not eliminate the crime. It may cause the state to involuntarily commit the suspect to treatment, and/or to declare the suspect a "mental defective."
