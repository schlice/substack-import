**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You\'ll have better luck deleting the Constitution and installing a monarchy. But let\'s review anyway. To implement a firearms registry, you\'ll need to first repeal federal law: the Firearm Owner's Protection Act of 1986, which prohibits the Federal government from maintaining a national firearms registry. But before you repeal the law, you\'ll need to explain the benefit of such a registry. You\'ll need to show how such a registry would prevent crime. Finally, you\'ll have to identify all the current gun owners without violating their rights. Good luck getting them all to come forward voluntarily. There are some records of some gun purchases from FFLs who went out of business and turned their Form 4473s over to the ATF, but that only shows the first registered owner of record. If the gun was sold privately since then, there would be no record of where it went. Similarly, if the gun was lost or stolen, those records would also be useless. The truth of the matter is that very few criminals who use guns bought them legally. The vast majority of criminals bought their guns from unscrupulous FFLs who "conveniently lose paperwork," or guns illegally transported and sold with no paperwork at all. Some crimes are committed with legally purchased firearms which have been stolen, and a registry still does no good in this case. Finally, nearly all of the mass shooters who were mentally unstable passed all background checks. And what sort of \"monitoring\" would you find acceptable? A drone that follows a gun owner around 24x7? That doesn\'t seem a little intrusive to you? And that doesn\'t even address why you think conservatives are overwhelmingly criminals. I\'d love to see your statistics on that. I think that says more about you than about criminals.
