**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} According to the Americans with Disabilities Act, this is a reasonable accommodation (emphasis mine): > Examples of reasonable accommodations include making existing > facilities accessible; job restructuring; **part-time or modified work > schedules**; acquiring or modifying equipment; changing tests, > training materials, or policies; providing qualified readers or > interpreters; and reassignment to a vacant position. That said, the employer is not unreasonably cutting your hours. You were only going to be working for 5 1/2 hours anyway, assuming they gave you a 30 minute break. Cutting your hours to 4 hours does not change the essential nature of the job (it's still a part-time job), and since they are only cutting your hours from 5 1/2 to 4, they are only cutting 1 1/2 hours from your work day. If it is easier for them to schedule that as a business, it sounds like it would be a reasonable accommodation. I understand you're losing hours, but modifying your schedule in this way is probably not something you can consider unreasonable from a business perspective, and it appears to me to be reasonable. However, if this is a major concern to you (I know it is because you're losing hours), you should consider contacting an employment attorney who is knowledgeable about ADA law in your state.
