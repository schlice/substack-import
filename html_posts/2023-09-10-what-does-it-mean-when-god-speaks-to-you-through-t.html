--- title: '"What does it mean when God speaks to you through the Bible or other things that are not Him speaking directly to you (audibly)?"' date: '2023-09-10' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} God rarely speaks to people audibly these days. Is that a sign that He has changed? Or is that a sign that *we* have changed? Actually, I don\'t think either of those are true. I think God doesn\'t speak audibly to us anymore because ***the message*** has changed. We don\'t need the audible voice of God in our lives anymore. We have the Bible. If we read the Bible with an open heart and we ask the Holy Spirit to help us understand it, that\'s where the voice of God comes from. So if you are hearing the voice of God while you read the Bible, rejoice! God speaks to us through His Word, which is timeless. Some Christians take a really long time to get to the point where you are, where you\'re actually hearing the voice of God through the Bible. Being able to quiet your mind and open your heart enough to let the Spirit come in and speak to you is a skill, one that not even all active Christians have mastered (myself included). I actually have to be cautious myself, because I can easily slip into envy when I see how easily some people hear God\'s word. **TL;DR**: if you read the Bible with an open heart, and you hear the voice of God from its pages, you are doing something right!
