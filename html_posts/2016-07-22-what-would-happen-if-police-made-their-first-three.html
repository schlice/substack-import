--- title: '"What would happen if police made their first three shots non-lethal and loaded them with rubber bullets? It seems like most bad shootings are one or two shots. In cases where police have to actually stop a threat, they just keep firing anyways."' date: '2016-07-22' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is, again, another attempt to absolve criminals of poor behavior by removing lethal force from the police arsenal. By "dumbing down" the gun and making it do something which you think is "non-lethal," you think you can solve the problems of people being stupid. The gun is lethal force. The officer uses lethal force when he reasonably believes he or the public is at risk of death or grave bodily harm. As long as his belief is reasonable based on the circumstances and the facts at hand, the officer is justified in using deadly force. What if he's in a gunfight? An armed suspect is firing on him? His gun is loaded with rubber bullets! It's like bringing a wet noodle to a gun fight. Now you've actually hurt the officer. We need better/more advanced/more accurate less-than-lethal options. We don't need to dumb down the gun. The officer is supposed to be smart enough to know when to use the gun and when not to. When the officer reasonably believes his life is in danger or he is at risk of grave bodily harm, his use of force is justified.
