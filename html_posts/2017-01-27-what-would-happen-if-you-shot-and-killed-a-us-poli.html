--- title: '"What would happen if you shot and killed a US police officer in self-defense while he was raiding your home?"' date: '2017-01-27' tags: - theology - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You would be arrested and charged with manslaughter, the fact that the person you killed was a police officer would be an aggravating factor in most jurisdictions. As we've said many times on Quora, the intersection between the Castle Doctrine and the No-Knock Warrant is a dangerous one. First, we must assume that both of those exist (you live in a jurisdiction with a strong Castle Doctrine and the police had a No-Knock Warrant). If it was not a no-knock warrant and the police announced themselves before and during their entry, shooting them is murder, and you go to jail if you're still alive. The only scenario in which you might go to trial for aggravated manslaughter is if the police were executing a no-knock warrant. If you have no clear Castle Doctrine in your locality, you also go down for murder, because you don't have an absolute right to defend your house with deadly force. So for the sake of this answer, let's assume both of these are true. You get arrested and charged with aggravated manslaughter. Then you go to trial. Do you win? Hard to say. It hasn't happened very often. But homeowners have indeed won in some of these cases. It is tough; public opinion tends to favor law enforcement when they are operating in accordance with the law. So there's no sure answer for how it will go at trial.
