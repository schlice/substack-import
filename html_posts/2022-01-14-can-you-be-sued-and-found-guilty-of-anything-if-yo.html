**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} (US answer only) Not really, but as this is a legal question, the answer really is "it depends." The statement "you are an asshole" is an *opinion*. Opinions cannot be construed as fact, so you cannot legally be lying about someone by calling them an asshole. You certainly can't be found guilty of slander or libel. What you *can* potentially be charged with would be *harassment* if you call someone an asshole so many times that the behavior is considered to be - Targeting someone - With behavior meant to alarm, annoy, torment, or terrorize, *and* - Creating reasonable fear in the victim for their safety or the safety of their family or property Otherwise, just telling someone they're an asshole is just an opinion. You can't be sued for expressing your opinion. \ Original question: Can you be sued and found guilty of anything, if you tell someone \"You are an ass hole\" in an email context?
