**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **The family of Breonna Taylor received 12 million in civil court. If this event led to a cash settlement and law changes needed to be made, doesn\'t it make more sense to charge the officers and force them to pay rather than the uninvolved taxpayers?** Maybe. But those investigations are still ongoing. That's why most people say that this payout by Louisville was premature and that the mayor's office does not support the officers who work there.[\[1\]](#tkYGl){#cite-tkYGl .citation_link} While the payout may still have been made, most people still believe that the payout should have waited until the conclusion of the investigation into the officers' conduct. Kentucky Attorney General Daniel Cameron appointed himself special prosecutor to look into whether criminal charges should be filed against the officers in the case. No timeline has been released for that decision. The FBI also is conducting a separate investigation in her shooting. And usually, it is the Department of Justice which investigates shootings to see if the victim's civil rights were violated. None of this was allowed to complete before the city decided to pay.
