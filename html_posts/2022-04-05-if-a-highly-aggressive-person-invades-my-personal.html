**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Question Details:* > > *He got out of a car twice and approached me. I warned him both times. > The second time he ignored me and got within arms reach just barely. > Seemingly to throw my warning in my face. No direct verbal threats or > arm actions. But HIGHLY angry, shouting and approaching me VERY > aggressively. Proof burden?* As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." In general, in "stand your ground" states, there is no duty to retreat from aggressive people. Obviously, the best choice is to do so, *if you can*. If the aggressive person is blocking your path, then retreat may not be an option. In such a case, you must still believe that there is a threat of *imminent use of unlawful force* before you can respond with non-lethal force in order to prevent that assault. This is the part which varies wildly from state to state. Some states say that an assault must occur first; others say that the threat of assault is enough. TL;DR: Check your state laws on assault and your ability to defend against it before you go popping some aggressive person in the nose and getting arrested for assault yourself. \ Original question: If a highly aggressive person invades my personal space (arms length) after I\'ve repeatedly warned them not to am I legally allowed to strike first?
