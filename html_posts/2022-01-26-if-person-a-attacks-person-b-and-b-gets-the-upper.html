**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(US answer only)* If person A believes that his life is in danger, or he is in danger of grave bodily harm, he can use whatever force he has at his disposal, ***up to and including deadly force***, to defend his life. This is known in the United States as **the right to self-defense.** Now, every state has different rules around the use of force, and what justifies it, and when can you use it, etc., but nearly every state is similar in that you have the right to defend your life if you are in fear of losing it. \ Original question: If person A attacks person B and B gets the upper hand and begins beating person A to a pulp, does person A have the right to defend himself with a gun if he had one? \ *This answer is not professional legal advice, nor is it a substitute for professional legal advice, or a solicitation to offer professional legal advice.
