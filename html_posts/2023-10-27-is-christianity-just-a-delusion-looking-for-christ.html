**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The "evidence against our faith?" You mean the evidence that God does not exist? Because I have yet to see that evidence. No one has yet been able to give me conclusive evidence that God does not exist. But I do have positive, empirically detectable evidence of design in the human cell, and the human brain. I do have positive, empirically detectable evidence to prove that the universe had a beginning and therefore is not eternal and could not have created us. In other words, the evidence we do have lines up pretty well with the theistic worldview as opposed to the atheistic worldview. We know that the universe had a beginning and thus a cause. You can't get something out of nothing, so something must have caused the universe to come into existence. Does it make more sense to say that *no one* created something out of nothing, or that *someone* created something out of nothing? Which one of those positions is more reasonable? The theistic worldview is more reasonable. Which position requires more faith to believe? Does it require more faith to believe that *no one* created something out of nothing, or that *someone* created something out of nothing? The atheistic worldview requires more faith. Therefore, when it comes to the question of evidence against my faith, it takes more faith to be an atheist.
