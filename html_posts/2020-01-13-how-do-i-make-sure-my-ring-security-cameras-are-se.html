**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I make sure my Ring security cameras are secure from hackers?** [[You probably have heard news reports](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0bce20&url=https://blog.ring.com/2019/12/12/rings-services-have-not-been-compromised-heres-what-you-need-to-know/?medium%3Dtsa%26gclid%3DCjwKCAiAx_DwBRAfEiwA3vwZYhfMSNlU8_zdBvcU5IrP92hG2-K8Q738WbQ6IIWicbCo81NTj6pwEBoCDjYQAvD_BwE%26gclsrc%3Daw.ds){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ring.com"}]{.qlink_container} that Ring cameras were compromised. This isn't exactly what happened. Ring users re-used the same username and password for separate, external, non-Ring services, and malicious actors gained access to those credentials and used them to access Ring accounts. **Ring itself is secure, it was never directly compromised.** But in case you haven't heard this a million times before, let me say it one more time: ***DO NOT REUSE YOUR PASSWORDS ACROSS WEBSITES!*** Oftentimes, your username is your email address, so malicious actors can learn your username simply by scraping email addresses. However, you should be implementing some smart, common-sense security practices to keep all your accounts safe, not just Ring: - **Never re-use passwords between websites.** Use a password manager like [[LastPass](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2813ec&url=http://lastpass.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="lastpass.com"}]{.qlink_container}, [[1Password](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=bb2366&url=https://1password.com){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="1password.com"}]{.qlink_container}, or [[KeePass](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8b7a71&url=https://keepass.info/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="keepass.info"}]{.qlink_container}. Generate strong passwords for every one of your accounts on every website. Never re-use passwords between websites. LastPass even offers a "security challenge" where it checks over all of the stored passwords in your browser and looks for reused passwords, helping you reset them to strong passwords which it securely stores. - **Use two-factor authentication (when offered).** Many web-enabled services now offer two-factor authentication: something you know, and something you have. Your two factors are usually your password (something you know) and a token delivered by mobile phone in a text message or by email, or by a token generator app (something you have). Ring offers the ability to secure your account with two-factor authentication. If a website offers you the ability to do so, I strongly recommend you do this. - (Ring-specific) **Use "shared users" to give others permission to see your Ring account**. Ring offers you the ability to create authorized users who can access your account and view your cameras. They are called "shared users." If you need to give others the ability to see and control your Ring cameras, create shared users; **do not share your account credentials**.
