--- title: "What happens if you miss a payment to your bail bondsmen in California?" date: "2017-08-09" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What does **NOT** happen is you getting thrown back in jail. In fact, this is illegal (and counter-productive, as you'll *definitely* not be able to make payments if you're not working). What will happen will be the same as if you miss a payment to any other business to which you owe money. If you don't make a payment within 30 days of the due date, the delinquency can be reported to the credit reporting agencies and negatively impact your credit score. They can turn the delinquent account over to a collections agency, or they can take you to small claims court and have your wages garnished. What you should do is to call your bail bondsman and explain the situation and work out a payment plan with them. Being honest with a debtor is always better than simply not paying and not communicating.
