--- title: "Is a defendant entitled to equitable tolling of a habeas petition beyond the one year statute of limitations if they are cooperating with law enforcement?" date: "2017-11-13" tags: ["veteran-benefits", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Tolling is not a function of equity, nor is tolling reimbursement for law enforcement cooperation. In general, the statute of limitations is in place to protect the defendant. It is the *plaintiff* who benefits through tolling of the statute of limitations, so that he can avoid having the statute lapse for failure to bring an action. Tolling of the statute of limitations on a particular action occurs when some outside force causes "legal disability," or a lack of legal capacity to take an action. Once that disability is removed, the time starts again. > \[For example, a\] child or a person with a mental illness is regarded > as being incapable of initiating a legal action on her own behalf. > Therefore, the time limit will be tolled until some fixed time after > the disability has been removed. \[O\]nce \[the\] child reaches the > age of majority, the counting of time will be resumed. \[...\] Once > the statute begins to run, it will not be suspended by the subsequent > disability of any of the parties unless specified by statute. > > Mere ignorance of the existence of a cause of action generally does > not toll the statute of limitations, particularly when the facts could > have been learned by inquiry or diligence. In cases where a cause of > action has been fraudulently concealed, the statute of limitations is > tolled until the action is, or could have been, discovered through the > exercise of due diligence. Ordinarily, silence or failure to disclose > the existence of a cause of action does not toll the statute. The > absence of the plaintiff or defendant from the jurisdiction does not > suspend the running of the statute of limitations, unless the statute > so provides. > > The statute of limitations for a debt or obligation may be tolled by > either an unconditional promise to pay the debt or an acknowledgement > of the debt. The time limitation on bringing a lawsuit to enforce > payment of the debt is suspended until the time for payment > established under the promise or acknowledgement has arrived. Upon > that due date, the period of limitations will start again. > [\[1\]](#ahMZO){#cite-ahMZO .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-ahMZO){.citation_index} [[Statute of Limitations - Tolling The Statute](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8d12ef&url=http://law.jrank.org/pages/10500/Statute-Limitations-Tolling-Statute.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="jrank.org"}]{.qlink_container}
