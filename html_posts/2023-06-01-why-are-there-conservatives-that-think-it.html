**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because political affiliation says nothing about whether or not you're a Christ-like person. You, dear questioner, have conflated "conservatism" with "Christianity." While it's true that most Christians are conservative, it does not work the other way round. Most Christians are conservative because Christianity comports nicely with conservatism: most of the values held by conservatives mirror the values held by Christians. However, you don't have to be Christian to be conservative. To wit: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-01b71480a5258cdfa5d4afe19fdb4eef){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-01b71480a5258cdfa5d4afe19fdb4eef" master_w="900" master_h="900"} ::: :::: Ben Shapiro. Conservative, and a Jew. Christianity does not hold a lock on conservatism. Some conservatives are assholes as people (I'm not talking about Mr. Shapiro, who happens to treat people as they deserve to be treated, even though he is not Christian). Some (other) conservatives treat people like shit. But they're still conservative when it comes to things like defense spending, fiscal policy, social programs, etc. Being conservative doesn't mean you're automatically a nice person.
