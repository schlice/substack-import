Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **According to the US Constitution, if there is a massive national crisis, can the presidential election be suspended?** No, because we don't have one Presidential election governed by the Constitution. We have 51 Presidential elections, all controlled by entities outside of the Federal government. The Federal government doesn't control elections, the states do. And there's no provision in the Constitution for it to govern how the states elect presidents. There's no provision for a President's term to be extended because of a crisis. We must hold an election. It would be up to the state to effect an election during a massive national crisis.
