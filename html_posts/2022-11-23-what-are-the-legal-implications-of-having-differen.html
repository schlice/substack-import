--- title: "What are the legal implications of having different rules for two homeowners associations that are owned by the same developer but have different board members?" date: "2022-11-23" tags: ["law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Absolutely zero legal implications. Regardless of how many neighborhoods are owned by a developer, each association is bound by its own Governing Documents, and each has its own Board of Directors. The developer can choose to enact whatever Governing Documents he chooses for each neighborhood he owns, in accordance with state law. Once the homes in the neighborhood are all sold, the developer has no further stake in the Association (unless given so by the Governing Documents), and the Board can change that when the Board transitions from developer control to homeowner control. In other words, when the developer is done developing, the Board shifts to homeowner control and becomes independent of the developer. \ Original question: What are the legal implications of having different rules for two homeowners associations that are owned by the same developer but have different board members?
