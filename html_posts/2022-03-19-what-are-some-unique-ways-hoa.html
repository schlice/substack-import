**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} HOAs in the United States are 501(c)(4) non profit corporations. This means that none of the money they collect is disbursed as *profit* to anyone or any entity. All of the money they bring in goes back into things the HOA must pay for (like insurance, electricity, water, etc), and in to capital improvement projects for common use. If an HOA needs money for a community project, they can either increase dues, or implement a special assessment (if their governing documents allow them to do so). \ Original question: What are some unique ways HOA's can generate revenue?
