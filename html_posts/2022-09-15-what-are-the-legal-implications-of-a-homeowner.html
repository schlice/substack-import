**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Before we find out whether or not the fines are legal, you must be sure that there is no possible justification at all in the Governing Documents which would give the HOA the authority to levy that fine. For example, my HOA includes a provision for their Architectural Review Board indicating that they have the **exclusive right** to deny **any** exterior change to your house. This means that it doesn't actually have to be listed in the document for them to deny it. Since they have the exclusive right to deny **any** exterior change to your home, if you make a change which is specifically not called out in the document, the ARB can still deny it. It is your responsibility to make sure that your Governing Documents do not have a "catch-all" phrase like this. There's a good chance that they do, however, so look carefully. This is the clause which gives the ARB the authority to control how the exterior of your house looks, and so it's likely they have such a phrase. If you have a real estate attorney in your area who specializes in HOA law, and offers free consultations, you might run the Governing Documents by him or her and ask about the HOA's authority, and ask if you have a case.
