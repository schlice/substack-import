**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You can buy your iPhone X [[directly from Apple](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5a2929&url=https://www.apple.com/iphone-x/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container}. When you buy from Apple, your iPhone X is not tied to a carrier. > - What does "SIM-free" mean?\ > "SIM-free" means your iPhone doesn't ship with a carrier SIM card. > The SIM-free iPhone sold on > [[apple.com](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0da39d&url=http://apple.com){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container} and at the Apple Store is > unlocked. That means you're free to use a SIM card from any carrier > that provides service for iPhone. > - When I buy an iPhone from > [[apple.com](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0da39d&url=http://apple.com){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container}, is it tied to a carrier > or does it come "unlocked"?\ > Nearly all iPhone models sold on > [[apple.com](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0da39d&url=http://apple.com){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container} are "unlocked" --- which > means your iPhone isn't tied to a single carrier. Your iPhone will > only be tied to your carrier if you choose carrier financing from > AT&T or Sprint. Now, this said, you still have to pick your network. If you choose a Verizon or Sprint iPhone, it will only work on those networks. If you choose an AT&T or T-Mobile iPhone, you can use it on either network. This is not because the phone is "locked" to that network, but because of hardware differences between the iPhone radios. Your phone will be locked to a network, as it says above, if you choose carrier financing from AT&T or Sprint.
