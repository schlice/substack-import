**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My ex and I have joint custody of our 16 yr old. She lives with him. He lets her drive alone with only a permit that she\'s had for a month. Can I be held liable if she has an accident?** The answer to that is "it depends." Liability insurance typically follows the driver, and comprehensive/collision insurance follows the car. In a joint custody arrangement, the parent who has more visitation, especially during school months, typically carries the child on their policy. To that end, financial responsibility for a driver with a GDL (graduated driver's license) will be with the parent who has that driver on their policy. If that policyholder did not follow the rules when it comes to their teen driver, that insurance company could increase that parent's rates, or could potentially drop them from coverage. This is true whether or not the child got into an accident; if the child is stopped by the police and they are found to not be in compliance with the GDL law, insurance can still act. If the child gets into an accident and their GDL doesn't allow them to be driving without an adult, the child will be fined and will likely lose her license for a period of time. Additionally, if someone is hurt in the accident, the child could be labeled as a "youthful offender" under the juvenile justice system and could face probation, mandatory court-ordered counseling, community service, or even juvenile detention. The child's guardian at the time of the incident could also face punishment, especially if that guardian was supposed to be in the car at the time.
