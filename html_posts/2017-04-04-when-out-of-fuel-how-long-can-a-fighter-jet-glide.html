Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." It depends on the fighter, the weight of the fighter at the time, the ambient air temperature and air pressure, the current speed of the aircraft at the time it runs out of fuel, and the current altitude above ground level (AGL). Every aircraft has what they call an "optimum glide slope" which is the optimum attitude for the aircraft to be placed in when in an "engine out" situation. Every pilot trains on the optimum glide slope when you are out in training and your instructor pulls your throttle all the way to idle and says, "You just lost your engine." Instincts and muscle memory take over, and you trim the aircraft for its optimum glide and start looking for a place to set it down. On the Cessna 172R, we roll the trim wheel three turns back to trim for engine out. But it is literally different for every aircraft. There is a [[glide ratio calculator](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=72f78a&url=http://www.csgnetwork.com/glideratiocalc.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="csgnetwork.com"}]{.qlink_container} on the Internet you can use to calculate the optimum glide.
