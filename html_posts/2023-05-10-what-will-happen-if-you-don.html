**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} 1. If you live in a community with an HOA, you will have to pay your dues. If you don't, they will charge you late fees on top of the dues you owe, and eventually they will send your account to the association attorney, who will place a lien on your house for the unpaid dues and late fees, and then will add their lawyer fees on top of that. When you have accumulated enough debt, they will foreclose on their lien, selling your house out from under you to pay for your debt. Short answer: don't refuse to pay your HOA dues. 2. The other way to stop paying HOA dues is to sell your house and move to an area where there is no HOA.
