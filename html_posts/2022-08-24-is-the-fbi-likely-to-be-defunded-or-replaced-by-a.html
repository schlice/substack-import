**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Is it *likely*? No. *Should* it happen? Absolutely. > When you have an agency that has gone rogue and is interfering at the > highest level of the country to affect an election; and the directors > of those agencies are willing to alter or leak documents that they > shouldn't or lie under oath to federal investigators or lie to a > committee by claiming amnesia; and they oversee a bureau that will > wipe clean phone records that are under subpoena; or they will not > prosecute one person, but they will another; then it's > institutionalized. And you've got to get rid of it.\ > --- Victor Davis Hanson So should it be defuned or replaced? No. It should be **broken up** and its agents spread to other Federal agencies within the government. We have a lot of investigatory agencies within the federal government. So take the people in the FBI who investigate currency and give them to the Treasury (Secret Service). Take the people who deal with terrorists, foreign agents, etc. and give them to Homeland Security. Take the ones who work with the Pentagon and make a special unit at the Pentagon. The bottom line is that this single organization has too much centralized power, and is a threat to democracy. We don't need a federal police force. We have state level resources, and we have federal agencies to perform specific tasks. There isn't anything the FBI does that other agencies can't (or don't) handle. It was a great idea when it was needed. But its time has passed, and it has become corrupt at an institutional level. But as the old song says, *Breakin' up is hard to do.* It certainly won't be an easy task, but it is necessary. And it is a noble task. To preserve the Republic, it **must** be done.
