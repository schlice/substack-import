**Content:** [Caffeine is very similar, pharmacologically, to theophylline, which is a bronchodialator. Studies have shown that it functions similarly (opening up airways and relieving some of the symptoms of asthma). [[Some studies](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8edec5&url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmedhealth/PMH0010864/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} have shown that caffeine is beneficial to those with asthma, relieving symptoms for up to four hours.\ \ Now, for the important stuff:\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - Don\'t drink caffeine for at least four hours before you are scheduled to take a lung function test. The caffeine may alter your bronchial tubes and you will not get an accurate indication of lung function. - DON\'T STOP TAKING YOUR ASTHMA MEDICINE AND SWITCH TO MOUNTAIN DEW OR MONSTER!!! (or anything with caffeine in it) Never stop or change your medications without consulting your doctor! ***Disclaimer:*** ***This answer is not professional medical advice****, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
