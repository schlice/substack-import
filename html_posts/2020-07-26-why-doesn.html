**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why doesn\'t Amazon\'s Alexa services have a text interface? For people with a speech impediment, this is a barrier to service. Does that violate the ADA?** Amazon is required to include **reasonable** accommodations; they are not required to include **every** accommodation. The Alexa app and Alexa-enabled products provide plenty of features for customers with disabilities. This does not violate ADA, because Amazon provides **reasonable** accommodations for people with disabilities, to the extent that their devices are capable of doing so. For those with speech impediments, you can buy an Alexa-enabled device which has a screen to interact with the service, and Amazon has plenty of features built in to assist those with speech issues. Perhaps they are working on a text interface to Alexa. Perhaps that would be too big of an engineering challenge and would require a complete re-tool of their business, which they are not required to do under ADA. We don't know. What we do know is that Amazon does have reasonable accommodations for those with disabilities.
