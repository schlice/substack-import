**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} HSCT (hematopoietic stem cell therapy) is a very dangerous and risky procedure. People seem to think that you can take a shot of stem cells and it will magically cure you. The current therapy is not this at all. HSCT works in this way: - Stem cells from the patient are harvested, genetically modified and reproduced in a lab. - The patient is given high-dose chemotherapy to destroy their immune system as well as their bone marrow, where immune cells are produced. - Once all of their immune system (and bone marrow) is completely gone, the patient's lab-grown stem cells are re-injected into their body. - The stem cells form new bone marrow, which produces new immune cells. During this time, the patient is extremely susceptible to disease, as they essentially have no immune system. After the bone marrow re-grows and the immune system starts to work again, the patient has to get new vaccines to combat all the childhood diseases again, as if they were "reborn." This is part of what makes this procedure so dangerous: there is literally no immune system during that time. The patient must be isolated in a clean environment (ICU, isolation) because of the danger they are in. For now, this therapy is reserved for people who have not responded to any traditional therapies, and whose health warrants such invasive and dangerous intervention.
