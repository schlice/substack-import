**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So I just want to clarify for you what I think I've understood from everyone (as well as my own knowledge). There are no violations of law here. If you're pulled over, and it's your car, and you've authorized another licensed driver to drive it, you're perfectly legal. If you get into an accident, you won't be charged with unlawfully operating a motor vehicle. You have the right to allow any licensed driver to operate your vehicle, and they don't need to be listed on your policy to do it. You may run into trouble with your insurance company if you get into an accident or try to make a claim on your car, because of your non-disclosure. Your best bet: if you are living together and have been doing so for any significant period of time, you should contact your insurance company and advise them of the new living arrangements. If you ever drive his car, he should do the same. It will be best for you if you tell them now (sooner, rather than later), and certainly better to tell them now than after you have a claim. Again, as long as he's a good driver and you've been living together for any significant period of time, it's not likely that the insurance company would have changed your premium (much). So again, you should be fine, and you get credit for doing the right thing **before you had to**.
