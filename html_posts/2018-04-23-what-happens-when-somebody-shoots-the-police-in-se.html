Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What happens when somebody shoots the police in self-defence?** You can only defend yourself against someone who is ***unlawfully*** using force against you. In the United States, when a police officer is acting "under color of law," which means a police officer is functioning as a law enforcement officer and carrying out his duties under the law, he is not acting unlawfully. Using force against a police officer (including deadly force) who is lawfully performing his duties is a crime. It is called **resisting arrest**. Some jurisdictions also have a crime called **failure to follow lawful order or directive.** If you shoot a police officer and you believe your action was permissible because you were acting in self-defense, you would have to prove that the police officer was not acting under color of law, or the police officer's actions put him outside of the law. That is the only way your actions would be considered self-defense. Very rarely in this country is this ever found to be the case.
