**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why does the media continue to focus on gun control when most of the recent shooters have been on psychiatric drugs?** You have two questions here: - Why does the media focus on gun control - Do recent shootings have a correlation to psychiatric drugs? To your first question, the media focuses on gun control because it sells. It makes them money. When they focus on guns, people watch/listen/read. When people do that, the media outlet gets more money. This really isn't that difficult. To your second question, there may be a ***correlation*** between mass shootings and psychiatric drugs. I am reasonably certain this is what your article is pointing out. However, let's not confuse ***correlation*** with ***causation***. Just because there is a correlation between mass shootings and psychiatric drugs does not mean that there is a *causal link*. In other words, there is no evidence that psychiatric drugs cause mass shootings. For example, there is a positive correlation between US crude oil imports from Norway, and drivers killed in collisions with railway trains. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-7ff9ae998cda40b8392aa94c9b91951c){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-7ff9ae998cda40b8392aa94c9b91951c" master_w="2080" master_h="820"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*tylervigen.com*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=50be2c&url=http://www.tylervigen.com/spurious-correlations){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="tylervigen.com"}]{.qlink_container}*)* Should we conclude that in order to reduce the number of deaths caused by collision with railway trains that we should reduce US crude oil imports from Norway? Of course not. That is because *correlation does not imply causation.* Similarly, while there may be a correlation between mass shooters and psychiatric drugs, this does not mean that those mass shootings were caused by the drugs. If anything, it points out the fact that these people were under the care and guidance of physicians who could prescribe them psychiatric drugs. That's all it indicates. However, were the people in counseling? Were they making progress? Psychiatrists (physicians who specialize in mental diseases and conditions) don't often perform counseling; that is usually left to counselors. Counselors and psychologists (PhDs in mental diseases and conditions) cannot prescribe psychiatric medications. And ne'er the twain shall meet. Yet another problem with our mental health system in America. The people who prescribe the drugs don't do the counseling, and vice versa. Unless there is tight coordination between the two (and in most cases, there is not), it is highly unlikely that one knows much about what the other is doing, or why.
