Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Follow all of the basic rules for dealing with police officers in general. These rules will also protect you against the corrupt cops.\ ]{.rendered_qtext} - Start by following the law. If an officer, corrupt or not, has no reason to make contact with you, he won\'t waste his time. - If you do get stopped, comply with all directives of the police at the scene. Note that I said \"directives,\" not \"requests.\" Unless an officer has probable cause or a search warrant, he can\'t search your car. That would be a request. \"*Will you consent to a search of your car?*\" That\'s a request. The correct answer is \"*Not without probable cause or a warrant.*\" \"Will you consent\" means \"will you give up your right to privacy voluntarily\". \"*Move over to the curb and give me your driver\'s license*\" is a directive. If you were the driver, you\'re required to have a license. Give it to him. - Do not argue at the scene. Save your arguments for later. As many of us have said on Quora for a very long time, you have many avenues for redress of your complaints. NONE of those avenues take place at the scene. Arguing with the officer at the scene, even if you are right, will probably just cause more headache for you in the long run.\ - Do not answer questions without a lawyer. - If you are not arrested and you are free to leave, write down everything that happened, to the best of your ability.
