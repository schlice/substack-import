Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The military is not a democracy because often, superiors have to give subordinates distasteful orders. They may even have to give orders that they have a reasonable suspicion might result in their men being injured. Or dying. But those orders must still be given, and those orders must be obeyed, without question. \"The discipline which makes the soldiers of a free country reliable in battle\" is only achieved when soldiers, sailors, and airmen follow orders. We have a structure of rank, and a system by which lower-ranking personnel are ***subordinate*** to higher-ranking personnel. This means that when the higher-ranking personnel provide orders, the lower ranking person is required by military law to follow them, unless they are unlawful orders. A democracy in the military simply wouldn\'t work. That said, I heed the famously misattributed words of Patton: \"No bastard ever won a war by dying for his country. He won it by making the other poor bastard die for his country.\"
