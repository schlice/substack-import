**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It sounds as if the DA and the cops are performing a little bit of "scared straight" tactics. As far as I know, there is no such thing anywhere, let alone Oklahoma, as a "non-registered sex offender" list. That is simply a fiction created by the DA. You're either a registered sex offender, or you are not. There is no legal "in-between." Perhaps what the DA meant was that **he** would consider the person a sex offender *even though he wasn't on the list.* And the other respondents explained how you get on the real list. Since none of that happened here, this person is not a registered sex offender, period. You also stated that "according to a police department in Oklahoma, he is a sex offender." I would get that in writing and then take it to a criminal defense attorney specializing in sex crimes. We would write an official letter to the DA demanding that they correct that police department or face a libel lawsuit.
