**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Building on [[Tom Martin](https://www.quora.com/profile/Tom-Martin-385)]{.qlink_container}\'s answer, it really depends on what is important to you and your family. If you are looking for the best schools, I will agree that [[Academy District 20](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b892ff&url=http://www.asd20.org){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="asd20.org"}]{.qlink_container} is your best bet. This will influence where you decide to live, although you can choice into the district. It\'s just much easier to get your child enrolled in a D20 school if you actually live in D20. D20 also encompass the North end of the city, which is much newer. You\'ll be looking in the Briargate and Pine Creek neighborhoods, and further north into Northgate and Gleneagle, and east to Cordera. You\'ll find the newest neighborhoods there. But clearly, you\'ll pay more for these houses, which are frequently 10 years old or newer. If you\'re looking to save money on a house, you might look a little further south towards Woodmen, or west towards Rockrimmon. Still in D20, but older, more established neighborhoods.
