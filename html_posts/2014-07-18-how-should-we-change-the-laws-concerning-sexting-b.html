**Content:** [Why does the law need to be changed? If teens are sending nude pictures of themselves through text messaging, they are already guilty of distributing (the sender) and/or possessing (the receiver) child pornography, and I think the laws surrounding that offense are fairly robust as they are. Do we really need new laws? Will a new law change teen behavior? Should we ban teens from using cell phones until they turn 18 should they be convicted? Like convicted felons or suspected spouse abusers can\'t own guns?\ \ My point is that I don\'t think changing the law will change teen behavior. But I think you are questioning why we think that it is okay that we take pictures of a 17 year old\'s penis to compare them to the pictures he sent via text so that he can be convicted of distributing child pornography.\ \ I think that if teens want to send nude pictures of themselves to each other, they\'re going to do it, one way or another. Instead of text, they\'ll use Snapchat (which I greatly discourage anyone from using) or FaceTime, or some other technology. The problem is not the law or the technology, it is the teens, and the parents who do (or don\'t) supervise them.\ \ My kids don\'t use Snapchat because it is banned in my house. If I catch them using it, they lose their phone for an indeterminate amount of time. I regularly monitor my kids\' phones; I have all of their passwords and their lock codes for their phones; they surrender their phones to me on demand and I can search them at any time for violations of MY texting policies. I own the phones, I pay for the phones, I pay the monthly bills for the phones, and by being the account owner, I am responsible for what the phone users do using my account, so in essence, I too could be charged with trafficking in child pornography because it\'s my account. I can\'t claim ignorance because I am the \"responsible party.\"\ \ Some may argue that this is Draconian; that somehow I\'m not allowing my kids to have freedom because I\'m so controlling of their phones. HELLO???? I\'M THE PARENT! If I don\'t control my teen, who will? I certainly won\'t take a laissez-faire attitude when it comes to what they are doing; they\'d run amok! I don\'t expect anyone to control my teen but me.\ \ So using that line of thinking, maybe we should change the law by forcing parental involvement in teens\' cell phone usage by extending the child pornography charges to them, since they are the responsible parties on the account. Maybe then you\'ll get parents a little more involved in their kids\' behaviors, which is indeed what needs to happen here.]{.rendered_qtext}
