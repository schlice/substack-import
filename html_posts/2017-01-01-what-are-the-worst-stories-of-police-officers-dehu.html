**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I was seated in the squad room of a detective's squad in a precinct and department which shall remain nameless to protect the reputation of the department. My dad was a squad detective in this precinct, which is why I was there. I spent a lot of time there as a kid. They had a drunk in the holding room. He was sleeping off a terrible bender, handcuffed to the bench in the room. The room didn't have one of those massive picture windows with the two-way mirrors. It instead had a small teller-style window, also two-way, but you could slide it sideways to open it. It was almost drive-thru window size. Anyway, the detectives decided to mess with the drunk. One detective got a coffee mug from the break room and urinated into it in the hallway next to the holding room. He then quietly opened the window, reached his arm in up to his shoulder while holding the mug, and proceeded to throw the entire mug of urine onto the drunk. The drunk awoke violently and flailed at the end of his tether, until he finally got his senses about him and probably thought that he had pissed himself. After a minute or two, he was asleep again. The squad had a great laugh over this. I was in high school and was still na√Øve to many of the ways of the world, so I was quietly in shock. Is messing with drunks dehumanizing? To the drunk, probably not. He likely didn't remember a thing. To the detectives? I think that in order to throw a mug of urine onto a person, you have to believe you are somehow better than them, even if not at a conscious level.
