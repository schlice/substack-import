**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What would you rather have: kids feeling safe at school or allow anyone to buy a gun?** You have committed the logical fallacy of the [[false dilemma](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7bb497&url=https://www.logicallyfallacious.com/tools/lp/Bo/LogicalFallacies/94/False-Dilemma){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="logicallyfallacious.com"}]{.qlink_container}. Why is this a logical fallacy? You have presented the two choices as if they were the only two choices available: **either** kids feel safe at school **or** anyone can buy a gun, and you can only pick one. However, this is not true at all. An entire spectrum of choices exists here; there are far more than just two choices. Anyone *except for violent felons or the mentally ill* can purchase guns, and kids can feel safe at school *while still being vigilant and watching out for suspect behavior*. There are far more choices here than you let on. And that's why this is a terrible comparison.
