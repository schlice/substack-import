**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do people say hurtful and very offensive things whilst inciting hate and try to justify it as \"freedom of speech\"?** Because "hate speech" *is* free speech. **The First Amendment is in place specifically to protect the speech that you don't like.** You don't need to protect speech that you agree with, right? You'd never need a First Amendment in the first place if everyone said nice things all the time. No speech would ever need to be protected. The First Amendment specifically allows people to say things with which we disagree. And yes, sometimes those things are offensive and hurtful and filled with hate. But they are, indeed, allowed to say them. That's what the First Amendment is all about. However, and we can't put too fine a point on this, the First Amendment does not protect you from the *consequences* of your words. For example, if your employer hears you or finds out you used those words, you could be looking for a new job. If you are a business owner, people could choose to do business elsewhere, or people in business may choose not to do business with you. People do not have to agree with you. People do not have to listen to you, either. You can say what you like, but you will still have to face the consequences of your words.
