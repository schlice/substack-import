**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What will the (legal) impact on Trump be, should it be found that his Stormy Daniels payments are in contravention of campaign financing laws and that the payments were made under his direction? Other than impeachment, what is the penalty?** *If* they find that the payments violated campaign finance law, the campaign gets fined. However, campaign finance law is tricky. *Even if* he did make the payment to her, the courts would have to determine whether or not there was any non-political reason for him to make that payment. In other words, would he have made the payment if he was not a candidate? Trump's history of paying off the women with whom he's had affairs should speak volumes about the fact that he most definitely would have made the payment even if he was not a candidate. Trump can even go so far as to claim that he had *both* reasons in mind: personal and political. Trump does not have to deny that politics played some part in the decision to pay. Clearly, if he had not paid, the press would have been all over it, and it would have compounded the personal and business issues that the revelation would have brought. So having both interests in mind when he made the payment is enough to make the payment not a campaign finance violation. As far as I know, campaign finance laws are not criminal charges; no one gets arrested for violating them. Obama's campaign was found to have violated campaign finance laws and they were fined. In and of itself, violating a campaign finance law doesn't rise to the level of "high crimes and misdemeanors," but we know that this is all up to Congress. If the Democrats win the House back in November, violating campaign finance laws *will* rise to that level, and they will impeach him for it. The Senate will not vote to remove him from office, and life will go on. The only thing that this will do will be to weaken the power of the impeachment, making it little more than a glorified censure. It's odd: Democrats know that impeachment would be a Pyrrhic victory, yet they doggedly pursue it anyway. Oh, and I hate to mention the elephant in the room: *none of this has anything whatsoever to do with Russian collusion, which is what Mueller was supposedly investigating in the first place.* We can only hope that impeachment and non-removal will be the end of this circus, but I suspect that even that won't end the controversy.
