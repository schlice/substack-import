**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Can a person still be charged with manslaughter if they accidently > killed a person but did not do anything illegal or dangerous that > caused that person\'s death?* **Yes.** Manslaughter (in the US) is *the unlawful taking of a life*. The definition of the crime says nothing of forethought (planning to, or thinking about, harming someone), and it says nothing of malice (intention to take a life, otherwise called *mens rea,* or the guilty mind*).* It is simply the *actus reus* (or the guilty act) of unlawfully taking a life. Whether or not the act that caused the death was illegal or dangerous is also not considered. Taking a life unlawfully is manslaughter. Manslaughter is the physical element of the crime without the mental knowledge that what one was doing was a crime. Manslaughter is a **strict liability** crime, one in which only the actus reus is required. The only thing required is that the act was voluntary, since involuntary acts are not criminal unless they stem from recklessness or criminal negligence. Hopefully, the state of mind and the fact that what the killer did was not illegal or dangerous will be factored in when sentencing takes place.
