**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why is Quora happy for people to ask how to commit criminal acts, and censure those who object?** Because [[Quora's Conduct Policy](https://www.quora.com/about/conduct)]{.qlink_container} doesn't prohibit it. Specifically, the Conduct Policy prohibits the following in relation to illegal acts: > **1. Illegal activity** > > Do not use Quora to engage in unlawful activity or to promote illegal > acts. When someone answers a question about how to commit a criminal act, the answerer is not "promoting an illegal act." They are merely answering a question on how to do a thing, as many people all over the Internet are wont to do. That thing may be illegal in one or more locations, but the choice about whether or not to break the law remains with the reader. This is the very nature of free speech. Quora ensures that people do not engage in unlawful activity, and they don't support the promotion of unlawful activity, but when people are *genuinely curious* about how criminals do things, they can feel safe asking, and people can feel safe answering. For example, one of my more popular answers is in regards to[ [a way to defeat traffic cameras and red light cameras](https://www.quora.com/Is-there-a-way-to-trick-traffic-cameras-so-they-wont-have-the-license-number/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container}. I began my answer explaining that in nearly all states, adding any accessory or treatment to your license plates that enables you to evade a traffic camera is illegal, but then I discussed those treatments and accessories. We had a lively discussion that veered off into the Mythbusters testing of some of the myths surrounding radar speed measuring devices. It was a generally well-received answer. But nowhere in my answer did I *promote* doing any of the things we discussed. As we've already mentioned, whether or not you do a thing is entirely up to you. Additionally, the laws may be different where you live. We reach a global audience as well, so this is important. Now, after all of this, if you see an answer that you believe violates the Conduct Policy, please report it. Quora's Moderation Team will gladly review the content, and if it is found to violate a policy, it will be removed.
