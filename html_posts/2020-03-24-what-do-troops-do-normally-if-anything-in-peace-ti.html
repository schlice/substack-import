**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What do troops do normally, if anything, in peace time if they can now be sent out to support the police and emergency services? Why are they not usefully deployed across the country at all times?** The Posse Comitatus Act makes it illegal for the military to be used to execute the law unless specifically authorized by the Constitution or by an act of Congress. And since we can't even get Congress to agree to an aid package to assist with COVID-19, I find it hard to believe we'd get Congress to agree to deploy the military to do anything. But it can be done. Generally speaking, the military can assist with things not specifically related to executing the law. They can provide second-level assistance, while leaving the front lines to the local police. A state governor can appeal to the Department of Defense and request assistance.
