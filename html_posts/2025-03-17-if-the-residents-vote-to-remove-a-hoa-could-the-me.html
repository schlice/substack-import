--- title: '"If the residents vote to remove a HOA could the members of the association bring them back to the community?"' date: '2025-03-17' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Once the residents vote to dissolve a mandatory HOA, the members can go through the process to re-establish a new HOA following the rules of that state to establish an HOA in an existing community which does not have one. It's two separate processes: one to dissolve a mandatory HOA, and another to establish a new HOA. **But no one in their right mind would ever do this, because you're literally doubling the legal cost.** It seems like you're asking whether or not one group of homeowners can dissolve an HOA and another group of residents bring it back. And within the boundaries of that particular question, the answer is no. The reason is that because each one of those discreet actions (dissolving an HOA or forming a new HOA) either requires a **supermajority** or **unanimous** support. And if you have a supermajority of homeowners voting to dissolve an HOA, you're not going to find another supermajority of the same homeowners voting to once again create an HOA. The people doing the voting for either dissolving or forming an HOA are *the affected homeowners*. No one else votes to dissolve an existing HOA, or votes to establish an HOA where one did not exist.
