--- title: '"What are some other things that are considered true besides the concept of God?"' date: '2024-05-31' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It sounds like you might be asking, "What are some other things that we are rational to believe that science can't prove?" There are plenty of things that we are all rational to believe exist even though science can't prove them. For example: - Math and logic. Science can't prove them, because science *presupposes them.* - Aesthetic judgements. Science can't prove that a sunset is beautiful. - Ethical judgements. Science can't prove that the Nazis were evil. The beautiful, like the good, cannot be proven with a scientific test. - Metaphysical truths such as the fact that there are minds which exist outside of my own. And surprisingly, - Science itself. The belief that the scientific method discovers truth can't be proven by the scientific method. You cannot prove the tools of science (the laws of logic, the Law of Causality, the Principle of Uniformity, or the reliability of observation) by running some kind of experiment. You have to assume those things are true in order to do the experiment! So while it's true that science can't prove the "concept of God," there are plenty of other things that we are all rational to believe even though science can't prove them.
