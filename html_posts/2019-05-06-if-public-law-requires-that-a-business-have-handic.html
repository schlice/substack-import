--- title: '"If public law requires that a business have handicapped spaces near entrances on business parking lots, does that mean the business is giving law enforcement agencies permission to give tickets or tow vehicles without handicap plates or tags?"' date: '2019-05-06' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If public law requires that a business have handicapped spaces near entrances on business parking lots, does that mean the business is giving law enforcement agencies permission to give tickets or tow vehicles without handicap plates or tags?** No. The business cannot grant permission, the business cannot similarly revoke permission. Only the city has permission to enforce its laws as it sees fit. The city requires the business to designate handicapped spaces, and the city retains the authority to enforce that statute.
