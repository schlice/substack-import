**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Would you give up your 2nd amendment rights to bring back one police officer killed in the line of duty?** Nice, a clever twist on the "[[false dilemma](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5c946b&url=https://yourlogicalfallacyis.com/black-or-white){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="yourlogicalfallacyis.com"}]{.qlink_container}" logical fallacy intermixed with the "[[false equivalence](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=22885e&url=https://yourlogicalfallacyis.com/false-cause){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="yourlogicalfallacyis.com"}]{.qlink_container}" logical fallacy. The False Dilemma: If you don't give up your rights, you're a monster because you don't want to bring dead cops back to life. If you do vote to bring dead cops back to life, the Second Amendment is meaningless because you don't choose to fight for it. The False Equivalence: You presume by your question that giving up your 2nd Amendment rights will somehow mean that cops won't get killed by criminals, when there is no such equivalence. Your question is loaded with logical fallacies, which makes it unanswerable.
