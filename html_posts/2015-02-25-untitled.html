But the vast majority of the population are good, honest, hard-working citizens who deserve protection.\ \ Your notion of police-citizen relations is skewed. I can say that because I grew up in the Tri-State area. And my dad was a cop there.\ \ I saw the good cops and I saw the bad cops. I saw what the cops there did when no one was looking, so I know what good cops and bad cops look like. I could tell stories, but what would be the point of that? I also know that many police ***administrations*** in that area of the country were bureaucratic nightmares in which corruption was rampant, but at the line level, it was very infrequent. It happened, and more so in the Tri-State area than in other parts of the country, but not more so than other departments around the country, on average. Remember that those areas of the country have a much higher number of people living there than in other areas around the country, and things \*appear\* to happen more frequently there because there is a larger population there, as well as a larger police force.\ \ But cops are people too, and cops have good days and bad days.\ \ If you\'re spending your entire life living in fear of the police, you\'re not living right, and you\'re wasting your fear on the police.\ \ Cops don\'t want to bother you!
