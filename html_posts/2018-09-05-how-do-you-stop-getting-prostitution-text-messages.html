**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do you stop getting prostitution text messages on a Sprint phone? My daughter keeps getting messages, each from a different number, from women who tell her they know \"what a man needs.\" Is there a report option on Android phones for texts?** Here are a few steps to can text message spam: > - Delete text messages that ask you to confirm or provide personal > information: Legitimate companies don't ask for information like > your account numbers or passwords by email or text. > - Don't reply, and don't click on links provided in the message: Links > can take you to spoof sites that look real but whose purpose is to > steal your information. > - Treat your personal information like cash: Your Social Security > number, credit card numbers, and bank and utility account numbers > can be used to steal your money or open new accounts in your name. > Don't give them out in response to a text. > - Place your cell phone number on the [[National Do Not Call > Registry](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=de6d4a&url=https://www.donotcall.gov/){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="donotcall.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. > - If you are an AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, Sprint or Bell subscriber, > you can report spam texts to your carrier by copying the original > message and forwarding it to the number 7726 (SPAM), free of charge. > - Review your cell phone bill for unauthorized charges, and report > them to your carrier. If you receive unwanted commercial text messages, you can also [[file a complaint](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=893aaf&url=https://www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/0341-file-complaint-ftc){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ftc.gov"}]{.qlink_container} with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC). The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) also accepts complaints about [[unwanted text messages](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=018b4c&url=http://www.fcc.gov/guides/spam-unwanted-text-messages-and-email){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="fcc.gov"}]{.qlink_container}.[\[1\]](#XAngv){#cite-XAngv .citation_link} Here are a few steps to *prevent* text message spam: - Don't text someone unless you know exactly who you are texting. If you send a text in order to get some free thing (a program, a picture, a newsletter, etc), or because they promised things like cheap mortgages, credit cards, or debt relief services, they have your number and can start sending you unsolicited messages, *which may be legal because* ***you*** *started the transaction!* This is likely how her number made it on to someone's list. - Treat your cell phone number like it is protected personal information. You don't give your Social Security number out to anyone; don't give your cell phone number out either. If you end up getting on a spammer's list, you may be on it for life. It may then be resold a hundred times. Unfortunately, the only remedy in that case would be to get a new number from your carrier.
