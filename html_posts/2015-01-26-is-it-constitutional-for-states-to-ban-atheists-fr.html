**Content:** [It does, actually.\ \ The Constitution also says that there will be no \"tests\" for holding public office. A \"religion\" test is therefore also a violation of the Constitution, as well as a violation of the First Amendment.\ \ I would suppose that an atheist would have to apply to run for office, and then be denied, in order to bring a Constitutional objection to the law.]{.rendered_qtext}
