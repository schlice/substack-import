**Content:** [This is the \"golden egg,\" the magic endpoint that everyone who works in the field of artificial intelligence is after: a \"brain\" that is infinitely adaptable and infinitely learning. There are so many sub-problems that go along with them, that it\'s possible, but it\'s very far away. Before a computer is considered to be \"artificially intelligent,\" it has to be able to do a number of things, and do them as well as, or better than, humans. A short list of these things (from [[Artificial intelligence](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a33ead&url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} on Wikipedia):\ \ Deduction, reasoning, problem solving\ Knowledge representation\ Planning\ Learning\ Natural language processing\ Motion and manipulation\ Perception\ Social intelligence\ Creativity\ \ These are all very complex and multi-faceted areas of study that will require a machine to accomplish all of them together in order to be considered \"generally intelligent.\"]{.rendered_qtext}
