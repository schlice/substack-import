**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} We don't know, and Samsung's not talking. They're investigating. We should either hear by today or early next week what the exact issue is. In response to the issue, Samsung has announced that it is recalling all Note 7's. They have already pulled them off of store shelves, and they plan on starting a full recall of the devices when replacement plans have been made. An anonymous Samsung employee has confirmed they think the problem is with the battery, but they also claim that the issue affects "less than 0.1 percent" of the Note 7s sold to date. They also state that the batteries for the Note 7 are sourced from China and Korea, and it is the Korean batteries that are experiencing the issue. The issue only occurs when the phone is being charged. Even though only the Korean batteries appear to be affected, Samsung is proceeding with a full recall. To date, 35 cases of the issue have been reported. Based on the number sold, this issue affects approximately one in every 42,000 devices. This is a big deal for Samsung, as over a million phones will need to be replaced, and the recall is happening during their initial rollout phase. The phone has already shipped to ten countries, and this is not the best time to have a recall. The Note 7 is projected to be the most popular Samsung phone to date, so this is a huge problem for Samsung. They want to get this fixed and shipping again.
