**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answers here have covered the issue of the perception of transphobia, since that is the real thrust of the question. But most of our answerers here glossed over the last part where the questioner says that Jesus tells us to not judge. I want to take a moment to address this, as it is a *very* common misinterpretation of the Bible. Many people use this verse in an attempt to silence their critics, interpreting Jesus' meaning as "You don't have the right to tell me I'm wrong." Taken in isolation, Jesus' command "Do not judge" does indeed seem to preclude all negative assessments. However, there is much more to the passage than those three words. Christians are often accused of "judging" when we point out sin, but doing so is not wrong. When you point out what is true and right, it naturally defines what is *not* true and right, and the attacks start to come from those who have chosen what is not right. But the simple fact is that the right thing is always the right thing, regardless of how many people have chosen the wrong thing. Let's dig in to that concept of biblical judgement.
