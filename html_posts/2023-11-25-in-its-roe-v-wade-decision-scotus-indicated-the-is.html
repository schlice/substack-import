**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is the *effect* of what SCOTUS said. What they *actually* said is that *Roe v. Wade* ***itself*** was decided wrongly. There is no right to abortion in the Constitution. That's it. That's all they said. The majority in *Dobbs* held that there is no constitutional right to abortion under either the Due Process Clause or the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. Relatedly, although the Dobbs majority did not directly address the argument, Justice Kavanaugh's concurrence observed that "no Justice of this Court has ever advanced" the so-called "fetal personhood" argument that the Fourteenth Amendment requires states to prohibit abortion (a view advocated by some amici in *Dobbs*). The *effect* of this ruling is that there are only a few *potential* methods by which Congress could have the authority to regulate abortion. Potentially the Commerce Clause or the Spending Clause could be used to either enhance or restrict the ability of states to regulate abortion, but ultimately SCOTUS shut down most of what the Federal government could do to either support or restrict abortion. This means that the ability to either permit or restrict abortion is a state issue, not a Federal one. Biden's handlers are well aware of this fact. They'll continue to have him tell anyone who will listen that he wants Congress to codify *Roe* into law, even though he knows full well that there's no way in Hades that such a thing will ever be attempted, let alone succeed. Those sorts of talking points are just pandering to one's base. The SCOTUS ruling does not mean that making laws about abortion is unconstitutional. It simply means that there is no Constitutional right to get an abortion (like the right to freedom of speech or the right to be free from unreasonable search and seizure). Laws can be passed...if you can get consensus to do so.
