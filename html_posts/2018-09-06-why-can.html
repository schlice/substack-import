**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why can't you donate blood if you have multiple sclerosis?** In the past, you could not donate blood if you had MS. Up until recently, American Red Cross guidelines excluded people with MS from donating blood. However, as of May, 2007, those guidelines were updated, since no scientific evidence exists to show that MS is contagious or can be transmitted by blood. So long as other qualifications are met, and there are no local or FDA restrictions, an otherwise healthy person with MS is permitted to donate blood. For more details about other eligibility requirements, see the [[Red Cross website](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2bc608&url=https://www.redcrossblood.org/donate-blood/how-to-donate/eligibility-requirements.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="redcrossblood.org"}]{.qlink_container}.
