**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It's probably not illegal because of a flawed interpretation of the first amendment. Remember that the Bill of Rights was originally meant to protect you against **government action**. For example, the government cannot throw you in jail for protesting against them. However, over time, and through case law, the first amendment has been interpreted to mean that ***anyone*** is free to say ***anything*** **to** ***anyone*** at ***any time***. Whether or not this is a good thing depends on the particular speech. Clearly, protesting at a funeral is in bad taste. It is a time of mourning, and whether or not you agree with the person who died, letting the family grieve is right and proper. So not a good thing, but under current interpretations of the Constitution, it is legal. Trying to make it illegal would be constitutionally problematic.
