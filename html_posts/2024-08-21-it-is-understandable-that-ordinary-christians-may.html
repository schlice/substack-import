**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, it's actually the opposite. Properly Bible-trained scholars are *more certain* that the Bible contains no contradictions. It is those who are *poorly* Bible-trained who believe that genuine contradictions exist in the Bible. I suppose it really boils down to what you believe is a *contradiction*. Yes, there are *differences* in the Bible. There is no way for there to *not* be, as the Bible was written by forty different authors in three languages, on three continents, over the span of 1500 years. **But a difference is not a contradiction.** It is only a contradiction if there is *no conceivable way* that two "differing" verses can be reconciled. Even if an answer is not available right now, that does not mean an answer does not exist. Many have found a supposed error in the Bible in relation to history or geography only to find out that the Bible is correct once further archaeological evidence is discovered. Some things that people bring up as "contradictions" are indeed hard to reconcile. But it is the Christian belief that every one of those supposed contradictions can be reconciled in a viable and intellectually plausible way. There are entire books written showing "all of the errors in the Bible." And there are *other* books which refute every one of those supposed errors. But the truth is that most people who attack the Bible using those "errors" as their ammunition aren't really interested in finding an answer. They simply parrot the people showing the "errors" because they want to attack Christianity, not because they seek genuine answers. Jesus' warning about not casting pearls before swine is apropos here.
