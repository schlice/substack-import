**Content:** [Oh, I\'ve argued for the Army to take over the A-10 program for a while. It fits much better as a flying tank; it fits the Army\'s (and as you noted, the Marines\') missions much more closely than the Air Force.\ \ The problem isn\'t mission fit, however. The problem is \"everything that comes along with flying an airplane.\" It\'s the logistics and the pilots. The Army and the Marines currently only fly limited aircraft, and for the most part, they are rotary-wing. They would have to ramp up an entirely new logistics chain to support an airplane. They would have to retrofit their facilities to include runways capable of handling fixed-wing aircraft (their current runways are only large enough for rotary-wing aircraft). The new logistics channels alone would be an incredible expenditure, not to mention training new pilots for fixed wing flight, basically duplicating what the Air Force already does.\ \ With unlimited funding, I assume that the Army or the Marines could easily take over the A-10 program. But no one has unlimited funding.]{.rendered_qtext}
