**Content:** [\"Parole Officers\" monitor inmates who have been released on \"parole.\" Generally, this means before the completion of the person\'s prison sentence. This is also generally done because prisons are overcrowded, and hardly anyone stays in prison for their full term. If they are well-behaved, and the Parole Board approves, a prisoner can be released to serve the remainder of his sentence on \"parole.\"\ \ Parole is basically modified prison. It\'s sort of a \"halfway house\" for criminals. Criminals released on parole are under restrictions. They generally cannot leave the state in which they are living. They generally must completely and totally follow the law. They generally can\'t use drugs or alcohol. They generally must find gainful employment, they can\'t have any contact with their victim, and finally, they must report in to their parole officer, generally weekly. If they are found to have violated the terms of their parole release, they go back in to prison to complete their sentence and serve any additional time related to the reason they violated, if any.\ \ Prisoners who serve their entire term in prison walk out the front door and go on with their lives, no further contact needed. It does happen, but these days it is rare.\ \ The only exception that I know of is, of course, my own state, Colorado. Parole is an additional punishment, after the entire sentence is served. \[1\]\ \ There is another concept called \"mandatory supervision\" which some states like Texas have. Mandatory supervision is a system wherein inmates are compensated with \"good time\" which then counts towards their term as time served. So an inmate can have a six year term, can have served three years, and also have three years \"good time,\" after which they must then be released unless the parole board says otherwise in writing (usually because they are deemed a threat to society). There is no parole board to meet before mandatory supervision releases kick in.\ \ Hope this helps.\ \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\ \[1\] C.R.S. 18-1.3-401(1)(a)(V)(B)]{.rendered_qtext}
