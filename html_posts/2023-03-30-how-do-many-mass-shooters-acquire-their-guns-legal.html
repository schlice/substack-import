Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} School shooters? Who are universally young and probably not too far out of school themselves? Virtually all of them. Adam Lanza stole his mother's gun, killed her with it, and then went to the school where she taught and killed kindergarteners with it. So that would be an illegal acquisition. But the others bought their guns mostly legally. They weren't criminals yet, until they were. And how do you stop someone who isn't a criminal? You can't. The best you can do is to make it so costly for them to commit their crimes that they choose to do something else. Make it impossible to get into a school any way but the front door, and then fortify the front door. We do this with airports and courthouses all over the country, but we can't do this to a school building? So back to the question. **HOW** do mass shooters acquire their guns legally? ***Because at the time they acquire their guns, they are not prohibited persons.*** > According to a recent National Institute of Justice (NIJ) > [[report](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=72f4be&url=https://nij.ojp.gov/topics/articles/public-mass-shootings-database-amasses-details-half-century-us-mass-shootings){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="ojp.gov"}]{.qlink_container} on public mass shootings from > 1966 through 2019, 77 percent of the perpetrators bought guns legally. > In some cases, teenagers or young adults obtained guns from their > families. Just 13 percent of mass shooters obtained firearms through > illegal transactions. **In other words, background checks would have > been no obstacle in 87 percent of the > cases.**[\[1\]](#Eiidr){#cite-Eiidr .citation_link} In the vast majority of cases, mass shooters are not prohibited people.
