**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should Facebook be legally required to comply with the first amendment and stop banning people for breaking its community guidelines?** Short answer: no, because they are not bound by the 1st Amendment. The 1st Amendment says generally that government cannot make a law banning speech. It says nothing about how a private business writes its Terms and Conditions as well as its User Agreement as to how people use the system. Facebook is a private business, not a government agency. It is under no obligation to allow you to say anything you like on its platform, and it can indeed ban people for speech it doesn't like. If that weren't the case, Quora wouldn't be allowed to have a [[BNBR policy](https://www.quora.com/What-is-Quoras-Be-Nice-Be-Respectful-policy)]{.qlink_container}, and wouldn't be able to enforce it. You may have a First Amendment right to speak, but that doesn't mean you can walk into Walmart and start using racial epithets and slurs simply because you *can*. You'd better believe that Walmart will ask you to leave, and will call the police if you don't, and they would not be violating your First Amendment rights for doing so. Now, if you go out to the street corner and say the same thing, you'd definitely have a First Amendment right to do so. You might get your ass beat as well, but that would be the natural consequence of such language. Not to say that violence is justified, but it might be the natural follow-on to that kind of speech.
