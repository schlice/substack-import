**Content:** [Here\'s the rub: most manual labor jobs don\'t require four-year degrees. But they do require some level of training and/or experience. And they all pay more than minimum wage. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, there are about three million unfilled jobs which require some level of training, and most don\'t require four-year degrees.\ \ I do know that garbage collectors make around \$15/hr, and they don\'t require any specific skillset, nor do they require a four-year degree.\ \ If you\'re looking for well-paying manual labor jobs, I\'d be looking to vocational tech school. [[Mike Rowe](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=382d4f&url=https://www.facebook.com/TheRealMikeRowe/posts/938382126171931:0){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="facebook.com"}]{.qlink_container} points out that a particular construction manager would be willing to pay \$40/hour to a reliable welder, but he just can\'t find one.\ \ My advice: look for some manual labor job that you\'re willing to do, but requires some technical training, then go get that training. Many technical training institutes offer job assistance at the conclusion of your training, and offer financial aid as well.\ \ As an aside, we in the United States need to do better at offering vocational technical training to students. By removing this from schools, we seem to have \"devalued\" manual labor in the eyes of students. To them, only a degree matters. And what of the millions of skilled labor jobs which go unfilled? Many find them \"unsuitable\" for their tastes. Oh well, better for those who actually get the training and go after those jobs, eh?]{.rendered_qtext}
