**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A2A > *Why should the budget for Medicaid increase more than 18% in ten > years?* Answer: **ObamaCare.** ObamaCare is also called the "Affordable Care Act." Because it is anything but affordable, I hesitate to use that name. Suffice it to say that any reference to ACA or the Affordable Care Act or ObamaCare refer to the same thing. ObamaCare accomplished its goal of providing insurance to everyone at all costs by opening the floodgates and enrolling more people in Medicaid. It changed the coverage rules, allowing more people to enroll in Medicaid in order to get them insured. It then spent over \$1 trillion in tax revenue to subsidize premiums, and added massive regulation and taxes on insurance companies, many of which, counterproductively, raised premiums. Despite incessant claims by the left, these people will not lose coverage if they want to keep it. Therefore, we are going to have to prop up Medicaid for the foreseeable future, thanks to ObamaCare. What we do with *new* people entering the workforce, on the other hand, is a completely different story. Hopefully, it's what gets addressed under the American Health Care Act.
