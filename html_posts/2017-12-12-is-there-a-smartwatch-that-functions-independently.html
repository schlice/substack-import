**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there a smartwatch that functions independently as a cell phone?** Yes, there are many smartwatches which function independently of a mobile phone. When you're shopping for a smartwatch, look for keywords like "4G" or "LTE" or "SIM card" support. These indicate models of smartwatch which support standalone cellular operation, separate from other connectivity methods such as Bluetooth or Wi-Fi. This is an example of a [[Samsung Gear S3](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d8c1de&url=https://www.amazon.com/Samsung-Gear-Frontier-Smartwatch-SM-R765A){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} smartwatch which supports 4G, which means it has a cellular radio. **Could this be used without a monthly cell phone bill payment solely for the purpose of calling 911 in case of emergencies?** The smartwatch can physically be used in lieu of a smartphone. There are a few caveats, however. - **The Carrier.** The trick is getting a cellular carrier to give you a "smartwatch-only" plan. Usually, smartwatch support is an "add-on" to an existing cellular contract, so getting a smartwatch-only plan may take some doing. You will likely have to shop around to find this. - **The Battery**. On a good day, the battery on a smartwatch will last you the whole day before it needs to be recharged. With smart battery management, it will last you a day and a half. But that's all *without cellular support*. Add in a cellular radio and your battery life drops dramatically. You may not even make it through the day with the cellular radio on all the time, and without a connected smartphone, the smartwatch with no radios on is just a watch. - **The Sound Quality.** The smartwatch would be good for a quick call or an emergency call, but using it as your primary phone may not be wonderful, as sound quality on both ends may not be stellar. I also don't think any carriers have "prepaid" smartwatch plans which only have minutes you buy and use when you need them (as in the case of emergencies). I'm reasonably sure most carriers only support "postpaid" plans for smartwatches and other accessories.
