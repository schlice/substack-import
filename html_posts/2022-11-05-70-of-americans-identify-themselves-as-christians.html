**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is because Democrats *still* think abortion is a winning issue. They think they have the upper hand on abortion. They think that simply saying, "I want to codify *Roe* into federal law" will induce a massive blue wave, because they've been told that a majority of people supported *Roe*. Well, as usual, the pollsters got it wrong. A majority of Americans supported not messing around with *Roe*. But that same majority of Americans support ***some*** restrictions on abortion, **because that's actually what** ***Roe*** **said**. It was the *other case law, Planned Parenthood v. Casey,* which allowed abortion for all nine months. Yes, most Americans did support *Roe*, but not *Casey.* More Americans support an abortion ban after 15 weeks than those who do not.[\[1\]](#qSxuW){#cite-qSxuW .citation_link} And support for abortions *beyond* 15 weeks drops *sharply*, to the point that the **vast** majority of Americans do not support abortions in the third trimester.[\[2\]](#WfHYc){#cite-WfHYc .citation_link} The "codifying *Roe* into law" that Democrats support is **full legalization of abortion for all nine months of pregnancy**. Literally, under a Democrat *Roe-*like law, the mother could be in active labor and decide to abort her baby, and it would be legal. Again, the vast majority of Americans do not support this; the numbers are sharply against this. Republicans decided (a bit late, but decided anyway) to *lean in* to this fact, and it has propelled them to victory in several key races already. When faced with their *radical* stance on abortion, the Dems have nowhere to fall back to. The economy under Biden is a shambles, and it's mostly due to his energy policies, and consequently, prices are going up, and jobs are going down. \$2/gallon gas looks pretty good right about now. The border is wide open; anyone can come in and stay. Our politicians are sending billions and billions of our tax dollars overseas to countries to fight wars that have no effect on us. People are concerned about the economy first, and abortion second. Abortion will not overcome a poor economy. But Democrats think it will. I suppose we shall see.
