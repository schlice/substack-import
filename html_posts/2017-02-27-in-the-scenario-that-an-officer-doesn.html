**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Your question is convoluted, but it is like many other questions on Quora looking for that *magic bullet* that you can show that the officer didn't do that will get you off scot-free. Unlike the movies and TV, the police do not read you your Miranda rights statement when you are arrested. You are only read your rights and asked to sign a Miranda statement *when you are being questioned*. The officer at the scene is under no obligation to read you your Miranda rights unless he has to ask you questions besides basic identity questions, which you are generally required to give (truthfully) under law. If your rights were not read to you, it's probably because you were not being interrogated. Work this out with your lawyer. Make sure your lawyer knows everything that happened, when it happened, and what was said. If there was an error, your lawyer will help you sort it out.
