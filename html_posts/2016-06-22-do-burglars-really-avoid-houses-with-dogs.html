**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I've written this on other answers regarding home security, but if you can remember this story, you'll remember all you need to know about home security. Two hunters were hunting in the African bush when they stumbled on the den of a few lions and lionesses. As the hungry cats started stirring and preparing to capture their next meal, one of the hunters stopped, reached into his bag, and produced a pair of running shoes. The second hunter looked incredulously at him and said, "What are you doing??? You can't outrun a lion!" The first hunter said, "I don't have to outrun a lion. I only have to outrun *you*." The same applies to home security. You don't have to make your home a veritable fortress. You just have to make your home *less attractive* to burglars than your *neighbor*'s home. Now, if you live out in the country and your nearest neighbor is miles away, well then, you're on your own. But if you're like most people, in a suburban setting, in a community of other houses, you only need to ensure your home is less attractive to burglars than other houses in your neighborhood. Burglars, above all, don't want to be seen or heard. They want to do their business and get out without being seen. This means that, for the most part, they will be doing their work at one of two times: 1. In broad daylight 2. In the middle of the night *In broad daylight? Are you out of your mind? That's when they'd be the most visible!* Yeah, assuming anyone was **home**, which in most of Suburbia is simply not true during the day. Most people are at work, kids are at school, etc. And with more than one wage earner in a household becoming more and more common, having most people in the home gone during the day leaves your home open for intrusion, and no one around to see what's going on. The middle of the night is far more sketchy, because people are home but sleeping. You have to be really stealthy to rob houses while people are home, and most burglars simply aren't interested in the possibility of waking someone up. In either case, making your home less of a target is the goal. Clearing away places where burglars can hide, planting pointy bushes beneath windows, adding security lighting to the exterior of the home, **and getting a dog** are all part of the ***security in layers*** methodology you should be employing when looking at the security of your home. You can add security systems and cameras and panic rooms as your funding allows, but some of these things that you can do without a tremendous cost would be: 1. Adding a timer to your exterior lights so they come on at dusk and go off at dawn. Along with this, replacing the bulbs with high-efficiency LED or CFL bulbs will negate some of the cost associated with leaving your exterior lights on all night. In the long run, this will probably *save* you money, as these bulbs are far more cost-efficient to run than a traditional incandescent light bulb. 2. Clear away big shrubs from in front of your home's windows. Any place a burglar can hide from view while he does his business is a bad thing for home security. You can also plant pointy shrubs underneath your exterior windows, making it even tougher for burglars to get in.
