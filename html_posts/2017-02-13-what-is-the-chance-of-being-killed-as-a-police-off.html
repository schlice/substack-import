**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In 2008. there were roughly 800,000 sworn peace officers in the United States.[\[1\]](#UimgO){#cite-UimgO .citation_link} That same year, there were 140 officers who died in the line of duty, of all causes. This computes out to 17.5 deaths per 100,000 officers This means that your risk of dying, that year, was 0.0175%. Compare this to other occupations: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-31d92146a2d23762df73ff97688f0a97){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-31d92146a2d23762df73ff97688f0a97" master_w="1484" master_h="1900"} ::: :::: You can see where that 17.5 lies on the chart, under "police and sheriff's patrol officers." Also note that in any given year, almost half of the line of duty deaths were caused by car accidents, either while the officer was driving, or while the officer was at the side of the road outside of his/her vehicle.
