**Content:** [I\'ll add the Military Police perspective.\ \ As Tim and Chris mentioned for police officers, military police must also write pages of reports if a bullet is fired outside of the range.\ \ However, military police are issued weapons, magazines and bullets at the beginning of each shift. Each bullet is stamped with a lot number. These bullets ***cannot*** be fired unless you are shooting at someone, on duty, for an authorized reason. These bullets are delivered to the range at the end of their useful life, to be fired during normal range operations. They are then replaced with new, stamped, bullets, and the lot numbers issued are recorded when a military police officer checks a weapon out of the armory.\ \ Accordingly, at random intervals, military police will be required to **block** their ammo when they turn in their weapons at the end of a shift. You are presented with an appropriatelly-sized 1x6 piece of wood (colloquially known as *the block*) with either 30 holes or 120 holes, depending on what weapon you were carrying (a military police officer carrying an M9 carried two 15-round magazines holding a total of 30 rounds or an officer carrying an M-16 carried four 30-round magazines for a total of 120 rounds). You must remove every round from your magazines and place them nose-down in the block. The block of ammo is then inspected by the armorer, and you will be held accountable for any rounds that are not part of the same lot number that you were issued at the beginning of your shift. Once your lot numbers were confirmed, you had to re-load your mags. I was a SRT (Security Response Team) team leader, and routinely carried an M-16. *Huge pain in the butt.*\ \ Of course, that didn\'t prevent you from buying your own ammo, emptying your mag into your pocket, loading the mag with your own ammo and firing them off, re-loading the government ammo in the mag and going on about your day. Of course, this was illegal according to regulation, and anyone found doing this would face severe penalties under UCMJ.]{.rendered_qtext}
