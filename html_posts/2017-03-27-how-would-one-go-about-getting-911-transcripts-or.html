--- title: "How would one go about getting 911 transcripts or the actual audio call that they made?" date: "2017-03-27" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A2A You can file a Freedom Of Information Act (FOIA) request with the department for 911 transcripts. Contact the main number at the department in question and ask to speak to the FOIA Officer. They will provide you with the procedure to make your FOIA request. You may also get the same information (i.e. how to submit a FOIA request) from the department's website, but if you can't find it, go ahead and call them. Be aware, however, that depending on your reason for making the request, the contents of the conversation may be privileged information (i.e. you are unrelated to the subjects in the call, you are not using it for media purposes, etc), and may not be releasable. Also, if there are still open investigations related to the information on those recordings, they also may not be releasable. I assume by "audio call" you are referring to recordings or transcripts of the radio calls made between the dispatcher and the officers. These days, those communications may be a bit thin for any one particular incident. Most communications and dispatches go over MDT (mobile data terminal), and not over voice radio. You may not be able to get that information, either. If you are the subject and your investigation is still ongoing, contact the investigating officer and make your request. Feel free to go up the chain if you don't get the information you need. If you're involved in a legal matter (i.e. a lawsuit), your lawyer will subpoena the information during the discovery phase of the trial.
