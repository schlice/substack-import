--- title: '"How freedom of press is related to freedom of speech under Article 19?"' date: '2017-03-27' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Article 19 of the [[Universal Declaration of Human Rights](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=aeca24&url=http://www.un.org/en/universal-declaration-human-rights/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="un.org"}]{.qlink_container} states (**emphasis** mine): > Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression; this > right includes freedom to hold opinions without interference and **to > seek, receive and impart information and ideas through any media** and > regardless of frontiers. Clearly, the writers of this Article intended that any media sources are protected, because it allows for the ability to seek (look for), receive (get), and impart (pass on) information through any media. This covers both the people looking for information, and the people providing it (such as media outlets). Remember, though, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights is not legally binding on any nation-state. It is a "common standard" that all member nations pledge to work towards. The individual nation-state is still free to choose to incorporate these rights into their own state actions, or not.
