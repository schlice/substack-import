**Content:** [While I agree that it\'s horribly insensitive and personally offensive to me, I wore the (real) uniform to protect the rights of those who want to wear (fake) uniforms.\ \ It reminds me of Voltaire\'s quote: \"I disapprove of what you say, but I will defend to the death your right to say it\".\ \ You\'ve crossed the line from free speech to fraud when you attempt to gain some form of benefit by wearing the uniform. If you wear a uniform to get 10% off of your latte and you were never in the military, you deserve to be arrested for fraud.\ \ Starting in 2014, Colorado driver\'s licenses now include a \"Veteran\" designation if you choose. You have to bring the standard military discharge form (DD-214) which every service member receives upon being discharged from the armed forces to DMV to have the tag applied to your license. Since I am a disabled veteran and left the service before serving long enough to retire, I no longer have a military ID card. The veteran tag on my license helps, as I am definitely a military veteran, albeit not retired.]{.rendered_qtext}
