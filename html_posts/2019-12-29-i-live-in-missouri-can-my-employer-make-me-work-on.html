--- title: "I live in Missouri. Can my employer make me work on my day off?" date: "2019-12-29" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes your employer can make you work on your day off. However, if you work more than 40 hours a week, you must be paid overtime. That\'s under Federal law, not state law. There are some exceptions to the Federal overtime law, but in general, this applies to everyone. For example, some technical jobs are called *exempt* and are not covered by the hourly requirements. In Missouri, there is no minimum or maximum amount of time you can be scheduled to work. Missouri follows Federal law when it comes to employment and overtime.
