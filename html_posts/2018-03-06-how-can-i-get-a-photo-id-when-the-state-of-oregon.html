**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can I get a photo ID when the state of Oregon says I need to get a new birth certificate from the state that I was born in, but the state I was born in will not give me a copy without a photo ID?** It does seem like a Catch-22, doesn't it? But at least one of these should be able to happen: you should be able to get a new birth certificate without a photo ID, or you should be able to get a photo ID without a birth certificate. You must check with each agency involved to see what the process is if you don't have the one document or the other. In my opinion, I would start with the birth certificate. Most states have procedures for obtaining a birth certificate without a photo ID. It does sometimes happen that vital documents are destroyed simultaneously (think house fire). There must be a way to get a copy without a photo ID. For example, some states allow you to submit a sworn notarized statement in lieu of a photo ID. Some states allow your parents (who are listed on your birth certificate) to submit a notarized letter and a copy of their photo ID in lieu of your own. But if your state absolutely will not give you a copy of your birth certificate without a photo ID, then work on part 2: getting a replacement photo ID without a birth certificate. Every state is different when it comes to this, but just as in the case of vital documents, there must be a procedure which permits you to get a duplicate photo ID if you don't have a birth certificate. Usually you can provide supporting information, like your checking account (your checks show your name and address), a credit card statement, your water or electric bill, or other locally-recognized documents which show who you are. Some states even allow you to request a new photo ID online.
