**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A simple Google search would have helped you here. > Georgia officials have dissolved a committee responsible for > investigating deaths of pregnant women in the state, after one or more > members leaked confidential information about deaths linked to the > state\'s strict abortion laws. > > In a letter sent to members of the Maternal Mortality Review Committee > (MMRC), Georgia health commissioner Kathleen Toomey said an > investigation failed to identify those responsible for the leak, so > all current members would be removed. > > The news - first reported by ProPublica - comes two months after the > outlet [[published stories on the deaths of two > women](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=1ac946&url=https://www.propublica.org/article/georgia-abortion-ban-amber-thurman-death){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="propublica.org"}]{.qlink_container} the panel ruled were > preventable and linked to the state\'s strict abortion > ban.[\[1\]](#hamER){#cite-hamER .citation_link} So pretty straightforward: the committee violated the state's rules about confidentiality, and when the investigation couldn't find the leaker, all of the members of the committee were dismissed. But the quote that really destroys the narrative you were probably going for in asking this question: > \[A\]ll new member seats would be filled through a new application > process, \[with new\] on-boarding processes for \"better ensuring > confidentiality\". So **the committee still exists.** All the seats on the committee will be re-filled, but they're going to do a better job ensuring that the people who fill those seats can maintain their oath of confidentiality better than the previous committee members.
