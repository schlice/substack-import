Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Fogleman has my vote for the best. Dugan was a bit before my time, but he comes in close second. I had some of the worst experiences during the McPeak era, and not just because he oversaw the drawdown that cost me a pilot\'s slot. The uniform changes were ridiculous, downward-directed, and almost universally hated. The whole Total Quality Management (TQM) movement made the Air Force feel more like a civilian company rather than a military. We felt like we were the red-headed stepchildren of the Armed Forces under McPeak. Even Wikipedia has detailed the angst over the McPeak years: > McPeak\'s legacy as Chief of Staff has been considered in several > quarters as one of the most controversial in U.S. Air Force history > and has been the subject of much debate. Many Air Force senior > officers and senior enlisted personnel, both active and retired from > the Regular Air Force, the Air Force Reserve and the Air National > Guard, have accused him of trying to run the Air Force as a > corporation, with his introduction of Total Quality Management (TQM) > under the moniker of \"Quality Air Force\" (QAF), as well as the > uniform changes he unilaterally implemented which strayed from a > traditional land-based military style. Some of his uniform changes > were soon undone after his retirement by his successor as USAF Chief > of Staff.
