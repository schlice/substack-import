--- title: "As a Christian, do you hate watching movies because it makes you covetous (or itching you had the life of the person in the film)?" date: "2023-08-01" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not exactly. As a Christian, I hate watching movies because the values of the people who are portrayed in the movie are *so different from me.* I see how they live, and I know that they live by the desires of their flesh. They love their lives. They love the life they live, and the Bible talks about this. You often hear the kitchy phrase, "Love the sinner, hate the sin." That's not directly out of the Bible, but it is referenced in a roundabout way. We hate sin by recognizing it for what it is, refusing to take part in it, and condemning it as contrary to God's nature. Sin is to be hated, not excused or taken lightly. We love sinners by showing them respect (1 Peter 2:17), praying for them (1 Timothy 2:1), and witnessing to them of Christ. It is a true act of love to treat someone with respect and kindness even though you do not approve of his or her lifestyle or sinful choices, and this is something that Christians have great difficulty with. It is not loving to allow a person to remain stuck in sin. It is not hateful to tell a person he or she is in sin. In fact, the exact opposites are true. Sin leads to death (James 1:15), and we love the sinner by speaking the truth in love (Ephesians 4:15). We hate the sin by refusing to condone, ignore, or excuse it. So if I'm itching to live that life that I see on the screen, then I wish to live in sin. If I desire to live in sin, I am not saved. I'd have some serious work to do before I'd be comfortable calling myself Christian. So if you call yourself Christian and you find yourself envious of the people you see around you, you also have some work to do, as we all do, as we are indeed all sinners. We need to recognize the fact that we are living in sin and only faith in Christ can save us.
