**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What conclusion do you think the US Justice Department investigating itself will come to?** The same conclusions that it comes to every time; however, I think you mischaracterize the DOJ Office of the Inspector General when you say that the Department is "investigating itself." The [[USDOJ Office of the Inspector General](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f02640&url=https://oig.justice.gov/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="justice.gov"}]{.qlink_container} (DOJ OIG) was formed by statute and is an organization which is "part" of the DOJ, but does not report to it. The Inspector General (IG) does not report to the Attorney General; the IG reports directly to the President. The IG is appointed by the President and the nomination is confirmed directly by the US Senate. Therefore, the IG is outside of the framework it is investigating. To that end, any investigation done into fraud, waste, abuse and misconduct in the DOJ has the best chance of being neutral and fair.
