**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Thanks for the question [[Isak](https://www.quora.com/profile/Isak-Pau)]{.qlink_container}. From the Academy Admissions website: > In order to be considered for admission to the United States Air Force > Academy you must be nominated by your country. Nominations from your > government are processed through the United States Defense Attaché > Office/American Embassy serving your country. Nominations must be > received by the Air Force Academy by 14 December for the class > entering the following June. > > To apply for a nomination, write your country's government. Your > letter should contain complete details about your background and your > potential for success at the Academy. It should be received at least > one year prior to the date of admission. The Defense Attaché Office at > the American Embassy can direct you to the correct ministry in your > country. DO NOT apply directly to the Air Force Academy or other > United States government officials. I would just write the letter. Add bullets if you like. For complete information regarding international students, check out the [[International Students](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f1f440&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/admissions/advice-to-applicants/international-students/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} page on the [[Academy Admissions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60e33c&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} website. The Academy is allowed to host 60 international students (i.e. non-US citizens), and generally admit 15 international students per year.
