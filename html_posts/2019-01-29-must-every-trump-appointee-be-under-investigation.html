**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Must every Trump appointee be under investigation? Brett Kavanaugh \'likely\' to be investigated for perjury.** Brett Kavanaugh cannot be impeached for perjury, because he wasn't on the Supreme Court during the time he supposedly perjured himself. The Constitution says that Supreme Court justices "shall hold their offices during good behavior" and this is taken to mean that behavior ***before*** they held their offices does not count towards their impeachment. Only behavior *while they hold their offices* is impeachable. So unless someone is going to argue that Kavanaugh perjured himself *while on the Court*, then Constitutionally he cannot be impeached for perjuring himself before he was on the court. He wasn't actually *on the court* until he was approved by the Senate and sworn in by the President; therefore, his nomination hearings do not apply to perjury charges.
