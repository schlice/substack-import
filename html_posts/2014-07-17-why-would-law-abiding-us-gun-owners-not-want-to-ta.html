**Content:** [Thank you for your question, and I see that you\'re at least trying to take positive steps in the right direction. However, you\'re a little confused about some of the points you made.\ \ 1. **Registration is the first step towards confiscation**. This is not hysteria talking, we are only learning from history. It happened in England, it happened in Australia, and it will happen here too if we allow guns to be registered by the government.\ \ What exactly would be the purpose of registering a gun? There is no other purchase in America that you have to register. You have to register cars, but you don\'t register the \*purchase\* of a car with the government. Registering your car tells the government that you paid the road use tax to use the roads (that\'s why \"license plates\" is a misnomer; in the UK, they are called \"tax tags\"). What good does gun registration further? All it does is allow the government to know who has guns and how many. What purpose, exactly, does that serve? Weapons would be registered by the government so the government can control them. If only to identify to the public, in another 20 years following the passing of such legislation, that the weapons used to commit crimes are either stolen from law-abiding citizens, or were never registered in the first place (i.e. sold on the black market). But we already know that. So why register such a purchase? When would weapons be confiscated? When crimes are committed with them, in which case they are confiscated anyway. You suggest going door to door to search for unregistered weapons? Law-abiding citizens certainly won\'t have unregistered weapons, only criminals will, and their guns get confiscated anyway. The only reason, based on history, to register guns, is so that there is a national registry of which houses to go to to confiscate the same purchased items when the government decides to repeal the Second Amendment and take guns away from people.\ \ 2. **You can\'t require a \"license to own.\"** That clearly violates the Second Amendment, and licensure to keep an bear arms would be Congress passing a law that restricts people from keeping and bearing arms. Again, until the Second Amendment is repealed, this will not happen. Yes, essentially Illinois has this, but the people in that state clearly have no desire to stand up to the government and tell them that it\'s a Second Amendment violation, so they get what they deserve. That\'s why I only lived in Illinois when I was assigned there, not by choice.\ \ 3. **Taking a health test to validate that you are sane isn\'t a workable solution**, and also could be seen as a violation of the Second Amendment. While I do agree that we have to do something to prevent guns from falling into the hands of the mentally ill, forcing everyone to take tests to determine their mental capacity is not a workable solution, and again focuses on the legal owners instead of focusing on the people we want to keep away from the guns. Requiring that tests be passed and other hoops be jumped through before purchasing a gun is also a \"restriction\" on lawful firearms ownership, which is a 2A violation. We have to do a better job at determining whether or not people are mentally ill, and then flag them so that they cannot purchase firearms from the white market. We don\'t \"test\" people for mental capacity when making any other purchase, and cars would be a far better choice for testing people before making purchases, since a person with diminished mental capacity can kill lots of people in a really short amount of time with a car, even more so than with a gun. I can kill twice as many people as James Holmes did in a matter of seconds by driving my car down the middle of an open-air mall. Yet I don\'t have to take a mental health test before getting behind the wheel of a car. The logic doesn\'t pass muster.\ \ 4. **The focus is wrongly on the weapon, when it should be on the person.** We\'ve answered this question many times on Quora, but we\'ve proven that it is not the gun that kills the person. We tend to focus on guns because they\'re an easy target; they are the only device purpose-built to kill. But killing is justified in certain circumstances, so how I kill in those circumstances should be with the tool that provides me the maximum benefit with the minimum possibility of collateral damage (to myself or others) and when I have to kill someone, a gun is the best way to do it. It\'s the best tool for the job, but people are concerned because its job is to kill. We should focus on the persons who should not be allowed to possess a gun. We also have answered this question many times, but improving the mental health system in this country would be a great place to start.]{.rendered_qtext}
