**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it possible that Barr\'s earlier written statements regarding the Mueller probe be construed as obstruction since the now published report contradicts many of his judgements?** Obstruction of what? Obstruction of justice is a specific process crime, and usually involves three elements: 1. There was a pending federal judicial proceeding; 2. The defendant knew of the proceeding; and 3. The defendant had corrupt intent to interfere with or attempted to interfere with the proceeding. Barr found that there was not enough evidence to even start a federal judicial proceeding, so obstruction simply does not apply. If the Mueller report contradicts the DOJ's findings, then that just means you have a bad AG and a poor DOJ. It doesn't mean they committed a crime by failing to prosecute.
