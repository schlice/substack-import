--- title: '"Can it be proven that a police officer neglected to turn their body camera on before shooting someone in a court of law? Are and should there be any laws forbidding this?"' date: '2018-01-03' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can it be proven that a police officer neglected to turn their body camera on before shooting someone in a court of law?** No, in general it cannot be proven that a police officer neglected to turn their body-worn camera (BWC) on before a shooting, at least not beyond a reasonable doubt. And most cameras are very simplistic in their feedback to the user, so while there may be a hard switch to turn it on and off, there is really very little to indicate that it is actually recording something. You'll really only know this for sure when you plug it in at the end of your shift and attempt to download the video. Therefore, it is very easy to produce reasonable doubt, and very difficult to prove that an officer willfully neglected to turn the camera on, or even accidentally neglected to do so. **Are and should there be any laws forbidding this?** In general, there should not be law surrounding this. Generally, there are department policies that require camera use in certain situations in departments that currently field BWCs to their officers, and this is rightly where the policy should be maintained. These policies are greatly dependent on the amount of storage space available to the department to maintain the video footage, as this is the largest cost to the department. Camera maintenance and upkeep will be a factor, but not nearly as much of a factor as video storage. When law is involved, it mandates monetary expenditures, and most often, the law itself doesn't come with money attached to it. It's what we call an "unfunded mandate," and if funding is not provided, it can chew up a significant portion of a department's budget just to keep up with the new laws, which will mean taking funding from other areas of the department.
