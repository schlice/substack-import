**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You tried to malign too many groups all at once. You're bashing on Republicans, pro-lifers, and whites (with your "brown baby" comment). Honestly, if you wanted to craft a question hating on one group or the other, you should have picked one. For now, I'm going to ignore the "brown baby" comment and stick with Republicans and pro-life people. These two groups are not the same. One position is political, and the other is philosophical. Most Republicans believe that abortion should be permitted up until about 15 to 20 weeks, which would actually permit more than 90% of all abortions. Pro-life people value all human life equally, and that value doesn't change based on the circumstances of that child's conception. So for Republican pro-life politicians, the view they hold should mirror the majority of people in the district they represent. So if the majority say that sensible limits on abortion (i.e. the aforementioned 15--20 weeks) are acceptable, then that's what they should support legislatively. But what they do *in their own homes*, with their own family, may be different. And that's by design. Legislatively, they should support whatever their constituents support; they may legislate in opposition to their values because they are *representatives*. As such, they *represent* the people who elected them. Good legislators set aside their personal feelings and beliefs, and write laws that their people want. So a Republican who is also pro-life will probably live his pro-life values in his *personal* life, not so much in his *legislative* life. What that means is that he will ensure that his 12-year-old daughter gets the best medical care available, and when the baby is born, he will likely help her put it up for adoption. You then asked another question: > **What kind of a parent would let her die in childbirth?** None. No parent would let their child die in childbirth. Neither will any hospital in the United States. **Every** state in the United States permits doctors to save the lives of mothers, regardless of any abortion laws. The cases you hear in the news are actually examples of **medical malpractice**. They are unrelated to abortion laws. No doctor would ever fail to save the life of a mother because he can't perform an abortion. Such a doctor would be sued for malpractice and would likely lose their license. You think that because the girl is 12 that childbirth will kill her? Hardly. What kills pregnant women is a lack of competent prenatal care, not the fact that she is pregnant. If her body allows her to carry a child, her body will allow her to birth that child as well. So long as she has good medical care (which a Congresscritter can likely afford), she should be just fine.
