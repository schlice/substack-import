--- title: "I am a Googler who have received an offer from Uber. Should I ask for RSUs to be vested upfront?" date: "2016-09-11" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You can ask, but you\'re better off asking for a signing bonus rather than RSUs that really aren\'t RSUs. The whole point of RSUs is to give you stock without giving you stock. It\'s an incentive to stay, because if you leave, any unvested RSUs go away. It\'s an investment in your future, but it also is banking on the company succeeding. It ensures you will do your part to make the company profitable. If you want money up front, you\'re looking for a signing bonus, or a hiring bonus, not immediately-vesting RSUs.
