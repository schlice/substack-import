--- title: '"What is a good way to signal to a police officer that you are looking for a better place to pull over?"' date: '2017-01-10' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: that\'s not for you to decide. Longer answer: The officer who lights you up expects you to safely move your vehicle to the shoulder when he does. The law doesn\'t allow you to determine whether or not the area is "safe\" to stop in. If you stop and the officer believes the area is unsafe, he will tell you to move using the PA system. If you abrogate a police officer\'s responsibility onto yourself, you risk additional charges. The only time I would ever suggest not doing is if it is an unmarked car attempting to stop you and you are alone. In that case, immediately call 911 and find out if the car behind you is real. If it is, you must immediately stop. In that case, and only in that case, slow down, move to the right-hand lane, and activate your hazard flashers. You should be doing this while you are on the phone with the 911 dispatcher. It's still illegal, but the officer should understand. I know some departments (ours included) generally will not use unmarked vehicles for traffic enforcement, for that very reason. Some agencies, like our state patrol, however, specifically use unmarked vehicles to catch willful traffic violators. That said, the above likely doesn't apply on the interstate. It's well-lit, and you're never alone. It would be difficult to claim you're all alone on a well-lit interstate with hundreds of cars passing you by. If you get lit up on the freeway, you'd better be stopping.
