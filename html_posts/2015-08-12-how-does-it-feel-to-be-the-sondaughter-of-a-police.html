Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Like [[Rick Bruno](https://www.quora.com/profile/Rick-Bruno-1)]{.qlink_container} mentioned in his answer, I saw my dad as invincible. He was a hero to me. Also, he was never involved much in my life. I was raised basically by my mom. She was the boss in our house. And when dad stepped in, it was usually with a heavy hand. Don\'t get me wrong, I wasn\'t abused as a child, but corporal punishment dealt by dad was of the harsher variety.\ \ I learned to be comfortable around police officers. I got to see a side of them that few get to see: a *human* side. I learned that they are real people, and they also tend to socialize best with each other.\ \ His selflessness fostered in me what my wife calls a \"servant\'s heart\" which has guided my chosen career paths to this day.\ \ Also, being the child of a cop gives you some great war stories, but I will save those for another question.]{.rendered_qtext}
