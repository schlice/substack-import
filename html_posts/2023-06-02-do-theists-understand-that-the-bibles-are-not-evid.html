{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I would venture to say that many theists haven't really thought about it all that much. But if they had, they would agree with you: the Bible doesn't prove the existence of God. But that's because, primarily, it was never designed to do that. You can prove God's existence without ever referencing the Bible or any religion at all. The purpose of the Bible is to tell a story. It's a story in three main acts: 1. Paradise 2. Paradise Lost 3. Paradise Regained The bulk of the Bible centers around explaining #2, whereas #1 takes up only two chapters of the book of Genesis, and #3 takes up the last two chapters of Revelation. TL;DR: The Bible was never designed to prove God's existence. You do that *first*, and *then* you read the Bible.
