**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: no. Longer answer: While you don't have to speak English to drive a car, or get a license, you still have to follow the law regarding DUI. The native language of the person driving the car doesn't change his or her blood alcohol content, and there is no provision in the driving laws of any state that I know of which would require that the driver fully understand exactly what the officers were doing in order for them to do their jobs. Officers can suspect a driver of DUI (they did), stop that driver (they did), and perform field sobriety tests for DUI (and clearly, he understood enough to perform the field sobriety test or to blow into a BAC tester), which are required of every driver, regardless of native language. Therefore, whether or not the driver had a clear understanding of the officers is not material. Unless you're claiming that he wasn't actually drunk, he just failed his field sobriety test because he didn't understand what the officers were asking him. This is highly unlikely, but perhaps that is the case you can make. I suspect, however, that the officers stopped the car which was being operated in a manner which suggested the driver may be drunk, there was a strong odor of alcohol from the driver, and the driver blew a XXX on the meter, which is X times over the legal limit. If the police have this sort of evidence, language is irrelevant.
