Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Normal white blood cell count is between 0 and 5. Abnormally high WBC count in CSF usually means inflammation or infection or bleeding. Some potential causes: - Abscess - Encephalitis - Hemorrhage - Meningitis - Multiple Sclerosis - Other infections - Tumor - Neurosarcoidosis[\[1\]](#zPAtX){#cite-zPAtX .citation_link} This is why your doctor is potentially investigating whether or not your symptoms are caused by MS. \ Original question What do white blood cells mean in a lumbar puncture?
