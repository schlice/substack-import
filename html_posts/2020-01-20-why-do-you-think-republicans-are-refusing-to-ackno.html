**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do you think Republicans are refusing to acknowledge that Trump is hiding impeachment information by not letting key witnesses testify?** Because if this were really the case, and Republicans were certain that "impeachment information" was being deliberately restricted by the President's invocation of executive privilege, they would have pushed their Democrat counterparts in the House to take their subpoenas to the courts to determine whose privilege wins: the House's impeachment investigation power, or the White House's executive privilege. The House failed to do this. This indicates that the House Democrats did not think that the testimony contained any critical "impeachment information." Clearly, they had enough information from the "witnesses" they did have to proceed with Articles of Impeachment, so clearly, there was nothing that the "key witnesses" could have said which would have strengthened their position that impeachable offenses occurred. And to forestall the next question, the only authority under which the President can restrict a government employee from testifying in front of another branch of government is executive privilege. So if Trump in any way convinced someone to not testify in front of Congress, the authority to do so is contained in the executive privilege doctrine.
