Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **You are mistakenly arrested as a suspect in a serious crime. Are you wiser to say nothing (except name and address with ID as required) until you get a lawyer or cooperate fully with the police and answer all their questions?** You are never "mistakenly arrested" until after a police officer says, "We didn't mean to arrest you, you're free to go" or a judge says "there's not enough evidence to support this arrest, you're free to go." You are under arrest, and the police are working hard to ensure that you go to jail for the crime, otherwise they wouldn't have wasted their time arresting you. You are arrested, and you are a suspect. Period. **IF YOU HAVE BEEN ARRESTED, SAY NOTHING UNTIL YOU TALK TO A LAWYER.** The police are not there to get you coffee and BS with you and be your friend. Not that they are nefarious, just that they are not there to hang out and chit-chat. The police are there to find the person who did the crime, and you're under arrest because they have reasonable suspicion that you're the person who did it. You must be clear on this fact. And the police are expert at making cases. Sometimes they make mistakes, but by and large, they are really good at what they do, and they will find a suspect and enough evidence to convict that suspect. To that end, you need an expert to help you, otherwise you may find yourself on the wrong side of the bars. Once you get that shiny new pair of silver bracelets and a one way trip to the Gray Bar Inn, your mouth closes. You say nothing until you have a lawyer. Period.
