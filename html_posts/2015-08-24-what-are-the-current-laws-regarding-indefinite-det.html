--- title: "What are the current laws regarding indefinite detentions?" date: "2015-08-24" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There is no relevant law surrounding indefinite detentions. Case law is being created as legal challenges to the detentions in Guantanamo are being heard by the courts.\ \ The law is tough because the accused are not US citizens, and they are not being held in the United States. They don\'t fall under the (complete) Geneva Convention because they are not fighting for a nation that is a signatory to the Convention, nor are they fighters for a nation, in uniform, under a state flag. There are some provisions that apply (such as the treatment of prisoners of war), but most provisions of the Geneva Convention do not apply to terrorists.]{.rendered_qtext}
