--- title: '"Is an HOA legally empowered to trespass a person from the subdivision if said person is a homeowner there?"' date: '2022-12-12' tags: - law-enforcement - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." If there is a scenario in the Governing Documents in which a homeowner can be forcibly removed from the subdivision, then the HOA could call the police and "trespass" that homeowner. Otherwise, no, the homeowner is a homeowner, and has rights. Now, can the HOA trespass a person from ***the clubhouse***? That's a little more likely. Many Governing Documents disallow homeowners using shared facilities when they are delinquent on their HOA dues payments. But I fail to see how an HOA can trespass a person from the subdivision when they actually own a residence there.
