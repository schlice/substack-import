**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Would it be legal/constitutional to pass a law forcing people to use all bio-fuel in the US?** Yes, it would be both legal and constitutional outlawing traditional gasoline and mandating bio fuel instead. But it wouldn't go over too well. All cars not able to burn 100% biofuel would be rendered immediately useless. The vast majority of auto makers have not approved their vehicles to run on 100% ethanol, the primary biofuel for use in automobiles, let alone the 85% ethanol we currently have in the market (you've probably seen it in the yellow pumps as E85). In fact, most vehicles today are using some blend of ethanol in their gas (E15 is relatively common, but virtually no auto maker has approved E100 for their vehicles). It would take years of redesigns to allow cars to burn 100% ethanol. Second, we don't have enough ethanol available to create enough biofuel to completely replace gasoline and diesel. In parts of the world, deforestation is taking place so manufacturers have enough arable land to grow the crops needed to turn into biofuel. And we would have to substantially increase the amount of crops we grow specifically for biofuel to meet the new demand, causing increased deforestation as we clear area to grow those crops, making the problem worse instead of better. Also, some biofuels made out of waste cooking oil can freeze in cold weather, meaning not all biofuels are viable gas alternatives in all areas.
