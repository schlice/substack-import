**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Neither. We already have many gun laws, and better parenting can't really be legislated. The problem, which we all know in our hearts but dare not speak it, is the decline of the nuclear family. The lack of ethics being taught and modeled at home. The dissolving of the family and the rise (and celebration) of single parenting. The near-necessity of two parents working. The abortion culture, with its near-militant, but yet somehow oddly casual, disregard for human life. The lack of focus on (and the veritable shunning of ) mental health. After all of this, are we surprised *at all* that mass shootings happen? But thanks to the rise of liberalism and progressivism, that ship has sailed. It will take *decades* to restore the family as the core unit to train children on morals and values, and to model the same effectively for them, if we even try. No, it's far more likely that the family will continue to erode, along with morals and values. Therefore, the only way we can avoid more school shootings is to harden schools even further. - Employ full-time armed security - Improve physical security. One-way doors, alarm systems, AI-powered scanners, monitored camera systems, etc. - Dissolution of "Gun-Free Zones" and permitting fully-licensed concealed carry teachers to carry during school hours Or even better, don't send your kids to traditional schools. Online learning has gotten [[vastly](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6ad2b5&url=https://www.k12.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="k12.com"}]{.qlink_container} [[better](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=01aa8f&url=https://www.connectionsacademy.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="connectionsacademy.com"}]{.qlink_container}, and achievement rivals that of traditional school settings. But again, that whole both-parents-at-work thing kinda precludes homeschooling because your kids need a babysitter. See how the government has both caused the problem and then provided the solution?
