--- title: "How do the reforms with ACA benefit mentally ill patients who are over 65?" date: "2015-02-20" tags: ["veteran-benefits", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [While I am not a fan of the Affordable Care Act (because it is anything but affordable, and doesn\'t provide needed care), there are some provisions that benefit the mentally ill. You can find these and more answers on the [[http://www.healthcare.gov](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a7ee2d&url=http://www.healthcare.gov){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"}]{.qlink_container} website.\ \ The ACA mandates that most individual and small employer healthcare plans, including all the plans offered in the Marketplace, have coverage for mental health and substance abuse services. Because of this law change, most plans must now cover preventative screenings as well, such as depression screenings for adults, and behavioral assessments for children. Finally, as of 2014, the ACA removed any \"pre-existing condition\" denials from insurance, so you can buy insurance and be covered for any pre-existing conditions you may have, including any mental illness.\ \ I\'m also reasonably certain that there are no age limitations on this coverage, but you should check with your individual insurance carrier for more information.]{.rendered_qtext}
