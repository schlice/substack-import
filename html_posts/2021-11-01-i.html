**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As you can see, there is a great deal of ambiguity over your mere possession of such pictures. This is partially because whether or not a nude picture rises to the level of child pornography (CP) is *subjective*. It depends on whether or not a picture is *sexually suggestive enough* to be considered pornography. If it is, then it is *possible* that in your state, you may be eligible to be charged with "possession of CP." You could *then* commit any number of both Federal and state crimes for sending those pictures to someone else, as you could then be guilty of "distribution of CP" which becomes a Federal crime when it crosses state lines, as well as state crimes for possession in either state. Age of consent does not factor in to whether or not a photo constitutes CP, or whether possession of it counts as a crime. **There is no age at which possession or distribution of CP becomes lawful.** \ Original question: I'm 16 and I privately own nude images of myself on my phone, is this illegal in all 50 states and would it be considered CP by law all over the U.S for a teen my age? And would sending it to someone be illegal even if the age of consent is 16?
