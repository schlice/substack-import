**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I disagree with some of the other answerers on the ease at which you can "stumble" onto a true child pornography site. Those sites are not part of the surface web; i.e. the 4% of the Internet indexed by Google: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6e0714e86d18c9408d3b1e8f3a8b6f28){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6e0714e86d18c9408d3b1e8f3a8b6f28" master_w="600" master_h="540"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*Elixir Of Knowledge*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fbfee2&url=http://www.elixirofknowledge.com/search/label/Dark%2520Web){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="elixirofknowledge.com"}]{.qlink_container}*)* True child porn sites are hosted in the dark web. The web servers are part of the Tor network, and are only accessible through specific pathways. One example would be ".onion" links which will only open in a Tor Browser; you simply cannot open them in vanilla Firefox or Chrome or (ugh) Internet Explorer. They are not indexed by Google, nor are they linked to surface web sites. The main way by which you can accidentally stumble on to deep web kiddie porn sites (not dark web sites) is when you're already surfing porn. Some kiddie porn site operators will leave their teaser information in the public porn ad networks. They will leave a teaser photo or video to entice you to go to their sites. So if you're already surfing porn, you get into the porn ad networks and you start clicking around, you might "stumble" onto a kiddie porn site. If this happens, follow all of the instructions the other answerers gave (collect WHOIS data, disconnect your computer from the Internet, run a full virus scan, run a full malware scan, etc). Beyond that, you really have to be searching for true kiddie porn sites to find them.
