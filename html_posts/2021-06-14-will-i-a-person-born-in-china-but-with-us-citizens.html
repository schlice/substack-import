**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Let's take your questions one at a time: > **Will I, a person born in China (but with U.S. citizenship) have > trouble getting into USAFA, AFROTC, or OTS?** As long as you are a US citizen, you are eligible to be appointed to USAFA. You can also attend AFROTC, or OTS after your college graduation. > **Will I have a problem getting a senator\'s recommendation letter?** For USAFA admission, you need a *nomination*. Nominations come from either a US Congressional representative, a US Senator, the Vice President, or the President. You will need to work with the office of your local representatives to the US Congress, but your country of birth should not prevent you from securing a nomination. > **Further, will I have trouble getting into certain career fields or > ranks?** You will have no trouble getting into any career field or achieving rank. Ranks are granted based on demonstrated performance which occurs once you start working. As others have noted, security clearance will take a little longer to process for you, as your country of birth is, understandably, a concern for the military. They are going to want to ensure that your ties to your country of birth do not constitute a risk to national security. Your investigation may take a little longer, but it will not *prevent* you from participating in any career fields. For USAFA, make sure you check out the [[Academy Admissions](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60e33c&url=https://www.academyadmissions.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="academyadmissions.com"}]{.qlink_container} website to find out what you need to do and when. Good luck!
