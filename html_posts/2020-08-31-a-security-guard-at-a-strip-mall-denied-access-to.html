**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **A security guard at a strip mall denied access to stairs for my child with prosthetic legs. He said the owners told him they didn't want kids with prosthetics using their stairs. Is this okay in California?** In the United States, businesses must provide reasonable accommodations to those with disabilities. So long as there is an alternate method to get upstairs, a business can certainly restrict stairs to those with prosthetics, especially considering the liability involved should that person fall. If there is no elevator or other method for the disabled child to get upstairs, the business could get in trouble for the denial.
