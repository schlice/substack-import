**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is Donald Trump slowly turning into a dictator vowing to veto any effort to block the national emergency he has called at the border?** Is that true, or has Congress become the dictator, vowing to veto anything that he does? I'm not sure. But which political party has #RESIST as its motto? Veto power is the President's alone. It's in the Constitution. He is certainly free to use it. There are always political ramifications for everything you do, but this is a no-brainer to him. And in most cases, you can't "block a national emergency" through an act of Congress. The National Emergencies Act says that every six months, Congress can review the list of active national emergencies, and they can end one by passing a **joint resolution**. Congress isn't passing a joint resolution ending this national emergency any time soon. Even if they do, Trump can still veto it. Congress would still need a 2/3 majority in both houses to override it and end the national emergency. That requires massive negative political capital against the President, and it doesn't exist in either house.
