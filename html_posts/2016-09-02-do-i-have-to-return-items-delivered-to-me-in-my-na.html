--- title: '"Do I have to return items delivered to me in my name if the company says they were ordered fraudulently?"' date: '2016-09-02' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with any legal answer, "it depends." If the company wants their money back from you, they will have to file a lawsuit against you, and it will be up to them to prove that you knowingly were involved with fraud in order to recoup their losses. **If you had no involvement with any fraud, and had no knowledge of fraudulent activity**, then US law regarding receiving unsolicited merchandise through the mail applies. Whether or not the items made it to your doorstep through fraud is irrelevant to you; you were not involved with the fraud. You simply were the surprise recipient of unsolicited merchandise. If that is truly the case, you are protected. You are not "profiting from fraud" because in order be charged under this statute, you must be involved with the fraud in some way, or have knowledge of the fraudulent activity that led to the unsolicited merchandise arriving at your doorstep. **If you were involved in some way with the fraud**, or if you had knowledge **at all** of how this merchandise was making its way to you, **you are liable.** You are responsible for every dime you earned from the sale of the items, and you are responsible for covering the losses the company suffered. You can also likely be charged with mail-order fraud, and the same organizations who would protect you if you were not involved (the USPIS, the state AG, etc) will come after you instead, and you'll face some hefty fines and possible jail time for perpetrating, or participating in, or profiting from, the fraud.
