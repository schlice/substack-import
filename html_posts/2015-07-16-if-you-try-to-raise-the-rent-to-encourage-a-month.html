--- title: '"If you try to raise the rent to encourage a month to month tenant to move, do you have to raise for all tenants or just can you just pick one?"' date: '2015-07-16' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [As with everything, it depends.\ \ If you live in a \"just cause eviction\" city, a landlord must have \"just cause\" to terminate your lease. Month to month leases automatically renew at the end of the month. Raiaing rent to above market value is illegal since it is an attempt to cause the tenant to move (otherwise known as \"constructive eviction\"). Raising everyone\'s rent may be enough of an excuse to avoid constructive eviction charges if the landlord can show that the rent increase to above market value is justified. Beyond this, a landlord would have to find another reason to evict the tenant.\ \ If you do not live in a \"just cause eviction\" city, and you have a month-to-month lease, a landlord would never do this. He would simply tell you the agreement is up and give you 30 days to vacate. If you have lived in the unit for over one year, he must give you 60 days.]{.rendered_qtext}
