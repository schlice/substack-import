Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [On the positive side, the people who are promoted are the ones who have **earned** it.\ \ On the negative side, implementing such a system would be problematic at best, and next to impossible at worst. Every specialty in the military requires a different type of person. Not all persons who perform all specialties can be so easily \"tested\" to progress on in their ranks.\ \ That\'s why, in the US military, we use a combination of performance reviews, time in grade, and successful completion of required training (whatever that may be) to determine whether or not someone can advance to the next higher grade. Switching to a completely merit-based advancement system would not be a benefit to the military.]{.rendered_qtext}
