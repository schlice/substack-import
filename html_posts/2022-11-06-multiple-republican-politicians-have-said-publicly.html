Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, there are plenty of legal reasons to impeach Biden. For more, see my previous answer: [[Bill Stein\'s answer to If Republicans win the House, and perhaps the Senate, should they impeach Joe Biden? On what charges would be of greatest effect?](/If-Republicans-win-the-House-and-perhaps-the-Senate-should-they-impeach-Joe-Biden-On-what-charges-would-be-of-greatest-effect/answer/Bill-Stein?__filter__=5&__nsrc__=notif_page&__sncid__=33043962029&__snid3__=44419617520)]{.qlink_container} Secondly, I guess it's time for another edition of Constitution 101. **You don't need a legal reason to impeach a president, because impeachment is not a legal process.** Impeachment is a *political* process. The Framers used the words "high crimes and misdemeanors" because that's the "level" of crime for which impeachment was to be reserved. It should be things *akin* to what we would charge *anyone else* with as a crime. But for a president, we would instead impeach. Remember what then-Representative Gerald Ford said about impeachment in 1970: \"An impeachable offense is whatever a majority of the House of Representatives considers it to be at a given moment in history.\" In other words, the House can literally impeach the President for any reason they see fit, or no reason at all. But as I said in my previous answer, Clinton, W, Obama and Trump all committed impeachable offenses, and only Clinton and Trump were impeached, and Trump not even for the thing he did that was truly impeachable. Biden too has committed impeachable offenses. And if we don't start holding presidents to some standard, then the office of the President will gain *de facto* power and we will have no mechanism to stop it. If you do what you've always done, you'll get what you've always gotten.
