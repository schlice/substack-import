--- title: "Are Christians more hateful and judgmental than atheists?" date: "2023-07-03" tags: ["theology"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, I don't think so. I think atheists can be just as hateful and judgmental as theists. Sure, there are some atheists who live more morally than many Christians, and there are some Christians who live more morally than many atheists. And there are plenty of Christians who wrongly believe that their judgement is somehow acceptable because they are saved, and the atheist is not. Clearly, this is the Christian's problem, and they are absolutely wrong for thinking this way. But this "moral high ground" fallacy works both ways. Some atheists believe they are more moral because they don't wrongly judge, but believing this places them in the position of being the arbiter of morals, since they have no foundation for their purported morality. But I don't think there's anything special about Christians which makes them especially more judgmental than atheists.
