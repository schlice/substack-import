**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How will self-driving cars overcome the dilemma of a child stepping in front of the car? Because most people would swerve into danger if there was no time to brake but a self-driving car will choose the safety of the driver as number 1 priority.** That's not necessarily true. It depends on how the car is programmed. The car may be programmed to protect its occupants above all else. Or the car may be programmed to protect pedestrians above all else. To solve this question, let's ask what the most complex computer that drives cars currently would do: **you**. *What would you do?* You would evaluate the situation, and you would swerve to avoid the pedestrian, placing yourself in harm's way to avoid hitting the pedestrian. If that's how we would react, there is no reason to believe we would program a self-driving car to do otherwise. It is clear, therefore, that self-driving cars will not eliminate accidents, as this scenario clearly illustrates. But they should reduce accidents as they reduce driver inattentiveness and distractedness.
