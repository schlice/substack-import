**Content:** [[[Better technology to track planes exists so why don\'t more airlines use it?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=79bf41&url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/better-technology-to-track-planes-exists-so-why-dont-more-airlines-use-it/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="cbsnews.com"}]{.qlink_container}\ \ ADFRs, or Automatically Deployable Flight Recorders, eject from the aircraft just before a crash. They float on water and send their location to satellites for retrieval. This gives searchers a better idea of where the aircraft actually went down, even in water.\ \ The ADFRs have been in use by the US Navy since the 60\'s (since their aircraft generally fly over water more often than others). Most models of F/A-18 Hornets have ADFRs installed.\ \ Governmental grants could fully fund ADFRs in commercial aircraft in the US, but Congress has failed to act in getting that funding to the aviation industry to implement the new system. The United States has been trying to get them installed since 1999, when the NTSB began recommending upgrades to the CVR/FDR units in commercial aircraft.]{.rendered_qtext}
