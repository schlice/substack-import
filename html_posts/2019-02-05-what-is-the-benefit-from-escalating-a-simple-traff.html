**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What is the benefit from escalating a simple traffic stop into a felony arrest by refusing to give your name and ID to a police officer?** None. There really is no benefit to escalating a simple traffic stop into a felony (or misdemeanor) arrest. You won't beat the ticket by increasing your belligerence. You will *only* make the situation worse by escalating noncompliance. There simply is no scenario in which things get *better* by turning a simple traffic stop into an arrest of any kind. But withholding your identification, which you are *required* to give when demanded by a police officer, and which you *agreed* to give when you received your license, and for which the DMV can *revoke* your license (automatically in some states)? And FYI, in most jurisdictions, it's at best a misdemeanor to fail to identify yourself to a police officer. At which point you'll be arrested, and you'll be held in jail until you can be positively identified.
