**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What's the difference between an executive order and a national emergency?** They are overlapping insofar as a national emergency is declared by using an Executive Order. An Executive Order (EO) is an order written by the President directing operations of a Federal agency. He can change policy, create new policies, rescind policies, and direct other activities of Federal agencies, as long as those changes are consistent with Federal law and the limits imposed on the President by the Constitution. Basically, the President is the CEO of the Executive Branch Company, and he can make new rules for his departments to follow whenever he chooses. A "national emergency" is a function spelled out by the [[National Emergencies Act](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=eaf2df&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Emergencies_Act){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"} ]{.qlink_container}which was passed by Congress in 1976. It clearly defines the powers the President is allowed to invoke by declaring a national emergency, provides structure and procedure around what he has to do when doing so, and provides a framework for Federal law to define what those powers are. To date, there are approximately 470 different provisions of Federal law that a President may invoke by declaring a national emergency. It is declared through an Executive Order. There are currently 28 national emergencies which are ongoing. The longest was declared by President Carter in 1978 and is still ongoing today.
