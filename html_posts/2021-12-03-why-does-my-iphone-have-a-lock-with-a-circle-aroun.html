**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} While I'm not exactly sure which icon you are referring to, most likely what you are seeing is the "Orientation Lock" icon. Your iPhone is locked in Portrait mode, so even if you turn your iPhone sideways while you watch a YouTube video, the screen will not rotate. This is useful if you often lay down on your side while you look at your phone, and you don't want it to rotate to where it will be sideways from how you are looking at it. To release the lock (assuming you are running iOS 12 or higher), from the home screen, drag down from the top right corner. You'll see your Control Panel (note that if you are running an older version of iOS, you may need to drag up from the bottom right corner). :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-d8c8b891bdff3ee55d1e688251b1db03){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-d8c8b891bdff3ee55d1e688251b1db03" master_w="132" master_h="263"} ::: :::: If you see the same lock with circle highlighted (as it is in the example), then your Rotation Lock is enabled. Click that icon to toggle it off, and when you turn your phone sideways in compatible apps, the screen will rotate to a Landscape orientation (long-ways) instead of Portrait orientation (normal tall orientation). \ Original question: Why does my iPhone have a lock with a circle around it?
