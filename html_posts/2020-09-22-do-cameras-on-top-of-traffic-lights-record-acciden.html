**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do cameras on top of traffic lights record accidents?** No, for a variety of reasons, not the least of which is that there is no infrastructure to do so. The camera (a low-resolution inexpensive camera) replaces the old ground loop sensor to tell the traffic light system that a car is waiting at the light. That's all it does: presence of car, absence of car. It's not powerful enough to provide any useful information, and it is focused on the lane or lanes it is monitoring, it's not a wide-angle security camera. Its feed is connected to the light controller. Some cities have broadband feeds to the light controllers and can watch live the information from the cameras, but again, the feed is low-res and mostly useless for much else besides sensing the presence of a car.
