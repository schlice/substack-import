**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Unfortunately, your assumption is incorrect. It is only the court that is concerned with your innocence or guilt. The police do not adjudicate innocence or guilt, the police only deal with preponderance of evidence. Only a court of law in the jurisdiction in which the incident occurred can determine whether or not you are guilty of that offense. The police do not have any say in that determination. If the police are called as witnesses, it is only to clarify facts in evidence. If the police had any concern with innocence or guilt, that would make the police judge, jury, and executioner.
