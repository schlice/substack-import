Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Flying as a passenger on a military transport is no more or less dangerous than flying on a commercial airliner from an aircraft safety point of view, but there are several factors that make military travel slightly safer in other respects than civilian air travel. On military transport, when you are not sitting in jump seats, you are seated backwards. Studies have shown that sitting facing rearward is a safer position to sit, as it minimizes blunt force traumas which may occur if the aircraft lurches during landing, or if there is a...ahem..."sudden deceleration" from an "unscheduled landing." If that particular unscheduled landing is survivable, you have a better chance of minimizing injuries by sitting facing rearward. But John Q. Public likes to ride in their airplanes the same way they ride in the bus or in the car: facing the front, even though it is demonstrably safer to face the rear while flying. Additionally, military airfields are significantly less crowded than commercial airports, so you are less likely to encounter ground impingement at a military airfield. Finally (and not to be terribly underestimated), because the general public does not ride on military aircraft, there is far less threat of nefarious or unruly characters on board than on a civilian passenger plane.
