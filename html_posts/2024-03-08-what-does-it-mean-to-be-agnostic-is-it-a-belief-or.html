**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Agonstics are different than atheists. Atheists say "I know there is no god," while agnostics say "I don't know if there is a god." But there are two types of agnostics. Ordinary agnostics say "We don't know anything for sure." Ornery agnostics say "We can't know anything for sure." The ornery agnostic's position, however, is logically unsupportable. If you say, "We can't know anything for sure," you are making an *absolute* truth statement to try to prove something which you are claiming you can't know absolutely. In other words, when you say you can't know anything for sure, *how do you know* ***that*** *for sure?* You'd have to know what you claim you can't know in order to make that statement. So it is logically unsupportable. It's a self-defeating position. So let's look at the ordinary agnostic's position. If, as the ordinary agnostic does, you say, "We don't know anything for sure," then why don't you know? Most ordinary agnostics at this point claim that the reason that they don't know is because no one has shown them the evidence. The problem is that the evidence they think they want just isn't available.
