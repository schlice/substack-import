**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, large magazines do not violate any rights. But the question becomes, what is "large?" To a gun owner, "large" means "anything which greatly exceeds the standard magazine designed for the firearm" To a gun control advocate, "large" means "whatever I think is too many." So a 100-round magazine is probably too large for an M-4, but not too large for a SAW. But a 30-round magazine is just right for an M-4, because that's the standard magazine for that firearm. However, if we're talking about my Springfield XD-S compact .45 EDC pistol, the "standard" magazine doesn't allow me to grip with my full hand; my pinky barely catches the bottom of the grip. However, I can replace the standard mag with the "extended" mag, get one extra round, and have a place to rest my pinky, thus providing me with a better grip. So does that make the "extended" magazine "large?" Ultimately, I go back to my original point, the large magazine doesn't violate anyone's rights. I'd argue that in some cases, the standard magazine violates my right to grip my firearm firmly. \ Original question: Do large magazines violate any rights?
