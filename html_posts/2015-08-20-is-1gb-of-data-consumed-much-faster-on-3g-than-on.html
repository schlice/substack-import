--- title: '"Is 1GB of data consumed much faster on 3G than on WiFi?"' date: '2015-08-20' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There are so many varieties of 3G service around the world, but in general, you\'re talking about data rates on the order of 400 Kbps to 4 Mbps, on average. Your mileage may vary, as this is variable by carrier, handset manufacturer, signal strength, etc. 4G signals (LTE) come a little closer, with some cellular networks getting 40Mbps down and around 19Mbps up.\ \ WiFi connections also vary depending on signal strength, handset manufacturer, access point manufacturer, etc. But in general, WiFi data rates are as follows:\ \ 802.11b: 11Mbps\ 802.11g: 54Mbps\ 802.11n: 300-450Mbps\ \ Most phones today connect comfortably to G networks. Not as many connect to N networks, and even those that do only have a single receiving antenna (so they\'re stuck at the lower number).\ \ So the answer is no, you would absolutely consume data faster on WiFi than on 3G.\ \ The good news is that most US-based cellular carriers do not count your data usage when you use your own WiFi connection. They only count your data usage when you use the cellular (3G or 4G) network. So not only is WiFi faster, it\'s free (usually - check with your carrier on this).]{.rendered_qtext}
