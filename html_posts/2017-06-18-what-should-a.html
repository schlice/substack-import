**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There was an inherent problem in the Castile case. Castile was working under two separate orders: reach for your license, but don't reach for your gun. In my opinion, the police erred by telling Castile to simultaneously reach for his license but not reach for his gun, making it very difficult for the officer to distinguish between the two "reaches." The officer still feared for his life, which was the only reason why he was acquitted of murder charges. That doesn't make the incident any less tragic. It also doesn't fit the BLM mantra of racist cops just looking for reasons to kill blacks. It makes no difference why he was in fear for his life: whether Castile was black, whether or not the officer was racist, or because Castile had a gun. It doesn't matter. The police officer told Castile to do something, and then shot him for doing exactly what he was told. The police officer should have been held accountable for causing the error that led to Castile's death, and I'm sure he will be held to account in civil court, when Castile's family follows with their wrongful death suit. Murder wasn't going to stick, but I see a very clear case of wrongful death here. There's no evidence that the officer was afraid for his life because Castile was black. That's not why the officer should have been convicted. He should have been convicted because Castile died following the lawful orders of the police. Don't turn his death into something that it's not.
