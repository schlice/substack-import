**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What surprises **me** is that Britons are willing to accept that unarmed officers should have no personal ability to defend themselves against armed assailants, regardless of how good their hand-to-hand skills may be. While I've worked as a US police officer, I would never personally volunteer to be an unarmed officer, ever. I applaud, and am in continual awe of, the work [[Adey Hill](https://www.quora.com/profile/Adey-Hill)]{.qlink_container} and his comrades do every day, and we in the US are saddened and horrified by their loss, but being a PC in the UK is not a job you'd be able to get me to do. Yes, in America, individual liberty is paramount. We have **guaranteed individual rights** which the government cannot infringe upon. Even for the collective good. In our view, collective welfare is socialism. The United States government ***promotes the general welfare***, but does not ***provide*** it. And we like it just fine that way.
