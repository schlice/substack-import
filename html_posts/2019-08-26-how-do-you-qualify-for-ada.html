**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do you qualify for ADA?** An individual qualifies for protection under the ADA if they have a recognized disability. Remember that this definition of disability is a *legal* one, which differs from a *medical diagnosis*. A **person with a** **disability** *under ADA* (which means the legal definition) is defined as a person who has a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activity. This includes people who have a record of such an impairment, even if they do not currently have a disability. The definition also includes individuals who do not have a disability but are regarded as having a disability.
