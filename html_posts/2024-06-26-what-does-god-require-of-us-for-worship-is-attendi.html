--- title: '"What does God require of us for worship? Is attending church once or twice a week sufficient, or is more expected of us?"' date: '2024-06-26' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} God doesn't require worship. He doesn't need our worship. We don't need to worship Him. **We worship Him because He is worthy of worship.** We worship Him because of what He has done for us. Regarding church attendance, the Bible talks about worshipping together: > *not giving up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, > but encouraging one another---and all the more as you see the Day > approaching. --- Hebrews 10:25 NIV* In the early church, people were avoiding meeting together, and the author of Hebrews says that's not a good idea. We need the encouragement of other believers, especially as we approach the end times. Our friends at [[GotQuestions.org](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e4ff7d&url=http://GotQuestions.org){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="gotquestions.org"}]{.qlink_container} talked about all the reasons why we need to regularly attend church. Their list is so exhaustive and comprehensive that I figured I'd just reproduce it here: > Church is the place where believers can love one another (1 John > 4:12), encourage one another (Hebrews 3:13), "spur" one another to > love and good works (Hebrews 10:24), serve one another (Galatians > 5:13), instruct one another (Romans 15:14), honor one another (Romans > 12:10), and be kind and compassionate to one another (Ephesians 4:32). > > When a person trusts Jesus Christ for salvation, he or she is made a > member of the body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12:27). For a church body > to function properly, all of its "body parts" need to be present and > working (1 Corinthians 12:14--20). It's not enough to just attend a > church; we should be involved in some type of ministry to others, > using the spiritual gifts God has given us (Ephesians 4:11--13). A > believer will never reach full spiritual maturity without having that > outlet for his gifts, and we all need the assistance and encouragement > of other believers (1 Corinthians > 12:21--26).[\[1\]](#WWxDR){#cite-WWxDR .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-WWxDR){.citation_index} [[Why is church attendance / going to church important?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4a32d7&url=https://www.gotquestions.org/church-attendance.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="gotquestions.org"}]{.qlink_container}
