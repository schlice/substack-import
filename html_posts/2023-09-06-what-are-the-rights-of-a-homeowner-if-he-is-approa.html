--- title: '"What are the rights of a homeowner if he is approached by an individual who claims to have the right to live on his property under some obscure law?"' date: '2023-09-06' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The homeowner in possession of the title deed to the property (or the named individual on the mortgage) retains ownership of the property while the claimant hires an attorney and attempts to make a claim on the property. The process works its way through the courts until either the claimant or the title holder is successful. If the claimant is found to be the rightful owner, the current deed will be declared null and void, and new lawsuits will ensue because the current residents have been paying for a property which they are not entitled to own, and they will want just compensation for that error. The two parties will work out a suitable arrangement which will allow the current residents to vacate the property in a reasonable amount of time.
