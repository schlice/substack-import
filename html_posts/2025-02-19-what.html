**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No, they are not above the law. But they know that they're never going to be called out on it, as it is "technically" not a Constitutional rights violation. While using their foot to keep your door open is "technically" an entry into your home, they don't actually "enter" your home. So at best, it's a legal gray area. (EDIT) Some states have actually legislated this already, defining when someone "enters" the home. For every state which has passed this legislation, "breaking the plane" of the door constitutes "entry" into the home. So in those states, sticking a foot in the door would be an unlawful entry without exigent circumstances or a warrant. So for the states which do not have this protection, how do you prevent this? How can you legally protect yourself? There are a number of ways to combat this type of activity. - The one I like the best is ***installing a screen door.*** I can open my main door and leave my screen door closed. I can talk to the officer through my screen door without ever going outside or leaving an open path. Now, they cannot legally open your screen door, but you can still have a full conversation right through it. Besides, there are a number of benefits to having a screen door on your entry door. Officers have been known to cover security cameras when they arrive on your front porch, and *some of this activity is actually department policy.* They will actually stand in front of your doorbell camera to block that, so you can't rely on any camera which is physically at body level. - At the same time, if a police officer is talking to you at your door, ***record them with your phone***. They have body cams, you need your own. You should always have two recordings going, your phone and your cameras.
