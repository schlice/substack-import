**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do you get arrested for false imprisonment?** Detain someone against their will. This is either called "false arrest" or "false imprisonment" Stores have to be keenly aware of this, as "shopkeeper's privilege" can easily turn into false imprisonment if you detain someone's free movement against their will and you don't have probable cause to suspect the person is a shoplifter. In most cases, only security guards or loss prevention can detain someone from leaving a store citing shopkeeper's privilege, but as in all things legal. these shopkeeper's privilege rules vary wildly from state to state.
