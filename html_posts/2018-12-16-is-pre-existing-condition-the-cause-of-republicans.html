**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is pre-existing condition the cause of Republicans damning ACA, ObamaCare? Are they being thoughtful to American needs or just trying to undo Obama policies?** Close. The real reason Republicans didn't like ACA was the pre-existing condition's requirement: the individual mandate. Mostly however, it's a Democrat talking point. Every plan Republicans brought forward to replace ACA with, even before ACA was passed, protected pre-existing conditions. Every single one. Not a single Republican has ever talked about denying coverage to anyone with pre-existing conditions. However, actions speak louder than words. It is true that many Republican-proposed plans in the past had "holes" where their protections were not nearly as strong as ACA. The reason? **ACA could force pre-existing condition coverage because of the individual mandate.** In other words, in order to force insurance companies to pay for all pre-existing conditions, there has to be a way to pay for it. ACA did it with the individual mandate. Since Republican plans did not have an individual mandate, their plans may have had sliding scale deductibles, or loopholes like insurance plans could deny coverage for specific conditions if they couldn't deliver services adequately. This means they could still offer coverage, but they could deny individual services because they couldn't adequately deliver them. Ergo, Republicans want to deny you coverage for pre-existing conditions. Not exactly true, but not exactly untrue either. A perfect talking point.
