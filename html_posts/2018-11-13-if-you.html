**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If your flying an F-18 and your plane alerts you that a Surface to air missle \[sic\] has been launched at you what is the first thing you do and how would you remain calm and not panic?** This is a valid question, and it goes to the type and the quality of the training you received. In training, we were placed into situations which induced great stress. This was done repeatedly and regularly throughout training. This is designed to *numb* you to the stress. Eventually, you start *thinking* through the stress. You can ignore the stress, and you can continue to think, and act. I can remember the point at which I recognized that the stress no longer affected me, and I was able to think through it. It's an out-of-body sort of experience. Then, when your C&W (caution and warning) panel is lit up like a Christmas tree, you don't panic. You think through the stress, and you continue to do the primary job: *flying the plane*. And, if you can, you get your plane home safely. And, if you can, you complete your mission. Or, as in this question, when your [[MAW](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f9fc53&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Missile_approach_warning_system){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} starts to yell at you, you don't panic. You follow your training, and you employ the appropriate countermeasures.
