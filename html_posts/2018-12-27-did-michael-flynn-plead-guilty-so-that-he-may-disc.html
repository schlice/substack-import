--- title: '"Did Michael Flynn plead guilty so that he may disclose secrets of wrongdoing that he cannot disclose due to NDA and State secrets privilege?"' date: '2018-12-27' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Did Michael Flynn plead guilty so that he may disclose secrets of wrongdoing that he cannot disclose due to NDA and State secrets privilege?** No. Non-disclosure agreements are considered to be unenforceable if they conceal criminal activity. Therefore, even if criminal activity was covered under an NDA, it would not be unlawful to discuss it, as the NDA would not apply. Flynn plead guilty to lying to the FBI, not to committing any other crimes. The guilty plea does not remove any valid non-disclosure agreements that may be in place. If he violates any such agreements, he is still liable for whatever penalties were specified in those agreements (unless, as I said, the agreement was designed to prevent the disclosure of criminal acts). Remember: NDAs impose civil penalties, not criminal ones. In fact, the standard boilerplate of most NDAs includes language that exempts the agreement for disclosures required by law. To that end, if an NDA is attempting to prevent you from disclosing information about criminal activity, the NDA doesn't apply. State secrets probably doesn't apply, since this is more related to things like human intelligence operations which require secrecy, or the inability to publish certain classified information, or the use of classified information in criminal cases. It's unlikely that Flynn was involved in classified dealings while part of the *campaign*. He was more likely to be involved with classified material while part of the *transition team*, after the election. Flynn's investigation was about his time *before* the election.
