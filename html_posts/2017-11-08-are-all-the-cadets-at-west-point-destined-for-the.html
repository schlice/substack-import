--- title: '"Are all the cadets at West Point destined for the Army? Or do some graduate and then move directly to the USN or the USAF?"' date: '2017-11-08' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} U. S. military academy cadets from any academy can **cross-commission** into another service. They can apply for, and accept, a commission in any service, depending on availability of slots in the other service. I had several USAFA classmates who took Army or Marine Corps commissions. The number who cross-commission is very low; generally if you're at the **Air Force** Academy, you'd really like to be in the **Air Force**, and similarly for the other service academies. But perhaps there was some other factor that didn't allow you to get into the service academy of your choice, so you entered whatever service academy you could make it into with hopes of cross-commissioning. Or perhaps, as in my classmates' cases, you have a better chance of doing the job you really want to do in another service than in the Air Force. Regardless, we had roughly 1200 graduates in my class, and I think there were maybe 10 cadets who cross-commissioned.
