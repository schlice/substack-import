--- title: "Why is it not legal for people to get together to shun those who offend them?" date: "2015-07-10" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [It is legal to ostracize people who offend you. It may actually be the only way that you can communicate to some people that they are offensive.\ \ In commerce, it is not legal to refuse service to someone if they are a member of a protected class (race, color, creed, sex, national origin, and in some states, sexual orientation). So it is not legal to say \"Blacks offend me, I will not serve them\" in your restaurant.\ \ Otherwise, you and your group can absolutely refuse to do business with someone who offends you. There is no requirement that you engage in business with someone. That\'s called a \"boycott\" and it is perfectly legal and an appropriate free-market response to personal offense.]{.rendered_qtext}
