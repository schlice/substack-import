--- title: "Do we still have the right to travel by road even though governments require us to pass tests, obtain licenses, insurance, registration, and wear safety gear while using public roads?" date: "2024-11-26" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, you still have the right to travel by road. The government simply limits what you must do in order to **operate a motor vehicle on a public road**. You can travel *on a bicycle* by road. You can travel *using your feet* by road. You can even travel *as a passenger* in any motor vehicle on any road. Governments only require you to pass tests, obtain licenses, insurance, and registration when you are ***operating a motor vehicle*** on a public road. Wearing safety gear is only applicable when operating a motorcycle or a bicycle on public roads. But "traveling" using **any other method besides "driving a car" (legally known as "operating a motor vehicle") does not require any of the conditions you listed.**
