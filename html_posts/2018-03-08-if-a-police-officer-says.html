**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a police officer says, "I\'m not arresting you, just detaining you with handcuffs," could a case be made that this is in essence an arrest?** No. Detention (custody) and arrest are two separate, legal terms. You are under arrest when a police officer has a reasonable belief that you have committed a crime. The officer must be able to articulate that belief as well as present the relevant facts which lead him to that belief. You can be detained by the police for lawful reasons, which include but are not limited to, your safety, the officer's safety, de-escalation of an emotionally charged incident, etc. You can be detained by the police without being placed under arrest, and usually the officer will tell you that you are not under arrest but simply being detained for your safety as well as his.
