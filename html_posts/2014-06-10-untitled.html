**Content:** [You contradicted yourself. You can call it semantics, but you said \"**Guns kill people**\" and then you said \"They are built, designed, \[\...\] distributed, and employed **to give one the means to take the life of another**\" (emphasis mine). This is a very important distinction, because on one hand you said that \"the gun killed someone\" and on the other hand you said \"someone used a gun to kill someone else\" which means \"someone killed someone else (with a gun)\". That\'s more than just semantics; that\'s the difference between someone killing someone and a gun killing someone.\ \ You are correct that a gun is purpose-built to kill, just as a car is purpose-built to drive. While cars and knives can cause death, they are not purpose-built to kill. This is the reason that guns are so often targeted: because they are purpose-built to kill. However, **homicide** is a crime in which one **person** takes the life of another **person** using a tool. Guns, of their own volition, do not take the life of another person. A gun cannot commit homicide. A gun can be used by someone to commit the crime of homicide, but a gun, by itself, cannot kill. Despite what you might hear in the media, guns do not mysteriously \"go off\" without someone pulling the trigger. There are numerous internal safeties within a gun to prevent such an occurrence. The trigger has to actually be pulled in order for the gun to fire (with the notable exception of some automatic weapons with free-floating firing pins, but that\'s outside the scope of the question).\ \ Therefore, a gun cannot kill, just like \"pencils do not misspell words and forks do not make people fat\" (thanks [[Chris Bast](https://www.quora.com/profile/Chris-Bast)]{.qlink_container}). It\'s people that do all these things, not the implements they use to do them.]{.rendered_qtext}
