**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **I just bought a used car from a dealership. I came home and realized that the price on the receipt is higher than the price of the car on the internet. What should I do? What are my rights?** While the correct answer is *caveat emptor,* you may have a claim if you believe the dealership quoted you one price and sold you on another. An *auto dealer fraud lawyer* can help with unfair business practices such as failure to disclose damages, price packing or deal packing, and misleading dealer add-ons and bundled extras. They can also help you with bait-and-switch scams and misquoted or intentional overcharging. If you think this is what happened to you, consider contacting an auto dealer fraud lawyer in your area. [[Auto Dealer Fraud Lawyer - Auto Dealer Fraud Attorney, Law Firm Directory](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7ade37&url=https://lawyers.findlaw.com/lawyer/practice/auto-dealer-fraud){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="findlaw.com"}]{.qlink_container} If this didn't happen to you, it may be that the price on the Internet was only valid if you did certain things (like use their preferred financing, or buy a certain add-on package). If this is true, then you're probably out of luck. Check the fine print on the Internet ad. It would usually state something like the price is only valid if you finance through the dealer, or something else you have to do to get that price.
