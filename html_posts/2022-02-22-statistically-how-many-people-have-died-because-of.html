Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is not a valid statistic that can be known. There are several things that would need to be true before you could know what you are asking: - SImply being unvaccinated, or not wearing a mask, would have to be a guaranteed cause of death, and it is not. - Being vaccinated or wearing a mask would have to be a guaranteed prevention of transmission of COVID, and it is not. Therefore, you can never know how many people have died because of people refusing vaccination or masking, because vaccination and masking do not prevent transmission of the disease, nor do they prevent death, in themselves or others. And before the comments start coming in, *slowing down* the rate of transmission does not equal *preventing* transmission. *Reducing* the likelihood of being hospitalized with severe illness does not equal *preventing* death. \ Original question: Statistically, how many people have died because of people refusing vaccination or masking? Themselves or others?
