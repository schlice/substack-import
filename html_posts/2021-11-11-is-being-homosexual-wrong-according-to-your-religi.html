**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} With relation to Christianity, "being homosexual" and "wrong" are terms of art which must be nuanced before we can answer the question. Love in the Bible is a strange thing. The big issue we have is translation. There are [[six different words for love](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=23034c&url=https://www.yesmagazine.org/health-happiness/2013/12/28/the-ancient-greeks-6-words-for-love-and-why-knowing-them-can-change-your-life){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="yesmagazine.org"}]{.qlink_container} in Greek. So you can't simply say that "homosexual love is wrong." You have to *define* which love we're talking about. Most of the Greek forms of love are allowed, and even encouraged, between same-sex people in Christianity. But one type of love, *eros*, is limited to married couples, and the Bible only recognizes marriage as something which God has ordained between one man and one woman. So "being homosexual" in and of itself is not "wrong" according to my religion. "Engaging in homosexual sex" is considered to be a sin in my religion, and could rightly be classified as "wrong." But that sin makes the homosexual *exactly like everyone else in Christianity:* ***a sinner.*** We are all sinners. We may sin differently, from one to another, but we're all sinners. I'd rather not dwell on my past sin, but I was a sinner, just like the homosexual. That said, we are *saved* through God's grace: he gives us what we do not deserve. He pays for our past sin. He does this when we **repent** and **believe.** Repentance involves a "turning away" from our past sins. We acknowledge that we are sinners and that we have fallen short of the glory of God. And crucially, we resolve to *turn away* from our sins and to not sin any more. In short, we admit we did the bad things, and we stop doing the bad things. In return, we're given, freely, everlasting life. Sounds like a pretty good deal. Being a homosexual is no different than being addicted to porn or drugs, or allowing money to supplant God in your life, or being an adulterer, or being \. The difference is that the Christian recognizes his or her sinful ways, and resolves to not sin any more. No one said it would be easy. It is never easy to struggle against the desires of the flesh. Sin is pleasurable, of this there is no doubt. But we recognize our sinful nature and we resolve to be *better* than our sin. This is what God wants for us. He knows we are human. He took human form so he could experience everything we experience, including the temptation of sin. So he truly knows what we go through. But he overcame it. He lived a sinless life. While we fall short of that standard, he recognizes who we are, and he knows our heart. The question becomes: do you desire the sin more, or do you desire God more? \ Original question: Is being homosexual wrong according to your religion?
