In those states, you can still legally get an abortion. If *Roe* is overturned, 23 states have enacted laws banning abortion. 10 of those states banned abortion since before *Roe*, but their laws were unenforceable. 13 states have "trigger laws" which will automatically go into effect if *Roe* is overturned. Florida just passed a 15 week abortion ban which will go into effect in July. This is the immediate effect after *Roe*, but this map can and will change over time. Justice Ginsburg predicted this would happen. She predicted that *Roe* would be overturned, and from the way things look today, she was right.
