**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can my employer force the vaccine on me or fire me? I like my job and don't have any skills or a degree. I live in Texas and I have worked there for five years at a senior retirement home.** Texas is a "right to work" state, and their retaliation laws are weak. They have anti-retaliation laws for discrimination and for very specific federal law violations, but they do not have anti-retaliation law regarding failing to participate in a violation of federal law. While forcing someone to get a vaccine or face termination would be grounds for a wrongful termination lawsuit, retaliation for failing to participate in a violation of federal law is not protected under Texas' anti-retaliation law. I've [[already answered](https://www.quora.com/Is-this-legal-I-was-fired-for-refusing-the-covid-vaccine-because-I-believe-there-s-not-enough-testing-and-EUA-approval-only-I-wanted-to-wait-for-long-term-results-and-FDA-approval-first-Can-they-fire-me-In-Oregon/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} this[ [in other places](https://www.quora.com/Could-a-boss-fire-someone-that-refuses-to-get-the-COVID-19-vaccine/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} about strong employee protection states, but Texas is not one of them. Sorry about that. While it is still possible to sue, since violation of the Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act can carry criminal penalties, it's unlikely to succeed.
