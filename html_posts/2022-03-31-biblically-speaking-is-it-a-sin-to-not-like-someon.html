**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it a sin to not like someone?** Don't confuse the biblical *love* for liking someone, or being friends with them. Mark 12:28--31 says: > One of the teachers of the law came and heard them debating. Noticing > that Jesus had given them a good answer, he asked him, "Of all the > commandments, which is the most important?" > > "The most important one," answered Jesus, "is this: 'Hear, O Israel: > The Lord our God, the Lord is one. Love the Lord your God with all > your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind and with all > your strength.' The second is this: '**Love** your neighbor as > yourself.' There is no commandment greater than these." The Hebrew word for love used in the bold is *agapÄ“seis,* the root of which is [[*agape*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=388322&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agape){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}*,* which is "love of mankind." It is "the highest form of love" that the Hebrew language could express. It's the kind of love God has for his people. So Jesus is commanding us to love our neighbor with the same kind of love that God has for his people, and also the same kind of love we have for ourselves. But *agape* doesn't mean you have to like them. It doesn't even mean you have to be friends with them. Indeed, if you love them and you have an issue with them, you should be trying to working it out. If you love them and they are sinful, you are to show them through your love that there is a better way to live. **Is it also biblical to love the sinner but hate their ways?** Absolutely. In fact, if you love the sinner (you should), you should "hate" their sinful ways. This doesn't mean you can't be friends with a sinner. Hello, we're *all* sinners. But that doesn't mean we must accept their "sinful ways." We love the sinner, we don't love the sin. If I wasn't friends with sinners, I'd have zero friends. We're all sinners. This doesn't mean we condone their sin, though. We don't have to accept it. We have to *love them through it.* \ Original question: Biblically speaking, is it a sin to not like someone?
