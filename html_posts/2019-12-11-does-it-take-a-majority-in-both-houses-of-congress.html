Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does it take a majority in both houses of Congress to impeach a President?** No. It takes a **simple majority in the House of Representatives** to impeach a President. It takes a **supermajority in the Senate** to *then* remove the President from office. The Senate is a check against partisan impeachments in the House. You would think that this would be enough to dissuade a rogue House from filing a biased, one-sided, partisan impeachment, but when you want to use impeachment as an election ploy instead of to combat high crimes and misdemeanors, facts don't matter.
