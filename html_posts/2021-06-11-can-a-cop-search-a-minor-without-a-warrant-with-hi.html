**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Generally, yes. Parental consent is permissible to allow a search of a minor. Without consent, probable cause would be next required. What constitutes probable cause is an area of Fourth Amendment law that is continuously evolving and being refined by case law. Without parental consent or probable cause, the police would need a warrant to conduct a search. If the minor is in a school, the lesser standard of reasonable suspicion is applied, in order to maintain a safe and orderly school environment, and with the additional understanding that the school acts *in loco parentis*. Like probable cause, what constitutes reasonable suspicion is continuously evolving.
