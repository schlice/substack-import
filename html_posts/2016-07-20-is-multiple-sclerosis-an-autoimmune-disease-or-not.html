--- title: '"Is multiple sclerosis an autoimmune disease or not?"' date: '2016-07-20' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Multiple Sclerosis is "autoimmune" not because of the blood-brain barrier, but because it is the immune system that is mistakenly attacking normal, healthy body tissue. The definition of an autoimmune disease is one in which the body's own immune system turns on it and attacks normal body tissue. In the case of MS, T lymphocytes will attack the myelin sheath surrounding nerve fibers in the CNS (yes, after penetrating the blood-brain barrier) and leaves plaques, or "scars" in its place where nerve signals are degraded. This is why the disease is called "multiple sclerosis," or "many scars."
