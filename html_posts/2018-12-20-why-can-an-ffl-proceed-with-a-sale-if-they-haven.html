**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why can an FFL proceed with a sale if they haven\'t received an answer on an NICS background check in three business days?** Primarily because it prevents government from violating the Second Amendment. The "default allow" policy prevents the government from shutting down the NICS system and imposing a *de facto ban* on gun sales simply by failing to process any NICS requests. If that policy was not in place, the government could bypass the 2nd Amendment and create a *de facto* gun ban simply by shutting down the NICS system. In this way, it is incumbent on the government to not only provide an answer, but to provide a *timely* answer.
