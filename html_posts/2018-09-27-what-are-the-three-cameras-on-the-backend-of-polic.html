**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You're probably asking about Automatic License Plate Recognition (ALPR) systems :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-eeca5d5cdf9263a17aebeb69a42962ad){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-eeca5d5cdf9263a17aebeb69a42962ad" master_w="1280" master_h="960"} ::: :::: *\[Photo by Mike Katz-Lacabe (*[[*CC-BY*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cee8bb&url=https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/legalcode){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="creativecommons.org"}]{.qlink_container}*)\]* These systems scan and capture the license plates of vehicles all around the patrol car throughout the shift. as well as the date and time, and the GPS location. The license plates are uploaded into a database, which can then be referenced to show patterns of movement, show whether or not a vehicle was near a crime scene, or show vehicles which may be associated with each other.
