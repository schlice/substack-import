**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As most of the answerers have alluded to, the answer depends greatly on where you are located. Because I am located in the US, I will base my answer on their laws (generally), but your situation may be different where you live, as well as different laws between states in the US, so be very careful and make sure you know the law beforehand. Let's start with what to do *before* a burglar enters your home. There's an old story told about two hunters in Africa who stumble upon a den of sleeping lions. As the lions begin to stir and eye their unfortunate meal, one of the hunters kneels down, removes his backpack, and extracts a pair of running shoes. The second hunter looks at him incredulously and says, "What are you, crazy? You can't outrun a lion!" The first hunter replies, "Oh, I don't have to outrun a lion. I just have to outrun *you*." Now, I bet you're wondering what this has to do with home security. The answer is this: You don't have to make your home a fortress. You only need to make your home *less* appealing to the burglar than your neighbor's home. So how do you do that? 1. **Lights**. Make sure your home is well lit on the outside. This used to be a fairly expensive proposition, especially the cost of running all those light bulbs all night. But with the advent of CFL and LED light bulbs, you can run many bulbs outside all night for nearly the cost of running a single 40-watt bulb. Setting your lights on a timer is a "set-it-and-forget-it" way to make sure your home is secure. You can also purchase lights which have ambient light sensors built in (they come on and dusk and turn off at dawn). Either way, burglars want to operate in the dark. Don't give them dark to work with. 2. **Shrubbery**. Along with the dark, burglars like to be concealed while they break into your house. If there are large shrubs or ground cover which provides cover for a burglar to work in, clear it out. You don't want to give a burglar the means to work on opening a window while not being seen from the sidewalk. 3. **Get a dog**. My Golden is the sweetest girl in the world. If you come in the house, she will lick your face to say hello and take you on a tour of all of her favorite toys. But if you're outside and you make even the slightest noise, it will sound like there's an angry horde of zombies outside and she's trying to wake up some very heavy sleepers. The point is that *if you're outside, you can't tell that she's a nice dog*. All you hear is the extremely vicious sounding barking. If you don't want to incur the expense of all the dog food, there are some pretty realistic dog barking simulators you can pick up on Amazon which are sound-activated. 4. While you're at it, **create a safe room**. A safe room is a small room (generally a closet, but can be any small room with limited access) in which you should pre-stage a cell phone (it doesn't need a service plan to call 911) and a charger, and some form of weapon (a baseball bat, a throwing weapon, or if you are so inclined and are legally able, a gun safe, preferably with a gun in it). Ideally, you should replace the door to this room with a solid-core door (most interior doors in US homes are hollow-core) on which you can put a deadbolt lock. But even if you can't replace the door, just having a locking door is a good start. Now, we can move on to what to do if a burglar *enters* your home. Most states in the United States have a very strong "Castle Doctrine." The Castle Doctrine stems from the old phrase, "A man's home is his castle." This means that you should feel completely safe inside your home, and no one should be able to threaten that safety. Castle Doctrine laws codify the fact that you can defend yourself inside your home if an intruder enters. You may not live in such a state. Some states require you to attempt to retreat (called "duty to retreat" states). You should investigate your state's laws regarding self-defense inside your home. If you hear a burglar enter your home, get to your safe room. Get inside, lock the door, and call 911. If you have a weapon in the room, equip it. Be ready to use it in case the person on the other side of the door is not a police officer. Stay on the phone with 911 until the police arrive. If the burglar enters your safe room, use your weapon. The only reason they would be trying to get in there is to do you harm. Attack them, and when they are incapacitated, ***run***. Get out of the house and get away. The house and the stuff are not more important than your life.
