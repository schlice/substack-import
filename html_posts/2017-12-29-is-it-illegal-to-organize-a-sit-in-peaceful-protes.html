--- title: '"Is it illegal to organize a sit-in (peaceful protest)?"' date: '2017-12-29' tags: - policy-analysis - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You have a 1st Amendment right to peaceably assemble. That said, and as with all Constitutional rights, that right is not unlimited, and there are some ways you can run into legal trouble. Think about where you are going to perform your sit-in. If you are inside a private facility (even one which is open to the public), you are at the mercy of the administrators and owners of that facility. They can revoke your "right" to be inside that facility at any time, and as soon as they do, you are immediately trespassing and are subject to arrest. Similarly, you do not have an unlimited right to "loiter" (i.e. be present without any business) within a government building, and you can be similarly arrested and removed. Your best bet? **Get permission before your sit-in.** Work with the management of the facility and help them understand the social benefits of your demonstration. In most cases, they should be accommodating, but you must be accommodating as well. If they tell you that you cannot block hallways and fire escapes, then don't do it, and expect to be removed if you do. Also, if you are school-age students, the administration may tolerate a time-limited protest, but not an all=day or all-week affair, and at some point your protest will be deemed a disruption to the academic environment and you'll be ordered back to class. In general, the more accommodating you are, the more accommodating they will be, and you'll be permitted to hold your protest. If you're going to be outside, you don't need a permit to have a protest in public parks or sidewalks, but you might want to get one anyway so that you can ensure that not only you have permission, but that counter-protests cannot occupy the same space as you. Despite what you may have seen in social media making you think that blocking streets is legal, it is most definitely not. You cannot march in the street without a permit. It is not Constitutionally-protected free speech when you impede traffic flow. Good luck!
