**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are many components which go into whether or not a telehealth session, in general, is HIPAA-compliant. Just conducting the session on FaceTime does not make the entire session HIPAA-compliant or not. But looking at FaceTime, in isolation, one can see that it can be used in a telehealth setting and provide a HIPAA-compliant experience. FaceTime is peer-to-peer, uses end-to-end encryption, and Apple does not store any FaceTime sessions on their servers, nor do they have the ability to decrypt live FaceTime sessions. With this information in mind, we can say that FaceTime is a HIPAA-compliant video chat solution, when employed with all of the other HIPAA safeguards that a clinician and a patient would normally employ. Doctors must still protect Personal Information as they normally would, and only provide it in accordance with pre-determined sharing agreements, just as a face-to-face appointment would work.
