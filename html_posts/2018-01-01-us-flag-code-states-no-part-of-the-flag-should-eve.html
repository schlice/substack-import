--- title: "U.S. Flag code states: No part of the flag should ever be used as a costume or athletic uniform. The NFL includes the US Flag decal on the helmet. Would this violate the Flag code or is the decal something different?" date: "2018-01-01" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is no, a flag decal is not subject to this portion of the flag code. Similarly, people wear flag patches and flag pins and flags embroidered on their jerseys for baseball and soccer and all sorts of sports, and none of this is in violation of the flag code. If the flag code was actually enforceable (see other answers about why it is not), *this* would actually be a violation of that portion of the flag code: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-ab81cda3c13f4f14e7467df1dfa126d9){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-ab81cda3c13f4f14e7467df1dfa126d9" master_w="300" master_h="300"} ::: :::: BTW, I have nothing against Kid Rock. But this particular American Flag Poncho would be a violation of the flag code. Notice how **the flag itself** is being used as a piece of clothing. This is what the flag code is referring to.
