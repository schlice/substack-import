Emotional support animals are **not** trained to perform a specific function to assist with a disability. A trained animal is not an "emotional support animal," it is a "service animal." The law is very clear about what a service animal is as opposed to an emotional support animal. To that end, **emotional support animals are not covered by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).** They do not have the same rights as service animals like seeing eye dogs or seizure dogs which have been specifically trained to perform a *service.* **Emotional support animals, therefore, are not covered by the same rules as service dogs. Businesses have the right to restrict emotional support animals from their premises, although frequently, they do not.** Keep in mind that businesses retain the right to restrict emotional service animals at any time, even if they initially allow the animal. At any time, they can ask you and the animal to leave.
