--- title: "Can you be arrested for choosing not to inform the police when you know something unlawful is occurring?" date: "2015-09-08" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There are a small handful of states that provide some \"duty to report\" in certain situations, and there are some professions which have professional (and in some cases, legal) obligations to report unlawful activity, but for the vast majority of states, for the vast majority of citizens, there is no legal requirement that you report anything to the police.\ \ If you take positive steps to conceal a crime, or aid or abet a known criminal, or do something to further the crime, these in and of themselves are crimes.\ \ However, simply having knowledge that something unlawful is occurring and not acting on that knowledge is not a crime.\ \ We would hope, however, that the moral code of the individual would dictate to them that they take some positive step to mitigate the crime, or its effect (such as contacting the police).]{.rendered_qtext}
