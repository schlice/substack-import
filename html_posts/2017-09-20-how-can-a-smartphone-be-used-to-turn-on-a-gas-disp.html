--- title: "How can a smartphone be used to turn on a gas dispenser?" date: "2017-09-20" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because of the design of the reader, you can't get a smartphone to directly communicate with the magnetic strip. Samsung's MST (Magnetic Secure Transfer) won't work with today's card readers. So there are a few solutions: 1. Add an external reader to the pump that will accept NFC. Any smartphone that supports NFC-based payment systems (Apple Pay, Android Pay, etc) can then be used at the pump. The smartphone would be initially scanned and the pump would place a hold (probably about \$50) on the card. Once the transaction is complete, the hold would clear and the actual charge would be made. 2. Implement a cloud-based solution where the pump and the smartphone connect to the same portal, and the payment is authorized to the card on file. When the user scans the QR code, the pump is authorized. At the conclusion of the dispense, the payment on file gets charged.
