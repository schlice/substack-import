--- title: "Do cops know if a car has insurance if they scan the car license plate?" date: "2015-01-12" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [In most states, insurance information is only provided to the DMV at the time of vehicle registration. So the DMV only knows that the vehicle was insured at the time that it was registered.\ \ Officers require an insurance card during a traffic stop because they need to see that the insurance is \"current.\" For the most part, insurance companies do not routinely provide this information to the DMV, and this information is not normally transmitted to the officer when running the plate.\ \ It is the driver\'s responsibility to carry an updated insurance card for the vehicle to show that the vehicle has active, current insurance.]{.rendered_qtext}
