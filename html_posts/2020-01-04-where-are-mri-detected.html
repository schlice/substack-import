**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Where are MRI-detected \"lesions\" located in the brain? Are they on nodes of the brain that actually control certain neurological functions, like sight, sound, memory, etc.** In Multiple Sclerosis, the immune system attacks myelinated axons in the central nervous system, destroying the myelin and the axon in variable degrees. Areas of white matter in the brain are affected, including those of the cerebral hemispheres, infratentorium, and spinal cord. MS lesions, known as plaques, may form in CNS white matter **in any location**, which makes diagnosis difficult.[\[1\]](#eEKda){#cite-eEKda .citation_link} Additionally, location of lesions does not necessarily correlate directly with clinical presentation, which makes MRI reading that much more challenging.
