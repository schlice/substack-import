Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Short answer: no.\ \ Longer answer: detection of deception is as much art as it is science. People deceive for many reasons, and the reasons span the emotional and rational gamut. Whether or not there are physical ramifications (change in sweating, tightening of the sphincter muscle, change in blood pressure, change in heartbeat) to whether or not we actually tell a lie is not a medical certainty. Polygraphers say they can accurately detect lying, but under actual scientific scrutiny, the percentage of detection of deception isn\'t much better than a coin toss.\ \ Because the odds aren\'t wonderful at actually being able to detect deception, such tools are used to add to the body of evidence and to aid investigators. They do not provide the final, definitive, and/or only answer as to whether or not a person is truthful.]{.rendered_qtext}
