Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Here's a good one: In Florida, if you buy a home which is part of a Homeowner's Association, the **buyer** is responsible for ensuring that the home meets all of the HOA's ongoing architectural approvals, and if it does not, the **buyer** becomes responsible for anything unapproved by the HOA when they take ownership. I have always thought this was a patently ridiculous idea. The HOA is under no obligation to tell a prospective buyer what is wrong with a given property, and there isn't a mechanism for a buyer to get an inspection of a home by the HOA before they buy it. But they become liable for it once they buy it. The buyer, for his part, would have to learn the entire Architectural Approval guidelines before they buy, and that just seems unreasonable to me.
