--- title: "How can you find out if a neighborhood Homeowners Association (HOA) exists?" date: "2022-09-09" tags: ["homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It's actually pretty simple, and you can do it all from your PC. **Step 1: Find the name of the HOA.** If an HOA exists in an area, it will be noted on the property information located on the county property assessor's website. Property details are public record. So take an address from the neighborhood and plug it in to the county's property assessor's website, and the detailed listing for that property should give you the name of the HOA which controls that neighborhood. **Step 2. Get info about the HOA** HOAs aren't "kid's clubs." They are 501(c)(4) non-profit corporations. This means they all have actual corporate filing documents and bylaws and articles of incorporation, which will all be on file with the state. So your next stop will be the state licensing agency which handles corporations within the state. Different agencies handle corporations in each state, but most states seem to allocate that practice under the Secretary of State's office. Regardless, find that office for that state and you can do a record search, plugging in the name of the corporation you learned in Step 1, and get all of its corporate information and Governing Documents. There you have it, you can now find HOA information for nearly any neighborhood in the US. \ Original question: How can you find out if a neighborhood Homeowners Association (HOA) exists?
