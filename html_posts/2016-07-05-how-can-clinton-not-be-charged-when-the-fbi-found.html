**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The question the FBI had to answer was whether or not she did something *criminal*. That means something that violates Federal law. They were unable to find clear evidence that she did something criminal. However, they did go so far as to say that Clinton was "extremely careless" in using a personal email address and server for sensitive information, declaring that an ordinary government official could have faced administrative sanction for such conduct. To warrant a criminal charge, there had to be evidence that Mrs. Clinton intentionally sent or received classified information --- something that the FBI did not find.
