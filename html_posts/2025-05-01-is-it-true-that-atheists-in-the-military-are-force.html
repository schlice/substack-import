**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Along with [[Chaplain Frank](https://www.quora.com/profile/Chaplain-Frank-1)]{.qlink_container}'s excellent answer, I used to have the same problem with people taking "smoke breaks." It was a bit unfair, no? I mean, I didn't smoke at the time, so how did they get to leave their duty stations for 15 minute smoke breaks while I didn't? The answer: I was just as permitted to take "fresh air" breaks at the same interval as they were to take "smoke breaks." Sundays are time off for just about everyone who had a M-F 9--5 job in the military. How you choose to spend your Sunday mornings is up to you. You can use that time to attend church services if you like, or you can just take that time to relax and unwind. When it came to positions which were 24x7, sometimes the mission takes priority and you have to work on a Sunday. Christians and other believers understand and accept this, so long as accommodations are made when building schedules for predicted work.
