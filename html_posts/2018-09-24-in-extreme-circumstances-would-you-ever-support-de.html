**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In extreme circumstances, would you ever support defamation being a crime in the US?** I don't think "extreme" circumstances are necessary. I do think "appropriate" circumstances are necessary. Those circumstances exist when the traditional civil relief for libel or slander do not, and cannot, adequately address the harm caused by the action. For example, in the "ripped from the headlines" department, I would support criminal defamation in the case where an accuser knowingly lies about a sexual assault accusation. In today's society, an accusation of sexual assault brings incredible harms to the accused, even without evidence and without any presumption of innocence. The accused can lose his job and his livelihood. An accusation can destroy marriages and families, and even leads some to suicide, and all without evidence. If this were to happen maliciously or falsely, a civil penalty could never be enough to undo the damage done. Sexual assault is a horrible crime and should be punished to the fullest extent of the law. I don't want to take anything away from victims, and the majority of women who claim they were assaulted actually were. But we know from some recent high profile cases that not all women always tell the truth, and some women use the accusation as a club to inflict harm instead of as a tool for justice. I have no problem making false allegations of sexual assault a crime of criminal defamation.
