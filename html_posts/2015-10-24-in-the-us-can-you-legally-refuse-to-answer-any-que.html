--- title: '"In the US, can you legally refuse to answer ANY question from a police officer without a lawyer present? Even your name, or where you live?"' date: '2015-10-24' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [In addition to what [[Cliff G.](https://www.quora.com/profile/Cliff-G-13)]{.qlink_container} already said, the courts have also determined that Miranda doesn\'t apply when you are pulled over and talking to an officer there. The \"temporary detention\" you experience when an officer stops your vehicle does not amount to \"custodial interrogation,\" so you don\'t have the right to remain silent until you\'ve had the chance to talk to your attorney. You do have to answer questions related to your driving, ownership of the car, license, registration, insurance, etc. You don\'t have to answer quetions ***not related to why you were stopped.***\ \ If you were stopped because you and your vehicle matched the description of someone who just robbed the gas station a half a block away, you\'re in a whole different ballgame. This is a custodial interrogation, and if you refuse to answer questions, you can expect to be arrested, and you\'ll likely have to sort it all out at the police station. But if you\'re being accused of a crime, you might do well to not answer questions, allow yourself to be arrested, and call your lawyer from the police station.]{.rendered_qtext}
