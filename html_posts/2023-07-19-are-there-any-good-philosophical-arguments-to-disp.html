--- title: '"Are there any good philosophical arguments to disprove the existence of God and an afterlife (not related to religion)?"' date: '2023-07-19' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Philosopher and scholar Mortimer Adler said, \"An affirmative existential proposition can be proved, but a negative existential proposition\--one that denies the existence of something\--cannot be proved.\" So no, there are no good philosophical arguments to disprove the existence of God. There are only philosophical arguments which argue *against* the philosophical arguments which **prove** the existence of God: the Cosmological, Teleological, Kalam, and Moral Arguments. But those are only arguments against the arguments which prove God's existence. There are no arguments which prove that God does not exist, because you cannot prove a negative existential proposition.
