Answer

**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Morals are developed throughout your life, and there are many influences which help you develop your morals. Your parents and your community are closely involved. You also develop morals based on your empathy and your sense of fairness. Humans have a desire to do what is right and to avoid what is wrong, and so your personal morals develop throughout your lifetime while you follow your "inner compass" which influences you to do what is right, and avoid what is wrong. Does a deity guide your moral development? No. You develop morals on your own, following your inner compass, and your empathy and sense of fairness. The question that atheists cannot answer: ***from where did your inner compass and your inbuilt sense of empathy and fairness derive?*** There is no evolutionary process which explains why all humans everywhere, regardless of country of origin or family or societal structure, have an inbuilt sense of empathy and strive for fairness and equality. These things are particularly inconsistent with Darwinism. "Survival of the fittest" is specifically inconsistent with empathy or fairness. Evolutionary biologists are coming out and saying that the currently accepted evolutionary model is insufficient to explain how things like fairness and empathy developed. At the Royal Society in 2016, they said that they need a "new paradigm" to explain how irreducibly complex biological structures and inbuilt morality could have evolved, because neo-Darwinian models are insufficient to explain them. So if the universe didn't evolve your sense of fairness and equity, and if the universe didn't evolve your sense of empathy, where did it come from, and why does every human have it? Because every human has it, it must come from some place which is overarching across all humans, and evolution is insufficient to explain it. The existence of this sense of empathy and fairness is what we call the Moral Law, and it is one of the logical proofs which point to the existence of a creator. The logic goes like this: 1. Every law has a law giver. 2. The Moral Law exists. 3. Therefore, there exists a Moral Law giver. Some call this inbuilt morality "conscience"; others call it "Natural Law"; still others (like our Founding Fathers) refer to it as "Nature's Law." Today, we refer to it as "the Moral Law." But whatever you call it, the fact that a moral standard has been prescribed on the minds of all human beings points to a Moral Law Prescriber. Every prescription has a prescriber. The Moral Law is no different. **Someone must have given us these moral obligations.** So you don't need a deity to develop morals. You need a deity to *explain why you develop specific morals*. Are there perfect morals? Yes. If the deity is the law giver, morals which develop perfectly in line with the dictates of the deity in question would be "perfect morals." In Christianity, there was one person in history who had "perfect morals" and that was Jesus Christ. Outside of Christ, humans are born into sinful bodies, and will never possess perfect morals. Additionally, people suppress what they know about morals all the time. In other words, even murderers know that murder is wrong, but they have instead found ways to justify it (this of course ignores the fact that some people have brains which do not function properly and cannot even sense their inbuilt moral law).
