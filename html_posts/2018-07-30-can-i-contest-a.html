**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can I contest a \"special event restriction\" parking ticket on the grounds that the parking app (ParkChicago) accepted the payment, and the city of Chicago cannot both accept a parking fee and also fine for parking?** You can, and you might even be successful. However, I'm certain that there is "fine print" in there somewhere (probably in the app) which says that you're still required to follow all local ordinances and posted signs at the actual location and ParkChicago is not responsible to inform you every time a space is unavailable due to local government action. For example, if you paid for a spot on a street that the police have closed, can you barge into the closed street and park there simply because the app accepted your payment? The answer to that clear hypothetical is, of course, no. Similarly, if the coordination between the local government and the app administrator is not up to snuff, there is probably a clause in the app which states that it's your responsibility to ensure that the street is accessible and the spot is not restricted. Now, if you arrived at the spot and it was not marked with a "special event - no parking" sign, then you may have a legitimate grievance. The app accepted your payment, and there was no marking that indicated that the space was closed. But I bet there was signage somewhere that indicated that there was no parking on that street or in that spot because of a special event.
