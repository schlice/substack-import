**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} AkA [[Christopher Hawk](https://www.quora.com/profile/Christopher-Hawk)]{.qlink_container} is, sadly, spot on. It\'s not the fact that it\'s a misdemeanor. It is what the offense says about your character. And despite what the media would lead you to believe, police departments look for candidates of exceptional character and candidates who possess a moral compass. I would think such an offense would be a serious impediment to getting hired as a police officer. That said, candidates are in short supply these days (courtesy of the good folks over at Black Lives Matter), so who knows, maybe your department might overlook such a transgression if you could show you had a stellar record since then. It will likely be an uphill climb, the odds are definitely not in your favor, but there is still a chance. You should definitely apply until you are told no by the department. Don\'t conceal it. Be up front about it, show how you have changed since then, and maybe you\'ll have a shot. No guarantees, but a shot nonetheless.
