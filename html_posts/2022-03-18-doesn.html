**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The Tetragrammaton is the literal 4 letter word for the name of God from the Torah. Transliterated, it comes out to be YHWH, which is most closely pronounced "Yahweh," even though the Hebrew letters are unpronounceable. Some sects transliterate it to YHVH, which would be pronounced "Yehovah," or Americanized as "Jehovah." \ Oirginal question: Doesn\'t the name Yahweh sound like ancient Egyptian stuff as opposed to the God of Gods?
