**Content:** [Fantastic question! There are many great resources you can use to find out everything you want to know, and not the least of these is your local police department. No one wants more than to see you own, carry, and use a firearm safely and legally than the police. So check their website; they probably have links to the pertinent laws in your jurisdiction about ownership, carry, and storage of firearms.\ \ I would suggest taking a firearms course from a nationally-accredited instructor. Whether you like their policies or not, the NRA has a list of over 97,000 instructors and range safety officers, more than 5,700 coaches, and more than 1,800 training counselors. [[Check out their website](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0f5e7e&url=http://training.nra.org/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nra.org"}]{.qlink_container} for more information.\ \ EDIT: This is a better link for where [[NRA Instructors](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7ea809&url=http://www.nrainstructors.org/searchcourse.aspx){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nrainstructors.org"}]{.qlink_container} can be found near you.]{.rendered_qtext}
