**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I have personal experience with this issue. Not me personally, but *my pastor* is independently wealthy (from his former life, and he married well also). So we talk about personal wealth and things like what we wear (and how we accessorize) quite a bit, because it's a very outwardly visible thing in my church. The problem is not the fact that you wear expensive clothes. The problem is *why* you wear expensive clothes. Peter talks about "outward adornment" in parallel to what Paul says to Timothy. > Your beauty should not come from outward adornment, such as elaborate > hairstyles and the wearing of gold jewelry or fine clothes. Rather, it > should be that of your inner self, the unfading beauty of a gentle and > quiet spirit, which is of great worth in God's sight. --- 1 Peter > 3:3--4 NIV As you can see, "outward adornment" is less important than the "inner self." Christians should focus less on outward adornment and more on the development of the "inner self." The Bible also talks about doing everything we do (including how we dress) for the glory of God: > So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the > glory of God. --- 1 Cor 10:31 NIV So the motivation of our heart is well known to God. If you're wearing expensive clothes to "stand out" or to "fit in," your motivation may fall short of God's plan for us. The clothes themselves may not be sinful, but our motivation for wearing them may be. Finally, we are also God's ambassadors: > We are therefore Christ's ambassadors, as though God were making his > appeal through us. --- 2 Cor 5:20 NIV What message does our clothing send? Does our clothing *enhance* God's message, or does it *detract* from the delivery of that message? Since what clothing exactly we should be wearing is never really discussed, it's sort of a "gray area" where you should listen to the Spirit and be sensitive to those around you. If you're standing out, you might consider "dressing down" a bit.
