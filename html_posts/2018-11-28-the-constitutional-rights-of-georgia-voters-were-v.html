**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **The constitutional rights of Georgia voters were violated this month by a system overseen by the man who won the governors' race, a lawsuit backed by Stacey Abrams has claimed. Why?** The lawsuit claims that some voter practices in Georgia not only violate the right to vote for the citizens of Georgia, but also that they are discriminatory because they unequally impact African-Americans. Her lawsuit claims that it is unconstitutional for a state to purge its voter rolls of those who have registered to vote but have not voted for "several years." This is a matter of Georgia law. Her lawsuit also claims that the "signature check" that the state does to compare the voter's signature to the signature on the voter roll is also unconstitutional. The signature check is also a matter of Georgia state law. Finally, her lawsuit claims that because Georgia does not have adequate voting machines, voters had to wait in "long lines" which I guess is also unconstitutional. Who knows, maybe they will find somewhere in the "emanations and penumbras" of the Constitution that purging voter rolls of those that don't vote is unconstitutional somehow, while I'm not sure what particular Constitutional provision you would be violating by properly and adequately maintaining your voter rolls. Personally, this sounds a bit like sour grapes to me. Also, it's interesting that when a Democrat claims that an election was mishandled (such as in Georgia, no matter how unfounded the complaint may be), they are lauded as a "class act" and a patriot, but when a Republican claims that an election was mishandled (such as in neighboring Florida, using documented, court-confirmed irregularities in two counties), then somehow they're "undermining democracy." Hrmph.
