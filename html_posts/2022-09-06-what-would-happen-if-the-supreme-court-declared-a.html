**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} People would be testing their drinking water for foreign substances. A Constitutional amendment is, *de facto*, Constitutional. A Constitutional amendment cannot be unconstitutional. A Constitutional amendment can be *repealed* by another Constitutional amendment, but that doesn't make the original amendment unconstitutional. It only makes it repealed. It still exists, but it has no force. \ Original question: What would happen if the Supreme Court declared a constitutional amendment unconstitutional?
