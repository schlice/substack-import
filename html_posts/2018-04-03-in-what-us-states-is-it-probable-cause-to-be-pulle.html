**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In what US states is it probable cause to be pulled over for bearing a Colorado license plate?** None. Simply having an out-of-state registration does not constitute reasonable suspicion for a traffic stop. Remember, officers need reasonable suspicion for a stop, and probable cause for an *arrest*. Now, if the officer patrols the same area routinely, and sees the same vehicle with the same out-of-state registration for three months, this would be reasonable suspicion to stop the vehicle. New state residents are required to register their vehicles generally within 60 days of moving in to the state, so seeing the same out-of-state registration for three months would cause suspicion. Just as one example of an exception to the registration requirement: military members stationed at a military facility are not automatically state residents unless they choose to change their state of permanent residency. This means they can retain their resident status in their home state, even while stationed in another state. They can maintain their home state driver's license, and their home state registration. They can pay taxes in their home state instead of the state where they are stationed.
