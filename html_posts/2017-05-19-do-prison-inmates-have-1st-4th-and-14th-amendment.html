--- title: "Do prison inmates have 1st, 4th and 14th Amendment rights?" date: "2017-05-19" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Let's go through these one at a time, and understand that these are different in every state: - 1st Amendment: While inmates retain this right, it is most definitely restricted. Prisons generally restrict certain religious practices, they don't restrict religious belief. For example, some things may be restricted due to safety in the prison population, such as wearing certain religious symbols, dietary restrictions, and other practices which might cause a safety concern in a prison. Prisoners' speech rights are most often called into question when it comes to sending or receiving mail, and who and when prisoners can communicate or meet with. In general, if the prison can show a safety concern served by the restriction, the prison gets deference. - The 4th Amendment is the one which is probably the most restricted, both in prison and on parole. Prisoners can most definitely be searched, physically as well as in their cells, at any time of the day or night, or in any way which the prison or PO feels is necessary to promote safety or to ensure the parolee remains in compliance with the details of their sentence. This is an overriding concern in prisons, and searching prisoners for contraband is part of that safety protocol. - However, the courts have found that prisoners do have 14th Amendment rights, most notably the Equal Protection clause. They are protected against unequal treatment based on race, color, creed, sex, or national origin.
