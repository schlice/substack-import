--- title: "When should people with multiple sclerosis take Tysabri?" date: "2021-06-27" tags: ["health-autoimmune"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Tysabri has always been a great "second-line" MS drug, meaning you should only take it after you have tried other MS therapies and they have not been successful. It's a great drug: its efficacy is unmatched in the realm of MS drugs, which makes it an obvious choice if you can tolerate it. But Tysabri has always had one really negative thing that caused it to get relegated to second string status: the risk of [[progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML)](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9f0d6d&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Progressive_multifocal_leukoencephalopathy){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. However, research by the National Institute of Health may cause neurologists to re-think that choice. NIH recommends that Tysabri be considered for "first-line" use for some patients: - Patients who are "JCV negative" (who test negative for [[the JC Virus](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4ede7e&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_polyomavirus_2){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}, which causes PML) - Patients who are "JCV low-positive" who have not taken any previous immunosuppressant, and - Patients who are "JCV high-positive" who have an aggressive disease course, so long as its use is limited to 12--24 months.[\[1\]](#RfneB){#cite-RfneB .citation_link} The risk of PML is greatest in patients who are both JC positive and who have previously been treated by an immunosuppressant. The risk of PML is lowest in patients who are JC negative. So depending where you are on the immunosuppressant scale and where you are on the JCV scale, Tysabri may be a good option. It's a good time to ask your neurologist. \ Original question: When should people with multiple sclerosis take Tysabri?
