**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it possible to reverse the \"Trump\" tax debacle when the Democrats come into power in 2018 and 2020?** The pleading in your question which states "when" the Democrats come into power in 2018 and 2020 did not go unnoticed. So let's assume what you *really* meant to ask was "Hypothetically, if the Democrats retake both Houses of Congress in 2018, and the Presidency in 2020, can these tax cuts be reversed?" Since you're asking about the tax cuts themselves and not whether or not Democrats have a chance at re-taking Congress or the White House, we will focus exclusively on that. If you're curious, there have already been numerous askers who have asked about whether or not the Democrats can re-take Congress and the White House in 2018 and 2020 respectively. The answer to your (revised) question is that while it is physically possible to pass another bill that restores taxes to the way they were in 2017, **history tells us that when Republicans pass tax cuts, even if Democrats rail against them, they rarely repeal them.** The last time Republicans got a tax cut passed was in 2003, and Democrats complained about it for years. But when the cuts were set to expire in 2010, Obama, even though he claimed that those tax cuts hurt the deficit and that it was a huge problem, cut a deal to extend them for two years. Then in 2012, he cut another deal which made most of the tax cuts permanent and only raised taxes on those who made more than \$400,000. So while Democrats *en bloc* voted against the tax bill, and will likely try to use it as political fodder for the foreseeable future, it's unlikely that they will repeal it.
