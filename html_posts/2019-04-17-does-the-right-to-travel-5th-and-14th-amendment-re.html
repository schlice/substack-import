**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the right to travel 5th and 14 amendment really mean you dont need a license?** No. Not even close. The federal government is extremely limited in its authority, by Constitutional design, to essential functions in making the states work together. But the state government is the main source of governmental authority. The states make the laws that govern your everyday life. To that end, while freedom of movement is most definitely a Constitutional right derived from the 5th and 14th Amendments, as well as the "privileges and immunities clause," the courts have held consistently that the federal government does not ***protect*** freedom of movement. That duty falls squarely on the state, under that same privileges and immunities clause of the Constitution. The courts have consistently held that the state is responsible for protecting freedom of movement. In the same way, subsequent court rulings have found that states cannot restrict freedom of movement. To that end, if the state has the Constitutional responsibility to protect freedom of movement, they also have the concomitant responsibility to ensure that drivers are duly trained and licensed to operate motor vehicles in order to effect their Constitutional responsibility. States are free to license drivers in the way in which they see fit, and licenses are recognized by other states when those licenses meet certain minimum standards for driving ability. Since all states have met those minimum standards, all states recognize driving privileges from other states. Since the state is the primary protector of freedom of movement, it falls to the state to ensure the safety of that movement, and the Constitutional provision of freedom of movement does not override the state's authority in licensing drivers.
