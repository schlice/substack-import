**Content:** [It\'s very unlikely that armed civil war between the populace and the military (which is completely illegal) or police would ever take place. The [[United States Armed Forces](https://www.quora.com/topic/United-States-Armed-Forces)]{.qlink_container} are forbidden to act against US citizens by the [[Posse Comitatus Act of 1878](https://www.quora.com/topic/Posse-Comitatus-Act-of-1878)]{.qlink_container} and the Insurrection Act. According to those laws, there would have to be a civilian uprising that a fully-constituted state militia could not control before the US armed forces could be engaged legally, not the other way around.\ \ And if such an event were to occur (the government ordering the US military to fire on its own citizens) you\'ll find a lot of military personnel believing that to be an illegal order and refusing to follow it. Those two laws, which have been around since the beginning of the nation, would need to be repealed in order to legally engage the military against the civilian populace.\ \ The police feel the same way. The police are there to protect and serve, not to enslave and oppress. You\'d find very few officers actually following such an order, and most likely, the whole lot would turn on the government and force an armed revolution.\ \ Similarly, I don\'t think that people believe that their semi-automatic rifles stand any chance against fully-automatic weapons and mass-casualty weapons (rocket launchers, grenades, anti-personnel claymores, etc). People understand that an armed populace will fight for their rights. One anonymous poster said that [[tyranny](https://www.quora.com/topic/Tyranny)]{.qlink_container} is when a group lacking legitimacy uses force to impose its will. What about a group that loses its legitimacy? What if our President were to unilaterally order US troops to engage any US citizen with a gun? This would be highly illegal, and the President would therefore lose his legitimacy, because he would be acting outside of the law.]{.rendered_qtext}
