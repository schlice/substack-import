**Content:** [More social services are great, but there are limits to government benevolence.\ \ For example, the government could do so much more in the United States to help the mentally ill. We are ignoring a problem which, I think, goes much deeper than most people understand. There are so many different levels of mental health issues; most people are totally functional and have some level of mental health issue. If there wasn\'t such a stigma surrounding mental health, we\'d make far more progress. The government could do so much more to furthering that discussion, and adding more social programs would be something that would benefit many, many people.\ \ But on the flip side, we throw so much money at education, and we\'re not seeing the ROI from that money. We will approve any tax increase on the ballot as long as the word \"education\" is tied to it. We have steadily increased spending on education over the last 20 years, yet student achievement has been essentially flat. Throwing more money at education doesn\'t help.\ \ Finally, I\'d mention that our National Parks Service says don\'t feed the animals because doing so \"will make them dependent\" on those handouts and they will never learn to feed themselves.
