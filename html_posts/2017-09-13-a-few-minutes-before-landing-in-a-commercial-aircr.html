**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} [[User-12189549108945173418](https://www.quora.com/profile/User-12189549108945173418)]{.qlink_container} is correct in that cabin pressure begins to normalize during that time. Also note that the air packs run off of the engines. When the aircraft is coming in for landing, the engines are running at their slowest, until just before touchdown, when they are literally running at idle speed. The decrease in available engine bleed air which runs the pneumatic systems which drives the air handlers causes the entire system to slow down. You'll notice they ramp back up right at the time the thrust reversers are engaged and the engines spool back up, right after landing.
