--- title: '"Both Testaments of the Bible demands not to marry a unbeliever, who is called Belial (2 Cor 6:15). Can a Bible-believer say that s/he has not drifted away from the faith when s/he intentionally selects a unbeliever over believers as the spouse?"' date: '2023-02-07' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Be sure to not take this passage out of its context: > Do not be unequally yoked together with unbelievers. For what > fellowship has righteousness with lawlessness? And what communion has > light with darkness? And what accord has Christ with Belial? Or what > part has a believer with an unbeliever? And what agreement has the > temple of God with idols? For you are the temple of the living God. > --- 2 Cor 6:12--16 NKJV First, the unbeliever is not called Belial in the passage. The unbeliever is *equated* with Belial, another name for Satan (interestingly only being used here in this passage). The reason that the word Belial is used here is because, while it is supposed to ultimately represent Satan, what it actually represents is "worthlessness," "vileness," and "wickedness.\" Its use here is to stress Satan's deep-seated wickedness: \"the one who is utterly worthless because he is vile.\" It is the opposite of the person of Christ, and therefore should be thought of as the adversary. But, in context Belial represents all unbelievers, just as all believers are in Christ. The whole point of this passage is the *comparison of opposites,* and the imagery is that of oxen on a yoke. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-d4f13fcedd30b08f07c53e1f2e111552){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-d4f13fcedd30b08f07c53e1f2e111552" master_w="470" master_h="275"} ::: :::: In order for the team to make progress, they have to pull in the same direction. Imagine what would happen if these two were *pulling in opposite directions*. They wouldn't get very far, would they. The same goes for a Christian being "yoked" to a non-Christian. How could a Christ-follower be united in any meaningful way to a non-Christ follower? Unbelievers are still under the authority of "the god of this age" who has blinded their minds "so that they cannot see the light of the gospel that displays the glory of Christ, who is the image of God." (2 Cor 4:4 NIV). When you are "yoked" in marriage, how is it possible that a believer and an unbeliever could ever "pull in the same direction?" One of them would have to change course. Not only that, but the believer and the unbeliever have completely different eternal destinies. How could you ever walk the same direction in your marriage? You'll always be walking on different paths. Perhaps you are banking on the fact that one of you will alter course later in life. I won't say that this is not possible, because it does happen. But Paul warns against this, because as I mentioned, one of you is going to have to change direction, and Paul warns that it may be the believer. Does the passage *demand* that you *not* marry an unbeliever? No, it does not. Instead, it *warns* the believer not to marry an unbeliever because of the potential eternal consequences of doing so.
