**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you think the new cars should come with inbuilt dashcams as a standard? Especially since it's become inexpensive now?** I like the idea, and I think it would be smart for auto makers to install new cameras to do this. *But they already have 360 degree cameras on these cars! Just set those to always record!* Those cameras are not designed for continuous recording: - They are relatively low-res. You want something a lot higher resolution to make out things like license plates. Most of those cameras are installed for specific applications like lane departure warnings, parking space viewers, or backup cameras. - They are not designed for continuous use. The optical sensors are not designed to such tolerances that they can be continuously operated for the entire time the vehicle is running. They work fine for brief periods of operation, but not all the time. However, I do think that manufacturers should think about installing front and rear cameras in vehicles which are designed for continuous recording. An SD card slot in the dashboard somewhere would be all that is required to enable the always-on recording.
