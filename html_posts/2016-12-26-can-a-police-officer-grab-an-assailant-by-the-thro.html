**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As [[Ralph](https://www.quora.com/profile/Ralph-1517)]{.qlink_container} mentioned, **anyone** who is faced with an attack which could cause grave bodily harm can use **any force**, up to and including deadly force, to defend themselves from that attack. This includes law enforcement officers and civilians alike. While the person will still face legal scrutiny, the doctrine of self-defense indemnifies the victim against charges. So to answer your specific question, yes, a police officer can grab an assailant by the throat, or any other part of the assailant's body, if he is attacked, in order to defend himself.
