Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The question was "Does the US Second Ammendment \[sic\] permit personal ownership of WMDs?" Technically, the answer to that is yes, it does. Remember that letters of marque and reprisal were issued to ***privateers*** who owned their own *battleships*. I think it would be reasonable to presume that a personally-owned warship (the mightiest thing on the ocean at the time) would be equivalent to a WMD. Practically, we realize that for every *right*, there are commensurate *responsibilities*. Therefore, some of our rights have *restrictions* or *limits* on them, because as a practical matter, we are not our own islands. We must live together in communities and cities, and laws don't protect me from me; they protect me from you. Specifically, the National Firearms Act has long regulated **destructive devices** which covers nuclear, chemical and biological weapons. It also covers area-effect weapons like grenades or anti-personnel weapons like landmines, tripmines or Bouncing Betties.
