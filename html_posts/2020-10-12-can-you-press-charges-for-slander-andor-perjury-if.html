--- title: "Can you press charges for slander and/or perjury if a woman falsely accuses you of rape?" date: "2020-10-12" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You can file a lawsuit for slander, not press charges. A slander lawsuit alleges that you were damaged by a false statement. It seeks *monetary* damages for harm caused by the statement, such as pain and suffering, damage to reputation, lost wages or a loss of ability to earn a living, and personal emotional reactions such as shame, humiliation, and anxiety. The key, however, is **false statements**. Statements are slanderous only if they are first **false**. The absolute defense to slander is truth. Perjury is lying under oath, but it's highly unlikely you'll see charges for perjury in rape cases, even if the accusation is false. Perjury charges are rare in and of themselves, but even so, courts are unlikely to charge potential rape victims, even if they are actual liars, because of the chilling effect that charging a victim may have. The courts do not want to discourage anyone from reporting, and potential charges if you are accused of lying about the incident may have a chilling effect on rape reporting. \ Original question: ***Can you press charges for slander and/or perjury if a woman falsely accuses you of rape?***
