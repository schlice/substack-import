--- title: '"Civil Liberties: Are American gay people looking for new rights or are they asking the law to acknowledge rights they already have?"' date: '2013-08-27' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [As a matter of technicality, they are asking for new rights.\ \ Previously, discrimination laws were based on physical, immutable characteristics, with the exception of religion which is already protected under the Constitution. For example, discrimination can be based on race, color, creed, sex, or national origin. These things are physical and immutable (sex change operations aside). Religious discrimination is covered under the first amendment.\ \ Gays are asking for a new right. They want to add sexual orientation to the list of items that can be held as discriminatory if used as a means to discriminate. We are moving into uncharted territory; we are now going to use people\'s thoughts and feelings as something which can be discriminatory if violated.]{.rendered_qtext}
