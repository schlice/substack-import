--- title: "What should you do if you are concerned that the police car trying to stop you is not really a police car?" date: "2017-12-22" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you are being stopped by a **marked** car, you should have much less cause for concern than if you are being stopped by an **unmarked** car. Marked cars display the name of the agency and usually have light bars on the top of the car. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-12cf07809b05ee89ef1243251b0b7362){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-12cf07809b05ee89ef1243251b0b7362" master_w="920" master_h="540"} ::: :::: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-624d14b273736c7b1720bdd1b8d11c0c){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-624d14b273736c7b1720bdd1b8d11c0c" master_w="620" master_h="413"} ::: :::: Unmarked cars are much more easily created by someone attempting to impersonate a police officer. They are sometimes, but not always, simply unpainted police vehicles, with lights mounted inside windows and under grills: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-76ca9ff6e50c62c2d315e4c755e483ce){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-76ca9ff6e50c62c2d315e4c755e483ce" master_w="602" master_h="361"} ::: :::: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-9ed71ade08c621c974274d1d80666b2d){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-9ed71ade08c621c974274d1d80666b2d" master_w="614" master_h="345"} ::: :::: In either case, you should call 911 if you don't recognize the vehicle attempting to stop you. If you are being stopped by a marked vehicle, call 911 while you stop. If you are being stopped by an unmarked vehicle, while you are calling 911, drive slowly along the side of the road with your flashers on. Note that in most jurisdictions, there is no lawful reason for you to avoid stopping when an officer attempts to stop you, so if you failed to stop for a real officer, you could potentially face consequences for that failure, but the idea is that you feel unsafe, and so you simply throw yourself at the mercy of the officer, and ultimately, the courts if you do get a ticket for failing to stop. Any time you fail to stop, for whatever reason, you must bear this in mind.
