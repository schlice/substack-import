--- title: '"How can a US state legalise marijuana if it is illegal federally? Could a state technically legalise murder?"' date: '2022-11-10' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You're absolutely right, a state cannot "make legal" something which is illegal at the federal level. But what a state *can* do is to eliminate any of the penalties for that federal crime in their state. This is what Colorado did. They eliminated the penalties for possession of marijuana for personal use. There are still penalties for distribution without a license and other drug crimes, but possession of marijuana in amounts considered to be for personal use is penalty-free. The upshot of what happens is that, unless the feds actually want to go into Colorado and actually start cracking heads (which they have done for heavy-duty cartel traffickers, someday ask me how I know), the state isn't going to prosecute the crime. And the state simply never bothers to prosecute the federal crime because penalties are proscribed in the state constitution.
