**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you think kids can return to school in 2 months without the fear of a shooting accident?** Of course they can! There are *50 million students* in schools. The number of students who died in school shootings in the 2017--2018 school year was around 50 (that's September, 2017 through June, 2018). This means any one individual student, even in an aberrant school year like 2017--2018, still has a **one in one million** chance of dying in a school shooting, and that's the high risk. The risk in a normal (non-aberrant) school year is even lower than that. I think those are pretty safe odds. And with school security becoming more of an issue in places where it is not (and some high profile shootings detailing both what good and bad security can do), schools will only become safer over the next few school years as security is given a much more serious look in schools that don't yet have it. It is up to parents to review your childrens' school security plans, and if you find it lacking, maybe you should either consider moving, changing schools, or homeschooling until the district can get its act together. Don't leave your children's safety in the hands of those who do not believe it precious enough to protect it!
