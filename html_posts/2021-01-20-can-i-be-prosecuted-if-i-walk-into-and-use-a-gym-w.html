**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can I be prosecuted if I walk into (and use) a gym where I'm not a member, if the staff don't notice what I'm doing?** You qualified your crime with "if the staff don't notice what I'm doing." So you're trying to say *it's only a crime if you get caught,* right? Well technically, that's not true. It's a crime, whether or not you get caught. The crimes include trespassing and theft of service. Trespassing would be for being present on private property without permission, and theft of service would be using the gym for which you did not pay the appropriate fee to use. You did ask if you can be prosecuted if the staff didn't notice what you were doing. So you're trying to claim that if you do get caught, you can't be prosecuted because the staff didn't detect your presence or your theft. However, the definition of the crime does not include detection by the staff. In other words, it's still a crime, even if the staff doesn't see you. If evidence is produced that you did what you were accused of doing, you can still be prosecuted, even if the staff didn't notice what you were doing.
