**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **The Senate has voted 49 51 against calling for additional witnesses in President Trump\'s impeachment hearing. Is this good for the Republicans?** Republicans, by and large, believe that this impeachment has gone on long enough. Most Republicans, even if they individually believe that the President's actions with respect to Ukraine may have contained a personal motive, they also believe that the actions had a national interest motive as well. Finally, most Republicans believe that none of that rises to the level of an impeachable offense. So is the vote against additional witnesses good for Republicans? I can't say what is "good" for anyone. I can say that wrapping up this impeachment trial based on the strength of the case presented by the House is Constitutionally appropriate. There is no need for additional witnesses, since the House voted to pass these articles of impeachment with the evidence contained in them. The Senate's job is to judge the articles as they are written, not to re-investigate everything the House already investigated. This vote signals that the Senate wishes to do just that.
