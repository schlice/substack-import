**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How will Trump likely respond to the national emergency veto?** I don\'t understand what a national emergency veto is. They\'re isn\'t actually any such thing. The process works like this: 1. The President declares a national emergency. 2. If Congress chooses to end the national emergency, they must issue a **joint resolution** to do so. That means that both the House and the Senate must pass the same resolution. 3. The resolution goes to the President\'s desk for signature. The President can veto the resolution. If a joint resolution actually made it to his desk (which is highly unlikely), he would likely veto it. 4. Congress would need a 2/3 majority in both Houses in order to override the veto. So in this case, there isn\'t much that can stop a national emergency.
