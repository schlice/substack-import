**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does not having a HOA create a Tinder box for sparking off the mass devaluation of real estate in a neighborhood, due to homeowners with bad taste, bad etiquette, poor upkeep habits, etc?** A tinderbox? You mean like where one person starts bad habits and then the whole neighborhood catches on? No. Because *most* people have **pride of ownership**. Most people will take pride in the home that they own, and they'll take care of it. But there are always those who don't. They don't care about the upkeep of the exterior of their home, and they don't care that their ugly exterior negatively impacts your property value. And it is abundantly clear that home values are lower in ugly neighborhoods, where there is a choice in neighborhoods. Sure, you can go to Code Enforcement to try to get him back in line, but then it's him vs you. He'll hate you and blame you for every dime in fines that Code Enforcement levies on him. Does it work? Most of the time. *But at what cost?* In an HOA-controlled neighborhood, ***everyone voluntarily joined the HOA.*** *But that's not true! Some people didn't want the HOA!* They may not have wanted it, but they still voluntarily joined it **by purchasing a home in an HOA-controlled neighborhood**. They signed on the dotted line, being of sound mind and fully in control of their faculties. They actively joined the HOA, and in doing so, agreed to follow the collective rules in order to maintain everyone's home values. Then, if they choose to violate the rules, it's the HOA that takes action against them, not the individual homeowner. And the HOA can go so far as to place a lien on their home for failing to pay their fines, and then foreclose on that lien. The HOA can remove the violator from the neighborhood. Can Code Enforcement do that? Let's be clear: HOAs are voluntarily joined, and the HOA can foreclose on your house if you don't participate. Without the HOA, it's you versus your non-caring neighbor, and no amount of visitations by Code Enforcement will make them care more.
