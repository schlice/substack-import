**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **Christians faith is belief without evidence** Says who? Christian faith is not belief *without* evidence. Faith bridges the gap between the *undeniable* and the *unknowable*. For example, we know that the universe was created, because it had a beginning, and if it had a beginning, it had a cause (see the Law of Causality). If you're a non-Christian, you might say that cause is *nothing.* Random chance. But Christians believe that the cause is *something*, and that cause is what we refer to as "God." Faith bridges that gap between what we know (the universe had a cause) and what we can never know with certainty (God created the universe). But just because we can't know it with certainty, is that reason to disbelieve it? Which is more likely: *nothing created something*, or *something created something?* It takes less faith to believe something created something than to believe nothing created something. So while the Christian and the non-Christian both have faith, the non-Christian has to have more faith to believe that nothing created something. \ Faith is not unique to Christians. Everyone has faith. The difference is not that we have faith, it's *where we place our faith.*
