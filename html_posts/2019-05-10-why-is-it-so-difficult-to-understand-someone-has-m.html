**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why is it so difficult to understand someone has MS?** There are a few reasons for this. MS is a very difficult disease to diagnose. We have really good diagnostic tools today, but there is nothing specific in the disease that conclusively says that you have MS. There's no biomarker in a blood test that will positively show you have it. Diagnosing MS is as much an art as it is a science. Also, people with MS live with this awful dichotomy of how good you look on the outside as opposed to how bad you feel on the inside. Unless you're walking with assistance, or in a wheelchair (which is rare in MS), it's virtually impossible to tell that someone has it without them telling you. Or even better is someone who only knows of MS from famous people who have died from its complications, like Richard Pryor, and they say, "You have MS? *But you look so good!*" That one always strikes me, because I'm never quite sure what they think someone with MS is *supposed* to look like.
