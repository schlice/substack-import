**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you think professionals (doctors, police officers, teachers) should be held accountable after being convicted of a crime, including paying restitution to their victims?** Of course they should; that's what the civil court system is for. The criminal courts find people guilty of crimes and send them to jail; the civil courts find people responsible for actions and pay restitution to their victims. The only penalty in a civil court is money to a victim. The penalty in a criminal court is jail time (but also fines), a punishment owed to the state (or the people in general). Whether or not someone is convicted of a crime, they can be sued in civil court and can be forced to pay restitution to their victims (or their victim's estate). The most famous recent example of this is OJ Simpson. He was found not guilty for the murder of his ex-wife and her friend, but he was sued and was found to be responsible for her death and the remainder of his fortune was paid out to the estates of the victims.
