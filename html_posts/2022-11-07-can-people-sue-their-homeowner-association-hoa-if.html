--- title: '"Can people sue their homeowner association (HOA) if they feel they have been charged fines unfairly by them?"' date: '2022-11-07' tags: - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You can sue anyone for anything. The question is, of course, what your odds are of being successful in your suit. And in this case, the odds are pretty slim that you'll be successful. Because unless you can prove that the HOA is fining you for something that they're not fining your neighbor for, and you're both doing the same wrong thing, then your fines are not "unfair." If you broke the HOA's rules, you pay the fines. If it's in your Governing Documents which you signed when you bought your house, you have to play by the same rules. You might *feel* that your fines are unfair. But if the HOA has the power to levy fines, and they have documentation which shows you broke the rules, they can fine you, and you must pay. You should probably fix whatever it is that they're fining you over, or work with them to show that you're making progress to get it fixed. That would be a much better use of your time than telling the Internet that the HOA is unfair.
