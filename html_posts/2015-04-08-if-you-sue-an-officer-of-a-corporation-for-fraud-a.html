**Content:** [Depends on the type of corporation. There are some differences in personal liability between LLCs and S Corporations (thus the term \"Limited Liability Corporation\").\ \ First of all, most corporations have liability insurance for their officers that protects them in the event that they are personally sued while acting on behalf of the corporation.\ \ In most cases, an officer of the corporation can be held personally liable if he or she:]{.rendered_qtext} - personally and directly injures someone - personally guarantees a bank loan or a business debt on which the corporation defaults - fails to deposit taxes withheld from employees\' wages - intentionally does something fraudulent, illegal, or reckless that causes harm to the company or to someone else, or - treats the company as an extension of his or her personal affairs, rather than as a separate legal entity. \ That last one is important, because if the officer treats the company and his personal affairs the same, a court could find that the company doesn\'t exist separately from his personal affairs and he could be liable for the sum total of any judgments against the corporation.\ \ \[1\] [[http://www.nolo.com/legal\....](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=28fb2a&url=http://www.nolo.com/legal-encyclopedia/llc-basics-30163.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nolo.com"}]{.qlink_container}
