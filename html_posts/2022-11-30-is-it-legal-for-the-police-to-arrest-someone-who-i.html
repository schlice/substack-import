**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes. Want to know the worst part? They don't even have to tell you why you are being arrested. They only have to tell the judge. I mean generally, it makes it a little easier when they tell you why you're being arrested, but there is no law (usually, I can't speak for every state) requiring a police officer to tell you why you are being arrested. They simply must be able to articulate their probable cause to a judge. The police have to have **probable cause** to arrest you, which means that they have a reasonable basis for believing that a crime may have been committed. And if they can articulate that probable cause to the judge, their arrest is valid.
