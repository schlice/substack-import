**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I am sorry but no one is going to be able to answer that question for you. There are many reasons why God may not answer your prayers in the way you want. Perhaps you are not ready for what He has planned for you. You may need to grow more before you are ready for what you're asking for. Perhaps He is not ready (in other words, it's not in His timing yet). Or perhaps it's not part of His good plan for you. We do know that He has never not kept one of His promises, ever. If He has promised something, He has kept that promise. And He has promised that He has good plans for you. > For I know the plans I have for you, declares the LORD, plans for > welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope. (Jeremiah > 29:11 ESV) As Christians, we were never promised an easy life. But God does promise us a ***glorious eternity.*** So maybe instead of praying for God to open a door when clearly He is not, perhaps we should pray for Him to reveal His plans for us, and to bring us alongside Him in those plans. His will **will** be done whether we like it or not, so isn't it better that we just learn His plans for us, and we help in accomplishing those plans?
