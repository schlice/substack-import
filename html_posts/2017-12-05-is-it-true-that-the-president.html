**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it true that the president \"cannot obstruct justice because he is the chief law enforcement officer\"?** No. While it is true that the president is the head of the executive branch under Article II of the Constitution, this does not make the president immune from obstruction of justice. For example, President Nixon obstructed justice when he destroyed evidence, paid hush money, and instructed people to lie. President Clinton attempted to obstruct justice when he advised potential witnesses to lie. I will assume that this question is related to President Trump, and the accusation by [[certain Democrats that he obstructed justice](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a6ee32&url=http://www.slate.com/blogs/the_slatest/2017/12/03/dianne_feinstein_sees_possible_obstruction_of_justice_case_against_trump.html){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="slate.com"}]{.qlink_container} when he fired the director of the FBI. **In this context**, **the President cannot be guilty of obstruction of justice.** The President has the Constitutional authority as the head of the executive branch to run that branch as he sees fit, including making personnel decisions (i.e. hiring and firing people), under Article II of the Constitution. Therefore, making a personnel decision consistent with the Constitution ***cannot*** be a crime. If a law contradicts the Constitution, the *law* is unconstitutional, not the Constitution.
