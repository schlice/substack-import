**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What authority does a condo Board have? I am an owner in a small condo development in MA and the Board 'sold' our extra parking spaces to 2 owners using a Quit Claim. Is the Board allowed to do this?** A quitcliam deed is the primary method by which property is transferred in Massachusetts. Basically, it says that the property being transferred has no other claim on it, and that the person accepting the property places no other claims on it. That the property was transferred by quitclaim deed is normal in Massachusetts. The question is whether or not the Board has the authority to dispose of community property. There are two items which the Board must navigate: state law, and their applicable Governing Documents. Common areas and facilities are undivided (this means individual condo owners do not have an *individual* ownership interest in them) and those areas are managed by the Board. So long as the profits from any sale of common areas are distributed to owners through an addition to the operating fund, then as far as state law is concerned, the sale should be legal. In addition to prevailing state or local laws, a Board of Directors can always only operate in accordance with its Governing Documents. Disbursement of property must only take place in accordance with the rules in that document. In nearly every case, the Board must get approval from the members before they convey property. The Board, in most cases, would have to arrange a vote of the membership before they could go through the state process to transfer the land. But this requirement may vary depending on what is stated in the relevant Governing Documents.
