**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If congresspeople cannot sue the President over the emoluments clause, who can?** Congress cannot *sue* the President if he violates the Constitution. Violating the Constitution (or any of its provisions) would be grounds for *impeachment* and removal from office. In order to impeach the President, Congress must *prove* that he violated the Constitution. As of today, Congress has not done this.
