--- title: "Why do statutes of limitations on criminal offenses exist?" date: "2015-03-16" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Statutes of limitations exist to protect the defendant. They exist for a number of reasons, but primarily the idea is to make sure that defendants are prosecuted using evidence that has not deteriorated with time. Physical evidence as well as peoples\' memories can deteriorate with time. Some common law philosophy behind them include the fact that plaintiffs who have cause to bring charges should do so within a reasonable amount of time, and the longer the gap between the commission of the crime and its prosecution constitutes an unjust burden on the defendant. Also, as more time passes, you have less possibility of providing a fair outcome for a defendant as evidence and memory may have deteriorated.]{.rendered_qtext}
