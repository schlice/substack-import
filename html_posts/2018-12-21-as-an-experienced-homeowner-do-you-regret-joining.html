**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **As an experienced homeowner, do you regret joining a HOA (Homeowners Association)?** First of all, you don't "join" an HOA. You buy a house in a community which is controlled by an HOA. If you buy that house, you have no choice but to join. You can't "opt out" of he HOA. No one likes being told that they did something wrong. And no one enjoys violating the rules. HOAs get a bad reputation because it seems that most of the time, you don't hear from them unless you've done something wrong. But if you know the rules and you live by them, HOAs serve a valuable purpose. They keep home values high. They do this by making sure that your neighbors don't trash their houses, and they charge them money when they do. Everyone buying a house in a covenant controlled community should be fully aware of what they're getting into. You have to sign the covenants when you buy your house, and you get a paper copy of the R&R (Rules and Regulations) and the P&P (Policies and Procedures) for your neighborhood. In Colorado, we separated those documents. In most states, it's one document: the PPR&R (Policies, Procedures, Rules & Regulations). You also get the covenants, which are on file with the city or county.
