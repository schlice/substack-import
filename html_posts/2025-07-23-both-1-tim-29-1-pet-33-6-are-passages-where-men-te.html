--- title: '"Both 1 Tim 2:9 & 1 Pet 3:3-6 are passages where men tell women how to dress. Why do they single women out? Does the Bible anywhere specifically tell men how to dress?"' date: '2025-07-23' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Actually, these verses are specific to women, but the *broader passages* around them are not. These two letters were written for different audiences and different purposes. **1 Tim 2:9.** Paul's first letter to Timothy was to encourage him in leading his church. A large portion of the letter is taken up with Paul\'s comments on the role of women in worship services. Rather than focusing on flashy clothes and dramatic behavior, women are to be modest and godly. In addition, women should not take on specific spiritual leadership roles reserved for men. Specifically, this passage (more specifically, 1 Tim 2:9--15, is one of the most controversial passages in the New Testament. And while there are some passages which are slightly unclear, the overall theme of the passage is not: women are not to focus on dramatic appearance or behavior, but to put their efforts into godliness and good works. Verse 9 begins with the word *hōsautōs,* which means "likewise." That's a follow-on from verse 8, which applies to men. Paul was talking to the men about prayer and its importance. So when he says "likewise" to the women, prayer and its importance applies to them as well. What he does in verse 9 is to add a *specific* item which caused concern to the community regarding women. Specifically, he addressed how women dress and style their hair for church. Just as it is today, church is not the appropriate venue to wear skimpy attire or to spend an inordinate amount of time and effort on "braiding their hair," which could be costly both in time and money in those days. The idea wasn't to control women. The idea was to suggest that all of that effort put emphasis on the self rather than on worshipping God, which was the whole purpose of church. You don't "dress to impress" for church. Not only does it distract others from the point of gathering together, it distracts the woman herself. **1 Peter 3:3--9.** Peter, in this letter, is teaching about Christian submission to human authorities. He was talking about a Christian wife trying to influence an unbelieving husband. Women of the time felt enormous societal pressure to *compete* for the attention of their husbands, and they would often use jewelry, clothing and hairstyles to attempt to do so. Peter\'s instruction from God urged women to understand, in part, that their husbands would not be won to Christ by their efforts to beautify themselves externally. It's not that ***we*** put emphasis on how women dress. It's that at that time, ***they*** put emphasis on it, and sometimes it interfered with what they were really trying to accomplish.
