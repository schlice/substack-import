--- title: "What happens after Air Force Academy Graduation?" date: "2016-06-03" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Lots of hugs, some tears, lots of pictures, probably lots of adult beverages, etc. Once the ceremony is done, the newly-minted Second Lieutenants usually have a little bit of leave left over (around a week or so). Following that, they ship out to their first duty assignment, which is normally some form of tech school to start their new jobs. If they are pilots, there is usually a little more downtime before UPT begins, but that's generally how it goes. They already have their personal effects boxed up and ready to leave, and Transportation will ship them to their next duty station. Transportation will also coordinate their transport to their next duty station. If available, they will fly on military transport directly to their next duty station. If not, tickets will be provided for civilian air transport. There's been a famous meme that has been running around the Academy for decades that the best view of the Academy is the one in your rear-view mirror. It's certainly an amazing feeling to see that, and consciously or subconsciously, everyone takes an extra-long look in their rear-view mirror after they have graduated and are literally leaving the base for the last time. But it doesn't last.
