**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If using Tails OS and maintaining proper Opsec, what would be the chances of being de-anonymized by the FBI and NSA?** TAILS (The Amnesic Incognito Live System) has as its primary purpose leaving no trace on the host computer. It also can communicate over TOR networks using a TOR Browser. You don't need TAILS to be anonymous online. Using the TOR Browser and being careful with what you access is all you need for anonymous browsing. Using the [[TOR Browser](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fc2a38&url=https://www.torproject.org/download/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="torproject.org"}]{.qlink_container} is simple enough. Install and go. Watching where you log in to is more critical. For example, if you use TOR to log in to your Gmail account, then they at least know you logged in to your Gmail account. They may not know from where, but they do know you logged in. So the OPSEC you discuss in your question is critical. In other words, you're more likely to be de-anonymized by the site you visit than you are by having your TOR circuit back-traced. At best, they can tell you connected to a TOR entry node, but even that packet is encrypted, so what you said and where you went from there is still a mystery. There was talk for a while about the NSA having sleeper nodes in the TOR network, but even a sleeper node would still need to decrypt the entire packet, and you'd only be able to do that if you had the private keys of all three nodes in the circuit. Since you randomly generate a circuit (and can do so again at any time), having all three private keys is highly unlikely. As I said at the beginning, the primary purpose of TAILS is to be able to use any computer and leave no trace. Indeed, you can do this with nearly any version of Linux which has a Live installer, such as Ubuntu. What sets TAILS apart is that it has security inbuilt, so it boots into a live session, AND uses a TOR circuit by default, AND communicates over a TOR Browser. It also cleans memory and ensures that nothing is ever communicate to the local hard drive at all. And unless you specifically enable it, nothing is written to the USB stick either. So to answer the question: **If using Tails OS and maintaining proper Opsec, what would be the chances of being de-anonymized by the FBI and NSA?** If you did each one of those things perfectly, *virtually zero*. I'm in Tech Support and I know better than to assure you that it will never happen (remember, nothing is foolproof to the talented fool), but it is so unlikely that it is *effectively* zero.
