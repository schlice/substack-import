**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are a few ways. 1. Depending on your model of Vizio TV, you may have a direct audio output. It may be a digital audio out (which you can run into an amplifier), or even a headphone jack (which you can run into the Aux input on your stereo --- just be very careful of the output volume on the TV. Adjust them both carefully). But either way, most Vizio TVs, even if they don't directly have an Audio Out port of some kind at least usually have a headphone out jack. 2. If there is no audio out at all, you can grab the audio *on the way to* the TV. Since it's most likely that your audio is being sent to your TV over HDMI, use something like this: [[Amazon.com: Musou HDMI Audio Extractor,HDMI to HDMI + Optical Toslink(SPDIF) + RCA(L/R) Stereo Analog Outputs Video Audio Splitter](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a66b4f&url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B06XDNJ6RM){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"} ]{.qlink_container}This device will extract the audio from the HDMI signal and send it to either S-PDIF (which is optical audio out) or to L/R RCA jacks, to be sent to an amplifier. Hope this helps.
