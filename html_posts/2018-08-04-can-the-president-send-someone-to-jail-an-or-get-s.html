**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the president send someone to jail an or get someone out because they are president?** The president cannot "send anyone to jail." The president does have the power of the pardon, as it is an enumerated power in the Constitution under Article II Section 2: > *\[The President\] shall have power to grant reprieves and pardons for > offenses against the United States, except in cases of > impeachment\[.\]* > > The U.S. Supreme Court has interpreted this language to include the > power to grant pardons, conditional pardons, commutations of sentence, > conditional commutations of sentence, remissions of fines and > forfeitures, respites, and amnesties.[\[1\]](#MaAwB){#cite-MaAwB > .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-MaAwB){.citation_index} [[Pardon - Wikipedia](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6a3580&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pardon%23United_States){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}
