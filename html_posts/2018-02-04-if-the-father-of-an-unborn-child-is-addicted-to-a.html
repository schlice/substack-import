**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There's a lot of great information here from some really wonderful people, but none of it really answered the question. There are two components to addiction. One of them is a predisposition to addictive behavior, and the other is exposure to addictive activities. It is currently unknown how addictive behavior actually, physically works in the brain. We know the mechanism in the brain which malfunctions during addiction, but we don't know *why*. Because we don't know why that area of the brain malfunctions, we have no idea if that malfunction is genetic or environmental or hereditary. We know what's broken, but we don't know why it's broken. I know personally several parents who used drugs and then just quit, cold turkey, without any (known) side effects, who went on to have kids who became full-blown addicts, and they **wrestle** with the fact that it was *so easy* for them to simply quit, but a) their kids use, and b) their kids can't stop. So we know that the broken bit in the brain which leads to addiction is not necessarily, 100%, hereditary. As for exposure to addictive behaviors, it may be that the old PSA from the 80's is still true: *Parents who use drugs will have children who use drugs.* Do you remember that PSA (assuming you are old enough to remember it)? Well for those of you who missed it (or who were born after it quit airing), here it is: [[https://youtu.be/n5XakEKSIaM](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ea1c41&url=https://youtu.be/n5XakEKSIaM){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"}]{.qlink_container} I seriously can't tell you whether or not this is true, but I do know that if you combine both the addictive personality (the broken bit in the brain that leads to addiction) and exposure to addictive behaviors (may not necessarily be drugs or alcohol, could be gambling, or Internet porn, or any number of addictive behaviors) there is a much higher likelihood that someone will become an addict.
