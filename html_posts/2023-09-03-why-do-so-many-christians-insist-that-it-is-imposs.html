**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember, there is a difference between someone who calls themselves Christian, who goes to church every week, reads the bible, helps the poor, *but is not saved*, and someone who calls themselves Christian *because they are saved.* When I say that someone is "Christian," it is because they are *saved*. When someone becomes Christian (i.e. they accept Christ as their Savior and commit their lives to Him), it is because God has already called them to belief, and He has changed their heart from their old "hearts of stone" and has given them hearts which can only love Him. > I will sprinkle clean water on you, and you will be clean; I will > cleanse you from all your impurities and from all your idols. I will > give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you; I will remove from > you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh. And I will put > my Spirit in you and move you to follow my decrees and be careful to > keep my laws. Then you will live in the land I gave your ancestors; > you will be my people, and I will be your God. --- Ezekiel 36:25--28 > NIV This idea is echoed by Jesus: > "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me. I give > them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch > them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater > than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand. > I and the Father are one." (John 10:27-30) This process is what we called "being born again," or the more correct terminology is ***regeneration.*** This new "heart of flesh" we receive is only capable of loving God. Notice that it says that God will put His Spirit in us which will move us to "follow His decrees and keep His laws." **If God has done this, then you will love Him.** It's also another principle: "Once saved, always saved." These passages, among others, indicate that once a person is saved, they are always saved. They can never "lose" their salvation. Therefore, logically speaking, if you "stop" loving Him, you did not have His Spirit in you, because His Spirit is incapable of not loving Him, and therefore you too would be incapable of not loving Him. So for someone to say they are an "ex-Christian," it is perfectly reasonable for Christians to question whether or not that person was ever truly saved in the first place.
