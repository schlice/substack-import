**Content:** [*I was under the impression that a lie was a bad thing to do no matter who does it*\ \ Sadly, the law doesn\'t share your view. Lying, in and of itself, isn\'t illegal. While it can be if you\'re lying about your own identity, in most states, lying to the police is not illegal. It\'s not in your best interest, but it\'s not illegal in and of itself (unless you are deliberately lying to mislead the police and hamper an investigation, which can be a crime). Similarly, the police lying to you is also not illegal. Sometimes, the police have to use deceptive interrogation techniques to get suspects to talk, and as you probably already know, criminals usually aren\'t the sharpest knives in the drawer. A little bit of deception can lead a criminal to divulge information he normally wouldn\'t when directly asked.\ \ Check Chris\' answer again. He didn\'t actually lie, he said \"Do you think your partner is going to protect you?\" You could expand on that: \"When faced with \, do you really think he\'s going to hold to the \'Bro Code\' and not roll on you? I\'ll bet you he\'s doing it right now! You need to do the smart thing and get ahead of this before it gets really bad for you.\" The officer doesn\'t know that things are going to get really bad for the suspect. But the small bit of deception can convince a suspect that it\'s in his best interest to talk, and talk now.]{.rendered_qtext}
