--- title: '"Do babies who die face the same spiritual test that the angels went through, because they could not make a choice whether or not to follow Christ?"' date: '2023-12-15' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Babies, as well as those who cannot comprehend right and wrong (such as mentally disabled people), are not held accountable for their sin. Jesus lays down this principle in John 9. In essence, God does not condemn people for things they are unable to do. So babies, and people who cannot comprehend right from wrong, are not held accountable for their original sin. It is only after a person is able to recognize right from wrong (sometimes called the age of accountability) that they are held accountable for unbelief.
