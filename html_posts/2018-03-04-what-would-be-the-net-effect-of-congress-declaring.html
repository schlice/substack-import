**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What would be the net effect of congress declaring all adult citizens to be members of the militia and mandating their firearms and physical fitness training?** Congress already did part of this, in the Militia Act of 1903. > **10 USC ยง 246 - Militia, Composition and Classes**: > > \(a\) The militia of the United States consists of all able-bodied > males at least 17 years of age and, except as provided in [[section > 313 of title > 32](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=47e529&url=https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/313){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="cornell.edu"}]{.qlink_container}, under 45 years of age > who are, or who have made a declaration of intention to become, > citizens of the United States and of female citizens of the United > States who are members of the National Guard. > > \(b\) The classes of the militia are--- > > \(1\) the organized militia, which consists of the National Guard and > the Naval Militia; and > > \(2\) the unorganized militia, which consists of the members of the > militia who are not members of the National Guard or the Naval > Militia. The only thing they have not done is tried to "organize" the "unorganized" militia in a way you are suggesting, such as adding physical fitness and training requirements. That actually makes them part of the "organized" militia, also known as the National Guard. If you're suggesting that we change the law and remove the unorganized militia and make all citizens part of the organized militia, I'd see that there would be as much backlash to that as you'd get if you reinstated the draft.
