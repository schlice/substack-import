--- title: "What exactly was the reason or reasons Roe v. Wade got overturned by the Supreme Court? Despite rape and INCEST happening all the time?" date: "2023-12-15" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The exact reason *Roe* was overturned was because it was built on a faulty premise. *Roe* was the case which established the precedent that there is a Constitutional right to abortion. The Court examined *Roe*'s reasoning and found that the Constitutional right which *Roe* claimed existed really doesn't exist. Therefore, if there is no Constitutional right to abortion, *Roe*'s legal foundations are gone. *Roe* (and *Casey*, which mostly referenced the precedent of *Roe*) was overruled, and the power to control abortion was returned to the states and to the people respectively. As for rape and incest, their presence or absence has absolutely nothing to do with whether or not abortion is a Constitutional right. The only thing that *Dobbs* decided was that abortion is not a Constitutional right. The states can decide whether or not (and if so, when) abortion will be allowed. Some states completely permit abortion with no restrictions. Ohio just recently added a right to abortion into their state constitution. Some states restrict abortion after a certain number of weeks, but allow it in the case of rape or incest, or to save the life of the mother. Some states completely restrict abortion, including in the case of rape or incest. It comes down to the will of the people in those states.
