**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How often should you work out when you have multiple sclerosis?** As MS is different for every individual, so too should your workout be individually tailored to your specific needs and abilities. The general rule of thumb is to not over-exert yourself. You already know that [[MS lassitude](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=828f93&url=https://www.nationalmssociety.org/Symptoms-Diagnosis/MS-Symptoms/Fatigue){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} is a thing, but again, how it affects you is different than how it affects me. It's important that you tailor your workout to ensure you're not causing yourself to get fatigued such that you can't continue your day. Overall, the goal should be building up endurance and muscle mass in advance of a period of time where you may be unable to do these things. MS tries to prevent you from moving, and muscles that don't move will atrophy. Understanding the possibility that this eventuality may come to pass, your workout should strive to increase lean muscle mass as well as strength and endurance.
