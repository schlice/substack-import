**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there any reason why a homeowners association would react negatively to a homeowner with bee hives in their backyard?** There are several reasons why an HOA would react negatively to a homeowner maintaining an apiary (bee hives) in their backyard. In all cases, you must check your Governing Documents to see exactly what issues your HOA may have with apiaries. All Governing Documents are specific, and none are exactly the same. I can't tell you specifically what your HOA may be concerned with, but it may be one or more of the following: 1. The bees may be prohibited under the "pets" category of the HOA Rules and Regulations. In that case, the R&R may specify what pets you are allowed to keep, and what pets are not. In general, where pets are limited, they are limited to "common" household pets (dogs, cats, birds, etc). "Exotic" or otherwise uncommon pets are generally prohibited. 2. Also common in HOA R&Rs: "nuisance" rules. Anything which may constitute a "nuisance" is generally prohibited. 3. The apiary may be covered as a "business" you run out of your home. While generally allowed, the business cannot cause things like increased vehicle traffic in the neighborhood, or other nuisances. An inordinate amount of bees in the neighborhood might be considered a nuisance caused by your business, which might give them pause. 4. Allergies are a big deal these days. For the safety of neighborhood children, the HOA may restrict your apiary so that they can mediate the increased risk of bee stings by not artificially increasing the number of bees in the neighborhood. In addition to reviewing your Governing Documents, I would suggest getting in contact with the Board and pre-approving your apiary before starting it. If they do allow it, there may be other pre-requisites they have (i.e. getting industry certifications before starting, etc).
