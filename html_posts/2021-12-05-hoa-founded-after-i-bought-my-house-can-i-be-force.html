Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Generally, in order for an HOA to be mandatory, it must exist prior to homes being built on that property. It must be put in place by the builder and included as part of the master agreement for using that land. Once the first house is sold without that agreement, in order to make a mandatory HOA, you must have the agreement of a supermajority of the homeowners. The builder can represent the interests of the unsold properties, but in order to create a mandatory HOA when the builder does not own all the property, you must have the approval of a supermajority of the homeowners. If you only have a small number of homes sold, this may not be difficult. But if you have many homes sold already, you may not be able to create a mandatory HOA. There have been instances in the past where one homeowner out of hundreds who didn't want to be part of the HOA was excluded from its provisions and remained outside of the HOA's control. But this is the exception rather than the rule. **TL;DR:** In other words, if the builder didn't set it up before he started selling homes, in most cases he cannot force you to join. He can create an *optional* HOA, but he cannot create a *mandatory* HOA. \ Original question: HOA founded after I bought my house. Can I be forced to join?
