**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You asked when a "selective enforcement violation" applies, as if practicing selective enforcement is illegal. Not only is selective enforcement (in this context, "prosecutorial discretion") legal, but in some cases, it's advisable, or even necessary. For example, what if *everyone* on the Interstate is exceeding the speed limit? It simply isn't practical for one police officer to stop *everyone* on the Interstate and give them speeding tickets; usually they just stop the most egregious violators. And in some cases, giving a teen a warning instead of arresting him for whatever crime he had committed is enough to "scare him straight" and get him back on the right path. Sometimes, during a riot, it's simply not practical to charge everyone who has any sort of violation, or we'd literally bog down the courts with all the violations. \ Original question: When does a selective enforcement of laws violation apply?
