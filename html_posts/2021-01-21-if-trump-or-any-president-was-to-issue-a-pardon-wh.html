**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If Trump or any President was to issue a pardon when leaving the White House, and the person who they selected was a mass murderer, terrorist or a sex offender against kids, and was a danger to the public, would the authorities refuse?** No. Considering that the authorities in question (the Federal Bureau of Prisons) works directly for the President, it's unlikely they would refuse a direct order from their boss. The President's pardon power is absolute. It's in the Constitution. It doesn't matter who he pardons, or what that person did. If he issues a pardon, that person's sentence is over, and he is released from incarceration *poste haste*. It doesn't matter how much of a continuing danger that person is to society.
