--- title: '"Would banning guns for people on psychiatric medication reduce gun violence?"' date: '2016-07-07' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Effective psychiatric care is a combination of therapy and medications. Being on one or the other is much less effective than both. Mental health professionals are keenly aware of this. However, the people who provide therapy are not the people who provide medications, and the people who provide medications are not the people who provide therapy, and often, the two professions don't see eye to eye. Let's be clear: our mental health system is ***broken***. Simply trying to apply a band-aid to the system (i.e. medications=guns banned) is not going to fix the problem. It will simply mean that more mentally-unstable people will use other methods to kill, and we've simply turned a toothache into a headache; we haven't solved anything. Therefore, it would be wrong for us to say "medications=guns banned". Being on medication alone is not an indication that someone is not mentally stable enough to own a gun.
