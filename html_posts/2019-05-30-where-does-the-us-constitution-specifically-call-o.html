**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Where does the US Constitution specifically call out the responsibility of Congress to oversee the Executive branch of government?** As with many things in the Constitution, it may not necessarily contain those words, but it contains those ideas. We can also look at the thoughts of the people who wrote the Constitution. A collection of documents known as *The Federalist Papers* details what the framers of the Constitution were thinking when they wrote what they did. Federalist number 51 defines the principles we're discussing: *checks and balances.* Checks and balances has a full Constitutional tradition, even though those words were never listed in the Constitution. Each one of the three branches of government is *co-equal*; that is, no one branch is more powerful than the others. The powers of each branch are carefully defined, and in each case, the power to *oversee the other branches* is also clearly defined. Our laws, as allowed by our Constitution, call for [[Congressional oversight](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=27dc8b&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congressional_oversight){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}. This power allows them to review how Federal agencies are implementing their laws, and how they may need to modify those laws.
