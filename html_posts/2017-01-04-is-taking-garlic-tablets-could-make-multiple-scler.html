**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Could it? Sure. Is it likely? Probably not. [[A study](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=790062&url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25961060){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nih.gov"}]{.qlink_container} published at the National Institute of Health found that "garlic appears to enhance the functioning of the immune system by stimulating certain cell types, such as macrophages, lymphocytes, natural killer (NK) cells, dendritic cells, and eosinophils, by mechanisms including modulation of cytokine secretion, immunoglobulin production, phagocytosis, and macrophage activation."[\[1\]](#xTuZe){#cite-xTuZe .citation_link} So is it possible that a simple stimulation of white blood cells could cause increased disease activity? Sure, it's possible. However, at the same time, garlic also modulates the secretion of cytokines, which regulate the immune system. which could contribute to the regulation and/or treatment of many diseases, such as "obesity, metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disorders, gastric ulcer, and even cancer."[\[2\]](#kkzfO){#cite-kkzfO .citation_link} Cytokine modulation may be critical, as there is evidence of increased cytokine activation during MS relapse.[\[3\]](#VYFYy){#cite-VYFYy .citation_link} So garlic may also *regulate* improper immune system function. Garlic, therefore, could make things worse by increasing white blood cell activity, but it could also make things better by modulating cytokines. No one has yet studied this in humans, to the best of my knowledge. So what should you do? Should you start pounding the garlic because you have MS? **Absolutely not**. This is all "bleeding-edge" medicine. There is no consensus yet regarding this issue. It's only been studied, on paper, using mouse models of a disease which mimics MS. Changing things now is not only not indicated, but without further study, it could be harmful. If you think that garlic might help you, talk to your neurologist and your nutritionist. Let them work together to decide what will be best to treat your disease.
