**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The two states that don't actually require insurance from an insurance company do require other things. For example, in New Hampshire, where auto insurance is not required, you have to show that you are able to pay for damages in the event of an accident. In Virginia, the only other state which does not require you to have auto insurance, you can pay an "uninsured motorist fee" annually, but you remain personally liable in the event of an accident
