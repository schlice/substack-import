**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First, it's important to understand the difference between global warming and climate change. Climate change includes global warming, but encompasses a whole host of changes to the planet. According to NASA, climate change, "encompasses global warming, but refers to the broader range of changes that are happening to our planet, including rising sea levels; shrinking mountain glaciers; accelerating ice melt in Greenland, Antarctica and the Arctic; and shifts in flower/plant blooming times." Also according to NASA, global warming is "the long-term heating of the Earth's climate system observed since the pre-industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in the Earth's atmosphere."[\[1\]](#LkFIf){#cite-LkFIf .citation_link} From this definition, it is mankind's fault that we are experiencing global warming. It is important to note that not everyone agrees on whether it is mankind's fault, or if it is, to what degree, that we are experiencing global warming. Many people deny we are experiencing global warming at all. While the Bible does not specifically address global warming or climate change, it is important for people to take care of the environment, since God has given us dominion over the earth (Genesis 1:26-28). Since God has given us this great responsibility and privilege, we do need to care about the way we treat the environment. All mankind is to be good stewards of God's creation as the entire world is His, "The earth is the Lord's, and everything in it, the world, and all who live in it;" (Psalm 24:1). It is important that we do our part to help protect the earth. Activities such as recycling and smart usage of fossil fuels are small ways to help take care of the environment. But everyone taking small steps in their own spheres will eventually have a big impact.
