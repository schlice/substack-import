If a police officer has committed a crime, he goes to jail. Don't forget, police are under twice or three times the scrutiny that civilians are under when they fire their weapons. Not only is the district attorney investigating (just as a civilian), but the police run their own internal investigation to ensure the officer followed all department regulations and guidelines, that his training was up to date, and that he didn't violate any department policy. There could also be a third investigation by the US Justice Department to ensure that there were no civil rights violations. But to clarify your statement, police don't get arrested if they are wrong. Actually, police are protected from honest mistakes by the law. If they make honest mistakes of fact, fully believing they were following the law and their department's policies, they are legally indemnified against lawsuits. Police get arrested if they break the law, just like anyone else.
