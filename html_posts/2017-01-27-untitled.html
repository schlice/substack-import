**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Actually, there were two crimes. - First, there was a sexual assault. - This was immediately followed by a physical assault, which could easily be considered "self defense," which is an affirmative defense. So could the police charge her? Yes, but it would be ridiculous to do so, since it is extremely likely she'd get off (no innuendo intended) with a self-defense claim, and the DA complaining, "why are you bringing me these crap cases? I can't make this case!" Besides, while what she did was technically a crime, it was in self-defense, and it's generally bad public policy to charge victims. Local newspapers like that kind of thing because the incredulousness of the action makes money for them, but it's not something that could or should rightly be done. Even without her being criminally charged, he could try to sue her for assault and battery, and while his payout would most definitely be limited by his criminal conduct which incited the assault and battery, he might get enough money out of her to get his dry cleaning paid for. Emphasis on might, depending on the judge.
