--- title: '"What can I do when the police keeps someone in jail when I produced video to investigator that they are innocent? Is this an officer"' date: '2017-10-12' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(US answer)* Individuals can only remain incarcerated for no more than 72 hours without being charged. That means that the district attorney must charge the person within 72 hours or they must be released. If it has been more than 72 hours and the person is still in jail and formal charges have not been filed, that person's lawyer can go before a judge and get a *writ of habeas corpus*, which instructs the police to bring the person in front of a judge so the judge can decide if the person is being lawfully held in jail. If the person *has* been charged, it's because the district attorney believes there is enough evidence that a crime has been committed. Save your video for trial. Give it to the person's attorney.
