--- title: '"If a business operates in a state or city that has certain anti discrimination laws, do they HAVE to abide by them?"' date: '2016-04-30' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: Yes. Longer answer: Not every one of these businesses is run by a remote corporate office in some other city or state. Many of these \"chains of huge businesses\" are what we call ***franchises***. A franchise is a local business, owned by a local owner, which signs a *franchise agreement* with the **franchisee**, the owner of the franchise. The business can then operate as the franchise, even though the business is locally owned and operated. The McDonald\'s locations in my city are all franchises, none of them are owned by McDonald\'s corporate. However, the Taco Bueno locations here are all owned by Taco Bueno Corporate in Texas. Regardless of where the business is owned, or where the ownership is located, the local business must follow all state and local business regulations in order to be licensed by that state to do business in that state. This includes all state and local laws surrounding employment, and, of course, anti-discrimination.
