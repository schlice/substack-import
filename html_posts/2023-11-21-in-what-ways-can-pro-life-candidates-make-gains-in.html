**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They need to **not shy away from it**. While I'm not Ronna McDaniel's biggest fan, she's not wrong on the pro-life issue, and what Republican candidates need to do to win. > "We ... have to define ourselves before the Democrats define us," > Ronna explained on NBC. "And this is my number one message. If you're > digging yourself out of a hole, you're going to lose. But if you go on > TV and you say to the American people, 'Listen, we all are passionate > about this issue and it's confusing right now, but in a time of > consensus, can't we agree on reasonable limitations at 15 weeks when a > baby feels pain?'" > > She reiterated that position to \[Tony\] Perkins, \[President of > Family Research Council,\] insisting, "We shouldn't be silent. Listen, > we're proud to be a party that stands for the unborn. And I think > coming out of Roe, after 50 years of ... people not having to navigate > this issue, it's really important that we define ourselves before the > Democrats do. Let's talk about pregnancy \[care\] centers. Let's talk > about getting rid of cumbersome regulation to adoption. But let's also > put the Democrats on the defense because they stand for late-term > abortion. They stand for gender-selection abortion." > > It's time to say to them, McDaniel urged, "'Won't you agree that when > a baby feels pain, when a baby can physically feel its life being > taken away from them, that that is a bridge too far?' And they won't > meet us even there. They are the extreme on this issue. And when you > say that to the voters, we win."[\[1\]](#pqeNH){#cite-pqeNH > .citation_link} When Republicans go front and center and highlight the extremism of the Democrat platform, they win, every time. When they obfuscate and shy away from it, they allow Democrats to define them as "anti-woman" and in favor of women as nothing more than "baby factories." Candidates need to get out in front of the issue and define themselves. That's what voters want.
