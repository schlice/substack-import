**Content:** [There are businesses that think that it\'s okay to discriminate against a class of people because of their own religious beliefs.\ \ This is patently illegal, and no state law will ever legalize it.\ \ The law only allows a business to claim a religious exemption from government programs. It has nothing to do with individual actions.\ \ It has everything to do with the government placing restrictions on a business that violate the business owners\' religious beliefs.\ \ It has nothing to do with legalizing discrimination (although I\'m sure that some business owners wanted it to do that).\ \ The Federal law was successfully used to defend Hobby Lobby from provisions in the Affordable Care Act which violated the business owners\' religious beliefs, and they were granted an exemption from those provisions.\ \ There was some question that Indiana\'s RFRA might be a law that legalizes discrimination because of some words in the law which were slightly different from the Federal statute. It is very clear from what the legislature and the governor said that this is not the spirit of the law. These laws have existed for over 20 years, and it\'s very unlikely that such an interpretation of this law would ever be made.\ \ If such a thing were to happen, I would hope that the entire state (if not the entire country) came down on the governor and made sure that he repealed the law (which he pledged to do if it was misused).]{.rendered_qtext}
