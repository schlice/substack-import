--- title: '"I had a TBI (traumatic brain injury) and I had to learn to walk, talk, and eat again. I exercise about 5 days a week, but I was wondering if any of these balance or educational problems could be passed down if I were to have a child?"' date: '2014-11-13' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [To expound on [[Dean Carpenter](https://www.quora.com/profile/Dean-Carpenter-1)]{.qlink_container}\'s answer, your TBI does not affect your genetic makeup. Only your genetic pattern is passed to your children; injuries your body has sustained do not alter your genome. There may be some injuries that could alter some cells, but your DNA pattern can\'t be injured, and is present in every one of your body\'s cells. That\'s the beauty of the DNA structure of your body.]{.rendered_qtext}
