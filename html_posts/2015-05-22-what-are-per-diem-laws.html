**Content:** [Per diem is not required by law under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA). Paying your employees per diem for travel-related expenses is a good idea, and is also tax-deductible. [[The US GSA](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cec194&url=http://www.gsa.gov/portal/content/104877){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="gsa.gov"}]{.qlink_container} lists the Per Diem rate schedule by city, state, or ZIP code for employee meals plus lodging, and for incidental expenses. Paying at the approved rate is likely tax deductible.\ \ Corporations are also not legally required under FLSA to reimburse their employees for out-of-pocket expenses they incur for work purposes. However, if their required out-of-pocket expenses reduces their effective pay rate to below minimum-wage standards, the company could face a minimum wage violation claim. Therefore, it is in the best interest of the company to pay work-related out-of-pocket expenses to employees, and an even better deal for companies to pay per diem.]{.rendered_qtext}
