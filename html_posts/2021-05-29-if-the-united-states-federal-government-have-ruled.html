**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If the United States federal government have ruled that companies may mandate the Covid vaccine, how will that affect the states that have banned making it mandatory?** Whoa there, don't put the cart before the horse. Your article simply reports that the Federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) stated that their rules (surrounding equal employment opportunity) do not prevent companies from mandating that all employees which enter a workplace be vaccinated, so long as employers comply with the reasonable accommodation provisions of the Americans with Disabilities Act and other laws. This paves the way for employers to mandate all employees be vaccinated ***once the drug is fully approved by the US Food and Drug Administration.*** They ***still*** can't mandate its use ***today***. Today, these drugs are only approved under an Emergency Use Authorization, and *that* Federal law says that ***vaccination must be voluntary***. Forcing an employee to get vaccinated in order to retain their employment would be coercing an employee into violating Federal law (taking a vaccine against their will). If the only reason they are taking the vaccine is to retain their employment, and they otherwise refuse to take the vaccine, this would be a violation of Federal law.
