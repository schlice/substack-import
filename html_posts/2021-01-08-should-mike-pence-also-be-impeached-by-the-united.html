**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should Mike Pence also be impeached by the United States House of Representatives for refusing to perform his constitutional duties as Vice President, especially those specified in the 25th Amendment?** No. Failing to perform your job is not an impeachable offense. Failing to perform your job is an issue addressed at the ballot box. Impeachment is reserved for "high crimes and misdemeanors." In general, this means when a politician has done something so egregious that he cannot be left in office. This does not mean when a politician does their job *badly*. It also does not mean when a politician *doesn't even do what they're supposed to do*. There is no requirement for a Vice President to take action to remove a President who is doing his job *badly*. That's nowhere to be found in the 25th Amendment. The 25th Amendment does not apply when the president does his job, but he does it in a way that you don't like. The 25th Amendment only applies when the president is *physically incapable of doing his job*. In this case, he is doing his job, but we don't like how he is doing it.
