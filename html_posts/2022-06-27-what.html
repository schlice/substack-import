**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I hate to burst your bubble, but *Roe* is dead and isn't coming back. Whether or not abortion is legal, and up to what gestational age, is an issue for lawmakers in Congress or the states to decide, not the courts. Today, individual states can choose to allow completely, allow partially, or limit abortions at their discretion. So the quickest way to get *Roe*-like permission for abortion is to move to a state which completely allows abortions, like New York, California, Oregon, Washington, or Colorado. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-9723e40cf6b23a15890446a9320cf391){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-9723e40cf6b23a15890446a9320cf391" master_w="524" master_h="334"} ::: :::: *(Image courtesy* [[*NBC News*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=eefb05&url=https://www.nbcnews.com/data-graphics/map-23-states-ban-abortion-post-roe-america-rcna27081){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nbcnews.com"}]{.qlink_container}*)* The next quickest way is to elect people who will enact laws allowing abortions in your state. There are other, slower, ways to make it happen: - Propose a Constitutional amendment. If there is as much support for abortion as you believe there is, it should be no problem getting 2/3 of Congress and 3/4 of the states to sign on to an amendment to the Constitution declaring that the right to an abortion actually does exist. But that means you only need 12 states to say no and your amendment doesn't get added to the Constitution. So you have a really tough hill to climb for this one. You could also propose a Constitutional amendment guaranteeing the right to privacy, which was the faulty basis upon which *Roe*'s right to an abortion was based, and then try to say that abortion restrictions are unconstitutional based on the newly-discovered right to privacy. But you face exactly the same problem: you need majorities in 3/4 of the states to amend the Constitution. - Or, you could lobby Congress to pass Federal abortion protections. But clearly, while people want *some* access to abortion, they don't want 100% unfettered access to abortion at all gestational ages up until birth. So Congress would have to pass a *compromise* bill like, for example, allowing abortions up to at least 15 weeks, and states could decide to allow it for longer, but no less than 15 weeks. That would be a valid compromise bill: it sets a minimum threshold for the states, and allows them to permit it further if they choose, but they'd all have to allow at least a little. However, Congress really isn't interested in *compromise* these days. Some brave Representatives worked on a compromise bill, but the radical leadership of both the House and the Senate wouldn't even bring it to the floor for debate. So you're unlikely to get any motion out of Congress any time soon. \ Original question: What\'s the quickest way to ensure that Roe v. Wade is reinstated?
