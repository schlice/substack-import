**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the United States, you can sue anyone for anything. So the only question that remains is how likely are you to prevail. And as with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." If you make an inappropriate song about *a specific child*, and you can show that the child in question could be harmed by the song, you can sue for the damage that will come to the child, as well as for defamation of character. If you make an inappropriate song about *unspecified children,* it's unlikely that your lawsuit will prevail. There really aren't any decency standards for music (except for broadcast music over the radio), so saying that something is "inappropriate" is purely opinion. The last attempt to do so was the Communications Decency Act of 1996, and much of that was struck down so that only speech that was not subject to the First Amendment was unlawful. **TL;DR:** Most music is considered "artistic expression" or "artistic works" and are thus protected under the First Amendment.
