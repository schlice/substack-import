--- title: "(Matthew 5:28) How can that sin be forgiven?" date: "2024-07-20" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *"You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery.' > But I say to you that everyone who looks at a woman with lustful > intent has already committed adultery with her in his heart. \-- > Matthew 5:27--28 ESV* Jesus really made a big challenge here. Previously, people were under the Ten Commandments which said you shall not commit adultery. But then Jesus comes along and says "Now you're under the new law, and if you even *look* at a woman lustfully, you have already committed adultery with her in your heart." WOW. That's nuts, right? I can't control my mind! It's going to think what it wants to think! How is this even possible to do? **And that's the point.** Jesus is telling everyone that **on your own, it is impossible to be good enough.** Merely following the law is not enough. No one is righteous, not one (Romans 3:11). No one is "good enough" on their own to merit salvation. But with God, all things are possible. > *But Jesus looked at them and said, "With man this is impossible, but > with God all things are possible." \-- Matthew 19:26 ESV* It is **only Jesus** who can save you. You can do nothing on your own to earn salvation. Only through repentance and faith can you be saved. Therefore, that sin, along with every other sin, can be washed away by the blood of Christ. His death gives us life. His sacrifice redeems us. He alone is worthy. **TL;DR: How can that sin be forgiven? Repentance and faith.** ***This is the way.***
