**Content:** [We can\'t keep the mentally ill away from guns until we can do a better job of identifying ***who*** is mentally ill, and ***what*** constitutes a mental illness that is severe enough that it can\'t be treated. Basically, until we fix the mental health system in this country, there are no more laws or restrictions that we can pass that will make any substantive difference without unduly burdening law-abiding gun owners.\ \ The gun laws are not the issue. The treatment of mental health is the issue. [[Deinstitutionalization](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=18cb6f&url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalization){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} has been great for most inpatients, but it has indeed left a portion of the mental health population, literally, \"out in the cold.\" It has marginalized that group and has led to some not being treated at all. I want to quote the above-linked page from Wikipedia, because it says exactly what I was trying to say, but more succintly.\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} > With the closing of these state mental institutions it has become > increasingly difficult for people who suffer from severe mental > illness to receive treatment in a facility. Many mentally ill > individuals were left homeless after Deinstitutionalization, making up > one-third of the homeless population (D.E. Torrey). Today the most > prominent treatment for the severely mentally ill is incarceration in > a correctional facility, where mentally ill individuals are not > receiving adequate care for their > disorder.[[\[22\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5e572d&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalisation%23cite_note-22){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\ > \ > \[\...\]\ > \ > Although deinstitutionalisation has been positive for the majority of > patients, it also has severe shortcomings. Expectations that community > care would lead to fuller social integration have not been achieved; > many remain without work, have limited social contacts, and often live > in sheltered > environments.[[\[23\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e5d51a&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalisation%23cite_note-Torrey_Shadows-23){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\ > \ > New community services are often uncoordinated and unable to meet > complex needs. Services in the community sometimes isolate the > mentally ill within a new ghetto, where service users meet each other > but have little contact with the rest of the public community. It has > been said that instead of \"[[community > psychiatry](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=d392c0&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_psychiatry){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\", reforms established > a \"psychiatric > community\".[[\[7\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4981f8&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalisation%23cite_note-Fakhourya07-7){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\ > \ > Existing patients are often discharged without sufficient preparation > or support. A greater proportion of people with mental disorders > become homeless or go to > prison.[[\[7\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4981f8&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalisation%23cite_note-Fakhourya07-7){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}\ > \ And finally, the issue we are seeing most recently with the most violent (emphasis mine):\ > \ > Families can often play a crucial role in the care of those who would > typically be placed in long-term treatment centres. However, many > mentally ill people are resistant to such help due to the nature of > their conditions. **The majority of those who would be under > continuous care in long-stay psychiatric hospitals are paranoid and > delusional to the point that they refuse help, believing they do not > need it, which makes it difficult to treat > them**.[[\[24\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=60128a&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deinstitutionalisation%23cite_note-24){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} \ While deinstitutionalization has helped many rejoin active community life, a portion of those people **actually needed continuous psychiatric care** and are not getting it, because the laws have changed such that entry into psychiatric care is generally only court-ordered or emergency room physician ordered. This means that the vast majority of people that really require continuous psychiatric care are actually on the streets or incarcerated. The top three mental institutions in this country in terms of population are prisons, [[the largest being the Cook County Jail in Chicago, Illinois](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ba425b&url=http://thttp://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2015/06/americas-largest-mental-hospital-is-a-jail/395012/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);"}]{.qlink_container}.\ \ The sad fact is that by implementing a process of deinstitutionalization to try to re-integrate the mentally ill who can be successfully treated with antipsychotics, we have actually created a population of people who can\'t be treated on an outpatient basis. In other words, ***we have created this problem ourselves***.\ \ We need common-sense reform of psychiatric care in this country so that these people can be treated in continuous care facilities. We need to remove the stigma that accompanies mental health. We need better integration between psychiatrists (who generally do not counsel) and mental health counselors (who generally cannot prescribe medications).\ \ Our mental health system is a mess, and many seriously ill patients who need help either end up in jail or on the 6 o\'clock news.
