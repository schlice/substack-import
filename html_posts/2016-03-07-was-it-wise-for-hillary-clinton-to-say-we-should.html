**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The statement you quoted, on its face, wasn\'t very controversial. I think we can all agree that we need to do a better job of identifying those who are mentally unstable and getting them help. The ***problems*** with her statements come **after**. She proposed closing the \"gun show loophole\" which does not exist. She proposed closing the \"Charleston loophole\" which says that if your background check does not return an answer after three days you can purchase your firearm, which is not what happened in Charleston either, and is still not a cause for unhinged people going on shooting rampages. She then claimed that the Protection of Lawful Commerce in Arms Act (PLCAA) prevents people from suing gun manufacturers, ever. She called it \"blanket immunity,\" and this is an incorrect characterization of the law. She claims that PLCAA prevents people from suing gun manufacturers to make their products \"safer.\" This is patently false, and totally absurd. Guns are very safe tools to operate. Very rarely do you hear of someone being injured when they properly use the gun. It\'s easy to confuse \"Safer for the operator\" with \"Safer for the person at the business end of the gun\" when you fail to realize that guns are actually designed to kill. If they do kill, the gun is working properly. If the operator was uninjured, the gun is also operating safely. And under PLCAA, if a gun is unsafe to operate, the manufacturer can still be sued. The purpose of PLCAA was to prevent the Left\'s tactic of trying to sue gun manufacturers into oblivion because their products killed people. If their products are unsafe, you can still sue them. And if you want to pressure a manufacturer to make a \"safer\" handgun (whatever that means), you don\'t do that through the courts. You do it through your purse or wallet. You buy the safer one, you ignore the unsafe one. That\'s how free markets work. You don\'t see people trying to sue Ford when a drunk driver kills a family. You don\'t see people telling Ford they need to make a \"safer\" car. But this is exactly what Clinton wants to do. She wants to repeal PLCAA so that gun violence victims\' families can transfer the liability from the shooter to the manufacturer, something that is unprecedented in history. Don\'t let her rhetoric fool you. In fact, Bernie Sanders actually had it right: > Well, this is what I say, if I understand it --- and correct me if I'm > wrong. If you go to a gun store and you legally purchase a gun, and > then, three days later, if you go out and start killing people, is the > point of this lawsuit to hold the gun shop owner or the manufacturer > of that gun liable? > > If that is the point, I have to tell you I disagree. I disagree > because you hold people --- in terms of this liability thing, where > you hold manufacturers' liability is if they understand that they're > selling guns into an area that --- it's getting into the hands of > criminals, of course they should be held liable. > > But if they are selling a product to a person who buys it legally, > what you're really talking about is ending gun manufacturing in > America. I don't agree with that. While Bernie and I agree on very little, on this point we totally agree.
