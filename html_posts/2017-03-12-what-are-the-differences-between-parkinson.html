**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are some big differences. Parkinson's Disease is characterized by the death of brain cells which produce a neurotransmitter called dopamine. Over time (many years, actually), the loss of the ability to produce dopamine causes the patient to lose the ability to send brain signals which control movements of the body. This may be related to MS as there is some new research that indicates that the death of the dopaminergic neurons (which produce dopamine) may be caused by an [[autoimmune response](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=13b417&url=http://newsroom.cumc.columbia.edu/blog/2014/04/17/parkinsons-autoimmune-disease/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="columbia.edu"}]{.qlink_container}. Multiple Sclerosis is definitely an autoimmune disease, but in this disease the immune system attacks and destroys myelin, the fatty sheath surrounding nerve fibers. In its wake, it leaves plaques, or scars (thus the name "multiple sclerosis" meaning "many scars"). Nerve signals do not traverse the scarred neurons as easily or efficiently as they do through normal nerve cells (think an electrical wire with its insulation stripped off), and this causes the various and unpredictable symptoms of the disease.
