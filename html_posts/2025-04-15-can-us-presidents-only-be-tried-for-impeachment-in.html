Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Impeachment is a** ***political*** **function, not a** ***judicial*** **one.** This is why only the House can draft Articles of Impeachment, and only the Senate can hold a trial. I know the Constitution calls it a "trial" and that sounds a lot like what happens in a courtroom, and I know that the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court presides in the case of the trial of a president, but it is not a judicial proceeding, period. It is a political proceeding. Therefore, it takes place in the hallowed halls of politics (Congress), not in the hallowed halls of a courtroom.
