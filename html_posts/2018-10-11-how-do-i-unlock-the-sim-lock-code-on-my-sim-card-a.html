**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I unlock the SIM lock code on my SIM card and phone?** If you get a message that states your SIM card is locked, you will need your Personal Unlocking Key (PUK) which is unique to your SIM card. Your carrier will have this information in their Support section on their website. Just go to your carrier's website and search for "SIM card lock" and you should find where you can look in your online account (if you have one) for your PUK. Your carrier's website should also have detailed instructions for how to unlock your SIM card. If you need any help with this, contact your carrier's Customer Service department directly. You can get there directly by dialing 611 on your phone, but I recommend finding their toll-free number and dialing it from another phone, so you can work on yours while you speak to them. If you are talking about "carrier-locks," where your phone is locked to your carrier, whether or not you can unlock depends on whether or not you have paid off your phone. Once you have paid off your phone (if you have an equipment installment plan with your carrier), your carrier will give you information on how to unlock your phone. Once it is unlocked, you can use any compatible SIM card from any carrier in your phone.
