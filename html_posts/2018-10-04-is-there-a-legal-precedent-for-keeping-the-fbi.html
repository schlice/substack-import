**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is there a legal precedent for keeping the FBI\'s report on Kavanaugh from the public?** Yes. It's not really a "report," but it's a background investigation. It's a "supplemental" background investigation, in that it supplements other background investigations. As such, it contains personal and confidential information about a person. Additionally, it contains information gleaned from other people. There are so many privacy concerns, therefore, that it's unlikely that the information from a background check will ever be released to the public while the subjects are still alive. You will likely see a summary of what's found (and I'm certain that Senators will be happy to put their spin on what they read), but the actual documents themselves will likely remain unreleased.
