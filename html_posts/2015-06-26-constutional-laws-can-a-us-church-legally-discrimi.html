**Content:** [Churches are allowed to discriminate in their hiring practices and what they preach. That\'s what the First Amendment allows. But let\'s look at the ramifications of today\'s decision. I\'ll break down each one of your points.\ \ ***Allowing marriage equality would lead to people marrying animals***\ \ That\'s not going to happen. No one can honestly equate an animal to a person. Also, the argument that we\'ll be allowing adults to marry children will equally not stand, as there are physieological differences between men and boys, women and girls. And the decision-making centers in the brain are not reasonably formed until at least age 25 in men, possibly younger in women, but the age of majority has been set at 18 in most states. So that\'s not happening either.\ \ However, there is a cogent argument to be made that laws banning polyamory (polygamy and polygyny) are no more or less harmful than laws that previously banned same-sex marriage. I see legal challenges to those laws succeeding using today\'s same-sex marriage ruling as their legal precedent. If we have the power to re-interpret what marriage has exclusively been, there is no reason why any law restricting a combination of spouses should also stand. Any argument that the plaintiff in this same-sex marriage case would have made would be equally applicable to any argument a plaintiff in a polyamory case could make.\ \ That said, I think we can still reasonably draw the line at people.\ \ ***Churches being forced to accept and even perform weddings for gays***\ \ It could be argued that a church could not keep its tax-exempt status if it failed to follow Federal law. And the new law-of-the-land is that same-sex marriages are Constitutional. You could conceivably see the IRS denying tax-exempt status to any church that fails to follow Federal law and perform same-sex weddings. Doesn\'t matter what the church believes. You can choose to bury your head in the sand, but this is the first time that the law might actually be *applicable to a church*. Federal law says that same-sex weddings are now Constitutional, so there is no reason that a church, which is tax-exempt under Federal law, would be allowed to violate Federal law and refuse to marry a same-sex couple. Any church which thus refuses could lose its tax-exempt status. So I think this may still be a concern, and churches should brace for a new fight. Who does this new law apply to? State governments, or churches? Are churches still protected by the First Amendment, or will the Federal government try to force churches to perform same-sex weddings or lose their tax-exempt status? I would think that the broad precedent surrounding Federal law applying to churches would stand, but this court has now, twice in two days, overlooked the precepts of law and have ruled in favor of public opinion. So anything\'s fair game now.\ \ ***Forced hiring of gay ministers to spread the gay gospel***\ \ While the Federal government may be able to direct churches to perform same-sex weddings or face loss of tax-exempt status, the decision to influence churchs\' hiring practices has been debated, and lost. The government can only protect classes of people under the civil rights act, and gays are not listed there. This law was not changed today. A business can still only not discriminate based on race, color, creed, sex, or national origin. A church which does not believe in homosexuality can still choose to not hire a gay minister.\ \ ***Marriages between males and females falling apart***\ \ This has been happening for a very long time, and the government is as much a part of the problem then as it is now. The glamorization of the single-parent household, the weakening of divorce and adultery laws, states advocating no-fault divorce, etc. have all led to a culture that shows marriage\'s *impermanence*. This decision doesn\'t really contribute to that, other than to make marriage more about the *desires of adults* than about the *needs of children*, which was the previous focus of the marriage law. This decision won\'t have any bearing on what happens in the bedroom between a man and a woman. Those marriages are no more precarious today than they were yesterday. The decision does show that the government is suddenly less interested in the needs of children, and more interested in the desires of adults, to the detriment of our nation\'s children.\ \ ***Gays being allowed to teach young children in schools to be homosexual***\ \ The ruling simply says that same-sex marriage is Constitutional. So if a school is teaching a lesson on marriage, it can teach that male/female, male/male, and female/female marriages are all legal. It doesn\'t influence the curriculums of schools, other than to say what is legal.\ \ Does it pave the way for further erosion of the institute of marriage? Certainly. It can be used as a springboard to introduce all sorts of curricula into schools. Parents can still choose, as they always have, that their personal limits on left-leaning curriculum have been breached, and they can change their child\'s school to a private school, or homeschool. This will mean changes for the family (I have thought of buying a cheaper, smaller house in a worse part of town just so I\'d have enough money to send my kids to a private school; thankfully I didn\'t have to, but I would). But those choices still exist for families who disapprove of the school curriculum. It doesn\'t really hurt the school much. They lose PPOR (Per-Pupil Operating Revenue), but in the grand scheme of things, they\'re not too concerned that they lost one student. And overall, most families are just going to go along with it anyway.]{.rendered_qtext}
