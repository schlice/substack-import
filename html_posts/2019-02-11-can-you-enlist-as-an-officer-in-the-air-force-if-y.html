--- title: '"Can you enlist as an officer in the Air Force if you have 2 years of JROTC under your belt along with 2 years of trade school experience?"' date: '2019-02-11' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You cannot "enlist" as an officer. There are four paths to becoming an officer in the United States Air Force: - Graduate from the US Air Force Academy (USAFA) - Attend Officer Training School (OTS) after getting your Bachelor's Degree - Attend AFROTC in college - Enlist as an Airman and work towards becoming an officer As you can see, you need at least a Bachelor's Degree to become an officer. With the exception of OTS, you can get your Bachelor's Degree while you train to be a military officer. If you go the OTS path, it's because you already have your Bachelor's Degree. Visit the [[Officer Career Path](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2621a4&url=https://www.airforce.com/how-to-join/process/officer){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="airforce.com"}]{.qlink_container} page on the official Air Force web site for more information.
