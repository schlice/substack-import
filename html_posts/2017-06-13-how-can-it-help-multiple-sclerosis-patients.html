**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is a fantastic question! I think that IT can help MS patients immensely, and I think that IT as an industry has largely overlooked the MS community. The way to properly address how IT should do anything is to look at the problem itself, and see if there is an IT solution that can improve it. So what problems do MS patients face? There are quite a lot of them, but some of the most common are: - Fatigue - Walking issues - Numbness or tingling - Spasticity - Weakness - Vision issues - Dizziness or Vertigo - Bladder/Bowel Problems - Sexual dysfunction - Pain - Cognitive Changes - Emotional Changes - Depression How would you use IT to help combat any of these issues? For example, fatigue can be addressed using occupational therapy to simplify tasks at home or at work. IT can most definitely be used in occupational therapy to assist with simplification of tasks. Plenty of other items on this list, like vision issues, cognitive changes, and walking (gait) issues can also be aided through the appropriate use of technology. It's just a matter of a developer believing that a significant enough market exists to market a device or an application in order to bring the symptom and the mitigating tech together.
