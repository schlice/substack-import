{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [You are falling into the trap that the media is perpetuating: the police need to be feared because they look fearsome.\ \ First, to answer your question, the Posse Comitatus Act prevents the U.S. government from using the military against the population. Just because the police have to become increasingly \"militarized\" in order to protect themselves against growing threats does not make them \"the military.\" They still work for the Chief of Police, the Sheriff, or the Governor. While part of the Executive branch of government, they all ultimately work for the President, but only the military works for the Commander-in-Chief. See the difference? So you\'ll never see the US Army write you a speeding ticket. But some fancy bulletproof uniforms and vehicles do not make the police into the military, and thus unable to do their jobs under Posse Comitatus.\ \ Now, on to the meat of what you\'re getting at. The police have become more militarized over the last 50 years. But that\'s not because of some national conspiracy to make the police into some state military. It\'s in response to the growing threats against the police. The past few months have only proven that those threats are real, and only need a push to set some people off.\ \ Times are tough for cops right now. It\'s up to all of us to counter these threats with **reason** and **logic** and **compassion**, not threats and fear.]{.rendered_qtext}
