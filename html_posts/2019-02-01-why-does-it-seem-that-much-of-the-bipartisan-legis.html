**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why does it seem that much of the bipartisan legislation in the US seems worse than legislation created along party lines?** It *seems* worse because it *is* worse. It's worse for me or you, specifically. But it's best for ***us***. That's the very nature of compromise. Both sides give a little bit of what they don't want to give to reach a consensus. Neither side wants to give up what they do, but they do it for the sake of compromise. And that's why bipartisan legislation *seems* worse for me than the original partisan bill which gave me everything I wanted. But while it may not check 100% of all my boxes, it works for *everyone*, which is why it is a bipartisan consensus bill.
