--- title: "Can legal action be taken against an employer who does not let you work but does not fire you either?" date: "2016-08-11" tags: ["law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A2A In the US, there is no standard for a "minimum" amount of time an employer is required to pay an hourly employee who reports to work but then is told to leave. There are state standards for minimum work time for hourly employees, but they vary by state. For example, some states like California say that if you are an hourly worker who comes in expecting to work eight hours and you work less than half of the usual time (or the scheduled time), the employer is required to pay half of the usual hours. California calls this "reporting time." There are some instances in which the employer doesn't have to pay reporting time: 1. When the employer\'s operations cannot begin or continue due to threats to employees or property, or when civil authorities recommend that work not begin or continue. 2. When public utilities fail to supply electricity, water, or gas, or there is a failure in the public utilities, or sewer system. 3. When the interruption of work is caused by an Act of God or other cause not within the employer\'s control, for example, an earthquake. 4. If the employee is not fit to work. 5. If the employee has not reported to work on time and is fired or sent home as a disciplinary action. 6. If an unexpected or unusual occurrence during off hours makes it impossible for the employer to open for business and the employer has made every reasonable effort to notify employees not to report for work. Call your local Office of Labor Standards in your state, or you can also call the [[Wage and Hour division](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=7f4ee4&url=https://www.dol.gov/whd/america2.htm){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="dol.gov"}]{.qlink_container} of the US Department of Labor. Failing that, hiring a good labor attorney would be a great thing to do. In the meantime, use your down time to find another job. When they ask you why you want to leave your old job, it's easy enough to tell them you're not getting enough hours at your old job.
