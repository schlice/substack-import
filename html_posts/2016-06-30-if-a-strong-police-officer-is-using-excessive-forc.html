Your girlfriend never committed any crimes and is not part of > a bust, sting, or investigation.* > > *Do you ask the crowd to record a video and plead for her life? Do you > try to help her escape the choke and then cover her with your body? Do > you just stand there?* Keep in mind the bounds of this question. You have to be able to stop the police officer **without being killed** or **without being arrested yourself.** *The answer is:* ***there is no situation in the law which allows you to interfere with a police officer who is carrying out his duty.*** None. And whether or not he is actually carrying out his duty is a matter of law to be determined by a judge and a matter of fact to be determined by a jury, well after the incident is concluded. You are not qualified, nor permitted by law, to make that determination on your own, at the scene. As heartbreaking as it may seem to you, *you must let the officer proceed.* Remember that the OP asked how you can save her **without being killed or arrested.** The answer is that ***you cannot stop the officer from doing what he is doing without, at a minimum, being arrested, or at worst, being killed yourself.*** *What's more, you must understand that if the officer kills her, there was nothing you could have done without being arrested or killed yourself.* If you intervene, you will be arrested and charged with assault on a police officer, interfering with the duties of a police officer, aiding and abetting, and a myriad of other charges. **I CANNOT STRESS THIS ENOUGH. ANY INTERVENTION YOU TAKE FOR WHATEVER REASON YOU CHOOSE TO TAKE IT WILL DEFINITELY GET YOU ARRESTED AND MAY GET YOU KILLED.** **Your sense of chivalry (if that concept still exists any more) may make you willing to take that risk of arrest, but that's not what this question asked.** Your best bet is to get out your cell phone and record as much as you can. The problem is that you won't have video of what happened *before* you started recording, and if there is no video of that, we're down to testimony, in which case it will be his word over yours. You also have to understand that there are police arrest control holds which "look like" choking, but are not. Look up the Carotid Control Hold if you're interested. It looks, to an outsider, like the officer is "choking out" the suspect, but in fact there is little to no pressure on the windpipe at all. There are two types of neck restraints: vascular and respiratory. Respiratory neck restraints use direct pressure to constrict the anterior (front) portion of the neck, which causes asphyxiation. The police *never* intentionally, deliberately use this as it can cause significant injury and death (note that we're not excluding accidents, where an officer attempts a neck restraint and does it wrong). The second type of hold is a vascular hold. This applies no pressure to the anterior portion of the neck (the crook of the elbow is in front), while the pressure is applied bilaterally (on the *sides*) to the neck. This restricts blood flow to the brain, causing the suspect to briefly lose consciousness. If you're a WWE fan, it's what the "sleeper hold" is supposed to mimic. The Carotid Control Hold is very useful on a large and uncooperative suspect. It gives the officer enough time to calm the suspect down and safely apply handcuffs. If it is done correctly, it minimizes injury to the suspect and to the officer. \ Original question: If a strong police officer is using excessive force and choking out your 100 pound girlfriend, how can you save her without being killed or arrested?
