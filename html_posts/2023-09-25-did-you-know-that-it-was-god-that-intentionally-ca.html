--- title: '"Did you know that it was God that intentionally caused the fall of man using Eve?"' date: '2023-09-25' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I think you have misused the term "intentionally." There is a great difference between *omniscience* and *predestination*. People have a really hard time wrapping their heads around this. **God can simultaneously know what will happen without influencing the free will of the people involved.** God desires you to have free will. In fact, He desires it so greatly that He will voluntarily allow you to choose evil and condemn yourself to eternal death. He wants your choice to love Him to be **totally free.** But in order for it to be totally free, you must have a compelling alternative. Let me give you an example. If I set two bowls down in front of you, and one contains ice cream and the other contains dead decaying rats, which one will you choose to eat? Now, I don't even have the gift of omniscience and I can tell you with 99.9% certainty which one you will choose. Now, if I set two bowls down in front of you and one contains vanilla ice cream and the other contains chocolate ice cream, *now* which one will you choose? The fact that the choices are so closely desirable is what makes the decision truly free. In the first example, you have the "appearance" of choice, but no one would actually choose the dead rats to eat. So there really isn't a choice. In the second example, you are choosing between two equally palatable choices, so you really do have a free choice. In the Garden, God had to do the same thing. He gave Adam and Eve the chance to obey Him and love Him, but He also gave them a compelling alternative. It had to be flashy and attractive, otherwise it would be easy to resist. So the choice was clear: love God, obey God and ignore the fruit, or listen to the serpent and go for the potential gains of eating the fruit (and sin against God in doing so). God knew what choice they would make. But the point is to establish a life wherein you get to make that choice freely, without coercion. That's what God desired to do, and that's why He built the system in this way.
