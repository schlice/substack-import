Answer










Content: [The crime of assault, in most jurisdictions, includes the
offense of battery, as the tort assault doesn\'t rise to the level of a
crime without the included battery. In some jurisdictions, the crime of
assault is actually called \"assault and battery.\"\
\
The tort \"assault\" and \"battery\" are still separate entities for
tort law and civil court. You can be assaulted without a battery taking
place (a verbal assault, for example). A \"battery\" in civil court
would be constituted as unwanted touching where that touch was
intentional. Generally, you can sue for assault and battery, but in most
cases, where the incident is both an assault and a battery, it is a
crime.]
