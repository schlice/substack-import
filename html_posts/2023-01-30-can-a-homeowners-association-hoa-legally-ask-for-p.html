--- title: "Can a homeowners association (HOA) legally ask for proof that the owner actually lives at the residence, even if they pay their dues on time every month?" date: "2023-01-30" tags: ["law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is yes they can, so long as there is a valid reason for them to do so. For example, Governing Documents may require that the owner use their home in the HOA as a primary residence, especially if that HOA disallows rentals, or only allows rentals with Board approval. In that case, it would be appropriate, if questions were to arise, that the HOA ask for proof that the owner actually lives at the residence. How the HOA plans on validating that the owner actually lives at the residence, I cannot say, nor can I say what evidence the HOA would find acceptable.
