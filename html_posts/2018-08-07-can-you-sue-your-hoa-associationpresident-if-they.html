**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can you sue your HOA association/president if they caused a potential buyer to back out of their contract on your home? The HOA president confronted the new buyer for going over our 15 mph speed limit, and it escalated to the point of no return.** As we've often said here on Quora, you can sue anyone for anything. The real kicker is whether or not you have a chance of winning. In this case, you have little chance of prevailing. First of all, there is no affirmative obligation for anyone to buy your house. Even if there is an agreement to sign a contract, each individual is free to choose, for whatever reason they want, to not enter into that agreement. You may not be happy with the HOA president, but he incurs no liability for causing someone to change their attitude about the neighborhood and choose not to buy a home. Secondly, if the HOA owns the roads (i.e. it's a gated community), then the HOA is responsible for ensuring that the speed limit is enforced, regardless of whether or not the person is a resident, a guest, or a potential owner. The speed limits are presumably in place to keep people safe, so if people are so flippant with them, perhaps they weren't the right fit for the neighborhood anyway. The HOA president is completely within his rights to confront speeders. I'm sorry the contract fell through, and you might want to blame that loss on the HOA president, and you might even be right to do so.
