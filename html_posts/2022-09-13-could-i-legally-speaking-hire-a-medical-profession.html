**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The question isn't hiring a phlebotomist to draw your blood. The question isn't also about using your blood for art (although that's an interesting idea with its own challenges --- we'll get there in a minute). The big problem you're going to have is **how to store your blood**. In the U.S., the collection, testing, preparation, storage and transport of blood and blood components must comply with FDA regulations and guidance documents. And blood that is not going to be used for medical purposes is considered **medical waste** and is a **biohazard**. Blood is a biohazard because it has the potential to carry contagions which can infect others. Therefore, blood must be handled in accordance with its intended use, and storing blood for artistic use is similarly going to be a problem, both legally and practically. \ Original question: Could I, legally speaking, hire a medical professional to draw my blood so that I can store it and use it for artistic purposes?
