--- title: "Are US police officers taught to use the common peroneal strike?" date: "2016-08-15" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} All departments train differently, but officers most definitely learn this during baton training. The idea is incapacitation with a single, quick blow to avoid the appearance of "beating" the suspect with repeated strikes. Ideally, you want the minimum number of strikes to incapacitate the suspect, at which time you can finish the takedown and handcuff the suspect. This is a part of police training called "arrest control."
