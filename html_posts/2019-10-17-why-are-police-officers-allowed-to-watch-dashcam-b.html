**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why are police officers allowed to watch dashcam, bodycam, and surveillance video before making a statement and a citizen cannot?** Who says that you can't? Certainly not the article you quoted. In fact, the article you mentioned says that Americans are divided on whether or not police *should be* allowed to look at their video before they make an official statement. Unsurprisingly, the division is virtually exactly down political lines. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-e27dfe457da5fc07ff91dd92569f3d19){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-e27dfe457da5fc07ff91dd92569f3d19" master_w="572" master_h="759"} ::: :::: Most departments allow police to review their bodycam footage while they are making their reports for the day, as they will normally attach the relevant footage to their official report. So they need to be able to see it in order to ensure it's the correct footage, especially in departments that require cameras be continuously recording. But the policy is controlled at a state/local/department level, not nationwide.
