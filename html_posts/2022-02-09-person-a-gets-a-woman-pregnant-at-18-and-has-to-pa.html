Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Child support is not about punishing the higher-earning parent, or the non-custodial parent. Child support isn't about rewarding or enriching the custodial parent, or the lower-earning parent. In fact, child support isn't about the parents at all. And it's definitely not paid to "support the girl." **Child support is about the child.** Child support payments are determined by the state using a formula which takes several factors into consideration, only one of which is the non-custodial parent's ability to pay. It also takes into account the mother's ability to work. Does she have a degree? Does she have a high-paying job? Can she afford to raise a child? This wouldn't *negate* the child support requirement (Person A still has to contribute), but it might *lessen* it. Child support does not increase simply because the non-custodial parent has more money. So at the point at which Person A becomes a billionaire, the woman cannot haul him back to court and demand he pay more in child support just because he *can*. Most importantly, child support has nothing to do with "supporting the girl." If they were not married, he has no obligation to support her. He *might* if he's a nice guy and suddenly has billions to spend, but he is not *obligated* to. It is truly "his billions, his choice." Perhaps if they had been married and they got divorced, she could ask the court to take another look at *alimony* (called *maintenance* in some states), but *child support* is a fixed amount to ensure that the basic needs for a child are met until that child turns 18. \ Original question: Person A gets a woman pregnant at 18 and has to pay one thousand in child support.
