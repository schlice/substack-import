**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They were trying what we call "pain compliance" when you're dealing with an uncooperative suspect. However, they did it wrong. Pain compliance is useful in an actively struggling individual. Pain compliance isn't very effective in a docile, but simply non-compliant individual. Physical manipulation of a docile, non-struggling non-compliant person is most likely better than pain compliance. Pain compliance works better on an aggressive person as it may induce them to be less aggressive or face more pain. It simply doesn't work as well on the non-aggressive person. Regardless, it sounds like that department needs a refresher on pain compliance.
