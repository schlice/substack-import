**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} That's the thing about morals, isn't it? Morality is usually a person's intrinsic understanding of right and wrong. Morality differs from ethics as morality is personal (from within), and ethics are derived from an organization or group (from outside). A person's morals are generally shaped by their upbringing and their environment. The upshot of all of this: *morals can vary from one person to the next.* So what one person finds *moral,* another person may find *immoral*. \ So what does any of this have to do with the abortion question? Some people may find it morally acceptable to kill a fetus, while others may not. The difference between the pro-life and pro-choice camps turns specifically on the value the two groups place on human life: - Pro-life people are primarily focused on the preservation of all human life, regardless of its phase of development or its temporal location. Pro-life people generally believe that it is immoral to destroy any human life, outside of extenuating circumstances. - Pro-choice people place varying values on human life depending on its temporal location (i.e. its location in space and time). In other words, they place less value on human life when it is in its very earliest stages, and when it is present in a mother's womb. Pro-choice people largely believe that it is *less immoral* to destroy early-development human life. **TL;DR: The morality of abortion at any phase in the development of the baby varies from person to person.
