**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The HOA can make whatever rules they choose, so long as they follow their procedure for modifying the rules in the particular area of the Governing Documents they are modifying. The question you are apparently asking is "can the HOA make a rule that doesn't respect my right to freedom of speech?" And the unfortunate answer is **yes.** Homeowner's associations are generally regarded as private organizations. The HOA is, in most places, a 501(c)(4) non-profit corporation. And while the First Amendment prohibits the government from making rules that curtail your freedom of speech, this does not apply to private corporations. There are indeed some aspects of the Constitution that apply to HOAs (since they maintain property, collect "taxes", and are responsible for other municipal-style duties), but that's where the similarity ends. The HOA is a private corporation that you join by contract.
