Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not unconstitutional, just not what the military is doing. Some expenses may have to be repaid by some members, but not everyone. The military is using "administrative separations" to remove members for vaccine refusal. For those who don't qualify for exemptions and who flat-out refuse the vaccine, they are being administratively separated, and the character of their separation is "General (Under Honorable Conditions)". This means that the member will not be allowed to re-enlist or join another military branch, but they will still likely retain access to all of their VA benefits, just as if they were discharged with an Honorable characterization. As far as pay goes, no service member separated based on vaccine refusal will be eligible for involuntary separation pay. Also, officers separated for vaccine refusal but who have not yet completed their service obligation for attending a service academy or being awarded a Reserve Officer Training Scholarship will be required to repay their educational expenses. This policy includes service members who have received advanced education funds.
