**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with any legal answer, this one is also "it depends." Depending on your jurisdiction, the rules of your parole *may* allow **police officers** to search your home without a warrant. But in nearly every jurisdiction, **parole officers** and/or **probation officers** can search your house without a warrant. Typically, when you are paroled, you sign what is known as a "Fourth Amendment Waiver." This means that for the duration of your parole, you give up your Fourth Amendment rights regarding search and seizure. In practice, in most jurisdictions, this means that any police officer or parole officer can walk up to you any time they have a suspicion you are engaging in criminal activity and search you. Periodic random house checks conducted by your Probation Officer (PO) are often part of the terms of parole as well.
