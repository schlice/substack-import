**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How do I get a divorce when I don\'t know where my husband is?** As with all legal answers, this one depends on where you live. In the US. when you file for divorce, you have to serve your spouse with the divorce papers. Generally you have a specific period of time to do this. If you can't find your spouse, you can ask the court for an Order of Notice by Publication. This allows you to serve your spouse by running your notice of intent to divorce in the local newspaper in the last known area where your spouse was. You will probably have to file an affidavit of marshal service attesting to the publication, and the publicized notice will also have to be filed. Depending on your jurisdiction, you may also have to file an affidavit of military service showing that your spouse is not in the military and that you are trying to divorce him while he is deployed. Keep records of everything. Keep records of everything you tried to do to contact your spouse, and the responses. If you sent letters to his last known address and they were returned, keep those too. You can still divorce your spouse if you can't find him. The court can perform all of the divorce proceedings without him, but you must show that you have tried to locate him, and that you tried to get the notice of divorce to him.
