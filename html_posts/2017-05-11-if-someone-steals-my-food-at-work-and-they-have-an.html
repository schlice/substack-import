--- title: '"If someone steals my food at work and they have an allergic reaction for eating it, can I get into legal trouble?"' date: '2017-05-11' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In order for you to get into legal trouble in such a circumstance, you would either have to be actively violating rules regarding allergens in a shared location (such as an office refrigerator), or you were actively attempting to deceive the person into eating your (tainted) food over his own. There has to be some element of *mens rea* in order for you to get into trouble (i.e. some nefarious intent), or some active disregard of a rule designed to protect persons with allergies, which would constitute reckless endangerment, or something similar. If someone with an allergy actively takes your food, knowing that they do not have permission and have no control over the ingredients, and they eat it, they are knowingly taking food over which they have no control. At that point, they take their own health into their hands when they do that.
