**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do the United States kill people painfully with poison, instead of using a more humane way of execution?** The current lethal injection system is designed to kill in the same way that an anesthesiologist puts someone to sleep before surgery. It does this through the injection of three drugs: 1. Sodium thiopental or pentobarbital: an ultra short-action barbiturate, an anesthetic agent used at a high dose that renders the person unconscious in less than 30 seconds. Depression of respiratory activity is one of the characteristic actions of this drug. Consequently, the lethal-injection doses will---even in the absence of the next two drugs---cause death due to lack of breathing, as happens with overdoses of opioids. 2. Pancuronium bromide: a non-depolarizing muscle relaxant, which causes complete, fast, and sustained paralysis of the skeletal striated muscles, including the diaphragm and the rest of the respiratory muscles; this would eventually cause death by asphyxiation. 3. Potassium chloride: a potassium salt, which increases the blood and cardiac concentration of potassium to stop the heart via an abnormal heartbeat and thus cause death by cardiac arrest.[\[1\]](#xRrxS){#cite-xRrxS .citation_link} The drugs are administered in order: the first drug causes loss of consciousness, the second drug causes paralysis, and the third drug induces cardiac arrest. In some instances, the drugs did not work in the order or doses in which they were given. Especially in drug abusers who already have high tolerances to certain drugs like barbiturates, the pentobarbital may have little effect, rendering the inmate conscious while the second drug is administered. When the third drug is administered, if you are still conscious, you would essentially be experiencing painful cardiac arrest, while conscious and paralyzed. This, arguably, is torture. The problem is that the drugs are administered by prison guards, not by an anesthesiologist. Things like previous drug use and size of the inmate are not adequately taken into consideration when the doses of the drugs are determined. There are other ways to execute prisoners more humanely. [[Inert gas asphyxiation](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0759a1&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inert_gas_asphyxiation%23Capital_punishment){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} is one such method. Indeed, three states (Oklahoma, Mississippi, and Alabama) have inert gas asphyxiation listed as a primary or secondary method of capital punishment.
