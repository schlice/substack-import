**Content:** [Thanks for the A2A, but I don\'t have PPMS. I have Relapsing-Remitting MS (RRMS), so I can\'t relate to what it\'s like living with PPMS.\ \ What I do know is that PPMS is fundamentally different from all the other forms of the disease. The other forms of the disease, including RRMS and Secondary Progressive MS (SPMS) (to a degree), are characterized by demyelination and nervous system inflammation. In other words, the other forms of the disease are inflammatory in nature. The MS medications we have today treat the inflammation in the nervous system, reducing severity of symptoms and decreasing the duration of relapses.\ \ PPMS is primarily not inflammatory in nature, it is categorized by nerve degeneration without the relapsing/remitting inflammation. Therefore, none of our current treatments, which target the inflammatory response and not the degenerative response, are effective with PPMS. While we have a fair understanding of the inflammatory response, and we have a fair ability to control that response to a degree, the underlying degenerative response is still unknown.\ \ PPMS primarily affects the spinal column, so the most prevalent symptoms are gait, bowel/bladder problems, and sexual dysfunction.\ \ I can imagine what it would be like if my right-side weakness never got better. I think I\'d be using a cane by now.]{.rendered_qtext}
