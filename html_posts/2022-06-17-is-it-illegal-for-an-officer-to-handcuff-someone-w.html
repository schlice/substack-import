**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If the police handcuff someone, this amounts to a *detention*. The ability to detain someone temporarily is permitted under *Terry v. Ohio*, sometimes just called a *Terry stop*. *Terry* stops can take place when the officer has a reasonable suspicion that the person is involved in criminal activity. If the police were to handcuff someone (thus detaining them) without having a reasonable suspicion that the person is involved in criminal activity, this would constitute an *unlawful restraint,* which is a violation of Texas Penal Code ยง 20.02.
