Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You don't always; it depends on the state you are in. Many states allow you to keep your existing title. Nevada, for example, allows you to register your vehicle and get a Nevada license plate without a Nevada-issued title. Florida, on the other hand, requires you to surrender your old title and pay to get a Florida-issued title before you can register the vehicle in the state. Some states, like New York, require a new title when you pay off your car loan, but you can register your car with an out of state title. When it is not mandatory to re-title your vehicle, it might be a good idea anyway. It might make it easier when you go to sell your car. \ Original question: **Why do I need to surrender my title when registering my vehicle to a new state (USA)?**
