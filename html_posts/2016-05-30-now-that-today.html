**Content:** [First off, I have never been a fan of \"texting and driving\" laws. Here\'s why.\ \ Most traffic laws do not \"allow\" their use, but disallow certain behaviors rather than focusing on the technology. Besides, we already have laws against distracted driving; must we address every piece of new technology with a new law, or just find better ways to interpret our existing laws?\ \ The problem with laws is that the underlying technology changes much faster than the cycle of implementation of laws. We started seeing problems with texting and driving while people were using flip phones. By the time most states have implemented laws against texting and driving, we have moved on to Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, two technologies which allow you to use the most popular smartphones hands-free, along with texting by voice. This technology is included in many new cars today. The technology, as usual, has outpaced the law.\ \ It will be interesting to see how the current technology-focused laws cope with new technology.]{.rendered_qtext}
