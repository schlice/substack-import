**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Chocolate and "crisps" (you must be from the UK, in America they are potato chips) are fine to serve as "treats." Even daily. There's nothing inherently "wrong" with either chocolate or crisps. However, there are several reasons why you should delay the introduction of sweets and high-fat, high-sodium, high-calorie foods as long as you can. The main reason is that while you still have control over their diets, you want to introduce healthy eating habits, demonstrate healthy eating habits, and encourage those habits. If you can do this while showing that chocolate and crisps should be eaten in moderation, and only as "treats," then by all means, go ahead. Also, while your children are developing (and 18 months is during the "critical" development periods, while their brains are developing at the most rapid rates), and since you want to give your kids the best possible chance at a good, healthy life, giving them the most nutritious meals you can give them is crucial. Giving them "empty" calories (foods which are high calorie and high fat with very little additional nutrition) is not beneficial to their growth and development. During this period, they need the most nutritious foods you can give them, and snack foods are not them. Again, not to disparage chocolate and crisps as they are lovely, but while you have the opportunity to set your kids on the healthiest, best path, wouldn't you do so?
