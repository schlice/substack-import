**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **I keep getting mail for someone that does not live in my home. I have written \"return to sender\" on every mail piece but a lot of the companies just resend the mail. Is there anything I can do to stop this mail?** Yes! When writing "Return To Sender" stops working, or the senders won't stop sending you mail, you have a number of options. Your first option is to visit your local Post Office branch and fill out a **change of address card** for them. You can't use the online form because that requires a forwarding address. Simply fill out their name and your address and then indicate "Moved, No Forwarding Address." Sign it yourself, then write "Form filled in by current resident, \[your name\], agent for the above." That last part is important, since you are not the actual person in question. Hand the card to the postal worker, or directly to the postmaster if you must. This should get the bulk of the mail returned at the post office, before it ever gets to you. If the problem is related to **junk mail**, you can address the issue with the [[Direct Marketing Association](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=716e0e&url=http://thedma.org){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="thedma.org"}]{.qlink_container} and sign up for their Mail Preference Service. > The DMA purposefully obfuscates how to sign up for it (and be warned, > half of the links on their own site are circular or just don\'t work), > but it\'s pretty simple. You can either register for an account and > pay \$5 at > [[DMAChoice.org](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=66cfe2&url=https://www.dmachoice.org/index.php){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="dmachoice.org"}]{.qlink_container} to \"manage\" your bulk > mail (which includes the option to stop it entirely) or you can send a > letter/postcard to *Mail Preference Service, Direct Marketing > Association, PO Box 643, Carmel, NY 15012-0643*. Include \"Activate > Mail Preference Service\" in your note, and the other person\'s name, > and your full address. Each request has to come from a single > individual---just for kicks, make sure the letter is certified, > preferably with return receipt, so they know that you know they got > it. Each request is good for five years, after which it\'ll have to be > renewed (hopefully everyone\'s records will be updated by > then).[\[1\]](#WZrQn){#cite-WZrQn .citation_link} If this doesn't work either, you can **file a complaint** with the Postmaster at your local post office. Do this by writing a letter and handing it to the Postmaster, or you may need to set up an appointment to talk to them. You can file a [[Prohibitory Order](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=2c9a7b&url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prohibitory_Order){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} against the mailer, which if it is approved, would make it illegal for them to send you mail. Also keep in mind, don't throw the person's mail away (unless it's just junk mail). If it looks important, do continue to mark RTS on it and put it back in your outgoing mail. I'm sure you'd want someone to do the same for you if your mail was being incorrectly forwarded.
