Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} We are all sinners. No one is righteous, not one. > As it is written: > > "There is no one righteous, not even one;\ > there is no one who understands;\ > there is no one who seeks God.\ > All have turned away,\ > they have together become worthless;\ > there is no one who does good,\ > not even one." --- Romans 3:10--12 NIV All believers have the best intentions. But we fail. We fail because we're human, and we still have human frailty, and we still live in a fallen world, in sinful bodies. Paul talked about this in his letter to the Romans. He struggled with exactly what you're feeling now: > I do not understand what I do. For what I want to do I do not do, but > what I hate I do. And if I do what I do not want to do, I agree that > the law is good. As it is, it is no longer I myself who do it, but it > is sin living in me. For I know that good itself does not dwell in me, > that is, in my sinful nature. For I have the desire to do what is > good, but I cannot carry it out. For I do not do the good I want to > do, but the evil I do not want to do---this I keep on doing. Now if I > do what I do not want to do, it is no longer I who do it, but it is > sin living in me that does it. --- Romans 7:15--20 NIV Verse 20 is really important: " Now if I do what I do not want to do, it is no longer I who do it, but it is sin living in me that does it." It's not me who is doing it, but the sin that lives within me. We're all sinners. The sin lives within all of us. Don't beat yourself up over it. Recognize it, repent of it, and ask God to give you the strength to resist. Over time, it will get easier and easier to resist. But remember, we will still fail. Hang in there. God isn't going to give up on you.
