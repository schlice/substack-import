**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **On a business trip to the USA, I was asked for ID when entering a bar. The guys on the door were very surprised that I didn\'t routinely Carry ID on me (I\'m from the UK). Is this a normal \'thing\' in the USA?** You must understand the cultural differences. The entire layout of the country was built around the automobile. How we lay out communities and towns and cities and neighborhoods is all predicated on the presence of a personal automobile. While in the UK you can walk to a neighborhood store or to school or to the library, in the US, in nearly all cities, you have to drive there because the distances are usually too great. Some small strip malls are appearing in more populated suburbs to make up the difference, but even these may be too far to walk to. They may be just a few minutes drive, however. To that end, nearly everyone is a driver, so it is very common for almost everyone to routinely carry their license with them. It is used just as much as a form of personal identification as it is a validation that they are able to drive a car on public roads. Additionally, bars in the United States ID everyone who appears to be under 40 years old, and the most commonly accepted form of identification is a driver's license.
