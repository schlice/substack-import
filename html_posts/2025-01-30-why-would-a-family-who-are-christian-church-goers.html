Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} We can't speak to why one particular person, or one particular family, would do or not do something. People only act in a Godly way when they choose to follow God's will for their lives. And people choose every day to either act in a Godly way or not. Our free will often leads us to choose actions which are not in keeping with "God has prepared for those who love Him" (1 Cor 2:9). Their choice doesn't make them Christian or not Christian. It makes them *human*. And again, I can't speak to what they did because I don't know why they did what they did. I can only tell you that *as a Christian*, I can't speak to what they did because that's not what Christianity is about. When you become a Christian, you don't automatically do everything in a completely obedient manner. Your flesh remains. Your old habits remain. Your old proclivities remain. It is only through willing and active work and study that you become, over time, a better version of yourself. Sometimes, it's a lifelong process, one which Christians call ***sanctification***. Over time, through work and prayer, we become more like God in our thoughts, words, and actions.
