**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **My dash cam captured a hit-and-run on the interstate including license plates. It also shows that I was speeding. If I turn the video in to aid police, will I get cited for the speed?** In most cases, no. In Colorado, a speed violation must be observed by a peace officer in order to be a ticketable offense. Most states are similar. Therefore, unless a peace officer saw you speeding, they can't give you a ticket. In all practicality, you won't be cited for your speed simply because that's not the case they are after. It would be considered "bad optics" to cite the person turning over evidence in a crime, as it would probably not engender much more help from other witnesses. I think that in all cases, they'd let the speed violation slide so that they could catch the actual criminal.
