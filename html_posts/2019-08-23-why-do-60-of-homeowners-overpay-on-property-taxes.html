**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do 60% of homeowners overpay on property taxes?** [[Are You Paying Too Much in Taxes?](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=0b01ec&url=https://www.ntu.org/foundation/tax-page/are-you-paying-too-much-in-taxes){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ntu.org"}]{.qlink_container} Reading the article by the National Taxpayer's Union, you'll find that it's very likely that most Americans who overpay do so because they are blissfully unaware that anywhere from 30 to 60% of taxable property in the United States is over-assessed. No one expects that their property is going to be over-assessed. They think that government employees will always work for the public good, and would never intentionally over-assess a property. Sometimes, over-assessment is simply due to math errors. It could be that the assessor has an outdated, incorrect or inaccurate assessment of the actual property. But many Americans simply pay the difference because the cost (in time and money) to get the property re-assessed is too high to justify when compared to the amount of money you will actually save. The process by which you can appeal the assessor's decision is lengthy and cumbersome, while the actual reduction in taxes you see (while still qualifying as a reduction) may not be worth the effort to get it re-assessed and reduced.
