**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} We don't have a national ID because it's not useful for anything. You're a *citizen* of the United States, but you are a *resident* of a state. States generally use driver's licenses as ID cards. You can also get a state ID card if you don't drive. Even in a national election, you vote as part of your state. Especially for president, where the states decide the outcome, not the people. So why don't they just use your driver's license as voter ID? Some states do, but not all. I mean, it seems simple enough. Why can't you just get a driver's license or a state ID? What about this scenario: what if you're elderly and you haven't seen your birth certificate for the last 40 years? You don't have a car, you live on a fixed income, etc. So you can't get your state ID card to vote because you don't have enough documentation to do it and it's cumbersome to get around. You'd have to take the bus to city hall to get a copy of your birth certificate so that you can take another bus to go to DMV to get a state ID card so you can *vote*? *That's* disenfranchisement. The arguments about racism and illegals voting are red herrings. Don't get me wrong, I'm all for voter registration. I don't see a problem with showing some acceptable form of ID. You show ID to get your Social Security benefits. You show ID when you go to the doctor's office to get a checkup. You seriously can't show ID to vote? But there are some scenarios where I could see obtaining a state ID card is cumbersome or prohibitive for some.
