**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If an assault requires a witness, and crimes by nature are secretive, where does that leave justice for victims?** Your assumptions in the lead-up to your question are not correct. **If an assault requires a witness** Assaults do not "require" a witness. As in any crime, to prove "beyond a reasonable doubt" that the defendant committed the specified crime, you need ***evidence.*** Witness testimony is great evidence, but so too is DNA evidence, surveillance camera footage, doctor's medical examination reports, credit card receipts, electronic toll records, and many other items of evidence which can show when and where a person was located (and sometimes what they were doing) at any given time. All of the evidence is compiled and is used to reach above that "reasonable doubt" standard.
