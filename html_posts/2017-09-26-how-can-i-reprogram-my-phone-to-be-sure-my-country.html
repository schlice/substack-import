**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Let's start by assuming you're talking about your country *wiretapping* your phone and listening to your calls and intercepting your text messages. Since the listening would be taking place *outside* of your physical device, there is nothing you can do to prevent your service provider from listening in on your phone calls. The only way you can prevent someone from listening in on your calls while they are in flight is to use a secure phone. There are many types of secure phones available. Militaries and government agencies use them to communicate classified material over regular telephone lines. The phones encrypt the voice on the source side, send the encrypted voice to the target side, and decrypt the voice on the target side. They exchange keys at the beginning of the call, and once the two devices have sync'd, they begin encrypting the voice traffic. What you can do instead is use [[Signal](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f74393&url=https://signal.org/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="signal.org"}]{.qlink_container}. Signal is an app for your phone ([[Android](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c90720&url=https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id%3Dorg.thoughtcrime.securesms){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"} ]{.qlink_container}or [[iOS](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=df24a1&url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/signal-private-messenger/id874139669){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container}). So long as you and the party you've called both are using Signal, calls and texts will be end-to-end encrypted (from your handset to the called party's handset). Best part: Signal is free and open-source. Signal doesn't integrate tightly into iOS because of Apple restrictions, you have to use it as a standalone app. But in Android, Signal integrates with Android, and if the party you are calling also has Signal installed, it automatically asks if you want the call to be secure. Text messages using the Messages app works the same way. If instead you were talking about your country hacking your phone and enabling the microphone and camera to listen to you when you're not on the phone, try this little piece of awesome: [[**Anti-radiation Bag - Anti-tracking Pouch, EMF Protection for Phone**](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=e14a2d&url=https://www.amazon.com/Anti-radiation-Anti-tracking-Protection-Anti-spying-Function/dp/B074ZCHWGY/ref%3Dsr_1_2_sspa?ie%3DUTF8%26qid%3D1506463495%26sr%3D8-2-spons%26keywords%3Dcell%2Bphone%2Bpouch%2Banti%2Bsignal%26psc%3D1){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} The cool thing about this phone pouch is that once you slip it into that pouch, no signals will get in or out. No one can hack your phone and even if they did, they can't connect to your microphone or your camera, and they won't be able to spy on you. Of course, putting the phone in this pouch means that you won't get any calls or texts, either. You would have to consciously take the phone out, let it connect and update, and then when you're done, put it back. Just know while the phone is in the pouch, you're out of touch too.
