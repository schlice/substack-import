**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *They are demanding Apple build a backdoor into their product.* I\'m sorry that I must take exception at this point, because what you are saying is **patently false**. I know you truly believe that the FBI wants complete and unfettered access to everyone\'s smartphones all the time without a warrant, but that\'s simply not the case. If you read the court order, the FBI is not asking for a backdoor. A \"backdoor\" means something built in to the operating system that allows the FBI to decrypt the contents of the phone at will, without any intervention by anyone, with their own special \"key.\" ***This could not be further from the truth, and it is disingenuous of you to claim such.*** What the FBI wants to do to **THIS ONE PHONE** is the following: 1. Remove the \"10 strikes and you\'re wiped\" feature. 2. Remove the time delay between incorrect PIN entries 3. Enable external keyboards for PIN entry You also claim that \"the FBI will have the software and can use it on any phone.\" This too is untrue. The order states that Apple can keep the software at their headquarters. It never has to leave. In fact, all the work to weaken the security posture of the phone will be done at Apple\'s headquarters. It will be the FBI that then breaks into the phone **not by defeating the encryption**, but by using a \"brute-force attack\" against the phone, repeatedly guessing passcodes at a rapid rate (using an external keyboard and hacking software) until the phone is unlocked. The encryption algorithms will never be touched.
