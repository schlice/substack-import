**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How could Trump resign and get Pence to pardon him like people are suggesting, if he has not been formally charged of any crimes yet? Can Pence write a blanket pardon for any crimes Trump may have committed?** Clearly, the people who are telling you this are *presuming* that Trump committed a serious crime, is aware that he has done so, and is just waiting until they catch up with him. A presidential pardon can cover "unspecified crimes" over a period of time. Gerald Ford pardoned Richard Nixon in this way after he resigned. So people are looking at that bit of history and are saying that if Trump did something serious, he should resign and allow Pence to pardon him. In that way, he couldn't be prosecuted under Federal law. *(Clearly, and it should go without saying, these people are actually less interested in protecting Trump from Federal prosecution, and are more interested in Trump resigning. Sounds like the frenzied hysteria of a Never Trumper.)* However, pardons are only at the Federal level. Trump could still be prosecuted at the state level (and New York has shown that it is completely willing to prosecute him). Also, if it is shown that Pence engages in a corrupt bargain or bribery in the pardoning of Trump, he could be impeached and removed from office himself for that action. Remember, pardons are criminal, impeachments are political.
