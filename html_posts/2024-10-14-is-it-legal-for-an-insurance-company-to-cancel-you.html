--- title: "Is it legal for an insurance company to cancel your policy due to flood damage without providing written notification first?" date: "2024-10-14" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal answers, this one is the same: **it depends.** In most cases, insurance companies cannot cancel insurance policies without providing 30 days written notice. Most states require insurers to give policyholders a specific period of written notification before canceling a policy, regardless of the reason, including flood damage. Each state has different rules about ***how much*** notice must be given before a policy can be cancelled with a written notice, regardless of the reason. And yes, a flood can be a factor for your insurance company to not renew your policy. Typically though, insurance companies need to provide written notice before cancelling policies in the middle of the coverage term.
