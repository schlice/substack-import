**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You've already heard that there are no special exemptions for former military members when it comes to state laws regarding firearms ownership. NY and MA have some of the strictest firearms laws of any of the states. While you *can* own an AR-15 in those places, there are indeed state laws which limit your ability to own certain cosmetic features on the firearm, and there are many, many hoops to jump through in order to be able to legally acquire, store, transport, and use these firearms in those states. None of us are going to even attempt to list the applicable laws (although [[Wikipedia](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=091c70&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gun_laws_in_the_United_States_by_state){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} does an admirable job). You should always check with a gun shop in your local area or a law enforcement officer to find out what the current laws are regarding any particular weapon you wish to purchase where you live. \ Original question: Can anyone own an AR-15 in states like NY or MA? What about ex-military men?
