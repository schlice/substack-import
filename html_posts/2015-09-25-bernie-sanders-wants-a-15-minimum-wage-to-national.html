**Content:** [Mike Rowe (the comedic host of *Dirty Jobs*) discusses this in depth as he [[lobbies](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=382d4f&url=https://www.facebook.com/TheRealMikeRowe/posts/938382126171931:0){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="facebook.com"}]{.qlink_container} for people to fill the millions (currently around three million) of currently unfilled basic skilled labor jobs in this country. In a nutshell, he says that rather than paying more for *unskilled* labor, put that money towards vocational training, get some *skill*, and make yourself **worth more**. Entry-level jobs are just that: entry level. Once you have some experience, you\'re expected to *move on.* Entry level jobs were never expected to sustain you, or a family. He says, \"Very few of those jobs require a four-year degree, but nearly all require specific training. And all pay more than the current minimum wage. If we want a skilled workforce, (and believe me, we do,) should we really be demanding \$20 an hour for unskilled labor?\"\ \ Nationalizing the oil and gas industry would only serve to remove the profit. But profits aren\'t what drive cost at the pump; it\'s the cost of the raw fuel:\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-61d2a3bb6bfd53da77a397ac13ad52c1){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-61d2a3bb6bfd53da77a397ac13ad52c1" master_w="2550" master_h="3300"} ::: :::: \ As you can see in this graphic from Michigan (where gas is probably some the most expensive in the country), the vast majority of the cost of gasoline is the actual cost of fuel. Nationalizing will only affect the 2.8% of the cost which is taken by fuel stations (since they will now be government operations). Some have said that nearly 40% of the wholesale cost of gasoline, but [[this table](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=77a513&url=http://energyalmanac.ca.gov/gasoline/margins/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="ca.gov"}]{.qlink_container} from California (I know, Michigan vs California, but it\'s close, gimme a break) shows that when gas was \$3.89 (per gallon) in California, \"Distribution cost, Marketing costs, and profits\" were only \$0.39 per gallon. The actual crude oil cost \$1.31, refinery costs and their profit (which are mostly outside the United States since environmental regulations are so heavy on stateside refining) are \$1.60, and taxes were about \$0.50. We\'d be able to eliminate the taxes and the profit, which would cut the cost down to closer to \$3/gal. Not a significant benefit when you consider the massive tax increases and government expansion that will be required to sustain the new governmental oil and gas system. And you can bet that any refining companies which currently reside in the United States will pick up and leave should the oil and gas industry be nationalized. Their rates will go up, and with no chance of a President Sanders removing any environmental regulations to increase refining in the United States, fuel costs (the majority of which is refining cost) will go up. So ultimately, you\'ll see some volatility, but nothing will really change at the pump.\ \ Why a 90% top-tier tax rate? What is that expected to accomplish? Are you trying to pay off the national debt? You could make the top *three* tax tiers 100% and you still wouldn\'t pay off the national debt any time soon. I would much rather see us control runaway entitlement spending rather than simply increasing taxes, even if it is on \"those dirty stinking rich.\"\ \ Expanding Medicaid is what Obamacare did. We\'re going to expand it *more*? Who else is it going to cover that it didn\'t cover before?\ \ I agree that healthcare insurance is driving up the cost of medical care, but I don\'t think single payer is the answer. I think removing the health insurance industry will significantly reduce costs. I think instituting tiered healthcare will further allow all people access to basic healthcare. Heck, doctors go overseas and do it for *free* these days; why aren\'t they doing it here? Yes, specialty healthcare will still need some form of payment, as doctors still need to make a living, and they did attend years of schooling and have advanced knowledge that the average parent doesn\'t have. They do deserve to be paid fairly for their work. And specialists even more so.
