--- title: "Can I sue a grocery store for selling expired food?" date: "2017-10-30" tags: ["policy-analysis"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Remember, in many cases, it is the manufacturer (through their distributors) who directly stock store shelves, not always store employees. So in some cases, the food makes it to the store shelves without the store even touching it. Also remember that some foods spoil before their expiration dates due to faults in the manufacture or delivery process. Those foods should be returned to the store for refund. The store will take it up with the distributor. If you found expired food on the shelf, but haven't purchased it yet, immediately take it to the customer service desk at the store. You should expect to see a store employee accompany you to the area in the store where the expired stock is located, and you should expect to see it immediately removed from the shelf. If you get a lukewarm response (or no response at all, or worse, someone re-stocking the expired item), call your regional health department and inform them of the issue. If you inadvertently consumed expired food, you're probably going to be okay. If it's only a few days past expiration, you should be fine. Be more careful when you buy things. You should be checking the expiration on everything you pick up in the store. If you inadvertently consumed expired food and it made you sick, you might be able to sue the store for damages, but it's likely they will simply settle with you and pay your out-of-pocket medical costs, assuming you can prove when you bought it and when the expiration date was. They probably won't push too hard which is why I said they'll likely settle out of court.
