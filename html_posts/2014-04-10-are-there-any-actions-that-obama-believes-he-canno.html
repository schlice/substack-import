Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [AG Holder:\ \ \"It is possible \[\...\] to imagine an extraordinary circumstance in which it would be necessary and appropriate under the Constitution and applicable laws of the United States **for the President to authorize the military to use lethal force** ***[within the territory of the United States]{.underline}***.\"\ \ It may be possible to imagine such a circumstance, but that would only be because you have a very very vivid imagination. [[The Insurrection Act](https://www.quora.com/Is-the-statement-Theres-a-reason-you-separate-military-and-the-police-One-fights-the-enemies-of-the-state-the-other-serves-and-protects-the-people-When-the-military-becomes-both-then-the-enemies-of-the-state-tend-to-become-the-people-true)]{.qlink_container} is the only law which allows the US military to act to enforce the laws of the United States, and even then, the Act is so tightly worded and so tightly constrained, that you would have to have a situation develop in the United States that the fully-constituted strength of the states could not control (this means with full National Guard callout). I don\'t see that happening.\ \ And it certainly wouldn\'t be legal against a single person, because the states can control a single person. The President cannot take action with the US military against US citizens. The [[Posse Comitatus Act](https://www.quora.com/Is-the-statement-Theres-a-reason-you-separate-military-and-the-police-One-fights-the-enemies-of-the-state-the-other-serves-and-protects-the-people-When-the-military-becomes-both-then-the-enemies-of-the-state-tend-to-become-the-people-true)]{.qlink_container} strictly forbids this.]{.rendered_qtext}
