**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can stress such as losing your job cause a relapse in Multiple sclerosis?** Yes, stress can definitely trigger relapses. Stress, studies have shown, [[causes inflammation in the body](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5d1eff&url=https://www.everydayhealth.com/wellness/united-states-of-stress/link-between-stress-inflammation/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="everydayhealth.com"}]{.qlink_container}. MS exacerbations (relapses) are caused by inflammation in the central nervous system (CNS). The inflammation damages the myelin which slows or disrupts the transmission of nerve impulses and causes the symptoms of MS.[\[1\]](#tittr){#cite-tittr .citation_link} Therefore, stress is a big trigger for exacerbations. It's not a guarantee, but a trigger.
