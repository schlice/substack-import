**Content:** [If I sell you my bracelet (worth about 1200 INR) for 2000 INR, this is not illegal. Unless I misrepresented the quality, the heritage, or the actual cost of the bracelet, making a profit is not illegal in a free-market society.\ \ Additionally, the value of an item does not track directly with the market price for the raw commodity. If that particular style of gold item is in high demand, they can make more profit simply because the demand is higher. Other gold items which are not in as high demand could potentially sell for less than market value.\ \ The lesson here is ***caveat emptor***. It is your responsibility as the consumer to tell the jeweler the current market rate for gold and demand they sell you the bracelet for market price plus a reasonable amount for profit. If they do not, move on to the next vendor. This is how free-market capitalism works. The vendor who sells at the most competitive price will get the most business. Other vendors will have to match that price in order to stay competitive.]{.rendered_qtext}
