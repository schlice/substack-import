--- title: "What is the logic behind not making an image for God?" date: "2025-03-06" tags: ["uncategorized"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So as we know, God is *spirit*. He does not have a body. He does not have an image. If we create an image for God (such as the most common image: the old man with the long flowing white hair), we tend to think of God in *human form.* And when we think of God as a *human*, we think of God with **human limitations**. So the danger with thinking of God based on an image we create is that, even in our minds, we tend to ***limit*** God. We ***constrain*** Him to the image we created. Thinking of God with any limits at all does a disservice to our understanding of Him. God is limitless. He is omnipotent and omnipresent, two qualities which are uniquely unsuited to thinking of Him in any form we can imagine.
