--- title: '"Is there anyway for HoA to stop home owners from renting out their houses as half-way homes, if there has been trouble with law enforcing in the past?"' date: '2023-01-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, it is well within the power of the Board to do this. It can be accomplished in multiple ways. Here are just a few examples: - Amend the Declaration of Covenants or the Bylaws to disallow halfway houses in the neighborhood. This will require varying percentages of votes by the homeowners (depending on your state and your HOA), but it is the most iron-clad way to do it, and it would be virtually impossible to challenge in court. - Modify the Rules and Regulations to require that the Board approve any and all rental contracts before they are signed. This change can be voted on by the Board alone at a regular Board meeting, but it requires setting up rules for what types of rentals are "allowed" and what types of rentals are "disallowed." You could also add verbiage to say that the Board has the authority, in its sole discretion, to disallow any rentals if it believes that the rental would be detrimental to the community in any way. This also is more prone to legal challenge, but if it is well-written (use your HOA attorney to help draft the final wording of the Rule), it should be able to survive most challenges. These are not the only ways you can accomplish this change. I would recommend an interested Board contact their attorney to discuss methods by which this behavior could be restricted in their neighborhood.
