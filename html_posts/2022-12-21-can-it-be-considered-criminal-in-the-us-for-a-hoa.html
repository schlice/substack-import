--- title: '"Can it be considered criminal in the US for a HOA to demand payment for a made up fine on the threat of foreclosure?"' date: '2022-12-21' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. At best, the HOA doesn't have the authority to issue the fines it does. But *criminal?* No, not at all. First of all, let's discuss what is a "made-up fine." In the US, if an HOA is going to cite you for a violation of the Rules, the Rule in question must be specified. And it must be a Rule that the Board of Directors has voted to approve at a Board meeting. The HOA is required to let you know what that rule is that you are being accused of violating. It may be something as simple as, "No homeowner may participate in any activity which is a nuisance to other homeowners." Who decides what is a nuisance? Who decides whether or not that nuisance bothers other homeowners? The answer is that the HOA, in its *exclusive* authority, decides what is a nuisance and what is not. I don't know your specific situation, but I *do* know that everything the HOA fines you for must be something as recorded in the Governing Documents, or in the Rules and Regulations. So first, find out what provision within your Governing Documents was violated. If they cannot, or will not, cite the actual provision being violated, do not pay the fine. If they do cite a provision, was that provision approved at a Board meeting? Were those meeting minutes made public? Were you provided with an updated copy of whatever document is being cited (even posting on the HOA website is considered "providing a document")? These are all very important questions for you to ask. If there is an appropriate provision, and they provided you with the necessary documentation, and they can show they did, pay the fine and fix the violation. It's not worth pissing them off. Finally, be cautious if you think you don't want to pay the fine. Failing to pay a fine is a very dangerous proposition. I would never fail to pay a fine without already retaining a lawyer and discussing the situation with him/her. There is a great deal of risk in failing to pay a fine. If you accumulate enough fines, the HOA can turn your account over to their own attorney, who will not only continue to accumulate fines and late fees, but then their own attorney's fees as well. Once the dollar amount gets high enough, the HOA and their attorney can place a lien on your home until those fines are paid, and if they go unpaid for long enough, they can foreclose on that lien, sell your house out from under you, and use the proceeds of the sale to recoup the money they are owed. I STRONGLY suggest you talk to a real estate attorney in your local area who specializes in HOA law.
