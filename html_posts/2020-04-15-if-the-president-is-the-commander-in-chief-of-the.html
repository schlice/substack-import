**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If the President is the commander-in-chief of the US military, what is the role of the Secretary of Defense?** An excellent question! The role of Commander-in-Chief is defined by the Constitution, and the role of Secretary of Defense is defined by Federal law. According to the **Constitution**, the President is Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces and Chief Executive of the Federal government. According to **Federal law**, the Secretary of Defense is the "Principal Assistant to the President in all matters relating to the Department of Defense" and has **statutory authority** to lead the Department and all of its component agencies, *including military command authority second only to the President*.
