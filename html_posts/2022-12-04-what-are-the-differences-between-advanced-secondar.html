--- title: "What are the differences between advanced secondary progressive multiple sclerosis (MS) and primary progressive MS? Are there any ways to tell apart from symptoms alone?" date: "2022-12-04" tags: ["health-autoimmune"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The difference is in the name, not the symptoms. Primary Progressive MS (PPMS) is MS that begins its journey with worsening symptoms with no signs of remission. Secondary Progressive MS (SPMS) usually begins as Relapsing-Remitting MS (RRMS), but then eventually a relapse will occur and never remit, thus signaling the transition to *secondary* progressive MS. Without treatment, RRMS will transition to SPMS within about 10 years.
