--- title: '"Are on-duty police officers allowed to use drive-thru at fast food or coffee shops?"' date: '2017-02-13' tags: - law-enforcement layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is generally left to the discretion of the officer. Most departments do not expressly forbid officers from using drive-thru lanes. However, understand that if an officer is getting a meal, he is on a meal break, specifically released from receiving calls, and other officers are covering any calls received in his sector while he is away. So being stuck in the drive-thru line isn't an issue, since the officer will not be receiving calls during that time. Officers have to use far more discretion these days, as fast food can all too easily be intentionally contaminated: [[Ohio Officer Hospitalized After Finding Glass in His Food](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4bf3c4&url=http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2016/07/19/ohio-officer-hospitalized-glass-food/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="breitbart.com"}]{.qlink_container} This sort of behavior has become all too common for officers today. The only fast food that the officers I know will eat is the kind where they either know the owner of the restaurant **very** well, or they can watch as the meal is prepared. The fresh Mexican chains (Qdoba, Chipotle) are popular, as well as sandwich shop chains like Subway and Schlotzsky's where your sandwich is prepared while you watch. None of that will happen from a drive-thru, so you can expect to see far fewer officers utilizing the drive-thru.
