**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **All of the bloodshed today makes me cry, even though the use and permitting of guns allow violence to be at an all-time low in history. When will guns be permanently banned in America?** God-willing, guns will never be permanently banned in America. There are many reasons for this. Here are just a few: 1. People will always be violent with each other, because that is human nature. The presence or absence of guns does not change this. 2. Criminals, by their very nature, do not follow laws. It is already illegal to commit crime. The criminal who is breaking the law to commit a crime is really unconcerned that he breaks a second law by using a gun to do so. Banning guns does nothing to stop the criminal, it only affects the citizens who follow the law, leaving them less able to defend themselves. 3. Criminals will always find a way to get guns. I know that some people say that if we ban guns from law-abiding citizens' hands, then criminals won't be able to steal them. That's literally like telling the Kindergarten class that no one can play with toys because one bully likes to steal them. We don't prevent the criminal from committing a criminal act by turning law-abiding citizens into criminals. 4. Not all gun use is bad. Defensive gun uses far outnumber offensive ones. Anywhere from 350,000 to 2.5 million times per year, law-abiding citizens will use firearms to defend themselves, often without a single shot being fired. The difference in the numbers? You don't report a crime that never took place to the police. Therefore, we have little more than survey data to indicate exactly how much crime is prevented by guns. Using representative sampling, we can *estimate*, but we know that at a certain level (at least 350,000), these defensive uses did occur. It is likely much more. 5. This nation was founded on **freedom** and rugged individualism. These ideals still live on in Americans today. Our laws were structured around this freedom; namely the fact that **your security is your responsibility**. In fact, many Supreme Court cases have found that the police have no responsibility to defend you from crime. Their responsibility is to find those who break the law and bring them to justice, hold them accountable for their crimes. While they do what they can to prevent crime, they cannot be there to prevent all crime. There are more reasons than this, but these are just the basics. We could go into resistance to tyranny and the unalienable right to self-defense, but this is enough for now.
