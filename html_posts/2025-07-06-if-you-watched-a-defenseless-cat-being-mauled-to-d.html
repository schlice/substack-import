**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, under United States law, pets are not people, they are property. If one house caught another house on fire and you didn't grab a garden hose, do you get saddled with the cost to rebuild the house? As sad as it sounds when you talk about pets (because everyone loves their pets), that's the truth. Second, there is no duty to protect in US law. Let's get away from cats and dogs for a second and get to a place where we at least have a general idea about when we should help. Let's say you came upon a bad guy mercilessly beating a little girl, and you took out your phone and recorded the whole thing, including right up to the point where the little girl actually died. Would you be responsible for the little girl's death because you failed to stop it? No, you would not. Again, there is no "duty to protect" in the United States. You cannot be held responsible for failing to act (certain very specific instances notwithstanding).
