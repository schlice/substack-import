Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [There is no law that prevents anyone from saying something racist. In fact, racist statements are protected under the First Amendment.\ \ Anyone has the right to be an ass. They don\'t have the right to *discriminate* because they are an ass. But making a racist statement is legal.\ \ However, that said, an officer should never make such statements. Members of the public have more power to effect change in such a situation than other officers, and should be reporting such incidents to the police department by filing an official statement or visiting Internal Affairs in that department.\ \ It would be very hard for fellow officers to report other officers for making racist statements. An officer might casually approach the racist officer\'s supervisor and say something about how officer X should probably learn some tact. But beyond that, it\'s hard to turn someone in for being an ass.]{.rendered_qtext}
