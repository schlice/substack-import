**Content:** [There was a study done that showed that people with MS had lower levels of copper (and zinc) in their system. They even came up with a hypothesis for how the lower levels of metals could relate to MS. They hypothesized that because copper has an ameliorative effect against demyelination (copper helps to prevent fat cell damage, and myelin is a fatty acid), a lower level of the mineral in the bloodstream could lead to more damage of myelin during disease activity. Therefore, malabsorption of metals like copper and zinc could have a contributory effect on MS.\ \ There are no studies that I am aware of that have otherwise reviewed copper\'s relationship to MS.\ \ There are no studies that I am aware of that have focused specifically on Cu77 and its relationship to MS.\ \ Remember though that correlation does not equal causation. Just because a correlation was discovered between MS patients and lower levels of heavy metals does not mean that lower levels of heavy metals causes MS.]{.rendered_qtext} ***Disclaimer:*** ***This answer is not professional medical advice****, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
