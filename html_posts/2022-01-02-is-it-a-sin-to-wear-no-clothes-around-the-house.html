**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Let me try to give you a *complete* answer, or as complete as I can. Nakedness, on its own, is not a sin. The only reason we are clothed at all is due to Adam and Eve's sin in the first place. We were naked in the Garden until Adam sinned, and then the first blood shed in the Bible was God killing an animal to make clothes for them, because they were naked (just as they were before), except this time, they knew it. So it's not the nakedness that constitutes the sin. So then, what is it? In the Western world, nakedness is often associated with sex and sexuality. And this is the problem. Whether you are alone or with someone else in the house, nakedness is still associated with sexuality. Nakedness can lead others who visualize you to have impure thoughts, even if they have no intention of having them. They don't have to actively think about sex in order to experience the impure thoughts. Nakedness could even lead *you* to have your own impure thoughts, if you think about yourself naked or if you see yourself naked. Maybe you don't think like that, ever. Maybe your mind doesn't work that way. You're lucky. So while it is not directly a sin to simply be naked, nakedness could lead *others* to impure thoughts; and at worst, nakedness could lead *you* to impure thoughts. \ Original question: Is it a sin to wear no clothes around the house?
