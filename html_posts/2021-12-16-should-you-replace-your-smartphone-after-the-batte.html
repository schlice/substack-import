Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer is "it depends." Most modern smartphones have batteries which should last around 2 years under normal use, which happens to coincide with the average amount of time smartphone users hang on to any particular handset. So most people will just trade in or sell their old handsets and get new ones. You could consider replacing your battery. For Android devices, many repair shops will sell you a new battery and install it for you, and it will probably run you about \$100. Apple [[also has a service](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=934eaf&url=https://support.apple.com/iphone/repair/service/battery-power){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="apple.com"}]{.qlink_container} which will allow you to get your battery replaced. If your phone is covered by AppleCare+, battery replacements are free. If it is not under warranty, a battery replacement will cost you anywhere from \$49 to \$69. If you have an Android smartphone, you can potentially flash a new operating system on to your device when your battery starts to hold less charge. LineageOS is a good one, and they support many different Android devices. You can breathe new life into your phone, while getting better battery life out of a battery which may be headed downhill. Just remember that **doing so would void any warranty you had** (if you still had hardware warranty), so don't even consider doing this until after your hardware warranty expires. \ Original question: Should you replace your smartphone after the battery life decreases?
