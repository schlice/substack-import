**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it legal in Pennsylvania to record a phone call that does not include the other persons consent, BUT it does not record their voice? In other words, only my side of the conversation is audible.** *(NOTE: IANAL and this is not legal advice)* Since you are only recording yourself, you're not really recording a phone call. Recording a phone call implies that you record both sides to the conversation. So in my opinion, this recording would be just fine, as long as you cannot hear the other party on the recording. Do be aware however that Pennsylvania is an "all-party" state.
