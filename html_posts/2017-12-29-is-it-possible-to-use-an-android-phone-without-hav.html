**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it possible to use an Android phone without having a Gmail account?** Short answer is **yes.** The easiest way to do this is to **Reset Your Phone**, and when you've done that, when it asks you to sign in with your Google account, look for the "Skip" option. You won't be able to download apps from the Google Play Store, but you can use the Amazon Appstore if you like, and if you're on a Samsung Android phone, you can use your Samsung account to back up/restore your phone, as well as download apps from the Samsung App Store. The selection of apps is not as great as the Google Play Store, but you can get most apps that way. You can use the phone's built-in email client to access POP or IMAP email from your favorite email provider (obviously not Gmail). You can also use Microsoft Outlook (formerly Hotmail) using their Outlook client, which should be available in the Samsung App Store, among other places. Thankfully, Android also lets you download apps from *Unknown Sources* (i.e. the Internet) and any apps you can't get from the Google Play Store are usually available online somewhere. Note that you open yourself up to the possibility of downloading a hacked version of the same app, but dem's da breaks. If you really want to go crazy and totally remove all of the Google apps from your phone, you can use an app called **Package Disabler Pro,** which you can buy and download directly from [[their website](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=447716&url=http://www.packagedisabler.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="packagedisabler.com"}]{.qlink_container}. You can then disable all of the Google applications without having to root your phone.
