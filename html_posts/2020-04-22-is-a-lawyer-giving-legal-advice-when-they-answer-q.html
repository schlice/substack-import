**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is a lawyer giving legal advice when they answer questions someone has about a stranger\'s legal situation?** There is a difference between *legal advice* and *legal information.* Only lawyers are qualified to give legal advice. Non-lawyers can give legal information. Even if lawyers answer a question, it wouldn't be considered legal advice unless the answer would affect the rights and responsibilities of the person who asked the question. Actual legal advice requires research to determine how the law applies to that specific person, which requires an attorney-client relationship, which only licensed attorneys in that jurisdiction can create, and they certainly would not use open forum spaces to answer those questions. True legal advice comes from establishing an attorney-client relationship and then working through that relationship only. When anyone answers legal questions on forums such as this one, they are providing legal information, not legal advice. That's why when I answer questions like these, I always append a footer: \ *This answer is not a substitute for professional legal advice. This answer does not create an attorney-client relationship, nor is it a solicitation to offer legal advice. This answer is for GENERAL INFORMATIONAL PURPOSES ONLY. If you ignore this warning and convey confidential information in a private message or comment, there is no duty to keep that information confidential or forego representation adverse to your interests. Seek the advice of a licensed attorney in the appropriate jurisdiction before taking any action that may affect your rights. If you believe you have a claim against someone, consult an attorney immediately, otherwise there is a risk that the time allotted to bring your claim may expire.* *Quora users who provide responses to legal questions are intended third-party beneficiaries with certain rights under Quora\'s* [[*Terms of Service*](https://www.quora.com/about/tos)]{.qlink_container}*.* \ (I can't use the automatic footer because I already have it set for medical answers, which I also give) Basically, this footer reminds readers that this is not legal advice, just legal information. Quora does not allow anyone to give legal advice, and we want to make it abundantly clear that this is not what they are getting here. If you want legal advice, hire a lawyer.
