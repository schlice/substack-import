--- title: '"Are DNA tests a violation of the 5th Amendment?"' date: '2018-08-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are DNA tests a violation of the 5th Amendment?** No. DNA tests are not in and of themselves *testimony*. They are biometric data, not testimony. This difference between "testimonial evidence" and "non-testimonial evidence" means that you cannot be compelled to give statements against yourself, but you can be compelled to give fingerprints, pictures, blood samples, and DNA evidence, and these are not protected under the 5th Amendment as "testimony." Clearly, this only applies when you've been arrested for a serious crime, at which point DNA can be taken, depending on state law. If you're just a suspect and haven't yet been arrested and charged, you can't be compelled to give your DNA. The court would have to issue a "compulsion order" in order to require you to give your DNA.
