**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the president have to meet certain criteria to declare a national emergency?** No. The National Emergencies Act does not require that there be any criteria met before the President can declare a national emergency. Courts and Congress have generally deferred to the President when it comes to what constitutes a national emergency. > Beyond the limitations outlined in \[10 USC\] ยง 2808(a), the > \[National Emergencies Act\] does not provide any explicit criteria or > guidance for the president regarding what type of circumstances would > qualify as a "national emergency" for purposes of this construction > authority. Both the courts and Congress have typically been > deferential to presidential declarations of national emergencies. For > example, in the context of economic sanctions under the International > Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)---which was enacted in part to > limit the president's ability to declare unending national > emergencies---courts have generally deferred to the executive's > decisions regarding whether a particular foreign policy issue creates > a national emergency sufficient to invoke IEEPA's authorities, along > with the underlying factual determinations by the executive > branch.[\[1\]](#EYUql){#cite-EYUql .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-EYUql){.citation_index} [[Declaring an Emergency to Build a Border Wall: The Statutory Arguments](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=58ade2&url=https://www.lawfareblog.com/declaring-emergency-build-border-wall-statutory-arguments){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="lawfareblog.com"}]{.qlink_container}
