**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the vast majority of cases in the United States, no. There are very few laws which criminalize *inaction* when it comes to intervening. For most of US society, there is no *duty to protect* in the law. For some professions, there is indeed a duty to intervene, but it is only for certain professions (i.e. doctors, etc), but for the ordinary citizen, there is no duty to aid written into the law. \ Original question: If someone is in danger, and saving him/her doesn\'t pose any kind of danger to you, but still you chose not to help and then he/she died, is it a crime?
