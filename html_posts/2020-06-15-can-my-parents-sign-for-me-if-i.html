**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can my parents sign for me if I'm too young? I entered an art competition that requires someone over 18 to submit artwork, so my parents agreed to sign any contracts if I win. Is this a problem and could I get into legal trouble if they sign for me** No one here can answer this question for you, because no one here has seen the actual agreement your parents may sign. If that agreement says that "you must be 18 in order to enter the contest," your parents could claim that they produced the artwork, receive the prize, and no one would be the wiser. You would be violating the rules of the contest, and if you were found out, you would have to return any prizes you were awarded, and you could indeed face legal liability. You could get sued for the monetary value of the extra effort the contest organizers had to put in to track you down, get the prizes back, contact the first runner up, and get the prizes to them. Now, if the agreement says that "Someone over the age of 18 must sign, but anyone can produce the art" then you're fine. It is up to you to proceed how you see fit. I am not going to advise you how to proceed, other than you should follow the rules of the contest as they are written.
