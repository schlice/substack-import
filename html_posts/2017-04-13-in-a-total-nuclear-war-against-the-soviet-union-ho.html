--- title: '"In a total nuclear war against the Soviet Union, how high were the chances that NORAD in Cheyenne Mountain would be completely destroyed?"' date: '2017-04-13' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As we learned from the [[History Channel episode](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=c30842&url=http://www.history.com/shows/modern-marvels/season-3/episode-9){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="history.com"}]{.qlink_container} on us (as part of their *Modern Marvels* series), Cheyenne Mountain was never designed to take a direct hit from any nuclear weapon. We know that a direct hit with a kiloton-sized weapon (which was all that was available when the Mountain was built) would have at least collapsed all the tunnels, effectively destroying the facility, or at least rendering it completely inoperative. A direct hit by today's megaton-sized weapons would turn Cheyenne Mountain into Cheyenne Valley. We know the former Soviet Union was targeting all the military installations in Colorado, but NORAD was definitely high on the list. What we learned *after* the collapse of the Soviet Union was that the missiles that they *did* have (which were greatly exaggerated) would have been mostly inaccurate. There's a fair chance that a kiloton weapon would have *missed* the Mountain, a fully-survivable scenario. Megaton weapons? Less likely, but if it wasn't a direct hit, it *might* have been survivable. However, we never based any of our mission parameters around surviving an attack. We had the capability and the resources to do so, but our mission directives never specified that we had to survive an attack in order to complete our mission. For this reason, our entire mission is complete in under 10 minutes, from observance of missile launch to notification delivered to the National Command Authority. Missile flight time from the former Soviet Union to Cheyenne Mountain is about 30 minutes. We would continue providing updates on the missile to the NCA, continually narrowing the impact zone as the missile got closer to its target. We used to say that our job was done in 10 minutes, and you can spend the next 20 minutes bending over and kissing your ass goodbye. It's just something you live with when you work in a place like NORAD. If you've done your job correctly, you get to tell the President that you're about to die.
