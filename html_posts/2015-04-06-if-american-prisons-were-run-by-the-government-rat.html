--- title: '"If American prisons were run by the government rather than by private institutions, would the prison population decline?"' date: '2015-04-06' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [The law does not allow sentencing guidelines to be dictated or influenced by the prison system. The prison system is not a party to the decisions made by judges or juries when determining sentencing.\ \ And most prisons in the United States are run by the government. There are some prisons which take over the sentences of certain long-term prisoners, and they do this under contract with a state. The terms of the contract determine which prisoners get sent to private prisons, not decisions by judges or juries.\ \ If you feel that private prisons create more prisoners, feel free to lobby your state\'s lawmakers and repeal the law which allows for private prisons in your state.]{.rendered_qtext}
