**Content:** [In order to have your anonymity removed, the court has to know who it is issuing its subpoena for. Therefore, the subpoena can\'t be for \"that anonymous user\" because it has to be written against an individual.\ \ That said, the court has issued \"[[Doe subpoena](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=b1870b&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doe_subpoena){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container}s\" in order to determine the identity of anonymous posters. Quora will reveal your identity to law enforcement should it be requested through a Doe subpoena.\ \ Try it. Make an anonymous post and click Submit. The pop-up that comes up explains that while your anonymity is protected, Quora can figure out who you are. Therefore, if they are technically able, and they comply with court-ordered disclosure, they will do so if they are ordered to.\ \ If the service does not know who you are (i.e. an anonymous blog comment), the Doe subpoena can get your IP address which can then be resolved to a user through the ISP. The Doe subpoena can allow the disclosure of this information.]{.rendered_qtext}
