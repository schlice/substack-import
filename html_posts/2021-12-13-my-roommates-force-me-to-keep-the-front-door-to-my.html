**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It is unreasonable of them for you to be unsafe in the apartment simply because they feel inconvenienced by having to reach for a key. It's also unreasonable for you to have to leave the apartment unlocked, even when you leave, because they feel inconvenienced by having to remember to take their keys. But there is a simple and inexpensive solution. Find out if your apartment will allow you to install a keypad lock. I would suggest one that incorporates *both* a key and a keypad. Something like this: [[https://www.amazon.com/AmazonBas\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=cb335d&url=https://www.amazon.com/AmazonBasics-Traditional-Electronic-Keypad-Deadbolt/dp/B07J4VLVPF){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6ea134914effcad7d37532f43fb9174c){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6ea134914effcad7d37532f43fb9174c" master_w="290" master_h="405"} ::: :::: The reason I linked this one is that it is *cheap*. It's less than \$50. Note that it doesn't have all the whiz-bang smart features that more expensive keypad locks have, but it achieves the purpose you're looking for: your roommates will never be locked out again. This particular lock also features an *auto-lock* function, customizable from 10--99 seconds after it is opened. So *you* don't have to worry about whether or not *you* locked the door. And if everyone has a code, it doesn't matter if it is locked or unlocked. Additionally, you can give the landlord one of the new keys and they won't need to remember a code, so it shouldn't inconvenience your landlord at all. Just keep all the old hardware (and the old keys) so you can reinstall their old lock when you leave. The last complex I lived in allowed us to install locks like this, so long as we gave the landlord a copy of the new key, and we put it back the way we found it when we left. Of course, you would be responsible for any damage to the door you cause by doing this, but that goes without saying. Just be careful and you'll be just fine. Finally, if this is something that is really important to your roommates, they'll happily chip in on the cost. For about \$15 a piece if there are at least three of you, you can eliminate the problem of lost or forgotten keys completely. And if your complex doesn't allow you to change the door lock, start locking the door anyway. Letting them get locked out a few times will certainly help them to remember their keys next time. \ Original question: My roommates force me to keep the front door to my apartment unlocked all day, every day. They say it's because they forget their keys or don't want to reach into their pockets to grab the keys. What should I do?
