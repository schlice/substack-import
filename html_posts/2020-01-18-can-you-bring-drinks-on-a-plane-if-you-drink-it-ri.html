**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can you bring drinks on a plane if you drink it right in front of the TSA agent\'s face to prove that it\'s a drink?** First of all, the problem isn't bringing drinks on a plane. The problem is bringing drinks through the security checkpoint. You can bring drinks on a plane; you just have to buy them inside the sterile area. Wanna hear the cool logic about why not? If I'm a suicide bomber, and I want to use a liquid bomb, I'll be happy to drink some of the liquid in front of you. Because I'm going to die in a horrible mid-air explosion anyway. So what's a little bit of poison before I die? If I'm truly committed to the cause, I'd be *happy* to drink poison in front of you. So that's the logic. But here's the good news: you can bring *empty bottles* through the security checkpoint and fill them once you get inside. Many large airports have "bottle stations" which deliver filtered water straight into your bottle. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-ce5bdc02b2e265e47c665a57ac5721c6){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-ce5bdc02b2e265e47c665a57ac5721c6" master_w="374" master_h="653"} ::: :::: Some even have integrated drinking stations built in to them: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-83a57b6a0db2e5e52455c225e5970bfd){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-83a57b6a0db2e5e52455c225e5970bfd" master_w="319" master_h="510"} ::: :::: So you can still get water without having to bring it through security.
