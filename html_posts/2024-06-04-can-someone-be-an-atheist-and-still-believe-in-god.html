**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > **Can someone be an atheist and still believe in God?** No. The word "atheist" means "no god." Atheists affirmatively believe that God does not exist. Some confused atheists say that it's not a belief that God does not exist, it's a lack of belief that God exists. Besides this being nothing more than semantics, *lacking* a belief is called **agnosticism**. Having an affirmative belief that God does not exist is **atheism**. There is a difference. > **Have there been any cases of believers becoming atheists?** Happens all the time. In fact, I bet the largest majority of atheists were once believers, but they were turned off of religion because of interactions with others in their church, or interactions or behaviors of other believers. While the logic of this is questionable¹, it does happen, and I posit it happens quite frequently. \ ¹ Would you stop going to the gym because of the poor behavior of other people working out? You might, but you'd probably complain to management first. How many of these ex-believers do you think complained to management before they left? I'm certain some did.
