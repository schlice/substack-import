**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is multiple sclerosis contagious by sex /saliva /blood?** No, multiple sclerosis is not contagious. You can't "catch" MS. There are several factors which go into determining your risk for MS. Age, sex, ethnicity, geography of birth, prevalence in your first-degree relatives, and exposure to an as-of-yet unidentified infectious trigger are all factors which can increase your risk of developing MS. Exposure to a person with MS is **not** one of them. We're so convinced that MS is not contagious, that eleven years ago, the Red Cross lifted its ban on MS patients donating blood. [[People with MS can now give blood at the Red Cross](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fc89ac&url=https://secure.nationalmssociety.org/site/SPageServer/%253Bjsessionid%3DDF09D5EA8D68BF2CA036C48F00BFB260.app354a?pagename%3DNTH_programs_giveblood){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container} > *Until recently, people with multiple sclerosis (MS) were not > permitted to donate blood at American Red Cross donation centers. The > ban has now been lifted because no scientific evidence exists that MS > is contagious or can be transmitted via blood.*
