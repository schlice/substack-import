**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First, there isn't an "original version of the Bible." The Bible is actually a compendium of 66 books, written over the course of 1500 years in three languages on three different continents. Second, in the vast majority of cases, "churches" have not altered the Bible.ยน But "translation" between languages involves altering some words, out of necessity. For example, Hebrew has fewer words than English. So some words in Hebrew have multiple meanings. The translator must determine which meaning was intended by the original author in order to choose the correct English word. Greek has the opposite problem where, for example, there are no fewer than six words in Greek which mean various forms of "love" (i.e. parental love, platonic love, love between close friends, erotic love, etc), yet in English, we just have "love." Translation, therefore, involves the alteration of words in order to convey the correct meaning. Bible translations can range from "word for word" translations (which accurately translate the words but the meaning may be more difficult to understand) to "thought for thought" translations (where the translator reads an entire sentence in the original language and then writes it in the new language, trying to retain as much of the original words and meaning as possible), or "paraphrase" translations (where an entire paragraph is read and is re-stated without fidelity to the original words whatsoever, but tries to retain the meaning of what was originally written). Generally, as you get towards the "paraphrase" side of the spectrum, the translations are easier to read and understand, but hold less fidelity to the original words, where as you get closer to the "word for word" side, the original words are retained, but you may not get the original author's meaning. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-6318b071f7b5cf5baf42863acee2fb10){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-6318b071f7b5cf5baf42863acee2fb10" master_w="730" master_h="417"} ::: :::: \ ยน The one notable exception is the New World Translation, translated by the Watchtower Bible and Tract Society, which is the first intentional, systematic effort at producing a complete version of the Bible that is edited and revised for the specific purpose of agreeing with their religious doctrine. The Jehovah's Witnesses and the Watchtower Society realized that their beliefs contradicted the Bible. So, rather than conforming their beliefs to the Bible, they altered the Bible to agree with their beliefs.
