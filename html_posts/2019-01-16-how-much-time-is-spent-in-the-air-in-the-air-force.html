**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Hi [[Emily](https://www.quora.com/profile/Emily-Gleave)]{.qlink_container}, thanks for the A2A. However, I don't have anything more to add other than what [[David Saylock](https://www.quora.com/profile/David-Saylock)]{.qlink_container} already said. How much time you spend in the air depends entirely on your AFSC. This is true for officers as well as enlisted personnel. However, even some AFSCs that wouldn't normally be considered flying AFSCs can have some airborne components. For example, as a Communications officer, most of my assignments would be in a ground station, but some jobs could be as part of an aircrew (such as on an airborne command center or on an airborne electronic warfare or surveillance platform). The majority of jobs in the Air Force are *support* jobs. They support the Air Force mission: Fly, Fight, and Win. Unless you're specifically in an aircrew, or in an airborne assignment, your time will be on the ground (unless you're travelling someplace).
