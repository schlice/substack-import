**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **The more time you spend in the Word and in communion with Him, the less likely you will be to sin.** There really isn't a more complex way to put it. As you grow in your faith, those desires for sinful acts will fade. It's not like a light switch; they don't just turn off. But they do fade. The love of God overpowers the lust for sin, every time. The process may be gradual, but it is continuous. It's part of what we call *sanctification*, the gradual process God uses to improve believers over time. In other words, *Don't worry, it gets better.* \ Original question: How does a person\'s spiritual health impact their tendency towards certain sins?
