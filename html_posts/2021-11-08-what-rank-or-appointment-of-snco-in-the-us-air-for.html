**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(NOTE: Any gender pronouns used in the answer are only for ease of illustration: TSgts can be any gender)* **Technical Sergeant (TSgt) - E-6** It's actually not five chevrons. It's three *stripes* and two *rockers*, for a total of 5 lines. The rank insignia looks like this: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-62fa69cfa3ccb52e4aef9e276e6f36e7){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-62fa69cfa3ccb52e4aef9e276e6f36e7" master_w="800" master_h="752"} ::: :::: This position is a senior Non-Commissioned Officer (NCO). He or she is technically proficient, and has been working at his job for several years. The average time in service before a Staff Sergeant is promoted to Technical Sergeant is 12 years. The TSgt is usually a shift lead or a shift supervisor, with several lower-grade Airmen and NCOs who work for him. The next rank above this is Master Sergeant (MSgt), and the rank insignia would add a single *chevron* over the top: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-78a669398975c2084a3f0dfe06610331){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-78a669398975c2084a3f0dfe06610331" master_w="440" master_h="599"} ::: :::: \ Original question: What rank or appointment of SNCO in the US Air Force wears five chevrons, point down with a star superimposed in the middle of the chevrons?
