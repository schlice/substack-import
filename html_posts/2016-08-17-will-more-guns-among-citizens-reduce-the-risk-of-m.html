--- title: "Will more guns among citizens reduce the risk of mass shootings?" date: "2016-08-17" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Short answer: no. What more guns among citizens ***won't*** do is ***increase*** the risk of mass shootings. The converse (more guns reduces risk of mass shootings) is not always true. It may be possible that one armed person might be able to offer some level of resistance to a mass shooter when they were expecting none. This may dissuade the shooter from continuing. *But remember, this is not why citizens are armed.* ***This is why the police are armed****.* Citizens are armed so that they can defend **themselves** or their families. Citizens are ***not*** armed to be quasi-police and try to take on every moron with a gun. It might be nice if an armed individual used his training to try to stop a mass shooter, but it is by no means a requirement. More power to him if he believes in his training enough to try to intervene, but we are not asking this of concealed carry holders.
