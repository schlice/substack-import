**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What do you say is the beginning of personhood?** Personhood begins at conception. The logic follows this way: 1. Embryos and fetuses are **human** beings from conception. "Human being" is nothing more than the philosophical term for an individual member of the species *homo sapiens*. 2. A baby is a living human organism. It grows, its cells divide, its cells respirate, and it is a unique life form separate from its mother (its DNA is unique from every other organism in existence). In other words, it is **alive**. 3. **Personhood** is defined as being **human** and being **alive**. 4. Since embryos are both alive and human from the moment of conception, they are, by definition, **persons**. 5. Therefore, unborn children are persons from conception.
