**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} If you are referring to H.B. 2 in North Carolina, or Mississippi\'s related bill, the answer is that the business can choose to run its business the same way it always has: biological men use the men\'s room, biological women use the women\'s room. Or it can choose to change how it runs its business, allowing people who identify as something other than their biological sex to use whichever restroom they feel comfortable in. Or it can say that private or public restrooms are available for anyone, and not specify a reason to use one or the other. And people can frequent whichever businesses meet their needs and whichever businesses are accommodating in the way they feel a business should accommodate, and the free market will decide which businesses succeed and which businesses fail. So to answer your question, the most abhorrent denial of service that could occur due to the latest religious freedom acts is that an LGBT customer may be forced to use the restroom of their biological sex because a business may choose to not accommodate them, and the state will not force them to act otherwise. The business may choose to keep everything as is, allow people to use facilities based on gender identity, or provide public and private facilities. The bills simply say that government cannot tell businesses how to operate. A business is free to operate based on its own conscience, and the free market will determine whether or not that business remains viable after that decision. In North Carolina, the entire legislature, the governor, and over 70% of the state voted for that legislation. And despite all the threats by big businesses and sports teams, you still manage to see businesses advertising during the NCAA Championship in Houston, even after Houston voted in similar legislation. You didn\'t see the NCAA Championship games moved out of Houston. (EDIT) The other thing that I find interesting is that these businesses which claim to be exercising their conscience rights by not doing business in locations like North Carolina or Mississippi haven\'t adopted ANY of the very same policies that they are boycotting in the states. Does PayPal allow transgender people to choose restrooms based on their perceived sex in their corporate offices? Does the NBA allow transgender players to use whatever showers they choose? (hint: the answer starts with n and ends with o). And PayPal is probably the biggest hypocrite in the bunch, as it is an international corporation which does business with **** *no fewer than 25 countries where homosexual behavior is illegal,* ***and 5 where the penalty for such behavior is death.*** But they are objecting to North Carolina overturning misguided legislation about letting men use women\'s showers? North Carolina hasn't outlawed homosexuality or discriminated against anyone. It hasn't even barred men from women's restrooms. All it did was guarantee that businesses have the freedom to set those policies themselves! If Target\'s announcement that it will allow transgender people into any restroom they choose, and the associated backlash they have received (almost a half a million consumers signing the pledge to never shop at Target again), you can tell that policies designed to appease 0.3% of the population might potentially be unpopular with much of the other 99.7%.
