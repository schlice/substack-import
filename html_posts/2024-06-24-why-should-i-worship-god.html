**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > ***Original question details:*** > > *Okay, today I have a lot of things to do: water my plants, do my > laundry, read up for an exam, help my neighbour, clean the place, etc. > Why should I add \"worship God\" to the list? What are the > consequences if I keep forgetting to do it or keep postponing to do > it? Will God favor me more if I worship or if I do LOTS and LOTS of > it? Please, tell me the importance of worship, because honestly I > already have a lot of things to do.* > > *Update: Sorry if the last bit made my question seem rhetorical. My > point is: Great thinkers and great men, both present and past, worship > (and some, devotedly), so there must be great reasons to do it. What > are they?* Let's talk first about why you shouldn't worship God. - You shouldn't worship God because you think it will buy you entrance into heaven. - You shouldn't worship God because you think He demands it. - You shouldn't worship God because you think He needs worship. - You shouldn't worship God because you feel like you're being forced to do so. If you believe any of those things, you have deeper issues than just "should I worship God." **We worship God because He is worthy of worship.** That's it. Nothing more, nothing less. We worship God because we love Him and we actively choose to give Him the worship He deserves. So if you read that last sentence and asked yourself, "what has God done that gives Him the right to be worshipped," then *that's where the problem lies*. You don't know what God has done for you. So the problem isn't one of understanding why you should worship God. That's just a symptom of your underlying problem, which is understanding what God has done for you which is deserving of worship.
