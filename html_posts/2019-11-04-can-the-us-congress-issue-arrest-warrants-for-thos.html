**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can the US Congress issue arrest warrants for those who ignore subpoenas and refuse to testify during the Trump impeachment proceedings?** Not directly, but there is a process by which they can have offenders arrested. Historically, Congress has used its own authority to arrest those in contempt of Congress, though very infrequently. Today, a Congressional subcommittee seeking to enforce a subpoena would deliver that subpoena to the US Attorney's Office (usually the one in DC is used) and a hearing would be scheduled. Attorneys from both sides would be heard, and the judge would rule first on the validity and enforce-ability of the subpoena, and then on whether or not the respondent can be compelled to comply with the subpoena. After a favorable ruling, the respondent will have a certain amount of time to comply. Failure to comply at that point could result in a bench warrant.
