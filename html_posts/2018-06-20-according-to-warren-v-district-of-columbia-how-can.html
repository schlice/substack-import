**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **According to Warren v. District of Columbia, how can I protect myself when the police won\'t if guns are banned?** According to another decision, *District of Columbia v. Heller*, 554 U.S. 570 (2008), the gun ban was unconstitutional, so you can protect yourself with a firearm in your home in the District of Columbia. You should check city laws to see how exactly this shook out in practice. To be honest, I'm unsure how it ended up, but we do know that the gun ban for guns in the home was unconstitutional. *Warren* held that the police are not under any legal obligation to protect **you** personally. Police have a general responsibility to protect *the public at large*, they just don't have a specific responsibility to protect you. That means that ultimately, you are responsible for your own personal safety. Within the confines of the law, you should ensure that you are sufficiently protected, and that you have taken steps to protect yourself and your family. What you choose to do and what you are legally permitted to do will vary wildly. Please talk to a lawyer in your area to find out what is legal and what is not.
