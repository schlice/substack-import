Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Yes, Congress can request that persons be incarcerated, as the other answers have stated. However, this authority does have limits. Congress must demonstrate that the testimony of the person in question will aid in its ability to create laws. If not, the original contempt charge would be invalid.
