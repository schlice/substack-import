Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it not undue search to systematically fingerprint and photograph American citizens upon entry to the USA?** Why would you want to photograph and fingerprint American citizens who enter the USA? They already have passports (which imply fingerprints) and photographs in those passports. Sounds like an unnecessary extra step when the information is already there. To the legality of the operation (should you decide to waste the money to do it), it should be legal. The appearance of your face is not protected information. Your fingerprints are also not PII (Personally Identifying Information) like your Social Security Number. You willingly leave your fingerprints everywhere you go, so if they are protected, you're doing a bad job at protecting them (unless you wear latex gloves all day which is just weird unless you're a doctor). But why would you do this to American citizens in Customs while they are returning to the USA? It simply makes no sense. There's no good reason to do it when the information is already on file.
