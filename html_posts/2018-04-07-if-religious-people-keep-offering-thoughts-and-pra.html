**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If religious people keep offering thoughts and prayers after school shootings, will the continuation of school shootings eventually lead them to believe there is no god?** So it sounds to me like you're asking whether or not unanswered prayers means there is no God. When you were a kid, and you asked your parents for that one piece of candy that you really really wanted, and you didn't get it, did you tell yourself that there are no parents? Or did you instead come to the realization that parents are not a vending machine? You can't put in a good word and get out a piece of candy. From your parents, you get what you need, when you need it, and you don't get what you don't need. And they don't always tell you why. Sometimes, they just give you what you need, when you need it, and don't give you things you don't need, without explanation. Do you feel like God is a vending machine? Do you pray to him, and when you don't get what you want, do you feel like the vending machine is broken? Or that it doesn't exist at all? Isn't it possible that God, instead of simply "not giving you what you want," has told you **no**? *But why would He want school shootings to continue? That doesn't make sense!* In truth, it doesn't make sense *to us.* > "For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my > ways," declares the Lord. "As the heavens are higher than the earth, > so are my ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than your > thoughts" (Isaiah 55:8--9). Do you think you're smart enough to understand God's plan in all of this? I know I'm certainly not, and not because I'm not a smart person, but because I'm a *person*. God's thoughts are so far above mine that I simply wouldn't be able to comprehend why He is doing what He is. And I know that there is a plan in what he's doing. "'For I know the plans I have for you,' declares the Lord, 'plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.'" (Jeremiah 29:11). He has a plan, even though He chooses not to tell us. Solomon wrote in Proverbs 25:2, "It is the glory of God to conceal a matter..." Isaiah 45:15 says, "Truly, you are a God who hides himself." Ecclesiastes 11:5 proclaims, "As you do not know the path of the wind, or how the body is formed in a mother's womb, so you cannot understand the work of God, the Maker of all things." To be fair, it's not my place to understand, either. I let God handle God's business, while I handle mine. I don't look at God and say, "Why aren't you doing what ***I*** want?" How impetuous of us if we think like this! In fact, I'm certain that God is very sad when he sees a school shooting. But I do know that everything He allows to happen is part of His plan. And don't think for a second that He didn't allow this stuff to happen. So therefore, it must be part of His plan. Where did you get this idea that God would "skip along in front of us with His great cosmic broom, sweeping aside each trial and every troubling uncertainty? To the contrary, Jesus told His disciples that they should anticipate suffering. He said, "I have told you these things, so that in me you may have peace. In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world" (John 16:33)." [[\[1\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6ede2d&url=https://books.google.com/books?id%3DuWqQ8myx5MoC){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container} Where did this idea come from that if God isn't acting the way we expect Him to, that we can safely ignore Him? It's almost blasphemous to think such a thing! Dr. Dobson goes on to say, "My concern is that many believers apparently feel God owes them smooth sailing or at least a full explanation (and perhaps an apology) for the hardships they encounter. We must never forget that He, after all, is God. He is majestic and holy and sovereign. He is accountable to no one. He is not an errand boy who chases the assignments we dole out. He is not a genie who pops out of the bottle to satisfy our whims. He is not our servant---we are His." [[\[2\]](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6ede2d&url=https://books.google.com/books?id%3DuWqQ8myx5MoC){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container} So instead of praying for fewer trials, perhaps we should pray to be stronger in the face of them, knowing that He has a plan for us, and while we may not understand it, we know He keeps his promises. > Do not pray for easy lives. Pray to be stronger men. Do not pray for > tasks equal to your powers. Pray for powers equal to your tasks. Then > the doing of your work shall be no miracle, but you shall be the > miracle. --- Phillips Brooks
