**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The simple fact is that, as of yet, no one has produced any amount of credible, actionable evidence that Donald Trump has done anything illegal. "Collusion" is not illegal. It may be unethical, but it's not illegal, and it doesn't rise to the level of "high crimes and misdemeanors" required to start impeachment proceedings. As for the other things being bandied about regarding both parties: - Former FBI Director James Comey [[detailed](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=03e17f&url=http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/437479/fbi-rewrites-federal-law-let-hillary-hook){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalreview.com"}]{.qlink_container} Hillary Clinton's illegal actions as related to her use of an unclassified email server to transmit classified data in violation of the Espionage Act (18 USC section 793(f)). This, of course, was while she was at the State Department, before she ran for president. Interestingly, the FBI decided to rewrite the Espionage Act on the fly, inserting an intent clause where there was none. This violation of law has yet to be taken up by the Justice Department. It is currently unknown whether or not they will ever look at it. - The DNC is also accused of "rigging" the primary in favor of Hillary Clinton and against Bernie Sanders. While this is also a violation of our public trust and possibly what we would call "collusion," again, it is not illegal. - In a highly-publicized meeting, Donald Trump, Jr. met with a Russian attorney, who had ties directly to Vladimir Putin, in order to receive what he was told was "opposition research" against Hillary Clinton. While that "collusion" word comes up again, it is still not illegal. By the way, no opposition research was actually received during that meeting, and no money changed hands. - Hillary's campaign also paid for "opposition research" against Donald Trump. Hillary's team paid an attorney who paid an opposition research firm, Fusion GPS, to produce a "dossier" on Trump which, as it turns out, was mostly false. Again, opposition research is not illegal, but the payment from Hillary's campaign to Fusion GPS through their lawyers may have been a violation of campaign finance laws.
