**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Tasers are "less-than-lethal" devices. On most healthy individuals, when employed correctly, they do not cause any serious or lasting harm. But some people, especially those with pre-existing medical conditions, can have those conditions exacerbated by the Taser, and in some cases, people have died from its use. That's not a reason to stop using it. Think about it. You're advocating removing a "less-than-lethal" force implement, and replacing it with...? Nothing? Meaning that if the police must escalate their level of force above pepper spray, they move to *lethal force* which is called that because it is designed to kill. Removing something from the force continuum and not replacing it with anything is never a smart idea. Leaving officers with fewer options will only result in **more** deaths, not **less**. \ Original question: Should police departments stop using \'stun guns\' as non-lethal deterrents due to the deaths caused by them?
