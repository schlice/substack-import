**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In California, can HOA be liable for damage caused by security to property left (against the rules) in the common area?** In general, a homeowner's association is not liable for the individual actions of anyone, even their contracted security. In general, only the security contractor would be liable for any damage to property they cause. The leaving of the property in a common area does not negate any liability of any person. What it *may* do is limit the amount you may be awarded, since the property was left in the common area in violation of the HOA rules. In your suit, you would have to establish that the security contractor actually was the person who damaged the property, and then you would have to establish that the contractor did so negligently, intentionally, or maliciously. If you cannot establish that the security contractor damaged the property, your case is probably over before it starts. So hopefully you have some video evidence that shows the contractor damaging the property. However, it is a common area, which implies that the security contractor is not the only person with access to the area. If anyone else has access to the common area (even if they weren't expected to be in the area at that time), you probably haven't firmly established that it was the security contractor who damaged the property. Moral of the story: don't leave your property in the common area when you are not permitted to do so.
