**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Because having an opinion isn't illegal (yet). Aiding and abetting (thankfully) has an *actual legal definition* which must be satisfied in order to charge someone with this crime. In general, *aiding and abetting can be charged when someone helps someone else commit a crime.* Now be aware, laws vary by state, but this is a pretty solid definition: > A person charged with these accomplice crimes is usually not present > when the crime itself is committed, but he or she has knowledge of the > crime before or after the fact, and may assist in its commission > through advice, actions, or financial support. Depending on the degree > of involvement, the offender\'s participation in the crime may rise to > the level of conspiracy.[\[1\]](#mwpkX){#cite-mwpkX .citation_link} So just looking at the general definition of the crime of aiding and abetting, you can probably see that a *supporter of a political party* is not "aiding and abetting a criminal" simply by supporting a political party or position. In order to be charged with the crime, a person had to: - know that the crime was going to take place - assist with the crime, usually through some action (and giving money is considered action). Giving advice on how to commit the crime is also covered. Anyone who knew or aided the protesters in unlawfully entering federal property could be charged with aiding and abetting in the crime of unlawfully entering federal property.
