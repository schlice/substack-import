**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you are arrested or simply held and are innocent, should you still refuse to speak to anyone without an attorney?** Yes. Let's shorten your answer: **If you are arrested, you should refuse to speak to anyone without an attorney.** That's what they get for arresting you. - The process is not designed to "let you go" after you are arrested. It is designed to "send you to jail" for the crime they are accusing you of. - You are not equipped to navigate this process on your own. No disrespect to your level of life knowledge, but this process is *highly* slanted against you. - The police are not your friends. They are doing a job. That job is sending you to jail in the most expeditious way possible. And by the way, they can't "simply hold you" for more than 72 hours without charging you with a crime.
