**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why is the US Constitution so seldom used as a model by other nations?** Because *a Constitution cannot create democracy.* Democracy must first exist among the people, and the people have to be prepared to be the final arbiter of governmental overreach. In other words, a government which is subservient to the will of the people must both exist and be able to be checked by the will of the people. In far too many cases, a constitution is drafted following US constitutional ideals, but the requisite will of the people to keep the government in check is simply not there to the degree necessary to actually do so. According to research by the [[The University of Chicago Law School](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=5bee7c&url=https://www.law.uchicago.edu/news/lifespan-written-constitutions){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="uchicago.edu"}]{.qlink_container}, the average age of a constitution in the wild is only 17 years.
