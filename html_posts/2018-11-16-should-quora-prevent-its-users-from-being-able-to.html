--- title: '"Should Quora prevent its users from being able to disallow comments to their answers?"' date: '2018-11-16' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Should Quora prevent its users from being able to disallow comments to their answers?** No. Despite what you all may think about the value of being able to question someone about their answer, there is one overriding point which remains about Quora: **Quora is not a debate site. Quora is a question-and-answer site.** If you don't like an answer, you are free to do several things: - You may downvote the answer if it is a low-quality answer unsupported by fact. Quora's upvote system is often misunderstood; I'll get there later. - If the answer is actually wrong, you can Report the answer as "Factually Incorrect." - If it's merely an opinion, you are free to answer the question yourself, and cite your own opinion. However, on Quora, someone asks a question, and someone answers it. As far as *ownership* goes, when I write an answer, it's my answer. I own the answer. If I choose to allow comments to my answer, I own the comments on my answer as well. In my case, if I feel a comment adds to the value of my answer, I leave it. If I feel it is unnecessarily argumentative, doesn't add any value to my answer, and even if I'm just not in the mood to argue, I will absolutely delete that comment, first advising the commenter that if he/she has a different opinion, they are more than welcome to answer the question themselves, but I'm just not in the mood to debate. If you think that all answers must be debated (and that all users should be free to debate all answers), then you not only must allow all comments, but you must also *force the original answerer to respond to any and all criticisms on their answers*, and that's not how Quora is designed. The other thing that often gets misunderstood about Quora: **Quora's upvote button is often confused with Facebook's "Like" button.** In the era of Facebook, the Upvote button is often confused with Facebook's "Like" button: you like things you agree with, and you see more of them in your Feed. However, in Quora, upvotes are designed to highlight *high-quality answers*, ***not answers with which you agree (or "like").*** I often upvote great answers from people with whose viewpoints I diametrically oppose. But their answers are well-sourced and are great arguments, so I upvote them. I don't agree with them, but I upvote them. But back to comments on answers: *it's my answer*. The comments on my answer are mine, and are under my control. I can even turn off comments completely on my answers. I know of quite a few TWs who have turned off comments completely, just because they're tired of the constant hate. They have that right. You do not have the right to comment on someone's answer.
