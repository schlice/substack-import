--- title: "Can a homeowner who is not on the board of directors or management company sue their homeowner\" date: "2022-09-12" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law", "homeowners-assn"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal questions, the answer is "it depends." Some HOAs have in their Governing Documents binding arbitration clauses. If yours does, then as a homeowner, you can sue, but you will have to go to binding arbitration rather than court. And since lawsuits are usually between a homeowner and the HOA (of which Board members and the management company are a part), it doesn't make sense that only the Board or the management company could sue the HOA.
