**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I would make only one correction to your answer. You said: *Politicians, especially those granted great power are supposed to be suitable for their roles.* In short, no they are not. If they were "supposed to be suitable," there would be a test for suitability built into the Constitution. In fact, it is quite the opposite. The requirements to become President are: - A natural born citizen - at least 35 years old - and has lived in the United States for at least fourteen years. Other than that, there are no other requirements for being President, and that includes any suitability requirement. What this means is that suitability is *subjective* and is left up to the voters. The voters have determined that Trump is suitable enough for the job (or that his opponent was wholly unsuitable, depending on your viewpoint) that they elected him.
