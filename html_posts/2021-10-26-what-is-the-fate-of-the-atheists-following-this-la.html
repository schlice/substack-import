**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is more specifically related to Catholic churches, as it is written dogma for them. This phrase was first written by Saint Cyprian of Carthage, a 3rd Century bishop. The phrase is generally accepted as truth in those churches, namely that the church is necessary for salvation. Catholic churches and Eastern Orthodox churches believe that outside the church there is no salvation, because salvation is the church. They also acknowledge, however, that not all in the church will be saved. But those *not* in the church (i.e. atheists) will definitely not be saved. Protestant denominations do not hold this doctrine quite as strongly, as they debate about what is the meaning of "the church" and what it means to be "outside" of it. \ Original question: What is the fate of the atheists, following this Latin phrase \"extra Ecclesiam nulla salus\" which means \"outside the Church there is no salvation\"?
