**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How many of those with Multiple sclerosis receiving disease modifying meds have had their ordering physician also order a Covid test to clear them before being given the meds that will drastically reduce their immunity?** I've never heard of this, but it makes sense. Before you start any new MS medication, you receive a battery of tests to ensure you are not infected with any specific diseases, and that certain other things specific to that medication are not out of whack. For instance, liver function tests are common when starting a new MS medication. White blood cell count is closely monitored for some meds, so getting a baseline before starting the medication is important. In order for COVID-19 vaccines to work, you have to allow your immune system to respond to the vaccine. Immunosuppressant medications, often used to treat MS, may interfere with that process, and may make the vaccine less efficacious for you. This is why MS patients may have to stop their medications before they get the vaccine, and then re-start their medication after their immunity builds up. Of course, this is highly dependent on your specific MS medication, and your doctor would advise you whether or not you should do this. I REPEAT: **DO NOT STOP YOUR MS MEDICATION UNLESS YOUR DOCTOR TELLS YOU TO DO SO.**
