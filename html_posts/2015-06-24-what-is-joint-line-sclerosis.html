Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Joint-line sclerosis is actually called **subchondral sclerosis**. It is a thickening, or an increas in bone density, in the subchondral layer. It shows up as a thick white bone along the joint line.\ \ Subchondral sclerosis often accompanies progressive osteoarthritis.]{.rendered_qtext}
