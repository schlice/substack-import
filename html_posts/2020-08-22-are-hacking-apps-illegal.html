**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Are hacking apps illegal?** We've had this controversy for decades. Does no one remember *The Anarchist Cookbook?* [[The Anarchist Cookbook: Powell, William: 9781607966128: Amazon.com: Books](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=6cd00d&url=https://www.amazon.com/Anarchist-Cookbook-William-Powell/dp/1607966123){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} As you can see, it's still freely available on Amazon for purchase. So why are we discussing this in a question about hacking apps? The apps themselves are not illegal. Creating an app which can hack other things is an exercise in programming, nothing more. ***Using that app could be illegal***. It could be legal if you use it against your own systems. It could be legal if you use it against a system you were asked to analyze for security holes. But it would probably be illegal if you use it to try to access a site that you did not have authorization to access. It's not about the *tool*. It's about *how you use the tool.*
