**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Perimenopause is affecting my multiple sclerosis. Would a hysterectomy be beneficial?** Unlikely. Menopause interacts with MS by way of estrogen, or the lack thereof. Estrogen depletion can cause MS symptoms to worsen. Indeed, during the pregnancy years, when estrogen flourishes (especially during active pregnancy), MS symptoms subside, and then worsen again after the baby is born. The link between estrogen and MS is well documented. The loss of estrogen is the key driver in MS worsening after menopause. A hysterectomy would not change your level of estrogen. Hormone replacement therapy, on the other hand, might. You should talk to your gynecologist about adding HRT to your health regimen. You might just find that your MS symptoms improve as well.[\[1\]](#hzBng){#cite-hzBng .citation_link} Footnotes [\[1\]](#cite-hzBng){.citation_index} [[Multiple Sclerosis and Menopause: What You Can Expect](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=9a5c51&url=https://www.healthline.com/health/ms/ms-menopause%23treatment){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="healthline.com"}]{.qlink_container}
