**Content:** [I\'ll start with MS, and move to Parkinson\'s. With MS, the short answer is: it depends. MS affects everyone who has it differently, so there is no easy answer about what a MS patient cannot do. Some of the most common symptoms of MS include vision loss, muscle weakness, and memory and cognition problems, but again, those are common problems. MS can manifest itself differently in different people.\ \ With Parkinson\'s, the short answer is the same, but Parkinson\'s produces gradual degradation in the patient\'s ability to perform gross motor movements. So eventually, Parkinson\'s patients will be unable to walk or talk.]{.rendered_qtext} ***Disclaimer:*** ***This answer is not professional medical advice****, nor is it a substitute for professional medical advice.
