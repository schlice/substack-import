**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Of course guns are a common denominator! **Gun crimes** necessarily involve **guns**. It's in the name. Just the same: > 13,384 people died in alcohol-related impaired driving accidents last > year. Do you see that cars are the common denominator in all of these > deaths? and > Approximately 1500 people were murdered with knives last year. Do you > see that knives are the common denominator in all these deaths? \ But you are making the logical leap that because there is a ***correlation*** between the killings, the object which is related is the ***cause.*** This is a logical fallacy, also known as the [["false cause" fallacy](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=22885e&url=https://yourlogicalfallacyis.com/false-cause){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="yourlogicalfallacyis.com"}]{.qlink_container}. Just because there is a correlation between events does not mean that the events are caused by the matching criteria. Just as the car did not cause the drunk driving deaths and the knives did not cause the murders, the gun did not cause the killings. It was the *instrument* of the killing, but it was not the *cause* of the killing. It was common among all the killings, but it did was not the *cause* of the deaths. A person with the will to take a life is what caused the death. Focusing on the instrument will not change the course of the killer.
