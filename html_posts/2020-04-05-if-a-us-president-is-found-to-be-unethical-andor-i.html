**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If a US President is found to be unethical and/or immoral during his or her term and steps down as a result of that fact, shouldn\'t that make every bill that he or she signed into law null and void by default?** No. Here are the primary reasons why: 1. There is no Constitutional requirement for a President to be "ethical" or "moral" while in office. 2. There is no standard for "ethics" or "morality" in the United States. The only standard we have is the law and the Constitution. 3. Even if a president acted unlawfully, while that president was duly elected and sworn in, and functioning in his elected role, as long as he signed legislation in accordance with the law, that legislation is still valid. 4. There are separate remedies for presidents who act unlawfully, and even who act "unethically" and "immoraly," if found to have done so by Congress. 5. Presidents who act unethically and/or immorally (by whatever standard they are judged) do not change the legality/ethicality/morality of the laws which they signed.
