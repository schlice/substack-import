--- title: '"Are blind people capable of figuring out on their own when day has transitioned to night and vice versa?"' date: '2017-08-11' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In short, no. The neurological disorder called *Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Rhythm Disorder* (or Non-24) affects more than half of totally blind people. It does affect some sighted people as well, but the cause is different. Research continues on this disorder.
