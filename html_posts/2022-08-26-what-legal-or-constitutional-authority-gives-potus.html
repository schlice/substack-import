**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} He's trying to use COVID to cover it up, but it's really too thin a justification for that. Ultimately, the president does not have the legal authority to cancel contracts, or change the terms of contracts. He's attempting to void a repayment contract, and he has no authority to do that. Not so long ago, Nancy Pelosi [[agreed](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=4b09ec&url=https://twitter.com/rncresearch/status/1562137015314948096){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="twitter.com"}]{.qlink_container}. "People think that the president of the United States has the power for \[student loan\] debt forgiveness," Pelosi told the media last July. "He does not have that power. That has to be an act of Congress." Biden's legal justification, to the extent that he has one, is that the HEROES Act "could be used to effectuate a program of categorical debt cancellation directed at addressing the financial harms caused by the COVID-19 pandemic." Except that *his own office* ensured that there was actually no harm from the loans caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, since they had initiated a pause on student loan repayments, which has been in effect since March 13, 2020. So they're claiming now that the COVID-19 pandemic is **so severe** that loan payments **which no one has been required to make for the last two and a half years** are still causing financial harms?
