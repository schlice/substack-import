**Content:** [There are two reasons that old, unenforced laws remain on the books:\ \ 1. Historical reference. Even the laws in a state show their own historical roots, and can be used for historical reference. Removing old laws removes the historical references which are present by their very existence.\ 2. Cost. It\'s expensive to change the law in a state. Law reference books need addendums, posters need to be re-printed, etc. If the law is not being enforced, why spend extra money when nothing is to be gained from it?]{.rendered_qtext}
