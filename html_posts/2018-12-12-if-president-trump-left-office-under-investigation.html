**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If President Trump left office under investigation, how would deciding to pardon him or not pardon him affect Mike Pence\'s future electability?** If Trump voluntarily "left office under investigation," in order to complete the hypothetical, he would then be indicted (as a regular citizen) and tried in a regular court. If he were found guilty, I highly doubt that Pence would pardon him. If Trump were impeached AND removed from office (a feat which is nigh impossible given the composition of the Senate), and then indicted and found guilty, Pence could conceivably claim that the whole thing was, to borrow Trump's words, a "witch hunt," and *maybe* pardon him. But Pence is actually a politician. He's keenly aware of one thing that Trump seems to be far less concerned with: *optics*. I doubt that if public opinion swayed against Trump, especially after he was indicted and convicted in a court, for real crimes, that Pence would pardon him. And if he was actually *removed from office,* he would be the first president to ever be removed from office. Nixon was close, but he resigned before he could be removed from office. Pardoning someone who was *so guilty* that he became the first president ever to be removed from office would play very poorly with everyone. Optics is key. Would it hurt his future electability? Not at all. It's not going to change anyone's votes whatsoever. The people who believe Trump is guilty weren't going to vote for Pence anyway, and you're certainly not going to lose any Trump voters because you *help* Trump when you think he's being railroaded.
