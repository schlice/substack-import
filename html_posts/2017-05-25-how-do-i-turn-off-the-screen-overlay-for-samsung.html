--- title: '"How do I turn off the screen overlay for Samsung?"' date: '2017-05-25' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} - Swipe down from the top of the screen and select the gear icon, this will take you into Settings. - Select **Applications** - Select **Application Manager** - Click **MORE** in the upper-right hand portion of the screen. - Select "Apps that can appear on top" - De-select any apps that you no longer wish to allow permission to draw over other apps. If you're having a specific problem with the Install button, for example, there is a great (ad-supported) app called [[Install Button Unlocker](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=95e599&url=https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id%3Djp.sfapps.installbuttonunlocker){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="google.com"}]{.qlink_container} by SFApps. You can find it in the Google Play Store. It is extremely effective at identifying and allowing access to these pesky buttons which always seem to be underneath a "screen overlay." You do have to contend with ads, but it's a small price to pay to fix the annoying screen overlay issue.
