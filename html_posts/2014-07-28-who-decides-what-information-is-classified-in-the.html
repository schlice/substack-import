--- title: '"Who decides what information is classified in the US and UK? Is classified information protected from destruction by the current government?"' date: '2014-07-28' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [(This answer is specifically tailored towards the US; UK rules may be different)\ \ Documents are classified by the document creator based on the nature of the information in the document, and the classification is approved by the classification approval authority (usually the commander of the organization producing the data). There are three major levels of classification\ \ **Confidential** (denoted by a blue cover sheet/sticker)\ **Secret** (denoted by a red cover sheet/sticker)\ **Top Secret** (denoted by an orange cover sheet/sticker)\ \ Each level has its own specific rules about protection, dissemination, and destruction of the data in that level. At the Top Secret level, there are further sublevels within there, called ***compartments.*** Information is compartmentalized based on the source of the data. For example, data generated by electronic intelligence-gathering equipment is segregated from information generated by other offices or other systems. In order to access a specific compartment, you have to be cleared to view the information in that compartment, which is essentially a documented need to know (i.e. because you work in an organization that regularly creates, uses, or modifies that information).\ To destroy a classified document, it has to be past its expiration date and the same authority that classified the document must approve its destruction. So no, the government can\'t come in and simply destroy classified data.]{.rendered_qtext}
