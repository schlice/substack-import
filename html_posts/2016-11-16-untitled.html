**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Thank you. In thinking about it, I really hung on to her too long. When she got the tumor on her brain stem, I probably should have put her down at that point. She had periods of lucidity where I could see her personality come through, but she was never really the same after that. I held on to her more for myself than for her, and as much as I beat myself up for keeping her alive during that time, I have to not do that because it was my first experience with such a thing. And while I will never forget her, and I will always miss her (next to my wife, she was my best friend), our new friend just turned one, and she has her own unique personality, and she is finding her place in our home. It's cool watching her carve out her little niche in our family.
