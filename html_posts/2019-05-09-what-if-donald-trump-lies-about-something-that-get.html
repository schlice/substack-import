**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What if Donald Trump lies about something that gets our country into a war and he acts like it the truth instead of a lie (just like he is doing about everything else)?** It's really not conceivable that the President could blatantly lie about something and that lie would be believed by so many people that it would actually start a war. Such a scenario is not outside the realm of possibility, but in reality, it's so unlikely that such a thing could actually happen that it's really inconceivable. (Sorry, I hear *Princess Bride* in my head any time I type inconceivable, so give me a second to compose myself) Now on to the second part of your question: **Can Congress protect our country from our President's mouth and tweeting?** The short answer is no. The reason: the 1st Amendment. > *Congress shall make no law...abridging the freedom of speech.* Congress cannot stop someone from saying or tweeting anything they like. However, if someone lies, in their speech (slander) or in their tweets (libel), we have laws which can take care of that. And if those lies are damaging enough, the House of Representatives *can* vote to impeach the President. But that's not enough. The Senate must see fit that those impeachable offenses are so damaging that the President cannot remain in office to complete his term, and they must have a trial and vote to remove him from office. And **even then,** the President is still a private citizen and can still speak and tweet to his heart's content (unless Twitter decides to ban him). So even then, Congress can't protect the country from Donald Trump's mouth and Twitter account.
