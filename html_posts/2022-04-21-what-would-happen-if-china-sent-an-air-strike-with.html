**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} They'd never make it near enough to the United States mainland to even see the beach. I've answered a similar question a while back: [[Bill Stein\'s answer to If an unidentified passenger plane flew into the American mainland and ignored all attempts at communicating with it, how would the military respond?](/If-an-unidentified-passenger-plane-flew-into-the-American-mainland-and-ignored-all-attempts-at-communicating-with-it-how-would-the-military-respond/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container} This answer has all the details about the ADIZ which I'm going to reference. If an invasion force of aircraft was headed towards the ADIZ, we would immediately launch our NORAD quick response assets to intercept and visually identify the attackers. Your invasion force would be met 200 nautical miles off the coast of the United States. They would be given ample opportunity to declare their intentions, and continuing on their existing heading would be considered a declaration of war. Once it was determined that China was openly sending an invasion force towards the mainland of the US, the National Command Authority would approve the destruction of that invasion force. So what would happen? You'd make it 9/10ths of the way to the United States and die in a fiery plane crash into the Pacific Ocean.
