--- title: '"Can God do anything? Is anything impossible?"' date: '2024-12-06' tags: - theology layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is **God can do anything that it is possible to do.** Some things just aren't possible. He can't violate His own nature. The Bible tells us these things. We know He cannot lie or deny Himself (Hebrews 6:18; 2 Timothy 2:13; Titus 1:2). He also can't violate the laws of the universe and the laws of physics which He created. So for example, the gotcha question that atheists love to ask me is, "Can God create a rock so heavy that even He can't lift it?" They think this is a gotcha because regardless of how you answer, it proves that God is not omnipotent. Either His inability to create such a rock, or His inability to move such a rock, would place His omnipotence into question. But the existence of this question is based on a flawed understanding of omnipotence. What truly is omnipotence? It's "unlimited power," the power to effect change, to make things happen. God (being unlimited) has unlimited power, and the Bible affirms this (Job 11:7-11, 37:23; 2 Corinthians 6:18; Revelation 4:8; etc.). Therefore, God can do whatever is possible to be done. God cannot, however, do that which is actually impossible. This is because ***true impossibility is not based on the amount of power one has, it is based on what is really possible.*** **The truly impossible is not made possible by adding more power.** Understanding this, you can understand why He cannot create a rock that even He cannot move. The reason is because of His nature and the nature of reality itself. God cannot do what is not actually possible to be done, like creating a square circle or a two-sided triangle. Just because you can stick words together this way does not make the impossible possible. These things are *contradictions*; they are truly impossible in reality. And even God cannot deny reality. To answer the theoretical rock question, in order for God to create a rock so heavy that He cannot lift it, He would have to create a rock of sufficient mass such that His infinite ability to exert force could not move it. Since His input force is unlimited, the size of the rock would have to be infinite such that His unlimited input force could not move it. And an infinitely massive rock cannot exist within this universe. Therefore, God cannot create such a rock because it would not fit inside of the universe He created and where rocks and matter exist.
