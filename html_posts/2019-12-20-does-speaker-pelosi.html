**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does Speaker Pelosi's delay in submitting her articles of impeachment to the Senate for certain conditions essentially amount to a 'quid pro quo'?** There's plenty of buzzwords you can apply to Pelosi's delay in submitting the Articles of Impeachment to the Senate, but "quid pro quo" is not one of them. The most notable and apropos buzzword to apply is **obstruction of Congress**. That Pelosi can't fathom her own hypocrisy is what is most telling. Just a few weeks ago, she railed on against President Trump for failing to allow staffers to testify in front of Congress, ostensibly exercising executive privilege without using the word. Predictably, the House impeached him for exercising his authority under the Constitution. Then, just a few weeks later, Pelosi decides that it's perfectly acceptable to obstruct the Senate in its trial obligation under the Constitution by failing to deliver the Articles of Impeachment to the Senate because she's dissatisfied with how the Senate plans on conducting its business. She has no Constitutional authority to fail to deliver the Articles of Impeachment to the Senate because she dislikes the Senate rules, or she disagrees with how the trial in the Senate is to take place. The Senate is the sole organization which can conduct the trial, not the House. The House has no authority here. But she has unilaterally decided to gum up the works. It's obstruction of Congress, plain and simple. McConnell should subpoena the House Manager and force them to deliver the Articles of Impeachment to the Senate floor, and if he/she fails to appear, he should petition the courts to intervene.
