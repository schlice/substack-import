--- title: '"How close are we to a cure for MS?"' date: '2016-08-13' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Think about this. We have known, medically, about MS for thousands of years. We haven\'t had effective treatments for it for most of that time. It\'s only been in the last 40 years or so that we have been able to slow the disease progression and lessen its effects. We\'ve gone from effectively "no treatment" to being able to make the lifespan of someone with MS basically match the lifespan of someone without MS, and we have lessened the impact of the disease as well as making the disease less active in the body. It has been the development of the MRI and other advanced diagnostic tools that has propelled the treatment of the disease. As diagnostic tools improve, and the technology to understand the disease improves, the race to the cure will be exponential, not linear. I think we will see a functional cure in my lifetime.
