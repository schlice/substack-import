**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} While I agree with all of the posters, I would have to echo [[Lisa\'s](https://www.quora.com/profile/Lisa-Scroggins)]{.qlink_container} advice first and foremost. If you are a person who is diagnosed with MS, I can\'t suggest strongly enough that you start one of the approved MS treatment drugs. I would caution anyone in following any other advice about any other "treatment" exclusively. Eating right and exercising are exceedingly helpful to anyone, but are especially helpful to anyone who has MS. Using complementary and alternative medicines can also help many people. However, doing these things **and at the same time excluding approved treatments** is strongly discouraged, since eating right and exercising **will not slow the disease progression**. They will definitely improve your quality of life and will maintain your overall health, but the disease will continue to silently progress. We must be clear about this point, regardless of what you may have heard on the Internet or what you heard anecdotally from someone. **Changing your diet alone will not slow down or stop Multiple Sclerosis. There is no \"magic diet\" that will cure MS.** If you \"had MS\" and you changed your diet and it \"cured your MS,\" you didn\'t have MS. You had something else that mimicked MS, but you did not have MS. There is not a single doctor out there who can show true randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled studies that prove that a diet will cure MS. I can\'t stress this enough: eat right, exercise, **and** take an approved MS therapy drug. Over 20 years of research show us that the approved therapies work, and using them in conjunction with lifestyle modification offers you the best chance for successfully managing the disease. (EDIT) I want to make this point clear as well. **There currently is no cure for MS.** So, what's a "cure?" A cure, in the medical context, means that the disease *reverses course*. Any damage done by the disease is healed, and there is *zero trace of the disease* anywhere in the body. The disease is *gone*. There is no further disease process, and further medications become unnecessary and superfluous. In this context, there is **no cure** for MS. There is no diet which will cure MS. There is no treatment of any kind which will cure MS. Even the approved MS ***therapy*** drugs cannot cure MS. However, the drugs *do* slow down the disease progression, reduce the severity and duration of relapses, and increase the amount of time between relapses. *Can't a diet do those things?* A diet can make the relapses less severe, **but it has zero effect on the disease course.** The disease will continue to progress, and the relapses will continue to come. For someone with MS, the relapses will not slow down due to diet. The closest we have to a "cure" right now is Autologous Hematopoietic Stem Cell Therapy (aHSCT). This treatment has been used for cancer patients for decades, and has only recently been approved for trials for MS patients in the US. Basically, it is a process by which the immune system of the body is "reset." The body's red blood cell-generating bone marrow is destroyed, and then it is re-created using the body's own stem cells. It is an extreme procedure, and it is not without risk. Once the chemotherapy is started, the *entire immune system is destroyed*. No T lymphocytes will be created.
