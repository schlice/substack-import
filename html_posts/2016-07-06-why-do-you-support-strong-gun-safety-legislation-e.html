--- title: '"Why do you support strong gun safety legislation (e.g., universal background checks)?"' date: '2016-07-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I support actual "gun safety" legislation. Universal background checks don't make anyone safer. Nearly every mass shooter passed all his background checks; mandating more background checks won't make anyone safer. I would support background checks for all gun transfers so long as we can simplify and expand NICS, which will take many many federal dollars, so I don't see it happening. Passing yet another cost on to gun owners is also not the right thing to do, as you yet again limit access to guns to the people who can afford all the government requirements. I support licensing guns in the same way we license drivers. Make easily-accessible testing, make inexpensive testing, make licenses valid in all 50 states, etc. Despite the home security argument, I support safe storage requirements. You may believe differently, but I believe technology has come far enough that you can nearly instantaneously get into your gun safe; fast enough that you can safely arm yourself within seconds, and still keep your gun out of the hands of criminals and the untrained/unauthorized. Mandating safe storage will also help to further separate legal and illegal gun transactions, and mandatory theft reporting will clearly show when a gun goes from legal to illegal. It will also prevent most guns from becoming illegal guns through theft. I think government should be (partially, at least) paying for gun safes in homes. I remember the last time we tried this; it was never mandated and they included enough funds to provide free cable locks for long guns. What a waste. If you're going to fund it, do it right.
