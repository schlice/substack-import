**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If you support Trump using \"national emergency\' power to build his wall, would you continue even if it created legal precedent for a future president to have unrestrained power to restrict assault and other military weapons?** It's clear you don't understand how this whole "national emergency" thing works. Many of our Federal laws have these little inbuilt "short-circuits" that allow the president to do certain things (which would otherwise have to go through Congressional approval or other processes) during a time of declared war or declared national emergency. Clearly, declared war is out, as declarations of war can only be made by Congress. But the president can declare a national emergency, and it's solely his job to do so. [[It's been done many times](https://www.quora.com/How-can-a-U-S-president-simply-declare-there-will-be-an-emergency-some-number-of-days-in-the-future-specifically-pending-the-outcome-of-a-funding-measure/answer/Bill-Stein)]{.qlink_container}, probably more often than you know. It's governed by the National Emergencies Act. The law outlines what the president needs to do in order to declare a national emergency. Once the national emergency is declared, all of these Federal laws with their little inbuilt short-circuits become available to be used. For instance, 10 USC ยง 2808 has the following in it (*emphasis* added): > \[I\]n the event of a declaration of war *or the declaration by the > President of a national emergency* in accordance with the National > Emergencies Act (50 USC ยง 1601 et seq.) *that requires use of the > armed forces*, the Secretary of Defense, without regard to any other > provision of law, may undertake military construction projects, and > may authorize the Secretaries of the military departments to undertake > military construction projects, not otherwise authorized by law *that > are necessary to support such use of the armed forces.* Such projects > may be undertaken *only within the total amount of funds that have > been appropriated for military construction*, including funds > appropriated for family housing, that have not been obligated. So in order for a national emergency to make guns illegal, there would have to be a law that gave some agency the authority to do so in the event of a national emergency. But not even a national emergency can override the Constitution. So such a law would be unconstitutional.
