**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "Bible literalists" (by whom I assume you mean Christians) believe that the Bible never contradicts itself. So by the very nature of your question, the answer must be **no**, Bible literalists do not believe that there are any contradictions between the stories in Genesis 1 and Genesis 2. They simply believe that the two chapters do not disagree as to the order in which things were created and therefore do not contradict one another. Genesis 1 describes the "six days of creation" (and a seventh day of rest); Genesis 2 covers only one day of that creation week---the sixth day---and so there is no contradiction. People who believe that the Bible is telling two different creation stories are, by definition, **not** Bible literalists, and also, by definition, are not Christian. So all the people who told you "yes, there are contradictions" believe the following: - The Bible can contain contradictions, which Christians do not believe, and - Genesis tells the story of two different creation events, which Christians also do not believe.
