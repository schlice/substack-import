Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} According to the Americans with Disabilities Act, service animals which are trained to assist with a recognized disability can go anywhere that people can go. Therefore, service animals are permitted in the passenger compartment of aircraft, as they likely must perform one or more of their duties for their human handler throughout the flight. Note that [[Stanton Nicholas](https://www.quora.com/profile/Stanton-Nicholas)]{.qlink_container} got it half right. "Service animals" are legally protected by the ADA. "Emotional support animals" are ***not*** trained to do anything special, and therefore are ***not protected by the ADA***. Establishments have the right to restrict emotional support animals from places of public accommodation. But they don't, because in today's hypersensitive, overly-maligned society, it would be bad PR to restrict someone's emotional support animal. So they just don't bother. So in truth, they can restrict emotional support animals, but in practice, they generally don't. Any animal, service animal or not, can be restricted if it is uncontrollable or if it is a danger or disruption to the other people.
