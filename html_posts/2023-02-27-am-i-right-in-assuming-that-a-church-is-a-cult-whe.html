**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Britannica Dictionary definition of **cult:** 1. a small religious group that is not part of a larger and more accepted religion and that has beliefs regarded by many people as extreme or dangerous. I'm pretty sure that a particular church misunderstanding some of the passages surrounding the necessity of water baptism does not make them a cult. They're simply a Christian church who has adopted a flawed understanding of the Bible. That makes them a *sect*, not a *cult*. *This is not unique* among Christian churches. We have many sects which develop over time. In fact, some have just developed in the last few years. Christian churches which believe in women pastors or homosexual or transsexual pastors or endorsing gay marriage by marrying gays are examples of *sects* in the church. They have "traditional beliefs and practices" but have deviated from the core teachings of the church. The United Methodist Church has begun the process of splitting off into a new sect because they have altered some of their core beliefs about the teachings of the religion. Rodney Stark and William Sims Bainbridge, in [[A Theory of Religion](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=64c930&url=https://www.amazon.com/Theory-Religion-Rodney-Stark-ebook/dp/B000RL6ENE/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="amazon.com"}]{.qlink_container} define church, sect, and cult as follows: 1. A *church* is a conventional religious organization. 2. A *sect* is a deviant religious organization with traditional beliefs and practices. 3. A *cult* is a deviant religious organization with extreme or dangerous beliefs and practices.
