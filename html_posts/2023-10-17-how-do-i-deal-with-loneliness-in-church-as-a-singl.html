**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It's 2023. Have you tried [[Christian Mingle](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=81c10f&url=https://www.christianmingle.com/en-us){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="christianmingle.com"}]{.qlink_container}? In all seriousness, have you noticed that **churches have gotten away from singles groups?** There are lots of reasons for this, and I'm sure if you asked your church why they don't have a singles group, they'd tell you why. Deep down, I think part of the problem is that Christians (wrongly) have an *expectation* that couples are married. There is a concomitant stigma placed on singles in a church, as if something must be *wrong* with them. Therefore, church singles groups feel very...desperate. As if they *need* to be married in order to be accepted. And some church singles groups may give off the unspoken message that your life is not meaningful unless you're married. Just look at some of the titles of singles group study topics I found online: - Discovering God's Plan for You as a Single - Finding Contentment as a Single Person - How to Know Your Purpose as a Single I'm certain that the churches in these cases were well-meaning, but the unintended implication in these titles is that people who are married have a greater calling in life than those who are single. This implication causes many Christian singles to believe they are *insufficient* as a single person. It\'s natural for singles to desire to be married, especially in a Christian context. But it\'s also important not to make this the central motivation of our lives. Instead, we should learn to be content in our circumstances and find joy in each day. I think we need to find a balance between seeking a spouse and waiting for God to bring the right person into our lives. The search for a spouse should always begin with prayer. When we take pleasure in knowing Him and we trust Him with our future, He will put His desires on our heart. In the context of a spouse, that means desiring the type of spouse that He would desire for us. Doing this means accepting His will for our lives. Whatever He says is best for us is what we should want. When we live in the peace of God's love for us, we can be happy in whatever season we find ourselves in. A person who lives joyfully and with God's purpose is *naturally* attractive! When you live this way, you will naturally attract people to you.
