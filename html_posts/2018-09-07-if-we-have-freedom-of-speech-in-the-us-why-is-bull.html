--- title: '"If we have freedom of speech in the U.S., why is bullying illegal?"' date: '2018-09-07' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If we have freedom of speech in the U.S., why is bullying illegal?** **Bullying is a** ***behavior*, not a set of words.** It's not illegal to speak the words, but if the words create a bullying act, it may be illegal. In California, bullying *behavior* has been made to violate education laws. Sections 234.2 and 48900 of the Education Code were amended to add *bullying acts.* The words themselves are protected, and the government has not prohibited you from speaking the words. But if the words create a *bullying act*, you have fallen afoul of the law. In just the same way that you can say whatever you want about a person, but if it's a lie and it harms them, you have *slandered* that person, and that's illegal. Bullying is not illegal at the Federal level, but bullying behavior may cross the line into *harassment*, which is a *behavior* which is illegal at the Federal level.
