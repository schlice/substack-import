**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The people who make television shows are morons. But the contention that no respectable thief would ever steal a running car is simply incorrect. We in Colorado have a tremendous problem with \"puffing,\" where someone goes out to start their car on a cold morning and leaves it running in their own driveway. It\'s called \"puffing\" because when the car is warming up on a cold morning, the exhaust from the car billows big clouds of \"puffy\" moisture, sort of a big sign for thieves that says, \"Hey, here\'s a car warming up with (potentially) no one in it, come steal me!\" We get roughly one theft a week of cars that were puffing. And it\'s a double-whammy for the owner, because not only was your car stolen, but insurance doesn\'t cover you when you do something illegal (leaving a car running with no occupant is illegal in Colorado). We don\'t have any puffing stings yet in our jurisdiction, but it\'s coming.
