**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How are the unilateral tariffs of Trump without congressional approval legal and/or constitutional?** Because over the course of American history since the Constitution was written, Congress has spent its time handing over its power of tariffs to the President. Over its long history, Congress has proven that it is absolutely terrible at national economic planning. Also, the power to levy tariffs tends to land more with matters of foreign policy, and foreign policy is squarely in the President's wheelhouse, not Congress. So little by little, Congress has granted tariff authority to the President. Initially, it was only for foreign policy reasons, but since FDR, the office of the president has had nearly complete control of tariffs, even in cases like Trump's tariffs which are not manifestly foreign policy related. Trump is just scoring points with the aluminum and steel industries, not projecting a foreign policy.
