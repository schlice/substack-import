**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Besides quoting the \"You shouldn\'t have signed the contract if you don\'t like it\" argument, what is your view on HOA\'s fining residents monthly, for having round trash cans instead of the square ones they recently commanded you must buy?** Unless the HOA is receiving kick-backs from trash can companies for forcing you to buy certain cans, there must be a reason why that rule was implemented. If I had to guess, I'd say it has something to do with your trash collection. Many locations have trash companies which utilize specialized trucks which grab cans and lift them up, dumping the trash. When a non-standard can is present, the worker must exit the truck, dump the can, and get back in, which makes trash collection less efficient, and makes the driver later finishing his route, which may cause more problems downstream. If the trash company contract requires a certain geometry of can, they may eventually start fining the HOA for every incorrectly-shaped can they have to stop and collect. So your round can may be costing the HOA (and by extension, you) more money. Let me give you a real life forinstance. In my neighborhood, we have single-stream recycling. Some residents were bagging their recycling before putting it in the collection bin. The trash company was finding that plastic bags were gumming up the sorting machines, so they told us absolutely no plastic bags in recycling. The problem got so bad (and they had to stop production lines so many times) that they told us they would fine the HOA for every plastic bag they found in our recycling. So we have a rule: no plastic bags in recycling. We can cite homeowners and fine them if they continually place plastic bags in recycling. Seems silly, doesn't it? Getting a fine for having a plastic bag in your recycling? But there is a reason. I'm certain that your HOA has a reason for mandating a certain shape of can. You should ask them why.
