**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Apple does not give away their software for free. You buy Apple's software every time you buy an Apple product. Here's the difference: Microsoft is a **software company** and Apple is a **hardware company.** Apple sells hardware. The sell iPhones and iPads and MacBooks and a variety of other hardware. They also distribute software that you can only legally run on that hardware. Legally, I can't take Mac OS X and install it on my Dell Precision Tower here under my desk. You are only licensed to install Apple software on Apple hardware. Apple does not independently sell software because that software is designed only to run on the hardware you already bought. Therefore, the cost of Apple's software is bundled into the hardware you already purchased. You didn't get Apple software for free. You paid for it when you bought Apple hardware. By contrast, Microsoft is a **software company**. Sure, they have made some forays into the hardware side with devices like the Surface, but by and large, Microsoft sells software. When you buy a PC with Windows pre-installed on it, you have received an **OEM** copy of Windows (or Original Equipment Manufacturer copy). Microsoft entered into a licensing agreement with that computer manufacturer and they sold copies of Windows at a bulk discount. The hardware manufacturer then passes on the lower cost of that copy of Windows to the end consumer in the price of their computer. Since you asked about Office, and not about Windows, let's compare apples to apples: Apple's iWork suite (which is similar to to Microsoft Office) is priced under a similar pricing structure. Once you have a license to install Apple software (which means you have purchased or are otherwise a valid user of Apple hardware), you can install iWork at no additional charge. Office 365 is priced under a new Microsoft pricing structure which recognizes the cloud-based nature of work today, and the requirement for portability. As a personal user, you can buy a license to Office 365, for one user, which includes cloud-based access to all of the Office applications from anywhere, tablet and mobile use, and full installation on one PC, for \$6.95 per month, or \$69.99 per year. If you want to purchase the standalone Office software package as with previous versions of Office, it's \$149.95. **TL;DR: You actually bought iWork when you bought your Apple hardware. The cost of Apple's software is rolled into the cost of Apple's hardware, which is why you cannot install Apple software onto anything but Apple hardware.**
