**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Primary Progressive MS (PPMS)** Primary Progressive MS is a form of MS which is characterized by continually worsening symptoms, with or without relapses. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-11e7c9c3b1324e85530a05f06dcf5a5d){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-11e7c9c3b1324e85530a05f06dcf5a5d" master_w="300" master_h="394"} ::: :::: *(Source:* [[*National MS Society*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=97541f&url=https://www.nationalmssociety.org/What-is-MS/Types-of-MS){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="nationalmssociety.org"}]{.qlink_container}*)* This graph shows what disease progression could look like for a patient with PPMS, but remember that each person's personal experience with the disease is unique. PPMS can have periods of time that the patient is stable (no active symptoms and no disease progression), the disease is not active (no change in symptoms) and either is or is not experiencing disease progression, and there can be periods of time where the disease is active (in a relapse or active disease process) and either is or is not experiencing disease progression.
