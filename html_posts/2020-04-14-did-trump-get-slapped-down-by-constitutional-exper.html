Check what the other guys are saying about it. Left-leaning website: [[Trump gets slapped down by constitutional experts after claiming his \'authority is total\'](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=14480b&url=https://www.dailykos.com/stories/2020/4/14/1937111/-Trump-gets-slapped-down-by-constitutional-experts-after-claiming-his-authority-is-total){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="dailykos.com"}]{.qlink_container} I'd find an example on a right-leaning website, but the comment is either glossed over as a Trump gaffe, or it's buried so far under the deluge of left-leaning "news" sites taking the low softball for a ride. In this case, yes, nearly ever Constitutional scholar rightly pointed out that the President does not have "total" authority over the states. In fact, the Constitution exists specifically to limit the power of the Federal government. Call it a "slap down" if you like. Let's all just remember that this isn't the first time we had a president who thought he could step outside the bounds of the Constitution. Let's all remember this the next time we have a president who says that he doesn't care whether or not Congress does its job, because he has a pen and a phone. Whether or not you like what the President is claiming he can do should not matter to the Constitutionality of the action.
