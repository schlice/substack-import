**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} First of all, not all cops misuse the various criminal records databases. Secondly, while the search itself is logged, we have no way of knowing why the officer searched that particular person, or license plate, or address or phone number. Finally, not all police departments track this data, and others track some of the data but not others (i.e. Florida reported that there were hundreds of unauthorized database searches but did not report on whether or not the officers were disciplined). That said, we know that cops do misuse criminal records databases, because FOIA requests for disciplinary actions against officers who misused databases produced results. Without consistent record-keeping and reporting of disciplinary actions across the more than 13,000 law enforcement agencies across the United States, we probably won't get very good answers as to how often and to what degree it occurs.[\[1\]](#fGrCB){#cite-fGrCB .citation_link} \ Original Question: Do cops put the names of all potential partners on the criminal database to see if they have a criminal record?
