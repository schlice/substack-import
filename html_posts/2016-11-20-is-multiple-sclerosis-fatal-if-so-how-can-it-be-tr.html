--- title: '"Is multiple sclerosis fatal? If so, how can it be treated?"' date: '2016-11-20' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Multiple sclerosis itself is not fatal. Patients can die from complications caused by MS. For example, you could die from asphyxiation caused by swallowing difficulties. But the disease itself does not cause death. In fact, with modern medications used to treat MS, lifespans of MS patients nearly equal the lifespans of those without the disease. We don't know what causes MS so we don't have a cure, but current medications can slow down the disease progression and lessen its effects.
