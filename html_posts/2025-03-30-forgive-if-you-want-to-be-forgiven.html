**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} "Forgive if you want to be forgiven" is a re-telling of the Golden Rule: "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you." It's actually in the Bible: > *"So whatever you wish that others would do to you, do also to them, > for this is the Law and the Prophets. \-- Matthew 7:12 ESV* One of the key points we need to understand about forgiveness is that the real issue is not so much between you and the other person. **It's between you and God.** As you can see in Matthew 7:12, God commands us to forgive. Jesus reiterates this when Peter asks Him about forgiveness: > *Then Peter came up and said to him, "Lord, how often will my brother > sin against me, and I forgive him? As many as seven times?" Jesus said > to him, "I do not say to you seven times, but seventy-seven times. \-- > Matthew 18:21--22 ESV* So when we forgive, we are following God's command. But, as you pointed out, what about being able to bring your worship to church if someone has something against us? - If you have offended that person, yes you should go to them, ask for forgiveness, and put things right, **as far as you are able**. *But your forgiveness is not contingent upon their acceptance of your apology.* Because as we just showed, forgiveness is between you and God, not you and the other person. - But if someone has offended you, don't go to them. Go to God. Your need to forgive others is first and foremost an issue between you and God. That actually makes sense if you stop and think about it. Your forgiveness cannot be dependent on someone else, otherwise it wouldn't be guaranteed. And after you have forgiven them, they may not accept it. And in this case, they may not be sorry, or they may not admit any wrongdoing. Whether or not they do is not up to you. But regardless, you forgiving them removes Satan's ability to use that issue against you. How do you move on? You forgive. And if you're still feeling pain, their hook is still in you. You haven't removed it yet, you haven't forgiven them. You haven't let them "off the hook." But you might be thinking, "But you don't know what they did to me! You don't know how much they hurt me!" ***Don't you see that by leaving that hook in you, they're still hurting you?*** So how do you stop the pain? Remove the hook! **You need to forgive them.** If you are still feeling the affects of that offense, you have not forgiven them. Yes, forgiving them lets them off the hook. If you choose to not forgive them, you stay hooked to that pain. Maybe you choose to not forgive them because when you do, you know that you are letting them off the hook, and you feel like they are getting away with it. But if you *don't*, you are the one with the hook still in you. You will continue to feel the pain of that hook until you remove it. But here is the beautiful part: ***while you may let them off of your hook, you don't let them off of God's hook.*** > *Beloved, never avenge yourselves, but leave it to the wrath of God, > for it is written, "Vengeance is mine, I will repay, says the Lord." > \-- Romans 12:19 ESV* Vengeance is mine. I will repay, says God. You don't have to worry about letting them off the hook, because God has promised that He will not let them off of **His** hook. So it's time to truly forgive them, knowing that in the fullness of time, God will judge them for what they have done, and they will receive the due penalty for hurting you. When you choose to forgive, you are taking a step of faith to trust God to be the judge who will weigh what has been done on the scales of justice and demand that the scales balance. Everyone who has sinned against you will have to stand before the throne of God and explain themselves. And the scales can only be balanced in one of two ways: the blood of Christ for those who believe in Him, or that person will have to pay the penalty themselves if they do not. "I will repay, says the Lord." God will balance the scales. We have to choose to trust His promise. Now it's time to hand over all of that pain and those demands for justice and vengeance to Him. He has promised to repay. You can walk free of that pain, safe in the knowledge of God's promise.
