**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why did Matthew Whitaker refuse to tell Ted Lieu that the U.S. Constitution does not say the Sitting President can not be indicted?** He's not there as a Constitutional scholar. He's not there to provide Constitutional insight. That's the Supreme Court's job. Because he is testifying as the acting head of the Department of Justice, he can only give answers in accordance with his job. And it's not his job to pontificate about the Constitution. He's there as acting head of the Department of Justice. Therefore, he provided established Department of Justice guidance that it is their opinion that a sitting president cannot be indicted.
