**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why do we feel shy about being naked? Is there an explanation for that?** Depends on who you ask. Christians and Jews will tell you that it was the events in the Garden of Eden which brought about our knowledge that we were naked. > When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and > pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took > some and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, > and he ate it. **Then the eyes of both of them were opened, and they > realized they were naked;** so they sewed fig leaves together and made > coverings for themselves. > > ---Genesis 3:6--7 (NIV) Science and physiology tells us that around age three or four, we begin to differentiate between what's private and what's public, and develop a sense of modesty not long after that.[\[1\]](#kOLFk){#cite-kOLFk .citation_link} Finally, as always, there are cultural influences. In the United States, people feel that the body is very private, and nudity has been sexualized. The influence of the culture plays a big part in how we feel about certain behaviors.
