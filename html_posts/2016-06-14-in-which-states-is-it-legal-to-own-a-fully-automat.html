--- title: '"In which states is it legal to own a fully automatic weapon?"' date: '2016-06-14' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What you are referring to are weapons which are heavily controlled under the National Firearms Act of 1934 and the Firearms Owners Protection Act of 1986. These are colloquially called "NFA Weapons." Most states do not provide additional restrictions on top of Federal requirements to own automatic weapons. It's actually shorter to list the states which restrict NFA weapons: CA, DE, DC, HI, IL, MA, MN, NJ, RI, WA, WI Most of these states restrict all automatic weapons, some have some other restrictions. New Jersey, for example, allows automatic weapons: "Possession of a machine gun requires a state license, which is granted on a 'may issue' basis by a county superior court judge. Machine gun licenses are extremely difficult to obtain."[\[1\]](#QRAKF){#cite-QRAKF .citation_link} If you do live in a state which allows automatic weapons, you must possess a Type 1 Federal Firearms License as well as a type 3 (Often confused with class 3) SOT (Special Occupation Tax) to sell if you're a gun dealer (private sellers do not need a FFL to sell an individual item that they legally own), and an ATF Form 4 with \$200 tax stamp to purchase. This makes it ***expensive*** to qualify a person to purchase an automatic weapon. In addition, no new automatic weapons can be registered for private ownership that have been manufactured since 19 May 1986, so fully automatic weapons are ***very expensive,*** upwards of \$10,000 and more. But to answer the question, only that list of states above prohibits automatic weapons, but most states allow them when their owners are in compliance with Federal regulations.
