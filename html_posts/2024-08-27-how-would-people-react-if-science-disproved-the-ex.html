--- title: '"How would people react if science disproved the existence of god and religion?"' date: '2024-08-27' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} People would know that the world has ended. Science cannot prove non-existence. Legal scholar and philosopher Mortimer Adler says, \"An affirmative existential proposition can be proved, but a negative existential proposition---one that denies the existence of something---cannot be proved.\" For example, I could tell you that red squirrels exist, and you could tell me that red squirrels do not exist. To prove my statement, I only need to find evidence of one red squirrel somewhere. But to prove your statement, you would literally need to be everywhere all at once in all of history so that you could prove that red squirrels do not exist, and never did exist. Doing that is literally not possible. And just because you haven't discovered a red squirrel doesn't mean that one does not exist.
