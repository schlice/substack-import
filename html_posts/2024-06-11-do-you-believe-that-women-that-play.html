**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} ***While I may have moral and religious objections to certain facets of IVF, it is patently not "creating life."*** That's like plugging your computer into your router and claiming you "created" the Internet. Creating a life is immensely more complex than just sticking a pre-fertilized egg into a uterus and hoping it attaches. Only God creates life. Again, don't get me wrong, there are indeed facets of IVF which any Christian should rightly find objectionable; namely, what is done with all of the remaining embryos which are not implanted. But let's not throw the baby out with the bathwater...er...yeah. Anyway, the point is that IVF should not be confused with the massive amount of knowledge it would take to create life. Humans absolutely cannot create life, despite how hard evolutionists have tried. And IVF doesn't "create life" any more than connecting your router and your computer "creates" the Internet.
