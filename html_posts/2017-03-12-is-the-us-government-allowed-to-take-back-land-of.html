--- title: '"Is the US Government allowed to take back land of the Free and the Brave with the Authority being Homestead Act of 1862?"' date: '2017-03-12' tags: - policy-analysis layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Maybe I'm misunderstanding the question, but the Homestead Act did not provide for any remaining ownership of land granted under it by the US government. Once that land was given, it was deeded to the homesteader. When you hold the deed and there are no riders on the deed, you own the land. Period. To the best of my knowledge, the government did not retain any ownership in the land deeded to homesteaders under the Homestead Act. Additionally, the Homestead Act was sunsetted by the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976. This officially ended homesteading in the United States (except in Alaska, where it continued until 1986).
