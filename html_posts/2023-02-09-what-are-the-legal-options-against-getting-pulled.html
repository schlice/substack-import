--- title: '"What are the legal options against getting pulled over for no reason, having your car searched without probable cause, or receiving a ticket when the officer was hiding behind something?"' date: '2023-02-09' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} There are no legal options against these things. There are only options for fighting them after they happen. If you get pulled over without probable cause that a traffic violation occurred or that there is criminal activity happening, you can argue that the stop was unlawful. If your car is searched but the officer did not have probable cause to do so, you can argue that the search was unlawful and anything found in the search is inadmissible. If your municipality has a law which says that officers clocking traffic must be in plain sight, or in marked vehicles, or have at a minimum marker lights turned on, and you were clocked by an officer not meeting the minimum standard as required in your jurisdiction, you can argue that the stop was unlawful. \ You cannot ***prevent*** the traffic stop from happening. You cannot ***prevent*** the search from happening. If the officer demands to search your car, you can decline because he needs probable cause or a warrant, but if he insists anyway, you must allow him to proceed. However, if the legal standards were not met, you and your attorney can argue that later.
