--- title: '"Can I use my phone which supports wireless charging to charge another device wirelessly?"' date: '2016-02-11' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} A Qi-capable device (Qi is pronounced \"chee\") is a device that supports \"wireless charging.\" Wireless charging uses a theory of magnetism called \"induction\" whereby a current is \"induced\" in an electrical wire by a magnetic field nearby. If you have an electric toothbrush, it uses inductive charging when you set it on its base. No contacts are exposed because of the waterproof housing, but the battery is charged anyway. In mobile phones, the standard is called \"Qi.\" Generally, a mobile phone is not designed to be both a Qi-capable receiver **and** separately a Qi-capable charger. No phone that I know of has a charging circuit that can provide a charge to another device. It adds too much bulk to the phone in a period of time when manufacturers are striving to make phones slimmer and lighter. (EDIT) Now, devices can both charge themselves wirelessly, and use their batteries to charge other wireless devices wirelessly as well. Samsung's version of this is called "Wireless Power Sharing." I use it on trips to charge my watch while I charge my phone with a cable. I only need one cable and I can charge my phone and my watch at the same time.
