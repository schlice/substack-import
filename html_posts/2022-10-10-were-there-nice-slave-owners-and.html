**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I'm certain that you are thinking of the American colonies when you are discussing slavery, but did you know that slavery existed in the Bible? But slavery in the Bible was very different than the slavery we experienced in the times of colonial America. **The slavery in the Bible was not based on race**. Most slavery in Biblical times was **voluntary servitude**: people sold themselves as slaves when they could not pay their bills or provide for their families. The Bible actually condemns race-based slavery when it says that all men are created by God in His image (Genesis 1:27). It also condemns, in both the Old and New Testaments, the practice of "man-stealing" wherein Africans were kidnapped by African slave hunters, sold to slave traders, and shipped to the New World to become slaves and work on plantations. The Old Testament says, "Whoever steals a man and sells him, and anyone found in possession of him, shall be put to death." (Exodus 21:16 ESV). The New Testament also condemns the practice: slave traders are called "ungodly and sinful" (1 Tim 1:8--10). However, the Old Testament did allow for economic-based slavery and regulated it. But that slavery looked nothing like the race-based slavery we experienced in early America. While I bet that no slave was truly "content," because man is designed to be free and to exercise free will, I'm certain that some of the Biblical slaves were a lot like indentured servants or office workers. They didn't really "love" their bosses, but they did the work because they needed the money. Our modern employment system has grown and matured significantly, but has as its foundation the voluntary and indentured servitude that has existed since Biblical times. \ Original question: Were there nice slave owners and \'content\' slaves?
