--- title: "Can an employer deny accommodation if they feel your medical condition is not severe enough or serious enough to need accommodations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)?" date: "2024-01-26" tags: ["veteran-benefits", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} No. An employer cannot deny your request for accommodation by evaluating your disability. They are not doctors. But an employer can deny your request for accommodation if it creates an **undue hardship** on the business. An undue hardship is when the cost of providing the accommodation is either too expensive or too disruptive to the organization. Some things which would be considered an undue hardship include, but is not limited to, accommodations which: - Diminish efficiency in other jobs - Infringe on other employees\' job rights or benefits - Impair workplace safety - Cause co-workers to carry the accommodated employee\'s share of potentially hazardous or burdensome work If you feel an employer has unlawfully denied your request for accommodation under ADA, reach out to your HR team, or consult an attorney in your area who specializes in ADA employment law. \ ***Disclaimer:*** *This answer is not a substitute for professional legal advice or a solicitation to offer legal advice regarding specific facts.
