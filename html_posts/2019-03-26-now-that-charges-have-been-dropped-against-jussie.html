**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Now that charges have been dropped against Jussie Smollett can conservatives finally admit that their demonization of him was a rush to judgement?** Oh lord, no. You see, questioner, "dropping charges" does not mean "innocent." It doesn't even mean "not guilty." It simply means the state is not going to exert the effort (and taxpayer dollars) needed to prosecute the crime. In this situation, one of two things may have happened: - Either the prosecution discovered a defect in their case, perhaps something in the background of the witnesses or the defendant that would make their prosecution problematic - or they simply decided to perform what is known as ***deferred prosecution***. In Chicago, there is no requirement to plead guilty in a case of deferred prosecution. We will probably never know which one of these two things actually happened. But while this crime was a travesty, there was no violence, there was no injury to anyone else (except for the alleged emotional injury to Smollett), and prosecutors could have simply used their discretion to drop the charges and not prosecute the case. Or someone could have gotten to them. Someone with great political power. Someone with Hollywood connections. And asked them, in the nicest way possible, to maybe not bother with this case. With all the implied force behind that "nice" request. The truth is, we can speculate all day long, but we'll never know exactly why they decided to drop their case.
