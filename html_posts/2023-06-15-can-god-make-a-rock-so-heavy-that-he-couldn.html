**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} This is a classic thought experiment, but most of these answers rest on a flaw of understanding God's omnipotence. Most people understand God's omnipotence as "God can do anything." But actually, omnipotence does not refer to what God can or can't do. *Omnipotence is a measure of power.* Power is the ability to effect change, to make something happen. God, being omnipotent, has unlimited power. But we know that there are some things which God cannot do. For example, the Bible tells us that God cannot deny His own nature (cf Hebrews 6:18; 2 Timothy 2:13; Titus 1:2). The reason He cannot do these things is because of His nature, and the nature of the universe He created. In other words, **God cannot do things which, in this universe, are impossible to do,** like creating a two-sided triangle. This is because true impossibility is not based on the amount of power used, but on what is actually possible to do. The truly impossible is not something which can be accomplished by simply adding more power. *Impossibility,* therefore, means the same thing whether or not God is involved. So is it possible for God to create a rock so heavy that even He cannot lift it? Considering that God has unlimited power, God has unlimited lifting force. So is it possible, in this universe, for a rock to exist which is of sufficient (infinite) mass such that God's unlimited lifting force cannot move it? The answer is no, but not because God is not omnipotent, but because such a rock cannot be created. You cannot have an infinitely large rock in our universe, because material objects cannot be infinite. Our universe (where matter, time, and space are) is finite, so you cannot have an infinitely-sized rock in a finite universe. Therefore, the question is really asking whether or not God can make a **contradiction**, which He cannot.
