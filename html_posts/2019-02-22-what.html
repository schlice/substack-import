**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What\'s the safest way to protect an infant during an emergency landing?** You can actually buy a seat for your infant, and strap their carrier in, facing rearward, into the seat using the seat's own belt. Ideally, this is the safest thing to do for an infant. If your child is over 22 pounds, they should be traveling in their own seat. Car seats will work, or you can purchase a [[CARES harness](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=25a66d&url=http://kidsflysafe.com/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="kidsflysafe.com"}]{.qlink_container}. Be aware that not all airlines allow you to use child restraints or car seats in their cabins; you will need to check with your carrier before you buy that extra seat. I will assume instead that you are discussing a lap-held infant. This is the way infants travel most of the time. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-0e716b648193b1ba228505a66211ccfd){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-0e716b648193b1ba228505a66211ccfd" master_w="400" master_h="237"} ::: :::: This is a supplemental loop belt. It can be used as a belt extender for plus-sized passengers, or it can be looped in with the passenger belt to form a supplemental belt for a lap-held infant. Infants should wear one of these during takeoff and landing. In the US and Canada, there is no mandate for the airline to provide a loop belt for lap-held children. If the aircraft has one on board, you can always ask for one.[\[1\]](#PzmGx){#cite-PzmGx .citation_link} The safest way to hold your infant in a brace position is this: :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-b08f2a2d2be905e90ef867c82cbb65d4){.portrait .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-b08f2a2d2be905e90ef867c82cbb65d4" master_w="330" master_h="390"} ::: :::: When you get on board, check the safety information card in the seat back pocket in front of you. It will show the most current guidance for lap-held children.
