**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **In light of efforts in community policing, should Warren v. District of Columbia be overturned?** There are a lot of moving parts that have to come together before the precedents begun in *Warren* can be removed. Primarily, what *Warren* did was to declare that law enforcement does not have an obligation to protect any one citizen. Law enforcement has a general duty to protect the public at large, but they do not have specific requirements to protect any one person. This declaration has wide-ranging effects, but primarily it goes to *liability*. For example, without *Warren*, if your child was killed by a drunk driver, you could theoretically sue the police for failing to protect him from that drunk driver. If the police have a specific duty to protect all citizens, the police become legally liable any time someone is hurt. Despite the advancements in community policing, this will never change because it physically can't. We simply don't have enough police officers to make protection of every citizen a reality. We'd literally need **100 times** the amount of police officers we have now, and even then, we'd still only have one cop to three citizens. Think of how high your taxes would have to be to support a police force that is 100 times as large as it is now. And with all of the talk these days about how we have a "police state" (we really don't; read a freakin' history book for crying out loud), do you really *want* the police department to be that large? **TL;DR:** We just don't have nearly enough police officers to make it possible to remove the liability limitations *Warren* grants to the police, and it would be physically impossible to have enough.
