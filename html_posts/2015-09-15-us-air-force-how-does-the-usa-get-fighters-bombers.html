--- title: '"U.S. Air Force: How does the USA get fighters, bombers and helicopters to East Asia (e.g. Japan)?"' date: '2015-09-15' tags: - uncategorized layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [For most normal operations, [[Mike Holovacs](https://www.quora.com/profile/Mike-Holovacs) ]{.qlink_container}is spot on: they fly them there and stop off along the way, or \"tank up\" while in flight. They will place mid-air refueling aircraft (i.e. KC-135 or KC-10) on \"orbit\" (flying a roughly oval pattern in the sky) in strategic locations along the way, allowing aircrews to stop off and \"get gas\" while in flight.\ \ In certain situations, some aircraft can be loaded in C-17 or C-5 cargo haulers and be flown directly to their destination, but it is far more common to simply fly the aircraft directly there.\ \ Also, some aircraft can be fitted with external wing-mounted fuel tanks to carry extra fuel for long-distance flights.]{.rendered_qtext}
