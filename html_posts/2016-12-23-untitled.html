**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *Reading intent into the 240 year old words of slaveowners concerning equality and applying your interpretation of them to governing today is I don't know, a touch presumptuous?* Interesting, because this is exactly what the Supreme Court does every single day: reading intent into the Constitution. That's why we have them: interpreting the Constitution and applying it to today's laws. Also interesting is your attempt to defame the character of the framers by referring to them by the politically-abhorrent moniker of "slaveowners." Yes, they all owned slaves. Our first President owned slaves, and few question his virtues or his leadership capabilities. It was only in later times that owning slaves fell out of fashion, so during the founding, slave ownership was not as politically incorrect (or illegal) as it is today. It's interesting because it sets the tone for your answer. Some of us see it for what it is. Finally, how do you ensure equality of opportunity? You ensure that everyone has the same access to educational and vocational opportunities through as many methods as you possibly can: government grants, industry partnering, education system improvements, family unit strengthening, etc. The Declaration of Independence says that people have the right to *pursue* happiness, which implies that there is no guarantee, from the government or otherwise. Writings from the founders (and writings they drew upon to write the Declaration) also display this thought about the pursuit of happiness. So how does the Supreme Court do it? Thankfully, we don't have to presume any of the framers' intentions, because we have a wealth of volumes of their writings to draw from to understand exactly what they meant by liberty, and that it actually meant freedom of opportunity, not equality of outcome. There are far too many of their writings to add here, so I will leave it to you and your fantastic Google skills to find them. The Supreme Court does it every day. You can do some, too. You don't need evidence that people believe all outcomes should be equal. You can see it in the functioning of the last eight years of progressive government. Candidate Obama said as much to Joe the Plumber before he was elected in 2008. He said, not implied, said, we need to redistribute wealth. That means that people who make too much money need to give it to others who don't make as much. This implies a fundamental philosophy of "equality of outcome" which characterizes Progressive character and thought on governance.
