Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Your Governing Documents (more specifically, your Bylaws) contain all the information about when elections must be held and how residents must be notified about those elections. Read your Bylaws and make sure your HOA is abiding by them. If you are not getting answers, request (in writing) a meeting with the Board. As a resident, you have the right to meet with the Board. If they are still unresponsive, you may be forced to bring legal action against the Board (as is your right as a resident) for failing to act in the best interests of the community, perform its duties reasonably and fairly, and not act in a discriminatory or capricious manner. Failing to meet with homeowners when requested and provide governance information, in an apparent attempt to prevent homeowners from seeking nomination to run for a Board position, would definitely qualify. Also, if the Board is not holding fair elections in accordance with the Bylaws, this is also grounds for legal action.
