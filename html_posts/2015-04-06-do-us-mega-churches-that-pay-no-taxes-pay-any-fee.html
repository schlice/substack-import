--- title: "Do US mega churches, that pay no taxes, pay any fee for the additional police support required to manage traffic in and out of their facilities?" date: "2015-04-06" tags: ["law-enforcement"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [Churches and other organizations requesting police support for their specific event pay for the time the officers provide to their events. This is not a tax. This is a fee for service that is not normally provided during the officer\'s normal shift. On-shift patrols are not tasked to these assignments; these officers are off-duty (i.e. not part of the on-duty crew). Therefore, since the duty falls outside of the officer\'s normal shift time, the department charges a fee for this service. Tax-exempt status does not apply.\ \ There are many \"pay assignments\" that officers have the option of working. In our department, the ones that are not picked up by full-time officers go to the Reserves. The organization will still pay, but any monies received go into the Reserve operating fund, not to the Reserve Deputies. In some rare cases, if a pay assignment has to be filled and no one is biting, they may offer the Reserves the option to receive the pay directly instead of having it funnel to the operating fund, but that is the exception rather than the rule.]{.rendered_qtext}
