**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} So since you asked a question about religion, it only makes sense that your answer come from a religious point of view. It literally makes no sense for atheists to answer religious questions. It's illogical. The Bible does not speak directly about masturbation. But there certainly are principles in the Bible which apply. > Everything that does not come from faith is sin ([[Romans > 14:23](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a0b342&url=https://biblia.com/bible/esv/Rom%252014.23){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="biblia.com"}]{.qlink_container}). This passage tells us that everything we do that is not done faithfully, anything we do that does not honor God, is sinful. Whoa, so like, lots of my thoughts and deeds are sinful. We are sinful creatures. We try to be good, but because we are human, we have a sinful nature. We are only *saved* by the grace of God, but that's another topic. Let's stick to the sin for the moment. > So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the > glory of God ([[1 Corinthians > 10:31](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=a808a1&url=https://biblia.com/bible/esv/1%2520Cor%252010.31){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="biblia.com"}]{.qlink_container}). > > Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit, who is > in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own; you > were bought at a price. Therefore, honor God with your body ([[1 > Corinthians > 6:19--20](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=223f78&url=https://biblia.com/bible/esv/1%2520Cor%25206.19%25E2%2580%259320){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="biblia.com"}]{.qlink_container}). Our bodies have been redeemed and belong to God. Everything we do with our bodies should be to God's glory. If we are not glorifying God with something that we do, it can be sinful. > The fruit of the Spirit is . . . self-control ([[Galatians > 5:22--23](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=54e527&url=https://biblia.com/bible/esv/Gal%25205.22%25E2%2580%259323){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="biblia.com"}]{.qlink_container}). One of the fruits of the Spirit is self-control. Masturbation is almost always a sign of a *lack* of self-control. Masturbation is often driven by *lust,* which in biblical times was defined as a lack of self-control, or a lack of restraint. Lust is desire without restraint, and oftentimes, when people masturbate, it is out of a lack of restraint. So, *can* you masturbate in a way that includes a clear mind, self-control, and a heart towards God? The best we can say is *possibly*. But masturbation is a slippery slope at best. At worst, it can lead to lustful thoughts, a lack of self-control, thinking of other people besides your spouse, and possibly use of pornography, all of which would be a *sinful* form of masturbation. Believers must be very cautious that their minds and hearts are in a right orientation if they choose to partake, in just the same way that they can sin in any number of normal, natural ways, if the heart and mind are not in a right orientation. **TL;DR:** Since the question was specifically related to sin, the answer is that in many cases, masturbation can be sinful. \ Original question: Is masturbation a sin? If yes, how can one stop it?
