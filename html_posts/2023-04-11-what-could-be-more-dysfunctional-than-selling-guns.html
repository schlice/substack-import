**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The ATF intentionally allowing guns to be sold illegally to straw purchasers to try to stop crime. *Fast and Furious.* Y'all remember that program? [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AT\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=fc9e75&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATF_gunwalking_scandal){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} In case you want to read up on it. So you know how people always complain that we have to enforce the laws we already have? The ATF was also the target of such criticism, being accused of going after relatively minor gun charges while leaving the "big fish" to operate freely. To counter this idea, **Project Gunrunner** was born. The idea was to allow southern states to illegally sell firearms to straw buyers so that they could track the guns to Mexican drug cartel leaders and arrest them. The program hoped to block the flow of firearms from the United States into Mexico by catching straw purchasers in the US. One operation under the program was titled **Operation Fast and Furious** when one of the straw purchasers they were tracking was found to be a member of a car club. They allowed over 2,000 firearms to "walk" under the program. They eventually recovered 710 of those firearms. They did arrest a few straw purchasers, but the firearms never made it up to big Mexican drug cartel leaders, and none were ever arrested. Worse, the guns found their way back into the United States. Guns from the program were found on both sides of the US-Mexico border, being responsible for the deaths of at least 150 Mexican civilians and a US Border Patrol agent (Brian Terry).
