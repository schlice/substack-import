**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You've got two questions in one, but i'll answer both. Let's split them up: **Does Jesus kill Satan or throw him in the lake of fire if he kills him is that considered murder?** I like how you're trying to apply human criminal law philosophy to the actions of God. That's cute. Like God is subject to our laws. Murder is a crime. But the basis of it is an unjustified killing. If Jesus were to kill Satan, it would most certainly be justified. **Will there be people left on earth when Jesus returns, could some of them be nonbelievers will the non believers be able to stand Jesus glory?** There are plenty of Bible passages which talk about the difference between those who are saved and those who are not saved. The upshot of all of this is that some people will not be saved. These are the people who reject Jesus' gift. If they are non-believers, then they will not be saved. It has nothing to do with not standing his glory. It has everything to do with whether or not they will accept Jesus as their savior.
