**Content:** [The answer is \"it depends on which one you think tastes better.\"\ \ Since \"which is better\" is a subjective answer, I will tell you about the definite difference between the two.\ \ In Italy, and anywhere traditional Italian pizza is made, it is cooked in a brick oven using hardwoods as the heat source. The pizza oven heats to very high temperature (approaching anywhere from 850-1000 degrees), cooking the pizza in minutes and producing a hard, crunchy crust. Italian pizzas are generally smaller, and are easy to eat like a cracker, retaining its shape as you pick it up.\ \ When Italian pizza recipes were brought to America (to New York City, where most immigrants came, and later to Chicago), pizza ovens were not wood-fired, as there wasn\'t much wood to be had in the big city. Coal-fired ovens were the order of the day, and the temperature was significantly (in pizza-land) cooler, around 450-550 degrees. This resulted in longer baking times and produced softer crusts than you could achieve with a wood oven. Thus, you see New York-style pizza as very large, thin slices that are routinely \"folded\" down the middle for ease of eating.\ \ Electric pizza ovens are usually set to around 500-550 degrees. They don\'t generate the same quality of heat that brick wood-fired ovens do, and the pizza cooks differently.]{.rendered_qtext}
