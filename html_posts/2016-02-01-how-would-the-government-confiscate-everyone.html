**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The first thing the government would have to do would be to repeal the 2nd, 4th, 5th and 14th Amendments to the Constitution, as well as Article 1, Paragraphs 9 and 10 (they could accomplish such a major re-write during a Constitutional Convention, but because it would be so easy to make wholesale changes to large swaths of the Constitution, states will never authorize such a measure). Specifically, problematic areas of each of those Amendments: - 2nd Amendment: Right to Keep and Bear Arms - 4th Amendment: Right to be Free from Unreasonable Search and Seizure - 5th Amendment: Due Process clause - 14th Amendment: Applied Due Process to states - Article 1 Paragraphs 9 and 10: Prevent *ex post facto* laws So assuming that they could pass a Constitutional amendment that would repeal the above-listed Amendments and repeal Paragraphs 9 and 10 from Article 1, they could then pass a law making all guns currently owned by citizens illegal. Since you are criminalizing behavior that was previously illegal, you would have to repeal Article 1 Paragraphs 9 and 10 to allow you write such an *ex post facto* law. Once you have made gun ownership illegal, you can force citizens to turn in their guns and you wouldn\'t have to reimburse them for the guns, since you repealed the 5th and 14th Amendments. That will save you tons of cash. Which is good, because you\'ll need it for the next phase. If there were still guns in circulation (you might, on a good day, get 5% of guns turned in voluntarily), you could then authorize the police to go door-to-door to search every house for weapons, and confiscate all the ones you find (your repeal of the 4th Amendment would make this legal). The military is specifically prevented from enforcing any civilian laws under the Posse Comitatus Act, so only local police can accomplish this task. The National Guard can only enforce state laws, so unless a state repealed its own gun protections and outlawed guns (also no guarantees there), the National Guard could also not enforce such a law. The police would be heavily stretched as they would have to employ off-duty officers, working overtime, to perform all the confiscations, and that funding would need to come from the Federal government. The cost for the overtime alone would be in the hundreds of millions of dollars. Then the weapons would have to be shipped somewhere and destroyed, another cost in the tens of millions of dollars. This wouldn\'t stop the illegal flow of weapons into the country, and also wouldn\'t prevent some gun nut from burying his guns (I assume many would), so you still would go through all this effort and 1. criminals would still have and acquire guns, 2. you\'d never get them all, and 3. you\'d instantly create criminals out of formerly law-abiding citizens, many will choose to remain that way (look at the mandatory registration in Connecticut as an example). All of those hundreds of millions of dollars spent and guns will still be there.
