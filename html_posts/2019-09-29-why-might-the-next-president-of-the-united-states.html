--- title: '"Why might the next President of the United States push for a constitutional amendment that says that the president can be indicted while in office, even if that unsealed indictment is then scheduled for hearings after the President leaves office?"' date: '2019-09-29' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Why might the next President of the United States push for a constitutional amendment that says that the president can be indicted while in office, even if that unsealed indictment is then scheduled for hearings after the President leaves office?** No President would do this, because it is unnecessary. The Constitution provides that ***impeachment*** is the method for dealing with Presidential lawlessness. Remember that impeachment is a *political* remedy, while prosecution is a *legal* remedy, and prosecution has a much higher bar to overcome than impeachment. Besides, the power of prosecution is resident in a single unitary President, under the Constitution. This wasn't done to allow for Presidential lawlessness (see Impeachment). It was done to provide a hedge against Congressional overreach and abuse of power. Congress was routinely seen as the most powerful of the three branches, and it was the vesting of prosecutorial authority in the President that was one of the means to counter that power.
