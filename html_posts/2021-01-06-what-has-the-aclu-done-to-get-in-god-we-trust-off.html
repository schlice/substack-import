**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What has the ACLU done to get In God We Trust off our government buildings and currency and stop official prayers from being asked in those same buildings?** They have done as much as they can do, but they are fighting an uphill battle. Groups trying to remove the phrase from our currency have filed many suits through the ACLU, and the firm has written many *amicus briefs* supporting the removal of the phrase. However, they have not been able to overcome the doctrine of *accommodationism,* which is a doctrine which basically states that governments can endorse religious groups so long as they do so equally. Also, "In God We Trust" is our *national motto* and has been so since 1956. It's been in our national anthem as our "motto" since the poem was penned in 1814 (although it wasn't actually adopted as our national anthem until later). It's not much of an argument to say that you want the national motto removed from the national currency because reasons.
