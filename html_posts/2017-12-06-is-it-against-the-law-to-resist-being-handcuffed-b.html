**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it against the law to resist being handcuffed by a police officer?** Yes, it is a crime. In most jurisdictions it is called "resisting arrest." It can be added to the list of crimes for which you were being arrested in the first place. It also raises the level of force authorized to the officer to bring you into compliance (in other words, you get hurt more). As we've said many times on Quora, if you don't feel that the arrest is legal, or if you feel that the officer has done something wrong, the time to argue this is when you're standing in front of the judge, not when the officer is applying handcuffs.
