**Content:** [Not all departments use the same terminology, so not everyone will know what a \"5150 hold\" is. You\'re asking about a \"psych hold,\" or what we commonly call \"emergency hospitalization for evaluation\" (EHE).\ \ Every state is different, and has different standards. The police in that state know the rules for that state. [[Here\'s a breakdown](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=ec1b8f&url=http://treatmentadvocacycenter.org/storage/documents/Emergency_Hospitalization_for_Evaluation.pdf){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="treatmentadvocacycenter.org"}]{.qlink_container} of the rules by state.\ \ Basically, in most states, if a police officer or other \"intervening official\" sees someone whom he reasonably believes has a mental defect or deficiency, and that person is deemed to be a potential threat to himself/herself or to others, the intervening official can place the person into custody or cause the person to be placed into custody, and have that person treated by a mental health professional for (usually) 72 hours.]{.rendered_qtext}
