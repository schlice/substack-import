**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **What percent of murders are committed with illegally obtained guns?** The actual answer is that we don't exactly know. We can come fairly close to a good answer. Interestingly, Joe Scarborough made the claim on his show that something like 3% of murders are committed with legally purchased guns. Politifact [[looked into that claim](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=61080e&url=http://www.politifact.com/punditfact/statements/2015/oct/05/joe-scarborough/msnbcs-joe-scarborough-tiny-fraction-crimes-commit/){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="politifact.com"}]{.qlink_container}. It seems that a lot of these numbers are being derived from interviews of prisoners. But here's what we do know: > In 2004, the government conducted its periodic [[*Survey of Inmates in > State and Federal Correctional > Facilities*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=440217&url=http://www.bjs.gov/content/pub/pdf/fv9311.pdf){.external_link > rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" > onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" > qt-tooltip="bjs.gov"}]{.qlink_container}. It found that among inmates > who had a gun when they committed their crime (16 percent of all > prisoners), about 11 percent had bought the firearm at a retail store, > a pawn shop, a flea market or a gun show. Another 37 percent had > gotten it from a friend or family member. About 40 percent said they > got it illegally on the black market, from a drug dealer or by > stealing it. > > \[...\] > > \[W\]hile 3 percent or 10 percent might not be the exact number of > legally purchased firearms used by criminals, the fraction is in that > ballpark. > > \"I think it's safe to say that a low percentage of criminal assaults > and robberies are committed with guns that were acquired by legal > purchase from a gun store." > > Joseph Olson, a professor at Hamline University School of Law and > former board member of the National Rifle Association, believes that > the number of legally acquired firearms used by criminals is > negligible. > > \"Criminals don't go through background checks because they know they > wouldn\'t pass them,\" Olson said.[\[1\]](#ArvcR){#cite-ArvcR > .citation_link} So I think the answer to your question is "a very low percentage" of firearms used by criminals in crimes are actually legally purchased. We know that the number of mass shooters below 21 who legally purchased their guns is strikingly low: **one**. The others either stole them or made straw purchases.
