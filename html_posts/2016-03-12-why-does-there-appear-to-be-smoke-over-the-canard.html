Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** [This is water vapor condensing when the air pressure over the top of the wing gets lower than the pressure needed to tip the dew point.\ \ Basically, the dew point is a combination of humidity, temperature and pressure, at which airborne water condenses. The air over the top of an aircraft wing is at a lower pressure than the surrounding air. When this happens at the correct humidity and temperature, the water in the air condenses and becomes visible water vapor. This is also why water condenses on a cold drink, for example.]{.rendered_qtext}
