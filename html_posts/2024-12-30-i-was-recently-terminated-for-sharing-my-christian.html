Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As a Christian, I tell you this in the most loving and caring way I possibly can. **You are free to say anything you like, even to share the Gospel.** **You are** ***not*** **free from the consequences of what you say.** You are free to tell an atheist that he's going to burn in hell. But if that atheist tells your employer what you did, your employer can choose to terminate you for any reason they like, or no reason at all. The Constitution grants you the right to say anything you want. The Constitution does not make you *immune* to any consequences from that speech. If your company decided that you are not the type of person that they wish to keep employed, then they can fire you. You don't have a right to work, and you don't have a right to say anything you choose without consequence. My employer could similarly fire me for what I write on Quora. I assume that risk when I voice my opinion on this platform. I am under no disillusion that I can somehow sue my former employer for unlawful termination because they didn't agree with the content of my free speech. Again, you are free to say whatever you like to whomever you like. But you are not free from the legitimate and expected consequences of such speech. Your speech indicates your character, and your company can absolutely hold its employees to minimum standards of character.
