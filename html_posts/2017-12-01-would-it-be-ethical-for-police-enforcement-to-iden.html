--- title: "Would it be ethical for police enforcement to identify potential future offenders by creating baits and honeypots?" date: "2017-12-01" tags: ["law-enforcement", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Would it be ethical for police enforcement to identify potential future offenders by creating baits and honeypots?** No, because you don't identify potential future offenders by offering them an opportunity to offend. That's entirely unethical and the absolute wrong way to find offenders. But word your question a little better and you'll get a better answer: **Would it be ethical for police enforcement to identify** ***current*** **offenders by creating baits and honeypots?** Yes, baits and honeypots are most definitely ethical. Baits and honeypots do not provide *motivation* to commit crime.\ Baits and honeypots do not provide *incentive* to motivate an otherwise law-abiding citizen to commit crime.\ Baits and honeypots do not *entice* otherwise law-abiding citizens to commit crime. The only people *enticed, motivated, or incentivized* to commit crime in the presence of a bait or a honeypot are *those who would have committed crime anyway.* Therefore, ethically, using baits and honeypots is acceptable.
