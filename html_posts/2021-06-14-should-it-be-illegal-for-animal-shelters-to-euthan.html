**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Not necessarily. It's not any more humane to leave an unwanted or potentially violent pet alive simply because we have no method to euthanize them. To live out a life devoid of love or human contact is torture, and that is a far worse fate than euthanasia. What ***should*** be illegal is using a gas chamber to euthanize animals. While the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA) has declared that euthanasia by injection is the most humane method of euthanasia currently available, it has failed to condemn the use of gas chambers in shelters. I'll spare you the details of euthanasia by gas chamber, but just know that it causes the animal to suffer in pain and fear for potentially minutes before it finally succumbs. No animal deserves to die like that. While we can't eliminate euthanasia for unwanted or potentially dangerous pets, we can at least make euthanasia as humane as possible. The number one way to do this is to endorse and promote euthanasia by injection, and end the use of the gas chamber for euthanasia. We are making progress. Since 2013, approximately 70 shelters in 13 states have voluntarily removed their gas chambers. But there are only 27 states with full bans in place, and gas chambers are known to still be in use in at least four states. The Humane Society of the US is driving to have bans passed in all 50 states so that no pet\'s life ever ends in a gas chamber. They have been helping shelters voluntarily transition away from the gas chamber to the more humane euthanasia by injection by providing grants and training and has assisted states with passing outright chamber bans. \ Original question: Should it be illegal for animal shelters to euthanize a healthy animal?
