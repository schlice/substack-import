--- title: '"Is it a crime to have a person of diminished capacity sign legal paperwork?"' date: '2016-03-04' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} (Question was edited) It is only a crime to have a person sign a power of attorney when they have diminished mental capacity if the recipient of such power intends to use it to criminal ends; i.e. to defraud the assignor. If it is done to facilitate decision making by a spouse when the other spouse begins to lose mental capacity due to disease or defect, it is probably not criminal. Most generally, if the power is assigned to a spouse, the marital contract takes over, as the spouse would be the beneficiary of the marital assets should the assignor pass. So the PoA doesn\'t pose any real additional risk to the financial situation if the recipient of the power is a spouse.
