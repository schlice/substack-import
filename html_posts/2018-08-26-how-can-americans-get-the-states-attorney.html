**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can Americans get the States Attorney\'s Office to enforce the Title 18 Sections 241, 242, 245, and 249, against Law Enforcement agencies?** Why would the state attorney be reviewing violations of Federal law? It's kinda not his department. The US Department of Justice would be reviewing the case, which they already (usually) do. However, I think you may be confusing *enforcement* with *findings you like*. Nearly every time there is an Officer-Involved Shooting (OIS), the Justice Department of the United States investigates the circumstances to see if there are any violations of the victim's rights under Title 18 USC Sections 241, 242, 245, or 249. It happens all the time when there is a police shooting, along with reviews of the incident by the District Attorney, and the police department which employs the officer. If you have standing (i.e. you are part of the estate of the victim), and the Justice Department has not investigated, you can request that they review the incident. You do have to have standing in order to make that request, however. Beyond this narrow scope, the Justice Department does not have enforcement jurisdiction over state law, so you can't get the Federal government to do something that they have not been authorized to do in the Constitution.
