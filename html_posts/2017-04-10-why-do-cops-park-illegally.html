--- title: '"Why do cops park illegally?"' date: '2017-04-10' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The short answer is that cops can't park "illegally." Police vehicles are considered "emergency vehicles" in most states' vehicle and traffic laws. This means that the traffic law doesn't apply to them. A police car can't pull over another police car and charge him with speeding. A police car technically can't be in excess of the speed limit, because the speed limit technically does not apply to police officers. Granted, there are always department policies that limit what an officer can and cannot do with a police cruiser , but a police vehicle cannot operate illegally as far as the law is concerned. But like [[Christopher Finch](https://www.quora.com/profile/Christopher-Finch-5)]{.qlink_container} said, police officers often have to curtail their stops quickly and head out to other emergency calls, so having their vehicle close by (which sometimes means parking against a red curb, or on a sidewalk, or someplace that you think might be otherwise illegal) is imperative. Also, that cruiser is their office. It has all of their emergency supplies, their police computer, and it is the temporary holding "cell" for people being placed under arrest. It needs to be as close as possible.
