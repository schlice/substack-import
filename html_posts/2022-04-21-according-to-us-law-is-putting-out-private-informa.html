**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} What you are referring to is an act called doxing. [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Do\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=68e742&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doxing){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} In the United States, there are very few laws surrounding doxing. There are two Federal laws which might address it, but they are old and were never intended to address this issue. States are coming up to speed with laws against doxing, and victims would be best served by looking to their state to address it: > Much of the conduct that is considered "doxing" may fall under > multiple state laws relating to **cyber stalking, stalking, > harassment, threats, or extortion** (e.g., threatening to make > information public if money is not paid).
