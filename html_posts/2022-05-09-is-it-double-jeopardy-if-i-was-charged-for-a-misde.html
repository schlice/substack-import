--- title: "Is it double jeopardy if I was charged for a misdemeanor theft first in one court, then charged with a felony burglary in a superior court?" date: "2022-05-09" tags: ["policy-analysis", "law"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Charging does not cause jeopardy to attach. In criminal cases, jeopardy attaches when a jury is sworn. In civil cases, jeopardy attaches when the first witness is sworn. If these things have not happened, then jeopardy has not attached, and double jeopardy does not apply. \ Original question: Is it double jeopardy if I was charged for a misdemeanor theft first in one court, then charged with a felony burglary in a superior court? \ *This answer is not professional legal advice, nor is it a substitute for professional legal advice, or a solicitation to offer professional legal advice.
