Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Once you understand the basis of the pro-life movement, you should be able to easily answer this question yourself. The very existence of this question means that people still refuse to accept the true basis of the pro-life movement. **Pro-life people value** ***all life equally,*** **regardless of its stage of development.** That's it. When you boil it all down, that's what we believe. So let's look at your parasitic twin example. Parasitic twins are conjoined twins, but one twin develops mostly normally, while the other twin does not. The incompletely formed twin is usually dependent on the other, mostly fully formed twin. **However, none of this changes the value of either life.** If separating the twins will kill one of them, then separation surgery is not justified. If they would live longer staying conjoined, then separation surgery is not justified. If, however, separation surgery will not kill them, but will leave them both permanently disabled, then separation surgery **is** morally justified, because neither twin will be killed by the surgery. They would each have to accept the results of being separated and the health problems that the separation will cause. This is completely in line with the pro-life movement, which values all life equally. No one should have the right to say that one twin should live and another should die, regardless of their status of formation or the dependence of one on the other. All life is valuable and deserves equal protection. Both twins have value because they are both human and they are both alive. We believe that all living humans possess an equal right to life, and to not have that life taken from them without due process.
