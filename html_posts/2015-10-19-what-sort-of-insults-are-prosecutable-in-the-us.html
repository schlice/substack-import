**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} > *Question Details:* > > *By this I want to know what are the boundaries of acceptable > constitutional free speech right against a person. So for example, > I\'m wondering if I call someone \'son of a bitch\', can he sue me for > false accusation/defamation? If not, when an insult becomes sue-able?* A2A Speaking like an asshat is absolutely protected by the First Amendment. You have the right to make as big a fool of yourself as you like. Calling names, cursing, and generally being a foul human being is Constitutionally protected in the United States. Once you cross the line into threatening speech, or speech that incites a riot, or speech that incites others to illegal or dangerous action, you start crossing into territory which can be prosecuted simply for speaking. \"Son of a bitch\" is an opinion, but simply stating your opinion about someone does not rise to an actionable level unless the speech causes some harm. In order to be sued for slander, your speech must cause demonstrable harm to someone else. For example, if you called Donald Trump a philanderer and it cost him the election, he could sue you for slander and defamation of character, because the lie had a demonstrable harm that can be proved.
