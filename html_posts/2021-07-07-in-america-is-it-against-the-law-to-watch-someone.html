{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} *(US answer only)* There is no "duty to intervene" law in the United States when it comes to suicide. The only laws that might apply are for certain categories of people who are "mandatory reporters" who learn of someone's desire to commit a crime, or to harm themselves, and are required by law to report that. Some mandatory reporters might be doctors, teachers, mental health professionals, etc. And again, this only applies if you "watch" and do nothing. If you did *anything at all* to "encourage" or "assist" the person in their suicide, then you can be held criminally and civilly liable for the death. But to answer your specific question, it is not against the law to watch someone commit suicide and not intervene to stop them. \ Original question: In America is it against the law to watch someone commit suicide and not stop it?
