--- title: '"Is it legal for my landlord to place a surveillance camera in a shared living room?"' date: '2017-07-18' tags: - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} As with all legal answers, the correct answer is "it depends." In order to be absolutely sure, you would need to consult with a lawyer in your area. I'll try to respond in a general way, for informational purposes only. So long as the camera **does not record audio** (there are separate laws which cover that), and is in a **shared space**, not a space in which there is a reasonable expectation of privacy (such as a bedroom or a bathroom), then a landlord can likely install a surveillance camera for security purposes. The laws in your area may require that he post signage advising all who enter that there are security cameras and entering the space implies consent to monitoring, but those laws vary. Your local laws may not require this.
