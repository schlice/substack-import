**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Is it against the law to agree to kill someone?** Let's find the exact point where you have run afoul of the law. Remember, there are many differences between the states with respect to other things with which you may be charged (solicitation to commit murder comes to mind), but I'll try to highlight the most common in the US. A woman says, "Will you kill my husband?" and you say "Yes." **NOT ILLEGAL.\ -** At this point, you have not yet technically done anything illegal, other than speak freely. In the United States, simply making the verbal agreement does not constitute conspiracy; there must be an "overt act" in furtherance of the crime by at least one of the parties. You plan out how you will commit a murder. **NOT ILLEGAL\ -** While pretty damning circumstantial evidence (especially if the husband turns up dead later and his death matches your plan), it's still not illegal to plan out how you might kill someone. You might be writing a murder mystery novel. Just making a plan to kill someone might be stretched to be the "overt act" needed to prove conspiracy, but it would be a stretch (as I said, you might be writing a murder mystery novel). A day later, you buy a gun. **POSSIBLY ILLEGAL\ -** Gun purchases are not illegal, even if you have conversations a day prior. However, in some cases, purchasing the gun after having the conversation could be considered the "overt act" needed to prove conspiracy to commit murder. You drive to the husband's house with the gun and the plan in your car. **DEFINITELY ILLEGAL\** - Driving around with a gun in your car and a plan on how to kill someone is not, by itself, illegal. However, this is the point at which you have actually engaged in criminal activity. You can now be charged with attempted murder when you have made a step towards committing the crime of which you are accused. You and the wife (with whom you made the verbal agreement) can still be charged with conspiracy to commit murder.
