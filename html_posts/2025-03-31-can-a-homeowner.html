**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In the VAST majority of cases, no. But as with all legal answers, this one is also "it depends." You'll need to check your Governing Documents, and what you'll usually find are two clauses which largely negate most claims: - The ARB is the **sole arbiter** of decisions regarding whether or not a proposed change will be approved. It is nothing more than *the subjective opinion of the members of the ARB* whether or not a change looks good and fits with the overall vibe of the neighborhood that they are trying to maintain. - A similar approval for one neighbor does not guarantee an approval for another neighbor. You *may* be able to claim a pattern of targeted denials if you can show a pattern in the overall neighborhood with respect to your requests versus everyone else's requests, but that would be difficult to substantiate. Bottom line, if you think you have a claim against your HOA, contact a real estate attorney in your local area who specializes in HOA law.
