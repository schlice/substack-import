--- title: '"What can a Homeowners Association (HOA) demand of its members?"' date: '2023-09-18' tags: - law - homeowners-assn layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} An HOA can only demand of its members what it is permitted to demand either by state law, or within its Covenants, Conditions, and Regulations (CC&Rs), the foundational documents which establish the authority of the HOA. The HOA can only demand that homeowners abide by the CC&Rs. But here's where people get tripped up. While the CC&Rs can usually only be modified through an amendment process and a percentage of homeowners agreeing to those changes, the CC&R usually permits the HOA to create Rules and Regulations (R&Rs) which assist in the administration of the rules in the CC&R, and those are created and edited by the Board of Directors. And you have to follow those rules as well. And yes, the HOA Board can demand you follow the R&Rs or face fines, liens and potential loss of your home. Usually, those rules revolve around what the outside of your home must look like, and who gets to approve those changes. You can certainly legally challenge those Rules and say that they violate the CC&R documents, or that they violate state law, but if they don't do either, and the Board can articulate a valid reason that the rule in question advances the responsibility of the Board (to keep home values as high as possible), then the rule is probably not illegal, and you have to do it.
