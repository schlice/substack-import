**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Weird police question, but can a cop pull you over at a red light and tell you to run the light to pull over? Can a cop demand that you break the law to pull you over or would that be considered entrapment?** *Can* a police officer do this? Yes, they can. Is it entrapment? No, it is not. You were following a lawful directive by a police officer. Entrapment is when a government agent entices you to break a law that you would not normally break. When an officer gives you a directive, you are bound by law to follow it, or you could be charged with failing to follow a lawful order. The order is lawful because a police officer gave you the order while performing his official duty under color of law, not because what he's asking you to do would be otherwise illegal. If it were entrapment, then it would be a natural catch-22. You either follow the directive and you are guilty of breaking the law, or you fail to follow the directive and you are guilty of breaking the law. In practice, the officer will simply wait behind you until the light turns green and you are safely through the intersection before he activates his lights. Or he'll activate his lights behind you at the red light and he'll use the PA system to advise you that once the light turns green you are to proceed through the intersection and then pull over.
