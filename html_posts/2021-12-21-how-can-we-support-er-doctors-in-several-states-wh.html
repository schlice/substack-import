**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Tell them to either: 1. Quit lying to you to try to scare you into supporting them 2. Go back to school and learn what triage is. I can guarantee you that no one is being refused life-saving care because they are "too busy" with COVID-19 patients. Patients are seen based on their *criticality*, not whether or not a patient has COVID-19. A process known as *triage* takes place when you arrive at a hospital. They determine just how critical your case is, and you are seen in the order of your criticality. :::: ui_qtext_image_outer ::: qtext_image_wrapper ![](images/qimg-31af4200a4154f1d08e90c1ee7804b9c){.landscape .qtext_image .zoomable_in_feed master_src="images/qimg-31af4200a4154f1d08e90c1ee7804b9c" master_w="1000" master_h="547"} ::: :::: *(From* [[*The Conversation*](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=37d016&url=https://theconversation.com/from-triage-to-discharge-a-users-guide-to-navigating-hospitals-54658){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="theconversation.com"}]{.qlink_container}*)* This is what determines when patients get seen. So it doesn't matter who has what, what matters is where they fall on the criticality chart. The highest category cases are seen first. Now, what you may be actually hearing is the fact that *hospitals are very busy*, and 30--40% of their cases may be COVID-19 cases. It is also true that in some smaller regional hospitals (which were already running at 80% of capacity under normal operating conditions), the surge of COVID-19 cases may be causing them to run out of regular beds. It is at that time that the hospital should be implementing its emergency plans. Just as we did at the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, we should be converting sections of the hospital into ICU space or COVID-19 space or triage space, depending on what is needed. And if the hospital implements their emergency plans and they *still* run out of space? They should be calling on the state for assistance. For a hospital to overrun its total beds and allow any patients to be turned away is a travesty of hospital administration, and a failure to implement emergency plans in a timely manner.
