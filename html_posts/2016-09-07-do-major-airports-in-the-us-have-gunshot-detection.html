**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The answer to any question that is worded in the **why doesn't X have Y technology which is currently available** is the same: money. When you are deciding whether or not to make a purchase (such as buying gunshot detection systems for an airport), you do what's known as a *cost-benefit analysis.* You have to compare the cost to the benefit you will receive from the purchase. If the benefit exceeds the cost, you make the purchase. If not, you do not. It's actually far more nuanced than that, but that's the basic thrust of the argument. In order for a gunshot detection system to be cost-effective, it must address a frequent problem in a better/more efficient/faster way than it is currently being addressed. So to start, you have to address a frequent problem. This, most assuredly, is not. So right off the bat, we're not addressing a frequent problem, so the cost will likely be considered too high to offset the minimal benefit you will see from implementing the system.
