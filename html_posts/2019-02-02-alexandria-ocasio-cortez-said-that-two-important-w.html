**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez said that two important ways to fix pay gap are to stop asking for salary history and allow salary info to be shared at work. What do you think about this proposal?** It would do absolutely nothing to fix the gender pay gap. First of all, it's already illegal to discriminate on the basis of sex. The Equal Pay Act of 1963 ensured that men and women with equal skills will get equal pay. But the *Paycheck Fairness Act*, which AOC is pushing, won't do anything to improve pay equality. Instead, it will tightly regulate how employers compensate their employees (in the name of ensuring "fairness"), which will do little more than eliminate things like the employer's ability to tie your compensation with the quality of your work, or your time in service. It will regulate what employers are allowed to pay employees, which will only reduce workplace flexibility in pay, which will make pay worse for everyone. But hey, if you're a lawyer, you'll love this legislation, because it will make it much easier for an employee to file a lawsuit alleging unfair pay practices. So the bill is certainly good for one group of employees. So why are we still talking about these things? Because the "women make 77 cents for every dollar a man makes" trope is still out there, even though it's been debunked hundreds of times over. Now, I'm not saying that there aren't dishonest employers who will seek to underpay their employees every step of the way They do exist, and no one who logically engages in this discussion supports that. No one truly believes that a woman should make less than a man simply based on what's between their legs. That is patently ridiculous.
