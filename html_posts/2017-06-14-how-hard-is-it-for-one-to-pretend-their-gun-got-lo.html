--- title: '"How hard is it for one to pretend their gun got lost/stolen when it was actually sold on the black market?"' date: '2017-06-14' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} Committing crimes is not hard. It's no harder to sell your gun illegally than it is to sell it legally. The "black market" is simply when you don't follow the law and/or regulations when you buy or sell your gun. The reason you sell your gun legally is because it protects **you**. If you sell your gun to someone who uses it to commit a crime and they manage to trace the gun back to you, some will seek to hold you liable for having a part in the crime. Now, I know that this is a tough connection for them to make, but they will try, at great personal expense to both your wallet and your reputation. Add to this the fact that you will be discovered as illegally selling a weapon, and the consequences could be staggering, as you can be separately prosecuted for that crime (which you actually did do), and that's a felony. You're looking at a minimum of a year in jail and more fines. Finally, as if that wasn't enough, you then want to lie to your insurance company and claim that your property was stolen. In order to do this, you need to file a police report. If you make it through the investigation (and you're not the first person to ever sell stuff and claim it was stolen), you have committed another crime: filing a false report. If you then take the false report and make an insurance claim using it, you have committed *yet another* crime: insurance fraud. Personally, I don't think the best remedy for committing a crime is to commit *more crimes.* So it's not that it is difficult to do. It's the consequences which could be difficult to live with if you are caught.
