--- title: '"My father has multiple sclerosis and he takes chemotherapy every 7 months. What can cure him completely from this illness?"' date: '2018-02-07' tags: - health-autoimmune layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I am sorry that your father has MS. I am even sorrier to report that there currently is no cure for MS. However, that does not mean your father will not live a long and happy life. With the current list of drugs for MS, lifespans for people with the disease nearly match the lifespans of people without MS. We can control the symptoms of the disease. We can keep relapses at bay, and when they do happen, we can make them as short as possible, along with increasing the amount of time between relapses. While not a cure, the drugs at least provide the ability to live as normal a life as possible with MS.
