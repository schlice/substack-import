Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} I've never heard of MS lesions referred to as "brain damage." I think generally we associate "damage" with injury rather than illness. But in a purely technical sense, the brain is "damaged" when myelin is destroyed by white blood cells and replaced by plaques (or scars, thus the term "multiple sclerosis," meaning "many scars"). And while [[Robert Pfaff](https://www.quora.com/profile/Robert-Pfaff-5) ]{.qlink_container}offers some valuable insight about Vitamin D, I must caution you against too much self-diagnosis or self-medicating, even with vitamins. Because while we do know that Vitamin D can have a prophylactic effect against development of the disease (while you are young), we don't know exactly what its action is, when it is effective, or how much you should take.
