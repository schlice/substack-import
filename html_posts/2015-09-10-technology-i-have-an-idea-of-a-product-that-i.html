**Content:** [It looks like you need to file for patent protection.\ \ You can patent your idea if it\'s a process, a machine, an article of manufacture, a composition of matter, or an improvement of any of these. Abstract ideas, natural phenomenon, and inventions deemed not useful are not eligible to be patented.\ \ The US Patent and Trademark Office has a list of what can and cannot be patented. That\'s where I would start.\ \ [[United States Patent and Trademark Office](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=313c74&url=http://uspto.gov){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="uspto.gov"}]{.qlink_container}\ \ Once you have patented your idea, it is protected from being copied. You can disclose your patented idea. You can turn over your patented idea to someone else using whatever terms you choose (i.e. payment, requirement that your name be used with the idea, etc). But before you do that, I strongly suggest you consult with a patent and trademark attorney to ensure that what you want to happen is really what will happen.]{.rendered_qtext}
