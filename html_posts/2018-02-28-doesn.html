**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Doesn\'t it make the public lose trust and respect for law enforcement when police as blatantly violate traffic laws and park illegally when clearly not on call, including speeding and parking in handicap spots?** First, police vehicles are not classified as "motor vehicles" in the vehicle and traffic laws of almost every city and state in the country. They are classified as "emergency vehicles" and are therefore not subject to those regulations. Have you ever seen a parking ticket on a police car? You can't, because they are not classified in the same way as motor vehicles which are subject to traffic laws. That said, there are always department rules regarding where vehicles should park and where they shouldn't. Usually, departments avoid parking cruisers in marked handicapped spots and instead will park in a fire lane or in a similar "No Parking" location, for reasons which we'll get to. Secondly, police officers need quick access to their vehicles if they get an emergency call. If you were trapped in a building and needed police to respond quickly, would you want the police officer to have quick access to his vehicle to respond as fast as possible, or would you rather have him make his way out to a parking spot and contend with all of the headaches of negotiating a parking lot rather than responding to your call? Because they need rapid access to their vehicles, they often park in areas which would be illegal for you to park your own personal car. If this practice makes you lose respect for the police, it's only because you don't have a clear understanding of why it happens. Therefore, the loss of respect is your problem, not the police officer's problem.
