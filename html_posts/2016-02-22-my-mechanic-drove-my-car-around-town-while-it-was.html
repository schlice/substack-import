--- title: "My mechanic drove my car around town while it was supposedly being repaired. Do I have any recourse?" date: "2016-02-22" tags: ["policy-analysis"] layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} When you drop your car off at the shop, you expect them to repair it and then take it for a test drive, so some driving of your vehicle to verify the fixes is expected. The question is reasonableness. Was the amount of mileage driven \"reasonable\" to verify the repairs performed? Was extra driving required to verify the repairs? The shop owner will likely be able to justify his use of the vehicle in testing his repairs, but it\'s not up to him or you, it\'s up to the court to determine what is \"reasonable.\" I would suggest following [[Nick Bauer](https://www.quora.com/profile/Nick-Bauer-10)]{.qlink_container}\'s advice and start by talking to the shop owner. Do not be accusatory in your tone. Show the shop owner the toll receipts, discuss the amount that the vehicle was driven and explain that you don\'t think it was reasonable. If the shop owner agrees with you, you might be able to persuade him to chip in on the cost of your rental car. If you are not satisfied after talking to the shop owner, you are certainly within your rights to sue the shop and request reimbursement of your rental car. Depending on how much money you are talking about, this could be handled in small-claims court, without lawyers. But if we are talking larger sums of money, you may need an attorney to assist you with filing your lawsuit.
