**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Do you think that a public housing authority can be sued if a person is murdered on the property?** Of course they can. Just because they're not actually criminally responsible for the murder doesn't mean that they are completely free from any culpability. For example, the facility still must provide basic security measures like lockable doors, and if they failed to do this, they can still be held to account for that failure. If they have a lockable lobby which is only accessible to tenants, and someone besides a tenant gained access to that lobby, they can be held responsible for that failure.
