**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **If it costs us millions of dollars every time Trump takes a trip, how much are his rallies costing us, and why is it okay? How can he campaign on U.S. tax money?** Some of the costs of any president's political campaign travel (not just Trump) is paid by the taxpayer. For example, Secret Service details guard the president 24x7, regardless of where he is. This is true whether he's visiting Pittsburgh for a funeral, traveling to a campaign rally, or going on vacation. This is just one of the costs we as taxpayers bear to protect the president, regardless of what he's doing or where he goes. But some other costs when campaigning, like the cost to run Air Force One, are not paid for by taxpayers. Federal law requires that campaign travel is reimbursed by the campaign. In Trump's case, the RNC pays for that portion of his travel. In the most recently available figures, it costs approximately \$180,118 per flight hour for Air Force One, which includes everything needed (spare parts, food, fuel, etc). The campaign pays for that. If someone flies on AF1 with the president while campaigning (like when Hillary Clinton flew with Obama while she was campaigning), they don't get a free ride. A formula is used to compute what they pay, and it is relative to what it would likely cost for a commercial first-class ticket[\[1\]](#ejDWk){#cite-ejDWk .citation_link}, and the campaign similarly pays for that.
