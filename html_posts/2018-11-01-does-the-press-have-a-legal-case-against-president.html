**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Does the press have a legal case against President Trump for inciting violence against them?** Not even close. We have a 1st Amendment in the United States, and we're pretty proud of it. We're also pretty protective of it. The only way you can be cited for "inciting violence" is if you say words which should reasonably be known to cause "imminent lawless action." There's a three-prong test to see if speech can be regulated as inciting violence: - Imminence. The speech must reasonably lead to "imminent" action, not some action at some unspecified future time. - Intent. The speaker must intend on causing imminent lawless behavior. - Likelihood. The speech must be likely to produce imminent lawless behavior. Trump may say mean things about the press. But the government can only restrict his speech if it reasonably incites imminent lawless behavior. Simply strongly advocating a point is not imminent lawlessness. Simply saying that "someone should take care of that issue" does not also incite imminent lawless behavior.
