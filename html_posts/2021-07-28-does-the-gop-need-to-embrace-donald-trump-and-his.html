**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} The GOP needs to embrace the *idea* of Trump. In other words, you need to support the same policies that Trump did, but without all the baggage of Trump. Trump did some really good things, and he did them well. He espoused the idea of "the political outsider" (even if he was not). He placed himself as someone who is willing to stand up to the special interests. But Trump *the man* came with significant baggage. He is unfortunately too polarizing as a person to continue to be a good candidate himself. His *policies*, however, were wildly popular and even for the ones he was able to accomplish, he probably didn't get the credit he deserved because of just how polarizing he was. **TL;DR: The GOP needs to embrace** ***Trump's vision*, but they need to keep Trump at arm's length.** \ Original question: Does the GOP need to embrace Donald Trump and his vision if they want to win the midterms next year?
