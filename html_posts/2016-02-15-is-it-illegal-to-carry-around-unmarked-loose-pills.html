--- title: '"Is it illegal to carry around unmarked loose pills in your purse? Or is there a law that states any pills you carry must be in their container."' date: '2016-02-15' tags: - law-enforcement - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} It is not illegal to carry around loose pills. If your purse happens to be searched by the police, you may have some \'splainin\' to do if they don\'t know what the pills are either. However, the police can look up the pills online to determine what they are. However, private venues that are \"open to the public\" (such as night clubs) can establish whatever entrance rules they choose as long as such rules are enforced equally against all patrons and do not discriminate based on race, religion, color, sex, or national origin (or sexual orientation in some states). I can tell you that if you were to visit Cheyenne Mountain Air Force Base (also known as NORAD), for example, you would find that they require any medication you carry to be legal and be prescribed to you. Therefore, to carry such medication into the Mountain, you would need to have them in their original prescription bottles (to show that they are both legal and prescribed to you).
