--- title: '"Is it illegal to take a photo or a video in an airport to get evidence that could be used in court?"' date: '2017-05-06' tags: - policy-analysis - law layout: post ---

Answer

{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} In general, unless the facility has a policy prohibiting photography in a sensitive area, the rule in the United States is "if you can see it, you can shoot it." Most areas in which ticketed passengers are allowed in an airport are okay for pictures, as far as I am aware. There may be some restrictions around TSA screening checkpoints; this will be posted at the checkpoint. Whether or not the photo will be admissible in court is another matter altogether, and I don't think it was the thrust of the question, so I'll leave that up to the judge and your lawyer to argue.
