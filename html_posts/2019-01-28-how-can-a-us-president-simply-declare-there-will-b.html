**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **How can a U.S. president simply declare there will be an emergency some number of days in the future, specifically pending the outcome of a funding measure?** I can only assume you're talking about President Trump declaring that there exists a national emergency at our southern border. The President can say whatever he likes; that alone doesn't constitute an official declaration of national emergency. An official declaration of a national emergency is made in accordance with the National Emergencies Act. The National Emergencies Act essentially allows the President to declare that a national emergency exists. What this allows the President to do is to "short-circuit" a law, bypassing certain checks and processes written into that law, in order to implement a portion of a law or activate special legal processes or protections that would normally have to go through Congress to activate. Note that the process allowing a President to bypass a portion of a law using a national emergency *is actually written into that law.* In other words, *Congress already allowed the President to declare a national emergency and bypass a Congressional check in the law that* ***they*** *passed.* Presidents declaring national emergencies is not new, but you haven't heard about it because the media hasn't told you. In case you were unaware: - Jimmy Carter declared two national emergencies, one of which is still active. - Ronald Reagan declared six national emergencies, and none are still active. - George H.W. Bush declared four national emergencies, and none are still active. - Bill Clinton declared seventeen national emergencies, six of which are still active. - George W. Bush declared twelve national emergencies, ten of which are still active. - Barack Obama declared thirteen national emergencies, eleven of which are still active.
