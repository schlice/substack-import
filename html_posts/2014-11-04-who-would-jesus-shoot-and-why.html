**Content:** [The most violent that Jesus is ever recorded to have been was when he threw the moneychangers out of the temple:\ \ ]{.rendered_qtext} > Jesus entered the temple courts and drove out all who were buying and > selling there. He overturned the tables of the money changers and the > benches of those selling doves. \"It is written,\" he said to them, > \"\'My house will be called a house of prayer,\' but you are making it > \'a den of robbers.\'\"\ > (Matthew 21:12-13, NIV)\ \ Even this display of anger on Jesus\' part did not rise to the level of anger needed to fire a weapon. An analogy would be Jesus using a sword or spear, which he never did.\ \ So who would Jesus have shot in biblical times? No one.\ \ I think the analogy is to today\'s times, implying that Jesus would never shoot anyone. I think that\'s a pretty poor analogy, as times are different. Jesus would shoot whoever needed to be shot, with an utter sadness in His heart for having to do it.
