{.rendered_qtext}
**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} **Can a person with multiple sclerosis die of a urinary tract infection?** Any person *can* die from a urinary tract infection (UTI), not just people with MS. Let's be more specific. The UTI itself won't kill you. However, that infection can triggger a disease called *sepsis*. In sepsis, chemicals released into the bloodstream to deal with an infection start to cause inflammation throughout the body. An out-of-control reaction to an infection can cause a chain reaction of inflammation throughout the body, damaging organs and leading to a condition called *septic shock*, which can be fatal. People with MS often experience bladder-related dysfunction due to the disease, so the incidence of UTIs in people with MS is higher than the general population. Therefore, the risk of septic shock from a UTI is greater in the population of MS patients than in the general population, because the risk of UTIs is greater.
