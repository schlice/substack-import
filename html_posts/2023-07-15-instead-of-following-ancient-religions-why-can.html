**Content:** []{.rendered_qtext} You certainly can do that. Nothing is stopping you. You can believe in anything you like, or nothing at all. In fact, if you're truly motivated enough, you can even start your own religion! [[https://www.wikihow.com/Start-a-\...](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=f2fe90&url=https://www.wikihow.com/Start-a-Religion){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikihow.com"}]{.qlink_container} Organized religions happen because *more than one person believes similarly*. Christianity has [[more followers](https://www.quora.com/_/redirect?sig=8d8bba&url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_religious_populations){.external_link rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this);" qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org"}]{.qlink_container} than any other world religion at 2.382 billion followers worldwide (31.11% of the world's population), based on the 2020 projection using 2010 numbers. That's a lot of people who believe similarly. But hey, if you are dissatisfied with all of the world's existing religions, you can just abandon them all and come up with your own "religion" which dictates how you should live your own life. Most people just call it "atheism." (Cue the atheists in the comments who will be quick to tell me that atheism is not a religion. Before you do, though, I suggest you read through the WikiHow identifying the elements of a religion). Anyway, here's my advice to you: don't bother with all that religion stuff. Just live your life in the best way you know how. Call it a religion if you want others to believe as you do, otherwise just call it "how I live." If you're just not interested in answers to questions like *why am I here*, or *is there anything more to this life*, then don't bother with religion. Because just building something to believe in, that will help you live this life, will not answer those deep, nagging questions, especially that one little voice in the back of your head, when you survey the splendor of this world, that whispers "*This is amazing, but why? There must be a greater purpose to this life than* ***just this****."* It is only religion that can answer such questions.
